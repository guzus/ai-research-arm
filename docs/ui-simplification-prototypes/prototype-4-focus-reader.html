Model routing

AI research workflows are moving from dashboards to focused reading loops

The strongest ARA direction is not another console. It is a narrowed reader that lets analysts move from a ranked queue to one briefing, with evidence close enough to verify without becoming a second workspace.

Signal score 72 12 sources 3 open claims Owner: research desk

The core interface question is mechanical: what does a researcher need in the minute after a briefing appears? They need to decide whether the item deserves attention, read the synthesized claim, and inspect the strongest supporting or conflicting evidence. Every extra navigation lane competes with that loop.

This layout keeps one primary axis. The left queue answers what to read next, the center carries the current briefing, and the right rail explains why the item is trusted. The header stays limited to search and two durable actions, avoiding a second set of tabs, filters, or modes.

Simplification here means fewer competing decisions: one queue, one article, one evidence rail, and no decorative surface that asks for attention.

The evidence panel is intentionally narrow. It exposes confidence, source shape, and analyst notes without becoming a full research table. If a reader needs deeper work, the source buttons can carry that transition while the default state remains quiet.
