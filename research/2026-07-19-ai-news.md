### Anthropic in Early Talks to Lease Up to $10 Billion in AI Computing Power From Meta

**Date:** July 17, 2026 | **Sources:** [CNBC — Anthropic in early talks with Meta to acquire compute power](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/17/anthropic-meta-ai-compute.html), [Bloomberg — Meta in Talks to Sell Computing Power to Anthropic, NYT Reports](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-17/meta-in-talks-to-sell-computing-power-to-anthropic-nyt-reports), [CNN Business — Meta in talks to rent some of its billions in AI infrastructure to Anthropic](https://www.cnn.com/2026/07/17/tech/meta-anthropic-ai-cloud-computing)
- Anthropic and Meta are in very preliminary talks for Anthropic to lease AI computing capacity from Meta in a deal that could be worth as much as $10 billion paid in monthly increments over two years, with either side able to exit early; both companies declined to comment.
- The prospective arrangement would be smaller than Anthropic's existing $45 billion, three-year compute deal with Elon Musk's SpaceX (using SpaceX's Colossus 1 data center) announced in May, and comes as Meta CEO Mark Zuckerberg has floated entering the cloud-computing business to show investors its roughly $145 billion 2026 AI capex can generate outside revenue.
- **Why it matters:** It's a striking sign of how compute-constrained even a well-funded frontier lab remains ahead of a potential IPO, and it would put two AI rivals — Meta the model-builder and Anthropic the model-builder — in an unusual infrastructure-customer relationship.

## Industry Moves

### AGIBOT Unveils Four New Embodied-AI Products at WAIC 2026, Including the A3 Ultra Humanoid

**Date:** July 18, 2026 | **Sources:** [AGIBOT — AGIBOT Unveils Four New Products at WAIC 2026](https://www.agibot.com/article/231/detail/85.html), [Tech Times — AGIBOT Debuts Four Robots at WAIC 2026: Global Export Push Meets China's Spy Law Obligation](https://www.techtimes.com/articles/320899/20260718/agibot-debuts-four-robots-waic-2026-global-export-push-meets-chinas-spy-law-obligation.htm), [Global Times — China's humanoid robot makers eye global expansion as 2026 WAIC highlights industry's rapid rise](https://www.globaltimes.cn/page/202607/1366238.shtml)
- At the World AI Conference in Shanghai, Chinese robotics maker AGIBOT unveiled four products simultaneously: the A3 Ultra full-size humanoid (1.74m, 60kg, 51 active degrees of freedom, NVIDIA Thor-powered, 5kg-per-arm payload, up to 8 hours of operating time via hot-swap batteries), the X2 Edu education platform, the G2 Max heavy-payload industrial robot, and the OmniHand 3 Ultra-M dexterous hand.
- The A3 Ultra was named a "Gem of the Exhibition," the only embodied-AI product among WAIC's ten award honorees this year, and more than 30 AGIBOT units are operating live across the conference venues for visitor guidance and demonstrations; AGIBOT's co-founder framed the showcase as part of a push to export the technology globally, with the company already holding roughly 39% of the global humanoid-robot market.
- **Why it matters:** It's a concrete, hardware-specific data point on how fast Chinese embodied-AI manufacturers are iterating and scaling deployment, arriving the same week as WAIC's Xi Jinping keynote and the 29-country WAICO founding agreement covered in the prior report — reinforcing Shanghai's WAIC as this year's focal point for China's global AI/robotics ambitions.

### Google's Gemini 3.5 Pro Slips Months Behind Schedule, Erasing ~$200 Billion From Alphabet's Market Cap

**Date:** July 16, 2026 | **Sources:** [CNBC — Alphabet shares fall on report its most powerful AI model Gemini 3.5 Pro is delayed](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/16/alphabet-stock-gemini-3-5-pro-ai.html), [Bloomberg — Google Gemini Launch Delayed as Tech Falls Short of Internal Goals](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-16/google-gemini-launch-delayed-as-tech-falls-short-of-internal-goals), [Seeking Alpha — Google's Gemini 3.5 Pro release faces delay: report](https://seekingalpha.com/news/4614636-googles-gemini-3_5-pro-release-faces-delay-report)
- Gemini 3.5 Pro — announced at Google I/O in May with an expected June release and already in internal use — is now months behind schedule after its coding capabilities fell short of internal targets, Bloomberg reported.
- Alphabet shares fell about 4.4% on July 16, wiping out roughly $200 billion in market value; the timing is notable given OpenAI's GPT-5.6 Sol (~July 8-9) and Meta's Muse Spark 1.1 (July 9) both shipped coding-focused updates in the preceding week.
- **Why it matters:** It's a concrete, market-moving data point that the "Big Three" closed-model race is no longer a given for Google, arriving the same week Moonshot's open-weight Kimi K3 topped a coding leaderboard (covered in the prior report) — reinforcing a narrative of Western closed-lab frontier slippage versus faster-moving rivals.

### Apple Briefly Overtakes Nvidia as World's Most Valuable Company Amid AI Investment Reassessment

**Date:** July 17, 2026 | **Sources:** [CNBC — Apple, Nvidia vie for title of world's most valuable company](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/17/apple-nvidia-aapl-nvda-market-cap.html), [Bloomberg — Apple Briefly Tops Nvidia as World's Most Valuable Company](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-17/apple-tops-nvidia-as-world-s-largest-company-amid-tech-rotation), [Forbes — Apple Briefly Unseats Nvidia As World's Largest Company](https://www.forbes.com/sites/tylerroush/2026/07/17/apple-unseats-nvidia-as-worlds-largest-company/)
- Apple's market cap briefly hit $4.88 trillion on July 17, edging past Nvidia (~$4.86 trillion, down 3.5% on the day) to reclaim the world's-most-valuable-company title for the first time since April 2025, ending Nvidia's roughly year-long run at the top.
- The swap reflects investors broadening away from pure AI-infrastructure plays like Nvidia toward diversified tech as Wall Street reassesses the pace of AI capex spending; Nvidia has held the top spot since surpassing Microsoft in June 2025.
- **Why it matters:** It's a market-sentiment signal, not a product announcement, but a real-money readout on AI-trade conviction the same week TSMC raised capex guidance (prior report) — suggesting investors are starting to price AI infrastructure spending and AI-model competitiveness as distinct bets.

### WAIC Academic (WAICA) Opens With 57 Papers Accepted From 282 Submissions

**Date:** July 18, 2026 | **Source:** WAICA opening-ceremony program, World Artificial Intelligence Conference, Shanghai
- The academic track of WAIC 2026 (WAICA) opened July 18 with its inaugural Best Paper Award session, accepting roughly 57 of 282 valid submissions (~20% acceptance rate), with contributing researchers from Princeton, Cambridge, Imperial College London, Tsinghua, Peking University, Shanghai Jiao Tong University, and Nanyang Technological University.
- **Why it matters:** It's a concrete signal that WAIC is building out a peer-reviewed academic component alongside its industry/product showcase (AGIBOT's robot unveilings, covered above), part of Shanghai's push to position WAIC as a venue with research legitimacy, not just a trade show.
