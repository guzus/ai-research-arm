### Anthropic in Early Talks to Lease Up to $10 Billion in AI Computing Power From Meta

**Date:** July 17, 2026 | **Sources:** [CNBC — Anthropic in early talks with Meta to acquire compute power](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/17/anthropic-meta-ai-compute.html), [Bloomberg — Meta in Talks to Sell Computing Power to Anthropic, NYT Reports](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-17/meta-in-talks-to-sell-computing-power-to-anthropic-nyt-reports), [CNN Business — Meta in talks to rent some of its billions in AI infrastructure to Anthropic](https://www.cnn.com/2026/07/17/tech/meta-anthropic-ai-cloud-computing)
- Anthropic and Meta are in very preliminary talks for Anthropic to lease AI computing capacity from Meta in a deal that could be worth as much as $10 billion paid in monthly increments over two years, with either side able to exit early; both companies declined to comment.
- The prospective arrangement would be smaller than Anthropic's existing $45 billion, three-year compute deal with Elon Musk's SpaceX (using SpaceX's Colossus 1 data center) announced in May, and comes as Meta CEO Mark Zuckerberg has floated entering the cloud-computing business to show investors its roughly $145 billion 2026 AI capex can generate outside revenue.
- **Why it matters:** It's a striking sign of how compute-constrained even a well-funded frontier lab remains ahead of a potential IPO, and it would put two AI rivals — Meta the model-builder and Anthropic the model-builder — in an unusual infrastructure-customer relationship.

## Industry Moves

### AGIBOT Unveils Four New Embodied-AI Products at WAIC 2026, Including the A3 Ultra Humanoid

**Date:** July 18, 2026 | **Sources:** [AGIBOT — AGIBOT Unveils Four New Products at WAIC 2026](https://www.agibot.com/article/231/detail/85.html), [Tech Times — AGIBOT Debuts Four Robots at WAIC 2026: Global Export Push Meets China's Spy Law Obligation](https://www.techtimes.com/articles/320899/20260718/agibot-debuts-four-robots-waic-2026-global-export-push-meets-chinas-spy-law-obligation.htm), [Global Times — China's humanoid robot makers eye global expansion as 2026 WAIC highlights industry's rapid rise](https://www.globaltimes.cn/page/202607/1366238.shtml)
- At the World AI Conference in Shanghai, Chinese robotics maker AGIBOT unveiled four products simultaneously: the A3 Ultra full-size humanoid (1.74m, 60kg, 51 active degrees of freedom, NVIDIA Thor-powered, 5kg-per-arm payload, up to 8 hours of operating time via hot-swap batteries), the X2 Edu education platform, the G2 Max heavy-payload industrial robot, and the OmniHand 3 Ultra-M dexterous hand.
- The A3 Ultra was named a "Gem of the Exhibition," the only embodied-AI product among WAIC's ten award honorees this year, and more than 30 AGIBOT units are operating live across the conference venues for visitor guidance and demonstrations; AGIBOT's co-founder framed the showcase as part of a push to export the technology globally, with the company already holding roughly 39% of the global humanoid-robot market.
- **Why it matters:** It's a concrete, hardware-specific data point on how fast Chinese embodied-AI manufacturers are iterating and scaling deployment, arriving the same week as WAIC's Xi Jinping keynote and the 29-country WAICO founding agreement covered in the prior report — reinforcing Shanghai's WAIC as this year's focal point for China's global AI/robotics ambitions.
