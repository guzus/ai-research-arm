## Breaking News

### Anthropic Poaches UC Berkeley EECS Chair Jelani Nelson — Another Marquee Academic Defection

**Date:** July 1, 2026 | **Sources:** [@minilek (Jelani Nelson)](https://x.com/minilek/status/2072322757908664728), [Jelani Nelson — People @EECS, UC Berkeley](https://people.eecs.berkeley.edu/~minilek/)

- **What's net-new:** Jelani Nelson — theoretical computer scientist and **chair of
  UC Berkeley's EECS department** — announced he has **joined Anthropic** and taken
  leave from the university, joining as a Member of Technical Staff: *"Excited to
  work with many talented, mission-driven people on the defining technology of our
  time."*
- **Why it matters:** This is the latest in a steady stream of senior academics and
  researchers moving into frontier labs (TechCrunch has tracked researchers leaving
  Google for rivals through June). Losing a *sitting department chair* — not just a
  professor — is a sharper signal of how hard the labs are pulling on academic
  talent, and it lands the same week Anthropic shipped Sonnet 5 and Claude Science.
- **Skeptic's note:** He took *leave*, not a full resignation, and the role is an
  individual-contributor MTS seat — this is a high-profile hire, not evidence of an
  org-level Berkeley→Anthropic pipeline. Sourced primarily to his own post.

### xAI Ships a No-Code Voice Agent Builder — $0.05/min Audio, 25+ Languages, ~2-Minute Setup

**Date:** July 1, 2026 | **Sources:** [xAI — Introducing the Voice Agent Builder](https://x.ai/news/grok-voice-agent-builder), [Crypto Briefing — xAI Voice Agent Builder beta pricing](https://cryptobriefing.com/xai-voice-agent-builder-beta-pricing/)

- **What's net-new:** xAI launched the **Voice Agent Builder**, a **no-code**
  platform on the xAI Console for building production voice agents on **Grok Voice**.
  You write a plain-language description of the call flow, attach documents, tools
  and guardrails, and go from zero to a working agent in **~2 minutes** — telephony,
  knowledge retrieval, tools, guardrails, MCP support and observability bundled in.
- **Specs/pricing:** **25+ languages**, **80+ voices**, sub-second-latency claims,
  priced at **$0.05/min for agent audio + $0.01/min for telephony** — positioning
  xAI as the budget option against Vapi/ElevenLabs/OpenAI Realtime.
- **Why it matters:** Extends xAI's April `grok-voice-think-fast-1.0` reasoning
  voice model into a packaged agent-ops product, moving the company up the stack
  from model to full voice-agent platform — the same "own the deployment surface"
  logic driving OpenAI/Anthropic/AWS forward-deployed pushes.
- **Skeptic's note:** Latency and "80+ voices" are xAI's own marketing numbers with
  no independent benchmark at launch; the aggressive price is a land-grab, not a
  proven unit economics story.

## Funding & Acquisitions

### Venice AI Becomes a Privacy-First Unicorn — $65M Series A at a $1B Valuation

**Date:** July 1, 2026 | **Sources:** [TechCrunch — Venice AI becomes a unicorn with $65M Series A](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/01/venice-ai-becomes-a-unicorn-with-65m-series-a-as-its-privacy-first-ai-platform-takes-off/), [The Block — Venice AI raises $65M at $1B valuation led by Dragonfly](https://www.theblock.co/post/406934/venice-ai-funding-equity-valuation-dragonfly), [Unite.AI](https://www.unite.ai/venice-ai-raises-65m-at-1b-valuation-as-private-ai-moves-into-the-mainstream/)

- **What's net-new:** **Venice AI** — the privacy-first inference platform founded by
  early-bitcoin advocate **Erik Voorhees** (ShapeShift, Satoshi Dice) — raised a
  **$65M Series A at a $1B valuation**, its **first external fundraise**. The round
  was **led by Dragonfly**, with Coinbase Ventures, North Island Ventures and others.
- **The numbers:** **~3M users**, **profitable** since Q1 2026, **>$70M annualized
  revenue run-rate**, **~1.7M API calls/day** across **200+ models**. Architecture is
  client-side-encrypted with requests relayed through an external proxy — Venice says
  it stores no user data on its own servers.
- **Use of funds:** Venice plans to **stop leasing third-party GPUs** and buy its own
  hardware / build proprietary data centers to improve gross margin — an
  application-layer player moving down the stack to own inference economics (echoing
  Base44's "Base1" defensibility move from July 1).

## Research Papers

- **Remote Labor Index (RLI) leaderboard refresh — Fable 5 out front, but real
  freelance work stays mostly un-automated.** The **Scale AI × Center for AI Safety**
  RLI — real, paid, self-contained freelance projects (software, design,
  architecture, data analysis; median ~11.5 human-hours, ~$200 value) — surfaced
  updated frontier-agent results this window, with **Claude Fable 5 reported leading
  at ~16%** (roughly 2× the next model, ahead of Opus 4.8 and Codex/GPT-5.5) per the
  Twitter research pulse. **Even the leader still fails ~84% of tasks.** **Sources:**
  [Scale Labs — RLI leaderboard](https://labs.scale.com/leaderboard/rli), [RLI paper (arXiv)](https://arxiv.org/html/2510.26787v1)
  - **Skeptic's note:** The **published RLI paper (Oct 2025) put the *floor* at
    2.5%** (best agent = Manus); the **~16%** is the newer *live* leaderboard with
    2026 frontier agents and comes via the internal Twitter pulse, **not** an
    independently reconfirmed Scale post at time of writing. Treat the *direction*
    (Fable 5 leads, absolute rates still low) as solid and the exact number as
    provisional. Note also Fable 5's suspension/restore churn may affect which runs
    are currently listed.
