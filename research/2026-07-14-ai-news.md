### Meta Commits More Than $50 Billion to Louisiana Hyperion Data Center, Expanding to 5 Gigawatts

**Date:** July 13, 2026 | **Sources:** [CNBC — Meta's Louisiana data center investment reaches $50 billion, aided by generous tax incentives](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/13/meta-louisiana-data-center-investment-reaches-50-billion-amid-ai-push.html), [Insurance Journal — Meta Expands Louisiana Data Center to 5 Gigawatts, Investment Crosses $50B](https://www.insurancejournal.com/news/southcentral/2026/07/13/877388.htm), [Fortune — Meta's AI data center cost went from $10 billion to $50 billion in under 2 years](https://fortune.com/2026/07/13/meta-hyperion-louisiana-50-billion-tax-breaks-locals/)

- Meta announced its Richland Parish, Louisiana facility — home to **Hyperion**, the company's largest AI training cluster — will expand to nearly 10 million square feet and **5 gigawatts** of IT capacity, with total investment now exceeding **$50 billion**.
- The project's estimated cost has grown from $10 billion at inception, to $27 billion when Meta and Blue Owl Capital formed a joint venture for the build-out in October, to over $50 billion now. The 5 GW phase is targeted for completion around 2032 (an initial 2 GW phase by 2030).
- Louisiana granted a 20-year sales tax exemption for data centers built before 2029 to help lure the project; Meta says it has awarded more than $1.6 billion in contracts to local businesses since construction began in December 2024.
- **Why it matters:** This is a distinct project from the previously reported $13B Alberta, Canada data center — Meta is now running parallel multi-billion-dollar AI infrastructure buildouts across at least two continents, underscoring how compute buildout costs keep escalating well past initial public estimates.
