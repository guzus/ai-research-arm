### Meta Commits More Than $50 Billion to Louisiana Hyperion Data Center, Expanding to 5 Gigawatts

**Date:** July 13, 2026 | **Sources:** [CNBC — Meta's Louisiana data center investment reaches $50 billion, aided by generous tax incentives](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/13/meta-louisiana-data-center-investment-reaches-50-billion-amid-ai-push.html), [Insurance Journal — Meta Expands Louisiana Data Center to 5 Gigawatts, Investment Crosses $50B](https://www.insurancejournal.com/news/southcentral/2026/07/13/877388.htm), [Fortune — Meta's AI data center cost went from $10 billion to $50 billion in under 2 years](https://fortune.com/2026/07/13/meta-hyperion-louisiana-50-billion-tax-breaks-locals/)

- Meta announced its Richland Parish, Louisiana facility — home to **Hyperion**, the company's largest AI training cluster — will expand to nearly 10 million square feet and **5 gigawatts** of IT capacity, with total investment now exceeding **$50 billion**.
- The project's estimated cost has grown from $10 billion at inception, to $27 billion when Meta and Blue Owl Capital formed a joint venture for the build-out in October, to over $50 billion now. The 5 GW phase is targeted for completion around 2032 (an initial 2 GW phase by 2030).
- Louisiana granted a 20-year sales tax exemption for data centers built before 2029 to help lure the project; Meta says it has awarded more than $1.6 billion in contracts to local businesses since construction began in December 2024.
- **Why it matters:** This is a distinct project from the previously reported $13B Alberta, Canada data center — Meta is now running parallel multi-billion-dollar AI infrastructure buildouts across at least two continents, underscoring how compute buildout costs keep escalating well past initial public estimates.

### OpenAI Asks Judge to Rule xAI's Trade-Secret Suit "Should Never Have Been Filed," Seeks $1M+ in Fees

**Date:** July 13, 2026 | **Sources:** [Bloomberg — OpenAI's Feud With xAI Carries On as Apple Secrets Fight Revs Up](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-14/openai-s-feud-with-xai-carries-on-as-apple-secrets-fight-revs-up), [The News (Pakistan) — Why OpenAI wants $1 million from Elon Musk's xAI amid Apple lawsuit](https://www.thenews.com.pk/latest/1409077-why-openai-wants-1-million-from-elon-musks-xai-amid-apple-lawsuit)

- OpenAI filed a motion Monday (July 13) asking a federal judge to formally find that xAI's trade-secret-theft lawsuit — accusing OpenAI of poaching employees and encouraging them to steal confidential information — "should never have been filed," and to award OpenAI more than $1 million in legal fees.
- The filing came hours after xAI gave notice it intends to appeal the same claims, which a federal judge in San Francisco already dismissed earlier this year for lack of evidence that OpenAI encouraged any misconduct.
- OpenAI's lawyers argued xAI "sued first and looked for evidence later, forcing OpenAI to spend substantial resources defeating a sprawling, aggressively litigated trade secret claim for which xAI had no evidentiary support."
- **Why it matters:** This is a distinct dispute from the previously reported Apple v. OpenAI trade-secret suit — OpenAI is now simultaneously defending against Apple's claims and going on the offensive against xAI's, underscoring how legal combat between frontier labs has intensified alongside the technology race.

### Nvidia Halves Its Approved Asian AI-Chip Buyer List Amid Tighter China-Diversion Screening

**Date:** July 13-14, 2026 | **Sources:** [Financial Times, via Yahoo Finance — Nvidia halves Asia buyer list in China chip crackdown, FT reports](https://finance.yahoo.com/technology/ai/articles/nvidia-halves-asia-buyer-list-033140201.html), [Benzinga — Nvidia Reportedly Cuts More Than Half of Its Asian AI Chip Customers Amid Tighter China Screening](https://www.benzinga.com/markets/tech/26/07/60433026/nvidia-reportedly-cuts-more-than-half-of-its-asian-ai-chip-customers-amid-tighter-china-screening), [Seeking Alpha — Nvidia halves Asian AI chip buyer list amid tighter China screening](https://seekingalpha.com/news/4613408-nvidia-halves-asian-ai-chip-buyer-list-amid-tighter-china-screening---report)

- Nvidia has more than halved the number of Asian customers authorized to buy its AI chips, after introducing a new "white list" of buyers that passed tougher compliance checks designed to keep chips from being diverted to China.
- The company has spent recent months intensifying due diligence in Singapore, Malaysia, and Japan — including staff visiting data centers, verifying contracts, and interviewing end users — with many prior customers, particularly neo-cloud providers, failing the initial review and being dropped (though they can reapply after remediation).
- The screening tightens compliance with U.S. export controls — including the rules announced in late May 2026 targeting Nvidia's most advanced processors (the Blackwell series) — that require licenses for any transfer to entities headquartered in China or Macau.
- **Why it matters:** This is Nvidia's most concrete self-imposed enforcement action yet against chip diversion to China, going beyond the letter of U.S. export law into unilateral, hands-on customer vetting — a sign the company is trying to preempt further regulatory escalation rather than wait for it.
