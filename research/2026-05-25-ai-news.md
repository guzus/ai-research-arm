**Date:** Monday May 25, 2026 (release/presentation; net-new) | **Sources:** [Vatican News — Pope Leo XIV's first encyclical *Magnifica humanitas* to be published May 25](https://www.vaticannews.va/en/pope/news/2026-05/pope-leo-xiv-first-encyclical-magnifica-humanitas.html), [RTÉ — Pope Leo's first encyclical to focus on AI concerns](https://www.rte.ie/news/world/2026/0525/1575047-pope-artificial-intelligence/), [PBS NewsHour — Pope Leo XIV to launch his first encyclical, on AI, with Anthropic's co-founder](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/world/pope-leo-xiv-to-launch-his-first-encylical-a-document-on-artificial-intelligence-with-anthropics-co-founder), [NBC News — Pope Leo will take on AI alongside an Anthropic co-founder](https://www.nbcnews.com/tech/tech-news/pope-leo-address-human-dignity-age-ai-rcna345744), [National Catholic Reporter — Pope Leo to present his encyclical on AI alongside Anthropic co-founder](https://www.ncronline.org/vatican/vatican-news/pope-leo-present-his-encyclical-ai-alongside-anthropic-co-founder), [Religion News Service — Why Anthropic is helping unveil the pope's new encyclical on AI](https://religionnews.com/2026/05/22/why-anthropic-is-helping-unveil-the-popes-new-encyclical-on-ai/), [eWeek — Pope Leo XIV's first AI encyclical will feature Anthropic co-founder](https://www.eweek.com/news/pope-leo-xiv-ai-encyclical-anthropic-christopher-olah/)

The Holy See published **Pope Leo XIV's first encyclical letter, *Magnifica Humanitas*** — the highest form of papal teaching document — and built the rollout directly around the AI industry:

- **The document:** *Magnifica Humanitas* ("Magnificent Humanity") is centered on **"the protection of the human person in the time of artificial intelligence."** Leo argues the **central challenge of AI is "not technological, but anthropological,"** and warns that promoting technology "at the expense of human dignity" reflects an **"eclipse of the sense of what it means to be human."** It was **signed May 15** — deliberately timed to the **135th anniversary** of Leo XIII's *Rerum Novarum*, the foundational Catholic social-teaching document on workers' rights in the Industrial Revolution.
- **The presentation:** The encyclical was **presented at 11:30 a.m. at the Vatican's Synod Hall**, with **Pope Leo personally taking part** — an **atypical move** for a pope at the launch of his own encyclical. He was joined by **Christopher Olah, co-founder of Anthropic** (maker of Claude), plus Cardinals **Víctor Manuel Fernández** (Doctrine of the Faith), **Michael Czerny, S.J.** (Integral Human Development), and **Pietro Parolin** (Secretary of State, concluding remarks), and theologian **Anna Rowlands** (Durham).
- **The friction:** Olah's presence drew a **"scattering of unease"** among some observers, and reporting frames it against an **escalating public clash between Pope Leo and the Trump administration** over the use of AI models in military and surveillance contexts.

**Read-through:** This is the **first time a sitting pope has made frontier AI the subject of his inaugural encyclical** — the genre Catholic teaching reserves for civilizational questions — and the choice to **share the stage with an AI-lab founder** signals the Vatican wants to be a **first-order moral authority shaping the AI debate**, not a bystander. For Anthropic specifically, the optics are double-edged: a **legitimacy signal** ("safety-first lab the Church will stand next to") that also **deepens its visibility in the regulation/values fight** at the exact moment Washington has retreated from a federal pre-deployment regime (the pulled executive order). It is the values-and-governance counterweight to a May dominated by capability and capital headlines.
