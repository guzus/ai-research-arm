### Dow Jones Crosses 50,000 for First Time as Markets Rebound from Tech Rout
**Date:** February 6, 2026

The Dow Jones Industrial Average closed above 50,000 for the first time in history, finishing at **50,115.67** — up 1,206 points (+2.5%) on the day. The milestone came during a sharp market rebound after the week-long tech selloff.

**Key Market Moves (Feb 6):**
- **S&P 500:** Up 2.0%, recovering to 6,846
- **Nasdaq:** Up 2.6%, led by semiconductor stocks
- **Nvidia:** Surged **8.0%** to $185.41, trimming weekly losses
- **Chipmaker index:** Up 5.7% on the day
- **Bitcoin:** Rebounded alongside equities after multi-day decline

**What Drove the Rebound:**
Nvidia CEO Jensen Huang appeared on CNBC's "Halftime Report" and declared AI demand is **"sky high"** and that infrastructure spending will continue for **7–8 years**. He called the ~$660 billion in planned hyperscaler capex "appropriate and sustainable," countering the investor panic that had driven the selloff earlier in the week.

### Mistral Voxtral Transcribe 2 — On-Device Speech-to-Text

### Varonis Acquires AllTrue.ai for $150M — AI Security Play
**Date:** February 3–5, 2026

Data security company **Varonis Systems** (NASDAQ: VRNS) acquired **AllTrue.ai**, an AI Trust, Risk, and Security Management (AI TRiSM) startup, in a deal valued at **$150 million**.

**What AllTrue.ai Does:**
- Provides visibility into all AI systems across an organization — including **shadow AI**
- Tracks what AI systems are connected to, what actions they can take, and whether they're safe
- Enforces guardrails in real-time to prevent unsafe, non-compliant, or unintended AI behavior

**Strategic Rationale:**
Combines AllTrue.ai's real-time AI observability and enforcement with Varonis's Data Security Platform, giving customers a unified way to understand what AI systems exist, what data they can access, and how they behave in production.

## Regulatory Updates

### State AI Legislation Surge — Oklahoma, Michigan, New York
**Date:** February 6, 2026

A wave of state-level AI regulation bills is moving through legislatures across the U.S., according to the Transparency Coalition's Feb 6 legislative update.

**Oklahoma (12+ AI Bills):**
- **HB 2321:** AI-Generated Content Accountability and Privacy Protection Act
- **HB 1742:** Provisions related to companion chatbots
- **HB 2862:** Prohibits digital impersonation via deepfakes
- **HB 665:** Regulates AI and algorithmic pricing
- **HB 524:** Penalties for AI models that suggest self-harm or harming others
- **HB 579:** Regulates AI use by health insurers
- **HB 469:** Declares AI systems nonsentient, prohibits legal personhood
- **HB 628:** Creates independent verification organization for AI risk mitigation

**Michigan:**
- **HB 4668:** Requires large AI developers to conduct regular risk assessments, third-party audits, and publicly disclose safety protocols. Carried over from 2025 session.

**New York:**
- **SB-8420A:** Signed into law Dec 11, 2025 — requires disclosure when ads contain AI-generated "synthetic performers." Takes effect June 9, 2026. Penalties: $1,000 first offense, $5,000 subsequent.
- **SB S8391:** Strengthens right of publicity for deceased performers in the AI era, requiring consent from heirs for digital replicas.
- **Stop Deepfakes Act:** Proposed bill requiring synthetic content metadata from AI providers and preservation by social media platforms.
