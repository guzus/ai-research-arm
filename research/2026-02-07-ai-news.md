### Dow Jones Crosses 50,000 for First Time as Markets Rebound from Tech Rout
**Date:** February 6, 2026

The Dow Jones Industrial Average closed above 50,000 for the first time in history, finishing at **50,115.67** — up 1,206 points (+2.5%) on the day. The milestone came during a sharp market rebound after the week-long tech selloff.

**Key Market Moves (Feb 6):**
- **S&P 500:** Up 2.0%, recovering to 6,846
- **Nasdaq:** Up 2.6%, led by semiconductor stocks
- **Nvidia:** Surged **8.0%** to $185.41, trimming weekly losses
- **Chipmaker index:** Up 5.7% on the day
- **Bitcoin:** Rebounded alongside equities after multi-day decline

**What Drove the Rebound:**
Nvidia CEO Jensen Huang appeared on CNBC's "Halftime Report" and declared AI demand is **"sky high"** and that infrastructure spending will continue for **7–8 years**. He called the ~$660 billion in planned hyperscaler capex "appropriate and sustainable," countering the investor panic that had driven the selloff earlier in the week.

### OpenAI vs. Anthropic Super Bowl Ad War Escalates
**Date:** February 4–6, 2026 (escalating through the week)

The rivalry between OpenAI and Anthropic reached peak public spectacle ahead of Super Bowl LX (Sunday, Feb 8), with Anthropic airing attack ads mocking OpenAI's plans to introduce ads into ChatGPT, and CEO Sam Altman firing back.

**Anthropic's Ads:**
- Four commercials titled **"Betrayal," "Deception," "Treachery,"** and **"Violation"**
- Tagline: **"Ads are coming to AI. But not to Claude."**
- Depict scenarios where a chatbot builds trust then pivots to product pitches (e.g., promoting a "mature dating site" mid-conversation)
- 60-second pregame + 30-second in-game spots during Super Bowl LX

**Sam Altman's Response:**
- Called the ads "funny" but **"clearly dishonest"**
- Said: "We would obviously never run ads in the way Anthropic depicts them. We are not stupid."
- Argued that Anthropic "serves an expensive product to rich people" while OpenAI needs ads to bring AI to billions who can't afford subscriptions
- Claimed: "More Texans use ChatGPT for free than total people use Claude in the U.S."
- Accused Anthropic of wanting to "control what people can and can't use AI for"

**OpenAI's Own Ad:**
- Separate 60-second Super Bowl spot focused on "builders, and how anyone can now build anything"
- Timed alongside the announcement that the **Codex app has surpassed 500,000 downloads** since launching Feb 2

### Mistral Voxtral Transcribe 2 — On-Device Speech-to-Text
**Date:** February 4, 2026

Mistral AI released **Voxtral Transcribe 2**, a next-generation speech-to-text model family focused on privacy, speed, and cost efficiency.

**Two Models:**
- **Voxtral Mini Transcribe V2:** Batch transcription with speaker diarization, context biasing, and word-level timestamps
- **Voxtral Realtime:** Live transcription with latency configurable down to **sub-200ms**, open-weights under Apache 2.0

**Performance:**
- **4% word error rate** on FLEURS benchmark
- **$0.003/minute** — claimed best price-performance of any transcription API
- Natively multilingual: **13 languages** (English, Chinese, Hindi, Spanish, Arabic, French, Portuguese, Russian, German, Japanese, Korean, Italian, Dutch)
- Designed to run locally on smartphones and laptops

### Goldman Sachs Partners with Anthropic to Build AI Agents for Banking
**Date:** February 6, 2026

Goldman Sachs revealed it has been working with Anthropic for **six months**, with embedded Anthropic engineers co-developing autonomous AI agents for core banking functions.

**Current Agent Development:**
- **Trade accounting:** Automating the accounting of trades and transactions
- **Client onboarding/vetting:** Automating compliance-heavy client vetting processes
- Both described as "digital co-workers" for process-intensive roles

**Key Quote:**

**Future Plans:**

### Jensen Huang: AI Infrastructure Buildout Will Last 7–8 Years
**Date:** February 6, 2026

In a major CNBC interview on Friday, Nvidia CEO Jensen Huang pushed back against investor fears about the sustainability of massive AI infrastructure spending.

**Key Statements:**
- AI demand is **"sky high"** and compute constraints are real for companies like Anthropic and OpenAI
- The infrastructure buildout will continue for **7–8 years**
- The ~$660 billion in planned hyperscaler capex is **"appropriate and sustainable"**

**Context:** The interview came after a week in which Amazon, Alphabet, Meta, and Microsoft collectively announced **$630 billion+** in 2026 AI capex, triggering panic selling. Huang's comments reassured markets that the spending reflects genuine demand, not speculation.

### Hyperscaler AI Capex Approaches $700 Billion for 2026
**Date:** February 6, 2026

Combined capital spending from the four major hyperscalers is now projected to approach **$700 billion** in 2026 — an increase of more than 60% from the historic levels reached in 2025.

**Updated 2026 Capex Forecasts:**
- **Amazon:** $200 billion
- **Alphabet:** $175–185 billion
- **Meta:** $115–135 billion
- **Microsoft:** ~$115 billion (run rate)
- **Total:** ~$605–635 billion (with other cloud providers pushing toward $700B)

Cash reserves are taking a significant hit. The companies are loading up on high-priced chips, building mammoth new facilities, and buying networking technology to connect it all.
