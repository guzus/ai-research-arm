### **An OpenAI Reasoning Model Autonomously Disproved The 80-Year-Old Erdős Unit-Distance Conjecture In Discrete Geometry — First Time An AI Has Independently Resolved A Prominent Open Problem; Used Deep Algebraic Number Theory (Golod–Shafarevich / Infinite Class-Field Towers) For A Polynomial Improvement Over Square Grids; Proof Checked By External Mathematicians; Fields Medalist Tim Gowers: "A Milestone In AI Mathematics"**

**Date:** Wednesday May 20, 2026 (not in any prior report) | **Sources:** [OpenAI — An OpenAI model has disproved a central conjecture in discrete geometry](https://openai.com/index/model-disproves-discrete-geometry-conjecture/), [Nature — AI cracks 80-year-old mathematics challenge — researchers are astonished](https://www.nature.com/articles/d41586-026-01651-0), [Scientific American — OpenAI announces AI's biggest math breakthrough yet](https://www.scientificamerican.com/article/ai-just-solved-an-80-year-old-erdos-problem-and-mathematicians-are-amazed/), [TechCrunch — OpenAI claims it solved an 80-year-old math problem — for real this time](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/20/openai-claims-it-solved-an-80-year-old-math-problem-for-real-this-time/), [Interesting Engineering — 80-year-old geometry mystery cracked by OpenAI using deep number theory](https://interestingengineering.com/ai-robotics/openai-paul-erdos-geometry-problem-cracked)

OpenAI says one of its **general-purpose reasoning models** — not a math-specialized system, not scaffolded to search proof strategies, and not targeted at this problem — **independently disproved the planar unit-distance conjecture** first posed by Paul Erdős in 1946:

- **The problem:** Given *n* points in the plane, what is the **maximum number of pairs exactly distance 1 apart**? The long-standing conjecture held that the square grid was essentially optimal.
- **The result:** The model produced an **infinite family of constructions** that **beat the square grid by a polynomial factor**, drawing on **deep algebraic number theory** (Golod–Shafarevich theory, infinite class-field towers) to attack an elementary geometric question.
- **Verification:** The proof was **checked by a group of external mathematicians**, and **Tim Gowers** (Fields medalist) wrote a **companion paper** providing background and context, calling the result **"a milestone in AI mathematics."**

**Read-through:** This is the **first time a frontier AI has autonomously resolved a prominent open problem central to a field** — discovering, not retrieving, a novel argument. It reframes the "AI as research assistant" narrative toward **AI as an originator of mathematical knowledge**, and it lands in the same week as Anthropic's Karpathy "use the model to train the next model" bet — both pointing at **recursive, model-driven discovery** as the next capability frontier rather than raw compute alone.
