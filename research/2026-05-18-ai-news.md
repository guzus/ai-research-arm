- **Ruling:** US District Judge **Yvonne Gonzalez Rogers** **agreed with the advisory jury** that Altman and OpenAI were **not liable**, and that Musk's **breach-of-charitable-trust and unjust-enrichment claims are dismissed as untimely** — the jury concluded Musk had **three years to sue** under the applicable statute of limitations and **did not file in time**.

- **Musk reaction:** Several hours after the verdict, Musk posted on X calling Judge Rogers **"a terrible activist judge who creates such a terrible precedent,"** writing: **"She just handed out a free license to loot charities if you can keep the looting quiet for a few years!"** Reiterated he would file an appeal: **"the judge & jury never actually ruled on the merits of the case, just on a calendar technicality."**
- **Trial-tape witnesses (carry):** Altman, Brockman, Microsoft CEO **Satya Nadella**, and Musk himself testified during the three weeks.

(2) **Musk's "appeal" posture is exactly what was priced in** — the Polymarket-level base case had Musk losing at trial and pushing the case toward the **Ninth Circuit**, where the **Tenth Circuit-style "discovery rule" exception** has been the long-tail bull case for the plaintiff.
