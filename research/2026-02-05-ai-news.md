### Alphabet Q4 2025 Earnings — Beat Expectations, Announces Massive $175–185B 2026 Capex
**Date:** February 4, 2026

Alphabet reported Q4 2025 earnings that beat Wall Street estimates, but shares fell ~3% in after-hours trading as the company announced a massive increase in capital expenditures for 2026.

**Q4 2025 Results:**
- **Revenue:** $113.83 billion (vs. $111.43B expected) — **+18% YoY**
- **EPS:** $2.82 (vs. $2.63 expected)
- **Net Income:** $34.46 billion — **+30% YoY**

**Revenue Breakdown:**
- **Advertising:** $82.28 billion (+13.5% YoY)
- **Google Cloud:** $17.66 billion (vs. $16.18B expected)
- **YouTube Ads:** $11.38 billion (vs. $11.84B expected)

**2026 Capital Expenditure Guidance:**
Alphabet announced 2026 capex guidance of **$175–185 billion**, with the top end being more than double its 2025 spend. This massive investment is primarily directed at AI infrastructure, data centers, and cloud capacity.

**Waymo Impact:**
Waymo took a $2.1 billion stock-based compensation charge for Q4 following its $16 billion funding round at a $126 billion valuation.

**Stock Reaction:**
Despite the earnings beat, shares fell ~3% in extended trading, likely due to investor concerns about the aggressive capex guidance.
