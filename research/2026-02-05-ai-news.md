### Anthropic Launches Claude Opus 4.6 with "Agent Teams" — Biggest Enterprise AI Release of 2026
**Date:** February 5, 2026

Anthropic released Claude Opus 4.6, its most powerful model yet, introducing "agent teams" — a new capability that lets multiple AI agents coordinate on complex tasks simultaneously.

**Agent Teams:**
- Users can decompose a big job into specialized roles — planner, researcher, coder, tester — and run them concurrently
- Agents share state and hand off work products as they progress
- Instead of a single model stepping through tasks one by one, Opus 4.6 assigns responsibilities to collaborating agents

**Technical Specifications:**
- **Context window:** 1 million tokens (up from previous limits), enabling processing of entire codebases
- **Pricing:** $5 per million input tokens / $25 per million output tokens (same as Opus 4.5)
- **Available:** Immediately on claude.ai, Claude API, and major cloud platforms (model ID: `claude-opus-4-6`)

**Benchmark Performance:**
- Highest score on Terminal-Bench 2.0 (agentic coding evaluation)
- Leads all frontier models on Humanity's Last Exam (complex multi-discipline reasoning)
- Outperforms OpenAI's GPT-5.2 by ~144 ELO points on GDPval-AA (economically valuable knowledge work in finance, legal, and other domains)

**Enterprise Features:**
- Direct integration into PowerPoint as an accessible side panel
- Improved spreadsheet and presentation creation
- Enhanced code review and debugging skills
- Better planning and sustained agentic task execution in large codebases

### Claude Opus 4.6 Uncovers 500 Zero-Day Vulnerabilities in Open-Source Code
**Date:** February 5, 2026

In a separate announcement, Anthropic revealed that Claude Opus 4.6 discovered more than 500 previously unknown zero-day vulnerabilities in open-source code using its "out-of-the-box" capabilities.

**Key Details:**
- Each vulnerability was validated by Anthropic team members or outside security researchers
- Testing was conducted in a sandboxed environment with access to Python, debuggers, and fuzzers — but no specialized instructions
- Opus 4.6 reads and reasons about code like a human researcher: analyzing past fixes, spotting patterns, and understanding logic to identify breaking inputs

**Notable Discoveries:**
- Buffer overflow flaws in **OpenSC** (smart card data processing utility)
- Buffer overflow flaws in **CGIF** (GIF file processing tool) — Claude proactively wrote a proof-of-concept exploit to validate the vulnerability

**Safety Measures:**
Anthropic added new security controls to identify and respond to adversaries who might abuse the model's enhanced cybersecurity capabilities.

### SaaSpocalypse Deepens — Nearly $1 Trillion Wiped from Software Stocks
**Date:** February 5, 2026

The "SaaSpocalypse" — the AI-driven selloff of software-as-a-service stocks — has deepened to nearly $1 trillion in losses over seven days, as investors fear AI will replace entire software categories.

**Scale of Losses:**
- **Total wiped out:** ~$1 trillion from software and services stocks tracked in the iShares ETF over the past 7 days
- S&P 500 software and services index fell ~4% on Tuesday, then slipped another 0.73% on Wednesday — sixth straight session of losses
- ~$830 billion in market value erased since January 28

**Stocks Hardest Hit (YTD 2026):**
- **HubSpot:** Down 39% YTD (after -42% in 2025)
- **Figma:** Down 40% YTD
- **Atlassian:** Down 35% YTD
- **Shopify:** Down 29% YTD

**BofA Pushback:**
Bank of America called the selloff "logically impossible," arguing it relies on two mutually exclusive scenarios: AI capex deteriorating (suggesting weak ROI) while simultaneously AI adoption being so pervasive it makes software obsolete. BofA compared it to the overblown DeepSeek selloff of January 2025.

**Expert Reactions:**
- Jason Lemkin ("godfather of SaaS") wrote that early 2026 was seeing a "crash" in SaaS stocks
- Tom Lee and Michael Lewis weighed in on the $1 trillion carnage
- Fortune's headline: "The 'dumb money' steps in as traders lose $1 trillion"

### Alphabet Q4 2025 Earnings — Beat Expectations, Announces Massive $175–185B 2026 Capex
**Date:** February 4, 2026

Alphabet reported Q4 2025 earnings that beat Wall Street estimates, but shares fell ~5% as the company announced a massive increase in capital expenditures for 2026.

**Q4 2025 Results:**
- **Revenue:** $113.83 billion (vs. $111.43B expected) — **+18% YoY**
- **EPS:** $2.82 (vs. $2.63 expected)
- **Net Income:** $34.46 billion — **+30% YoY**

**Revenue Breakdown:**
- **Advertising:** $82.28 billion (+13.5% YoY)
- **Google Cloud:** $17.66 billion (vs. $16.18B expected)
- **YouTube Ads:** $11.38 billion (vs. $11.84B expected)

**2026 Capital Expenditure Guidance:**
Alphabet announced 2026 capex guidance of **$175–185 billion**, roughly double the $91.4 billion spent in 2025. Wall Street had expected ~$120 billion. This massive investment is primarily directed at AI infrastructure.

**Waymo Impact:**
Waymo took a $2.1 billion stock-based compensation charge for Q4 following its $16 billion funding round.

**Stock Reaction:**
Shares fell ~5% in extended/early trading, driven by investor concerns about aggressive capex guidance.
