## Breaking News

### Cognition Acquires Poke Maker Interaction Company, Merging Devin's Coding Agent With a Consumer Texting Agent

**Date:** July 23, 2026 | **Sources:** [Cognition — Welcoming The Interaction Company](https://cognition.com/blog/interaction), [Phemex News — Cognition AI Acquires Poke Developer Interaction](https://phemex.com/news/article/cognition-ai-acquires-interaction-developer-of-poke-ai-agent-94373), [Digg — Devin creator Cognition acquires personal AI agent startup](https://digg.com/tech/rf3bzdfm)
- Cognition, maker of the autonomous coding agent Devin, announced it has acquired The Interaction Company of California, developer of Poke — a personal AI agent that lives inside text messages and is the only agent approved to text natively on Apple Messages. Poke has handled more than 100 million messages in the last three months. Deal terms were not disclosed; Cognition says it will speed up Poke using its own models and infrastructure while keeping the existing product experience.
- **Why it matters:** It's a concrete consolidation move in the AI-agent market — pairing an autonomous software-engineering agent with a phone-native consumer messaging agent under one roof — at the same time France's competition regulator has been warning about concentration among a handful of agent providers.

### Samsung in Talks to Invest Roughly €1 Billion in Mistral AI at a €20 Billion Valuation

**Date:** July 22-23, 2026 | **Sources:** [Axios — Mistral in talks with Samsung on €20B valuation](https://www.axios.com/2026/07/22/mistral-samsung-20-billion-valuation), [Manila Times — Samsung in talks to invest in Mistral](https://www.manilatimes.net/2026/07/23/business/foreign-business/samsung-in-talks-to-invest-in-mistral/2389282), [TechFundingNews — Samsung-Mistral investment talks](https://techfundingnews.com/samsung-mistral-investment-20bn-valuation/)
- Samsung Electronics is reportedly in talks to invest about €1 billion in French AI lab Mistral as part of a new funding round that would value the company at roughly €20 billion (~$22.8 billion) — nearly double its €11.7 billion valuation from ten months ago. Sweden's EQT Scaleup Europe Fund, Novo Holdings, and Santander are also said to be in talks to participate. Neither company has confirmed the deal.
- **Why it matters:** It would be Samsung's first direct investment in a foundation-model lab outside Google, and comes one day after Microsoft and Mistral expanded their own strategic partnership around sovereign/regulated-industry AI deployments.

## Research Papers

### New Relay-Bench Benchmark Finds GPT-5.5 Drops to 43% on Chained Cross-Domain Reasoning

**Date:** arXiv posting July 20, 2026; covered July 22, 2026 | **Source:** [Tech Times — New AI Benchmark Holds GPT-5.5 at 43% on Cross-Domain Reasoning Chains](https://www.techtimes.com/articles/321266/20260722/new-ai-benchmark-holds-gpt-55-43-cross-domain-reasoning-chains.htm)
- Researcher Liam Swayne's new "Relay-Bench" chains problems across seven reasoning domains within a single sequential prompt rather than testing each domain in isolation. GPT-5.5 scores 43.3% on the chained version, a 39-point gap versus its 82.7% score on standard agentic benchmarks, because small per-step errors compound into full chain failures.
- **Why it matters:** It's a concrete, reproducible demonstration that today's benchmark-topping reasoning scores don't transfer to the sequential, multi-domain reasoning that real agentic workflows require.

## Industry Moves

### OpenAI Launches Presence, an Enterprise Platform for Managed Voice and Chat Agents

**Date:** July 22, 2026 | **Sources:** [OpenAI — Introducing OpenAI Presence](https://openai.com/index/introducing-openai-presence/), [VentureBeat — OpenAI unveils Presence](https://venturebeat.com/orchestration/openai-unveils-presence-a-new-platform-that-lets-enterprises-launch-and-manage-realtime-voice-agents-and-chatbots), [The Register — OpenAI tries the consulting path with 'Presence'](https://www.theregister.com/ai-and-ml/2026/07/22/openai-tries-the-consulting-path-with-presence-charging-enterprises-boots-on-the-ground-prices-to-deploy-agents/5275867)
- OpenAI introduced Presence, a fully managed platform for building and governing production-grade voice and chat agents for customer support, sales, procurement, and IT/HR, combining model reasoning with company-defined policies and escalation rules. It is available through a limited general-availability program led by OpenAI's own Forward Deployed Engineers and select systems integrators (not self-service), and is already running on OpenAI's own English-language phone support line, where it resolves 75% of inbound calls without a human.
- **Why it matters:** It's OpenAI shifting further from selling raw model access toward selling a managed, consulting-style enterprise agent deployment service — competing directly with system integrators and CCaaS vendors, not just other model providers.

### Microsoft Replaces OpenAI's Image Models With Its Own MAI-Image-2.5-Pro in PowerPoint and Bing

**Date:** July 23, 2026 | **Source:** [Bloomberg — Microsoft Replacing OpenAI Image AI Models in Powerpoint, Bing](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-23/microsoft-replacing-openai-image-ai-models-in-powerpoint-bing)
- Microsoft is swapping OpenAI's image-generation models for its own MAI-Image-2.5-Pro in PowerPoint, Bing, and OneDrive. Microsoft AI chief Mustafa Suleyman said the in-house model is "faster, it's cheaper, it's higher quality, it drives better retention," and Microsoft cites up to an 84% reduction in GPU costs versus GPT-Image-2 for image-to-image tasks.
- **Why it matters:** It's a concrete, dated escalation of Microsoft's cost-driven pivot away from OpenAI models inside its own products, following its earlier swap of MAI models into Excel and Outlook.

### Amazon Cuts Jobs in Its AGI Unit Even as It Plans ~$200 Billion in AI Infrastructure Spending

**Date:** July 22, 2026 | **Source:** [Tech Startups — Amazon lays off employees in artificial intelligence unit as AI spending surges to $200 billion](https://techstartups.com/2026/07/22/amazon-lays-off-employees-in-artificial-intelligence-unit-as-ai-spending-surges-to-200-billion/)
- Amazon cut jobs within its artificial general intelligence (AGI) unit, extending a string of workforce reductions at the company even as it prepares to spend roughly $200 billion on AI infrastructure this year.
- **Why it matters:** It's a concrete data point on a now-familiar 2026 pattern — big tech simultaneously cutting AI-adjacent headcount while ramping capital spending on AI infrastructure.
