### Apple Sues OpenAI for Trade Secret Theft Over AI Hardware Plans

**Date:** July 10, 2026 (OpenAI response July 11) | **Sources:** [CNBC — Apple sues OpenAI alleging trade secret theft](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/10/apple-openai-lawsuit-trade-secrets.html), [TechCrunch — Apple sues OpenAI over alleged trade secret theft](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/10/apple-sues-openai-over-alleged-trade-secret-theft/), [Bloomberg — Apple Sues OpenAI for Trade Secret Theft](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-10/apple-sues-openai-for-trade-secret-theft-in-blockbuster-case), [MacRumors — OpenAI Responds After Being Sued by Apple](https://www.macrumors.com/2026/07/11/openai-responds-after-apple-lawsuit/)

- Apple filed suit against OpenAI in federal court in Northern California, alleging OpenAI "at every level, from members of its Technical Staff to its Chief Hardware Officer" stole Apple trade secrets to build its own consumer AI hardware.
- Apple names OpenAI hardware chief **Tang Tan**, a former Apple VP, alleging he directed Apple staff interviewing at OpenAI to bring "actual parts" from Apple devices to interviews for "show and tell." Apple also alleges a former Apple engineer, **Chang Liu**, kept an Apple-issued laptop after joining OpenAI and used it to access confidential unannounced-product documentation.
- OpenAI's Director of Strategic Communications, Drew Pusateri, responded on X: "We have no interest in other companies' trade secrets. We remain focused on building innovative technology that empowers people everywhere."
- **Why it matters:** This is a sharp reversal in the Apple–OpenAI relationship, which began with ChatGPT's 2024 iOS integration and has cooled since OpenAI acquired Jony Ive's hardware startup io Products for $6.4B. Apple says it first raised concerns in a February letter that went unanswered.

### OpenAI's Head of Safety Systems Departs as Safety Team Folds Into Research

**Date:** Reported July 11, 2026 | **Sources:** [Bloomberg — OpenAI Safety Head Heidecke to Leave Firm After Reshuffle](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-11/openai-safety-head-heidecke-to-leave-firm-after-reshuffle-wired), [Engadget — OpenAI's head of safety is reportedly leaving](https://www.engadget.com/2212941/openai-head-of-safety-leaving-company-reorganization/)

- **Johannes Heidecke**, who has led OpenAI's safety systems team since 2024, told colleagues he will leave the company by **July 24**.
- Chief Research Officer **Mark Chen** announced in an internal memo that safety will now report into the research division rather than operate independently, so safety has "an earlier and more direct role" in model and product decisions. **Saachi Jain** becomes interim head of safety systems; safety teams will report to **Mia Glaese**, OpenAI's new VP of Research and Safety.
- **Why it matters:** Heidecke is reported to be the sixth safety leader to leave OpenAI in two years, and the departure lands weeks ahead of OpenAI's anticipated IPO process, adding to scrutiny of its safety-org stability.

### SK Hynix Completes Record $26.5B Nasdaq ADR Listing

**Date:** Priced July 9, debut trading July 10, 2026 | **Sources:** [Bloomberg — SK Hynix ADR Stock Rises After $26.5 Billion US Listing](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-10/sk-hynix-indicated-to-climb-17-after-26-5-billion-adr-offering), [Yahoo Finance — SK Hynix Nasdaq debut](https://finance.yahoo.com/markets/stocks/articles/sk-hynix-nasdaq-debut-26-113118390.html)

- SK Hynix priced 177.9 million ADRs at $149 apiece, raising **$26.5 billion** — the largest U.S. share sale ever by a foreign company, surpassing Alibaba's $25B 2014 listing.
- Shares (provisional ticker SKHYV, becoming SKHY) jumped as much as 17% in early indications and closed their debut session at $168.01, well above the $149 offer price.
- Proceeds are earmarked for expanding South Korean manufacturing capacity and buying EUV lithography equipment to keep pace with AI memory (HBM) demand.
- **Why it matters:** SK Hynix is a primary HBM supplier for Nvidia's AI accelerators; the record raise is a direct market read on how much capital investors are willing to commit to AI memory-chip capacity right now.
