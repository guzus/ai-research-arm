### EU AI Act Omnibus Trilogue **COLLAPSED** April 28 — August 2 Deadline Now Definitively Stays Live; Next Trilogue May 13
**Date:** Wednesday, April 29, 2026 (post-mortem coverage) | **Sources:** [The Next Web — EU and Parliament fail to agree on AI Act changes after 12 hours of talks](https://thenextweb.com/news/eu-ai-act-omnibus-deal-fails-april-2026-talks), [IAPP — AI Act Omnibus: What just happened and what comes next](https://iapp.org/news/a/ai-act-omnibus-what-just-happened-and-what-comes-next), [Modulos — EU AI Act Omnibus: The Trilogue Failed, What Happens to the August 2026 Deadline?](https://www.modulos.ai/blog/ai-act-omnibus-trilogue-failed/), [LEOsphere — EU AI Act implementation status April 2026](https://leosphere.net/eu-ai-act-implementation-status-april-2026-digital-omnibus-trilogue-underway/), [euaiactnyc.com — April 2026 implementation status](https://euaiactnyc.com/blog/eu-ai-act-implementation-april-2026.html), [Ovidiu Suciu — EU AI Act 2026: Timeline, Delays & Global Comparison](https://ovidiusuciu.com/eu-ai-act-implementation-status-2026/)

**Material correction to the April 29 report:** the April 28 political trilogue did **not** converge on the December 2, 2027 / August 2, 2028 postponement framework — after **~12 hours** of negotiations, the Cypriot Council Presidency, Parliament, and Commission **failed to reach political agreement** on the EU AI Act Omnibus and **adjourned without a deal**. The next political trilogue is scheduled for **on or about May 13, 2026** in Brussels.

- **Sticking point:** **conformity-assessment architecture for Annex I product-embedded AI** (Section A products such as machinery and medical devices). Council pushing for combined AI Act + sectoral assessment; Parliament pushing to move Section A items to Section B for primarily sectoral handling.
- **Result:** **August 2, 2026 high-risk-AI deadline (Annex III + Article 50)** remains **legally binding** under the existing AI Act (Regulation 2024/1689, Article 113). T-94 days as of today.
- **Process risk:** Cypriot Presidency term ends **June 30, 2026**; if no deal by then, **Lithuanian Presidency** (July 1 onward) inherits negotiations *during* the live August 2 deadline window — operationally risking a **post-deadline retroactive Omnibus**.
- **Compliance posture:** EU industry associations (DigitalEurope, BusinessEurope) issued statements urging immediate trilogue follow-up; legal practitioners (Ropes & Gray, A&O Shearman) flagged that **any compliance pause based on the political agreement is now legally indefensible**.

**Read-through:** This is a **180-degree reversal** from the post-April 28 narrative captured in last night's report (which framed the trilogue as having converged). For Anthropic, OpenAI, Google DeepMind, Mistral, Cohere–Aleph Alpha, and Stability AI, **August 2 high-risk-systems compliance is back on the original timeline** — biometric, employment, and public-services deployments must conform by deadline. The **structural narrative shift**: rather than a slowdown, **the EU is now visibly the more uncertain regulatory environment** — neither slower nor faster, just *unpredictable*, which is the worst of all worlds for procurement and product roadmaps. Combined with the U.S. NY RAISE Act (effective March 19) and the China NDRC trial AI ethics framework (April), 2026 H1 is now **the highest-friction global AI-regulation period since the original AI Act's 2024 vote**.
