## Breaking News

### **SpaceX IPO Hardens Into The Largest Offering In History: Roadshow Launched June 4 (Accelerated After A Faster-Than-Expected SEC Review), Priced At A Fixed $135/Share For ~556.6M Shares — A ~$75B Raise At A ~$1.77 Trillion Valuation; Pricing After The Close June 11, First Trade June 12 On Nasdaq (SPCX), With ~30% Of Shares Reserved For Retail And Reported Demand Already Near ~$150B — Even As Some Wall Street Analysts Argue The Stock Is Worth Roughly Half The Ask**

**Date:** Reported June 3–4, 2026 (active into the June 11–12 pricing/trading window) | **Sources:** [CNBC — SpaceX targets fixed $135 IPO price for roadshow, source says](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/03/spacex-ipo-stock-price-roadshow-musk.html), [Crypto Briefing — SpaceX targets $75B IPO with 30% of shares reserved for retail investors](https://cryptobriefing.com/spacex-75b-ipo-retail-investors-2/), [Crypto Briefing — SpaceX targets mid-June 2026 IPO at $1.75 trillion valuation](https://cryptobriefing.com/spacex-ipo-june-2026-valuation/), [TechTimes — SpaceX IPO Roadshow Launches at Fixed Price: Wall Street Says Stock Is Worth Half the Ask](https://www.techtimes.com/articles/317793/20260604/spacex-ipo-roadshow-launches-fixed-price-wall-street-says-stock-worth-half-ask.htm), [CNBC — SpaceX (SPCX) IPO: Live updates](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/20/spacex-ipo-live-updates.html)

- **The terms:** SpaceX is selling **~556.6 million shares at a fixed $135/share**, targeting a **~$75B raise** at a **~$1.77 trillion valuation** (the $1.77T figure assumes the EchoStar spectrum and Cursor transactions close). Underwriters hold an option for **~83.33M additional shares** (~$11.2B). That would make SpaceX the **largest IPO ever** — more than triple Alibaba, the current US record — and the **~7th-largest US company by market cap**, above Tesla (~$1.6T).
- **The accelerated calendar:** The **roadshow launched June 4**, ahead of the previously-reported week-of-June-8 start, on a **quicker-than-expected SEC review**. **Pricing is expected after the close on June 11**; **first trade June 12** on **Nasdaq: SPCX**. Roughly **30% of shares are reserved for retail investors**, and reported demand had already reached **~$150B**.
- **The skeptics:** Some Wall Street coverage argues the **$135 ask is roughly double** a defensible fundamental value — a valuation-stretch debate that sits directly on top of the AI-compute thesis, given SpaceX's merged xAI unit and its new role (per the June 5 SEC filing) as a **compute landlord to Google and Anthropic**.

**Read-through:** This is the concrete crystallization of a timeline the prior reports only sketched — a fixed price, a hard date, and a record headline number. SpaceX going public at ~$1.77T while simultaneously renting frontier GPU capacity to two rival labs makes the IPO a **direct public-markets referendum on the AI-infrastructure trade**: investors are being asked to underwrite Starlink/Starship *and* an xAI/Colossus compute-landlord business in one ticker. The "worth half the ask" pushback is the key tension to watch through the June 11 pricing — it's the first time the AI-compute megacycle gets a daily, liquid price tag.
