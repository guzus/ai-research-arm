**China AI regulation** tracks Beijing's direct regulatory actions on
consumer-facing AI products — a distinct thread from the US
[[federal-ai-policy]] and [[california-ai-regulation]] storylines, which
center on frontier-model access and export control rather than
consumer-product design mandates. The opening entry is China's first
dedicated crackdown on **AI companion agents** as a category distinct
from task-execution agents.

## Why it matters

- **The AI companion law takes effect (2026-07-15).** China's "Interim
  Measures for the Administration of AI Anthropomorphic Interactive
  Services" — co-issued by the **Cyberspace Administration of China**,
  **NDRC**, **MIIT**, and other regulators — took effect today, requiring
  **anti-addiction systems**, **usage notifications**, and **instant-exit
  mechanisms** for AI companion services. It is Beijing's first regulation
  to explicitly single out humanlike companion agents (persistent,
  relationship-styled personas) as a category needing distinct guardrails
  from general-purpose task agents.
- **Immediate compliance impact: Doubao and Qwen forced offline.**
  **ByteDance's Doubao** (345M MAU) and **[[alibaba|Alibaba's Qwen]]** both
  shut down personalized AI-companion agent features to comply. Doubao
  users retain **read-only access** to old configurations/chats through
  **October 15, 2026**; Qwen has **no announced grace period**, with data
  **permanently deleted** — a materially harsher compliance path for
  Alibaba's product than ByteDance's.
- **A regulatory template, not an isolated rule.** The law's
  anti-addiction/notification/exit-mechanism structure reads as a
  candidate template for future Chinese AI-product regulation beyond
  companion agents specifically, the way California's Transparency in
  Frontier AI Act became a template other US states could follow (see
  [[california-ai-regulation]]).

## Open questions

- **Does the crackdown extend beyond named companion features?** Doubao
  and Qwen both offer general-purpose assistant modes alongside the
  companion features being shut down — does the "anthropomorphic
  interactive services" definition stay narrow, or expand to touch
  broader assistant products?
- **Do other Chinese platforms face the same deadline?** Only Doubao and
  Qwen are confirmed compliance actions so far; watch whether other
  China-based consumer AI products (from [[tencent-hunyuan-hy3|Tencent]],
  [[moonshot-kimi-k2-7-code|Moonshot]], or others) carry similar
  companion-style features subject to the same rule.
- **Grace-period asymmetry.** Why does Doubao get a three-month read-only
  window while Qwen users lose data immediately — a regulatory
  distinction, or a company-level compliance choice?
