**SK Hynix** is the South Korean memory-chip maker whose **HBM
(high-bandwidth memory)** underpins [[nvidia]]'s AI accelerator supply
chain. On 2026-07-11 it raised **$26.5 billion** through a **Nasdaq
listing** — reported as the **largest US share sale ever by a foreign
company** — with shares reportedly climbing roughly 14% on debut.

## Why it matters

HBM supply is a direct bottleneck on AI accelerator production. A raise
of this scale by a top-two HBM supplier is a concrete data point in the
memory/HBM supply crunch feeding AI datacenter buildouts, alongside
[[micron]] (Anthropic's primary memory supplier) on the US side. It also
lands the same week as reports that **Samsung** moved up its first Yongin
semiconductor fab by two years (now targeting October 2029), underscoring
how memory capacity is becoming a capex-supercycle chokepoint — see
[[ai-capex]].
