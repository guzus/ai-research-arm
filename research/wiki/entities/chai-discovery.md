**Chai Discovery** is an AI-drug-discovery startup building foundation
models for molecule design. It sits at the intersection of the
[[ai-capex]] funding supercycle and pharma AI adoption — one of the
clearer cases of AI-native biotech tooling already deployed by
established pharmaceutical companies rather than still in pilot.

## Why it matters

- **$400M Series C at $3.8B (2026-07-15).** Index Ventures led the round,
  joined by Kleiner Perkins, Sequoia, Dimension, and returning investors
  including [[openai]]. The **$3.8B valuation is nearly 3x** Chai
  Discovery's valuation from a **$130M round seven months earlier**
  (implied ~$1.3B), one of the sharper valuation re-rates in the AI-bio
  funding cycle.
- **Molecules already in pharma use.** Chai Discovery's AI-designed
  molecules are already used by **Eli Lilly, Novartis, and Pfizer** —
  distinguishing the company from AI-bio startups still pre-revenue or
  pre-deployment.
- **OpenAI as a returning investor.** [[openai]] participating again
  signals frontier labs treating AI-for-science / AI-drug-discovery as a
  strategic adjacency, alongside [[anthropic]]'s own **Claude Science**
  push into research workbenches.

## Open questions

- **Revenue vs. valuation.** No revenue figure was disclosed alongside
  the ~3x valuation jump — is the re-rate backed by comparable revenue
  growth, or is it primarily a funding-cycle multiple expansion?
- **Differentiation.** AI-drug-discovery has multiple well-funded
  entrants; what specifically keeps Eli Lilly, Novartis, and Pfizer on
  Chai Discovery's models rather than in-house or competing platforms?
