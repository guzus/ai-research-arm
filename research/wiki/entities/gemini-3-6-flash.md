**Gemini 3.6 Flash**, **Gemini 3.5 Flash-Lite**, and **Gemini 3.5 Flash
"Cyber"** shipped together from [[google]] on 2026-07-21 — the next Flash-tier
generation after [[gemini-3-5-flash]], plus a new cybersecurity-focused
variant. The trio launched the same day Google confirmed **[[gemini-3-5-pro|Gemini
3.5 Pro]] remains "in testing"** and reporting noted Google is already
training **Gemini 4** — the Flash tier is advancing a generation ahead of the
still-delayed Pro tier.

## Why it matters

- **Flash tier iterates again while Pro stalls.** Gemini 3.6 Flash and 3.5
  Flash-Lite continue Google's cadence of shipping fast/cheap models well
  ahead of the flagship Pro tier — [[gemini-3-5-pro]]'s schedule slip (tracked
  since 2026-07-16) persists into this cycle, with Google reportedly already
  training **Gemini 4** even as 3.5 Pro has not shipped.
- **"Cyber" — a dedicated AI-security model.** Gemini 3.5 Flash "Cyber" is
  pitched as a cheaper alternative to dedicated AI-security models for
  finding and patching vulnerabilities — Google entering the same
  security-model niche OpenAI staked out with GPT-5.5 Cyber, part of the
  broader [[agentic-ai-security]] theme of frontier labs shipping
  purpose-built security tooling.
- **Top Hacker News story of the day (2026-07-22 cycle).** The triple release
  was the top-voted Hacker News story (524 pts / 420 comments) in the day's
  later crawl run, ahead of Alibaba's [[alibaba|Qwen-Image-3.0]] and
  Moonshot's [[moonshot-kimi-k3|Kimi Work]] launch.

## Open questions

- **Does "Cyber" hold up against dedicated AI-security tools?** No
  independent benchmark has been published comparing it to purpose-built
  vulnerability-scanning models.
- **When does Gemini 3.5 Pro actually ship**, and does the reported Gemini 4
  training effort mean Pro is being effectively skipped or folded into the
  next major version? See [[gemini-3-5-pro]] for the dedicated tracking page.
