CoreWeave (NASDAQ: CRWV) is the reference public [[neocloud]] — a
GPU-as-a-service provider that buys NVIDIA capacity on debt and rents it back
under multi-year, take-or-pay contracts. It is the most data-rich proxy for the
entire independent GPU-cloud category, and the natural peer when sizing up
[[nebius]] and the new hyperscaler-backed JVs racing into the same market.

## Why it matters
ARA's first-principles audit of CoreWeave's economics quantifies what the whole
[[ai-capex]] / neocloud thesis rests on:

- **~$98.8B of contracted backlog** (RPO) as of Q1 2026, up ~$38B in a single
  quarter, with ~98% of FY2025 revenue from take-or-pay committed contracts (ARA
  generative research, 2026-05-16).
- **~$25.1B of on-balance-sheet debt** plus ~$60B of off-balance-sheet operating
  leases — the capital structure that the neocloud model depends on.
- **Customer concentration**: Microsoft, OpenAI, and Meta together account for
  ~80%+ of the backlog. "Customer A" (Microsoft) fell from 67% of FY2025 revenue
  to 45% in Q1 2026 — a *rotation* of anchor tenants, not broad diversification.
- A landmark **$8.5B DDTL 4.0 facility** rated A3 by Moody's at the SPV level —
  the first investment-grade-rated GPU-backed loan — versus Ba3 at the parent.

CoreWeave is the benchmark every other neocloud is measured against: when Google
× Blackstone announced their $5B TPU neocloud JV, coverage explicitly framed it
as targeting "CoreWeave/Nebius" (ARA digest 2026-05-20).

## Open questions
- **Counterparty risk.** With ~22% of backlog tied to OpenAI (private, ~$25B/yr
  operating burn), what happens to the equity if any one of three counterparties
  blinks?
- **Owned power.** A Core Scientific acquisition that would have brought 1.3 GW
  of owned power on-balance-sheet was rejected by CORZ shareholders 91.5%–8.5%.
  Can CoreWeave secure power without owning it?
- **Read-through to peers.** How much of CoreWeave's debt-financed,
  concentration-heavy structure also describes [[nebius]] and other independent
  neoclouds?
