**John Jumper** is the co-creator of **AlphaFold** — DeepMind's protein-structure
prediction system — and a joint **2024 Nobel laureate in Chemistry** for that
work. On **2026-06-20** he announced he is **leaving Google DeepMind for
[[anthropic]]**, in his own post, acknowledged by DeepMind CEO Demis Hassabis.

## Why it matters

- **The most prestigious science hire of the talent war.** Jumper's move lands in
  the same cycle as **[[noam-shazeer]]→[[openai]]** and follows the earlier
  Karpathy→Anthropic defection. A sitting Nobel laureate crossing from DeepMind to
  Anthropic is a scientific-credibility signal of a different order than a senior
  engineering poach.
- **Credibility cover while the frontier models sit embargoed.** The hire arrives
  with Anthropic's top models ([[claude-fable-5|Fable 5 / Mythos 5]]) dark under the
  US export-control order — a marquee science name is exactly the reputational asset
  a lab wants during a public standoff (see [[federal-ai-policy]]).
- **A science-for-AI bet.** Jumper's expertise is at the AI-for-science frontier
  (structural biology, scientific modeling) — a domain where both [[anthropic]] and
  [[openai]] (via GPT-Rosalind / [[lifescibench]]) are racing to show models can
  drive real research.

## Open questions

- **What will Jumper build at Anthropic?** A science-model program, a research
  org, or a broader AI-for-science push remains unstated.
- **What does the departure cost DeepMind?** Losing an AlphaFold principal during
  the Gemini 3.5 crunch is a second high-profile DeepMind exit in the cycle after
  [[noam-shazeer]].
