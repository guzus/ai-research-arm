**Prometheus** is the AI startup where **Jeff Bezos is co-CEO** alongside former
Google/Verily executive **Vik Bajaj**. It is building toward an
**[[artificial-general-engineer]]** — a system to take complex physical products
(jet engines, medical devices, consumer electronics) from concept through
production. It is tracked here because its 2026-06-11 megaround is the clearest
signal yet that the [[ai-capex]] capital wave is broadening beyond chatbots and
coding into **AI-for-manufacturing**.

## Why it matters

- **Industrial AI's first megaround (2026-06-11).** Prometheus disclosed a **$12B
  Series B at a ~$41B valuation**. Backers: **JPMorgan, BlackRock, Goldman Sachs,
  DST Global, and Arch Venture Partners**; Bezos — the largest backer of the
  earlier ~$6.2B Series A — participated again. Launched November 2025 with ~150
  employees and offices in San Francisco, London, and Zurich (ARA digest
  2026-06-12).
- **The capital wave broadens beyond software.** Where the 2026 [[ai-capex]]
  rounds have concentrated in frontier labs ([[anthropic]], [[openai]],
  [[deepseek]]) and the compute layer, Prometheus puts a ~$41B valuation behind
  **physical-product design and manufacturing** — the [[artificial-general-engineer]]
  thesis. It is the application-layer counterpart to humanoid-autonomy bets like
  [[figure-ai]], but aimed at the *engineering/design* stage rather than the
  factory floor.
- **Disambiguation.** Prometheus is **not** the separate ~$500M Bezos-backed
  **Flourish** round; the two are distinct vehicles (ARA digest 2026-06-12).

## Open questions

- **Does "artificial general engineer" produce shippable hardware, or is it a
  framing?** No third-party engineering result is attached to the raise yet — the
  valuation prices the thesis, not a demonstrated product.
- **Does industrial AI become its own funding lane?** A ~$41B mark this early
  tests whether AI-for-manufacturing pulls megaround appetite the way frontier
  labs and compute have, or stays a single-name bet.
