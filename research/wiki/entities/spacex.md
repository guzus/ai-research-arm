**SpaceX** is the aerospace and satellite-internet company whose 2026 IPO became
the public-markets anchor of the [[ai-capex]] capital-formation wave. It is
tracked in the LLM wiki not for rockets but because its **record-setting
listing** — the single largest IPO in history — is the hard, *filed* counterpart
to the cycle's private AI mega-rounds, and because of its compute ties to the
frontier labs (the Anthropic–SpaceX "Colossus" datacenter arrangement).

## Why it matters

- **The largest IPO in history goes live (2026-06-05).** A first-party @SpaceX
  post confirmed the listing: **~555.55M shares at a flat $135**, **~$74.4B net
  proceeds** (up to $85.7B with the greenshoe), a **~$1.75T target valuation**.
  Pricing **June 11**, **SPCX debuts on Nasdaq June 12**. It anchored a
  three-front mega-IPO calendar alongside [[anthropic]] (confidential S-1, June
  1) and [[openai]] (confidential S-1, May 22) (ARA digest 2026-06-05).
- **The prospectus is the counterweight.** Unlike the week's anonymously-sourced
  private rounds, SpaceX *filed*: the S-1 exposed **$18.7B 2025 revenue against a
  $4.9B net loss** and a **~94× sales multiple** — the hard, auditable
  counterweight to the headline valuation. In a week when [[deepseek]]'s ~$7B
  round had "no filing of any kind," SpaceX's filed paper is exactly the
  financing discipline the cycle's skeptics demanded (ARA digest 2026-06-05).
- **Public markets as the new capex channel.** SpaceX's listing, Alphabet's
  ~$85B oversubscribed convertible, and the [[anthropic]]/[[openai]] S-1s mark
  the [[ai-capex]] supercycle's shift from private rounds into public capital
  formation — a ~$340B+ aggregated "issuance wave" that doubles as the
  bubble-top debate's exhibit A.

## Open questions

- **Does SPCX hold its ~94× sales multiple post-debut?** A flat-priced $135 and a
  $1.75T valuation on a loss-making business is the cleanest live test of how
  much "AI/infra adjacency" public markets will pay for.
- **How much of the raise is compute-adjacent?** SpaceX's datacenter and
  Starlink-backhaul ties to the frontier labs (the [[anthropic]] "Colossus"
  arrangement) blur the line between an aerospace IPO and an AI-infrastructure
  one.
