**Gemini 3.5 Pro** is the Pro-tier follow-on to [[gemini-3-5-flash|Gemini 3.5
Flash]], confirmed by Google at **I/O 2026 (2026-05-19)** as "coming next
month." What followed instead was ten weeks of fragmented, contradictory,
single-source leak chatter (see [[gemini-3-5-flash]]'s prior open-questions
tracking) — and then, on 2026-07-16, the first on-record-adjacent signal that
the delay itself is real and consequential.

## Why it matters

- **Reported months behind schedule (2026-07-16).** Coding capability
  reportedly fell short of internal targets, pushing the release well past
  its original "next month" (June) commitment and past the unverified
  July 17 leak date this page's sibling entry had been tracking as noise.
  The report **wiped out roughly $200B of [[google|Alphabet]]'s market cap**
  the same day — a rare case of an AI-model schedule slip generating a
  quantified, market-verifiable data point rather than pure social-media
  chatter.
- **Landed the same week as [[moonshot-kimi-k3|Kimi K3]] topping a coding
  leaderboard** — reinforcing a 2026-07-20 market mood (see [[ai-capex]])
  that reads Google's coding-model execution as slipping just as Chinese
  open-weight models close in on frontier coding performance.
- **Resolves (partially) the prior rumor cluster.** The 07-04–07-12 leak
  chain (full-retrain reset, July 17 target, 2M context) had already been
  flagged unverified pending a primary Google statement (see the model
  ticket). The 07-16 schedule-slip reporting is the first data point
  consistent with those leaks' direction (delay) even though the specific
  claims (retrain, context size) remain unconfirmed.

## Open questions

- **What is the new target date?** No replacement date has been reported as
  of 2026-07-20 reporting.
- **Is the "months behind" framing about a full retrain, or incremental
  coding-quality misses?** The 07-16 reporting cites coding-capability
  shortfalls specifically; it does not confirm or deny the earlier
  full-pretrain-reset leak.
- **Does the delay affect the Nano Banana Pro follow-on** rumored to be
  built on the same base (see the model ticket)?
