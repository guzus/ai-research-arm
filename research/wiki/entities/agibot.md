**AGIBOT** is a Chinese embodied-AI / humanoid-robotics maker that unveiled
four new products at the **World AI Conference (WAIC) 2026** in Shanghai,
led by the **A3 Ultra** humanoid — named "Gem of the Exhibition" — as part
of a global export push. The company says it holds **~39% of the global
humanoid-robot market**, positioning it as the leading Chinese counterpart
to US humanoid players like [[figure-ai]].

## Why it matters

- **WAIC 2026 showcase (2026-07-19).** AGIBOT unveiled the **A3 Ultra**
  (1.74m, 60kg, 51 degrees of freedom, NVIDIA Thor-powered, 8-hour runtime
  via hot-swap batteries) alongside the **X2 Edu** education platform, the
  **G2 Max** industrial robot, and the **OmniHand 3 Ultra-M** end effector —
  with **30+ AGIBOT units operating live** across the conference venue, a
  concrete deployed-fleet demonstration rather than a single hero unit.
  *(The ~39% global-market-share figure is AGIBOT's own claim, not
  independently audited.)* (ARA digest 2026-07-19).
- **Positioning against the field.** AGIBOT's WAIC push lands in the same
  embodied-AI wave as [[figure-ai]]'s 200-hour autonomous Helix-02 shift and
  [[mistral-robostral-navigate]]'s natural-language robot navigation model —
  a 2026 cycle where humanoid/embodied deployments are moving from
  demo-stage to sustained multi-unit operation, with China and the US
  fielding competing entrants.
- **China's WAIC platform.** The showcase runs alongside China's broader
  WAIC 2026 diplomatic push (see [[china-ai-regulation]] and the World AI
  Cooperation Organization founding) — humanoid robotics as a visible export
  and soft-power showcase, not just a lab demo.

## Open questions

- **Does the ~39% market-share claim hold up?** AGIBOT's own figure has no
  named independent audit yet.
- **Export traction.** The showcase is framed as a "global export push" —
  watch for confirmed international deployments or orders beyond the WAIC
  demo floor.
