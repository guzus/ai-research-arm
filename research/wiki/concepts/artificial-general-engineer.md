The **artificial general engineer (AGE)** is the thesis — most prominently
pursued by [[prometheus]] — that an AI system can take a **complex physical
product** (jet engines, medical devices, consumer electronics) from concept
through design, simulation, and into production. It is the engineering-domain
analogue of the "general agent" idea: rather than answering questions or writing
code, the system reasons about materials, tolerances, manufacturability, and the
full product-development lifecycle.

## Why it matters

- **It marks AI capital broadening past software.** The 2026 [[ai-capex]]
  supercycle concentrated in frontier chatbots/coding labs and the compute layer.
  The AGE framing is the banner under which capital extends into
  **AI-for-manufacturing** — [[prometheus]]'s $12B Series B at ~$41B
  (2026-06-11) is the first megaround explicitly sold on it.
- **Distinct from embodied/humanoid autonomy.** The AGE targets the *design and
  engineering* stage; humanoid-autonomy bets like [[figure-ai]] target the
  *physical execution* (warehouse, factory floor). Both expand AI's addressable
  surface into the physical economy, from opposite ends of the product
  lifecycle.

## Open questions

- **Is it a demonstrable capability or a fundraising frame?** No third-party
  engineering result has been attached to the thesis yet.
- **Where does the design loop close?** An AGE that designs but cannot validate
  against real-world manufacturing constraints is a CAD copilot, not an engineer
  — the open question is how much of the concept→production loop AI actually
  closes.
