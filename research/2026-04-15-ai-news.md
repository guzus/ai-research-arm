### Anthropic Faces Developer Backlash Over Claude Performance Decline — Compute Crunch Accusations
**Date:** April 14, 2026 | **Sources:** [Fortune](https://fortune.com/2026/04/14/anthropic-claude-performance-decline-user-complaints-backlash-lack-of-transparency-accusations-compute-crunch/), [TipRanks](https://www.tipranks.com/news/claude-ai-performance-issues-trigger-sudden-backlash-against-anthropic), [Cryptonomist](https://en.cryptonomist.ch/2026/04/13/claude-code-performance/)

A wave of developer complaints hit Anthropic this week, with Fortune reporting on April 14 that Claude Opus 4.6 and Claude Code users are experiencing a marked performance decline and accusing the company of a lack of transparency:

- A viral analysis by user **Laurenzo**, amplified April 11 by @Hesamation and later summarized as a **67% performance drop** in coding tasks, fueled an "AI shrinkflation" narrative among paid users.
- Complaints on GitHub, X, and Reddit cite increased hallucinations, token waste, premature task abandonment, and a shift from research-first to edit-first behavior in complex coding workflows — reportedly starting around February 2026.
- Suspicions of **throttling tied to compute constraints** point to Anthropic's **March 26 policy change** that accelerated 5-hour session limits during peak Pacific hours (5–11 a.m. weekdays), affecting ~7% of Pro users via faster quota burn and cache costs.
- On **April 7**, defaults shifted to high-effort reasoning for API, Bedrock, Vertex, Team, and Enterprise users, but users still perceived regressions in reasoning depth.
- Anthropic has denied deliberate degradation, attributing issues to usage limits and reasoning defaults; the company had not responded to VentureBeat's inquiry by April 13.
- The episode comes as Anthropic continues to navigate the Pentagon supply-chain-risk litigation (see March reports) and compute-capacity pressure tied to its CoreWeave multi-year deal (April 10).

### Stanford AI Index 2026 Report — Expert-Public Disconnect Widens on Jobs and Healthcare
**Date:** April 13, 2026 | **Sources:** [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/04/13/stanford-report-highlights-growing-disconnect-between-ai-insiders-and-everyone-else/), [The Next Web](https://thenextweb.com/news/stanford-ai-index-2026-public-anxiety-expert-disconnect), [Stanford HAI](https://hai.stanford.edu/ai-index/2026-ai-index-report/public-opinion), [TechBuzz AI](https://www.techbuzz.ai/articles/stanford-ai-index-reveals-experts-public-worlds-apart-on-ai)

Stanford's Institute for Human-Centered AI released the **2026 AI Index Report** on April 13, finding a sharp and widening gap between AI experts' optimism and the public's anxiety:

- **64% of Americans** expect AI to reduce jobs over the next 20 years; only **5%** expect more jobs.
- Just **23%** of the public believes AI will positively affect how people do their jobs — versus **73% of experts**.
- On healthcare: **84% of experts** see positive impact, vs. **44% of the public**.
- On the economy: **69% of experts** see net benefit, vs. **21% of the public**.
- Gen Z excitement about AI dropped from **36% (2025) to 22% (2026)**; anger rose from 22% to 31%.
- U.S. trust in government AI regulation is the **lowest globally at 31%**.
- Generative AI is on track to assist **~18% of U.S. work hours by 2030**, roughly double public estimates.
- The report warns the divide may fuel regulatory pushback as deployment accelerates.

## Regulatory Updates

### EU AI Act — Enforcement Status Heading Into August 2026 Deadline
**Date:** Updated April 14, 2026 | **Sources:** [European Parliament Think Tank](https://www.europarl.europa.eu/thinktank/en/document/EPRS_ATA(2026)785670), [EPthinktank](https://epthinktank.eu/2026/03/18/enforcement-of-the-ai-act/), [IAPP](https://iapp.org/news/a/european-commission-misses-deadline-for-ai-act-guidance-on-high-risk-systems), [artificialintelligenceact.eu](https://artificialintelligenceact.eu)

As of mid-April 2026, the EU AI Act is in active rollout:

- **Prohibited practices**: in force since February 2025.
- **General-purpose AI (GPAI) rules**: in force since August 2025, overseen by the **AI Office**.
- **Full applicability**: August 2026, with high-risk systems in regulated products phased in through 2027.
- Member States must stand up **AI regulatory sandboxes** by August 2026.
- The Commission **missed its February 2026 deadline** for high-risk guidance under Article 6, stoking delay debates in the November 2025 **Digital Omnibus** proposal that would further centralize AI Office supervision.
- Penalties up to **7% of global turnover** remain available for serious violations.
