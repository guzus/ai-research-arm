## Breaking News

### OpenAI — Kevin Weil and Bill Peebles Exit as Company Sheds "Side Quests"
**Date:** April 17, 2026 | **Source:** [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/04/17/kevin-weil-and-bill-peebles-exit-openai-as-company-continues-to-shed-side-quests/)

Two senior OpenAI leaders announced their departures on Friday, April 17:
- **Kevin Weil**, who had moved from CPO to lead OpenAI's science research initiative (**OpenAI for Science**).
- **Bill Peebles**, the researcher behind the **Sora** video model.

The exits follow OpenAI's public pivot away from consumer-facing "side quests." The Sora web/app service is scheduled to go fully dark **April 26, 2026**, with the Sora API sunsetting **September 24, 2026** — a decision previously attributed to unsustainable compute economics ($1–15M/day at peak vs. sharply declining user retention). OpenAI for Science is being absorbed into adjacent teams; the recent **GPT-Rosalind** launch (April 17, covered separately) is framed as the consolidated life-sciences bet going forward.

The departures land in the middle of a broader OpenAI reorganization in April 2026 as the company concentrates resources on enterprise, agents, and its in-development "superapp."

### OpenAI — Internal Memo: Amazon Partnership Is the New Enterprise Growth Engine
**Date:** April 17, 2026 (week-of reporting on April 12 memo) | **Sources:** [GeekWire](https://www.geekwire.com/2026/openai-sees-staggering-demand-for-amazon-offering-says-microsoft-partnership-held-it-back/), [AlphaSpread](https://www.alphaspread.com/market-news/corporate-moves/openai-cites-amazon-partnership-as-key-growth-driver-notes-limits-of-microsoft-tie-up), [MarketWatch via Morningstar](https://www.morningstar.com/news/marketwatch/20260417192/amazons-stock-peeks-into-record-territory-as-the-ai-script-has-flipped-to-a-positive)

OpenAI CRO **Denise Dresser** circulated an internal memo (dated April 12, reported through April 17) describing **"frankly staggering"** inbound enterprise demand for OpenAI models via **AWS Bedrock** since the February 2026 Amazon partnership announcement.

- Dresser: the Microsoft partnership "has been foundational to our success" but has "limited our ability to meet enterprises where they are."
- Under the February deal, **Amazon committed up to $50B into OpenAI**, alongside a **>$100B, 8-year cloud agreement** giving OpenAI **2 GW of AWS Trainium** capacity.
- Microsoft retains Azure as exclusive host for OpenAI's core APIs, its IP license, and a revenue share on OpenAI's other cloud arrangements.
- Amazon stock gained **+5.3% in the week of April 17**, up **+20.5% MTD**, partly on this sentiment — first real market read on the deal since the Feb announcement.

## Funding & Acquisitions

### Factory — $150M Series C at $1.5B Valuation for Enterprise Coding Agents
**Date:** April 16, 2026 (surfaced/expanded April 17 coverage) | **Sources:** [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/04/16/factory-hits-1-5b-valuation-to-build-ai-coding-for-enterprises/), [Factory Blog](https://factory.ai/news/series-c), [The AI Insider](https://theaiinsider.tech/2026/04/17/factory-raises-150m-at-1-5b-valuation-to-scale-ai-coding-agents-for-enterprises/), [Tech Funding News](https://techfundingnews.com/factory-ai-150m-series-c-unicorn-ai-agents-developers/)

Factory, a 2023-founded AI coding-agent startup led by **Matan Grinberg** (ex-UC Berkeley PhD), closed a **$150M Series C** at a **$1.5B valuation**. Notable specifics:

- **Lead:** Khosla Ventures, with **Keith Rabois** joining the board.
- **Participants:** Sequoia Capital, Insight Partners, Blackstone, Evantic Capital, 20VC, NEA, Mantis VC.
- **Product:** "Droids" — AI agents for enterprise code generation, testing, review, documentation, and deployment; explicitly **model-agnostic**, routing across Anthropic Claude, DeepSeek, and others.
- **Customers:** Morgan Stanley, EY, Palo Alto Networks, Nvidia, Adobe, Adyen, MongoDB, Bayer, Zapier.
- **Growth signal:** Revenue **doubled monthly** for the past six months.
- **Competitive positioning:** Directly against Claude Code, Cursor, and Cognition, pitching enterprise governance and multi-model orchestration as the moat.
