### Half of xAI's Founding Team Has Now Left — Two Co-Founders Depart in Two Days
**Date:** February 10–11, 2026

Elon Musk's xAI lost two more co-founders in rapid succession, bringing the total departures to six of the original twelve founding members — exactly half the team — in less than three years.

**The Departures:**
- **Yuhuai (Tony) Wu** announced his exit on Monday night (February 10). Wu, formerly of Google, becomes the fifth co-founder to leave xAI
- **Jimmy Ba**, an influential AI researcher who reported directly to Musk, posted a gracious departure note on X on Tuesday (February 11), thanking Musk for the opportunity
- Five of the six co-founder departures have occurred in just the last year

**Previous Co-Founder Exits:**
- Kyle Kosic (2024) — left for OpenAI
- Christian Szegedy (February 2025) — former Google veteran
- Igor Babuschkin (2025) — left to found his own company
- Greg Yang (January 2026) — departed after a Lyme disease diagnosis

**Additional Engineer Departures:**
At least nine engineers total, including the two co-founders, have publicly disclosed exits in the past week. Shayan Salehian (product infrastructure, ex-Twitter/X) said he was leaving to "start something new," and Vahid Kazemi (machine learning) posted that he left weeks ago.

**Why It Matters:**
The exodus comes shortly after xAI merged with SpaceX in a deal valuing the combined entity at $1.25 trillion (SpaceX at $1T, xAI at $250B). The departures also coincide with regulatory probes across Europe, Asia, and the U.S. over Grok's role in enabling mass-creation of non-consensual deepfake images. Some employees have reportedly complained about unrealistic expectations set in the race against OpenAI and Anthropic.

### Google Pushes AI Shopping Features Into Search and Gemini Chatbot
**Date:** February 11, 2026

Google announced a major push to monetize AI interactions through shopping, adding purchase capabilities directly within its Gemini chatbot and AI Mode in Search.

**Key Details:**
- Users can now **buy items from Etsy and Wayfair directly within Gemini** — the first in-chatbot purchases for Google's AI assistant
- A new **"Direct Offers"** feature in AI Mode lets brands offer exclusive discounts to high-intent shoppers surfaced by Google's AI
- Google is testing new **ad formats in AI Mode** allowing retailers to place products directly within AI-generated search answers
- Pilot partners include **Petco, e.l.f. Cosmetics, Samsonite, Rugs USA**, and Shopify merchants
- Builds on Google's earlier "agentic commerce" rollout with Walmart, Shopify, and Target

**Political Scrutiny:**
Senator Elizabeth Warren has already raised concerns in a letter about consumer privacy and AI systems pushing users toward higher spending.

**Why It Matters:**
As OpenAI tests ads in ChatGPT and Anthropic pledges to remain ad-free, Google is taking a third path — integrating direct commerce into AI interactions. This signals that the AI monetization battle is fragmenting into three distinct strategies: ads (OpenAI), subscriptions (Anthropic), and shopping/commerce (Google).

### Zhipu Unveils GLM-5 — China's Latest Frontier Model Trained on Huawei Chips
**Date:** February 11, 2026

Chinese AI developer Zhipu released GLM-5, its fifth-generation frontier model, intensifying competition with DeepSeek ahead of an expected V4 launch.

**Key Details:**
- Built on a **Mixture of Experts (MoE)** architecture with roughly **double the parameters** of its predecessor
- Adopts **DeepSeek Sparse Attention** for improved efficiency — the same technique powering DeepSeek's models
- Trained **entirely on domestic Huawei Ascend chips**, demonstrating complete independence from Western semiconductor supply chains
- Features advanced reasoning, coding, creative writing, and agentic AI capabilities

**Competitive Context:**
The launch arrives as DeepSeek simultaneously expanded its chatbot's context window from 128K tokens to over 1 million — a nearly tenfold increase — ahead of the expected DeepSeek V4 release around February 17 (Lunar New Year). China's AI labs are racing to establish dominance before DeepSeek V4 sets a new benchmark.

**Key Details:**
- Users write a public Threads post starting with **"Dear Algo"** followed by what kind of content they want more or less of

### Microsoft Explores Superconducting Power Cables for AI Data Centers
**Date:** February 10–11, 2026

Microsoft revealed it is testing high-temperature superconducting (HTS) power cables as a solution to the growing bottleneck of powering AI data centers faster than electrical grids can be upgraded.

**Key Details:**
- HTS cables have **zero electrical resistance** — no voltage drops, no heat generation during power transmission
- A pilot with **VEIR** (Massachusetts-based superconductor vendor) delivered **3 megawatts via a single HTS cable** in a simulated data hall in November
- HTS cables require only a **2-meter-wide trench** vs. traditional overhead transmission lines
- Microsoft invested in VEIR, which closed a $75M Series B last year

**Challenge:**
The technology requires cryogenic cooling to maintain optimal superconductor temperatures. Microsoft has not disclosed when it could deploy HTS cables at scale.

**Why It Matters:**
With data centers being built faster than power grids can expand, and communities pushing back against massive substations, HTS cables could allow data centers to receive more power through smaller physical infrastructure — a critical enabler for AI's growing energy demands.

### Trump Administration Weighs Chip Tariff Exemptions for Big Tech
**Date:** February 10–11, 2026

The Trump administration is preparing to spare Amazon, Google, and Microsoft from the next wave of semiconductor tariffs, tying exemptions to TSMC's expansion of U.S. manufacturing, according to the Financial Times.

**Key Details:**
- Exemption framework would **link tariff relief to TSMC's U.S. investment scale** — the more TSMC builds in the U.S., the more tariff-free chips its American customers can import
- Companies building new fabrication plants could import up to **2.5x the capacity** of planned facilities tariff-free during construction
- Existing plants qualify for up to **1.5x their current capacity** in tariff-free imports
- TSMC has pledged **$165 billion** to expand its U.S. footprint
- Plans are **not yet signed** by President Trump and remain in flux

**Why It Matters:**
The exemptions would shield AI hyperscalers from higher chip costs at a critical moment — Alphabet alone plans $185B in 2026 capex, and any tariff-driven cost increase on imported semiconductors could slow the AI infrastructure buildout.
