### Meta Reportedly Weighing 20% Workforce Reduction (~16,000 Jobs) to Fund AI Spending
**Date:** March 14, 2026 | **Sources:** [Reuters (via Business Insider)](https://www.businessinsider.com/meta-weighing-major-layoffs-as-it-pours-billions-into-ai-2026-3), [Fox Business](https://www.foxbusiness.com/technology/meta-eyes-massive-20-workforce-cut-ai-infrastructure-costs-continue-soar-across-operations-report), [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/14/meta-reportedly-considering-layoffs-that-could-affect-20-of-the-company/), [Investing.com](https://www.investing.com/news/company-news/meta-prepares-for-20-workforce-cull-to-offset-600-billion-ai-bet-4561236)

Reuters reported on March 14 that Meta is considering layoffs affecting up to 20% of its ~79,000-person workforce — approximately 16,000 jobs — to offset soaring AI infrastructure costs:
- Would be Meta's largest reduction since the 2022–2023 layoffs that eliminated a combined 21,000 jobs.
- Cuts are designed to offset Meta's planned **$600 billion AI data center investment** through 2028 and hundreds of millions in compensation for top AI researchers.
- Some managers have been asked to prepare cost-cutting plans; timing and final scope are not yet determined. One person familiar with the company's thinking suggested cuts could come within a month.
- Meta spokesperson Andy Stone called the reporting "speculative" about "theoretical approaches."
- CEO Zuckerberg has noted that projects once requiring entire teams can now be accomplished by single individuals using AI. Meta recently created a new AI engineering organization with manager-to-employee ratios as high as **1:50**.
- Comes on top of Meta's delayed "Avocado" AI model (covered in previous report) and the broader wave of AI-driven tech layoffs in 2026.

- **N1X AI PC Superchip**: Partnership with MediaTek for an ARM SoC reportedly matching RTX 5070 performance.
- Jensen Huang's keynote is Monday March 16 at 11 a.m. PT from SAP Center; 30,000+ attendees from 190 countries.

### Microsoft Copilot Cowork Launches — Multi-Model Enterprise AI Agent Powered by Anthropic Claude
**Date:** March 9, 2026 | **Sources:** [Microsoft 365 Blog](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/microsoft-365/blog/2026/03/09/copilot-cowork-a-new-way-of-getting-work-done/), [Axios](https://www.axios.com/2026/03/09/microsoft-copilot-cowork-anthropic), [eWeek](https://www.eweek.com/news/microsoft-agentic-copilot-cowork-claude/), [TechFinitive](https://www.techfinitive.com/microsoft-goes-multi-model-with-copilot-cowork/)

Microsoft launched Copilot Cowork, shifting its AI assistant from chat to autonomous task execution:
- Executes **multi-step, long-running tasks** grounded in users' emails, meetings, files, and data across Outlook, Teams, and Excel.
- Powered by **"Work IQ"** for contextual understanding; integrates **Anthropic's Claude** for multi-step reasoning, making Copilot **multi-model** for the first time.
- Creates plans from user requests, runs them in the background with checkpoints for approval, clarification, or pausing.
- Use cases: calendar cleanup, meeting preparation with briefing documents, company research into Excel workbooks, product launch planning.
- Currently in limited **Research Preview** for select customers; broader rollout via the **Frontier program** in late March 2026.
- Part of **Microsoft 365 E7** (unifying E5, Copilot, Agent 365), generally available **May 1, 2026 at $99/user/month**.

### Nvidia NemoClaw: Open-Source Enterprise AI Agent Platform Set for GTC Reveal
**Date:** March 13–15, 2026 | **Sources:** [Techloy](https://www.techloy.com/nvidia-to-launch-open-source-ai-agent-nemoclaw-at-gtc-2026-what-we-know-so-far/), [The Next Web](https://thenextweb.com/news/nvidia-is-reportedly-building-an-enterprise-ai-agent-platform), [Tom's Hardware](https://www.tomshardware.com/tech-industry/artificial-intelligence/nvidia-reportedly-building-its-own-ai-agent-to-compete-with-openclaw-report-claims-nemoclaw-will-supposedly-be-open-source-and-designed-for-enterprise-use)

Ahead of GTC 2026, reports revealed Nvidia's NemoClaw — an open-source AI agent platform for enterprise deployment:
- Enables companies to deploy AI agents that execute multi-step tasks on behalf of employees, including processing data, managing workflows, and handling instructions with limited human oversight.
- Features **built-in enterprise security**: audit logs, permission controls, compliance features — positioned as a secure alternative to consumer-facing agents like OpenClaw (which suffered security incidents).
- Notably **hardware-agnostic** — runs on any infrastructure regardless of chip vendor (Nvidia, AMD, Intel, or CPU-only), a strategic shift from Nvidia's traditional CUDA lock-in.
- Nvidia has reportedly pitched NemoClaw to **Salesforce, Cisco, Google, Adobe, and CrowdStrike**, though none have confirmed partnerships.
- Competes directly with OpenAI's Frontier agent orchestration, Microsoft's Copilot stack, and Google's Vertex AI Agent Builder.
- Supports **multi-agent collaboration** with supervisor and worker agents that delegate tasks intelligently.
- Jensen Huang expected to formally announce NemoClaw at his GTC keynote on Monday March 16.
