## Breaking News

### *The Information*: U.S. Government Unlikely to Extend Anthropic Export Control to Other AI Labs

**Date:** June 13, 2026 | **Sources:** [The Information — Exclusive: U.S. Government Unlikely to Extend Anthropic Export Control to Other AI Companies](https://www.theinformation.com/briefings/u-s-government-unlikely-extend-anthropic-export-control-ai-companies), [Bloomberg — Anthropic Says US Orders Halt to Foreign Access for Fable 5, Mythos 5 AI Models](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-13/anthropic-says-us-limits-foreign-access-to-fable-5-mythos-5)

- **What's net-new:** A day after the June 12 export-control directive forced Anthropic to disable Fable 5 and Mythos 5 worldwide, *The Information* reported the Commerce Department's action is **unlikely to be extended to other frontier-model providers** — framing the move as targeted at Anthropic's specific models (tied to a claimed narrow jailbreak) rather than the start of a blanket frontier-AI export regime.
- **Anthropic's posture (June 13):** The company publicly characterized the directive as a **misunderstanding** and said it is **working to restore access as quickly as possible.** As of June 13, both models remained offline for all users; access to **Claude Opus 4.8** and Anthropic's other models was unaffected.
- **Why it matters:** This narrows the precedent's blast radius. If the export-control authority is *not* applied industry-wide, the June 12 takedown reads as a model-specific enforcement action against Anthropic rather than a new gating step every lab must clear before shipping — materially changing how OpenAI, Google, and xAI read their own deployment risk.

## Industry Moves

### *The Information*: "Anthropic Blindsides Its Business Partners"

**Date:** June 13, 2026 | **Source:** [The Information — Anthropic Blindsides Its Business Partners](https://www.theinformation.com/articles/anthropic-blindsides-business-partners) (see also [Customers Fret as Anthropic Launches Apps That Compete with Theirs](https://www.theinformation.com/briefings/customers-say-anthropic-plays-favorites))

- **What happened:** *The Information* reported that Anthropic has repeatedly **launched products and pricing changes that compete with or surprise its own business partners** with little advance notice. Two specifics: (1) when it released a version of the long-awaited **Mythos** model, Anthropic said it would **silently degrade performance** on tasks related to developing **advanced AI software/hardware** — then, after public criticism, **backtracked** to instead *alert* customers when a weaker model is substituted; (2) ahead of its April **Claude Design** prototyping tool, it courted firms like **Figma and Canva** as launch "partners" for a product that overlaps their own.
- **Why it matters:** It crystallizes a growing tension in the platform-vs.-application layer: as Anthropic ships more first-party apps, the labs it partners with increasingly compete with it. Combined with the export-control shutdown, it's a second June-13 storyline pressuring Anthropic's enterprise relationships.
