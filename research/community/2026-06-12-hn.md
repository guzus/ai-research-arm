### Notable Discussions

- **Anthropic Fable guardrail reversal** (298 comments): The Verge reports Anthropic apologized for shipping invisible safeguards in Claude Fable 5 that silently limited effectiveness for frontier LLM development tasks (pretraining pipelines, distributed training, accelerator design) via prompt modification, steering vectors, or PEFT. After backlash, they committed to visible notifications — [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48489229)

- **MiMo Code open-sourced** (237 comments): Xiaomi releases MiMo Code, its coding-focused LLM, as open source. Community comparing it against Qwen, DeepSeek, and Fable 5 on coding benchmarks — [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48490826)

- **AI coding hype vs. reality** (246 comments): "Lines of code got a better publicist" argues the discredited LoC productivity metric was revived specifically to make AI coding claims sound impressive. Heated debate about what actually measures AI developer productivity — [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48489402)

- **FablePool: crowd-funded AI builds** (136 comments): Pool money behind a natural-language prompt, then Claude Fable builds the software publicly. Sparks discussion on AI product commoditization and the sustainability of LLM-as-builder business models — [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48496539)

- **Claude Fable 5 benchmark critique** (100 comments): Endor Labs finds Fable 5 delivers "mid-tier" results on coding tasks despite marketing claims. Community debates evaluation methodology and whether Anthropic's guardrails contributed to lower scores — [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48492210)

- **AI nuclear war simulation** (174 comments): Researcher describes an LLM-driven nuclear crisis wargame (arXiv 2602.14740); models defaulted to nuclear escalation in the majority of scenarios, raising questions about AI in strategic decision-making — [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48495575)

- **Waymo Premier autonomous ride service** (393 comments): Waymo announces a premium autonomous vehicle tier. Largest comment thread of the snapshot, covering pricing, safety, geographic expansion, and competition with Cruise/Tesla — [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48492304)

- **DeepSeek-R1 open reproduction** (17 comments): HuggingFace's open-r1 repo continues gaining traction as the community tracks reproduction progress of DeepSeek's reasoning model — [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48489917)
