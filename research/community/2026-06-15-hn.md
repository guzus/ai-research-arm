### Notable Discussions

- **Not everyone is using AI for everything** (423 pts, 455 comments): DuckDuckGo founder Gabriel Weinberg argues AI adoption is more uneven than headlines suggest — most users treat AI like calculators for narrow tasks, not general assistants. Active thread on consumer AI vs. power-user AI and whether current usage patterns predict mainstream adoption - [discussion](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48527700)

- **Rio de Janeiro LLM provenance controversy** (280 pts, 149 comments): A GitHub issue teardown reveals that Nex-N2, promoted as a locally built Brazilian LLM, appears to be a merge of existing open-source models rather than a novel pretrained artifact. Raises broader questions about provenance claims in government-sponsored AI projects - [discussion](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48528371)

- **Local ML for 669 GB of personal video** (280 pts, 65 comments): Developer built an on-device pipeline on Apple M1 Max using open-source ML models to index, search, and cut clips from 628 GoPro videos — no cloud required, integrates with DaVinci Resolve - [discussion](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48528029)

- **Formal methods and the future of programming** (190 pts, 71 comments): Jane Street's post on formal verification as a mainstream software practice; thread surfaces interest in correctness guarantees as AI-generated code scales - [discussion](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48526633)

- **Stanford walkout on Sundar Pichai** (113 pts, 55 comments): Graduate students walked out during Google CEO's commencement speech in protest over AI ethics and labor concerns. Signals growing tension between tech leadership and the next generation of ML engineers - [discussion](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48533756)

- **Trace – on-device meeting transcription** (93 pts, 36 comments): Mac app uses on-device Whisper-style diarization + FastSpeech-based TTS, no audio uploaded, with mid-call key-moment flagging and live recap via keyboard shortcut - [discussion](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48521236)

- **AI is code, not magic** (69 pts, 39 comments): The Register opinion piece argues LLMs cannot self-improve beyond their training and prompting alone cannot substitute for architecture or data changes. Thread debates capability ceilings and emergent behavior - [discussion](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48532178)

- **Ask HN: What are you working on? (June 2026)** (156 pts, 571 comments): Monthly thread dense with AI/agent project reports — agentic coding tools, local inference stacks, RAG pipelines, and voice assistants feature heavily - [discussion](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48528779)
