- **Mistral AI Now Summit** (329 pts): First-hand notes from the summit — Mistral positioning itself as Europe's AI champion with a focus on open weights and enterprise deployments. Strong community discussion on European AI sovereignty. [link](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48325340)

- **You can just say it** (282 pts): Antirez (Redis author) piece on communicating directly with AI models — the insight that natural, direct language outperforms prompt-engineering tricks. Strong discussion on prompting philosophy. [link](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48324853)

- **Liquid AI LFM2.5-8B-A1B** (170 pts): A new 8B MoE model trained on 38T tokens with only 1B active parameters. - **We should be more tired than the model** (165 pts): Vicki Boykis essay arguing that AI-assisted knowledge work is displacing the productive exhaustion that leads to genuine expertise — we outsource the hard parts before internalizing them. [link](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48322118)

- **MCP is dead?** (146 pts): Engineering blog arguing Model Context Protocol has fundamental design flaws that will cause it to be superseded. Active debate between MCP advocates and critics; timing notable given rapid enterprise MCP adoption. [link](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48330436)

- **Mysterious Hy3 LLM** (116 pts): Max Woolf investigates an unknown model "Hy3" dominating OpenRouter ELO rankings by a wide margin with no public documentation. Community speculation includes a Hunyuan-based model from Tencent. [link](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48317294)

- **Shift robot training startup** (107 pts): Startup offering free home cleaning in exchange for using the sessions to train robot AI models. HN debate centers on labor value, consent, and whether this model is sustainable or exploitative of consumers. [link](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48327962)
