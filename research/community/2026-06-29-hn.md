### Notable Discussions
- **GLM 5.2 beats Claude in our benchmarks**: 417 points, 204 comments — the
  top AI story of the cycle. Semgrep's in-house cyber-security benchmark
  ("Mythos") puts Fireworks-hosted GLM 5.2 ahead of Claude. Notable because
  GLM 5.2 is the model powering this pipeline's scheduled lanes. Thread probes
  whether a vendor-tuned security benchmark generalizes — read methodology
  before quoting the headline. - [discuss](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48709670)
- **I used Claude Code to get a second opinion on my MRI**: 335 points, 444
  comments — highest engagement by comment count. A practitioner ran their MRI
  through Claude Code Opus as a clinical second opinion. Thread centers on
  reliability, liability, and the line between "research" and "medical
  advice." Key insight: useful as a prompt to seek a real specialist, not as
  a diagnostic authority. - [discuss](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48708941)
- **Professor denounces mass AI fraud on an exam at Brown**: 236 points, 327
  comments — El País coverage of large-scale LLM-assisted cheating at Brown.
  Discussion covers detection, academic-integrity policy, and whether
  traditional exams remain salvageable. - [discuss](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48708991)
- **A way to exclude sensitive files issue still open for OpenAI Codex**: 173
  points, 120 comments — long-standing GitHub issue (#2847) on Codex lacking a
  robust sensitive-file exclusion mechanism. Discussion covers agent
  sandboxing, secrets leakage in coding agents, and how Claude Code's
  allow/deny tool gating compares. - [discuss](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48706714)
- **Tokenmaxxing is dead, long live tokenmaxxing**: 111 points, 137 comments —
  essay arguing the context-stuffing era is ending as long-context and

- **TOP500 at ISC'26 — new #1 supercomputer**: 67 points, 35 comments — Chips
