### Notable Discussions

- **"AI psychosis" in companies** (1927 pts, 1124 comments): Mitchell Hashimoto's tweet arguing that entire firms are operating under a collective AI delusion — by far the day's largest thread, with HN engaging on whether enterprise AI buy-in has decoupled from observable productivity gains. [link](https://twitter.com/mitchellh/status/2055380239711457578)
- **Frontier AI broke open CTF** (353 pts, 352 comments): Long post-mortem arguing that current LLMs can solve nearly every public capture-the-flag challenge end-to-end, making the traditional jeopardy-style CTF format obsolete for human-vs-human play. [link](https://kabir.au/blog/the-ctf-scene-is-dead)
- **SANA-WM world model** (319 pts, 131 comments): NVIDIA Labs released a 2.6B-parameter open-source world model that generates 1-minute, 720p video from prompts — sized to run on consumer GPUs and being benchmarked against Sora/Veo by commenters. [link](https://nvlabs.github.io/Sana/WM/)
- **Zerostack coding agent in Rust** (263 pts, 94 comments): A Unix-philosophy coding agent (small composable binaries, pipe-friendly) written entirely in Rust — discussion mostly on whether the "do one thing well" approach can compete with monolithic agents like Claude Code and Codex. [link](https://crates.io/crates/zerostack/1.0.0)
- **δ-mem efficient online memory** (201 pts, 55 comments): New arXiv paper on a delta-based memory mechanism for LLMs that updates incrementally rather than recomputing — comments focus on whether it's competitive with the recent state-space and RWKV-style alternatives. [link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.12357)
- **OpenAI × Malta national rollout** (128 pts, 119 comments): Government of Malta will provide ChatGPT Plus to all citizens — first nation-state-level consumer AI rollout. Comments debate the precedent for state-funded AI access and the data-residency implications. [link](https://openai.com/index/malta-chatgpt-plus-partnership/)
- **MCP Hello Page** (72 pts, 25 comments): A minimal "hello world" for the Model Context Protocol — useful entry point as MCP adoption broadens to non-Anthropic agents. [link](https://www.hybridlogic.co.uk/blog/2026/05/mcp-hello-page)
- **Self-Distillation for Continual Learning** (25 pts, 12 comments): arXiv paper claiming self-distillation alone is sufficient to mitigate catastrophic forgetting — small but technically substantive thread on whether the result holds at frontier scale. [link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.19897)
