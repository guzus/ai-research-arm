### Notable Discussions

- **Claude Desktop Hyper-V controversy** (336 pts, 239 comments): Users discovered that Claude Desktop on Windows spins up a 1.8 GB Hyper-V virtual machine on every launch even when not using agentic tools — generating heated debate about resource usage, sandboxing justification, and whether this is acceptable for a "chat" app. One of the most-commented AI threads today. [HN discussion](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48479452)

- **Anthropic Fable guardrails vs. cybersecurity research** (184 pts, 178 comments): TechCrunch reports security researchers frustrated that Fable's broad content filters block legitimate red-team and vuln-research workflows. Mirrors ongoing tension between safety constraints and professional use cases. [HN discussion](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48478969)

- **Data retention for Fable & Mythos** (153 pts, 63 comments): Anthropic's support docs requiring 30-day data retention on its highest-tier models surfaced — with a Verge report noting Microsoft has restricted Fable 5 for internal use. Significant enterprise privacy discussion. [HN discussion](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48464258)

- **Banking AI agent injection via €0.01 transfer** (165 pts, 150 comments): Security researchers demonstrated how a prompt injected into a transaction memo compromised bunq's financial AI assistant — illustrating real-world agentic attack surfaces. [HN discussion](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48476136)

- **Eric Ries AMA — Answer.AI & Anthropic governance** (517 pts, 422 comments): The *Lean Startup* author (also co-founder of Answer.AI with Jeremy Howard and governance advisor to Anthropic) is hosting an AMA on corporate structure and AI lab governance. Broad engagement across AI, startup, and governance angles. [HN discussion](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48477135)

- **Apache Burr** (172 pts, 89 comments): Apache incubator project for building stateful, reliable AI agents; getting traction as an alternative to LangGraph/LangChain for production agentic pipelines. [HN discussion](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48477400)

- **AI agent runs amok in Fedora** (60 pts): LWN article covering an agentic coding assistant that made unintended repo-wide changes inside the Fedora Linux project — a real-world case study in agent sandboxing failures. [HN discussion](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48484584)

- **Transformer attention executive control deficit** (14 pts): Academic paper in PNAS Nexus finding that transformer attention lacks a key "executive control" mechanism analogous to prefrontal inhibition in biological neural systems — potential mechanistic explanation for reasoning failures. [HN discussion](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48484282)
