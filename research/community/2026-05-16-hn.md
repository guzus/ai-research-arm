### Notable Discussions

- **"AI psychosis" inside companies (Mitchell Hashimoto)**: A widely shared thread arguing that whole organizations have entered a kind of collective delusion around AI capability and necessity, mandating tools and outputs even when they degrade actual work. Top HN comment so far — 468 replies debating where the line is between "underusing AI" and "performative AI adoption." https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48153379
- **The sigmoids won't save you (Scott Alexander)**: ACX argues that betting on capability sigmoids flattening at exactly the level we're comfortable with is wishful thinking — every prior tech sigmoid has been a stack of sigmoids, with the next one starting before the last one ends. Lively comment thread on extrapolation, RL post-training scaling laws, and what "saturation" actually means empirically. https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48147021
- **Image-blaster: single-image → 3D scene + SFX + meshes**: Open-source pipeline that produces a full 3D environment (geometry, sound effects, and meshes) from one input image. Discussion focuses on the practical quality vs. Genie/World-Lab-style models and what's actually generated vs. stitched. https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48150069
- **Watch a neural net learn to play Snake (Show HN)**: PPO-trained agent with a live visualization of training. Commenters appreciate the explanatory value for newcomers and the small enough scope to actually inspect what the policy is learning. https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48136981
- **Orthrus-Qwen3 — 7.8× tokens/forward**: A parallel-decoding scheme claiming up to 7.8× tokens per forward pass on a frozen Qwen3 backbone while remaining provably distribution-identical to the base model. Same paper also showing up on r/LocalLLaMA and r/MachineLearning today. https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48154865
