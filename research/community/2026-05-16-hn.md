### Notable Discussions

- **"AI psychosis" inside companies (Mitchell Hashimoto)** — A widely shared thread arguing that whole organizations have entered a kind of collective delusion around AI capability and necessity, mandating tools and outputs even when they degrade actual work. Now the day's top HN story by a wide margin (1291 pts / 636 comments) with debate on where "underusing AI" ends and "performative AI adoption" begins. https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48153379
- **The sigmoids won't save you (Scott Alexander)** — ACX pushes back on the comfortable "capability sigmoid will plateau at exactly the level we're OK with" narrative: every prior tech sigmoid has been a stack of sigmoids with the next starting before the last ends. Comments dig into RL post-training scaling laws and what "saturation" empirically means. https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48147021
- **Frontier AI has broken the open CTF format** — New entrant to the front page: practical write-up on how LLM-augmented players and pure-LLM agents have collapsed the difficulty curve of public Capture The Flag competitions, with downstream implications for offensive-security training and benchmark design. https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48157559
- **Image-blaster: single-image → 3D scene + SFX + meshes** — Open-source pipeline producing a full 3D environment (geometry, sound effects, meshes) from one image. Discussion focuses on practical quality vs. Genie/World-Lab-style models and what's actually generated vs. stitched. https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48150069
- **Watch a neural net learn to play Snake (Show HN)** — PPO agent with live training visualization in the browser; comments praise the small enough scope for newcomers to actually inspect what the policy is learning, plus side-discussion on reward shaping. https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48136981
- **Orthrus-Qwen3 — 7.8× tokens/forward** — Parallel-decoding scheme claiming up to 7.8× tokens per forward pass on a frozen Qwen3 backbone while remaining provably distribution-identical to the base model. Same paper trending on r/LocalLLaMA and r/MachineLearning today, making it the most-discussed inference-speed result of the day. https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48154865
