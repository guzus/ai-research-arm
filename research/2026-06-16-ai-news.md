## Breaking News

### Net-New: First Restoration Timeline — Anthropic Models Locked Until U.S. Apparatus Is "Hardened," Possibly "Weeks"

**Date:** June 15, 2026 (not in prior reports) | **Sources:** [Quartz — Anthropic sends staff to Washington to fight Fable, Mythos export controls](https://qz.com/anthropic-staff-washington-export-controls-fable-mythos-061526), [TechTimes — Anthropic Races to Lift Fable 5 Export Ban: Top Engineers Sent to Washington for a Deal](https://www.techtimes.com/articles/318376/20260615/anthropic-races-lift-fable-5-export-ban-top-engineers-sent-washington-deal.htm), [HNGN — Anthropic Races to Lift Fable 5 Export Ban](https://www.hngn.com/articles/271603/20260615/anthropic-races-lift-fable-5-export-ban-top-engineers-sent-washington-deal.htm)

- **What's net-new:** For the first time, reporting attaches a **directional timeline** to the suspension. Per an administration official, the models **need to remain locked down until the U.S. government's national-security apparatus is "hardened," which could happen "in the next few weeks."** Every prior report (through June 15) characterized the situation as *no ETA / no restoration date*. This reframes the standoff from "indefinite" to "weeks, conditional on a government-side fix" — and notably puts the remediation burden partly on the *government*, not solely on Anthropic shipping a patch.
- **Net-new procedural detail:** The restoration negotiation ran through a **Saturday, June 13 call**. Representing the government: **Commerce Secretary Howard Lutnick** and **National Cyber Director Sean Cairncross.** Representing Anthropic: co-founder and **chief compute officer Tom Brown.** (Prior reports named these figures but not the Saturday-call structure or the "both sides want it back, government must harden first" framing.)
- **Status as of June 16:** **Fable 5 and Mythos 5 remain offline.** No deal has been announced, no public statement that the government's concerns are satisfied, and no confirmation access has been restored. Anthropic maintains the bypass was a **narrow, previously-known jailbreak** and the action an overreaction; the government's position (via the "hardening" framing) is that the lockdown is a precaution while it shores up its own posture.
- **Why it matters:** A soft "few weeks" estimate is the first signal that this is being treated as a *temporary, fixable* posture rather than a permanent retirement — but the conditionality ("until the apparatus is hardened") means the off-ramp is now partly outside Anthropic's control, which complicates the company's October IPO-prep window.
