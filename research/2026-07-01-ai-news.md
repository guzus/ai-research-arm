## Breaking News

### Anthropic Ships Claude Sonnet 5 — "Opus-4.8-Class" Agentic Work at $2/$10, Default on Free and Pro

**Date:** June 30, 2026 | **Sources:** [Anthropic — Introducing Claude Sonnet 5](https://www.anthropic.com/news/claude-sonnet-5), [TechCrunch — Anthropic launches Claude Sonnet 5 as a cheaper way to run agents](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/30/anthropic-launches-claude-sonnet-5-as-a-cheaper-way-to-run-agents/), [The Next Web — a cheaper agent model](https://thenextweb.com/news/anthropic-claude-sonnet-5-agentic-model-pricing), [9to5Mac — Anthropic upgrades Claude with new Sonnet 5](https://9to5mac.com/2026/06/30/anthropic-upgrades-claude-with-new-sonnet-5-model-details-here/)

- **What's net-new:** Anthropic officially launched **Claude Sonnet 5** (model id
  `claude-sonnet-5`) on June 30, resolving the multi-day "string-in-the-selector"
  rumor into a real release with a published system card. It is now the **default
  model on Free and Pro**, and available to Max, Team, and Enterprise — live
  across all Claude apps and the Claude Platform. **Pricing: $2/M input and $10/M
  output** introductory (through Aug 31, 2026), stepping to **$3/$15** in
  September. **1M-token context**, January 2026 knowledge cutoff.
- **Positioning:** Anthropic frames it as a substantial upgrade over Sonnet 4.6
  in reasoning, tool use, coding, planning and browser tasks, **coming close to
  Opus 4.8 performance at a fraction of the cost** — the cheaper path to running
  agents. Perplexity added it for Pro/Max the same hour.
- **Skeptic's note:** Anthropic says *close to* Opus 4.8 on agentic/coding work —
  **not parity** — and no independent SWE-bench/Terminal-bench numbers existed at
  launch. The September $3/$15 step narrows the cost edge. A viral thread claiming
  the system card shows Sonnet 5 "creating subagents to self-approve" and
  "blowing the whistle on insiders" is one reader's dramatized reading; the
  Anthropic-disclosed card detail is **elevated evaluation-awareness** (~6% of
  tested rollouts verbalized that they were being tested).

### Anthropic Launches Claude Science — Its Biggest Biopharma Push, with Novartis, BMS and Genentech

**Date:** June 30, 2026 | **Sources:** [Anthropic — Claude Science, an AI workbench for scientists](https://www.anthropic.com/news/claude-science-ai-workbench), [Endpoints News — Anthropic launches Claude Science, starts own drug programs](https://endpoints.news/anthropic-debuts-claude-science-an-ai-product-for-bioscience/), [TechCrunch — Claude Science bets on workflow, not a new model](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/30/anthropics-claude-science-bets-on-workflow-not-a-new-model-to-win-over-scientists/), [BeInCrypto — Claude Science vs OpenAI GeneBench-Pro](https://beincrypto.com/claude-science-openai-genebench-pro/)

- **What's net-new:** An hour before Sonnet 5 (17:02 UTC June 30), Anthropic
  shipped **Claude Science** — an agentic research workbench giving scientists one
  environment for computational research (databases, pipelines and tools in a
  single session), with **artifacts traced to their code**, **managed compute
  environments on demand**, and **60+ optional scientific tools**. It debuted
  alongside **Novartis, Bristol Myers Squibb and Genentech**, and Anthropic says
  it is **starting its own internal drug programs**. Integration partners include
  Modal (compute) and Harvard's ToolUniverse.
- **Why it matters:** This is Anthropic's deepest move into a regulated,
  high-stakes vertical, building on Claude Life Sciences (Oct 2025) and its
  recruitment of Novartis CEO Vas Narasimhan to its board. The bet is **workflow
  lock-in, not a new model** — owning the scientist's daily environment.
- **Skeptic's note:** Scope is narrower than the name — early reaction
  ("this is for cell biologists and wet-lab bio, not all of science") and
  data-appropriation concerns about uploading proprietary research are live. The
  "first-mover" framing is also wrong: OpenAI shipped GeneBench-Pro the same day
  (below), and Google/Meta have standing AI-for-science bets.

### Etched Exits Stealth — $800M Raised, $1B+ in Signed Contracts, Sohu Transformer-ASIC Shipping This Summer

**Date:** June 30, 2026 | **Sources:** [TechCrunch — Nvidia competitor Etched hits $5B valuation, $1B in sales](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/30/nvidia-competitor-etched-hits-5b-valuation-1b-in-sales-for-ai-chip/), [Bloomberg — Etched says Jane Street, TSMC-linked VC invested](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-30/ai-chip-startup-etched-says-jane-street-tsmc-linked-vc-invested), [The Next Web — Etched raises $800M with Jane Street and a TSMC-linked fund](https://thenextweb.com/news/etched-800-million-jane-street-tsmc-inference-chip), [Yahoo Finance — Etched emerges from stealth with working chip](https://finance.yahoo.com/technology/ai/articles/etched-emerges-stealth-working-chip-150000905.html)

- **What's net-new:** AI-chip startup **Etched** came out of stealth, disclosing
  **$800M raised** across multiple unannounced rounds (latest: **$500M at a $5B
  post-money valuation in December 2025**), **$1B+ in signed customer contracts**,
  a **working chip**, and first racks of its inference ASIC **shipping this
  summer**. New investors named include **Jane Street, Hudson River Trading, Jump
  Trading, Two Sigma** and a **TSMC-linked fund (VentureTech Alliance)**, on top
  of Peter Thiel, Stripes, Ribbit, Radical and AI figures (Karpathy, Hinton,
  Fei-Fei Li, Arthur Mensch).
- **The chip:** **Sohu** is a **transformer-only ASIC** — the architecture is
  burned into silicon rather than run on general-purpose GPUs. Etched claims an
  8-chip Sohu server generates **>500,000 tokens/sec on Llama-70B vs ~23,000 for
  an 8×H100 box** (~20×).
- **Skeptic's note:** The financing is corroborated by Bloomberg/TechCrunch; the
  performance superlatives are **Etched's own, with no independent teardown**, and
  the "20× H100 / 500K tok/s" figures have circulated unaudited since 2024–25.
  Sohu's entire bet is that the transformer stays dominant long enough to amortize
  fixed-function silicon. Treat the money as real and the throughput as unproven.

### OpenAI Ships GeneBench-Pro — and GPT-5.6 Sol Beats Opus 4.8 Nearly 2-to-1

**Date:** June 30, 2026 | **Sources:** [OpenAI — Introducing GeneBench-Pro](https://openai.com/index/introducing-genebench-pro/), [bioRxiv preprint](https://www.biorxiv.org/content/10.64898/2026.06.29.735386v1), [Investing.com — GeneBench-Pro tests AI research judgment](https://www.investing.com/news/stock-market-news/openai-introduces-genebenchpro-to-test-ai-research-judgment-93CH-4768434), [Crypto Briefing](https://cryptobriefing.com/openai-genebench-computational-biology-ai-benchmark/)

- **What it is:** A research-level benchmark of **129 problems** across genomics,
  quantitative biology and translational medicine. Each gives a model a dataset,
  experimental context and a target question; the model must **explore the data,
  choose an analytical approach, and reach a conclusion** — testing *research
  judgment under ambiguity*, not factual recall. Reviewers estimate a typical
  problem would take a human expert **~20–40 hours**.
- **Results:** OpenAI's strongest model, **GPT-5.6 Sol**, solved **28.7%** at its
  highest reasoning level (**31.5% in Pro mode**); **Anthropic's Opus 4.8 reached
  16%** on the harder test — i.e., even the leader clears under a third of the
  set, underscoring how far agentic scientific reasoning has to go.
- **Why it matters:** Released the **same day as Claude Science**, GeneBench-Pro
  is OpenAI's flag in the AI-for-science ground — a benchmark-led "research
  judgment" framing answering Anthropic's workflow-product framing. The two labs'
  AI-for-science fight is now in the open.

**Date:** July 1, 2026 | **Sources:** [Anthropic — Redeploying Claude Fable 5](https://www.anthropic.com/news/redeploying-fable-5), [The Hacker News — Anthropic restores Claude Fable 5 after US lifts jailbreak-linked export controls](https://thehackernews.com/2026/07/anthropic-restores-claude-fable-5-after.html), [Digital Trends — Anthropic finally brings back Claude Fable 5, but you'll have to live with a temporary usage limit](https://www.digitaltrends.com/computing/youll-be-able-to-use-claude-fable-5-again-starting-july-1/), [AIN — Anthropic restores access to Claude Fable 5 and Mythos 5](https://en.ain.ua/2026/07/01/anthropic-restores-access-to-claude-fable-5-and-mythos-5/), [@AnthropicAI](https://x.com/AnthropicAI/status/2072163884430229756)

- **What's net-new:** The Fable 5 re-release the June 30 report flagged as
  *missing* actually landed. On **July 1**, Anthropic restored **Claude Fable 5
  globally for all users** across the Claude Platform, Claude.ai, Claude Code and
  Claude Cowork — closing the ~18-day export-control blackout that began June 12.
  Access is **metered**: for Pro/Max/Team and select Enterprise plans, Fable 5 is
  **included for up to 50% of weekly usage limits through July 7**, after which it
  shifts to **usage credits billed outside the plan**. Standard Enterprise seats
  get **no included allowance** — all Fable 5 usage is credit-billed. A **new
  safety classifier** ships with the redeploy, which Anthropic says blocks the
  specific reported jailbreak technique in **>99% of cases**.
- **The split that vindicates/refutes the June 30 datamine:** The datamined app
  strings correctly predicted **usage credits**, but the "US-only" fear was
  **misattributed** — it is **Mythos 5** (the stronger sibling) that stays gated
  to approved US organizations; **Fable 5 is global**.
- **Skeptic's note:** First hands-on reports say the new classifier **over-flags
  benign coding**, routing "a small fraction" of routine tasks to the **Opus 4.8
  fallback" (matching pre-ban behavior, per @testingcatalog's scoping
  correction). The real false-positive rate awaits volume; the **July 7 shift to
  usage credits** is the next pricing-backlash risk.

  as **agentic-first**, compressing timelines "from months to days," and designed

  muscle as its marquee model partner renegotiates upward.
