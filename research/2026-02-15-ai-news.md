### Hollywood vs. Seedance 2.0 — Paramount Sends Cease-and-Desist Over AI Copyright Infringement
**Date:** February 15, 2026

Hollywood studios are pushing back against ByteDance's Seedance 2.0 AI video model, which they say has quickly become a tool for "blatant" copyright infringement. Paramount sent ByteDance a formal cease-and-desist letter on Saturday, alleging the model produces vivid depictions of its famous franchises and characters without authorization.

**Key Details:**
- **Paramount cease-and-desist:** Sent on February 15 to ByteDance, claiming Seedance 2.0 "produces vivid depictions of Paramount's famous and iconic franchises and characters"
- **Broader Hollywood backlash:** Multiple entertainment organizations are pushing back against the model
- **Rapid spread:** Seedance 2.0 went viral after its February 9 launch, with users generating clips featuring recognizable IP from major studios
- **Previous controversy:** ByteDance had already suspended Seedance 2.0's voice-from-photo feature within 48 hours of launch over deepfake concerns
- **Seedance 2.0 capabilities:** Generates ~20-second high-fidelity video from text, images, and audio with physics-aware rendering

**Why It Matters:**
This is one of the first direct legal confrontations between a major Hollywood studio and a Chinese AI video generator. The cease-and-desist letter from Paramount sets a precedent for how studios will respond to AI models that can reproduce copyrighted characters and settings with high fidelity. It also adds copyright liability to the growing list of legal risks facing AI video generation companies.

### Computer Science Enrollment Drops 6% — First Decline Since Dot-Com Crash
**Date:** February 15, 2026

For the first time since the dot-com crash of the early 2000s, computer science enrollment at U.S. universities has declined, falling 6% in 2025 after a 3% drop in 2024. The trend is being attributed to student fears that AI will automate programming jobs.

**Key Details:**
- **System-wide CS enrollment dropped 6%** in 2025, following a 3% decline in 2024
- This is the **first sustained decline since the dot-com crash** (2000–2003)
- Students are citing **fears that AI will replace software engineering jobs** as a primary reason for choosing other majors
- The decline coincides with **30,700+ tech layoffs** in the first six weeks of 2026, with companies increasingly citing AI as the driver
- Some universities report students shifting toward **AI/ML-specific programs**, data science, and interdisciplinary degrees rather than traditional CS

**Why It Matters:**
The CS enrollment drop creates a paradox: the AI revolution is powered by software engineers, yet fear of that very revolution is discouraging the next generation from pursuing the field. If the trend continues, it could create a talent shortage precisely when AI companies need more engineers to build, train, and maintain increasingly complex systems.

### Sarvam AI Launches "Sarvam Edge" — Offline AI Stack for India's 10 Most-Spoken Languages
**Date:** February 14–15, 2026

Indian AI startup Sarvam AI unveiled Sarvam Edge, an on-device AI stack designed to run entirely offline on smartphones and consumer hardware — a first for multilingual AI processing in India's most-spoken languages.

**Key Details:**
- **Fully offline:** Processes speech recognition, synthesis, translation, and document digitization without any cloud connectivity
- **10 Indic languages:** High-accuracy speech-to-text for India's 10 most popular languages
- **Zero per-query costs:** No usage-based pricing or server round-trips
- **Privacy-first:** "Your data never leaves your device"
- **India-specific value:** Addresses bandwidth variability and rural connectivity gaps that limit cloud-first AI services
- **Timing:** Announced ahead of Sarvam AI's participation in the **India AI Impact Summit** (Feb 16–20)

**Why It Matters:**
Sarvam Edge addresses a fundamental limitation of cloud-based AI: it doesn't work without internet. For India's 1.4 billion people — many in areas with intermittent connectivity — offline AI processing in local languages represents a genuine accessibility breakthrough. The approach also eliminates the privacy concerns inherent in cloud-based inference.

### Apple Quietly Acquired Graph Database Startup Kuzu for On-Device AI
**Date:** February 11, 2026 (reported)

Apple acquired Canadian graph database startup Kuzu, a company specializing in embedded graph databases optimized for complex analytical workloads. The acquisition, completed in October 2025, is aimed at enhancing on-device AI processing on Apple Silicon.

**Key Details:**
- **Company:** Kuzu — an embedded graph database built for query speed, scalability, and ease of use
- **Acquisition completed:** October 2025; reported publicly February 11, 2026
- **Founded by:** Turkish academicians at University of Waterloo (Kitchener-Waterloo, Canada)
- **Technology:** Graph database optimized for handling complex analytical workloads on large datasets, with features including full text search and vector indices
- **Strategic fit:** Kuzu's embedded design allows direct integration with **Apple Silicon processors**, enabling data analysis locally on devices instead of sending data to cloud servers
- **Post-acquisition:** Kuzu's website was taken down and its GitHub repository archived — standard for Apple acquisitions
- **Apple's AI strategy:** Strengthens on-device AI responsiveness while maintaining Apple's privacy-first approach

**Why It Matters:**
The Kuzu acquisition fits Apple's long-standing strategy of building AI that runs on-device rather than in the cloud. Graph databases are particularly useful for AI systems that need to understand relationships between entities — a capability that could enhance Siri, on-device search, and Apple Intelligence's contextual understanding.

### Gemini Closes Gap With ChatGPT — 750M Monthly Users as Alphabet Tops $400B Revenue
**Date:** February 15, 2026 (analysis)

A Motley Fool analysis published February 15 highlights that Google's Gemini AI app has reached 750 million monthly active users — up 100 million from the prior quarter — putting it within striking distance of ChatGPT's estimated 800 million MAUs. Alphabet simultaneously reported crossing $400 billion in annual revenue for the first time.

**Key Details:**
- **Gemini MAU:** 750 million, up 100 million quarter-over-quarter (disclosed in Alphabet Q4 2025 earnings on Feb 4)
- **ChatGPT MAU:** Estimated ~800 million — Gemini is now within 6% of catching up
- **Alphabet Q4 2025 revenue:** $113.8 billion, up 18% year-over-year
- **Annual revenue:** Surpassed **$400 billion** for the first time
- **Advertising:** $81.5 billion in Q4, up 14% YoY
- **Net income:** $35.0 billion in Q4, up 33% YoY
- **2026 capex guidance:** $175–$185 billion, primarily for AI infrastructure
- **Motley Fool assessment:** Gemini hasn't delivered "checkmate" to ChatGPT, but "along with other competitors, it makes the path forward that much more difficult" for OpenAI

**Why It Matters:**
The Gemini-ChatGPT gap has narrowed from a massive gulf to just ~50 million users. Google's combination of distribution advantages (Android, Search, Gmail, YouTube) and $175B+ in planned AI capex makes it the most formidable long-term competitor to OpenAI. The question is no longer whether Google can compete in AI — it's whether OpenAI can maintain its lead.
