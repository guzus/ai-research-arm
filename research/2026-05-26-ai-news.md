The May 25 report introduced *Magnifica Humanitas* and the Olah-at-the-Vatican event, but did not have the full document's text. With the **235-page booklet** now circulating, the May 25–26 coverage surfaces specifics that materially sharpen the political read:

- **AI and warfare:** AI use in war must be subject to **"the most rigorous ethical constraints."** Leo declares the traditional **"just war" theory** is **"now outdated"** — military force can only be used for **"self-defense in the strictest sense."** The encyclical is, in effect, a **theological argument against autonomous lethal AI**.
- **Concentration of power:** **"Control of AI must not remain in the hands of a few."** Leo's most robust critique targets the **concentration of power among a technocratic class of AI developers** — a direct challenge to the trillion-dollar-valuation frontier-lab ecosystem the encyclical is being launched into.
- **Developers' responsibility:** AI developers **"bear a particular ethical and spiritual responsibility, for every design choice reflects a vision of humanity."**
- **Risks to humanity:** AI risks **deepening inequality, weakening human relationships, and concentrating power unless guided by ethical oversight**.
- **Anthropic remarks:** **Chris Olah** delivered remarks at the Vatican (Anthropic published the text on its site), endorsing the encyclical's framing of the "anthropological" stakes.

**Read-through:** The "just war is outdated" line is the most operationally consequential passage — it lands during an **active US–China race to ship AI for military and surveillance use**, against the backdrop of Pentagon contract awards covered in earlier reports, and **directly challenges the doctrinal cover** that Western militaries lean on for AI-augmented force. Combined with the **"control must not be in the hands of a few"** framing, the encyclical is **not just a values document** — it is a **norm-shaping intervention squarely aimed at the trillion-dollar valuation tier** that this week's OpenAI S‑1 filing and Anthropic closing round embody.
