**Date:** Wednesday, May 6, 2026 | **Sources:** [CNBC — Nvidia to invest up to $3.2 billion in Corning as part of massive optical fiber deal with 3 new factories focused on AI](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/06/nvidia-corning-optical-factories-nc-texas-ai.html), [The Information — Nvidia Inks Deal To Invest Up To $3.2 Billion in Corning](https://www.theinformation.com/briefings/nvidia-inks-deal-invest-3-2-billion-corning), [Tom's Hardware — Nvidia invests $300 million in Corning to build three new US-based optical fiber plants](https://www.tomshardware.com/tech-industry/artificial-intelligence/nvidia-invests-usd300-million-in-corning-to-build-three-new-us-based-optical-fiber-plants-ai-infrastructure-deal-would-boost-fiber-production-capacity-by-over-50-percent), [TechSpot — Nvidia partners with Corning to build the fiber optic backbone for next-gen AI data centers](https://www.techspot.com/news/112320-nvidia-enters-multi-year-partnership-corning-supply-next.html), [TipRanks — Nvidia (NVDA) Looks Beyond Chips as Corning (GLW) Deal Targets AI's Next Big Bottleneck](https://www.tipranks.com/news/nvidia-nvda-looks-beyond-chips-as-corning-glw-deal-targets-ais-next-big-bottleneck), [Yahoo Finance — Nvidia, Corning partner on 3 U.S. fiber optic factories for AI](https://finance.yahoo.com/sectors/technology/articles/nvidia-corning-partner-3-u-121629850.html), [Claypier — NVIDIA, Corning Forge Long-Term Pact to 10x US Optical Capacity, Add 3,000 Jobs](https://claypier.com/en/nvidia-corning-us-optical-partnership/)

**NVIDIA and Corning announced a long-term strategic partnership** under which Nvidia will receive **warrants to invest up to $3.2 billion** in Corning — including an **immediate ~$300 million tranche** plus **warrants on up to 15 million Corning shares at a $180 strike price**. The deal funds **three new U.S. optical-fiber plants in North Carolina and Texas**, will **add at least 3,000 jobs**, and will **boost Corning's U.S. optical manufacturing capacity by ~10×**.

- **Industrial logic:** Nvidia is positioning to **replace copper with Corning's optical glass fibers in its rack-scale AI systems** — a transition known as **co-packaged optics (CPO)**. The factory output is **explicitly aligned with Vera Rubin** (the Hopper / Blackwell successor due late-2026 / 2027) **and Rubin Ultra** (2028 platform). Co-packaged optics are widely expected to be the **rack-interconnect bottleneck** at the **GW data-center scale Meta, Microsoft, Google, AWS, Anthropic, and OpenAI are all targeting through 2028**.
- **Market reaction:** Corning shares **closed +12%** on the news; Nvidia closed **+5.8%** on the same trading session — the rally rippled through the **optical-networking complex** (Coherent +6%, Lumentum +5%, Astera Labs +7%) and the **U.S. fiber-supply chain**.
- **Policy alignment:** The deal lands during the same week as the **CAISI pre-release-vetting MOUs** (May 5) and is being framed by Nvidia and Corning as **U.S.-onshore AI-infrastructure manufacturing** — extending the **Trump AI Action Plan reshoring narrative** beyond compute (TSMC Arizona, Samsung Taylor) into **photonics**.

**Read-through:** Three implications. (1) **Co-packaged optics is now the public 2026–2028 Nvidia hardware roadmap** — investors who had treated CPO as a 2027–2028 "maybe" should now treat it as a **commercial 2027 product** with a multi-year U.S. supply chain backing. (2) **For the "AI-power-and-interconnect" narrative**, the Corning deal **validates the bottleneck thesis** — Nvidia is willing to commit **$3.2B of warrants to a single optical-fiber supplier**, which is a stronger signal than earnings-call commentary alone. (3) **For Cisco / Arista / Juniper / Broadcom**, the announcement **structurally reframes the rack-scale interconnect competitive set** — Nvidia is now **vertically integrated through Corning** in a way that the merchant-silicon and merchant-switch incumbents are not, and the **Cisco-OpenAI rack deal** narrative looks more defensive than it did 24 hours ago.

**Bloomberg's Mark Gurman reported** that Apple is building an **"Extensions" system into iOS 27, macOS 27, and iPadOS 27** — Apple's fall-2026 OS release wave — that will let users **swap third-party AI models** as the engine behind Apple Intelligence features including **Siri, Writing Tools, and Image Playground**. The report names **Anthropic's Claude, Google's Gemini, and OpenAI's ChatGPT** as confirmed initial partners, alongside the existing ChatGPT integration that has been live since iOS 18. A new **App Store section** will surface compatible AI apps, and users will set their default model per-feature in **Settings**.

- **Strategic frame:** This is the **most significant change to Apple's AI strategy** since Apple Intelligence was announced at WWDC 2024. Apple's prior posture treated Apple Intelligence as a **closed Apple-models-plus-ChatGPT system**; the Extensions system **explicitly turns iOS / macOS / iPadOS into a multi-model AI platform** with Apple as the model-selection layer. The reported pairing — **Claude for writing / reasoning, Gemini for multimodal / image, ChatGPT for general** — mirrors how power users already split workloads.
- **China angle (TechNode):** TechNode highlights that the Extensions system **could resolve Apple's long-standing China-AI dilemma**: in mainland China, Apple Intelligence has been largely non-functional because **OpenAI / Google / Anthropic models are blocked**. The Extensions architecture **could let Apple plug in Baidu Ernie, Alibaba Qwen, or DeepSeek as a domestic-China extension**, satisfying Cyberspace Administration of China requirements while preserving the global Extensions UX.
- **Distribution implication:** Apple has **~2.35 billion active devices** (Q1 2026 disclosure). An Extensions system that **defaults to Apple's first-party models but allows swap-in to Claude, Gemini, or ChatGPT** is **the largest single distribution mechanism Anthropic, Google, or OpenAI has access to** outside their own apps — and **structurally the most consequential AI-distribution policy decision since OpenAI's original ChatGPT integration**.

**Read-through:** Three implications. (1) **For Anthropic**, this is an **enormous distribution win** — Claude has historically been **distribution-disadvantaged vs. ChatGPT** (no first-party iOS-default integration); Extensions effectively **catches Anthropic up to OpenAI's iOS distribution baseline**. (2) **For Google**, Extensions is a **complement to the Apple-Google Gemini foundation-model deal** (carried from prior reports — Apple Foundation Models reportedly built on Gemini). (3) **For Apple's antitrust posture**, opening Apple Intelligence to multiple AI providers is a **defensive move** ahead of likely DOJ / EU scrutiny of "Apple Intelligence vs. ChatGPT" exclusivity — and **largely echoes the Apple browser / search defaults model** of allowing user choice while collecting referral economics.

### **Google AI Search Adds "Expert Advice" Section — AI Overviews and AI Mode Now Surface Reddit, Quora, Stack Overflow, Forum, Blog, and Subscriber Content Inline; "Subscribed" Label Surfaces News-Subscription Sources First**

**Date:** Wednesday, May 6, 2026 | **Sources:** [TechCrunch — Google updates AI search to include quotes from Reddit and other sources](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/06/google-updates-ai-search-to-include-expert-advice-from-reddit-and-other-web-forums/), [Engadget — Google's AI search results will now turn to Reddit for expert advice](https://www.engadget.com/2166393/google-ai-search-results-will-now-turn-to-reddit-for-expert-advice/), [The Tech Portal — Google adds 'expert advice' from Reddit and online forums to its AI-powered search experience](https://thetechportal.com/2026/05/06/google-adds-expert-advice-from-reddit-and-online-forums-to-its-ai-powered-search-experience), [Slashdot — Google's AI Search Results Will Now Turn To Reddit For 'Expert Advice'](https://tech.slashdot.org/story/26/05/06/2050205/googles-ai-search-results-will-now-turn-to-reddit-for-expert-advice), [MacRumors — Google Search AI Mode Gets 'Expert Advice' From Reddit and Social Media](https://www.macrumors.com/2026/05/06/google-search-ai-mode-expert-advice/), [TechBooky — Google's AI Search Now Surfaces 'Expert Advice' From Reddit and Other Forums](https://www.techbooky.com/google-ai-search-surfaces-reddit-expert-advice/)

Google announced an update to **AI Overviews and AI Mode** that adds a labeled **"Expert Advice"** section (also surfaces as **"Community Perspectives"** depending on query) — pulling quoted excerpts from **Reddit threads, Quora answers, Stack Overflow posts, blogs, and other public forums** directly inline. Quoted content is attributed by **creator name, handle, and community**. The update also adds:

- A new **"Further Exploration"** section suggesting follow-up queries the AI thinks are relevant.
- A **"Subscribed"** label that **surfaces results from news outlets the user already subscribes to first**, ahead of the broader open-web result set.

**Why it matters:** This is **Google's most direct concession yet** to the long-observed user behavior of **appending "reddit" to queries to escape AI-Overview-style summaries** and reach human, lived-experience answers. By **internalizing Reddit / forum content into AI Overviews** rather than competing with it, Google is **removing the most common workaround pattern** users have developed against AI search. Google has had **a $60M / year Reddit data-licensing deal in place since Feb 2024**, which underpins the Expert Advice feature. The "Subscribed" label is a **publisher-friendly move** — news publishers have been the loudest critics of AI Overviews for cratering referral traffic.

**Read-through:** Three implications. (1) **For Reddit**, this is **structurally reinforcing** — the more deeply Reddit content is integrated into Google AI search, the **harder it becomes for any LLM-search competitor (ChatGPT-Search, Perplexity, You.com) to match the human-substrate quality** without their own Reddit licensing deal. (2) **For Perplexity**, the Expert Advice feature **partially closes the moat** Perplexity has historically claimed around "more human, less AI-summarized search." (3) **For news publishers**, the Subscribed label is **an incremental positive** but does not address the **fundamental zero-click-search referral-traffic collapse** that has dominated publisher-vs.-Google AI policy through 2025–26.

**Foxconn (Hon Hai)** posted a **record April month at NT$832.1B** and **Q1 2026 revenue of NT$2.13T (~$66.6B)**, **up 29.7% YoY**, on the back of **AI server rack demand**. For the **first time in the company's history**, the **Cloud & Networking segment (which assembles GB200 NVL72 + H100/H200/B200 racks for hyperscalers) hit 40% of total revenue, surpassing the Consumer Electronics segment (38%, including iPhone)**. Foxconn assembles **~40% of the global AI server rack market**.

**Read-through:** Foxconn's first quarter ever where **AI servers > iPhones by revenue share** is the **single cleanest empirical datapoint of the 2026 AI capex cycle** — and an **even stronger refutation of the "AI infrastructure spend is peaking" thesis** than the **AMD blowout (May 5)** that opened the AI-infrastructure-print sequence. The Q2 2026 outlook continues to project **AI-server-rack-driven growth**, structurally validating the **NVIDIA Q1 fiscal '27 print expected May 20** and the **Anthropic / OpenAI / Meta multi-year multi-GW capex programs**.
