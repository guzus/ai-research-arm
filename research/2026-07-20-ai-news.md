### Moonshot AI Plans Hong Kong IPO Within Six Months at a ~$30 Billion Valuation, Riding the Kimi K3 Shock

**Date:** July 19, 2026 | **Sources:** [Bloomberg — Moonshot Plans IPO in Six Months After China AI Breakthrough](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-19/china-s-moonshot-plans-ipo-in-six-months-after-ai-breakthrough), [Japan Times — China's Moonshot plans IPO in six months after AI breakthrough](https://www.japantimes.co.jp/business/2026/07/19/tech/china-moonshot-ai-ipo/), [Yahoo Finance — Moonshot AI Plans Hong Kong IPO After Kimi K3 Model Debut](https://finance.yahoo.com/markets/stocks/articles/moonshot-ai-plans-hong-kong-123000193.html), [TheNextWeb — Moonshot plans to list in Hong Kong within six months at a $30 billion valuation](https://thenextweb.com/news/moonshot-ai-ipo-six-months-30-billion-kimi-k3), [Taipei Times — China's Moonshot plans IPO within 6 months](https://www.taipeitimes.com/News/biz/archives/2026/07/20/2003861024)
- Beijing-based Moonshot AI, maker of the Kimi chatbot, has circulated a shareholder resolution seeking investor approval to list on the Hong Kong Stock Exchange within six months, while simultaneously finalizing a funding round that could value the company at more than $30 billion — nearly sevenfold its $4.3 billion valuation last December.
- The filing follows the July 16 release of the open-weight Kimi K3 model (~2.8 trillion parameters), which Moonshot says performs on par with leading systems from OpenAI and Anthropic and briefly rattled global tech markets; ARR reportedly hit $300 million in June, up from $200 million in April. CICC and Goldman Sachs are in talks to underwrite the offering, and Moonshot is unwinding its offshore VIE structure in favor of a mainland-linked joint-venture model to qualify for the listing.
- **Why it matters:** It's the clearest sign yet that a Chinese open-weight lab is moving to convert a technical breakthrough directly into public-market capital, and it names the specific model (Kimi K3) that market reporting ties to last week's ~10% weekly slide in the Philadelphia Semiconductor Index — investors pricing in the possibility that cheaper, competitive open models could dent AI infrastructure capex.

### Anthropic Restructures Claude Fable 5 Access: Free on Max/Team Premium at 50% Limits, Usage-Credit Pricing for Pro

**Date:** July 18-20, 2026 | **Sources:** [Anthropic — Redeploying Claude Fable 5](https://www.anthropic.com/news/redeploying-fable-5), [The Decoder — Anthropic slashes Claude Fable 5 limits in Max and Team Premium and pushes Pro users toward API pricing](https://the-decoder.com/anthropic-slashes-claude-fable-5-limits-in-max-and-team-premium-and-pushes-pro-users-toward-api-pricing/), [Tech Times — Claude Fable 5 Ends Subscription Limbo: Permanent for Max, Credits-Only for Pro](https://www.techtimes.com/articles/320905/20260718/claude-fable-5-ends-subscription-limbo-permanent-max-credits-only-pro.htm)
- Effective today, July 20, Claude Fable 5 becomes a permanent (not extended-trial) part of Max and Team Premium plans, but capped at 50% of weekly usage limits; Pro and Team Standard subscribers instead get Fable 5 only through prepaid usage credits ($10/million input tokens, $50/million output tokens) plus a one-time $100 credit, rather than through their subscription's weekly pool.
- This closes out a saga of three deadline extensions (originally set for July 7, pushed to July 12, then July 19) for Fable 5's move off free/included access; Anthropic cited unpredictable demand and staged compute availability as the reason for the repeated delays, and the move follows OpenAI's July 9 GPT-5.6 Sol launch at roughly half Fable 5's per-token rates.
- **Why it matters:** It's a concrete look at how compute scarcity is forcing even a well-capitalized frontier lab to ration its flagship consumer model tier-by-tier rather than simply eating the cost, arriving in the same week Anthropic was separately reported to be in talks to lease up to $10B in compute from Meta (prior report).

### Nonprofit Current AI Secures $400M in Commitments to Build "Public Interest" AI Infrastructure

**Date:** July 19, 2026 | **Source:** [TechCrunch — Nonprofit Current AI is racing to build the World Wide Web of AI, free for all](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/19/nonprofit-current-ai-is-racing-to-build-the-world-wide-web-of-ai-free-for-all/)
- Current AI, the nonprofit led by CEO Ayah Bdeir (former Mozilla AI strategy lead, littleBits founder), has locked in $400 million in committed funding — including $100 million from the French government plus contributions from the Ford Foundation, MacArthur Foundation, DeepMind, and Salesforce — toward a stated goal of mobilizing $2.5 billion over five years for open, public AI infrastructure.
- The organization's near-term work includes Alpha Chat, an open-source chatbot introduced at the AI for Good Summit in Geneva, and a partnership with India's Bhashini language-AI initiative on Suno Sutra, an offline device running AI in 22 Indian languages.
- **Why it matters:** It's a concrete, dollar-figure counterpoint to the private-capital-dominated AI funding narrative (Moonshot's ~$30B IPO push, Fireworks' $1.5B round, both covered in prior reports) — a coalition of governments and foundations explicitly positioning public/nonprofit AI infrastructure as a check on Big Tech and frontier-lab concentration.
