### Pentagon Official Confirms Grok AI Was Used to Direct 2,000+ Munitions in Iran Strikes

**Date:** July 15, 2026 | **Sources:** [The Hill — Elon Musk's Grok chatbot used to launch thousands of missiles at Iran: Pentagon AI chief](https://thehill.com/policy/technology/5928204-pentagon-musk-grok-chatbot-iran-strikes/), [Futurism — The US Military Has Been Using Elon Musk's Grok AI to Bomb Iran](https://futurism.com/artificial-intelligence/us-military-elon-musk-grok-bomb-iran), [Common Dreams — Pentagon Official Admits Musk AI Tool Grok Was Used to Help Fire '2,000 Munitions' at Iran](https://www.commondreams.org/news/us-military-grok-iran)
- Cameron Stanley, the Department of Defense's chief digital and artificial intelligence officer, disclosed in sworn court testimony that Grok "enabled U.S. forces to deploy over 2,000 munitions to 2,000 distinct targets within 96 hours" during "Operation Epic Fury," marking the first explicit acknowledgment by a senior U.S. official that the military used a commercial AI chatbot in live strikes on Iran.
- The disclosure surfaced in a Mississippi lawsuit over pollution from gas turbines at xAI's Colossus 2 data center; the Justice Department is seeking to have the case narrowed, calling the facility "vital" to national security missions, and Stanley's filing says Grok also supports military planning, logistics analytics, and red-teaming of adversary positioning.
- **Why it matters:** It's the most concrete public confirmation yet that a frontier consumer chatbot has been embedded directly into U.S. combat operations, intensifying an ongoing congressional fight — including Sen. Kirsten Gillibrand's bill to ban unsupervised LLM use in force and detention decisions.

### Daniel Ek's Neko Health Raises $700M Series C, Nears $7B Valuation Ahead of US Launch

**Date:** July 15, 2026 | **Sources:** [TechCrunch — Daniel Ek's body-scanning startup Neko Health raises another $700M](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/15/daniel-eks-body-scanning-startup-neko-health-raises-another-700m/), [Tech.eu — Neko Health raises $700M as demand grows for preventive health scans](https://tech.eu/2026/07/15/neko-health-raises-700m-as-demand-grows-for-preventive-health-scans/), [Neko Health — Neko Health raises $700m Series C ahead of US launch](https://www.nekohealth.com/gb/en/press/neko-health-raises-usd700m-series-c-ahead-of-us-launch)
- Neko Health, the Spotify founder's AI- and sensor-driven full-body health-scanning startup, raised a **$700 million Series C** co-led by Lightspeed Venture Partners and O.G. Venture Partners, with General Catalyst, Atomico, and new investors Liberty City Ventures and BDT & MSD also participating.
- The round pushes Neko's valuation to nearly **$7 billion**, up from $1.7 billion in early 2025, and will fund its US expansion (starting with New York) alongside continued buildout of its 60-minute scan platform that analyzes metabolic health, stroke/heart-attack risk factors, and pre-diabetes biomarkers.
- **Why it matters:** It's one of the largest health-tech raises of the year and shows investor appetite for AI-driven preventive diagnostics remains strong even outside core foundation-model and infrastructure plays.

## Industry Moves

### 26 Meta Employees Sue Over Alleged Use of AI to Target Disabled and On-Leave Workers for Layoffs

**Date:** July 14-15, 2026 | **Sources:** [Fortune — 26 Meta employees accuse Mark Zuckerberg of using AI to target 8,000 layoffs against workers on medical, parental or family leave](https://fortune.com/2026/07/15/meta-workers-sue-over-ai-layoff-math/), [U.S. News — Meta Used AI to Target Workers With Medical Conditions for Layoffs, Lawsuit Claims](https://www.usnews.com/news/top-news/articles/2026-07-14/meta-used-ai-to-target-workers-with-medical-conditions-for-layoffs-former-employees-lawsuit-claims), [HCAMag — Meta sued for AI-driven layoffs targeting employees on protected leave](https://www.hcamag.com/ca/news/general/meta-sued-for-ai-driven-layoffs-targeting-employees-on-protected-leave/582405)
- 26 current Meta employees filed suit in federal court in Oakland, California, alleging the company used internal AI tools — including the "Metamate" LLM assistant, an employee-tracking "second brain," and keystroke/screen-monitoring productivity scores — to rank and select workers for mass layoffs in a way that systematically disadvantaged people on medical, parental, or family leave (separations begin July 22).
- The suit alleges violations of the ADA, FMLA, and Pregnancy Discrimination Act, plus California/NYC AI-bias testing laws; Meta says "workforce management and organizational decisions were and are made by people, not AI."
- **Why it matters:** It's reported as the first lawsuit against a major U.S. employer specifically challenging AI-driven layoff selection, a test case for how algorithmic HR tools hold up under disability and leave-protection law.

### xAI Sues Grok User Over Alleged CSAM Deepfakes, an Early AI-Company-vs-User Legal Precedent

**Date:** July 15, 2026 | **Sources:** [Mezha — xAI sues South Carolina man in Texas federal court over Grok sexual deepfakes](https://mezha.net/eng/bukvy/14a569a7_xai_sues_south/), [The News — Elon Musk's xAI sues Grok user over alleged sexualized 'deepfakes'](https://www.thenews.com.pk/latest/1409246-elon-musks-xai-sues-grok-user-over-alleged-sexualized-deepfakes)
- xAI filed suit in federal court in Texas against Terry Harwood, a South Carolina man already facing criminal charges for sexual exploitation of minors, alleging he used Grok to generate sexually explicit deepfakes of both adults and minors from uploaded non-sexual images; xAI is seeking damages and a permanent injunction barring him from the platform.
- **Why it matters:** It's one of the first instances of an AI company suing its own user over generated content, a notable legal strategy shift as Grok continues to face broader scrutiny (including separate lawsuits from deepfake victims) over its image-generation guardrails.
