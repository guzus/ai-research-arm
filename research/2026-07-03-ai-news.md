## Breaking News

### Meta Plans "Meta Compute" — A New Cloud Business to Resell Surplus AI Capacity

**Date:** July 1, 2026 | **Sources:** [Bloomberg — Meta Is Planning a Cloud Business to Sell AI Computing Power](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-01/meta-is-building-a-cloud-business-to-sell-excess-ai-compute), [CNBC — Meta pops 9% as company makes cloud push](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/01/meta-stock-cloud-ai-compute.html), [MLQ News — Meta Unveils 'Meta Compute'](https://mlq.ai/news/meta-unveils-meta-compute-cloud-business-to-sell-excess-ai-infrastructure-to-outside-customers/)

- **What's net-new:** Bloomberg reported Meta is building **"Meta Compute,"** a
  cloud unit to sell its surplus AI computing capacity to outside customers —
  positioning Meta as a new entrant against AWS, Azure, and Google Cloud, and
  against neoclouds like CoreWeave and Nebius. Meta is weighing two models:
  reselling raw GPU capacity (CoreWeave-style) or selling access to hosted AI
  models (AWS-style, higher-margin).
- **Leadership:** The effort is led by infrastructure chief **Santosh
  Janardhan**, **Daniel Gross** (Meta Superintelligence Labs), and Meta
  President **Dina Powell McCormick**.
- **Market reaction:** Meta shares rose **8.8%** on the news; CoreWeave and
  Nebius fell **10.8%** and **12.4%** respectively on fears Meta — a major
  customer of both — will cut its own neocloud spend while adding a
  competitor.
- **Why it matters:** With ~$130B in 2026 capex, Meta has built AI capacity
  well beyond its own model-training needs; monetizing the surplus turns a
  spending figure that spooked markets into a potential revenue line — the
  same "own the infrastructure economics" logic running through recent Base44,
  AWS FDE, and Venice AI moves.
- **Skeptic's note:** No pricing, launch timeline, or named customers have
  been disclosed. Meta would enter a mature, margin-thin market as the least-
  proven vendor, competing for workloads from rivals who may be wary of
  handing a social-media giant their AI infrastructure.

## New Model Releases

### Nvidia Releases Nemotron-Labs-TwoTower — an Open-Weight Diffusion LLM Bolted Onto a Frozen Autoregressive Backbone

**Date:** July 1, 2026 | **Sources:** [MarkTechPost — NVIDIA Releases Nemotron-Labs-TwoTower](https://www.marktechpost.com/2026/07/01/nvidia-releases-nemotron-labs-twotower/), [arXiv — Nemotron-Labs-TwoTower: Diffusion Language Modeling with Pretrained Autoregressive Context](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.26493), [Hugging Face — nvidia/Nemotron-Labs-TwoTower-30B-A3B-Base-BF16](https://huggingface.co/nvidia/Nemotron-Labs-TwoTower-30B-A3B-Base-BF16)

- **What's net-new:** Nvidia Research open-sourced **Nemotron-Labs-TwoTower**, a
  diffusion language model adapted from its existing **Nemotron-3-Nano-30B-A3B**
  hybrid (Mamba-2 + attention + MoE) backbone. Architecture splits into two
  towers: a **frozen autoregressive context tower** that causally processes
  clean tokens, and a **trainable diffusion denoiser tower** with bidirectional
  block attention that refines noisy token blocks — enabling parallel
  (multi-token) generation instead of one-token-at-a-time decoding.
- **The numbers:** **2.42x higher wall-clock throughput** than the AR baseline
  while retaining **98.7% of its benchmark quality**; the denoiser tower was
  trained on only **~2.1T tokens**, a fraction of the 25T-token backbone
  pretrain — avoiding a full re-pretrain. Weights + training code released
  under the NVIDIA Nemotron Open Model License (commercial use permitted).
- **Why it matters:** A concrete, reproducible recipe for converting an
  existing AR model into a faster diffusion-hybrid without redoing the
  expensive pretrain — relevant to the industry-wide push (DeepSeek, Google,
  Inception Labs) toward diffusion LLMs for inference-cost reduction.
- **Skeptic's note:** 30B-parameter scale only; no independent third-party
  benchmark yet, and the quality/speed tradeoff (98.7% retained) is Nvidia's
  own reported number.

- **Carry:** No frontier flagship LLM shipped in the July 1–3 window. Grok 4.5
  still private-beta; GPT-5.6 Sol/Terra/Luna in gov-approved preview; DeepSeek
  V4 mid-July GA; Sonnet 5 default everywhere; Fable 5 back globally (metered).

## Funding & Acquisitions

### Abu Dhabi's MGX Closes $49B AI Fund — Above Its $45B Target

**Date:** July 1, 2026 | **Sources:** [CNBC — OpenAI, Anthropic backer MGX raises one of the biggest AI funds ever at $49 billion](https://www.cnbc.com/amp/2026/07/01/mgx-ai-fund-uae-49-billion.html), [MGX — Final close of MGX Fund I at $49 billion](https://www.mgx.ae/news-insights/mgx-pleased-confirm-final-close-mgx-fund-i-49-billion-total-commitments-significantly), [The National — Abu Dhabi's AI investment firm MGX raises $49bn for new fund](https://www.thenationalnews.com/business/markets/2026/07/01/abu-dhabis-ai-investment-firm-mgx-raises-49bn-for-new-fund/)

- **What's net-new:** **MGX**, the Abu Dhabi state-backed AI investment
  vehicle chaired by **Sheikh Tahnoon bin Zayed Al Nahyan**, closed **Fund I**
  at **$49B in total commitments** — above its original **$45B** target —
  drawing institutional and private capital from the Gulf, North America,
  Asia, and Europe. One of the largest dedicated AI investment vehicles to
  date.
- **Portfolio:** Since inception the fund has invested in **14 companies**
  across the AI stack (semiconductors, infrastructure, platforms), including
  co-leading **Anthropic's $30B round (February)** and **$65B Series H (May)**,
  alongside **OpenAI's $122B raise (March)** and **xAI's $20B raise (January)**.
  MGX plans up to **$10B/year** in ongoing investment.
- **Why it matters:** Confirms Gulf sovereign capital remains one of the
  largest funding sources underwriting the frontier-lab buildout, running in
  parallel with the U.S. VC-driven rounds (Together AI, TwelveLabs) covered
  above.
- **Skeptic's note:** Fund-level "final close" announcements are self-reported
  by the manager; portfolio company valuations cited are as of each company's
  own prior raise, not marked-to-market today.

## Regulatory Updates

- **White House AI Executive Order's 30-day implementation deadline landed
  July 2, 2026.** Section 2 of the June 2 EO ("Promoting Advanced Artificial
  Intelligence Innovation and Security") required federal agencies to begin
  preparing government and private-sector systems for advanced AI tools
  within 30 days — that window closed yesterday. The EO's broader
  voluntary-review/benchmarking framework for frontier developers still has
  an **August 1** deliverable deadline. **Source:** [The White House — Promoting Advanced Artificial Intelligence Innovation and Security](https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/2026/06/promoting-advanced-artificial-intelligence-innovation-and-security/)

    new policy action — no agency compliance report has been published yet.
- No other net-new regulatory action surfaced. Standing carries: Colorado AI
  Act (effective June 30); China CAC Anthropomorphic Interaction measures

## Bottom Line

Model launches took a back seat today to **who owns the compute**. Meta's
disclosed plan to resell surplus AI capacity through **"Meta Compute"** is
the sharpest new data point yet that the infrastructure buildout is starting
to look for a return beyond internal model training — and it rattled
neocloud stocks (CoreWeave, Nebius) in the process. On the capital side,
**Together AI's $800M round at $8.3B**, **TwelveLabs' $100M raise**, and
**Abu Dhabi's MGX closing a $49B AI fund** (above its $45B target) underline
that money — both U.S. VC and Gulf sovereign — is still flowing hard into
model, video, and infrastructure plays alike. On the research side, **Nvidia
open-sourced Nemotron-Labs-TwoTower**, a diffusion-hybrid retrofit of its
30B Nemotron backbone claiming 2.42x faster generation at 98.7% of baseline
quality — a concrete data point in the industry's push toward cheaper
inference via diffusion decoding. Regulatory/macro movement was more sobering
than new-policy: the White House AI EO's first 30-day compliance window came
due, and the **BLS's June jobs report (57K added, weakest in 4 months)**
landed alongside Challenger data showing **AI is now the single most-cited
layoff reason for a 4th straight month** — a running reminder that the
infrastructure and funding boom sits next to real labor-market churn.
