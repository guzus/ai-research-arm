### Musk v. Altman — Jury Selection Begins Today (Monday, April 27, 2026) in Oakland; Opening Statements Tuesday; Brockman's Unsealed Diary Surfaces as Plaintiffs' Strongest Evidence
**Date:** April 27, 2026 (live as of this writing) | **Sources:** [NPR](https://www.npr.org/2026/04/27/nx-s1-5795661/trial-openai-elon-musk-sam-altman), [CNN](https://edition.cnn.com/2026/04/27/tech/elon-musk-sam-altman-openai-lawsuit), [Local 10 News](https://www.local10.com/news/2026/04/27/elon-musk-and-openai-ceo-sam-altman-head-to-court-in-high-stakes-showdown-over-ai/), [KPBS Public Media](https://www.kpbs.org/news/economy/2026/04/27/musk-vs-altman-tech-ceos-head-to-court-monday-over-fate-of-openai), [WVIK NPR](https://www.wvik.org/npr-top-stories/2026-04-27/musk-vs-altman-tech-ceos-head-to-court-monday-over-fate-of-openai), [Christian Science Monitor](https://www.csmonitor.com/USA/Society/2026/0427/musk-openai-chatgpt-altman?icid=rss), [KRON4](https://www.kron4.com/news/technology-ai/jury-selection-starts-in-musk-vs-openai-legal-battle/), [Local News Matters (April 26 preview)](https://localnewsmatters.org/2026/04/26/musk-altman-openai-trial-oakland/), [The Implicator](https://www.implicator.ai/musk-v-altman-trial-opens-monday-with-150-billion-and-openai-nonprofit-at-stake/), [TheNextWeb](https://thenextweb.com/news/musk-altman-openai-trial-credibility-nonprofit), [Yahoo Finance](https://finance.yahoo.com/sectors/technology/article/elon-musks-years-long-legal-battle-with-openai-and-sam-altman-will-finally-head-to-trial-on-monday-130000137.html), [SF Standard](https://sfstandard.com/2026/04/24/elon-openai-trial/), [IBTimes Australia](https://www.ibtimes.com.au/elon-musk-vs-sam-altman-trial-kicks-off-jury-selection-begins-high-stakes-openai-lawsuit-1867586)

The most-watched AI courtroom proceeding of 2026 begins this morning at the **U.S. District Court for the Northern District of California, Oakland Division**, with **Judge Yvonne Gonzalez Rogers** presiding. Reuters, CNN, and NPR all describe it as "the most important tech case of a generation."

- **Schedule:** **Jury selection today (Monday, April 27)**; **opening statements Tuesday April 28**. Trial expected to run **roughly four weeks**, with a verdict potentially by **mid-May**.
- **Jury composition:** **9 jurors with no alternates** (unusual for a 4-week civil trial; Judge Gonzalez Rogers has called a venire pool **roughly 3× the size of a typical civil case** to account for celebrity-bias screening of Musk and Altman).
- **Time allocation:** **~20 hours each** for Musk and OpenAI; **5 hours for Microsoft** as co-defendant.
- **Witness list (confirmed):** **Elon Musk, Sam Altman, Greg Brockman, Microsoft CEO Satya Nadella**. Musk himself **could take the stand as early as Tuesday**.
- **Surviving claims (per April 24 narrowing):** **(1) breach of charitable trust; (2) unjust enrichment.** All **26 fraud claims dropped** at Musk's request April 24. Punitive damages barred; trial proceeds in two phases (advisory jury verdict → bench liability/disgorgement decision by Judge Gonzalez Rogers).
- **Damages frame:** Reuters / CNBC / TheNextWeb peg the relief sought at **~$150B** (originally $134B), with proceeds directed to **OpenAI's charitable arm** if Musk prevails.

**New evidence (not in prior reports) — Brockman's unsealed diary.** The plaintiffs' strongest piece of evidence, now publicly unsealed and admitted, is **OpenAI President Greg Brockman's private journal**. Two entries are expected to be presented to the jury:

> *"I cannot believe that we committed to non-profit if three months later we're doing b-corp then it was a lie."*
>
> *"It'd be wrong to steal the non-profit from Musk, to convert to a b-corp without him. That'd be pretty morally bankrupt."*

The **second** step — converting the for-profit arm without Musk — was carried out subsequently, which is precisely the act Musk's team will argue establishes both the breach-of-charitable-trust and unjust-enrichment claims.

**Read-through:** the case has narrowed from a sprawling fraud action to a **trust-law dispute over the 2015 nonprofit's charitable purpose**, judged by an Obama-appointee bench judge sitting in equity. The diary entries, surfacing on the eve of trial, replace what the **dropped fraud claims** were meant to show: contemporaneous evidence of intent. Combined with **Microsoft as co-defendant**, **Nadella on the witness list**, and **Anthropic's $40B Google round** providing the contemporary backdrop, the trial frames the most consequential question of 2026 enterprise AI: whether OpenAI's for-profit conversion can survive scrutiny under California charitable-trust law — and what disgorgement, if any, follows. Verdict scenarios materially affect Microsoft's $13B+ exposure, OpenAI's $852B post-money valuation, and the broader frontier-lab nonprofit-to-PBC playbook.

### OpenAI's Sam Altman Issues Public Apology to Tumbler Ridge, B.C. — OpenAI Reviewed but Failed to Report Shooter's ChatGPT Account in June 2025
**Date:** Apology letter delivered April 24–25, 2026 | **Sources:** [CNN](https://www.cnn.com/2026/04/24/world/sam-altman-openai-apologize-tumbler-ridge), [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/04/25/openai-ceo-apologizes-to-tumbler-ridge-community/), [CBC News (apology letter)](https://www.cbc.ca/news/canada/british-columbia/sam-altman-tumbler-ridge-apology-9.7176482), [CBC Player (video)](https://www.cbc.ca/player/play/video/9.7176800), [Global News](https://globalnews.ca/news/11816272/openai-ceo-apologizes-tumbler-ridge-not-alerting-police-shooters-account/), [Seattle Times](https://www.seattletimes.com/business/altman-apologizes-after-openai-failed-to-alert-police-before-tumbler-ridge-killings/), [Al Jazeera](https://www.aljazeera.com/economy/2026/4/25/chkopenaissamaltmanapologises-over-failure-to-report-canadian-mass-shooter), [Engadget](https://www.engadget.com/ai/openais-sam-altman-apologizes-for-not-reporting-chatgpt-account-of-tumbler-ridge-suspect-to-police-221400813.html), [The Hill](https://thehill.com/policy/technology/5848734-sam-altman-chatgpt-canada-shooting-messages/), [TheNextWeb](https://thenextweb.com/news/sam-altman-openai-apology-tumbler-ridge-shooting), [Wikipedia — 2026 Tumbler Ridge shooting](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2026_Tumbler_Ridge_shooting)

**OpenAI CEO Sam Altman** sent a **public letter of apology** to the community of **Tumbler Ridge, British Columbia**, where on **February 10, 2026** the perpetrator killed her mother, half-brother, and **six children at Tumbler Ridge Secondary School**, injuring twenty-seven others before taking her own life — **the deadliest school shooting in Canada since the 1989 École Polytechnique massacre**.

Key admissions in the letter and supporting reporting:

- **June 2025 review:** Approximately **a dozen OpenAI employees** reviewed the shooter's ChatGPT account in **June 2025** after it was flagged for **"violent" activity**. **Some recommended reporting to law enforcement**; **leadership overruled them**, applying a **"higher threshold"** that the conversations did not meet.
- **Account banned, RCMP not notified.** The account was disabled, but **no notification was sent** to Canadian law enforcement, the **RCMP**, or any U.S. agency. Altman's letter: *"I am deeply sorry that we did not alert law enforcement to the account that was banned in June."*
- **Political response:** **B.C. Premier David Eby** called the apology *"necessary, but grossly insufficient."*
- **Policy implications:** the case is now the **leading real-world test** of frontier-lab voluntary-reporting obligations, and lands during the **state-by-state AI legislative sprint** (1,561 bills introduced across 45 states; **NY RAISE Act effective March 19, 2026**; **Idaho Conversational AI Safety Act effective July 1**).

**Read-through:** the most consequential **child-safety / law-enforcement reporting failure** disclosed by a frontier lab in 2026, and a direct trust-and-safety counterweight to OpenAI's enterprise expansion (TBPN media acquisition, $122B funding round, Codex enterprise rollout). The disclosure that **leadership overruled employees who explicitly recommended reporting** is the part most likely to surface in (a) **Connecticut SB 5** rulemaking; (b) **EU AI Act Article 50 transparency rules** (live August 2, 2026 — T-98 days); (c) the **California state AG** review of charitable-trust governance; and (d) Sam Altman's **own testimony posture** in the trial that opens tomorrow. The Tumbler Ridge disclosure is the **single most damaging factual admission** OpenAI has made in 2026, and the timing — **48 hours before Altman's federal-court trial** — is unlikely to be coincidental from his counsel's perspective.

**Date:** Cable dated **Friday April 24, 2026**; broadly reported April 24–25 | **Sources:** [CNBC / Reuters](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/25/us-global-warning-alleged-china-ai-theft.html), [Tom's Hardware](https://www.tomshardware.com/tech-industry/artificial-intelligence/deepseek-launches-1-6-trillion-parameter-v4-on-huawei-chips-as-us-escalates-ai-theft-accusations), [Rappler](https://www.rappler.com/technology/us-state-department-orders-global-warning-ai-theft-deepseek-chinese-firms/), [U.S. News & World Report (Reuters)](https://money.usnews.com/investing/news/articles/2026-04-24/exclusive-us-state-dept-orders-global-warning-about-alleged-china-ai-thefts-by-deepseek-others), [Cybernews](https://cybernews.com/ai-news/us-warns-chinese-ai-firms-may-be-copying-american-models-through-distillation/), [KFGO Reuters wire](https://kfgo.com/2026/04/24/exclusive-us-state-dept-orders-global-warning-about-alleged-china-ai-thefts-by-deepseek-others/)

A **State Department cable** dated **Friday April 24** was sent to **diplomatic and consular posts worldwide** instructing diplomatic staff to raise with foreign counterparts *"concerns over adversaries' extraction and distillation of U.S. A.I. models."* The cable specifically named **DeepSeek, Moonshot AI, and MiniMax**.

- **Purpose (per cable text):** *"warn of the risks of utilizing AI models distilled from U.S. proprietary AI models, and lay the groundwork for potential follow-up and outreach by the U.S."*
- **Backdrop:** The cable is the diplomatic-channel companion to the **Frontier Model Forum's April 6 joint OpenAI/Anthropic/Google statement** alleging **24,000 fraudulent accounts** and **16M Claude exchanges** harvested by these three Chinese labs for adversarial distillation, and aligns with **OpenAI's prior congressional warnings**.
- **Chinese response:** **Chinese Embassy in Washington** issued a **flat denial** — *"groundless... deliberate attacks on China's development."*
- **Operational link to DeepSeek V4:** the cable was issued the **same day** DeepSeek launched its **V4 preview adapted for Huawei Ascend chips** (1.6T total / 49B active MoE), tying the diplomatic warning to a concrete model release.

**Read-through:** the cable is the **first formal diplomatic-channel U.S. government action** targeting Chinese frontier labs by name, and converts a previously industry-led complaint into an **active U.S. foreign-policy posture**. Combined with **Five Eyes onboarding for OpenAI GPT-5.4-Cyber** (April 22) and **Microsoft–Mythos SDL integration** (April 23), the West-vs-China model-IP frame now has all three legs: **industry coalition (Frontier Model Forum), defensive deployment (GPT-5.4-Cyber), and diplomatic outreach (State cable)** — converging on a coherent U.S./allied policy stack heading into the **August 2 EU AI Act** full-applicability date.

## Regulatory Updates

### Trial-Backed State-AG Posture: California Charitable-Trust Statute Becomes the De Facto AI-Governance Lever
**Status:** Live in court today. | **Sources:** see Musk v. Altman section above

The two surviving claims in **Musk v. Altman** — **breach of charitable trust** and **unjust enrichment** — center on **California Corporations Code §§ 5230, 5233, and 5237** (charitable-trust duties of nonprofit directors). With Judge Gonzalez Rogers handling **Phase 2 (liability + disgorgement) under bench equity jurisdiction**, the case will produce **the first federal-court decision on whether a nonprofit-to-PBC AI conversion violates charitable-trust duties** — applicable far beyond OpenAI to **Anthropic** (PBC since 2021) and to any future restructurings by **xAI, Meta Superintelligence Labs, Mistral**, or Cohere–Aleph Alpha. Material verdict scenarios include **partial or full disgorgement** of OpenAI for-profit equity to OpenAI Inc. (the 501(c)(3) parent), which would directly affect **Microsoft's stake** and **OpenAI's enterprise contracts**.
