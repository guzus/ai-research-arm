The most-watched AI courtroom proceeding of 2026 begins this morning at the **U.S. District Court for the Northern District of California, Oakland Division**, with **Judge Yvonne Gonzalez Rogers** presiding. Reuters, CNN, and NPR all describe it as "the most important tech case of a generation."

- **Schedule:** **Jury selection today (Monday, April 27)**; **opening statements Tuesday April 28**. Trial expected to run **roughly four weeks**, with a verdict potentially by **mid-May**.
- **Jury composition:** **9 jurors with no alternates** (unusual for a 4-week civil trial; Judge Gonzalez Rogers has called a venire pool **roughly 3× the size of a typical civil case** to account for celebrity-bias screening of Musk and Altman).
- **Time allocation:** **~20 hours each** for Musk and OpenAI; **5 hours for Microsoft** as co-defendant.
- **Witness list (confirmed):** **Elon Musk, Sam Altman, Greg Brockman, Microsoft CEO Satya Nadella**. Musk himself **could take the stand as early as Tuesday**.
- **Surviving claims (per April 24 narrowing):** **(1) breach of charitable trust; (2) unjust enrichment.** All **26 fraud claims dropped** at Musk's request April 24. Punitive damages barred; trial proceeds in two phases (advisory jury verdict → bench liability/disgorgement decision by Judge Gonzalez Rogers).
- **Damages frame:** Reuters / CNBC / TheNextWeb peg the relief sought at **~$150B** (originally $134B), with proceeds directed to **OpenAI's charitable arm** if Musk prevails.

**New evidence (not in prior reports) — Brockman's unsealed diary.** The plaintiffs' strongest piece of evidence, now publicly unsealed and admitted, is **OpenAI President Greg Brockman's private journal**. Two entries are expected to be presented to the jury:

> *"I cannot believe that we committed to non-profit if three months later we're doing b-corp then it was a lie."*
>
> *"It'd be wrong to steal the non-profit from Musk, to convert to a b-corp without him. That'd be pretty morally bankrupt."*

The **second** step — converting the for-profit arm without Musk — was carried out subsequently, which is precisely the act Musk's team will argue establishes both the breach-of-charitable-trust and unjust-enrichment claims.

**Read-through:** the case has narrowed from a sprawling fraud action to a **trust-law dispute over the 2015 nonprofit's charitable purpose**, judged by an Obama-appointee bench judge sitting in equity. The diary entries, surfacing on the eve of trial, replace what the **dropped fraud claims** were meant to show: contemporaneous evidence of intent. Combined with **Microsoft as co-defendant**, **Nadella on the witness list**, and **Anthropic's $40B Google round** providing the contemporary backdrop, the trial frames the most consequential question of 2026 enterprise AI: whether OpenAI's for-profit conversion can survive scrutiny under California charitable-trust law — and what disgorgement, if any, follows. Verdict scenarios materially affect Microsoft's $13B+ exposure, OpenAI's $852B post-money valuation, and the broader frontier-lab nonprofit-to-PBC playbook.

Key admissions in the letter and supporting reporting:

- **June 2025 review:** Approximately **a dozen OpenAI employees** reviewed the shooter's ChatGPT account in **June 2025** after it was flagged for **"violent" activity**. **Some recommended reporting to law enforcement**; **leadership overruled them**, applying a **"higher threshold"** that the conversations did not meet.

- **Policy implications:** the case is now the **leading real-world test** of frontier-lab voluntary-reporting obligations, and lands during the **state-by-state AI legislative sprint** (1,561 bills introduced across 45 states; **NY RAISE Act effective March 19, 2026**; **Idaho Conversational AI Safety Act effective July 1**).

**Read-through:** the most consequential **child-safety / law-enforcement reporting failure** disclosed by a frontier lab in 2026, and a direct trust-and-safety counterweight to OpenAI's enterprise expansion (TBPN media acquisition, $122B funding round, Codex enterprise rollout). The disclosure that **leadership overruled employees who explicitly recommended reporting** is the part most likely to surface in (a) **Connecticut SB 5** rulemaking; (b) **EU AI Act Article 50 transparency rules** (live August 2, 2026 — T-98 days); (c) the **California state AG** review of charitable-trust governance; and (d) Sam Altman's **own testimony posture** in the trial that opens tomorrow.
