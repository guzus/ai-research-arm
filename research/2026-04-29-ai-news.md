### EU AI Omnibus Trilogue — April 28 Political Meeting; Annex III Postponement to **December 2, 2027** Now in Play
**Date:** Tuesday, April 28, 2026 | **Sources:** [Ropes & Gray — Trilogue Underway](https://www.ropesgray.com/en/insights/viewpoints/102mquz/ai-omnibus-trilogue-underwaywhat-to-expect-as-negotiations-progress), [A&O Shearman — Key Issues](https://www.aoshearman.com/en/insights/digital-omnibus-on-ai-what-is-really-on-the-table-as-trilogues-begin), [Lynt-X Global — 101 Days to Deadline](https://www.lyntxglobal.com/blog/101-days-eu-ai-act-deadline-trilogue-april-28-non-eu-enterprises), [OneTrust — Digital Omnibus](https://www.onetrust.com/blog/how-the-eu-digital-omnibus-reshapes-ai-act-timelines-and-governance-in-2026/), [Kaizenner — April Update](https://www.kaizenner.eu/post/ai-act-omnibus), [European Parliament — Legislative Train](https://www.europarl.europa.eu/legislative-train/package-digital-package/file-digital-omnibus-on-ai), [TechjackSolutions — Aug 2 deadline still legally binding](https://techjacksolutions.com/ai-brief/eu-ai-act-high-risk-deadline-extension-reported-but-august-2/)

The **Cypriot Council Presidency** convened the **political trilogue** on the EU AI Omnibus on Tuesday, April 28, in Brussels — with both Council and Parliament now **converged on fixed postponement dates** for the original August 2, 2026 Annex III high-risk-AI deadline:

- **December 2, 2027** — stand-alone high-risk AI systems classified under **Annex III** (8 categories: law enforcement, recruitment, education, employment, biometric, public services, justice/democracy, critical infrastructure).
- **August 2, 2028** — AI embedded in regulated products under **Annex I** (medical devices, machinery, etc.).

**Process:** Endorsement by Parliament expected May; Council endorsement expected June; Official Journal publication targeted for July 2026 — **immediately ahead** of the existing August 2 deadline.

**Crucial caveat:** **The August 2, 2026 deadline remains legally binding** until the Omnibus is published in the Official Journal. Compliance teams pausing high-risk-AI prep based on the political agreement are taking explicit legal risk.

**Read-through:** This is the **single most consequential AI-regulatory policy shift of 2026 to date** for U.S. and EU multinationals. A **17-month delay** for stand-alone Annex III systems materially loosens timeline pressure on **Anthropic, OpenAI, Google DeepMind, Mistral, Cohere–Aleph Alpha, and Stability AI** for biometric/employment/public-service deployments. It also cuts the **August 2 deadline pressure on national AI sandboxes** — each Member State was due to establish ≥1 sandbox by that date. Combined with the U.S. NY RAISE Act state-level activity, the **EU is now visibly the slower-moving regulatory pole**, reversing the post-Brussels Effect narrative dominant Q4 2025 → Q1 2026.
