## New Model Releases

### Meta Llama 4 Series Launch
**Date:** January 2026
Meta introduced Llama 4 Scout and Llama 4 Maverick, the first open-weight natively multimodal models with unprecedented context length support and the first built using a mixture-of-experts (MoE) architecture.

**Key Model Details:**

- **Llama 4 Scout**: A 17 billion active parameter model with 16 experts, is the best multimodal model in the world in its class and more powerful than all previous generation Llama models, while fitting in a single NVIDIA H100 GPU. It offers an industry-leading context window of 10M tokens and delivers better results than Gemma 3, Gemini 2.0 Flash-Lite, and Mistral 3.1 across a broad range of benchmarks.

- **Llama 4 Maverick**: A 17 billion active parameter model with 128 experts, is the best multimodal model in its class, beating GPT-4o and Gemini 2.0 Flash across a broad range of benchmarks, while achieving comparable results to DeepSeek v3 on reasoning and coding—at less than half the active parameters.

- **Llama 4 Behemoth**: A teacher model that outperforms GPT-4.5, Claude Sonnet 3.7, and Gemini 2.0 Pro on STEM-focused benchmarks such as MATH-500 and GPQA Diamond. Still in training and not yet released.

**Availability**: Llama 4 Scout and Llama 4 Maverick are available for download on Hugging Face, and users can try Meta AI with Llama 4 in WhatsApp, Messenger, Instagram Direct, and on the Meta.AI website.

**Source:** [Meta AI - Llama 4 Announcement](https://ai.meta.com/blog/llama-4-multimodal-intelligence/)

## Research Papers

### DeepSeek's mHC Architecture Breakthrough
**Date:** January 2026
DeepSeek released a new technical paper proposing "mHC: Manifold-Constrained Hyper-Connections," a fundamental rethink of AI model training architecture. The paper solves the long-standing training instability problem in large neural networks by constraining how information moves between layers so it stays balanced. ML researcher Sebastian Raschka called mHC "a striking breakthrough that could shape the evolution of foundational models."

**Sources:**
- [Introl Blog - DeepSeek mHC Architecture](https://introl.com/blog/deepseek-mhc-architecture-breakthrough)
- [SCMP - DeepSeek Paper](https://www.scmp.com/tech/big-tech/article/3338427/deepseek-kicks-2026-paper-signalling-push-train-bigger-models-less)

### Mechanistic Interpretability Advances
**Date:** 2025-2026
Researchers at top AI companies developed new techniques called mechanistic interpretability to probe models' inner workings, aiming to map key features and pathways across entire models. In 2025, Anthropic revealed whole sequences of features and traced paths from prompt to response, while teams at OpenAI and Google DeepMind used similar techniques to explain unexpected behaviors. MIT Technology Review named this one of its "10 Breakthrough Technologies 2026."

**Source:** [MIT Technology Review - Mechanistic Interpretability](https://www.technologyreview.com/2026/01/12/1130003/mechanistic-interpretability-ai-research-models-2026-breakthrough-technologies/)

### Looped Language Models Research
**Date:** January 2026
Researchers are exploring "looped language models" that let AI models iterate through problems multiple times, similar to how humans think through complex issues. Early results suggest this could be a game-changer for tasks requiring deep reasoning like math problems, code debugging, or strategic planning.

**Source:** [Prime Intellect - Recursive Language Models](https://www.primeintellect.ai/blog/rlm)

## Regulatory Updates

### EU AI Act Enforcement Approaching
**Date:** January 2026
The European Union's AI Act becomes fully enforceable in August 2026, representing a watershed moment in global tech regulation. Prohibited AI practices and AI literacy obligations entered into application on February 2, 2025. The governance rules and obligations for general-purpose AI (GPAI) models became applicable on August 2, 2025. Each Member State must establish at least one AI regulatory sandbox at the national level by August 2, 2026. A Code of Practice on transparency of AI-generated content is expected to be finalized in May-June 2026.
