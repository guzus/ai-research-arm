## Breaking News

### NVIDIA Unveils DLSS 5 — "GPT Moment for Graphics" via Neural Rendering
**Date:** March 17, 2026 | **Sources:** [NVIDIA Newsroom](http://nvidianews.nvidia.com/news/nvidia-dlss-5-delivers-ai-powered-breakthrough-in-visual-fidelity-for-games), [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/16/nvidias-dlss-5-uses-generative-ai-to-boost-photo-realism-in-video-games-with-ambitions-beyond-gaming/), [Newegg Insider](https://www.newegg.com/insider/nvidia-gtc-2026-part-1-this-is-the-house-that-geforce-made-and-dlss-5-just-changed-everything/), [NVIDIA GeForce](https://www.nvidia.com/en-us/geforce/news/death-stranding-2-crimson-desert-dlss-4-multi-frame-gen/)

Jensen Huang unveiled DLSS 5 during his GTC 2026 keynote, calling it the biggest graphics breakthrough since real-time ray tracing in 2018:

- Introduces **real-time neural rendering** that fuses structured 3D graphics data (geometry, depth, lighting) with generative AI to synthesize photorealistic pixels, lighting, and materials.
- Described as a **"GPT moment for graphics"** — AI generates final pixels rather than just upscaling them.
- **Launches this fall** via driver update for existing **RTX 50 Series GPUs** — no new hardware required.
- Confirmed game support includes *Starfield*, *Resident Evil Requiem*, *Assassin's Creed Shadows*, *Hogwarts Legacy*, *EA Sports FC*, from publishers Bethesda, CAPCOM, Ubisoft, and EA.
- GTC demos showed visible fidelity improvements, though some early reactions noted stylized effects in certain scenes.
- Separately, **DLSS 4.5 beta** overrides arrive March 31 via NVIDIA App (GeForce driver 595.79+).

### ACLU and CDT File Amicus Brief Supporting Anthropic's Pentagon Lawsuit
**Date:** March 16, 2026 | **Sources:** [ACLU](https://www.aclu.org/press-releases/aclu-and-cdt-urge-court-to-stop-government-from-punishing-anthropic-for-important-advocacy-on-ai-guardrails), [MLex](https://www.mlex.com/mlex/artificial-intelligence/articles/2453729/anthropic-challenge-to-us-defense-shows-ai-s-privacy-risks-aclu-says), [Mayer Brown](https://www.mayerbrown.com/en/insights/publications/2026/03/anthropic-supply-chain-risk-designation-takes-effect--latest-developments-and-next-steps-for-government-contractors)

The ACLU and Center for Democracy & Technology filed an amicus brief in Anthropic's D.C. Circuit challenge to its Pentagon "supply-chain risk" designation:

- Brief argues the designation **retaliates against Anthropic's First Amendment-protected advocacy** for AI guardrails against military surveillance and autonomous weapons.
- Anthropic filed D.C. Circuit lawsuit on **March 9** seeking a temporary restraining order and preliminary injunction to halt the designation.
- A parallel California case has a **hearing scheduled for March 24, 2026**.
- The supply-chain risk designation — historically reserved for foreign adversaries — bars defense contractors from using Anthropic technology on Pentagon work.
- Over **100 enterprise customers** have raised concerns about the designation's broader implications.
- No court ruling on the emergency stay has been issued as of March 18.

### EU AI Act Omnibus — Committee Vote Today (March 18)
**Date:** March 18, 2026 | **Sources:** [IAPP](https://iapp.org/news/a/meps-reach-preliminary-political-agreement-on-AI-omnibus), [Table Media](https://table.media/en/europe/news-en/ai-omnibus-committees-to-vote-next-week-on-compromise), [AIActo.eu](https://www.aiacto.eu/en/blog/digital-omnibus-ai-act-report-2027)

The European Parliament's LIBE and IMCO committees vote today on the AI Act Omnibus compromise report:

- Proposes **delaying high-risk AI compliance deadlines**: Annex III requirements pushed from August 2026 to **December 2, 2027**; Annex I from August 2027 to **August 2, 2028**.
- Includes the **explicit ban on AI-generated non-consensual deepfakes** agreed in the March 11 political deal.
- Adds **stricter value chain duties** for general-purpose AI providers with sanctions for breaches.
- Industry groups have raised concerns over shortened generative AI transparency grace periods and reinstated registration requirements.
- If approved, the report advances to **plenary vote (potentially March 26)** and then trilogue negotiations with the Council.
- Final adoption not expected before mid-2026; until then, original AI Act timelines apply.
