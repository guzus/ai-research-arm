### Atlassian Cuts 1,600 Jobs to Fund AI Pivot, Replaces CTO
**Date:** March 11, 2026 | **Sources:** [The Next Web](https://thenextweb.com/news/atlassian-is-cutting-1600-jobs-and-replacing-its-cto), [Computerworld](https://www.computerworld.com/article/4144218/atlassian-cuts-1600-jobs-to-fund-ai-and-enterprise-expansion.html), [MarketRealist](https://marketrealist.com/atlassian-layoffs-to-invest-in-ai/), [Business Insider](https://www.businessinsider.com/atlassian-layoffs-ceo-retains-entry-level-employees-graduates-2026-3)

Atlassian announced 1,600 job cuts — approximately 10% of its global workforce — to self-fund accelerated investment in AI and enterprise sales:
- More than **900 of the affected roles** are in software research and development.
- Geographic distribution: North America (40%, ~640 people), Australia (30%, ~480), India (16%, ~250), with the rest across Japan, Philippines, EMEA.
- CTO Rajeev Rajan is stepping down on March 31; two executives — Taroon Mandhana and Vikram Rao — will split CTO responsibilities, described by Atlassian as "next-generation AI talent."
- Restructuring costs estimated at **$225–$236 million**, with cuts largely complete by end of June 2026.
- Departing employees receive minimum 16-week severance, plus one additional week per year of service, pro-rated FY26 bonus, and six months of healthcare.
- The cuts come despite strong performance: cloud revenue ~$1.067B (up 26% YoY), remaining performance obligations ~$3.814B (up 44%), and Rovo AI assistant crossing 5 million MAU in February.
- This is Atlassian's second major layoff in three years, following a 500-employee cut in March 2023.

### Meta Unveils Roadmap for Four Custom AI Chip Generations Through 2027
**Date:** March 12, 2026 | **Sources:** [TechBrew](https://www.techbrew.com/stories/2026/03/12/meta-custom-silicon-ai-chips), [NotebookCheck](https://www.notebookcheck.net/Meta-shatters-silicon-cycle-Four-new-AI-chips-by-2027.1249562.0.html), [TrendForce](https://www.trendforce.com/news/2026/03/12/news-metas-in-house-ai-chip-push-four-new-accelerators-gear-up-amid-memory-crunch-by-2027/)

Meta announced a roadmap for four generations of MTIA (Meta Training and Inference Accelerator) custom AI chips, with a new chip every six months through end of 2027:
- **MTIA 300**: Already deployed across Meta's infrastructure a few weeks before the announcement.
- **MTIA 400**: Completed testing and on the path to deployment — Meta's first in-house accelerator matching cost efficiency with performance comparable to commercial alternatives from Nvidia and AMD.
- **MTIA 450 and 500**: Planned on a six-month cadence through end of 2027.
- Chips target **inference workloads** — ranking, recommendations, and generative AI (including image/video generation from text prompts).
- Meta has already deployed **hundreds of thousands of MTIA chips** for inference across its apps.
- The aggressive roadmap signals a strategic push to reduce dependence on Nvidia, though Meta maintains major supply deals with Nvidia, AMD, and Google.

- Users can adjust variables in real-time and observe changes in formulas, graphs, and models — e.g., modifying triangle sides in the Pythagorean theorem, tweaking parameters in Hooke's law, Coulomb's law, or ideal gas law.
- Available to all logged-in ChatGPT users across all plans.

- OpenAI plans expansion to more STEM topics and subjects.
