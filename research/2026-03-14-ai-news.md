### Google DeepMind Launches Aletheia — AI Agent for Autonomous Mathematical Research
**Date:** March 12–13, 2026 | **Sources:** [Marktechpost](https://www.marktechpost.com/2026/03/13/google-deepmind-introduces-aletheia-the-ai-agent-moving-from-math-competitions-to-fully-autonomous-professional-research-discoveries/), [Mindplex](https://magazine.mindplex.ai/post/aletheia-and-the-era-of-autonomous-science), [DeepMind Blog](https://deepmind.google/blog/accelerating-mathematical-and-scientific-discovery-with-gemini-deep-think/)

Google DeepMind unveiled Aletheia, a specialized AI research agent that moves beyond competition mathematics to conduct autonomous professional-level scientific research:
- Powered by an advanced version of **Gemini 3 Deep Think**, Aletheia achieved **95.1% accuracy on the IMO-Proof Bench Advanced** benchmark.
- The system autonomously solved **four open problems from the Erdős Conjectures database** — longstanding unsolved mathematical questions.
- Aletheia can generate independent research papers without human intervention, navigating scientific literature, constructing rigorous long-horizon proofs, and systematically revising its own work.
- Named after the Greek concept of "truth as unconcealment," the system represents a qualitative shift from solving self-contained competition problems to open-ended research discovery.
- Signals a new class of AI reasoning systems capable of contributing meaningfully to professional mathematics and potentially other scientific disciplines.

### ByteDance Deploys 36,000 Nvidia B200 GPUs in Malaysia to Bypass U.S. Export Controls

### Meta Unveils Roadmap for Four Custom AI Chip Generations Through 2027
**Date:** March 12, 2026 | **Sources:** [TechBrew](https://www.techbrew.com/stories/2026/03/12/meta-custom-silicon-ai-chips), [NotebookCheck](https://www.notebookcheck.net/Meta-shatters-silicon-cycle-Four-new-AI-chips-by-2027.1249562.0.html), [TrendForce](https://www.trendforce.com/news/2026/03/12/news-metas-in-house-ai-chip-push-four-new-accelerators-gear-up-amid-memory-crunch-by-2027/)

Meta announced a roadmap for four generations of MTIA (Meta Training and Inference Accelerator) custom AI chips, with a new chip every six months through end of 2027:
- **MTIA 300**: Already deployed across Meta's infrastructure a few weeks before the announcement.
- **MTIA 400**: Completed testing and on the path to deployment — Meta's first in-house accelerator matching cost efficiency with performance comparable to commercial alternatives from Nvidia and AMD.
- **MTIA 450 and 500**: Planned on a six-month cadence through end of 2027.
- Chips target **inference workloads** — ranking, recommendations, and generative AI (including image/video generation from text prompts).
- Meta has already deployed **hundreds of thousands of MTIA chips** for inference across its apps.
- The aggressive roadmap signals a strategic push to reduce dependence on Nvidia, though Meta maintains major supply deals with Nvidia, AMD, and Google.

### Nvidia Releases Nemotron 3 Super — 120B Open Model with 1M Context Window
**Date:** March 11, 2026 | **Sources:** [Open Source For U](https://www.opensourceforu.com/2026/03/nvidia-pushes-open-ai-agents-with-nemotron-3-super-release/), [Nvidia Blog](https://blogs.nvidia.com/blog/nemotron-3-super-agentic-ai/), [LLM Stats](https://llm-stats.com/blog/research/nemotron-3-super-launch)

Nvidia released Nemotron 3 Super, a 120-billion-parameter open model designed for complex multi-agent systems:
- **12 billion active parameters per token** via a hybrid Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) architecture combining Mamba-Transformer with multi-token prediction.
- **1 million token context window** — among the largest for open models.
- Delivers **2.2x higher inference throughput** than GPT-OSS-120B and **5x higher throughput** vs. prior Nemotron Super versions.
- Released with **open weights** under a permissive license, plus 10 trillion tokens of synthetic training data and NVIDIA NeMo fine-tuning support.
- Available on build.nvidia.com, Hugging Face, Perplexity, OpenRouter, and DeepInfra (~$0.10/$0.50 API pricing).
- Requires 8x H100-80GB GPUs for self-hosting (BF16); supports vLLM, TensorRT-LLM, SGLang, and NIM microservices.
- Tops Artificial Analysis leaderboards for efficiency/openness; #1 on DeepResearch Bench.
- Optimized for multi-agent tasks including cybersecurity and codebase analysis.
