### 🚩 Skeptic's corner
- **"$1.5B JV" framing risk**: this is the WSJ-disclosed *gross JV size*, not net new capital flowing to Anthropic. Anthropic's own check is $300M; the rest is Blackstone/H&F (~$300M each) and Goldman (~$150M). The $1.5B headline conflates the JV's balance sheet with Anthropic's revenue. Watch for press writeups that call it a "$1.5B Anthropic deal" — that would be the wrong frame.
- **The "circular financing" critique deserves to be on the front page, not buried**: WSJ already reported (per @jeffreyleefunk May 2) that OpenAI's parallel PE-JV pattern involves OpenAI putting up $1.5B with PE adding $4B to *disseminate* the tools — i.e., paying portfolio companies to use OpenAI software. If the Anthropic JV has the same structure (Anthropic puts in $300M which then partly flows back as "revenue" for Claude licenses inside PE portfolios), the deal is closer to a marketing spend than enterprise sales. Anthropic's announcement language tomorrow will tell.
- **TrendForce 29%→22% Rubin share** is a research-firm forecast, not a NVIDIA disclosure. NVIDIA has not commented; Jensen's last on-record statement was the China share / Korean supply chain comment carried this weekend. Treat the magnitude as ⚠ until NVIDIA's next earnings call.
- **Midha's "$2.75/hr H100"** is a clean datum but the comparison to 2023 a16z-portfolio "$1.72/hr" is a bit unfair (those were heavily discounted founder-program rates, not market clearing prices). The directional claim that compute hasn't gotten dramatically cheaper for AI startups still holds — but the headline number overstates the gap.
