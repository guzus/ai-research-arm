### Nvidia GTC 2026 Keynote: Jensen Huang Unveils Vera Rubin GPU Architecture and Six New Chips
**Date:** March 16, 2026 | **Sources:** [Nvidia Investor Relations](https://investor.nvidia.com/news/press-release-details/2026/NVIDIA-Kicks-Off-the-Next-Generation-of-AI-With-Rubin--Six-New-Chips-One-Incredible-AI-Supercomputer/default.aspx), [Tom's Hardware](https://www.tomshardware.com/pc-components/gpus/nvidia-announces-rubin-gpus-in-2026-rubin-ultra-in-2027-feynam-after), [Oplexa](https://oplexa.com/jensen-huang-gtc-2026-keynote-nvidia-announcements/)

Jensen Huang delivered the GTC 2026 keynote at SAP Center in San Jose to 30,000+ attendees, formally unveiling the next-generation **Vera Rubin** GPU platform:

- **Vera Rubin GPU**: Successor to Blackwell, featuring **336 billion transistors** per package, **HBM4/HBM4e memory** at **13 TB/s bandwidth per GPU** (up from 8 TB/s on Blackwell B300), and **288 GB HBM4e** per GPU.
- **Vera CPU**: Replaces Grace CPU with **88 custom ARM cores** (176 threads) and **1.8 TB/s NVLink** connectivity to GPUs.
- **VR200 NVL72**: 72 GPUs + 36 Vera CPUs delivering **3.3x inference performance over Blackwell Ultra** (3.3 EFLOPS FP4 dense compute).
- **VR200 NVL144**: 144 GPUs producing **3.6 EFLOPS FP4** and **1.2 EFLOPS FP8** training performance.
- **NVL576 (Rubin Ultra, H2 2027)**: 576-GPU system targeting **15 EFLOPS FP4 inference** and **5 EFLOPS FP8 training** with **365 TB memory** and **4.6 PB/s HBM4e bandwidth**.
- **Token cost reduction**: Agentic/reasoning inference drops to **1/10th of Blackwell** costs.
- **Networking**: NVLink 6, ConnectX-9 SuperNIC, BlueField-4 DPU announced as part of full platform.
- **Roadmap**: Rubin Ultra in H2 2027; **Feynman architecture** follows in 2028.
- Nvidia market cap reportedly exceeded **$3 trillion** following the reveal.

### Nvidia Dynamo — Open-Source Inference OS for Data Center Scale
**Date:** March 16, 2026 | **Sources:** [Nvidia Dynamo](https://www.nvidia.com/en-us/ai/dynamo/), [Tom's Guide](https://www.tomsguide.com/computing/live/nvidia-gtc-2026-live), [Vik's Newsletter](https://www.viksnewsletter.com/p/gtc-2026-preview-implications-of-sram-decode)

Nvidia launched **Dynamo**, an open-source inference framework positioned as the "Operating System of AI":

- **Disaggregates inference** into prefill, decode, and orchestration phases across GPUs with intelligent request routing.
- **Up to 15x throughput improvement** for Mixture-of-Expert (MoE) models on GB200 NVL72 vs. Hopper systems.
- Key components: **Grove** (topology-optimized Kubernetes serving), **GPU Planner** (capacity monitoring and dynamic allocation), **NIXL** (low-latency cross-GPU/CPU/network data movement).
- Supports **TensorRT-LLM, vLLM, SGLang, and PyTorch** — scales seamlessly from one to thousands of GPUs.
- Already adopted by **Perplexity AI** and **Together AI**.
- Integrates with Groq LPU hardware (from Nvidia's $20B acquisition) for SRAM-based decode acceleration.

## Regulatory Updates

### Federal AI Preemption Push Creates Compliance Uncertainty as State Laws Advance
**Date:** March 11–15, 2026 | **Sources:** [Gunderson Dettmer](https://www.gunder.com/en/news-insights/insights/2026-ai-laws-update-key-regulations-and-practical-guidance), [JD Supra](https://www.jdsupra.com/legalnews/march-2026-federal-deadlines-that-will-9297133/), [National Law Review](https://natlawreview.com/article/several-state-ai-laws-set-go-effect-2026-despite-federal-governments-push-eliminate)

The Trump administration met key March 11 deadlines from its December 2025 AI Executive Order, intensifying the federal-vs-state regulatory clash:

- **DOJ Task Force report** (March 11): Identified state AI laws deemed "onerous" — those that hinder innovation, modify truthful AI outputs, or have extraterritorial reach. DOJ may challenge these laws directly.
- **FTC policy statement** (March 11): Clarified Section 5 of FTC Act preemption of state laws requiring changes to "truthful" AI model outputs.
- **FCC process initiated**: Evaluating a federal AI reporting standard to preempt conflicting state-level requirements.
- Federal framework **exempts** state laws on child safety, AI infrastructure, and government AI use.
- **State pushback continues**: Illinois, Texas, and Colorado implementing 2026 workforce AI laws regardless. California's **SB 5105** (deepfakes), Washington's **HB 4799** (frontier AI transparency), and numerous other bills advancing in state legislatures.
- EU AI Act high-risk obligations remain on track for **August 2, 2026**, though the European Commission missed its February 2026 deadline for publishing guidelines.
- Companies face a **fragmented compliance environment** across federal preemption, active state laws, and international regulations.
