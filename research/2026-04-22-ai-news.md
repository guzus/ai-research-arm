### Google Cloud Next 2026 — Google Unveils TPU 8t + TPU 8i (First Split of Training/Inference Silicon)
**Date:** April 22, 2026 | **Sources:** [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/22/google-launches-training-and-inference-tpus-in-latest-shot-at-nvidia.html), [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/04/22/google-cloud-next-new-tpu-ai-chips-compete-with-nvidia/), [SiliconANGLE](https://siliconangle.com/2026/04/22/google-unveils-new-tpus-power-next-wave-ai-training-inference/), [The Register](https://www.theregister.com/2026/04/22/google_tpu8_dual_track_training_inference), [Techzine](https://www.techzine.eu/blogs/infrastructure/140680/google-presents-tpu-8t-and-tpu-8i-chips-splits-training-and-inference/), [Implicator](https://www.implicator.ai/google-splits-tpu-8-into-training-and-inference-chips-to-chase-nvidia/)

At **Google Cloud Next 2026** on Wednesday April 22, Google unveiled its **eighth-generation TPU** in **two distinct variants** — the first time Google has split training and inference onto separate silicon in its TPU line. Amin Vahdat (SVP, chief technologist for AI/infrastructure) framed the split as the response to "the rise of AI agents … chips individually specialized to the needs of training and serving."

- **TPU 8t (training):** **2.8× performance** vs. the seventh-generation **Ironwood** TPU at the same price.
- **TPU 8i (inference):** **80% better performance** per chip than Ironwood, with **384 MB of on-die SRAM** per chip (**3×** the SRAM of Ironwood) — a deliberate bet that SRAM-heavy inference silicon will beat HBM-limited GPUs on agent workloads where KV-cache thrashing dominates.
- **Supply chain:** Google's inference push is backed by an **expanded four-partner supply chain** — Broadcom (existing design partner), with **MediaTek** and **Marvell** added to secure capacity outside TSMC N3.
- **Availability:** Later in 2026.
- **Read-through:** This is the most aggressive silicon differentiation Google has shipped since Trillium — and it ships in the same week Anthropic's **3.5 GW** Google/Broadcom TPU deal was finalized (Anthropic run-rate revenue **$30B**, per Tom's Hardware). Nvidia's inference moat is now under a credible, dual-vendor, SRAM-heavy assault from Google — the only hyperscaler that can take the architectural risk. Combined with the **Thinking Machines Lab** GB300 deal (below), Google Cloud Next 2026 is the single most consequential cloud/chip event since GTC.

OpenAI released **ChatGPT Images 2.0** on Tuesday April 21 — the first image model the company positions as a **"visual thought partner"** rather than a rendering tool. Backed by the new **gpt-image-2** model, it brings **native reasoning**, **2K output resolution**, and **multi-image consistency**, with the model verifying its own outputs and generating up to **eight coherent images** from a single prompt.

Paired with Codex Labs (below), this is the most significant OpenAI product release since the Rosalind research preview.

### Google DeepMind — Deep Research + Deep Research Max (Gemini 3.1 Pro, MCP Native)
**Date:** April 21, 2026 | **Sources:** [Google Blog](https://blog.google/innovation-and-ai/models-and-research/gemini-models/next-generation-gemini-deep-research/), [VentureBeat](https://venturebeat.com/technology/googles-new-deep-research-and-deep-research-max-agents-can-search-the-web-and-your-private-data), [The Decoder](https://the-decoder.com/google-launches-deep-research-and-deep-research-max-agents-to-automate-complex-research/), [Blockchain.news](https://blockchain.news/ainews/google-deepmind-unveils-deep-research-and-deep-research-max-powered-by-gemini-3-1-pro-latest-analysis-on-autonomous-ai-research-agents)

Google DeepMind unveiled two new autonomous research agents on April 21, both powered by **Gemini 3.1 Pro**, available now in **public preview** through paid tiers in the **Gemini API**.

- **Deep Research:** Optimized for **speed and lower latency/cost** at higher quality — targeted at research experiences embedded in interactive user surfaces.

### Nature — "Human Scientists Trounce the Best AI Agents on Complex Tasks"
**Date:** April 21, 2026 | **Source:** [Nature](https://www.nature.com/articles/d41586-026-01199-z)

A Nature news analysis published April 21 — reflecting findings from the **Stanford AI Index Report 2026** — formally documents that while AI-mention penetration in natural-sciences publications is already at **6–9%** depending on field, **expert human scientists substantially outperform the best AI agents** (including GPT-5 Pro, Gemini 3 Pro Preview, Claude Opus 4.6 Thinking Max) on complex, multi-step scientific workflows.

- On **Humanity's Last Exam** (2,500 expert-crafted questions), leading models sit at **Gemini 3 Pro Preview 37.5% / Claude Opus 4.6 Thinking Max 34.4% / GPT-5 Pro 31.6%** — while **human domain experts average ~90%**.
- Companion **Sakana AI "AI Scientist" paper**, also published in Nature, reports that the upgraded **AI Scientist-v2** produced the first fully AI-generated paper to pass a rigorous human peer-review and that its automated reviewer reached **69% balanced accuracy** (F1 above human-to-human agreement).
- **Read-through:** Tempering evidence for the "scientific discovery is automated" narrative pushed by Anthropic's "Automated Alignment Researchers" paper and by Recursive Superintelligence's $500M thesis. The capability gap on deep, open-ended tasks is still wide even as narrow subroutines close.

## Regulatory Updates

### Connecticut — Senate Bill 5 Passes 32–4 (Frontier AI Regulation, Chatbot Safety, AI Sandbox)
**Date:** April 21, 2026 | **Sources:** [CT Mirror](https://ctmirror.org/2026/04/21/artificial-intelligence-regulation-senate-ct/), [WTNH](https://www.wtnh.com/news/connecticut/hartford/state-senate-passes-amended-ai-regulation-bill/), [GovTech](https://www.govtech.com/artificial-intelligence/connecticut-senate-approves-major-ai-legislation), [FastDemocracy bill tracker](https://fastdemocracy.com/bill-search/ct/2026/bills/CTB00033095/), [Connecticut Senate Democrats](https://www.senatedems.ct.gov/senator-maher-joins-passage-of-bill-to-protect-residents-from-ai-harms-boost-workforce-ai-readiness)

The **Connecticut Senate** passed a significantly amended **SB 5** on Tuesday April 21, **32–4**. It is the most comprehensive state AI proposal advanced this session after a strike-all amendment from Sen. Maroney.

- **Frontier-model developer regulation:** Direct duties on developers of "frontier" AI models (definition tracks the EU/California thresholds on training compute).
- **AI "sandbox":** Authorizes a state AI sandbox for companies to test new technologies under relaxed regulatory conditions.
- **Chatbot safety:** Operators must make reasonable efforts to **detect suicidal ideations or self-harm** signals in user messages and route to appropriate resources — directly responsive to a national cluster of chatbot-harm suits.
- **Employment protections:** Workers must be notified if AI is used in hiring/employment decisions; employers barred from using AI decision tools for discriminatory outcomes.
- **Workforce:** Creates partnerships with the Department of Housing, Labor Department, and Secretary of State for a state **AI Academy**.
- **Next step:** House vote pending; would go to Gov. Lamont. If enacted, CT joins Colorado and California as the third state with an active frontier-model regulatory framework — directly relevant to the **federal preemption fight** under EO 14365 (Dec 11, 2025) and the **GUARDRAILS Act** in the U.S. House.
