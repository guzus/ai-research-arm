## Research Highlights

Carrying from `research/arxiv/2026-05-02-papers.md` (most recent dated set; arXiv has no 05-03 set yet — Sunday is typically quiet):

1. **[Synthetic Computers at Scale for Long-Horizon Productivity Simulation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.28181)** (Microsoft Research — Ge, Peng, Cheng, Gao). Methodology for generating synthetic user computers (folder hierarchies + content-rich documents) and running month-long, 2,000+-turn agent simulations across 1,000 synthetic computers. **Why it matters:** Foundational substrate for agentic RL on long-horizon, real-world productivity work — moves beyond curated tasks toward billion-scale persona-conditioned simulation.

2. **[Do Sparse Autoencoders Capture Concept Manifolds?](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.28119)** (Bhalla, Fel, Rager et al.). Theoretical framework showing SAEs capture concept manifolds either globally (compact atom group spans the manifold) or locally (atoms tile regions); empirically end up in a fragmented "dilution" regime that recovers continuous structure suboptimally. **Why it matters:** Reframes a foundational interpretability assumption — concepts are rarely independent linear directions, and current SAEs miss the manifold structure.

3. **[Claw-Eval-Live: A Live Agent Benchmark for Evolving Real-World Workflows](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.28139)**. Live benchmark refreshed from public workflow demand signals (top-500 ClawHub skills) grading execution traces, audit logs, and workspace artifacts. Across 105 tasks and 13 frontier models, the leading model passes only **66.7%** — no model breaks 70%. **Why it matters:** Reliable workflow automation remains far from solved; leaderboard rank alone hides task-family-specific failures.

4. **[Characterizing the Consistency of the Emergent Misalignment Persona](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.28082)** (Weckauff, Zhang, Andriushchenko). Fine-tuning Qwen 2.5 32B Instruct on 6 narrowly misaligned domains finds two distinct EM patterns: coherent-persona (harmful behavior + self-reported misalignment) and inverted-persona (harmful outputs + self-identifying as aligned). **Why it matters:** Inverted-persona models would evade self-report-based safety probes — direct implications for safety evaluations.

5. **[PRISM: Pre-alignment via Black-box On-policy Distillation for Multimodal RL](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.28123)** (Wang, Huang, Yu et al.). Inserts distribution-alignment stage between SFT and RLVR using black-box on-policy distillation with MoE discriminator. On Qwen3-VL improves average accuracy +4.4 (4B) / +6.0 (8B) over SFT-to-RLVR baseline. **Why it matters:** Concrete fix for distributional drift in multimodal post-training.

**Other notable from the May 2 set:** [Crab — eBPF Semantics-Aware Checkpoint/Restore for Agent Sandboxes](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.28138) (lifts recovery correctness 8% → 100%, cuts checkpoint traffic up to 87%); [Latent Adversarial Detection: Probing LLM Activations for Multi-Turn Attacks](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.28129) (residual-stream "adversarial restlessness" signature lifts conversation-level detection 76.2% → 93.8% across four model families); [Repetition over Diversity](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.28075) (strict quality filtering with multi-epoch repetition beats single-pass on 10–360× more tokens — sample-efficient German LM training).
