### Anthropic Project Deal — Agent-on-Agent Marketplace Results Published
Closed marketplace inside Anthropic's SF office; 69 employees delegated buy/sell decisions on personal items to Claude agents.
- **Volume:** 186 deals, >$4,000 in real transaction value.
- **Setup:** $100 gift-card credits per agent; multi-turn negotiations; four parallel runs varying Opus/Haiku assignment.
- **Headline finding — "agent-quality gap":** Opus seller pulled in $2.68 more on average; Opus buyer paid $2.45 less. Across 161 items sold in ≥2 runs, an Opus seller vs. Haiku buyer averaged $24.18 vs. $18.63 for Opus-on-Opus.
- **User perception:** participants on the losing end did not notice; 46% said they would pay for a similar service.
- **Implication:** structural-fairness issue most likely to surface in next round of state and EU AI rulemaking; EU AI Act Article 50 transparency obligations begin Aug 2, 2026.
