## Breaking News

### Google Threat Intelligence Group: First Confirmed AI-Generated Zero-Day Exploit Caught in the Wild

Google announced Monday that its Threat Intelligence Group disrupted a cybercrime group that used an AI model to develop a zero-day exploit — a two-factor authentication bypass in a widely used open-source web-based administration platform. Google says the threat actor was preparing a "mass exploitation event" and that its proactive disclosure to the impacted vendor likely prevented the attack from succeeding. Although Google ruled out its own Gemini model as the source, forensic analysis pointed to LLM-generated code: "the script contains an abundance of educational docstrings, including a hallucinated CVSS score, and uses a structured, textbook Pythonic format highly characteristic of LLMs training data."

This is the first confirmed real-world case of an AI model being used to weaponize an unknown vulnerability — sharpening the policy edge of the UK AISI / Anthropic Mythos "frontier cyber-offence capability doubling every four months" framing, and arriving inside the T-13-day window before the Hassett "FDA-for-AI" executive order is expected to be signed.
