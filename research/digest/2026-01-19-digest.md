## Research Highlights

### Top 5 Must-Read Papers

**1. PaCoRe: Learning to Scale Test-Time Compute with Parallel Coordinated Reasoning**
- arXiv: [2601.05593](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.05593)
- Introduces Parallel Coordinated Reasoning framework that decouples test-time compute scaling from context limits by shifting from sequential depth to coordinated breadth
- **Why it matters:** Addresses fundamental limitations of sequential test-time scaling by enabling parallel processing

**2. Thinking Long, but Short: Stable Sequential Test-Time Scaling**
- arXiv: [2601.09855](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.09855)
- Presents Min-Seek, a sequential test-time scaling method that stabilizes accuracy across wide ranges of induced thoughts
- **Why it matters:** Solves critical instability issues in test-time compute scaling without fine-tuning

**3. FLOP-Efficient Training: Early Stopping Based on Test-Time Compute Awareness**
- arXiv: [2601.01332](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.01332)
- Shows intermediate checkpoints with test-time compute can match fully trained model accuracy while requiring substantially fewer training FLOPs
- **Why it matters:** Fundamentally changes training efficiency paradigm by recognizing test-time compute can compensate for less training

**4. Benchmark²: Systematic Evaluation of LLM Benchmarks**
- arXiv: [2601.03986](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.03986) - Qi Qian, Chengsong Huang, et al.
- Comprehensive framework evaluating LLM benchmarks themselves through Cross-Benchmark Ranking Consistency, Discriminability Score, and Capability Alignment Deviation
- Tested across 15 benchmarks, 11 LLMs, and 4 model families
- **Why it matters:** Provides meta-evaluation tools to assess which benchmarks are most reliable

**5. The Molecular Structure of Thought: Mapping the Topology of Long Chain-of-Thought Reasoning**
- arXiv: [2601.06002](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.06002)
- Proposes effective Long CoT trajectories feature stable molecular-like structures formed by three interaction types: Deep-Reasoning (covalent-like), Self-Reflection (hydrogen-bond-like), and Self-Exploration (van der Waals-like)
- **Why it matters:** Provides theoretical framework for understanding what makes reasoning chains effective
