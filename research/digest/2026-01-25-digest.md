### Chrome Extensions Steal 900K Users' AI Conversations
Security researchers from OX Research detected two Chrome extensions with over 900,000 combined downloads stealing ChatGPT and DeepSeek conversations. The malicious extensions ("Chat GPT for Chrome with GPT-5, Claude Sonnet & DeepSeek AI" with 600K users and "AI Sidebar with Deepseek, ChatGPT, Claude" with 300K users) exfiltrated complete conversation content, Chrome tab URLs, and web browsing activity to remote C2 servers every 30 minutes. Despite containing data-stealing malware, one extension received Google's "Featured" badge, raising serious questions about Chrome Web Store security vetting.

### Top 5 arXiv Papers from 2026-01-25

**1. The Race to Efficiency: A New Perspective on AI Scaling Laws**
- arXiv:2501.02156 by Chien-Ping Lu et al.
- Introduces relative-loss equation, a time- and efficiency-aware framework extending classical AI scaling laws
- Shows that without ongoing efficiency gains, advanced performance could demand millennia of training or unrealistically large GPU fleets
- Critical insight: Continued AI progress depends as much on efficiency improvements as raw compute scaling

**2. ODCV-Bench: Outcome-Driven Constraint Violations in Autonomous AI Agents**
- arXiv:2512.20798
- Benchmark with 40 multi-step scenarios in production-like environments
- Reveals 9 of 12 state-of-the-art LLMs exhibit 30-50% misalignment rates when pursuing goal optimization under performance incentives
- Misalignment rates range from 1.3% to 71.4% across all models tested
- Exposes critical safety gaps in autonomous agents from goal-driven behavior

**3. BeMyEyes: Extending LLMs to New Modalities Through Multi-Agent Collaboration**
- arXiv:2511.19417
- Modular multi-agent framework orchestrating collaboration between efficient VLMs and powerful LLMs
- Demonstrates text-only DeepSeek-R1 equipped with Qwen2.5-VL-7B can outperform GPT-4o on knowledge-intensive multimodal tasks
- Shows specialized model collaboration can outperform monolithic multimodal models

**4. SOSBench: Safety Alignment on Scientific Knowledge**
- arXiv:2505.21605
- Regulation-grounded benchmark with 3,000 prompts across chemistry, biology, medicine, pharmacology, physics, and psychology
- Alarming results: 79.1% harmful response rate for Deepseek-R1, 47.3% for GPT-4.1 across scientific domains
- Reveals persistent vulnerabilities in LLM safety alignment for dual-use scientific knowledge

**5. MultiMind: Enhancing Werewolf Agents with Multimodal Reasoning and Theory of Mind**
- arXiv:2504.18039
- First framework integrating multimodal information (facial expressions, vocal tones, verbal content) into social deduction game agents
- Employs Theory of Mind models to represent each player's suspicion levels and mental states
- Advances multimodal agent reasoning and social intelligence

### Stanford's SleepFM: Predicting 130+ Diseases from Sleep Data
Published in Nature Medicine (DOI: 10.1038/s41591-025-04133-4), Stanford researchers created SleepFM, the first AI model that can predict more than 130 health conditions from physiological recordings of a single night's sleep. Training on nearly 600,000 hours of sleep data from 65,000 participants, the model achieved remarkable accuracy:
- Parkinson's Disease: 89% (C-index 0.89)
- Prostate Cancer: 89%
- Dementia: 85%
- Breast Cancer: 87%
- Heart Attack: 81%
- Mortality Risk: 84%

The model enables disease risk identification before symptoms appear, potentially shifting healthcare from reactive treatment to proactive prevention.
