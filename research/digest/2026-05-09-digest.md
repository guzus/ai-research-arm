### EU AI Omnibus — Provisional Trilogue Agreement Closed in Early-Hours May 7; Annex III Pushed to Dec 2 2027 / Annex I to Aug 2 2028; Article 5 CSAM/NCII Prohibition Added
**Date:** Thursday May 7 early-hours trilogue (carried into Saturday legal-press deep-dives) | **Sources:** [Council of the EU](https://www.consilium.europa.eu/en/press/press-releases/2026/05/07/artificial-intelligence-council-and-parliament-agree-to-simplify-and-streamline-rules/), [Bird & Bird](https://www.twobirds.com/en/insights/2026/digital-omnibus-on-ai-provisional-agreement-reached-at-the-may-trilogue), [Hogan Lovells](https://www.hoganlovells.com/en/publications/eu-legislators-agree-to-delay-for-highrisk-ai-rules), [Modulos](https://www.modulos.ai/blog/eu-ai-act-omnibus-deal/), [Lewis Silkin](https://www.lewissilkin.com/insights/2026/05/07/the-council-and-parliament-agree-to-slim-down-and-delay-parts-of-the-eu-ai-act-102ms0v), [Lexology](https://www.lexology.com/library/detail.aspx?g=f0b55f0c-2a09-4162-a452-a2fb248006b2)

**Correction to mid-week reporting (which expected a stalled trilogue to May 13):** Council and Parliament negotiators **reached a provisional agreement in the early hours of Thursday May 7** after the **April 28 trilogue collapse**. **Treat May 7 as the trilogue conclusion, not May 13.** Key terms:

- **Annex III high-risk obligations pushed to December 2, 2027** (biometrics, employment, education, law enforcement, critical infrastructure, border management).
- **Annex I product-embedded high-risk obligations pushed to August 2, 2028.**
- **Conformity-assessment compromise:** Two-layer architecture; **Machinery Regulation carved out** of direct AI-Act applicability — the sticking point that broke the April 28 trilogue is resolved.
- **New Article 5 prohibition** on AI systems generating **child sexual abuse material (CSAM)** or **non-consensual intimate imagery (NCII)** in three configurations; **Dec 2, 2026** compliance deadline.
- **Article 50(2) watermarking** deadline **holds at August 2, 2026** — generative-AI providers must mark synthetic audio/image/video/text as machine-readable; transitional period to Dec 2, 2026.
- **Bias-detection processing** of special-category personal data extended to **non-high-risk AI systems** and AI models.
- **SME exemptions extended to small mid-cap enterprises (SMCs).**

**Read-through:** (1) The **default August 2, 2026 Annex III applicability date is structurally averted** assuming Council and Parliament complete formal adoption on schedule — the 86-day cliff disclosed mid-week is defused. (2) **Article 5 CSAM/NCII** is the **most material substantive expansion of the AI Act since GPAI rules entered force in August 2025**; compliance burden falls hardest on **open-source image/video generators** (Stable Diffusion, Flux, Wan 2.5) and **Character.AI / Replika-class companion apps** — adjacent to **Pennsylvania v. Character.AI** (carry). (3) **Annex III delay to December 2027 structurally decouples the EU regulatory cliff from the late-2026 US election cycle**, materially easing political-cost pressure on the **CAISI MOUs / Hassett "FDA-for-AI" EO** trajectory.

## Research Highlights

### Weekend arXiv cluster (cs.AI / cs.LG / cs.CL)

From the active arXiv reasoning-RL cluster bridging the May 8 / 9 window:

1. **"Rethinking RL for LLM Reasoning: It's Sparse Policy Selection, Not Capability Learning"** ([2605.06241](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.06241)) — Argues RL **does not teach new strategies** but **redistributes probability mass** over solutions the base model already contains. The "beneficial footprint" is a **sparse, predictable correction concentrated at high-entropy decision points**. **Direct implication:** test-time scaling and RLVR are diagnostic-value tools, not capability-creation tools — refutes the "RL emergence" thesis dominant since DeepSeek-R1.
2. **"Enhanced LLM Reasoning by Optimizing Reward Functions with Search-Driven Reinforcement Learning"** ([2605.02073](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.02073)) — Treats reward specification itself as an optimization object; candidate reward functions are generated by a frontier LLM and screened through GRPO training runs on Llama-3.2-3B-Instruct. The first formalization of **reward-function-as-search-target** outside ProcessBench-style PRM work.
3. **"ResRL: Boosting LLM Reasoning via Negative Sample Projection Residual Reinforcement Learning"** ([2605.00380](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.00380)) — RLVR's known failure mode is **diversity collapse** under positive-reward over-incentivization; ResRL projects negative-sample residuals to **decouple positive/negative semantic distributions**, restoring diversity without losing reasoning gains.
4. **"AlphaEvolve impact retrospective"** ([DeepMind blog](https://deepmind.google/blog/alphaevolve-impact/)) — One-year-on review (carry from Friday): **30% reduction in DeepConsensus DNA-variant-detection errors**; **~10× lower error in Willow quantum-circuit molecular sims**; **multiple Erdős-problem solutions with Terence Tao**; **double-digit-percent efficiency gains** in production Google data-center / chip-design / training-pipeline workloads.
