## Research Highlights

### Top 5 Must-Read Papers from arXiv

**1. Structured Reasoning for Large Language Models** ([arXiv:2601.07180](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.07180))
Proposes Structured Reasoning (SCR) framework that decouples reasoning trajectories into explicit, evaluable, trainable components. Reduces output token length by up to **50%** while maintaining or improving accuracy.

**2. PROMISE: Process Reward Models Unlock Test-Time Scaling Laws** ([arXiv:2601.04674](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.04674))
Demonstrates test-time scaling laws apply to recommender systems - smaller models can match or surpass larger models through increased inference compute.

**3. Batch-of-Thought: Cross-Instance Learning** ([arXiv:2601.02950](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.02950))
Training-free method processing related queries jointly. Reduces inference costs by up to **61%** while improving accuracy.

**4. SAILS: Interpretable Safety Alignment** ([arXiv:2512.23260](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.23260))
Uses Sparse Autoencoders to construct interpretable safety subspace. Achieves **99.6% safety rate** on Gemma-2-9B while updating only **0.19% of parameters**.

**5. MedVL-SAM2: Unified 3D Medical Vision-Language Model** ([arXiv:2601.09879](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.09879))
First comprehensive 3D medical VLM unifying report generation, VQA, and multi-paradigm segmentation in single framework.

### Additional Notable Papers

**Energy Efficiency Scaling** ([arXiv:2601.08991](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.08991))
Energy efficiency decreases log-linearly with parameter count in transformers.

**The Art of Scaling Test-Time Compute** ([arXiv:2512.02008](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.02008))
First large-scale study across 30B+ tokens, 8 models showing no universal test-time scaling strategy.

**Continuous-Depth Transformers** ([arXiv:2601.10007](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.10007))
Learned control dynamics with 98%/88% sentiment steering accuracy.
