## Research Highlights

From the April 27 arXiv digest (`research/arxiv/2026-04-27-papers.md`):

- **Hyperloop Transformers** ([2604.21254](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21254)) — looped Transformers reuse layers across depth; competitive quality at far fewer parameters by treating loop iteration as the primary scaling axis. Serious challenger to vanilla depth-stacking.
- **LLM Reasoning Is Latent, Not the Chain of Thought** ([2604.15726](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.15726)) — position paper arguing reasoning is latent-state trajectory, not the literal CoT tokens models emit. Reframes a year of "faithfulness" debates and pushes interpretability work toward hidden states.
- **Coding Agents Are Effective Long-Context Processors** ([2603.20432](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.20432)) — externalizing long-context into explicit file-system manipulation outperforms published SOTA by 17.3% on average across long-context QA, RAG, reasoning. Signals "agent + tools > longer context window" for many real workloads.
- **AutoRule** ([2506.15651](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.15651)) — fully automated rule-based reward extraction from CoT traces; Llama-3-8B trained with AutoRule sees +28.6% on AlpacaEval 2.0 length-controlled win rate. Cuts the human-annotation bottleneck in RLHF.
- **R2IF** ([2604.20316](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.20316)) — composite rewards align CoT with tool-call decisions; moves agent training past "did the call succeed?" toward auditable reasoning.
- **AgentSearchBench** ([2604.22436](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.22436)) — benchmark over ~10k real-world AI agents shows semantic similarity ≠ agent performance; description-based ranking under-rates capable agents.
- **Conductor** (Sakana AI / hardmaru, ICLR 2026) — RL-trained orchestrator learns to write custom instructions for a pool of frontier models, dynamically routing simple queries to a single model and spinning up planner/coder/verifier pipelines for hard problems.
