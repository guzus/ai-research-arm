### OpenAI–Microsoft partnership restructured — Azure exclusivity ends, IP license through 2032
Reuters reports Microsoft and OpenAI have amended their foundational partnership: (a) **Microsoft no longer pays a revenue share to OpenAI**; (b) **OpenAI can serve customers across any cloud provider** — breaking Azure exclusivity; (c) Microsoft retains a **non-exclusive license to OpenAI IP through 2032**; (d) OpenAI→Microsoft revenue-share payments continue through 2030. **MSFT −4% intraday**; QCOM +12%, ARM +14% on the correlated edge-AI/smartphone-chip move. ### Musk v. Altman opens — Brockman diary unsealed
Jury selection begins this morning at the U.S. District Court for the Northern District of California, Oakland Division, before **Judge Yvonne Gonzalez Rogers**. Opening statements Tuesday April 28; trial expected to run ~4 weeks; verdict possibly mid-May. **9 jurors with no alternates.** Time: ~20 hours each Musk/OpenAI; 5 hours Microsoft. Witness list: **Musk, Altman, Brockman, Satya Nadella** — Musk could take the stand as early as Tuesday.

Surviving claims (after April 24 narrowing): **(1) breach of charitable trust, (2) unjust enrichment.** All 26 fraud claims dropped at Musk's request; punitive damages barred. ## Research Highlights

Top arXiv papers from 2026-04-27:

- **Hyperloop Transformers** ([2604.21254](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21254)) — Looped-Transformer architecture that reuses the same layers across depth instead of stacking unique ones; competitive quality at far fewer parameters by treating loop iteration as the primary scaling axis. A serious challenger to vanilla depth-stacking.
- **LLM Reasoning Is Latent, Not the Chain of Thought** ([2604.15726](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.15726)) — Position paper arguing surface CoT is often unfaithful to the actual computation; interpretability should target hidden states, not generated rationales.
- **Coding Agents are Effective Long-Context Processors** ([2603.20432](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.20432)) — Externalizes long-context handling from latent attention into explicit file-system manipulation; +17.3% over published SOTA on long-context QA, RAG, and reasoning. Implies "agent + tools" beats "longer context window" for many real workloads.
- **AutoRule** ([2506.15651](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.15651)) — Reasoning-CoT-extracted rule-based rewards for preference learning. Llama-3-8B sees +28.6% relative gain on AlpacaEval 2.0 length-controlled win rate. Cuts the human-annotation bottleneck in RLHF.
- **R2IF** ([2604.20316](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.20316)) — RL framework that ties tool-call decisions to faithful reasoning traces via composite rewards — auditable agents.

Other notable:
- **SpikingBrain2.0** ([2604.22575](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.22575)) — brain-inspired foundation models for efficient long-context and cross-platform inference
- **PermaFrost-Attack: Stealth Pretraining Seeding** ([2604.22117](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.22117)) — planting "logic landmines" during LLM training; new threat surface for supply-chain alignment
- **Removing Sandbagging in LLMs by Training with Weak Supervision** ([2604.22082](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.22082))
- **Brief Chatbot Interactions Produce Lasting Changes in Human Moral Values** ([2604.21430](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21430)) — measurable persistent shifts in users' moral judgments after short LLM chats
- **Involuntary In-Context Learning** ([2604.19461](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.19461)) — new jailbreak class on GPT-5.4 exploiting unintended ICL pattern completion
- **Spontaneous Persuasion** ([2604.22109](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.22109)) — audit of model persuasiveness in everyday conversations
