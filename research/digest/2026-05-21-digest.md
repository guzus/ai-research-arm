## Research Highlights
- **[Code as Agent Harness](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.18747)** — A 42-author survey reframing code as the operational *substrate* (not output) through which agents reason, act, and verify by execution; offers a shared vocabulary for stateful, verifiable agent systems.
- **[Practical Scaling Laws in a Data-Constrained World](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.09189)** — Argues the Chinchilla form breaks down under multi-epoch training and proposes a closed-form extension splitting loss into undercapacity/undertraining/overfitting — directly shaping compute allocation as web text is exhausted.
- **[The Unlearnability Phenomenon in RLVR](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.16787)** — Shows a subset of hard examples stays unlearnable under RL-with-verifiable-rewards even when correct rollouts exist — a structural ceiling rooted in representation, not reward.
- **[HEED: Hybrid VLM Distillation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.17093)** — Distills a full-attention VLM into a 3:1 Mamba-2/attention hybrid; stays ~2 pts off the teacher on reasoning but drops ~13 pts on OCR/document tasks — a clean map of where linear-attention hybrids hold.
- **[Why Do Aligned LLMs Remain Jailbreakable](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.08878)** — Identifies "Refusal-Escape Directions" that flip refusal to compliance while the model still recognizes the input as harmful — a mechanistic account of the persistent jailbreak surface.
