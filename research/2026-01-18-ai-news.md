### Multi-State Legal Action Against xAI Grok Over Deepfakes
**Dates:** January 14-17, 2026

Multiple state attorneys general are threatening legal action against xAI and its Grok chatbot over the generation of nonconsensual sexually explicit deepfake images.

**Michigan Attorney General Action (January 14-17):**
- Michigan Attorney General Dana Nessel is threatening potential legal action against Elon Musk and xAI if it does not disable a feature on its Grok chatbot allegedly being used to produce illegal deepfake pornography
- Nessel stated that a "spicy mode" feature on Grok has been allowing users to easily manipulate images of real people to "remove" clothing or sexualize individuals without consent
- She characterized this by saying "This is a feature, not a bug" regarding Grok's controversial mode
- Multiple attorneys general across the country are now pressing xAI to disable the feature
- Michigan has a deepfake statute from August 2025

**Ashley St. Clair Lawsuit Developments (January 17):**
- Ashley St. Clair, a mother of Elon Musk's 16-month-old son, has taken xAI to court, claiming its Grok chatbot created and spread sexually explicit deepfake images of her, including one allegedly built from a photo taken when she was 14
- xAI has yanked the case into federal court and filed its own countersuit in Texas

**Company Response:**
- Elon Musk's X says its AI chatbot Grok won't be able to edit photos of real people in revealing clothing in places where that is illegal
- xAI has limited image creation or editing to paid subscribers only "to ensure that individuals who attempt to abuse the Grok account to violate the law or our policies can be held accountable"

## Infrastructure & Energy

### Meta Announces 6.6 GW Nuclear Energy Deals for AI
**Date:** January 9, 2026

Meta announced landmark agreements to extend and expand the operation of three nuclear power plants, boost the development of new advanced nuclear technology, and foster job growth in several American communities. The three deals will support up to 6.6 gigawatts of new and existing clean energy by 2035.

**Scale:**
- A single gigawatt can power about 750,000 homes, meaning this represents enough energy to power approximately 5 million homes
- Meta's agreements with Vistra, TerraPower, and Oklo — along with one signed with Constellation Energy last year — make Meta one of the most significant corporate purchasers of nuclear energy in American history

**Key Partners:**

**Vistra:** Meta will buy more than 2.1 gigawatts of energy from two operating Vistra nuclear power plants in Ohio, plus energy from expansions at the two Ohio plants and a third Vistra plant, Beaver Valley, near Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania.

**Oklo:** The deal with Oklo will help to develop a 1.2 gigawatt power campus in Pike County, Ohio, to support Meta's data centers in the region. This advanced nuclear technology campus is expected to come online as soon as 2030.

**TerraPower:** Meta's deal with TerraPower will provide funding for two nuclear projects that could begin generating power by 2032, and Meta could obtain rights for more energy from up to six of TerraPower's other nuclear energy projects targeted for delivery by 2035.

**Purpose:**
- These energy deals are planning to eventually power Meta's Prometheus supercluster computing system being built at a data center in New Albany, Ohio
- Meta expects Prometheus to come online sometime in 2026
- The deals mark Meta's efforts to secure the energy needed to power its AI infrastructure as the company marches toward CEO Mark Zuckerberg's goal of developing superintelligence

## Product Launches & Updates

### Google Veo 3.1 Major Update
**Date:** January 13, 2026

Google announced a major update to Veo 3.1, introducing several significant improvements to its AI video generation model.

**Key New Features:**

**Native Vertical Video Support:**
- Veo 3.1 now supports native vertical (9:16) video output for platforms like YouTube Shorts
- Eliminates the need for post-production cropping and optimizes content for mobile-first viewing

**4K Resolution Upscaling:**
- Upscaling to 1080p and 4K resolution is available for sharper, high-fidelity video production
- Veo 3.1 is the only mainstream AI video generation model currently supporting 4K output, while OpenAI Sora 2's maximum resolution remains at 1080p

**Enhanced "Ingredients to Video":**
- The update improves "Ingredients to Video," which lets you create videos based on reference images
- Veo 3.1 now generates videos with better character expressions and movements, even with shorter prompts
- Improves character, object, and background consistency

**Improved Character Consistency:**
- Identity consistency is better than ever, allowing characters to look the same even as the setting changes
- Makes it easier to tell a full narrative across multiple scenes

**Availability:**
- You can try Veo 3.1 in the Gemini app, YouTube, Flow, Google Vids, the Gemini API, and Vertex AI
- High-resolution outputs are particularly available for enterprise users through professional tools
