### Federal Agencies Begin Shedding Anthropic Contracts Following Trump's Directive
**Date:** March 7–8, 2026 | **Sources:** [Nextgov/FCW](https://www.nextgov.com/acquisition/2026/03/agencies-begin-shed-anthropic-contracts-following-trumps-directive/411823/), [FedScoop](https://fedscoop.com/anthropic-claude-dod-federal-agency-fallout-trump-hegseth/), [Technology.org](https://www.technology.org/2026/03/03/us-government-dumps-anthropic-state-treasury-and-hhs-jump-ship-to-openai/), [CNN](https://www.cnn.com/2026/02/27/tech/anthropic-pentagon-deadline)

The fallout from the Pentagon's supply chain risk designation has spread beyond defense to civilian agencies:
- The Department of Treasury, State Department, and Health and Human Services have confirmed they will stop using Anthropic technology.
- William Pulte, head of the Federal Housing Finance Agency, announced his bureau along with Fannie Mae and Freddie Mac are dropping Anthropic.
- The General Services Administration terminated Anthropic's OneGov deal, ending availability across Executive, Legislative, and Judicial branches through GSA's pre-negotiated contracts.
- The Trump administration has drafted new AI contract rules requiring contractors to deliver "a neutral, non-partisan tool that does not manipulate responses in favour of ideological dogmas such as diversity, equity, inclusion."
- Anthropic has a six-month runway to be phased out of federal workflows.

### Anthropic Revenue Surges to $19B Run Rate Despite Government Crackdown

Despite the government ban, Anthropic's commercial business is booming:
- Revenue run rate has surged to approximately $19 billion, up from $14 billion just weeks ago — driven by the massive consumer influx following the Pentagon controversy.
- Investors including Amazon CEO Andy Jassy, Lightspeed, and Iconiq are in active contact with Anthropic executives and reaching out to Trump administration contacts to de-escalate.
- The Information Technology Industry Council (members include Nvidia, Amazon, Apple, and OpenAI) sent a letter expressing concern over the supply chain risk designation.
- OpenAI's Connie LaRossa stated at an Aspen Digital conference: "Our red lines were the same as Anthropic's" and said OpenAI is "actually working to have the secure risk designation removed from Anthropic."
- Amodei told investors at the Morgan Stanley conference that Anthropic is still in talks with the Pentagon "to try to deescalate the situation."

**Date:** March 8, 2026 | **Sources:** [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/08/will-the-pentagons-anthropic-controversy-scare-startups-away-from-defense-work/), [Defense One](https://www.defenseone.com/technology/2026/03/meet-startups-trying-build-military-specific-ai/411969/)

The broader implications of the Anthropic-Pentagon dispute are now reverberating across the defense tech ecosystem:
- TechCrunch's Equity podcast debated whether startups are "taking pause about whether they want to be going after federal dollars."

### Benioff vs. Dorsey: CEOs Clash Over AI-Driven Mass Layoffs
**Date:** March 8, 2026 | **Sources:** [Benzinga](https://www.benzinga.com/markets/tech/26/03/51115886/marc-benioff-said-mass-ai-layoffs-werent-coming-then-came-a-brutal-week-for-white-collar-jobs), [LatestLY](https://www.latestly.com/technology/salesforce-ceo-marc-benioff-dismisses-block-layoffs-as-company-specific-issues-rather-than-ai-driven-industry-trend-7346527.html), [Fortune](https://fortune.com/2026/02/27/block-jack-dorsey-ceo-xyz-stock-square-4000-ai-layoffs/)

A public debate between two major tech CEOs over AI's impact on employment intensified over the weekend:
- Salesforce CEO Marc Benioff on March 8 dismissed warnings of mass AI layoffs as overblown, calling Block's 40% workforce cut (4,000 employees) a "company-specific" problem.
- Block CEO Jack Dorsey countered that "most companies are late" and will make "similar structural changes" within the next year.

- Goldman Sachs estimates AI is eliminating only 5,000–10,000 jobs per month across all U.S. sectors.

### Apple's Gemini-Powered Siri Overhaul Delayed Beyond iOS 26.4
**Date:** Reported February 11, resurfacing March 8–9, 2026 | **Sources:** [9to5Mac](https://9to5mac.com/2026/02/11/apple-reportedly-pushing-back-gemini-powered-siri-features-beyond-ios-26-4/), [MacRumors](https://www.macrumors.com/2026/02/11/siri-features-delayed-ios-26-4/), [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/02/11/apples-siri-revamp-reportedly-delayed-again/)

Apple's AI-powered Siri revamp, originally targeted for March 2026 with iOS 26.4, has been pushed back:
- Engineers found Siri sometimes fails to process requests correctly or takes too long, and occasionally falls back to ChatGPT instead of the intended Gemini-powered technology.
- Features including expanded personal data access and voice control of in-app actions are being spread across iOS 26.5 (May) and iOS 27 (September).
- Apple is paying Google approximately $1 billion annually to license Gemini models, with all personal data processed on-device via Apple's Foundation Models.
- Bloomberg reports the situation is "fluid" and executives are reluctant to push the overhaul past spring 2026.

### February Jobs Report Reignites AI Employment Fears
**Date:** March 6–8, 2026 | **Sources:** [BLS](https://www.bls.gov/news.release/empsit.nr0.htm), [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/06/february-2026-jobs-report.html), [Indeed Hiring Lab](https://www.hiringlab.org/2026/03/06/february-2026-jobs-report-overwhelmingly-disappointing/)

### Florida's "AI Bill of Rights" Stalls as House Blocks DeSantis-Backed Bill
**Date:** March 6–8, 2026 | **Sources:** [Florida Phoenix](https://floridaphoenix.com/2026/03/04/desantis-ai-bill-of-rights-clears-senate-but-house-wont-touch-it/), [WUSF](https://www.wusf.org/politics-issues/2026-03-06/florida-senate-approves-ai-bill-of-rights-remains-halted-in-house), [Orlando Sentinel](https://www.orlandosentinel.com/2026/03/06/desantis-heightens-push-for-ai-rules-as-bill-falters/)

Florida's AI Bill of Rights (SB 482) passed the Senate 35-2 but is dead on arrival in the House:
- The bill would ban companion chatbots from speaking to minors without parental consent, require AI disclosure in political ads, and prohibit state agencies from contracting with AI firms tied to "foreign countries of concern."
- House Speaker Perez, aligned with Trump, refuses to bring the bill to the floor, saying AI regulation should be handled federally.
- The Trump administration reached out to Perez directly to oppose the bill.
- DeSantis held a roundtable on March 6 urging the House to act before the session ends March 13, arguing: "There is no federal rule, guys. There's nothing."
- The bill's House sponsor said: "My AI bill is not going to be heard."

With several state legislatures nearing adjournment, a flurry of AI bills are advancing:
