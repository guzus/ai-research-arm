## Regulatory Updates

### DOJ Creates AI Litigation Task Force (January 9, 2026)
The Justice Department announced creation of an AI Litigation Task Force to challenge state-level AI regulations, allowing AI companies to "be free to innovate without cumbersome regulation." The task force will challenge state laws on grounds they are illegal, unconstitutionally regulate interstate commerce, or are overridden by federal regulations. Senate Democrats led by Sen. Ed Markey introduced legislation to block the executive order.

**Source:**
- [CBS News - DOJ Creates Task Force to Challenge State AI Regulations](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/doj-creates-task-force-to-challenge-state-ai-regulations/)

### Trump Executive Order Challenges State AI Laws (December 2025 - Impact in January 2026)
President Trump's December 11, 2025 Executive Order titled "Ensuring a National Policy Framework for Artificial Intelligence" seeks to establish federal preemption over state AI laws. The order aims to encourage AI innovation, reduce barriers to development, lessen inconsistencies in state regulation, and target laws deemed to "embed ideological bias within models." The EO does not presently invalidate state/local AI laws but marks escalation in federal efforts to create a national framework.

**Source:**
- [King & Spalding - New State AI Laws and Executive Order](https://www.kslaw.com/news-and-insights/new-state-ai-laws-are-effective-on-january-1-2026-but-a-new-executive-order-signals-disruption)

### State AI Laws Taking Effect (January 1, 2026)
Despite federal challenges, multiple state AI laws went into effect:

**California:**
- **SB 53**: Transparency in Frontier Artificial Intelligence Act
- **AB 489**: Prohibits AI chatbots from presenting as licensed medical professionals (doctors, nurses, etc.)
- **SB 524**: Requires law enforcement to disclose when AI tools draft police reports
- Companion chatbot safety requirements (precautions against suicidal ideation/self-harm content, disclosure requirements for minors)

**Texas:**

**Colorado:**
- Colorado AI Act implementation postponed from February 1 to June 30, 2026

**Other States:**
38 states passed AI legislation in 2025 covering election integrity, medical information regulation, and algorithmic discrimination.
