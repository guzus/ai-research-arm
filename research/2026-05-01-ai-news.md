### White House **OPPOSES** Anthropic's Plan to Widen Mythos Access — First Direct Trump-Administration Intervention in a Frontier-Lab Product Roadmap
**Date:** Wednesday night, April 29, 2026 (broke late evening; full coverage April 30) | **Sources:** [Bloomberg — White House Opposes Anthropic's Mythos AI Expansion, WSJ Reports](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-30/white-house-opposes-anthropic-plan-for-mythos-access-wsj-says), [Yahoo Finance — Anthropic Plan to Expand Mythos Access Is Opposed by White House](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/anthropic-plan-to-expand-mythos-access-is-opposed-by-white-house-033018504.html), [The Next Web — Trump administration blocks Anthropic's Mythos rollout](https://thenextweb.com/news/white-house-opposes-anthropic-mythos-expansion), [Political Wire — White House Opposes Plan to Expand Access to Mythos](https://politicalwire.com/2026/04/30/white-house-opposes-plan-to-expand-access-to-mythos/), [Security Boulevard — White House Pushes Back Against Anthropic's Mythos Expansion](https://securityboulevard.com/2026/04/white-house-pushes-back-against-anthropics-mythos-expansion/), [Crypto Briefing — White House blocks Anthropic's Mythos expansion over security concerns](https://cryptobriefing.com/white-house-blocks-anthropics-mythos-expansion-over-security-concerns/), [Blockonomi — Trump Administration Halts Anthropic's Mythos AI Expansion](https://blockonomi.com/trump-administration-halts-anthropics-mythos-ai-expansion-plans-over-security-fears/), [Seeking Alpha](https://seekingalpha.com/news/4582781-white-house-objects-to-anthropics-plan-to-expand-access-to-mythos-report)

The **Trump White House publicly told Anthropic it does not agree** with the company's plan to widen access to **Claude Mythos** — Anthropic's Project-Glasswing-gated cybersecurity-capable frontier model — to **~70 additional companies and organizations** beyond the original April 7 Glasswing cohort (Amazon, Microsoft, Google, NVIDIA, ~46 others). The objection broke late Wednesday April 29 (WSJ original, picked up by Bloomberg overnight), and by Thursday morning had become the day's dominant federal-AI-policy story.

- **Stated administration concerns (two-fold):**
  1. **Misuse risk:** Mythos's vulnerability-discovery and exploit-development capabilities — described by Anthropic at April 7 launch as "the most powerful Anthropic has ever produced for cybersecurity applications" — could imperil **U.S. critical infrastructure** if leaked from a wider 70-org rollout.
  2. **Compute capacity:** Administration officials told Anthropic they are concerned the company **may not have enough compute headroom** to serve a meaningfully larger customer base **without degrading the existing government-tier access** that the **NSA and other Intelligence Community customers** currently rely on.
- **NSA breach context:** A "recent breach during the model's initial rollout" — first disclosed in late-April reporting — has heightened administration anxiety about Mythos OPSEC. NSA is already an active Mythos government user.
- **Anthropic posture:** Anthropic has not formally accepted or rejected the White House intervention as of Thursday close. The original April 7 Project Glasswing public-safety review process was understood internally to give Anthropic latitude to expand the cohort over time as safety telemetry matured.

**Read-through:** This is the **first time the Trump administration has publicly intervened in a frontier-lab product-distribution decision** — a **structural escalation** beyond the March 20 White House National AI Policy Framework's preemption-of-state-laws posture. Three implications: (1) **The April 30 administration objection materially changes the federal-policy backdrop for the Anthropic ~$900B funding round** disclosed Wednesday night — investor models that priced "Mythos enterprise expansion in 2H 2026" as part of Anthropic's ARR trajectory now must discount it. (2) Combined with the **April 24 Anthropic–Google $40B / 5 GW TPU commitment** and the **April 29 Amazon 5 GW Trainium disclosure**, the compute-capacity concern is **materially weaker than the misuse-risk concern** — but the administration's choice to lead with both suggests **the White House is asserting jurisdiction over distribution decisions even at frontier labs not under direct contract**. (3) For OpenAI and Google DeepMind, Mythos-equivalent capabilities (GPT-5.5 Cyber, Gemini 3.1 Pro Cyber) face the same regulatory ceiling — **expect parallel administration outreach to OpenAI within 2 weeks**, especially since Sam Altman publicly criticized Anthropic's Mythos rollout in mid-April.

**Read-through:** A **$100B IPO valuation for a not-yet-IPO'd robotics-construction spinout** is the **biggest pre-IPO valuation print of 2026 so far** and **doubles the size of CoreWeave's IPO peak** as a public AI-infrastructure listing. (3) For competitors **Bezos Project Prometheus, AMD, NVIDIA's robotics-OEM ecosystem (Foxconn, Aible)**, Roze's emergence is **simultaneously a partner and a moat threat**: SoftBank-owned ABB will compete head-to-head with NVIDIA's GR00T-built humanoid construction robots in Texas/Wyoming Stargate sites by 2027.

The U.S. Department of Defense (now branded "Department of War" on its press releases) announced Friday morning that **eight major tech firms** have signed agreements to deploy their AI products inside DoD's **Impact Level 6 (IL-6) and Impact Level 7 (IL-7) classified networks** for "lawful operational use." This is the **largest single-day federal-AI procurement announcement in the Trump second term** and the **single most consequential federal-AI policy news event of May 2026 so far.**

- **Companies cleared (initial 7 + 1 added later in the day):** **Amazon Web Services, Google, Microsoft, NVIDIA, OpenAI, Reflection, SpaceX** — and **Oracle** added later Friday.
- **Anthropic POINTEDLY EXCLUDED.** This is the **first formal U.S. federal-government procurement event since OpenAI's ChatGPT Enterprise launch where Anthropic is structurally locked out** — despite Claude already being live on classified networks via **Palantir's Maven toolkit** (which is unaffected by today's announcement).
- **Stated use cases:** "Streamline data synthesis, elevate situational understanding, and augment warfighter decision-making in complex operational environments." DoD framed the multi-vendor structure as deliberately preventing **vendor lock-in** — a direct repudiation of the OpenAI-Microsoft single-vendor pattern that dominated 2024–2025 federal-AI procurement.
- **DoD CTO Emil Michael (CNBC video interview, Friday morning):** *"Anthropic is still blacklisted"* as a **supply-chain risk** to U.S. national security — the designation that triggered Anthropic's **March 2026 lawsuit** against the Trump administration. **But** Michael added that **Mythos is "a separate national-security moment"** — the government must **harden networks** against Mythos's vulnerability-discovery and exploit-development capabilities **regardless** of the procurement spat. Michael said Mythos is being handled **government-wide**, not just at DoD.
- **Reflection — the dark-horse name on the list:** Reflection AI is the smallest and youngest firm among the 8; it raised **$2B in October 2025** and is backed by **1789 Capital** (the Trump-Jr.-affiliated VC firm). Reflection's inclusion alongside Microsoft / Google / AWS validates the firm's federal-procurement positioning and is **the single most material capital-markets impact** of today's announcement for a pre-IPO AI lab.
- **Anthropic context:** Talks broke down earlier in 2026 after Anthropic insisted on contractual carve-outs **prohibiting (i) fully autonomous weapons applications and (ii) domestic mass-surveillance use**. The Pentagon refused those restrictions; Anthropic was designated supply-chain risk; Anthropic sued; a federal judge **partially enjoined** the designation in March (CNN coverage); the Pentagon today re-affirmed it informally via Michael's CNBC interview.

**Read-through:** Today's announcement **completes the structural picture** that began with the **April 29 White House intervention against Anthropic's Mythos cohort expansion** and the **April 28 OpenAI-on-AWS Bedrock launch**: the Trump administration has now **bifurcated** the federal-AI procurement landscape into (a) **eight cleared "preferred vendors"** with classified-network access and unrestricted use rights, and (b) **Anthropic, alone outside the tent**, with one government foothold (Mythos cyber, via Palantir Maven) but no IL-6/IL-7 procurement vehicle. Three implications: (1) **Anthropic's $900B-valuation round** (announced April 29; allocation deadline now reportedly **48 hours** per TechCrunch — see next item) must be re-priced **down** to reflect the loss of the U.S. federal-government enterprise vertical, which Anthropic had been targeting as the next major ARR leg. (2) **Reflection AI is now the structurally most important "Trump-affiliated frontier lab"** in U.S. AI policy — and the **single biggest beneficiary** of Anthropic's ostracization. Its $2B October 2025 round was prescient. (3) **Michael's "Mythos is a separate national-security moment" framing** is the **first formal Trump-administration acknowledgment that frontier-LLM cyber capabilities have crossed a defensive-posture threshold** — expect parallel federal "harden the networks" guidance to land within 30 days, with downstream procurement implications for **CrowdStrike, Palo Alto, Wiz, SentinelOne**, and the cyber-incident-response SaaS layer broadly.
