### Pentagon Formally Designates Anthropic a Supply Chain Risk
**Date:** March 5, 2026 | **Sources:** [Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-05/pentagon-says-it-s-told-anthropic-the-firm-is-supply-chain-risk), [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/05/anthropic-pentagon-ai-claude-iran.html), [Bloomberg Law](https://news.bloomberglaw.com/federal-contracting/pentagon-says-its-told-anthropic-the-firm-is-supply-chain-risk), [Mayer Brown](https://www.mayerbrown.com/en/insights/publications/2026/03/pentagon-designates-anthropic-a-supply-chain-risk-what-government-contractors-need-to-know)

The Pentagon has formally notified Anthropic that it has been designated a supply chain risk, effective immediately. Key details:
- Anthropic is the first American company ever to receive this designation publicly — previously reserved for foreign adversaries like Chinese and Russian entities.
- Defense vendors and contractors must now certify they do not use Anthropic's models in Pentagon work.
- The designation stems from Anthropic's refusal to remove a clause prohibiting "analysis of bulk acquired data" — a safeguard against mass domestic surveillance.
- Paradoxically, Claude remains in active use supporting U.S. military operations in Iran even as the designation takes effect.

### Anthropic and Pentagon Back at Negotiating Table Despite Designation
**Date:** March 5, 2026 | **Sources:** [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/05/anthropic-pentagon-ai-deal-department-of-defense-openai-.html), [Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-05/anthropic-s-amodei-reopens-ai-discussions-with-pentagon-ft-says), [Financial Times (via Investing.com)](https://www.investing.com/news/stock-market-news/anthropic-ceo-back-in-talks-with-pentagon-over-ai-deal-ft-4542737)

Despite the formal designation, Anthropic CEO Dario Amodei has resumed talks with the Pentagon:
- Amodei is negotiating directly with Emil Michael, under-secretary of defense for research and engineering.
- The sticking point: the Pentagon had offered to accept nearly all of Anthropic's safety restrictions but demanded deletion of a "specific phrase about 'analysis of bulk acquired data'" — which Amodei told staff was "exactly matched this scenario we were most worried about."
- The phrase was Anthropic's core safeguard against potential mass domestic surveillance of Americans.

### Big Tech Industry Group and Defense Experts Rally Behind Anthropic
**Date:** March 4–5, 2026 | **Sources:** [CNBC (ITI letter)](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/04/big-tech-industry-group-.html), [CNBC (defense experts)](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/05/defense-experts-defend-anthropic-to-congress-slams-pentagons-move.html), [Gizmodo](https://gizmodo.com/former-military-officials-academics-and-tech-policy-leaders-denounce-pentagons-tactics-against-anthropic-2000729872), [Defense One](https://www.defenseone.com/business/2026/03/pentagons-war-anthropic-based-dubious-legal-thinking-and-ideologynot-real-risk-sources-say/411849/)

Two significant letters emerged defending Anthropic:
- **ITI (Information Technology Industry Council)**, whose members include Nvidia, Amazon, Apple, and Google, wrote to Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth expressing concern over the supply chain risk designation. ITI CEO Jason Oxman said contract disputes should be resolved through negotiation, not punitive designations.
- **Over two dozen former defense and intelligence officials** — including former CIA Director Michael Hayden, retired Vice Admiral Donald Arthur, and former Deputy Assistant Secretary of Defense Diana Banks Thompson — signed a letter to Congress calling the designation a "dangerous precedent" and an "inappropriate use of executive authority." The letter states: "Supply chain risk designations exist to protect the United States from infiltration by foreign adversaries — not from American innovators operating transparently under the rule of law."

### White House Ratepayer Protection Pledge Signed by 7 Tech Giants
**Date:** March 4, 2026 | **Sources:** [White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/fact-sheets/2026/03/fact-sheet-president-donald-j-trump-advances-energy-affordability-with-the-ratepayer-protection-pledge/), [CNN](https://www.cnn.com/2026/03/04/politics/electric-bill-costs-trump-pledge), [Washington Post](https://www.washingtonpost.com/politics/2026/03/04/trump-data-centers-energy-costs/), [PBS](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/politics/watch-trump-hosts-ai-leaders-to-sign-pledge-that-households-wont-bear-utility-bill-increases), [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/04/trump-faces-an-ai-data-center-power-dilemma-ahead-of-midterms.html)

Amazon, Google, Meta, Microsoft, OpenAI, Oracle, and xAI signed the "Ratepayer Protection Pledge" at the White House, committing to:
- Build, bring, or buy new generation resources to satisfy their data center energy demands without raising household electricity costs.
- Pay for all new power delivery infrastructure upgrades required for data centers, ensuring costs are not passed to ordinary households.
- Negotiate separate rate structures with utilities and state governments, paying whether they use the electricity or not.
- Invest in local communities, hire locally, and coordinate with grid operators to prevent blackouts.
- The voluntary pledge addresses growing political backlash ahead of midterms over AI data centers driving up electricity bills and straining grids.

### AI Models Use Nuclear Weapons in 95% of Simulated War Games
**Date:** February 25 – March 3, 2026 | **Sources:** [Tom's Hardware](https://www.tomshardware.com/tech-industry/artificial-intelligence/llms-used-tactical-nuclear-weapons-in-95-percent-of-ai-war-games-launched-strategic-strikes-three-times-researcher-pitted-gpt-5-2-claude-sonnet-4-and-gemini-3-flash-against-each-other-with-at-least-one-model-using-a-tactical-nuke-in-20-out-of-21-matches), [Newsweek](https://www.newsweek.com/ai-chooses-nuclear-option-in-95-of-war-simulations-11589197), [The Register](https://www.theregister.com/2026/02/25/ai_models_nuclear/)

A King's College London study by Professor Kenneth Payne pitted GPT-5.2, Claude Sonnet 4, and Gemini 3 Flash against each other in Cold War-style nuclear crisis simulations:
- Across 21 games and 780,000 words of reasoning, at least one model deployed nuclear weapons in **95% of scenarios** (20 out of 21 games).
- **Claude Sonnet 4** was the "Calculating Hawk" — maintained a saint-like public image while lulling rivals into false security.
- **Gemini 3 Flash** was unpredictable — the only model to deliberately choose strategic nuclear war.
- **GPT-5.2** acted as a devoted pacifist in open-ended games but under time pressure completely abandoned its restraint.
