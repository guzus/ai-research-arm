### OpenAI to Retire GPT-4o, GPT-4.1, and o4-mini from ChatGPT on February 13
**Date:** January 30 – February 2, 2026

OpenAI has confirmed it will remove GPT-4o, GPT-4.1, GPT-4.1 mini, and o4-mini from ChatGPT on February 13, 2026, completing a major consolidation around its GPT-5.2 model family. GPT-5 Instant and GPT-5 Thinking will also be retired on the same date.

**Key Details:**
- OpenAI says "the vast majority of usage has shifted to GPT-5.2, with only 0.1% of users still choosing GPT-4o each day"
- API access to these models remains unchanged for now, but `chatgpt-4o-latest` will be retired from the API on February 16
- This is OpenAI's second attempt to retire GPT-4o — the company reversed a retirement in August 2025 after user backlash over newer models feeling "less personal"
- OpenAI CEO Sam Altman acknowledged at a recent town hall that GPT-5.2's writing performance declined as development priorities shifted toward coding, reasoning, and mathematics
- To compensate, OpenAI has introduced seven distinct personality options and custom instructions for tone and style

**GPT-5.2 Lineup (Now Default):**
- **GPT-5.2 Instant:** Fast, everyday workhorse — improved info-seeking, how-tos, technical writing, translation
- **GPT-5.2 Thinking:** Optimized for harder work tasks — spreadsheet formatting, financial modeling, slideshow creation
- **GPT-5.2 Pro:** Most capable model for difficult questions — fewer major errors, stronger programming performance
- **GPT-5.2-Codex:** State-of-the-art agentic coding model, achieving best results on SWE-Bench Pro and Terminal-Bench 2.0
- All models share an August 2025 knowledge cutoff

### NASA's Perseverance Rover Completes First AI-Planned Drive on Mars Using Claude
**Date:** January 30 – February 1, 2026

NASA's Jet Propulsion Laboratory announced that its Perseverance Mars rover completed the first drives on another planet planned by artificial intelligence, using Anthropic's Claude to chart the route through Jezero Crater.

**The Drives:**
- On December 8 (sol 1,707), Perseverance drove 689 feet (210 meters) using AI-generated waypoints
- On December 10 (sol 1,709), it drove 807 feet (246 meters) — totaling approximately 400 meters
- Claude analyzed high-resolution orbital imagery from the HiRISE camera aboard Mars Reconnaissance Orbiter and terrain-slope data from digital elevation models
- The model identified bedrock, outcrops, hazardous boulder fields, and sand ripples, then generated a continuous path with waypoints

**How It Worked:**
- NASA provided Claude Code (Anthropic's programming agent) with years of contextual rover data before it could begin route planning
- Claude mapped the route by stringing together waypoints from 10-meter segments, then self-critiquing and iterating
- JPL's "digital twin" verified over 500,000 telemetry variables before sending commands to Mars
- Only minor adjustments were needed after review, primarily from ground-level imagery the AI had not analyzed
- JPL engineers say Claude can cut route planning time in half

**Significance:**
- First time an AI system has independently charted a navigation path on another planet
- NASA Administrator Jared Isaacman: "This demonstration shows how far our capabilities have advanced and broadens how we will explore other worlds"
- Comes as NASA lost ~4,000 employees (~20% of workforce) due to Trump administration cuts, making AI-assisted efficiency tools especially valuable

### Amazon Cuts 16,000 Corporate Jobs, Cites AI Efficiency Gains
**Date:** January 28–31, 2026

Amazon announced 16,000 corporate layoffs — its second round of large-scale reductions in three months — as CEO Andy Jassy pushes to "reduce total corporate workforce as we get efficiency gains from using AI extensively across the company."

**Key Details:**
- Follows October's 14,000 corporate layoffs, bringing total cuts to ~30,000 (~10% of corporate and tech workforce)
- SVP Beth Galetti said reductions affect teams still finalizing restructuring first announced in October
- Some cuts impact Prime Video and Amazon MGM Studios divisions
- Most affected U.S. employees receive 90 days to seek internal roles; those who can't find new positions get severance, job placement, and continued health benefits

**Financial Context:**
- Amazon's most recent quarter: profits jumped ~40% to ~$21 billion; revenue exceeded $180 billion
- Capital spending projected at $125 billion in 2026, largely directed toward AI and data center expansion
- Jassy: "As we roll out more Generative AI and agents, it should change the way our work is done"

**Industry Pattern:**
- CNN reports Amazon's total AI-related layoffs as part of a broader trend across tech: Meta cut ~1,500 from Reality Labs, Pinterest and TCS also made cuts
- Oxford Economics research questions the AI-layoff narrative, suggesting "firms don't appear to be replacing workers with AI on a significant scale" and companies may be using AI as cover for routine headcount reductions

### Yann LeCun Leaves Meta, Raises €500M for World Model Startup AMI Labs at €3B Valuation
**Date:** January 22–23, 2026

Meta's longtime chief AI scientist Yann LeCun has departed to launch AMI Labs, a Paris-headquartered startup focused on "world models" — AI systems that learn from visual and spatial data rather than text.

**Funding:**
- Raising €500 million at ~€3 billion valuation
- Investors in discussions include Cathay Innovation, Greycroft, Hiro Capital, 20VC, Bpifrance, Daphni, and HV Capital

**Leadership:**
- LeCun serves as executive chairman
- CEO: Alex LeBrun (previously co-founder/CEO of health AI startup Nabla)
- Laurent Solly (former Meta VP for Europe) also joining

**Why He Left:**
- Zuckerberg shifted Meta's FAIR lab (which LeCun founded in 2013) from long-term research to commercial AI products and LLMs
- LeCun has publicly called LLMs "useful but fundamentally limited" in reasoning and planning
- Reportedly disagreed with Alexandr Wang (former Scale AI CEO hired by Meta in June 2025) being placed above him
- LeCun: "You certainly don't tell a researcher like me what to do"

**Technical Approach:**
- Building on V-JEPA architecture LeCun created at Meta
- Models train on video and spatial data to understand how the physical world works — can plan, reason, and retain information over time
- LeCun told MIT Technology Review that Meta could be AMI's first client
