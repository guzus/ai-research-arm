## Breaking News

### **Anthropic Publicly Argues For Hitting The Brakes — Days Before Its IPO: A New Paper Warns AI Is On Track For "Recursive Self-Improvement" (Task Ability Doubling ~Every Four Months), Discloses That >80% Of Code Merged Into Its Own Codebase Is Now Claude-Written, And Calls For A Coordinated, Cross-Border, *Verifiable* Multi-Lab Pause Mechanism; The Anthropic Institute Will Convene Policymakers, Researchers, Civil Society And Rival Labs In The Coming Months**

**Date:** Friday, June 5, 2026 | **Sources:** [CNBC — Anthropic warns of AI's rapid development, societal risk ahead of IPO](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/05/anthropic-warns-of-ais-rapid-development-societal-risk-ahead-of-ipo.html), [Fortune — Anthropic warns AI could soon build itself without human involvement—and urges a global pause](https://fortune.com/2026/06/05/anthropic-ai-pause-development-recursive-self-improvement/), [SiliconANGLE — Anthropic calls for global pause in AI development before humans lose control](https://siliconangle.com/2026/06/04/anthropic-calls-global-pause-ai-development-humans-lose-control/), [Quartz — Anthropic calls for coordinated AI slowdown or pause](https://qz.com/anthropic-ai-pause-brake-pedal-coordination-labs-060526), [Business Standard — Anthropic urges AI labs to pause development, warns of risks](https://www.business-standard.com/technology/tech-news/anthropic-urges-ai-labs-to-pause-development-warns-of-risks-126060501193_1.html), [Crypto Briefing — Anthropic calls for global pause over self-improvement risks](https://cryptobriefing.com/anthropic-global-pause-ai-self-improvement/)

- **The warning:** Anthropic published an account arguing its models may soon improve themselves **without meaningful human involvement** — the trajectory it labels **"recursive self-improvement,"** where AI systems autonomously design, build, and train their own successors. It cites AI's ability to complete tasks on its own **doubling roughly every four months**.
- **The internal data point:** **More than 80% of the code merged into Anthropic's own codebase is now written by Claude**, and engineers ship roughly **8x as much code per quarter** as before 2025 — offered as concrete evidence the self-improvement loop is already partially live inside frontier labs.
- **The proposal:** Any credible slowdown would require **multiple well-resourced labs at or near the frontier, across multiple countries, agreeing to stop under the same conditions**, with each able to **verify** the others have actually stopped — an explicit acknowledgment that a unilateral pause just cedes the lead. Anthropic's research arm, the **Anthropic Institute**, says it will study the systems needed to support a slowdown and **convene policymakers, researchers, civil society groups and rival AI firms** in the coming months.
- **The timing:** The paper lands while Anthropic is raising **$65B at a ~$965B valuation** (closed late May) and **days after filing a confidential IPO prospectus with the SEC** (June 1).

**Read-through:** This is the year's sharpest example of a frontier lab's commercial and safety postures pulling in opposite directions — Anthropic is simultaneously pitching public-market investors on Claude's accelerating capability and warning that same acceleration may outrun human control. The load-bearing word is **"verifiable"**: by framing a pause as a coordination/verification problem rather than a moral plea, Anthropic shifts the debate from "should anyone slow down" (which no single lab can afford) to "what enforcement and audit machinery would make a *mutual* slowdown stable" — the same logic underneath arms-control regimes. It also reads as IPO-risk-disclosure positioning (recursive self-improvement as a named, governed risk) and as a counterweight to the Great American AI Act's preemption fight: a lab voluntarily asking for the brakes is a strong card in the "who do labs owe what" debate now opening in Congress.

**Date:** Friday, June 5, 2026 | **Sources:** [TechCrunch — Google will pay SpaceX $920M per month for compute](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/05/google-will-pay-spacex-920m-per-month-for-compute/), [CNBC — Google to pay SpaceX $920 million a month for compute capacity at xAI data centers](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/05/google-to-pay-spacex-920-million-a-month-for-xai-compute-capacity.html), [SEC — SpaceX Form FWP (Cloud Service Agreement)](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/0001181412/000162828026041150/spacexagreementfwp.htm), [TechTimes — Google Will Pay SpaceX $920M A Month For NVIDIA GPU Capacity At xAI Data Centers](https://www.techtimes.com/articles/317914/20260606/google-will-pay-spacex-920-million-month-nvidia-gpu-capacity-xai-data-centers.htm), [The Next Web — Google to pay SpaceX $920M/month for AI compute](https://thenextweb.com/news/google-spacex-920-million-month-compute-deal)

- **The deal:** Per a June 5 SEC filing, SpaceX entered a **Cloud Service Agreement with Google LLC** under which Google pays **~$920M/month from October 2026 through June 2029** (capacity ramps up through September at a reduced fee) for access to **~110,000 NVIDIA GPUs, plus CPUs, memory and related components** — roughly **$32B** in total contract value.
- **Performance/termination terms:** If SpaceX **fails to deliver the committed GPU access by September 30, 2026**, then after a one-month grace period Google may terminate or accept a pro-rata-reduced fee for the GPUs actually provided. After **December 31, 2026**, either party may terminate on **90 days' notice**.
- **The infrastructure context:** SpaceX **merged with xAI earlier this year** and owns the two **Colossus** supercomputer centers in **Memphis** (a third on the way in Mississippi). This is its **second** hyperscaler offtake deal — it follows **Anthropic's ~$1.25B/month** commitment to rent all available compute from **Colossus 1**. Reporting frames the Google capacity as tied to xAI's move off **Colossus 1's mixed GPU fleet**.

**Read-through:** This cements SpaceX/xAI as a **neutral-ish compute landlord** to the very labs xAI competes with — Google and Anthropic are now both paying Elon Musk's merged entity for frontier capacity, an extraordinary inversion of the "build your own data centers" orthodoxy. For Google, leasing ~110k GPUs rather than waiting on its own buildout signals just how binding the **compute-and-power bottleneck** has become even for a hyperscaler with TPUs. The hard September-30 delivery milestone and the ~$32B headline make this one of the largest AI-compute rental contracts disclosed to date, and a direct datapoint for the "energy/compute-for-AI" megadeal theme running through this week's Helion and infrastructure stories.

## New Model Releases

### **OpenAI Rolls Out "Dreaming V3" — A Hierarchical ChatGPT Memory Architecture That Continuously Synthesizes Memory In The Background Across Years Of Conversations (No "Remember This" Needed), Replaces The Manually Curated Saved-Memories List, Cuts Memory-Serving Compute ~5x, And Begins June 4 With US Plus/Pro Users (Free Tier And More Countries To Follow)**

**Date:** Thursday, June 4, 2026 (broad coverage June 5) | **Sources:** [gHacks — OpenAI Upgrades ChatGPT Memory With New Dreaming Architecture for Plus and Pro Users](https://www.ghacks.net/2026/06/05/openai-upgrades-chatgpt-memory-with-new-dreaming-architecture-for-plus-and-pro-users/), [TechTimes — ChatGPT Memory Dreaming Update: OpenAI Rewrites Personalization Engine, Limits Audit Trail](https://www.techtimes.com/articles/317840/20260605/chatgpt-memory-dreaming-update-openai-rewrites-personalization-engine-limits-audit-trail.htm), [Windows News — ChatGPT Dreaming V3: New memory architecture for smarter, persistent AI](https://windowsnews.ai/article/chatgpt-dreaming-v3-new-memory-architecture-for-smarter-persistent-ai.422983), [Digital Applied — ChatGPT 'Dreaming V3' Memory: Self-Updating AI Recall](https://www.digitalapplied.com/blog/chatgpt-memory-dreaming-v3-openai-2026-guide), [The Cryptonomist — OpenAI ChatGPT Dreaming V3 memory upgrade: how it works](https://en.cryptonomist.ch/2026/06/05/openai-chatgpt-dreaming-v3-memory/)

- **What it is:** A **background synthesis process** ("dreaming") that learns across many conversations and continuously updates ChatGPT's memory state, capturing context that arises naturally **rather than waiting for an explicit "remember this"** instruction. It **replaces the manually curated saved-memories list** with a hierarchical architecture that reads across **years of past conversations**.
- **Efficiency:** A claimed **~5x reduction in the compute** required to serve memory features — the headroom OpenAI says lets it **expand memory capacity for Plus/Pro** and, for the first time, begin extending advanced memory to **Free** users.
- **User control:** A **memory summary page** lets users review what ChatGPT knows about them, correct specific details, and set topic priorities. (Some coverage flags a reduced/limited audit trail vs. the old explicit saved-memories list as the privacy tradeoff.)
- **Rollout:** Began **June 4, 2026** with **US ChatGPT Plus and Pro** subscribers; expansion to more countries, the Free tier, and enterprise "in the coming weeks."

**Read-through:** This is an infrastructure-grade change disguised as a settings tweak: moving from user-curated facts to autonomous, cross-session memory synthesis is the personalization analog of agentic autonomy — the system decides what's worth remembering. The ~5x compute cut is the enabling move (memory at ChatGPT's scale is a serving-cost problem), and pushing it to Free users is a retention/lock-in play as Gemini and Claude court the same users. The audit-trail tradeoff is the watch-item: continuous background synthesis is harder to inspect than a visible list, which will draw EU AI Act / data-rights scrutiny as it expands beyond the US.
