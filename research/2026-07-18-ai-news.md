### Microsoft CEO Satya Nadella Publicly Criticizes Anthropic's Fable Model as "Editorially Controlled"

**Date:** July 16-17, 2026 | **Sources:** [CNBC — Microsoft's Nadella criticizes Anthropic's Fable for being 'editorially controlled'](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/16/microsoft-ceo-says-anthropic-fable-request-policy-doesnt-make-sense.html), [BusinessToday — 'It doesn't make sense': Satya Nadella criticises Anthropic's Fable 5 control and restrictions](https://www.businesstoday.in/technology/artificial-intelligence/story/it-doesnt-make-sense-satya-nadella-criticises-anthropics-fable-5-control-and-restrictions-543532-2026-07-17), [Proactive Investors — Microsoft CEO Nadella criticizes Anthropic's Fable AI over refusals in internal Copilot meeting](https://www.proactiveinvestors.com/companies/news/1095644/microsoft-ceo-nadella-criticizes-anthropic-s-fable-ai-over-refusals-in-internal-copilot-meeting-1095644.html)
- In remarks to Microsoft Copilot engineers, later obtained by CNBC, Nadella said Fable's request-refusal behavior "doesn't make sense," asking: "when was the last time you had a creation tool that was so editorially controlled?" He also noted Anthropic sometimes routes flagged requests to an older model version rather than refusing outright.
- The criticism comes despite a deep commercial tie between the companies: Microsoft committed $5 billion to Anthropic in November while Anthropic pledged $30 billion toward Azure spend.
- **Why it matters:** It's an unusually blunt public shot from a major investor/infrastructure partner at Anthropic's content-moderation approach, surfacing tension between Microsoft's product ambitions for Copilot and Anthropic's safety posture on its flagship consumer-facing model.

## Funding & Acquisitions

### Fireworks AI Raises $1.505 Billion Series D at $17.5 Billion Valuation

**Date:** July 16, 2026 | **Sources:** [Business Wire — Fireworks Raises a $1.5 Billion Series D to Lead the Specialized Intelligence Revolution](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20260716264405/en/Fireworks-Raises-a-$1.5-Billion-Series-D-to-Lead-the-Specialized-Intelligence-Revolution), [Qz — Fireworks AI raises $1.5 billion Series D at $17.5 billion valuation](https://qz.com/fireworks-ai-series-d-fundraise-valuation-open-source-071626), [HPCwire/AIwire — Fireworks Raises $1.5B to Expand Enterprise AI Model Platform](https://www.hpcwire.com/aiwire/2026/07/16/fireworks-raises-1-5b-to-expand-enterprise-ai-model-platform/)
- Fireworks AI, the inference platform that serves 200+ models across text, image, and multimodal formats, closed a **$1.505 billion Series D** led by Atreides Management, Index Ventures, and TCV, with Nvidia, Lightspeed, Insight Partners, and 15+ other investors participating.
- The company says it has surpassed $1 billion in annualized revenue run rate (5x year-over-year) and now serves more than 40 trillion tokens/day, up from 15 trillion at its last raise; funds go toward engineering headcount and global compute capacity, deepening ties with Microsoft and Nvidia.
- **Why it matters:** It's one of the largest single funding rounds of the year for AI infrastructure specifically (as opposed to model labs or app-layer startups), and a concrete data point on how much revenue is now flowing through third-party inference platforms rather than direct foundation-model APIs.

## Industry Moves

### Google Renames NotebookLM to "Gemini Notebook," Adds Native Cloud Code Execution

**Date:** July 16, 2026 | **Sources:** [TechCrunch — Google continues its renaming streak by turning NotebookLM to Gemini Notebook](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/16/google-continues-its-renaming-streak-by-turning-notebooklm-to-gemini-notebook/), [Google Blog — NotebookLM is now Gemini Notebook](https://blog.google/innovation-and-ai/products/gemini-notebook/notebooklm-gemini-notebook/), [9to5Google — Google renames NotebookLM to Gemini Notebook](https://9to5google.com/2026/07/16/notebooklm-gemini-notebook/)
- Google rebranded NotebookLM as Gemini Notebook, ties it visually to the main Gemini brand, and added a secure per-notebook cloud computer that lets it write and execute code natively for grounded data analysis; the tool is accessible inside the main Gemini app and is coming to AI Mode in Search.
- The product itself (URL, notebooks, core features) is unchanged; Google says it now has 30 million+ users and 600,000+ organizations.
- **Why it matters:** It's the latest step in Google folding standalone AI products into the unified Gemini brand, and the added code-execution capability pushes Gemini Notebook further from a research tool toward an agentic data-analysis product.

### TSMC Raises 2026 Capex Guidance to $60-64 Billion on Sustained AI Chip Demand

**Date:** July 17, 2026 | **Sources:** [Yahoo Finance — TSMC Lifts 2026 Capex Outlook as AI Demand Stays Strong](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/tsmc-lifts-2026-capex-outlook-154053660.html), [Benzinga — Taiwan Semi's AI Spending Spree Tests Investor Nerves as Capex Surges](https://www.benzinga.com/markets/tech/26/07/60495577/taiwan-semis-ai-spending-spree-tests-investor-nerves-as-capex-surges-past-60-billion), [FX Leaders — TSM Stock Slips 2.3% Despite Record Profit as AI Demand Meets Capex Concerns](https://www.fxleaders.com/news/2026/07/17/tsm-stock-slips-2-3-despite-record-profit-as-ai-demand-meets-capex-concerns/)
- TSMC lifted 2026 capex guidance to **$60-64 billion** (from $52-56 billion) and raised its full-year revenue growth forecast to "slightly more than 40%" (from "more than 30%"), with the increase earmarked mostly for advanced nodes serving AI/HPC customers; Q2 revenue hit $40.2 billion with net profit near $22 billion (up 77.4% year-over-year) and gross margin at 67.7%.
- Shares still fell roughly 2-4.6% as investors weighed the aggressive spending ramp against near-term margin pressure; TSMC separately confirmed an additional $100 billion Arizona investment on top of the $165 billion already committed.
- **Why it matters:** It's the clearest hard-numbers signal yet that AI chip demand is still accelerating supply-side spending industry-wide, even as investors start pricing in capex-driven margin risk at the world's dominant advanced-node foundry.

## Update (later pass, 2026-07-18)

### Microsoft Reportedly Building "Project Perception" to Undercut Anthropic's Mythos on AI Security Pricing

**Date:** July 17, 2026 | **Sources:** [TechRepublic — Microsoft's 'Project Perception' Could Challenge Anthropic's Mythos in AI Security](https://www.techrepublic.com/article/news-microsoft-project-perception-ai-security-tool/), [The Information — Exclusive: Microsoft Preps Mythos-Like AI Bug Finder](https://www.theinformation.com/briefings/exclusive-microsoft-preps-mythos-like-ai-bug-finder)
- Microsoft is reportedly preparing to launch, as soon as this month, an AI vulnerability-scanning product internally codenamed Project Perception, deployed inside an organization's IT systems to find and fix security holes — functionally similar to Anthropic's Mythos.
- Perception would use a model router to send each scanning task to whichever of Anthropic's, OpenAI's, or Microsoft's own models fits best, with its central pitch being cost: Mythos's estimated API cost runs roughly 100% higher than Opus and 82% higher than GPT, two of the priciest publicly available models.
- **Why it matters:** It's a second, more concrete example (alongside the Nadella/Fable friction reported above) of Microsoft positioning cheaper multi-model products directly against flagship Anthropic offerings, despite the two companies' deep commercial ties.

### NetApp Acquires AI Data-Infrastructure Startup DataPelago

**Date:** July 16, 2026 | **Sources:** [NetApp Newsroom — NetApp Acquires DataPelago, Making Data AI-Ready at the Infrastructure Layer](https://www.netapp.com/newsroom/press-releases/news-rel-20260716-210092/), [BigDATAwire/HPCwire — NetApp Acquires DataPelago, Making Data AI-Ready at the Infrastructure Layer](https://www.hpcwire.com/bigdatawire/this-just-in/netapp-acquires-datapelago-making-data-ai-ready-at-the-infrastructure-layer/)
- NetApp acquired DataPelago, a California-based startup whose "Nucleus" engine processes data at the storage layer using heterogeneous CPU/GPU compute instead of moving it to external clusters, claiming up to 80% lower infrastructure cost and up to 10x faster performance for AI/analytics workloads; DataPelago becomes a wholly owned NetApp subsidiary. Financial terms were not disclosed.
- **Why it matters:** It's a concrete storage-vendor move to capture AI-pipeline spend at the infrastructure layer rather than ceding it to compute-side GPU providers, and one more data point in the broader AI-infrastructure consolidation wave alongside this week's Fireworks and TSMC news above.
