### Anthropic Releases Claude Sonnet 4.6 — Approaches Opus-Level Performance at 60% Lower Cost
**Date:** February 17, 2026

Anthropic released Claude Sonnet 4.6, a major upgrade to its mid-tier model lineup that approaches flagship Opus 4.6 performance across coding, computer use, and reasoning — while maintaining the same pricing as its predecessor Sonnet 4.5. The model is now the default for all Claude users, including the free tier.

**Key Details:**
- **Computer Use:** 72.5% on OSWorld-Verified benchmark, up from Sonnet 4.5's 61.4% and far ahead of GPT-5.2's 38.2%
- **Coding:** 79.6% on SWE-bench Verified — comparable to other frontier models
- **Reasoning:** 60.4% on ARC-AGI-2, above most comparable models (trails Opus 4.6, Gemini 3 Deep Think, and refined GPT-5.2)
- **Context window:** 1 million tokens (beta) — processing entire codebases in a single pass
- **Pricing:** $3/$15 per million input/output tokens — same as Sonnet 4.5, one-fifth the cost of Opus 4.6 ($5/$25)
- **Availability:** Immediate across all plans; free tier upgraded to Sonnet 4.6 by default
- **Key strength:** Described as a "full upgrade" across coding, computer use, long-context reasoning, agent planning, knowledge work, and design

**Why It Matters:**
Sonnet 4.6 blurs the line between Anthropic's mid-tier and flagship models. At one-fifth the cost of Opus 4.6, it delivers near-Opus performance on coding and computer use benchmarks — making frontier-class AI accessible to a much broader range of developers and enterprises. The 72.5% computer use score in particular represents a significant leap in agentic AI capability.

### Adani Pledges $100 Billion for Renewable-Powered AI Data Centers in India by 2035
**Date:** February 17, 2026

Indian conglomerate Adani Group announced plans to invest $100 billion to build renewable energy-powered AI-ready data centers by 2035, seeking to establish the world's largest integrated data center platform. The investment is expected to catalyze an additional $150 billion across the broader AI infrastructure ecosystem.

**Key Details:**
- **Total investment:** $100 billion by 2035 in AI-ready data centers powered by renewable energy
- **Ecosystem impact:** Expected to catalyze $150 billion more in server manufacturing, advanced electrical infrastructure, sovereign cloud platforms, and related sectors
- **Infrastructure:** Building on AdaniConnex's existing 2 GW national data center network, with plans to expand to a 5 GW target
- **Joint venture:** AdaniConnex is a partnership between Adani Group and EdgeConnex, a global data center provider
- **Energy backbone:** Adani's 30 GW Khavda renewable project in western India (10+ GW already operational) will power the expansion, with an additional $55 billion planned for renewable generation and battery storage
- **Google partnership:** Alphabet committed $15 billion over five years for an AI data center hub in southern India (announced October 2025)
- **Timing:** Announced at the India AI Impact Summit, Day 2

**Why It Matters:**
This is the single largest AI infrastructure investment pledge ever made in India — and one of the largest globally. By tying AI compute to renewable energy at scale, Adani is positioning India as a destination for sustainable AI infrastructure. The $250 billion total ecosystem impact (direct + catalyzed) would transform India's AI compute capacity and reduce dependency on foreign cloud providers.

### X (Twitter) Suffers Major Global Outage — Dark Storm Team Claims DDoS Attack
**Date:** February 16–17, 2026

Elon Musk's X platform experienced a widespread global outage affecting millions of users. The outage, which peaked at over 43,000 concurrent user reports, was claimed by Dark Storm Team, a prolific hacktivist group specializing in DDoS attacks.

**Key Details:**
- **Outage start:** Shortly after 8 a.m. ET on February 16, 2026
- **Peak reports:** Over 43,000 concurrent user reports on Downdetector
- **Symptoms:** Blank timelines, "Something went wrong" notices on mobile and desktop
- **Recovery:** Service restored by approximately 10 a.m. ET (~2 hours of disruption)
- **Affected regions:** United States, United Kingdom, and parts of Europe
- **Claimed by:** Dark Storm Team — a hacktivist group that backed up its claim with screenshots on Telegram and a Check-Host.net availability report
- **Pattern:** X has suffered repeated outages in 2026: February 16, February 5, February 1, January 22, January 16, and January 13
- **Context:** The frequency of outages has raised concerns about infrastructure resilience since Musk's staffing reductions

**Why It Matters:**
The DDoS claim by Dark Storm Team, if accurate, marks a significant escalation in hacktivist activity targeting major social platforms. The frequency of X's outages — six in under two months — raises serious questions about the platform's infrastructure stability and its ability to defend against sustained attacks.

### Cohere Launches "Tiny Aya" — Open-Weight Multilingual Models for 70+ Languages
**Date:** February 17, 2026

Cohere launched Tiny Aya, a new family of open-weight multilingual models designed to run on everyday devices without an internet connection. The models support over 70 languages and were released at the India AI Impact Summit.

**Key Details:**
- **Model family:** Tiny Aya — open-weight, downloadable, and modifiable
- **Languages:** 70+ languages, including South Asian languages (Bengali, Hindi, Punjabi, Urdu, Gujarati, Tamil, Telugu, Marathi)
- **Base model:** 3.35 billion parameters
- **Regional variants:** TinyAya-Global (broad), TinyAya-Earth (African languages), TinyAya-Fire (South Asia), TinyAya-Water (Asia Pacific + Europe)
- **Training:** Single cluster of 64 H100 GPUs — relatively modest compute for a multilingual model
- **Deployment:** Available on HuggingFace, Kaggle, Ollama, and the Cohere Platform
- **Offline capable:** Runs entirely on laptops and consumer hardware without internet

**Why It Matters:**
Tiny Aya is one of the first open-weight model families designed specifically for multilingual use on consumer hardware. Supporting 70+ languages with regional variants — and trainable on just 64 GPUs — it demonstrates that effective multilingual AI doesn't require hyperscale compute. The offline capability makes it particularly valuable in regions with limited connectivity.

### OpenAI Deprecates `chatgpt-4o-latest` API Endpoint — Final GPT-4o API Sunset
**Date:** February 17, 2026
