### Gnani.ai Unveils Inya VoiceOS — India's First 5-Billion-Parameter Voice-to-Voice AI Model
**Date:** February 17, 2026

Gnani.ai launched Inya VoiceOS, a 5-billion-parameter voice-to-voice foundational model, at the India AI Impact Summit. The model was inaugurated by Prime Minister Modi and represents the first indigenous voice-to-voice AI model built under the IndiaAI Mission.

**Key Details:**
- **Architecture:** 5-billion-parameter voice-to-voice model — processes audio directly without speech-to-text conversion
- **Languages:** Supports 15+ Indian languages
- **Training data:** 14 million+ hours of multilingual speech data, 1.2 million hours of task-specific fine-tuning, 8 trillion+ text tokens for linguistic grounding
- **Performance:** 24 kHz audio output, sub-second end-to-end latency
- **Next step:** A larger 14-billion-parameter model is planned
- **Built in India:** Entirely designed, trained, and deployed within India — supporting data sovereignty goals
- **Use cases:** Government helplines, emergency response, banking, healthcare, logistics

**Why It Matters:**
Inya VoiceOS is significant because it processes audio directly rather than converting to text first, enabling more natural conversations with lower latency. As the first indigenous voice-to-voice model under the IndiaAI Mission, it demonstrates India's capability to build foundational AI infrastructure for its unique linguistic landscape — 15+ languages with distinct phonologies and grammars.

### OpenAI Deprecates `chatgpt-4o-latest` API Endpoint — Final GPT-4o API Sunset
**Date:** February 17, 2026

OpenAI's `chatgpt-4o-latest` model snapshot was officially deprecated from the API on February 17, marking the final step in GPT-4o's retirement. This follows the ChatGPT platform removal of GPT-4o, GPT-4.1, GPT-4.1 mini, and o4-mini on February 13.

**Key Details:**
- **API deprecation:** `chatgpt-4o-latest` model snapshot removed from the API on February 17, 2026
- **ChatGPT platform:** GPT-4o, GPT-4.1, GPT-4.1 mini, and o4-mini retired from ChatGPT on February 13
- **Enterprise extension:** Business, Enterprise, and Edu customers retain GPT-4o access in Custom GPTs until April 3, 2026
- **Developer notice:** Originally announced November 18, 2025 — giving developers ~3 months to migrate
- **Community backlash:** Significant developer pushback, with users noting replacement models lack GPT-4o's conversational warmth and personality

**Why It Matters:**
The GPT-4o API sunset marks the end of an era — GPT-4o was one of OpenAI's most beloved models, particularly for its conversational style and "personality." The backlash has highlighted an underappreciated aspect of AI models: users form attachments to model behavior that go beyond raw capability benchmarks. A Change.org petition to keep GPT-4o available garnered significant attention.

### India Unveils 12 Indigenous Foundation Models at AI Impact Summit
**Date:** February 17, 2026

India showcased 12 indigenous foundation models developed by homegrown startups and consortia under the IndiaAI Mission. The models are trained on Indian datasets and tailored for the nation's 22 official languages, covering voice AI, multilingual reasoning, and sector-specific applications.

**Key Details:**
- **Scope:** 12 startups selected under IndiaAI Mission developing foundation models
- **Languages:** Tailored for India's 22 official languages
- **Capabilities:** Large language models and multimodal AI systems for linguistic diversity, sector-specific needs, and governance requirements
- **Sarvam AI:** Contributing a sovereign foundation model for text generation, translation, and conversational AI across multiple Indian languages
- **Government backing:** Part of the ₹10,370 crore IndiaAI Mission
- **MeitY's vision:** Additional Secretary Abhishek Singh stated India is "3 to 5 years away from complete AI sovereignty" across the full stack

**Why It Matters:**
Twelve indigenous foundation models represent the most ambitious sovereign AI development program outside of the U.S., China, and the EU. India is betting that models trained specifically on Indian data — with built-in support for 22 languages and local cultural context — will outperform Western models in domestic applications. The 3-to-5-year "full stack sovereignty" timeline signals India's intent to reduce dependency on American and Chinese AI infrastructure.

## Regulatory Updates

### ISRO Declares Sovereign AI a "Strategic Autonomy Requirement" — Calls for Indian-Controlled Models
**Date:** February 17, 2026

ISRO Director Rajiv Chetwani, speaking at the India AI Impact Summit, declared that sovereign AI is "not just a tool, but a strategic autonomy requirement and critical infrastructure." He warned that India has "limited visibility and legal control" over AI models operated by overseas companies.

**Key Details:**
- **ISRO's position:** Sovereign AI is a national security requirement, not just a technology preference
- **Warning:** Internet-connected models operated by foreign companies leave India with "limited visibility and legal control"
- **ISRO's AI use:** Relying on massive high-resolution geospatial data for terrestrial applications — agriculture, climate, disaster management, urban monitoring, and space operations
- **Recommendation:** India must ensure its critical AI infrastructure is domestically controlled
- **Global AI Commons:** MeitY's Abhishek Singh proposed an open, shared repository of AI tools, datasets, and computing resources

**Why It Matters:**
ISRO's framing of sovereign AI as a national security imperative — not just an economic goal — elevates the debate beyond technology policy to strategic defense. When India's space agency warns about dependency on foreign AI models, it signals that sovereign AI is becoming a military and intelligence priority, not just a commercial one.
