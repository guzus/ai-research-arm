### OpenAI and Google Employees File Amicus Brief Defending Anthropic in DOD Lawsuit
**Date:** March 9, 2026 | **Sources:** [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/09/openai-and-google-employees-rush-to-anthropics-defense-in-dod-lawsuit/), [The Information](https://www.theinformation.com/briefings/google-ai-leader-jeff-dean-openai-employees-defend-anthropic-court), [BusinessToday](https://www.businesstoday.in/technology/news/story/openai-and-google-employees-defend-anthropic-in-lawsuit-against-us-government-519835-2026-03-10), [Interesting Engineering](https://interestingengineering.com/ai-robotics/openai-google-researchers-back-athropic)

More than 30 OpenAI and Google DeepMind employees filed an amicus brief Monday supporting Anthropic's lawsuit against the U.S. Defense Department:
- The brief appeared on the docket hours after Anthropic filed two lawsuits against the DOD and other federal agencies over its supply-chain risk designation.
- Among the signatories is Jeff Dean, Google's chief scientist and leader of the Gemini AI program.
- The brief states: "The government's designation of Anthropic as a supply chain risk was an improper and arbitrary use of power that has serious ramifications for our industry."
- The employees argue that if the Pentagon was "no longer satisfied with the agreed-upon terms of its contract with Anthropic," it could have "simply canceled the contract and purchased the services of another leading AI company."
- The filing warns that the Pentagon's decision "introduces an unpredictability in [their] industry that undermines American innovation and competitiveness" and "chills professional debate on the benefits and risks of frontier AI systems."
- OpenAI CEO Sam Altman posted that "enforcing the SCR designation on Anthropic would be very bad for our industry and our country."
- Anthropic is seeking a temporary restraining order to continue its work with military partners as the lawsuit progresses.

### Anthropic Launches Code Review in Claude Code
**Date:** March 9, 2026 | **Sources:** [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/09/anthropic-launches-code-review-tool-to-check-flood-of-ai-generated-code/), [VentureBeat](https://venturebeat.com/technology/anthropic-rolls-out-code-review-for-claude-code-as-it-sues-over-pentagon), [The Register](https://www.theregister.com/2026/03/09/anthropic_debuts_code_review/), [MarkTechPost](https://www.marktechpost.com/2026/03/09/anthropic-introduces-code-review-via-claude-code-to-automate-complex-security-research-using-advanced-agentic-multi-step-reasoning-loops/)

Anthropic launched Code Review in Claude Code, a multi-agent system for automated code analysis:
- Available in research preview for Team and Enterprise customers.
- Integrates with GitHub to analyze pull request code changes, posting a unified summary and inline comments.
- Uses multiple specialized AI agents that collaborate: some search for defects and risky patterns, others verify findings to limit noise, and a final pass ranks issues by severity.
- Reviews typically complete in about 20 minutes; priced at an estimated $15–$25 per review depending on code size and complexity.
- Internal results: with the system active, PRs receiving "substantive" feedback rose from 16% to 54%. Human developers reject fewer than 1% of flagged issues.
- Anthropic reports its own engineers increased code output ~200% YoY, making automated review essential.
- Code Review does not approve PRs — that decision remains with human reviewers.
- Claude Code's run-rate revenue has surpassed $2.5 billion since launch, with Uber, Salesforce, and Accenture among enterprise users.

## Regulatory Updates

### FTC and Commerce Department Face Critical AI Policy Deadline Tomorrow (March 11)
**Date:** March 10, 2026 | **Sources:** [Baker Botts](https://ourtake.bakerbotts.com/post/102mirs/march-2026-federal-deadlines-that-will-reshape-the-ai-regulatory-landscape), [Digital Applied](https://www.digitalapplied.com/blog/ftc-ai-policy-deadline-march-11-compliance-readiness), [IAPP](https://iapp.org/news/a/a-view-from-dc-can-the-ftc-preempt-state-ai-laws-)

Two major federal AI policy statements are due tomorrow, March 11, per Trump's December 2025 executive order:
- **FTC Policy Statement:** The FTC must publish its policy on how Section 5 of the FTC Act applies to AI, developed in consultation with David Sacks (Special Advisor for AI and Crypto). The statement must address whether state laws requiring alterations to "truthful outputs" of AI models are preempted by federal law.
- **Commerce Department Review:** The Secretary of Commerce must publish a comprehensive review of existing state AI laws, identifying those deemed overly burdensome or in conflict with federal policy, and flag laws for referral to the DOJ AI Litigation Task Force.
- **DOJ AI Litigation Task Force:** Established January 9, 2026, tasked with challenging state AI laws in federal court. No lawsuits have been filed yet, but the Commerce Department review could trigger immediate action.
- **State preemption concerns:** Colorado's AI Act, Illinois' AI Video Interview Act, and California's proposed AB-331 could all be superseded by the FTC's federal framework.
- The Trump Administration's legal theory posits that forcing AI developers to alter model outputs to mitigate bias renders the model less "truthful" and therefore deceptive under the FTC Act — a theory courts may reject.
