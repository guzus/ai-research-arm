**Read-through:** Three implications. (1) The H200 clearance **directly validates** the morning report's call that Huang's late addition to the delegation raised the odds of a narrow chip-export-framework outcome — and **materially reopens China-revenue commentary** for NVIDIA's May 20 earnings. (2) An H200 thaw **modestly softens the Chinese AI-self-sufficiency tailwind** for DeepSeek, Moonshot, and Alibaba's in-house silicon push. (3) The "strategic stability" framing is **stabilization, not reset** — analysts read it as a managed three-year truce rather than a structural rollback of export-control architecture.

### **Musk v. Altman — Closing Arguments Delivered; Musk's Lead Counsel Apologizes To The Jury For Musk's Absence (He Flew To Beijing With Trump); Molo Hammers Altman's Trustworthiness And Self-Dealing; Advisory-Jury And Judge Decision Possible Next Week**

**Date:** Thursday May 14, 2026 | **Sources:** [Axios — Musk lawyers accuse OpenAI of deception in close of mega-trial](https://www.axios.com/2026/05/14/musk-closing-arguments-openai-altman), [CNBC — Musk's China trip during OpenAI trial prompts apology from his lawyer for CEO's absence](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/14/musk-lawyer-trial-jury-china-trip-openai-altman.html), [NBC News — Musk's attorney apologizes for his absence at trial during closing arguments](https://www.nbcnews.com/tech/tech-news/musks-attorney-apologizes-absence-closing-remarks-rcna345153), [Baltimore Sun — Musk, OpenAI lawyers begin closing arguments in landmark trial](https://www.baltimoresun.com/2026/05/14/musk-openai-trial-closing-arguments/)

Closing arguments in *Musk v. Altman* were delivered Thursday before Judge Yvonne Gonzalez Rogers:

- **Musk's close:** Lawyer **Steven Molo** argued OpenAI **violated its nonprofit mission** and that executives sought **personal gain through stock grants and self-dealing** with entities in which they held stakes. He pressed **Altman's trustworthiness**, citing testimony from **Ilya Sutskever, Mira Murati, Helen Toner, and Tasha McCauley**.
- **Musk's ask:** Altman **ousted as CEO and removed from the board**, plus **billions in damages** — which Musk says he would donate back to OpenAI's nonprofit arm.
- **The absence:** Musk's **lead counsel apologized to the jury** for Musk's absence — he is in **Beijing with the Trump delegation**, after Judge Gonzalez Rogers had warned he could be recalled.
- **Next:** A decision from the **advisory jury** and from **Gonzalez Rogers** (who is not bound by the jury) is **possible next week**.

**Read-through:** Three implications. (1) With closings done, the case is **fully in Gonzalez Rogers' hands** — the advisory-jury number is guidance, not outcome. (2) Musk's physical absence during his own closing is an **optics liability** Molo had to manage directly, and leaves **live recall risk** unresolved. (3) The self-dealing thread (Helion, Stripe, Cerebras — the last of which closed a ~$95B-valuation debut the same day) **remains the spine of Musk's case** and continues to feed Anthropic's comparative-governance positioning into the Q3/Q4 enterprise cycle.
