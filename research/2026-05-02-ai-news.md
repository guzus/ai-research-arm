Anthropic shipped **Claude Security** into **public beta for Claude Enterprise customers** on **May 1, 2026** — the **same day** the Pentagon publicly excluded the company from its classified-network procurement and DoD CTO Emil Michael called Mythos a "separate national-security moment." The timing is **deliberate**: with Mythos's offensive-cyber capabilities now structurally locked behind Project Glasswing and the White House publicly opposing wider distribution, Claude Security is Anthropic's **defensive-cyber commercial wedge** — the same Opus-4.7 reasoning capability turned 180 degrees onto enterprise codebase hardening.

- **Underlying model:** **Claude Opus 4.7** (Anthropic's GA flagship). The product is positioned as a **codebase scanner that reasons about data flows, cross-file interactions, and module boundaries** — explicitly **NOT** a signature/pattern matcher (the SAST/DAST incumbents Snyk, Veracode, Checkmarx). Anthropic's marketing language: it scans "as a security researcher would."
- **Output schema per finding:** confidence rating, severity, likely impact, reproduction steps, **and a generated targeted patch** with documented rationale.
- **Multi-stage validation pipeline:** every finding passes through an internal validation step **before** it reaches a human analyst — a direct response to LLM-vulnerability-scanner false-positive rates that have been the core enterprise objection in 2025–2026 SAST AI bake-offs.
- **Beta-period adoption:** "Hundreds of organizations" have used Claude Security in the prior closed-preview phase (originally branded **Claude Code Security**, launched February 2026 limited to Enterprise/Team), and surfaced **vulnerabilities that existing tools missed for years.**
- **New features added in beta:** scheduled scans, dismiss-with-comment workflow, CSV/Markdown export to integrate into existing ticket-tracking and audit pipelines.
- **Availability:** Public beta on Enterprise plans now; **Team and Max tiers "coming soon."**

**Read-through:** Three implications stack on top of the May 1 Pentagon exclusion. (1) **Anthropic is now executing a structural pivot from federal-procurement-led GTM to enterprise-commercial-cyber-defense GTM in real-time** — Claude Security is the **first public deliverable** of that pivot, and the timing is too clean to be coincidental. Mythos can't sell to Pentagon IL-6/IL-7; Claude Security can sell to every Fortune 500 CISO. (2) **The competitive positioning directly threatens CrowdStrike, Wiz, Snyk, and Veracode** — Claude Security's "reasoning-based" framing is a head-on attack on rule-based SAST and signature-based EDR, and the Opus-4.7 underlying model is **strictly stronger on code reasoning** than any incumbent's in-house transformer. Watch for **CrowdStrike and Wiz to announce competing reasoning-LLM scanners within 2 weeks** — likely powered by GPT-5.5 (which is GA on AWS Bedrock per April 28). (3) **Claude Security is the most concrete commercial monetization of Mythos research to date** — Anthropic has been criticized since April 7 for productizing Mythos's offensive-cyber capabilities without clear defensive analogues; Claude Security closes that critique and gives Anthropic's $900B-valuation round a **fresh "defensive-cyber TAM" line item** the day after the round's compute/distribution overhang from the White House intervention.
