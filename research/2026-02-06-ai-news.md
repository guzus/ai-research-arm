### Amazon Q4 2025 Earnings — Revenue Beat, Stock Plunges 10% on $200B Capex Shock
**Date:** February 5–6, 2026

Amazon reported Q4 2025 earnings after market close on February 5, delivering strong revenue but shocking investors with a $200 billion 2026 capex forecast — far above the ~$147B Wall Street expected.

**Q4 2025 Results:**
- **Revenue:** $213.4 billion (+14% YoY) — beat estimates
- **EPS:** $1.95 (diluted) — slightly below analyst expectations of ~$1.97
- **Net Income:** $21.2 billion
- **AWS Revenue:** $35.58 billion (vs. $34.93B expected) — **+24% YoY**, fastest growth in 13 quarters
- **Advertising Revenue:** $21.32 billion (vs. $21.16B expected)

**2026 Capital Expenditure Guidance:**
Amazon projected capex of **$200 billion** for 2026, up from ~$131 billion in 2025. Wall Street had expected ~$146.6 billion. CEO Andy Jassy cited "strong demand for existing offerings and seminal opportunities in AI, chips, robotics and low Earth orbit satellites."

**Q1 2026 Guidance:**
Net sales expected between $173.5B and $178.5B (+11–15% YoY).

**Stock Reaction:**
Shares plunged **~10%** in after-hours trading, falling below $200, as investors recoiled from the massive spending increase and its impact on free cash flow projections.

### OpenAI GPT-5.3-Codex — "First Self-Developing" AI Coding Model
**Date:** February 5, 2026

OpenAI released GPT-5.3-Codex, its most capable agentic coding model, notable for being the **first model that was instrumental in creating itself** — the Codex team used early versions to debug its own training, manage deployment, and diagnose evaluations.

**Key Capabilities:**
- Combines frontier coding performance of GPT-5.2-Codex with reasoning capabilities of GPT-5.2 in one model
- **25% faster** than predecessor
- Designed for agent-style development: tool use, computer operation, and long-running end-to-end tasks

**Benchmark Performance:**
- New industry high on **SWE-Bench Pro** and **Terminal-Bench**
- Strong performance on **OSWorld** and **GDPval**

**Security Classification:**
This is the first model classified as **High capability in the Cybersecurity domain** under OpenAI's Preparedness Framework, activating associated safeguards. Fortune warned of "unprecedented cybersecurity risks."

**Availability:**
Available with paid ChatGPT plans via Codex app, CLI, IDE extension, and web. API access to follow.

**Date:** February 5, 2026

**What They Do:** Goodfire builds tools to understand, debug, and intentionally design AI models at scale using frontier interpretability techniques — allowing users to identify specific model components responsible for behaviors and retrain them.
