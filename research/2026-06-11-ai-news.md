### OpenAI + Oracle: Frontier Models & Codex Through Your Existing OCI Cloud Commitment

**Date:** June 10, 2026 (distributed June 11) | **Sources:** [OpenAI — Access OpenAI models and Codex through your Oracle cloud commitment](https://openai.com/index/openai-on-oracle-cloud/), [StartupHub.ai — OpenAI Teams Up With Oracle Cloud](https://www.startuphub.ai/ai-news/artificial-intelligence/2026/openai-teams-up-with-oracle-cloud), [Public/WorldNews syndication (June 11)](https://article.wn.com/view/2026/06/11/Access_OpenAI_models_and_Codex_through_your_Oracle_cloud_com/), [Data Center Dynamics — OpenAI's ~$300B Oracle cloud deal (context)](https://www.datacenterdynamics.com/en/news/openai-signs-300bn-cloud-deal-with-oracle-report/)

- **What was announced:** OpenAI and Oracle are making **OpenAI's frontier models and Codex** directly accessible to **Oracle Cloud Infrastructure (OCI)** customers. In the coming weeks, customers will be able to **apply eligible Oracle Universal Credits** toward OpenAI models and Codex through OCI.
- **Why it matters:** The pitch is procurement, not capability. Enterprises that already have an Oracle cloud commitment can reach OpenAI's models **under their existing purchasing workflow** — no new vendor onboarding, no separate procurement cycle. It plugs OpenAI into Oracle's enterprise install base for building apps, analyzing data, and automating workflows.
- **The bigger relationship:** This is the **demand/consumption-side counterpart** to the supply-side **Stargate ↔ Oracle** arrangement (reported as a multi-year, ~$300B-scale OCI compute commitment / 4.5 GW partnership). OpenAI is simultaneously Oracle's largest compute customer *and*, now, a product Oracle resells to its own customers — a two-sided dependency that analysts have flagged as both a revenue flywheel and a concentration risk for Oracle.
- **Distribution read-through:** Mirrors the **multi-cloud, channel-first** posture across the frontier labs this month — Anthropic's Fable 5 launching day-one on AWS Bedrock + vertical partners (June 9), and now OpenAI riding Oracle's enterprise credits. The battleground is increasingly *how frontier models get bought*, not just how they benchmark.

## Regulatory Updates

### EU Commission Publishes Final Code of Practice on Marking & Labelling of AI-Generated Content

**Date:** June 10, 2026 | **Sources:** [European Commission press release (IP/26/1328)](https://ec.europa.eu/commission/presscorner/detail/en/ip_26_1328), [Commission — Code of Practice on AI-generated content (policy page)](https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/code-practice-ai-generated-content), [Agence Europe — Commission publishes final voluntary Code of Practice to label AI-generated content](https://agenceurope.eu/en/bulletin/article/13885/7/european-commission-publishes-final-voluntary-code-of-practice-to-help-label-content-generated-by-ai)

- **What shipped:** The European Commission published the **final, voluntary Code of Practice** giving providers and deployers of generative AI **practical steps to meet the AI Act transparency obligations that apply from Aug 2, 2026.**
- **For providers:** specifies how AI-generated/manipulated **audio, images, video, and text** should be marked in a **machine-readable** way so the content is detectable as artificially created or altered.
- **For deployers:** explains how to clearly disclose **deepfakes** and AI-generated/manipulated **text published to inform the public on matters of general interest** where no human editorial review occurred.
- **Tooling:** the Code suggests a freely usable **icon set** — distinct marks for "deepfake created with AI," "content generated entirely by AI," and "content partially altered by AI."
- **Read-through:** This is the **operational bridge** between the AI Act's high-level transparency mandate and the Aug 2 compliance cliff. It's voluntary (a safe-harbor-style adherence path), but it's the concrete template providers will be measured against — and it lands the same week Anthropic's Fable 5 was shipping with **SynthID-style** provenance baked in, signaling the labs and regulators are converging on watermarking as the default.
