### OpenAI Nears Record $100 Billion+ Funding Round — Valuation Could Exceed $850 Billion
**Date:** February 19, 2026

OpenAI is close to finalizing the first phase of a new funding round that is expected to bring in more than $100 billion, making it the largest private funding deal in history. The overall valuation of the company, including the eventual funding, could exceed $850 billion.

**Key Details:**
- **Amazon:** Expected to invest up to $50 billion
- **SoftBank:** As much as $30 billion
- **Nvidia:** Has discussed contributing $20 billion
- **Microsoft:** Also participating as a strategic investor
- **Pre-money valuation:** $730 billion (higher than the initially reported $830B overall target)
- **Timeline:** Companies expected to finalize allocations by end of February; a second phase with VCs, sovereign wealth funds, and financial investors will follow and could lift the total substantially higher
- **Context:** Sam Altman recently touted ChatGPT's "reaccelerating growth" to employees as the round neared completion

**Why It Matters:**
This would be the largest private funding round ever by a massive margin — nearly an order of magnitude larger than the previous record. The sheer concentration of capital (Amazon alone at $50B) signals that the hyperscaler-AI lab relationship is evolving from customer-vendor to deep strategic interdependence. If completed, OpenAI's valuation would exceed the market cap of most S&P 500 companies.

### Alibaba Releases Qwen3.5 — Open-Source 397B Parameter Model for the "Agentic Era"
**Date:** February 16–17, 2026

Alibaba Cloud released Qwen3.5, an open-source multimodal mixture-of-experts model designed for autonomous AI agent workflows, marking a major step in China's AI race.

**Key Details:**
- **Architecture:** 397 billion total parameters, activates only 17B per task (mixture of experts)
- **Languages:** 201 languages and dialects (up from 82 in previous generation)
- **Multimodal:** Native understanding of text, images, and video within one system
- **Agentic capabilities:** "Visual agentic capabilities" — autonomous actions across mobile and desktop applications
- **Cost:** 60% cheaper to operate than predecessor; processes large workloads 8x more effectively
- **Benchmarks:** Alibaba claims performance on par with leading models from OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google DeepMind (self-reported)
- **Availability:** Open-weight (download, fine-tune, deploy) plus Alibaba-hosted version

**Why It Matters:**
Qwen3.5's mixture-of-experts architecture (397B total, 17B active) represents the efficiency frontier for open-source models — achieving frontier-class performance at dramatically lower inference costs. The 201-language support and visual agentic capabilities position it as particularly strong for non-English markets and automated workflows. This release intensifies the US-China AI model competition, especially on cost efficiency.

### Ineffable Intelligence Raises $1 Billion Seed — Largest European Seed Round Ever
**Date:** February 18, 2026

David Silver, the former Google DeepMind principal research scientist who led the development of AlphaGo, is raising $1 billion for his London-based startup Ineffable Intelligence, in what would be the largest seed round ever secured by a European company.

**Key Details:**
- **Amount:** $1 billion seed round
- **Valuation:** $4 billion
- **Lead investor:** Sequoia Capital (managing partner Alfred Lin and partner Sonya Huang flew to London to meet Silver)
- **Other investors in talks:** Nvidia, Alphabet (Google), Microsoft
- **Founder:** David Silver, 49, spent 10+ years at DeepMind; led AlphaGo, AlphaStar, contributed to Gemini models
- **Focus:** Reinforcement learning — AI that improves through environmental interaction rather than training on internet text
- **Vision:** "An endlessly learning superintelligence that self-discovers the foundations of all knowledge"
- **No product yet:** Company is pre-product, founded November 2025

**Why It Matters:**
A $1B seed at $4B valuation — for a company with no product — reflects the market's extraordinary bet on reinforcement learning as the next paradigm shift beyond LLMs. Silver's AlphaGo pedigree makes this credible: he proved RL could achieve superhuman performance in constrained domains. The question is whether Ineffable can generalize that to open-ended intelligence. If completed, this would be the largest European seed round in history, signaling London as a serious AI research hub.

### Anthropic-Pentagon Feud Escalates — Defense Secretary "Close" to Cutting Ties
**Date:** February 15–18, 2026 (ongoing)

The dispute between Anthropic and the U.S. Department of Defense over Claude's military use escalated sharply, with Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth reportedly "close" to cutting business ties with the AI company and potentially labeling it a "supply chain risk."

**Key Details:**
- **Core dispute:** Pentagon demands AI labs allow military use of tools for "all lawful purposes" including weapons development, intelligence collection, and battlefield operations
- **Anthropic's red lines:** Mass surveillance of Americans and fully autonomous weaponry remain off-limits
- **Flashpoint:** The military's use of Claude in the operation to capture Venezuela's Nicolás Maduro (via Palantir partnership) — an Anthropic executive contacted Palantir to ask whether Claude was used during the kinetic operation
- **Pentagon threat:** Senior official stated: "It will be an enormous pain in the ass to disentangle, and we are going to make sure they pay a price for forcing our hand like this"
- **Financial exposure:** Anthropic's Pentagon contract valued up to $200 million — small relative to Anthropic's $14B annual run-rate revenue
- **Broader context:** Pentagon is pushing the same demands on four leading AI labs

**Why It Matters:**
This is the most significant confrontation between a frontier AI lab and the U.S. military to date. Anthropic is the only major lab publicly drawing red lines around autonomous weapons and mass surveillance, creating a test case for whether AI safety commitments survive government pressure. The "supply chain risk" designation would have broader implications, potentially affecting Anthropic's contracts across the federal government.
