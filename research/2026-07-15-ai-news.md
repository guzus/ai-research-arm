## Breaking News

### China's AI Companion Law Takes Effect, Forcing Doubao and Qwen to Shut Down Agent Features Today

**Date:** July 15, 2026 | **Sources:** [South China Morning Post — ByteDance and Alibaba to disable humanlike AI custom agents as new rules loom](https://www.scmp.com/tech/big-tech/article/3359482/bytedance-and-alibaba-disable-humanlike-ai-custom-agents-new-rules-loom), [Bloomberg — ByteDance, Alibaba Pull AI Companions as Beijing Tightens Rules](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-06/bytedance-alibaba-pull-ai-companions-as-beijing-tightens-rules), [TechNode — ByteDance's Doubao and Alibaba's Qwen to shut down AI agent features on July 15](https://technode.com/2026/07/06/bytedances-doubao-and-alibabas-qwen-to-shut-down-ai-agent-features-on-july-15/), [Tech Times — China AI Companion Law Arrives July 15: Doubao and Qwen Agent Data Will Be Deleted](https://www.techtimes.com/articles/319703/20260704/china-ai-companion-law-arrives-july-15-doubao-qwen-agent-data-will-deleted.htm)
- China's "Interim Measures for the Administration of AI Anthropomorphic Interactive Services" — co-issued in April 2026 by the Cyberspace Administration of China, the National Development and Reform Commission, MIIT, the Ministry of Public Security, and the State Administration for Market Regulation — takes effect **today, July 15, 2026**, requiring AI companion services to implement anti-addiction systems, mandatory usage notifications, and instant-exit mechanisms.
- ByteDance's Doubao, China's most-used AI app with **345 million monthly active users**, is pulling its personalized agent features today; users retain read-only access to old configurations and chat histories until October 15, 2026, after which the data becomes permanently inaccessible.
- Alibaba's Qwen is also shutting down agent features today, but with no announced grace period — configurations and conversation histories will be permanently deleted with no migration path, per Alibaba's own confirmation.
- **Why it matters:** This is Beijing's first dedicated regulatory action specifically against "humanlike" companion-style AI agents (as distinct from task-execution agents), and it lands today across two of China's largest consumer AI platforms simultaneously — a concrete test of how fast major AI products can be forced to restructure under a hard compliance deadline.

**Date:** July 14, 2026 | **Sources:** [Fierce Biotech — Chai brews up $400M series C to fuel AI molecule models used by Lilly, Novartis and Pfizer](https://www.fiercebiotech.com/biotech/chai-brews-400m-series-c-fuel-ai-used-lilly-novartis-and-pfizer), [SiliconANGLE — Chai Discovery nabs $400M Series C as AI-designed antibodies reach Big Pharma](https://siliconangle.com/2026/07/14/chai-discovery-nabs-400m-series-c-ai-designed-antibodies-reach-big-pharma/), [Endpoints News — Chai Discovery gets $400M, tripling valuation from seven months ago](https://endpoints.news/chai-discovery-gets-400m-tripling-valuation-from-seven-months-ago/)
- Chai Discovery, whose AI models generate novel molecular designs used by Eli Lilly, Novartis, and Pfizer, announced a **$400 million Series C** led by Index Ventures alongside Kleiner Perkins, Sequoia Capital, and Dimension, valuing the company at **$3.8 billion**.
- The round nearly triples Chai's valuation from $1.3 billion just seven months ago (a $130 million round in December) and brings total funding to roughly $630 million. Existing investors Thrive Capital, OpenAI, Oak HC/FT, Menlo Ventures, and General Catalyst all participated again.
- **Why it matters:** It's one of the largest AI-biotech rounds of the year and signals continued investor conviction that AI-designed molecules are moving from research pipelines into real pharma partnerships, not just proof-of-concept.

### Anthropic Launches Claude for Teachers, Free for Verified US K-12 Educators

**Date:** July 14, 2026 | **Sources:** [Anthropic — Introducing Claude for Teachers](https://www.anthropic.com/news/claude-for-teachers), [Chalkbeat — Anthropic launches Claude for Teachers in AI race to influence America's classrooms](https://www.chalkbeat.org/2026/07/14/anthropic-launches-claude-for-teachers-as-ai-companies-battle-for-classrooms/), [EdSurge — Anthropic Introduces Claude for Teachers](https://www.edsurge.com/news/anthropic-introduces-claude-for-teachers)
- Anthropic launched **Claude for Teachers**, giving verified US K-12 educators free access (normally $20/month) to Claude Opus 4.6, higher usage limits, unlimited "Projects," and a teaching-skills library mapped to academic standards in all 50 states; teachers who sign up by June 30, 2027 get a full year of access.
- The launch includes nine education-tool connectors (ASSISTments, Brisk Teaching, Canva Education, Coteach, Diffit, Eedi, MagicSchool, Snorkl, TeachFX), a new "AI Fluency for K-12 Teachers" course co-created with Teach for America, and a pilot evaluation program with Detroit Public Schools Community District. Anthropic says student data isn't used for model training and is covered by a FERPA-oriented K-12 Data Processing Addendum.
- **Why it matters:** It follows Anthropic's June 30 Claude Sonnet 5 launch and continued Claude Fable 5 promotions — Anthropic is now extending its consumer land-grab specifically into K-12 classrooms, directly competing with OpenAI's and Google's existing education pushes.
