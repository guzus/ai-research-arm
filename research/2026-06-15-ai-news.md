## Breaking News

### David Sacks: Anthropic "Refused" to Patch Fable 5 Jailbreak Before Export Controls Hit

**Date:** Surfaced June 13–14, 2026 (not in prior reports) | **Sources:** [Tom's Hardware — Trump adviser David Sacks says Anthropic refused to fix Fable 5 jailbreak before US export controls](https://www.tomshardware.com/tech-industry/artificial-intelligence/trump-adviser-david-sacks-says-anthropic-refused-to-fix-fable-5-jailbreak-before-us-export-controls), [Yellow.com — Anthropic Refused To Patch Claude Fable's Jailbreak, So The US Banned It, David Sacks Says](https://yellow.com/news/anthropic-claude-fable-jailbreak-ban), [The Tribune — US Govt's export controls on Anthropic tied to safety fix, says David Sacks](https://www.tribuneindia.com/news/business/us-govts-export-controls-on-anthropic-tied-to-safety-fix-says-us-president-advisory-council-member-david-sacks/)

- **What's net-new:** For the first time, the **administration's side of the story** is on the record. **David Sacks** (co-chair, President's Council of Advisors on Science and Technology / AI & crypto czar) said the government warned Anthropic that Fable 5 had been **jailbroken**, that CEO **Dario Amodei** characterized the flaw as **"not serious" and declined to fix it**, and that the export control was therefore issued **"reluctantly."** His framing: **"the ball is in Anthropic's court"** — i.e., the company can get the models restored by addressing the safety fix.
- **Anthropic's rebuttal (unchanged):** The bypass is **narrow, already known, and present in rival models**; recalling a model "deployed to hundreds of millions of people" over a minor vulnerability sets a standard that "would essentially halt all new model deployments for all frontier model providers." Anthropic continues to call the directive a **misunderstanding.**
- **Why it matters:** Until now the public record was Anthropic's account plus *The Information*'s reporting. Sacks moving the dispute into **"refused to fix"** territory reframes it from an opaque export action into a **safety-compliance standoff** — and signals the path back is a patch, not a courtroom.

### Semafor Exclusive: Export Limits Tied to Suspected Chinese Access to Mythos

**Date:** June 13–14, 2026 (not in prior reports) | **Sources:** [Semafor — White House move to limit Anthropic linked to concerns about Chinese access to Mythos](https://www.semafor.com/article/06/13/2026/white-house-move-to-limit-anthropic-linked-to-concerns-about-chinese-access-to-mythos), [Washington Examiner — Anthropic export limits tied to suspected Chinese access: Report](https://www.washingtonexaminer.com/news/white-house/4607862/white-house-anthropic-export-limits-chinese-access-report/), [Al Jazeera — US asks Anthropic to block global access to top AI models: Why it matters](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/6/14/us-asks-anthropic-to-block-global-access-to-top-ai-models-why-it-matters)

- **What's net-new:** The White House imposed the controls **partly over suspicions that a China-linked group had accessed Mythos 5**, per a person familiar with the matter. The stated worry: if Beijing obtained access, it could pose national-security risk and attempt to **reverse-engineer / distill** the model.
- **The dispute:** An **Anthropic spokesperson said the White House did NOT raise Chinese access** to Mythos in its conversations around the Fable jailbreak and export controls — putting the company's account and the administration's reported rationale directly at odds.
- **Why it matters:** This shifts the public read of the action from a pure jailbreak-safety story to a **China-distillation / model-weight-security** story — the same logic underpinning chip export controls now applied to frontier *model access*. It's the first time a concrete adversary-access motive has been attached to the takedown.
