## Breaking News

### Net-New: SK Telecom Named as the Korean Carrier at the Center of the Mythos 5 Export Directive

**Date:** Reporting surfaced June 17–19, 2026 (sharpening through the June 20–21 window) | **Sources:** [Tom's Hardware — SK Telecom named as the Korean carrier at the center of Anthropic's Mythos export controls](https://www.tomshardware.com/tech-industry/artificial-intelligence/sk-telecom-named-as-the-korean-carrier-at-the-center-of-anthropics-mythos-export-controls), [Korea JoongAng Daily — White House officials pin Anthropic AI export block on Korean telecom: Report](https://www.koreajoongangdaily.com/business/white-house-officials-pin-anthropic-ai-export-block-on-korean-telecom-report/12726842), [The Elec — US Export Controls on Anthropic's Mythos Model Raise Questions for Korean Telecom Sector](https://www.thelec.net/news/articleView.html?idxno=11390)

- **What's net-new (vs. prior reports, which framed the trigger as a Fable 5 "jailbreak"):** The June 12 Lutnick directive is now reported to stem, *in part*, from Washington's discovery that a **South Korean telecom suspected of ties to China gained early access to Mythos 5** through Anthropic's **Project Glasswing** cybersecurity consortium. WIRED subsequently **named that carrier as SK Telecom** — South Korea's largest wireless operator and a **~$100M Anthropic investor since 2023**.
- **The mechanics:** SK Telecom was reportedly among **~150 organizations added to Project Glasswing in early June**; its Mythos access was **revoked days before** the White House pulled Fable 5 + Mythos 5 offline for all foreign nationals. This reframes the suspension from a pure model-capability/jailbreak story into an **access-control / supply-chain-trust** story.
- **The denial:** SK Telecom **flatly rejects the China linkage** — "It is absolutely untrue that there is any linkage with China" — and says it uses no Chinese (e.g., Huawei) gear in its core networks.
- **Why it matters:** It puts a concrete named entity (and a current Anthropic investor) at the heart of the most consequential AI export action of the year, and helps explain why a *capability* concern produced a *nationality-scoped* shutdown — which is exactly what the new ID-verification policy below is built to police.

### Net-New: Anthropic's Rewritten Privacy Policy Reads as the ID-Verification Path Around the Fable Ban

**Date:** Policy published June 8, 2026; effective July 8, 2026; reporting/analysis ran June 15–19 (carried into this window) | **Sources:** [Computerworld — Anthropic's new privacy policy offers US consumers a way around the Fable ban](https://www.computerworld.com/article/4185515/anthropics-new-privacy-policy-offers-us-consumers-a-way-around-fable-ban-2.html), [The Register — Anthropic reserves right to check ID for Claude subs](https://www.theregister.com/ai-and-ml/2026/06/15/anthropic-reserves-right-to-check-id-for-claude-subs/5255804), [The Print — Claude maker Anthropic to ask some users for govt-issued IDs & facial scans](https://theprint.in/tech/claude-maker-anthropic-to-ask-some-users-for-govt-issued-ids-facial-scans-under-new-privacy-rules/2960516/)

- **What's net-new:** Anthropic's revised privacy policy lets it ask **Free, Pro, and Max** users to **verify identity**, collecting an **image of a government ID** (plus ID number / DOB), a **photo or video of the user**, and **facial-geometry templates** (treated as **biometric data** in some jurisdictions). It **takes effect July 8, 2026** and **does not apply to business subscriptions**.
- **The timing tell:** The "verification data" language was **published June 8 — the day before the Fable 5 launch and four days before the export ban** (flagged by Simon Willison), suggesting the mechanism was staged *before* the suspension, not improvised after it.
- **Why it matters:** If the foreign-national block persists, Anthropic could **reopen Fable 5 / Mythos 5 to verified US citizens** rather than keep both models dark for everyone. This is the first concrete sign of *how* a partial, nationality-scoped restoration would actually be enforced — i.e., a path out of the blanket worldwide shutoff.
