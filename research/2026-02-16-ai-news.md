### India AI Impact Summit 2026 Opens — PM Modi Inaugurates First Global AI Summit in the Global South
**Date:** February 16, 2026

Prime Minister Narendra Modi inaugurated the India AI Impact Summit 2026 at Bharat Mandapam in New Delhi, marking the opening of the largest global AI gathering ever held in the Global South. The five-day event (Feb 16–20) brings together world leaders, tech CEOs, and policymakers to shape a shared roadmap for AI governance and deployment.

**Key Details:**
- **Scale:** 700+ sessions, 3,250+ speakers, 300+ exhibition pavilions across 70,000+ square metres; organizers expect 250,000+ visitors
- **World leaders attending:** French President Emmanuel Macron, Brazilian President Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva, UN Secretary-General António Guterres, heads of state from ~20 countries, ministerial delegations from 45+ nations
- **Tech CEOs confirmed:** Google CEO Sundar Pichai, OpenAI CEO Sam Altman, Anthropic CEO Dario Amodei, Google DeepMind CEO Demis Hassabis, Microsoft VP Brad Smith, Scale AI CEO Alexandr Wang, Qualcomm CEO Cristiano Amon
- **13 country pavilions:** Australia, Japan, Russia, UK, France, Germany, Italy, Netherlands, Switzerland, Serbia, Estonia, Tajikistan, and Africa
- **India's AI showcase:** Eight indigenous foundational AI models to be unveiled; Sarvam AI presenting a new larger voice-focused model
- **Theme:** "Sarvajana Hitaya, Sarvajana Sukhaya" — AI for the benefit and happiness of all people
- **Day 1 focus:** AI for road safety — data-driven methods for crash prediction and safer mobility systems

**Why It Matters:**
This is the first global AI summit of this scale hosted in the Global South, signaling a shift in AI governance away from exclusively Western-led forums. With 20 heads of state and every major AI company CEO present, India is positioning itself as a key voice in setting global AI standards — particularly around inclusive deployment, multilingual AI, and governance frameworks that account for developing economies.

### Alibaba Launches Qwen 3.5 — "Built for the Agentic AI Era"
**Date:** February 16, 2026

Alibaba unveiled Qwen 3.5, a major upgrade to its flagship AI model designed for autonomous task execution and agentic workflows. The release comes ahead of DeepSeek's anticipated V4 launch and intensifies the competitive race among Chinese AI model makers.

**Key Details:**
- **Cost reduction:** 60% cheaper to use than its predecessor
- **Performance:** 8x better at processing large workloads than the previous Qwen generation
- **Visual agentic capabilities:** Can independently take actions across mobile and desktop apps — a first for Alibaba's model lineup
- **Multimodal:** Understands text, photo, and video inputs; can analyze videos up to two hours long
- **Positioning:** Alibaba explicitly markets Qwen 3.5 as "built for the agentic AI era," targeting developers and enterprises
- **Competitive context:** Alibaba is trying to claw back users from ByteDance's Doubao and DeepSeek in China's domestic market

**Why It Matters:**
Qwen 3.5 represents a significant shift in what Chinese AI models can do — moving beyond chatbot-style interactions toward autonomous task execution across applications. The 60% cost reduction and 8x throughput improvement make it substantially more competitive for enterprise deployment. With DeepSeek V4 expected within days, the timing signals an intensifying AI model arms race in China.

Multiple reports indicate DeepSeek is poised to release its V4 model around February 17, 2026. The anticipated launch has fueled investor anxiety and industry anticipation given the global tech selloff DeepSeek triggered a year ago with R1.

**Key Details:**
- **Expected release:** Around February 17, 2026 (not officially confirmed by DeepSeek)
- **Context window:** 1M+ tokens — processing entire codebases in a single pass
- **Architecture:** Integrates Engram conditional memory technology for efficient long-context retrieval
- **Hardware:** Designed to run on consumer-grade GPUs (dual RTX 4090s or single RTX 5090)
- **Open weights:** Continuing DeepSeek's tradition of open model releases

Consumer GPU support and 1M+ context windows would make frontier-class long-context coding accessible to individual developers and small teams — without cloud API costs. Markets are watching closely after R1's release triggered a global tech selloff one year ago.

### Blackstone Leads $1.2B Funding for India's Neysa AI — Largest AI Infrastructure Deal in India
**Date:** February 15–16, 2026

Blackstone and co-investors committed to a $1.2 billion capital raise for Neysa, a Mumbai-based AI acceleration cloud platform, in what is the largest AI infrastructure financing deal in India's history. The announcement was timed to coincide with the India AI Impact Summit.

**Key Details:**
- **Total raise:** $1.2 billion ($600M equity + $600M planned debt financing)
- **Lead investor:** Blackstone (majority stake), with up to $600M in equity
- **Co-investors:** Teachers' Venture Growth, TVS Capital, 360 ONE Asset, Nexus Venture Partners
- **Valuation:** $1.4 billion enterprise valuation
- **GPU deployment:** Funding will support deployment of 20,000+ GPUs in India
- **Company profile:** Founded 2023, 110 employees across Mumbai, Bengaluru, and Chennai; provides AI cloud infrastructure to enterprises and government
- **Strategic context:** India is racing to build sovereign AI compute capacity ahead of the AI Impact Summit

**Why It Matters:**
This is the largest AI infrastructure investment in India, and it signals that global private equity sees massive opportunity in building India's domestic AI compute capacity. With 20,000+ GPUs planned for deployment, Neysa is positioning itself as the backbone of India's AI infrastructure — a critical piece of the country's ambition to be self-sufficient in AI compute rather than dependent on U.S. cloud providers.

## Regulatory Updates

### India AI Impact Summit to Set "Shared Roadmap for Global AI Governance"
**Date:** February 16, 2026

The India AI Impact Summit aims to produce a shared roadmap for global AI governance — a significant development given that previous AI governance summits (Bletchley Park, Seoul) were held exclusively in wealthy nations. The summit's governance track will address AI safety, data protection, ethical deployment, and regulatory frameworks tailored for developing economies.

**Key Details:**
- **Governance focus:** AI safety, data protection, ethical deployment, and frameworks for developing economies
- **Legal milestone:** The summit is being described as a potential milestone for India's AI legal and regulatory framework, moving beyond the IT Act
- **Global South perspective:** First major AI governance summit to incorporate perspectives from Global South nations on regulation
- **Policy dialogues:** Centre for Policy Research and Governance (CPRG) hosting key policy dialogues on AI regulation
- **Parallel development:** In the U.S., the Commerce Department has until March 11, 2026, to publish an evaluation of state AI laws that may merit federal preemption under the December 2025 executive order

**Why It Matters:**
AI governance discussions have so far been dominated by Western perspectives. India's summit — with 20 heads of state and 45+ ministerial delegations — could produce the first globally representative framework for AI regulation. This matters because AI deployment in developing economies faces fundamentally different challenges (connectivity, language diversity, digital literacy) that Western-centric frameworks don't address.
