Claude Surges to #1 on App Store as #QuitGPT Movement Erupts; 500 Tech Workers Back Anthropic
ChatGPT uninstalls spike 295% following Pentagon deal; open letter accuses DoD of “divide and conquer” strategy against AI safety
Claude AI
Anthropic’s Claude has overtaken ChatGPT as the number one free application on the United States App Store, marking a dramatic shift in the consumer AI landscape driven by mounting public backlash against OpenAI’s controversial Pentagon partnership. The surge follows a 295% spike in ChatGPT uninstalls, with Claude’s free active users climbing 60% since January and daily sign-ups quadrupling over the same period. The movement, coalescing under the hashtag #QuitGPT on social media, gained powerful institutional support when nearly 500 employees at OpenAI and Google signed an open letter endorsing Anthropic’s refusal to enable mass surveillance or autonomous weapons systems. The signatories accused the Pentagon of pursuing a “divide and conquer” strategy to co-opt leading AI companies. Meanwhile, the Pentagon has labeled Anthropic a “supply chain risk,” potentially forcing military contractors including Palantir—whose $4.5 billion government revenue accounts for 42% of total—to sever ties. Max Schwarzer, who led OpenAI’s post-training team and shipped GPT-5 through 5.3, announced he is joining Anthropic for reinforcement learning research, saying he is “longing to return to IC research work.”
Apple Unveils M5 Chip Family With 4x AI Boost
Apple held simultaneous “Special Apple Experience” events in New York, London, and Shanghai to unveil its most aggressive AI hardware lineup yet. The MacBook Pro with M5 Pro and M5 Max features an 18-core CPU with six “super cores,” delivering up to 4x faster AI performance versus the prior generation and 8x versus M1. The MacBook Air M5 starts at $1,099 with doubled base storage. Pre-orders open today with shipments beginning March 11.
OpenAI Ships GPT-5.3, Teases 5.4 With 2M Context
OpenAI released GPT-5.3 Instant globally, promising “more accurate, less cringe” conversations with fewer unnecessary refusals and a more natural writing style. Just one hour after launch, the company teased GPT-5.4 with the message: “5.4 sooner than you Think.” Three separate code leaks in the Codex GitHub repo revealed a 2-million token context window and full-resolution image processing. Prediction markets give it 55% odds of shipping before April.
Qwen Tech Lead Ousted; Open-Source Future in Doubt
Junyang Lin, architect of Alibaba’s Qwen—the most-forked open-source model family on Hugging Face with 300 million downloads—was pushed out just one day after Qwen 3.5’s launch. Team member Chen Chang wrote: “I know leaving wasn’t your choice.” Multiple researchers followed him out the door. Analysts fear Alibaba may pivot Qwen toward revenue over its open-weight mission. The community response has been swift: “Wherever he lands next, the community will follow.”
