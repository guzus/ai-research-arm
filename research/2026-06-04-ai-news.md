**Date:** Wednesday June 3, 2026 (newsroom reaffirmation; policy originally announced Feb 4, 2026) | **Sources:** [Anthropic Newsroom](https://www.anthropic.com/news), [B&T — Anthropic Plans To List On US Stock Market Despite Claude Remaining Ad-Free](https://www.bandt.com.au/anthropic-plans-to-list-on-us-stock-market-despite-claude-remaining-ad-free/), [CNBC — Anthropic takes aim at OpenAI's ad push in Super Bowl commercial](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/02/04/anthropic-no-ads-claude-chatbot-openai-chatgpt.html)

- Anthropic reiterates that **Claude users will not see "sponsored" links** adjacent to conversations, that **responses won't be influenced by advertisers** or carry third-party product placements, and that it will **avoid ads even in a separate window** — arguing ads create an incentive to optimize for engagement when the most useful interaction is often a short one.
- The reaffirmation lands in Anthropic's **pre-IPO posture** (confidential S-1 filed June 1), positioning the ad-free stance as a durable product/trust commitment heading into public markets — a deliberate contrast with OpenAI's advertising experiments.

**Read-through:** Minor as net-new (the underlying policy is February-old), but notable for *timing*: restating "no ads, ever" days after the confidential S-1 is Anthropic locking in a trust-and-differentiation narrative for IPO investors, betting that subscription/enterprise economics (run-rate guidance toward ~$50B) make ad revenue unnecessary — and that ad-free is a moat against OpenAI's consumer-monetization path.
