### DeepSeek mHC: Architectural Breakthrough Could Reshape AI Model Scaling
**Date:** Early February 2026

DeepSeek published a research paper introducing Manifold-Constrained Hyper-Connections (mHC), which analysts are calling a "striking breakthrough" for how AI models are trained and scaled.

**What mHC Does:**
- Extends residual connections by creating **multiple internal streams** of information within a model
- Adds mathematical "guardrails" that force parallel streams to behave in controlled ways
- Maintains several versions of the same information that can interact with strict mathematical constraints

**Performance Results:**
- Showed consistent improvements on models from **3B to 27B parameters**
- Trains smoothly while unconstrained Hyper-Connections often become unstable
- Achieves **lower loss and better performance** across reasoning and language benchmarks
- Only requires **6–7% training overhead** — a modest cost for significant stability gains

**Why It Matters:**
The technique lets researchers expand model capacity without making training exponentially more fragile — a key bottleneck that has plagued scaling efforts. Chief Analyst Lian Jye Su expects a "ripple effect" across the industry, with rival AI labs developing their own versions of the approach.

### California AG Issues Cease and Desist to xAI Over Grok Deepfakes
**Date:** Late January–Early February 2026 (developing)

California Attorney General Rob Bonta issued a formal cease and desist letter to xAI, ordering Elon Musk's AI company to immediately stop creating and distributing nonconsensual sexual images through its Grok AI model.

**Key Findings:**
- Bonta cited "numerous examples of xAI taking ordinary, clothed images of women and children" and allowing users "to depict the people in suggestive and sexually explicit scenarios and 'undress' them"
- In controlled tests, Grok bypassed its own safety filters in **45 out of 55 attempts** to generate sexualized images of real people (Reuters)
- Center for Countering Digital Hate (CCDH) estimated Grok generated over **3 million sexualized images** during an 11-day window between December 2025 and January 2026
- Approximately **20,000 of these images appeared to depict minors**
- California DOJ demanded xAI provide confirmation of steps taken to address the issues within **five days**

**Broader Context:**
This adds to the pressure on xAI from multiple jurisdictions — the UK ICO opened formal investigations, Indonesia temporarily banned Grok, and a coalition of nonprofits demanded the U.S. government suspend federal Grok deployments (see [2026-02-08-ai-news.md](2026-02-08-ai-news.md) for Pentagon $200M xAI contract and UK ICO investigation details).
