### Apple Crushes Q1 FY2026 Earnings With Record $143.8 Billion Revenue
**Date:** January 29, 2026

Apple reported fiscal Q1 2026 results that shattered expectations, posting all-time records for total revenue, earnings per share, iPhone revenue, and services revenue. The quarter was fueled by extraordinary iPhone 17 demand and a massive China rebound.

**Q1 FY2026 Results (Oct-Dec 2025):**
- **Revenue:** $143.8 billion (vs. $138.35 billion expected), up 16% YoY — all-time record
- **Net Income:** $42.1 billion
- **EPS:** $2.84 (vs. $2.67 expected), up 19% YoY — all-time record
- **Gross Margin:** 48.2% (vs. 46.9% year-ago)
- **Operating Cash Flow:** ~$54 billion
- **Shareholder Returns:** ~$32 billion in the quarter

**Segment Breakdown:**
- **iPhone:** $85.3 billion (vs. $78.3 billion expected), up 23% YoY — all-time record across every geographic segment
- **Services:** All-time revenue record, up 14% YoY (over $30 billion)
- **iPad:** $8.6 billion, up 6% YoY
- **Mac:** $8.4 billion, down 7% YoY

**China Rebound:**
China sales surged 38% to $25.53 billion, driven by record iPhone upgraders and double-digit switcher growth. CEO Tim Cook: "We saw a lift that, frankly, was much greater than we thought we would see... it was product-driven." Apple set an all-time record for upgraders in mainland China, with iPhones ranking as the top three smartphones in urban China during the quarter.

**Key Metrics:**
- Active installed base: 2.5 billion devices (up from 2.35 billion a year ago)
- Tariff costs: $1.4 billion this quarter
- Tim Cook on demand: "The demand for iPhone was just simply staggering"

**Memory Cost Warning:**

**Q2 FY2026 Guidance:**
- Revenue growth: 13-16% YoY (implying $107.8B-$110.7B)

- Operating expenses: $18.4-$18.7 billion

**M5 MacBook Pro Update:**

**Stock Reaction:**

**Analyst Commentary:**

### Microsoft Shares Plunge 10-12% in Biggest Drop Since 2020 on AI Spending Fears

### Microsoft Maia 200 AI Chip Begins Data Center Deployment
**Date:** January 26, 2026

Microsoft began deploying its second-generation custom AI chip, the Maia 200, to data centers — marking the company's most aggressive move to reduce reliance on NVIDIA GPUs for AI inference workloads.

**Specifications:**
- 140 billion transistors on TSMC 3nm process
- 216GB HBM3e memory
- 10+ petaFLOPS in 4-bit precision, ~5 petaFLOPS in 8-bit
- Four chips per server, connected via Ethernet (not InfiniBand)
- Up to 6,144 chips can be wired together

**Performance Claims:**
- 30% higher performance than alternatives at the same price point
- 3x compute performance vs. Amazon's latest Trainium processor on popular AI benchmarks
- Exceeds Google's seventh-generation TPU on some benchmarks
- More HBM per chip than AWS Trainium or Google TPUv7

**Current Deployments:**
- Already running workloads at Microsoft's Des Moines, Iowa data center
- Running: Copilot 365, OpenAI GPT-5.2 models, and internal AI Superintelligence team projects
- Phoenix-area deployments next

**Developer Access:**
Microsoft opened a software development kit for third-party developers, academics, and frontier AI labs — a first, as the original Maia 100 was never made available externally. "Wider customer availability" planned for the future.

**Strategic Significance:**
The Maia 200 focuses specifically on inference rather than training, reflecting Microsoft's calculation that inference costs — which are continuous — will dominate AI economics. The chip directly competes with NVIDIA's inference GPUs, Amazon Trainium, and Google TPUs.

### Global Memory Crunch Hits AI Supply Chain — Apple Confirms Impact
**Date:** January 29, 2026

Apple CEO Tim Cook's disclosure during the Q1 earnings call that DRAM prices are up 55-60% and NAND prices are up 33-38% confirms what industry analysts have been warning: AI-driven demand is creating a structural shortage in the global memory market.

**Impact Across the Supply Chain:**
- DRAM prices up 55-60% (driven by HBM demand for AI accelerators)
- NAND prices up 33-38%
- Apple expects constrained iPhone supply in Q2 due to memory availability
- SK Hynix and Samsung are prioritizing HBM production for AI chips over consumer-grade memory

**AI Connection:**
The memory crunch is directly linked to the explosive growth in AI infrastructure spending. NVIDIA's H200 and Blackwell GPUs require massive amounts of HBM3e memory, and hyperscaler orders (Microsoft $37.5B/quarter, Meta $115-135B/year) are consuming available capacity. This creates a trickle-down effect where consumer electronics companies like Apple face higher component costs and constrained supply.

### LLMjacking Emerges as Major AI Security Threat
**Date:** January 2026

Security researchers have publicly confirmed that threat actors are actively scanning and probing enterprise AI systems for exploitation. A new class of attack called "LLMjacking" — credential theft targeting AI API endpoints — has become prevalent enough to receive its own designation in the cybersecurity community.

**How It Works:**
Attackers exploit misconfigured proxy servers to hijack access to commercial AI endpoints from OpenAI, Anthropic, Meta, Google, Mistral, and xAI. Starting December 28, 2025, two IPs launched a methodical probe of 73+ LLM model endpoints, generating 80,469 sessions over eleven days — systematic reconnaissance hunting for misconfigured proxies that leak access to commercial APIs.

**Financial Impact:**
- Large-scale API abuse can generate costs exceeding $100,000 per day for credential owners
- Victims often don't discover the theft until they receive massive cloud bills
- Microsoft filed a civil lawsuit in 2025 against a gang specializing in stolen LLM credentials

**Industry Response:**
Sysdig's Michael Clark warned: "Compute power will become the new cryptocurrency. Attackers may hijack infrastructure to train LLMs or run autonomous AI agents." 93% of security leaders are bracing for daily AI-powered attacks in 2026.

### Federal AI Policy Framework: Commerce Department Evaluation Due March 11
**Date:** January 29-30, 2026 (Ongoing)

With the Commerce Department's March 11 deadline approaching for its evaluation of "burdensome" state AI laws, the federal-state AI regulation battle is intensifying.

**Key Developments:**
- Senator Marsha Blackburn unveiled the "TRUMP AMERICA AI Act" to codify the December 2025 executive order into legislation
- Colorado AI Act (June 30 effective date) remains the primary federal target — the executive order claims it "forces AI models to produce false results"
- 38 states passed AI legislation in 2025, creating a patchwork that the federal government seeks to preempt
- EU AI Act high-risk system compliance deadline: August 2, 2026

**AI Litigation Task Force:**
The December 2025 executive order directed the Attorney General to establish an AI Litigation Task Force within 30 days. Its mandate is to challenge state AI laws the administration considers inconsistent with maintaining U.S. AI dominance. Key state laws in the crosshairs include California's Transparency in Frontier AI Act and Texas's Responsible AI Governance Act (both effective January 1, 2026).

**FTC Directive:**
The FTC is directed to issue a policy statement by March 11, 2026, describing how the FTC Act applies to AI and when state laws requiring alteration of truthful outputs are preempted by federal law.

**Pentagon GenAI.mil:**
The Department of Defense announced the GenAI.mil platform as part of the White House's AI Action Plan, tapping Alphabet (Google) to lead the initiative with Gemini AI as the backbone.

**Status:** Constitutional scholars continue to note that executive orders cannot preempt state law — only Congressional legislation can do so. The AG's AI Litigation Task Force is expected to begin challenging state laws in Q1 2026.
