## Breaking News

### Meta Launches Llama 4: First Natively Multimodal Open-Weight Models
**Date:** January 20-21, 2026

Meta introduced Llama 4 Scout and Llama 4 Maverick, marking a transformative evolution in the LLaMA model family with natively multimodal capabilities and mixture-of-experts architecture.

**Key Innovations:**
- **Natively Multimodal**: First LLaMA models designed from the ground up for multimodal input/output, moving beyond text-only capabilities
- **Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) Architecture**: First time Meta has implemented MoE in the LLaMA series, enabling more efficient scaling
- **Unprecedented Context Length**: Extended context window support significantly beyond previous LLaMA versions
- **Open-Weight Approach**: Maintains Meta's commitment to open-weight releases despite increasing complexity

**Strategic Significance:**
Llama 4 represents Meta's response to competitors like GPT-4 Vision, Claude 3 with vision capabilities, and Google's multimodal Gemini models. The shift to native multimodality and MoE architecture positions Meta's open ecosystem to compete more directly with closed-source multimodal foundation models while maintaining accessibility for researchers and developers.

**Market Context:**
This launch continues Meta's aggressive LLaMA release cadence (Llama 1: February 2023, Llama 2: July 2023, Llama 3: April 2024, Llama 4: January 2026) and represents the beginning of what Meta calls "a new era of natively multimodal AI innovation."

## Regulatory & Policy Updates

### Trump AI Litigation Task Force Deadlines (January-March 2026)
**Context:** Following President Trump's December 11, 2025 executive order "Ensuring a National Policy Framework for Artificial Intelligence"

**Key January-March 2026 Deadlines:**

**January 10, 2026:**
- Attorney General must establish AI Litigation Task Force to challenge state AI laws deemed inconsistent with federal framework
- Task Force to evaluate state laws for unconstitutional regulation of interstate commerce and federal preemption

**March 11, 2026:**
- Secretary of Commerce must publish evaluation identifying "burdensome" state AI laws conflicting with federal policy
- FTC must issue policy statement classifying state-mandated bias mitigation as per se deceptive trade practice

**Funding Leverage:**
- Department of Commerce instructed to condition $42 billion in BEAD (Broadband Equity, Access and Deployment) program funding on repeal of state AI regulations deemed onerous
- Creates financial pressure on states to align with federal AI policy

**Legal Questions:**
- Constitutional scholars note the Constitution doesn't grant the president power to preempt state regulations through executive order
- Congress has not delegated this authority to the executive branch
- Federal government cannot directly enforce executive order against state laws through litigation based solely on presidential directive
- Executive order characterized by some legal analysts as "more bark than bite"

**State Law Status:**
As of January 1, 2026, state AI laws went into effect including:
- California's Transparency in Frontier Artificial Intelligence Act
- Texas's Responsible Artificial Intelligence Governance Act
- 38 states total with varying AI legislation
- State laws address deepfakes, medical information, employment, and other AI applications

**Industry Impact:**
Companies face continued regulatory uncertainty as they navigate:
- Conflicting federal policy direction and state legal requirements
- Potential for AI Litigation Task Force challenges to state laws
- Unclear timeline for resolution of federal-state conflicts
- Need to maintain compliance across multiple jurisdictions
