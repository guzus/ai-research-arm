**26 current Meta employees** have filed a lawsuit in federal court in
**Oakland, California**, alleging that Meta's internal **"Metamate"**
LLM assistant, combined with employee-tracking and productivity-scoring
tools, was used to **select workers on medical, parental, or family
leave** for inclusion in roughly **8,000 planned layoffs**. The
separations are set to begin **2026-07-22**. The suit alleges violations
of the **Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA)**, the **Family and
Medical Leave Act (FMLA)**, and the **Pregnancy Discrimination Act**.

**Meta's response.** Meta disputes that AI made the layoff decisions.

**Why tracked.** This is reported as the **first lawsuit specifically
challenging AI-driven layoff selection** — a notable AI-governance/legal
precedent case distinct from prior AI-and-employment coverage, and
directly relevant to the broader "AI making consequential HR decisions"
storyline this desk tracks.

**Corroboration read.** Three independent, named outlets — **Fortune**,
**U.S. News**, and **HCAMag** — report matching core facts (plaintiff
count, venue, the Metamate allegation, the ~8,000-layoff context, the
statutory claims), clearing the bar for `status: confirmed`. None of the
three sources links the underlying court docket directly, so
`verification` stays `partial` per the contract's definition (multiple
secondary corroborations, no primary source yet).
