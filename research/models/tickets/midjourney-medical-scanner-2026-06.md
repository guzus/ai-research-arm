On **2026-06-17**, at an in-person San Francisco launch, **Midjourney** —
the community-funded, VC-free AI image lab (~$100M revenue in its first 9
months) — unveiled its **first hardware product**: a **full-body
ultrasound-based scanner** ("Midjourney Scanner"), launched through a new
**Midjourney Medical** division, with the stated goal of making full-body
medical imaging available "to every person on earth." Reporting indicates
Midjourney is pursuing **4 hardware and 4 software projects**.

**Why its own ticket.** A hardware/medical-device reveal from a frontier
AI lab is a **shipping-artifact event** worth tracking, distinct from
Midjourney's image/video model line. It fits a cross-lab 2026 pattern of
AI firms reaching into the physical world (NVIDIA's ENPIRE robotics pitch,
OpenAI's wet-lab chemistry result).

**Confirmed vs. unclear.** The **announcement is confirmed** — carried by
**The Verge** via Techmeme, not just X aggregators — so `verification:
confirmed` on the reveal. But two things are genuinely **unclear**: **how
AI is even involved** (The Verge's own caveat), and whether the device
clears the very different bar of a **regulated medical device**. Because
it is an announced product, not a purchasable/cleared one, status is
`confirmed`, not `released`.
