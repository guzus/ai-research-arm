Two independent accounts — @jukan05 and @grandpazibi, both relaying a
Bloomberg-style report — said on 2026-07-14 that **Samsung Electronics is
exploring a potential American Depositary Receipt (ADR) offering** in the
US, and has held **preliminary discussions with banks**. Both sources are
explicit that **no decision has been made**.

**Why this is its own ticket, and distinct from [[sk-hynix-nasdaq-ipo-2026-07]].**
SK Hynix's Nasdaq listing already priced and closed ($26.5B raised); this is
a separate company (Samsung Electronics) at a much earlier, pre-decision
exploratory stage. Both are part of the same broader theme — Korean
memory/chip suppliers tapping US capital markets around the AI hardware
buildout — but they are different corporate events and should not be
conflated. Also distinct from [[anthropic-samsung-chip-talks-2026-06]],
which tracks Samsung Foundry's reported custom-chip manufacturing talks
with Anthropic, a separate business line from a corporate ADR listing.

**Why `rumored` / `partial`.** No Samsung primary statement, no filed
prospectus, no confirmed offering size or timeline — just two accounts
relaying preliminary, explicitly-undecided bank discussions.

**Transition triggers:**
- A Samsung on-record statement or SEC/exchange filing → UPDATE, advance
  toward `confirmed`.
- A priced ADR offering → UPDATE (status `confirmed`, then `released` once
  shares trade).
- 15+ daily cycles with no fresh corroboration → `closed:
  stale-rumor-unverified`.
