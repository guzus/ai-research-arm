OpenAI **confirmed on record, via its own newsroom**, that it has
**confidentially submitted an S-1** to the SEC, choosing to announce the
filing pre-emptively rather than let it leak. The statement is deliberately
hedged on timing: OpenAI says it has **not decided when (or whether) to go
public**, framing the confidential filing as preserving the *option* to list
sooner if conditions favor it.

This is the rare case where the IPO process itself is **first-party
confirmed** — hence `confirmed` / `verification: confirmed`. What is **not**
confirmed is everything around it: the **up-to-$1T valuation**, the **~$2B/month
revenue** run-rate, a possible **September 2026 debut**, and **Goldman
Sachs / Morgan Stanley** as underwriters all trace to Reuters/FT reporting and
analyst inference, **not** to OpenAI's statement. Treat those as directional.

**Why this is its own ticket.** It crystallizes a 2026 **AI IPO supercycle**:
OpenAI's confidential S-1 lands days after **Anthropic's** 2026-06-01
confidential filing (~$965B, [[anthropic-ipo-2026-06]]) and a week before
**SpaceX's** Nasdaq debut (2026-06-12, [[spacex-ipo-2026-06]]) — roughly
$3.5–4T of combined private valuation lining up for public markets inside a
single quarter. The OpenAI product/IPO-positioning roadmap (the "super app"
overhaul) stays on [[openai-chatgpt-superapp-2026-06]]; the US-government
equity-stake talks stay on [[openai-us-govt-stake-2026-06]].
