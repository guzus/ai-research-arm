**Florida has sued OpenAI and CEO Sam Altman**, becoming the **first US state**
to do so, per The Decoder (2026-06-05). The **83-page complaint** advances a
notable legal theory: it treats **ChatGPT as a consumer product subject to
product-liability law** and as a **public nuisance**, centers on **risks to
minors**, and threatens **billions in penalties**.

**Why this is a ticket.** It is a discrete **legal action** against a frontier
lab — exactly the event class this timeline tracks alongside model releases,
partnerships, and funding rounds. The "AI chatbot as a liable consumer
product" framing, if it survives, could reshape how frontier products are
regulated in the US.

**Verification.** A filed court complaint is a primary artifact, so the
existence of the suit is `confirmed`. (Note: Twitter chatter on 2026-06-06 also
floated this at near-zero reach without a primary; the RSS/The Decoder coverage
of the actual 83-page complaint is the corroborating source here.)
