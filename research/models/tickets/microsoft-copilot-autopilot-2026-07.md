Microsoft reportedly plans to **merge its consumer and enterprise Copilot
apps into a single app in August 2026**, cutting rarely-used features
(e.g. Copilot Podcasts) and introducing new background-task agents called
**"AutoPilot"** for an additional fee, per The Decoder (2026-07-03). The
outlet frames this as Microsoft joining Anthropic and OpenAI in the AI
"super app" race — see the parallel OpenAI thread on
[[openai-chatgpt-superapp-2026-06]].

**Why its own ticket.** This is a distinct Microsoft **product-roadmap**
event (a different product from **Copilot Cowork**, tracked on
[[microsoft-copilot-cowork-2026-06]]) — the consumer/enterprise Copilot
*app* merge and its new agentic "AutoPilot" tier.

**Confirmed vs. sourced.** The plan is reported by a single named tech
outlet → `confirmed` event, but no Microsoft on-record statement, pricing,
or final feature-cut list was captured in-window → `verification:
partial`.

**Transition triggers:**
- A Microsoft on-record confirmation, pricing, or launch date → UPDATE,
  advance verification.
- The August launch actually ships → UPDATE status framing (still
  `confirmed`, note GA in history) or reassess for `released` if this
  ticket should track the shipped product going forward.
- Plan is delayed, denied, or dropped after ≥15 cycles with no
  corroboration → `closed: stale-rumor-unverified`.
