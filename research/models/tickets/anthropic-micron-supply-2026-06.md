The **Micron × Anthropic** pact, announced **2026-06-22**, makes **Micron
Anthropic's primary supplier** of HBM, DRAM, and SSDs under a multi-year
agreement, layered with a **co-design** relationship on AI-optimized
memory/storage architecture and an enterprise Claude deployment across
Micron. Micron's IR desk posted it directly, and Micron stock rose **~5.5%
to a record** on the news.

**The Series H wrinkle.** Micron also **participated in Anthropic's Series H**
([[anthropic-series-h-2026-05]]) on undisclosed terms — the **first named
participant** to surface on that $65B / $965B round, which had been disclosed
without an investor breakdown beyond its leads.

**Why this is its own ticket.** Per the dedup boundary on
[[anthropic-series-h-2026-05]], downstream business deals enabled by the
capital get their own tickets; the round itself records Micron only as a
newly-named participant. This ticket carries the supply + co-design
relationship.

**Why it matters.** It is a **supply-side** move arriving precisely as
Anthropic's frontier models are **suspended under export control**
([[anthropic-fable-mythos-export-control-2026-06]]) — a bet on the company's
compute-scale recovery rather than its near-term model availability, and
pairs with the multi-gigawatt compute commitments on
[[anthropic-spacex-colossus-2026-05]].
