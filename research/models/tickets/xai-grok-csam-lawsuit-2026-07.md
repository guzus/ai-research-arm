**xAI** has sued **Terry Harwood**, a South Carolina resident already
facing separate criminal charges for sexual exploitation of minors, in
**federal court in Texas**. The suit alleges Harwood used **Grok** to
generate **sexually explicit deepfakes of adults and minors** from
uploaded non-sexual images. xAI is seeking damages and a **permanent
injunction**.

**Why tracked.** A frontier AI lab initiating civil litigation against a
user over CSAM-adjacent misuse of its own model is a significant
AI-safety/governance event — both for the precedent of a lab
self-policing via lawsuit and for its child-safety dimension.

**Sourcing caveat and how it was resolved.** The initial viral spread of
this story was a single relay account (@cb_doge) plus Elon Musk's
zero-context retweet — no case name, court, or filing link visible in
that trail, which this desk's skeptic's corner correctly flagged as
insufficient on its own. Independent reporting from **Mezha** and **The
News (Pakistan)** subsequently surfaced concrete case details (the
defendant's name, the venue, and the specific allegations) that
corroborate a genuine underlying legal action. Because Mezha and The
News (Pakistan) are not top-tier US legal/business outlets, verification
stays `partial` rather than `confirmed`, even though `status` clears to
`confirmed` given the converging case-detail corroboration.

**Transition triggers:**
- A named top-tier outlet (Reuters, AP, Bloomberg, a court-filing
