**Apple has sued OpenAI**, alleging a systematic campaign to steal trade
secrets in order to build out OpenAI's hardware business. The suit names
**Chang Liu**, a former Apple employee, and OpenAI's **Chief Hardware
Officer Tang Tan** as central figures in the alleged theft.

**Why this is tracked here.** A major-platform-vs-frontier-lab legal
action over hardware trade secrets bears directly on OpenAI's device
roadmap (the same hardware push tracked contextually alongside
[[openai-jalapeno-chip-2026-06]] and [[openai-codex-hardware-2026-06]]).

**Corroboration.** Two independent, high-authority sources — Andrew
Curran (citing Bloomberg) and The Information — report matching
specifics (named defendants, dates, and allegations), clearing the bar
for `confirmed`. OpenAI issued a public denial, disputing that it has any
interest in other companies' trade secrets.
