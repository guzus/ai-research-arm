A federal judge granted final approval to Anthropic's $1.5 billion
settlement with authors who alleged their books were used without
permission to train Claude. Multiple independent accounts reported the
ruling the same day, describing the ~7M-book training corpus as the
basis for the payout and noting that the court's reasoning distinguishes
training-as-fair-use from downloading/storing pirated copies (the latter
being what triggered liability).

This is a resolution of litigation that has been tracked informally
across the pipeline's legal/policy signal; no prior ticket existed for
the underlying case, so this is filed fresh as the settlement's approval
itself is the concrete, dated event.
