**Sriram Krishnan**, the former a16z partner and a central architect of the
administration's **AI Action Plan**, confirmed he will **leave his White House
senior AI-policy-adviser role at the end of June 2026**, after roughly 18
months as Senior White House Policy Advisor on Artificial Intelligence. ANI
quoted him directly; SCMP carried the report (~20:11 UTC Jun 6); by ~23:43 UTC
AI Czar **David Sacks** had publicly thanked him and credited him as a
co-author of the administration's AI agenda. TechCrunch adds that Krishnan is
**reportedly starting a new institution** to continue shaping Trump's AI
policy from outside government.

**Why this is a ticket.** It is an **executive move that affects the AI-policy
roadmap** — the kind of personnel event this timeline tracks. The departure
matters in context: a policy-leadership seat empties precisely as the
administration is reportedly negotiating an unprecedented government equity
position in OpenAI (see
[openai-us-govt-stake-2026-06](./openai-us-govt-stake-2026-06.md)).

**Why `confirmed`.** This is as close to first-party as a personnel move gets —
the subject confirmed it himself, a senior colleague acknowledged it, and two
independent outlets reported it. No evidence of a forced exit.
