**Anthropic**, together with **Blackstone** and **Hellman & Friedman**,
has launched **Ode**, a standalone **enterprise AI services firm**. Ode
combines Anthropic's models with applied-AI engineering talent — built
on top of Anthropic's previously-acquired **Fractional AI** — aimed at
helping large enterprises deploy AI into core operations rather than
bolt-on pilots.

**Leadership and backers.** Ode is led by **CEO Chris Taylor** and
**CTO Eddie Siegel**. Beyond Blackstone and Hellman & Friedman, reported
backers include **Goldman Sachs**, **General Atlantic**, **Apollo**,
**GIC**, and **Sequoia** — a heavyweight investor bench for a
services-layer spinout rather than a model release.

**Why tracked.** This is a structurally distinct move from a typical
Anthropic model release: a jointly-backed, standalone applied-AI
services company is a new go-to-market vehicle for enterprise adoption,
and worth its own ticket separate from any Anthropic model tickets.

**Corroboration read.** Three independent accounts — @AIStockSavvy,
@Investingcom, @AShmueil — converged on matching details (name,
leadership, backers) within a tight 5-minute window on 2026-07-15,
clearing the bar for `status: confirmed` under the multi-source
corroboration rule. However, **AnthropicAI's own account has not
tweeted about this launch** as of this check — no primary company
confirmation yet — so `verification` stays `partial` rather than
`confirmed` despite the strong secondary corroboration.
