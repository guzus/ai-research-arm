On **2026-06-29**, Secretary of War **Pete Hegseth** appointed **Marc
Andreessen** to the Pentagon's newly **reconstituted Defense Policy Board**
(reported by @AndrewCurran_, ~1.2K likes; independently relayed by
@Polymarket and news accounts). The seat adds formal defense-advisory
standing to the a16z founder's existing perch on **PCAST**, the presidential
science council, alongside other tech principals.

**Why a separate ticket.** This is a discrete **exec/policy move** — the
event class this timeline tracks for moves that affect frontier-model access
and roadmaps. It places the loudest AI-policy voice inside the body that
helps decide who receives the gated frontier models
([[anthropic-fable-mythos-export-control-2026-06]], [[openai-gpt-5-6]]).

**Confirmed vs. inference.** The appointment is multi-sourced (named
official + multiple relays) → `confirmed` / `verification: confirmed`. Two
caveats: **no primary DoD/Pentagon press release** naming the board's full
roster and mandate was surfaced this cycle, and Curran's follow-up that the
seat confers **"Mythos access via the Department of War"** is **his
inference, not a confirmed grant**. Andreessen's documented stance is
pro-open-source-AI while restricting *Chinese* models, so reading the seat
purely as a "lock down the frontier" signal is premature.

**Context.** It lands atop the week's frontier-gating arc — Washington
clearing Mythos 5 for ~100 critical-infrastructure orgs and signing off on
GPT-5.6 access client-by-client. The access regime and its gatekeepers are
converging.
