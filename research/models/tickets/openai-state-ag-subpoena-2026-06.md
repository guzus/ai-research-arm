A **coalition of US state attorneys general, led by New York**, served
**OpenAI** with a broad **subpoena on Friday 2026-06-12**, demanding
documents across an unusually wide surface: **advertising, user
engagement/retention, handling of consumer and health data, activities
involving minors and seniors, deep-learning models, model sycophancy, and
company policies**.

**Why it's tracked here.** This is a discrete **legal/regulatory action**
against a frontier lab — the event class this timeline tracks alongside
releases, funding, and acquisitions. It is a roadmap-relevant pressure on
OpenAI, not a model artifact, so it gets its own ticket rather than rolling
into the product or IPO tickets.

**What's confirmed vs. reported.** The core fact — a multi-state AG
subpoena served Friday — is corroborated by WSJ-attributed reporting,
TechCrunch, the NYT, and AI reporter @AndrewCurran_, clearing `confirmed`.
The widely-cited **~42-state** count and the exact document scope trace
mostly to secondary accounts; the NY AG's filing is not yet public, so
`verification` stays `partial` until the primary filing or OpenAI's
response lands.

**Why it matters.** It arrives the same week as the Anthropic
export-control shutdown ([[anthropic-fable-mythos-export-control-2026-06]])
and against OpenAI's pending IPO ([[openai-ipo-2026-06]]) — concentrating
"AI regulatory pressure" into the cycle's dominant frame across two labs at
once. The sycophancy and minors/seniors angles echo the consumer-harm
themes also live in OpenAI's Florida lawsuit
([[openai-florida-lawsuit-2026-06]]).

- OpenAI files a formal response, or the named participating states / NY
