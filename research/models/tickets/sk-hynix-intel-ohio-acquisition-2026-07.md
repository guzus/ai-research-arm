**SK Hynix** — the HBM (high-bandwidth memory) supplier underpinning
Nvidia's AI accelerator supply chain, and already tracked here for its
Nasdaq capital raise ([[sk-hynix-nasdaq-ipo-2026-07]]) — is reportedly in
negotiations to **acquire Intel's Ohio One semiconductor campus**. Per the
report, SK Hynix aims to begin front-end memory-chip production at the
site within five years, contingent on government approval.

**Why this is its own ticket, not an update to [[sk-hynix-nasdaq-ipo-2026-07]].**
That ticket tracks SK Hynix's $26.5B Nasdaq share sale — a capital-raise
event. This is a separate manufacturing-capacity acquisition targeting a
specific Intel fab site; different transaction, different strategic
question (memory production capacity vs. public equity capital).

**Why it's tracked.** HBM supply is a direct bottleneck on AI accelerator
production, and a major fab acquisition by a top-two HBM supplier is
directly relevant to the AI hardware supply-chain theme this timeline
tracks alongside [[anthropic-micron-supply-2026-06]] and
[[google-frozen-v2-chip-2026-07]].
