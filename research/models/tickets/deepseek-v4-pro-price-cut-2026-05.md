DeepSeek converted its temporary 75% V4-Pro discount — originally set to
expire 2026-05-31 — into a **permanent** price structure, reported across
multiple outlets (Bloomberg, Engadget, The Tech Portal) on 2026-05-23 and
echoed on X. Uncached input drops from roughly **$1.74 → $0.435 per
MTok** and output from **$3.48 → $0.87 per MTok**.

This is a pricing/competitive event rather than a model release, but it
sits squarely on the hyperscaler roadmap: DeepSeek is pressing a
cost-efficiency advantage at the moment Western labs are drawing
complaints over rate limits and API costs. Coverage frames V4-Pro as a
leader in "intelligence per dollar," undercutting comparable OpenAI and
Anthropic tiers on benchmark cost.

The structural enabler reported alongside the cut is hardware: the V4
series is DeepSeek's first major family **optimized for Huawei Ascend**
(950 / 950PR supernodes) instead of Nvidia, which the reporting ties to
DeepSeek's confidence in sustaining lower prices permanently. Separately,
DeepSeek-V4-Flash was cited as topping global model-usage rankings the
same week.
