**Meta has reportedly begun unwinding its ~$2 billion acquisition of
Manus** after **Beijing ordered the deal reversed**, per TechCrunch
reporting surfaced 2026-06-14. The move is a **geopolitical M&A reversal**:
Chinese authorities demanded the transaction be undone, and Meta has
started dismantling it.

**Why it's tracked here.** A forced unwind of a multi-billion-dollar AI
acquisition under state pressure is a discrete corporate/regulatory event
that materially shapes a frontier player's roadmap — the class this
timeline tracks alongside releases, funding, and ordinary acquisitions.

**Why `confirmed` / `partial`.** The unwind is reported as fact by a tier-1
tech outlet (TechCrunch), clearing `confirmed` for the event. It is, in
this window, **single-outlet** with no captured primary statement from
Meta, Manus, or the Chinese regulator, and the terms/mechanism/asset
disposition are undisclosed — so `verification` stays `partial`.

**Context.** It is another data point in the **cross-border AI-deal
friction** story, parallel to the US-side China-outbound-deal rules tracked
on [[china-outbound-deal-rules-2026-06]] — both sides increasingly using
deal review to police strategically sensitive AI assets.

  `verification`.
- The unwind completes, or is contested/abandoned → UPDATE.
