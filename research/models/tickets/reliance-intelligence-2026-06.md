At **Reliance Industries' 49th AGM on 2026-06-19**, Mukesh Ambani declared
that **Reliance Intelligence** — the group's AI venture announced last year —
has moved **"from planning to execution."** This ticket tracks that buildout
as a concrete infrastructure-and-partnership event, alongside the Jio
Platforms IPO filing announced the same day.

**The compute buildout.** Reliance committed to commissioning a **first 120 MW**
of AI compute in **Jamnagar by the end of 2026**, with the first phase built
on an **NVIDIA GB300** fleet (compute equivalent to **~75,000 H100 GPUs** for
inference) and headroom to scale to **more than 200,000 H100-equivalent GPUs** —
positioning it among the world's larger AI compute platforms. It is to be
**powered entirely by renewable energy** from Reliance's Kutch solar
generation, framed as "India's sovereign AI backbone."

**The partnership prongs.** Two external deals anchor the strategy: **Google
AI Pro, powered by Gemini, made free to hundreds of millions of Jio users**
(a major consumer-distribution channel for Gemini), and a **Meta joint venture**
focused on **operationalising the open-source Llama family** for Indian
enterprises. Reliance also showcased a **multilingual AI application suite** —
JioBharatIQ, JioVyapar, JioHealthIQ, JioLearnIQ, JioKrishiIQ — targeting
consumers, merchants, healthcare, education, and agriculture across **22 Indian
languages**.

**The IPO.** Separately and the same day, **Jio Platforms filed draft papers
(DRHP)** for what could become **India's largest-ever IPO** — a fresh issue of
~270M shares potentially raising **~₹40,000 crore**, with ~₹27,500 crore
earmarked to prepay Reliance Jio Infocomm borrowings. Brokerages had previously
estimated Jio Platforms at **$120-150 billion**.
