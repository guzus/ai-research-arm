On **2026-06-19**, **John Jumper** — the researcher who led **AlphaFold**,
shared the **2024 Nobel Prize in Chemistry**, and was one of **Google
DeepMind's most senior science figures** — posted on his **own verified
account** that he is **leaving Google DeepMind to join Anthropic**.
**Demis Hassabis acknowledged the departure on the record** the same day,
quote-tweeting Jumper's post with a public send-off.

**Why its own ticket.** This is a senior **exec/talent move** of the kind
this timeline tracks alongside releases, partnerships, and acquisitions.
Jumper is not a generic hire: he is a Nobel-laureate research leader whose
move signals where the **AI-for-science** frontier is consolidating, and
his departure from DeepMind — the lab that built AlphaFold around him — is
materially notable.

**Confirmed vs. reported.** The **move itself is confirmed** — first-party
on Jumper's account and acknowledged on-record by Hassabis — so
`verification: confirmed`. What remains thin is the **exact Anthropic
remit** (which science / AI-for-science org he will lead or join); Jumper
described his motivation in broad terms but Anthropic has not published an
org-chart placement in-window.

**Context.** Lands one cycle after **Noam Shazeer's Google→OpenAI** defection
([[openai-shazeer-hire-2026-06]]) and the same week Anthropic's frontier
models sit US export-embargoed
([[anthropic-fable-mythos-export-control-2026-06]]). Read together, the
frontier labs are visibly trading the small set of people who can lead a
training run or a science program — and Anthropic is the net gainer this week.
