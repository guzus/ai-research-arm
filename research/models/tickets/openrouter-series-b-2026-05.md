**OpenRouter** closed a **$113M Series B at a $1.3B post-money
valuation** in late-May 2026, led by **CapitalG (Alphabet)** with
**NVentures (NVIDIA)** participating. The round became Hacker News
front-page material from roughly **2026-05-31 02:56 UTC** at 223
points, with broad Twitter relay through 2026-05-31 and into 2026-06-01.

**The growth disclosures around the round:**

- **Weekly processed token volume jumped 5× to 25 trillion tokens.**
- **On track for >1 quadrillion tokens in 2026.**
- **400+ models** routed.
- **8M+ developers** on the platform.
- The prior-cycle @deedydas claim, recirculating in this funding
  window: OpenRouter accounts for roughly **15–30% of Google APIs,
  20–40% of OpenAI, >50% of Microsoft Azure Foundry**. Those numbers
  are venture-aggregator estimates, not OpenRouter-disclosed.

**The investors are the strategic frame.** CapitalG (Alphabet's
growth fund) and NVentures (NVIDIA's strategic venture arm) sit on
**both ends of the token economy** — Alphabet supplies the model
(Gemini) and NVIDIA supplies the inference silicon. A funded model-
router with both labels on the cap table is a cleaner cross-vendor
arbitration point than either Alphabet or NVIDIA hosting model
routing themselves.

**Why this is its own ticket** (not folded into a generic AI-funding
bucket): OpenRouter is structurally the **third routing layer** in
the AI stack — after frontier-lab inference (Anthropic, OpenAI, Google,
xAI) and inference startups (Fireworks $800M ARR, Baseten, Modal,
Together AI). The CapitalG + NVentures syndicate signals that the
routing margin is treated as fundable infrastructure by *both* the
model lab and the silicon supplier — a distinct strategic story from
the inference-startup margin-pressure narrative The Information ran
2026-05-31 14:30 UTC.

**Why `confirmed` rather than `partial`:** the round size, valuation,
and lead investor are consistent across multiple in-window relays
plus the HN front-page coverage; CapitalG and NVentures are named on
the funding-announcement metadata. Upgrade to a primary OpenRouter
blog post / @openrouterai disclosure UPDATES this ticket but does not
change verification level.

**Skeptic note on the "next-cloud" framing.** Real growth (5× weekly
tokens), real funding ($113M Series B), real syndicate (CapitalG +
NVentures) — but the **"next cloud-computing layer"** framing is
venture-aggregator language, not an operating-margin disclosure.
OpenRouter is a routing margin on third-party token sales; its
competitive moat against direct frontier-lab partnerships is real but
not yet stress-tested.
