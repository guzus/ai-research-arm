A **German regional court** ruled that **Google is directly liable for the
content of its AI search overviews**. The court held that the limited-liability
shield search engines have long relied on **does not extend to AI-generated
summaries** — a landmark precedent for **AI-output accountability** in the EU.

**Why this is a ticket.** It is a discrete **legal action** with direct
roadmap and product implications for a frontier lab's flagship surface — the
event class this timeline tracks alongside releases, partnerships, and funding
(cf. [[openai-florida-lawsuit-2026-06]]). If the holding stands, AI providers
own the legal risk of what their **generative summaries assert**, not merely
what they index and link — a materially different liability posture than
classic search.

**Verification.** A court ruling is a primary artifact, so the existence and
substance of the decision are `confirmed`. The specific downstream
consequences (appeal, EU-wide effect, damages) are not yet determined.
