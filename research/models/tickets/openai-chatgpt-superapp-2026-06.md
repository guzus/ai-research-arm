The **Financial Times** reported (Jun 6–7, 2026) that OpenAI is preparing the
**largest redesign of ChatGPT since its 2022 launch**, recasting it as a
**"super app"** that blends **coding tools and AI agents** into a single,
higher-margin product — explicitly framed around a **future public listing**
for the roughly **$850bn** company. The item was relayed through the cycle by
Reuters and aggregators (@Polymarket ≈389 likes) plus dozens of lower-reach
echoes, all tracing to the one FT story.

**Why `rumored` / `partial`.** Every version traces to a **single FT report**;
OpenAI has **not confirmed**, and **no feature list, pricing, or ship date** is
attached. FT is a credible primary-journalism source, which lifts this above a
single-tweet tease (hence `partial`, not `unverified`), but a reported strategy
relayed by volume is roadmap-talk, not a shipped product (hence `rumored`).
