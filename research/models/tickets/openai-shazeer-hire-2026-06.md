On **2026-06-18**, **Noam Shazeer** — co-author of *Attention Is All You
Need*, T5 and the Switch Transformer, a pioneer of sparse-MoE models, and
Google's **Gemini co-lead / VP of Engineering** — posted on his **own
verified account** that he is **joining OpenAI**. Within hours **Sam
Altman confirmed the hire on the record**: "noam is one of the people I
have most wanted to work with since the very beginning of openai. only
took 10 years."

**Why its own ticket.** This is a senior **exec/talent move** that
directly bears on model roadmaps — the kind of event this timeline tracks
alongside releases and acquisitions. It **unwinds the ~$2.7B deal Google
struck in late 2024** to bring Shazeer back from Character.AI and make
him Gemini co-lead.

**Confirmed vs. reported.** The **move itself is confirmed** — first-party
on Shazeer's account and corroborated on-record by Altman — so
`verification: confirmed`. What remains *reported* (not self-stated) is
the **exact OpenAI role**, "lead of architecture research," carried via
Reuters / The Information relays. The "bearish for Gemini / what's going
on at Google" framing is commentary; Google retains DeepMind depth, but
losing a named Transformer author and Gemini co-lead to the direct rival
is a real, material defection.

**Context.** Lands the same week Anthropic's frontier models sit
US-embargoed ([[anthropic-fable-mythos-export-control-2026-06]]) and
Musk's stack moved to field its own coding model
([[cursor-spacexai-model-2026-06]]) — the frontier labs are visibly
competing for the small pool of people who can architect a training run.
