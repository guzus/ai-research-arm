## Intelligence Notes

- **Gemini 3 Pro EOL is TODAY (Mar 9).** Google is shutting down Gemini 3 Pro Preview on AI Studio today. The `gemini-pro-latest` alias already moved to 3.1 Pro on Mar 6. Vertex AI users may have until Mar 23. Logan Kilpatrick told developers "we need to defragment compute" — 3.1 Pro's ARC-AGI-2 score is more than double 3 Pro's. Developers report 503 errors and latencies up to 104s on 3.1 Pro.
- **DeepSeek V4 status: overdue and increasingly doubted.** Both TechNode's "this week" (Mar 2-7) and FT's "first week of March" windows have now closed. DeepSeek's official site still shows V3.2 as latest. Twitter skeptics are calling media "sources" financial speculation. However, the model is confirmed by multiple independent sources and community consensus still expects imminent release. The V4 merges V-series and R-series into one model, potentially making DeepSeek R2 redundant.
- **NVIDIA GTC 2026 in 7 days (Mar 16-19).** Jensen Huang keynote Mar 16 at 11am PT. Nemotron 3 Super (100B) remains the most likely model announcement. Early adopters include Cursor, Perplexity, and ServiceNow. Q1 window for Super closes Mar 31.
- **OpenAI employees hinting at new "omni" model.** Multiple tweet signals today reference OpenAI developing a new omni model handling text/image/video/audio natively. This could be a GPT-5.5 or a separate product line. The "BiDi" bidirectional audio model also in development for H2 2026 hardware device launch.
- **Gemma 4 leak: ~120B total, ~15B active.** Tweet from @legit_api (646 likes) claims Gemma 4 is coming soon with MoE architecture at 120B/15B. No official Google confirmation. Given annual Gemma cadence, a late-2026 release is more likely than near-term.
- **GPT-5.4 computer use shifts competitive dynamics.** OpenAI's first general-purpose model with native computer use directly challenges Anthropic's computer use in Claude and Google's capabilities. The 75.0% OSWorld-Verified score surpassing the 72.4% human baseline is a significant milestone. ChatGPT reportedly lost ~1.5M users after DoD partnership announcement — GPT-5.4 is seen as a "desperate need of a win" (Gizmodo).
- **Apple Siri AI team restructured.** John Giannandrea removed from Siri leadership, Vision Pro chief Mike Rockwell now leads under Federighi. The hybrid old/new architecture failed ~1/3 of test cases. iOS 26.4 still targeted for late March/April but full LLM Siri features may be staggered through iOS 26.5 and iOS 27.
- **Claude Sonnet 5 "Fennec" status unchanged.** Third-party coverage continues to treat it as released (Feb 3), but Anthropic's official channels still show 4.5/4.6 as latest. Third-party analysis confirms 82.1% SWE-bench and $3/$15 pricing — 50% cheaper than Opus while outperforming it on coding. No new signals today.
- **Meta Llama 4 Behemoth — internal turmoil.** Beyond the model delays, Meta is contemplating "significant management changes" to the AI product group. Smaller Llama models (Scout, Maverick) improved faster than expected, narrowing the gap and reducing justification for Behemoth's release. Meta may pivot to Llama 4.5 instead of jumping to Llama 5.
- **Mistral Large 3 Reasoning still pending.** Announced as "coming soon" in Dec 2025 alongside the Mistral 3 family. No March 2026 update. Mistral's focus appears to be on enterprise distribution (Azure Foundry integration) rather than new model releases.
- **Cohere IPO trajectory accelerating.** $240M ARR, Aston Martin F1 partnership, Command A Vision/Translate, Embed 4, Tiny Aya multilingual models, Rerank 4, and Model Vault for enterprise VPC. CFO hire with IPO experience. 2026 IPO widely anticipated.

- **Pricing floor continues dropping.** Gemini 3.1 Flash-Lite at $0.25/M. GPT-5.4 at $2.50/M delivers flagship performance at former mid-tier pricing. Phi-4-reasoning-vision trained with 5x less data on MIT license. Open-source alternatives (Qwen 3.5, MiniMax M2.5, Nemotron 3 Nano) pushing zero-cost frontier.
