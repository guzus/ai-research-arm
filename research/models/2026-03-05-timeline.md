## Intelligence Notes

- **GPT-5.4 RELEASED TODAY (Mar 5).** OpenAI shipped GPT-5.4, GPT-5.4 Thinking, and GPT-5.4 Pro — fulfilling the "sooner than you think" tease from Mar 3 (the capital "T" theories were close — it was Thursday). This is OpenAI's first general-purpose model with native computer use, directly competing with Anthropic's computer use capabilities. Key numbers: 75.0% OSWorld-Verified (surpassing 72.4% human baseline), 83.0% GDPval (vs Opus 4.6's 78.0%), 1M token context window, 33% fewer false claims vs GPT-5.2. Pricing at $2.50/$15 per M tokens undercuts Opus 4.6 while delivering competitive benchmarks. Financial plugins for Excel/Google Sheets signal aggressive enterprise positioning. GPT-5.4 Thinking replaces GPT-5.2 Thinking as the default reasoning model for Plus/Team/Pro users.
- **DeepSeek V4 enters Day 3 of Two Sessions without release (Mar 5 19:39 UTC).** The "this week" window from TechNode runs through Mar 7. Multiple delays (mid-Feb, Lunar New Year, late Feb) have preceded this window. Meanwhile, Anthropic publicly alleged industrial-scale capability extraction by DeepSeek across 16M interactions using ~24K unauthorized accounts — adding a controversy dimension to the release. The model remains the most anticipated open-weight release, with Polymarket active on a Mar 31 deadline.
- **Claude Sonnet 5 "Fennec" status remains ambiguous.** Multiple third-party sources claim a Feb 3 release with 82.1% SWE-Bench (would be SOTA), 1M context, and $3/$15 pricing. A Vertex AI log leaked the model ID claude-sonnet-5@20260203. However, Anthropic has not made an official blog announcement, and current official model listings show Claude 4.5/4.6 as the latest family. Status remains "In Testing" — either a limited/soft launch occurred or release is imminent.
- **GPT-5.4 vs Opus 4.6: head-to-head benchmark shift.** GPT-5.4 outperforms Opus 4.6 on GDPval (83.0% vs 78.0%) and OSWorld-Verified (75.0% vs unknown). Opus 4.6 still leads on SWE-bench Verified (~80.8%) and Chatbot Arena coding (Elo 1545). The computer use race is now a three-way contest between OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google.
- **AWS-OpenAI $50B partnership context.** Amazon investing $50B in OpenAI; co-creating a Stateful Runtime Environment on Amazon Bedrock; AWS becomes exclusive third-party cloud for OpenAI Frontier; $100B infrastructure expansion over 8 years with 2GW Trainium commitment. Today's GPT-5.4 release is the first major model drop under this new partnership structure.
- **Apple LLM Siri may slip to May.** Bloomberg reports the LLM Siri update has been internally pushed back from March/April to May or later. Apple officially reaffirms "on track for 2026" without giving specifics. The second-gen Siri architecture rebuild required starting from scratch on conversation management and app framework.
- **Grok 5 timeline conflicting.** xAI confirmed Q1 2026 arrival but delayed to Q2. Public beta likely Mar-Apr 2026. At 6T parameters it would be the largest publicly announced model. GPT-5.4's release today adds pressure to deliver.
- **NVIDIA expanding Nemotron 3 with specialized models.** New Nemotron Speech (ASR), Nemotron RAG (multimodal embed/rerank VLMs), and Nemotron Safety (PII detection, content safety) models supplement the existing Nano and Telco variants. Super (100B) and Ultra (500B) still expected H1 2026.
- **Mistral 3 family** (675B Large 3 + 14B/8B/3B dense models) is their most capable generation, with Vibe 2.0 coding agent and Devstral 2 released in Feb. Large 3 ranked #2 in OSS non-reasoning models on LMArena.
- **Microsoft Copilot Chat entering preview in March** with Agent Mode in Word, Excel, and PowerPoint. Phi-4 reasoning family available including vision-15B for edge multimodal reasoning.
- **Pricing pressure continues to intensify.** GPT-5.4 at $2.50/1M input is competitive with Gemini 3.1 Flash-Lite ($0.25/1M) and MiniMax M2.5 ($0.30/1M) at the low end, while delivering flagship-tier performance. The gap between "cheap" and "good" continues to narrow.
- **ARC-AGI-3 Launch Party set for March 25** at Y Combinator, with Sam Altman fireside. New benchmark expected to drive the next wave of model improvements.
- **Cohere positioned for 2026 IPO** at $240M ARR, with Tiny Aya multilingual family, Rerank 4, and Command A Vision as latest releases. Joined Aston Martin F1 as AI partner.
- **Meta reportedly signing multi-billion TPU deal with Google** to diversify AI compute away from NVIDIA dependency, with purchases potentially starting 2027. Llama 4 Behemoth remains in testing with no release date — iRoPE architecture supports up to 10M token context.
