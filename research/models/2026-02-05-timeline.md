## Intelligence Notes

### BREAKING: Dual Frontier Launch — Feb 5, 2026

- **Claude Opus 4.6 and GPT-5.3-Codex released within 30 minutes of each other.** This is the most significant same-day frontier model release in AI history. Both companies dropped major upgrades simultaneously, igniting immediate benchmark comparison wars on Twitter.

- **Benchmark overlap is minimal** — only Terminal-Bench 2.0 and OSWorld appear in both companies' reported metrics. GPT-5.3-Codex leads Terminal-Bench 2.0 (77.3% vs 65.4%); Opus 4.6 leads OSWorld (72.7% vs 64.7%) and SWE-bench Verified (80.8% vs 56.8% SWE-bench Pro — different variants). Industry consensus: **Codex is the better terminal agent; Claude is the better computer use agent and generalist.**

- **GPT-5.3-Codex "helped build itself"** — Sam Altman stated the model participated in its own improvement, a notable milestone for recursive self-improvement. Also the first OpenAI model rated "high" for cybersecurity on the preparedness framework, prompting a $10M cyber defense commitment.

- **Claude Opus 4.6 introduces Agent Teams** — multiple AI agents working in parallel (code writing, reviewing, testing simultaneously). Combined with 1M context window (beta) and 128K output, this positions Opus 4.6 as the enterprise generalist champion. Also: PowerPoint integration research preview and availability on Azure Foundry.

- **OpenAI Frontier Platform** launched alongside GPT-5.3-Codex — enterprise platform for building, deploying, and managing AI coworkers. Sam Altman: "The companies that succeed in the future are going to make very heavy use of AI."
