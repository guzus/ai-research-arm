## Intelligence Notes

- **GPT-5.4 details confirmed by The Information (Mar 4).** The report reveals an "extreme" reasoning mode designed for researchers, enabling hours-long deliberation on complex problems. Context window expands to 1M tokens (2.5x GPT-5.2's 400K). The model shows improved memory retention across multi-step tasks and lower error rates. OpenAI is shifting to a monthly release cadence. Combined with three Codex leak incidents and the "sooner than you think" tease, GPT-5.4 is now the most-discussed upcoming release. Manifold: 55% before April, 74% before June.
- **DeepSeek V4 remains unreleased as of Mar 4 19:09 UTC.** A community HuggingFace monitor confirmed zero new repos or commits across all 34 DeepSeek repositories in the past 4+ hours. Day one of China's "Two Sessions" has passed without a release. TechNode's "this week" window still holds through Mar 7. Polymarket remains active for V4 by March 31. J.P. Morgan continues to warn of a "DeepSeek Part Two moment" for markets.
- **Google shipped TWO major models on Mar 3: Gemini 3 Flash AND Gemini 3.1 Flash-Lite.** Gemini 3 Flash is now the default model in the Gemini app and AI Mode in Search. It achieves 78% SWE-bench Verified (outperforming Gemini 3 Pro on coding), 86.9% Video-MMMU, and is 3x faster than 2.5 Pro with 30% fewer thinking tokens. Gemini 3 Pro will shut down on March 9. Flash-Lite at $0.25/1M input is the cheapest Gemini 3 model.
- **Grok 5 now projected for Q2 2026** — delayed from Q1. At 6T parameters it would be the largest publicly announced model. Training continues on the Colossus 2 supercluster. Meanwhile Grok 4.20 Beta 2 posted record benchmarks: 50.7% Humanity's Last Exam (SOTA), 61.9% USAMO'25, 15.9% ARC-AGI V2 for closed models. Elon Musk posted "Grok hits new high" (40K+ likes) citing surging Google Trends interest.
- **Yuan 3.0 Ultra (1010B) appeared today** — a Chinese open-source MoE model from Yuan Lab with only 68.8B active params. Key innovation is Layer-Adaptive Expert Pruning (LAEP), which prunes 33% of parameters during pretraining while boosting training efficiency by 49%. Claims to beat GPT-5.2, Gemini 3.1 Pro, and Opus 4.6 on RAG benchmarks. Notable for MoE self-compression during training rather than post-training.
- **Four major releases on the same day (Mar 3)** — GPT-5.3 Instant, Gemini 3 Flash, Gemini 3.1 Flash-Lite, and Grok 4.20 Beta 2. Highest single-day release count tracked.
- **Mistral 3 family** (675B Large 3 + 14B/8B/3B dense models) is their most capable generation. Large 3 ranked #2 in OSS non-reasoning models on LMArena. Supports 256K context, multimodal, open weights (Apache 2.0). Small models run on devices with as little as 4GB VRAM.
- **Microsoft Phi-4 reasoning family** now available — Phi-4-reasoning, Phi-4-reasoning-plus, and Phi-4-mini-reasoning. New today: Phi-4-reasoning-vision-15B, a compact multimodal reasoning model pushing the compute/perf Pareto frontier for edge applications. Available on Azure AI Foundry and HuggingFace.
- **Meta reportedly signing multi-billion TPU deal with Google** to diversify AI compute away from NVIDIA dependency. Meta may begin purchasing TPUs for its own data centers as early as 2027. Signals a structural shift from GPU dominance to multi-platform compute strategies.
- **Cohere launched Rerank 4** with 32K context windows and SOTA retrieval across 100+ languages, alongside Model Vault for secure enterprise deployment. Cohere has reached $240M ARR and is positioning for a 2026 IPO.
- **Amazon Nova 2 family** (Lite, Sonic, Omni, Pro) launched December 2025. Nova 2 Omni is the industry's first reasoning model with native text+image+video+speech input and text+image output. Nova Forge enables custom model training. No new Amazon model releases in Feb–Mar 2026.
- **Pricing pressure continues to intensify.** Gemini 3.1 Flash-Lite at $0.25/1M input, MiniMax M2.5 at $0.30/1M, DeepSeek V4 rumored at $0.27/1M. Gemini 3 Flash offers Pro-grade reasoning at Flash-level pricing. The floor for high-quality inference continues to drop.
- **ARC-AGI-3 Launch Party set for March 25** at Y Combinator, with Sam Altman fireside moderated by @deedydas. New benchmark expected to drive the next wave of model improvements.
- **Claude topped the App Store** as ChatGPT faces user backlash over a Pentagon defense deal. Consumer sentiment shift notable.
- **Alibaba's Qwen technical lead Junyang Lin departed** after shipping the entire Qwen 3.5 generation. Multiple observers note the pattern of technical leaders finishing product cycles then departing when the next cycle requires resources the org won't provide.
