## Intelligence Notes

- **BREAKING: NVIDIA Nemotron 3 Super released today (Mar 11).** The 120B-parameter hybrid Mamba-Transformer MoE model is now available with open weights, marking NVIDIA's biggest open-model release to date. 12B active parameters, 1M token context, 5x throughput over predecessor. NVIDIA also open-sourced the full training stack: 10T+ tokens of pre/post-training data, 15 RL environments, and evaluation recipes. The model powers the AI-Q research agent to #1 on DeepResearch Bench. Available immediately on build.nvidia.com, HuggingFace, OpenRouter, Perplexity, and multiple cloud partners. This was released ahead of GTC 2026 (Mar 16-19); Jensen Huang's keynote is expected to additionally unveil NemoClaw and provide Nemotron 3 Ultra (500B) timelines.
- **NemoClaw announcement expected at GTC keynote (Mar 16).** NVIDIA's open-source enterprise AI agent platform has been pitched to Salesforce, Cisco, Google, Adobe, and CrowdStrike. Hardware-agnostic (runs on NVIDIA, Intel, AMD). Inspired by the open-source "claw" agent movement (OpenClaw/Clawdbot/Moltbot, acquired by OpenAI). Code availability may lag the announcement by a quarter.
- **DeepSeek V4 status: 9+ days overdue, but V4-Lite surfaced and Manifold confidence rising.** Manifold now at 88% for V4 in March (up from ~69% yesterday). "V4 Lite" (codename "sealion-lite") quietly appeared on DeepSeek's website Mar 9 — ~200B params, 1M context, native multimodal, notable SVG generation. DeepSeek has not officially confirmed V4-Lite or provided specs. Possible "dual release" of V4 + R2 reasoning model. Reuters reports DeepSeek withheld early builds from US GPU vendors (Nvidia, AMD), possibly violating export rules by training on Blackwell GPUs inside China.
