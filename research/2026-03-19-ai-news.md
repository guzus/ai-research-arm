## Breaking News

### NVIDIA GTC 2026 Wraps — Cosmos 3 World Model, Feynman Architecture Roadmap, and Exhibit Hall Demos
**Date:** March 19, 2026 | **Sources:** [NVIDIA Blog](https://blogs.nvidia.com/blog/gtc-2026-news/), [Deeper Insights](https://deeperinsights.com/ai-blog/nvidia-gtc-2026-highlights/), [NVIDIA Newsroom](https://nvidianews.nvidia.com/online-press-kit/gtc-2026-news)

NVIDIA's GTC 2026 concluded its four-day run (March 16–19) with Day 4 focused on live demos, partner showcases, and forward-looking roadmap reveals:

- **Cosmos 3**: NVIDIA unveiled the first unified foundational model for **world generation, visual reasoning, and action simulation**. Designed to accelerate general robotic intelligence by generating synthetic worlds for training autonomous systems. Paired with **Cosmos Evaluator** for scoring generative video outputs.
- **Feynman Architecture (2028 roadmap)**: Next-generation platform after Vera Rubin, featuring **Rosa CPU** (named for Rosalind Franklin), **LP40 LPU** co-developed with Groq for dedicated inference, **BlueField-5 DPU**, CX10 SuperNIC, and **Kyber rack-scale systems** with copper and co-packaged optics. Will use **3D die-stacked GPUs on TSMC A16 node** with custom HBM variants.
- **OpenClaw and NemoClaw**: Demonstrated agentic AI agents for secure on-premises assistants via NVIDIA Agent Toolkit and OpenShell runtime, showcased in exhibit hall demos alongside multi-node DGX Spark capabilities.
- **DSX AI Factory reference designs** and **Omniverse DSX Blueprint** for simulating AI factory deployments were demonstrated.
- **Isaac GR00T N** for humanoid robots entered early access, with N2 preview shown; partnerships with ABB, Figure, and KUKA confirmed.
- **cuEST** announced for **30x faster quantum simulations**.

### NVIDIA Receives H200 Chip Orders from Chinese Customers
**Date:** March 18, 2026 | **Sources:** [YouTube/CNBC](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0uSr5O39Xu0), [YouTube/Bloomberg](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3cAwMcpsS7M), [LA Times](https://www.latimes.com/business/story/2026-03-03/u-s-considers-caps-on-nvidia-chips-for-china)

Jensen Huang announced at GTC that NVIDIA has received purchase orders for H200 AI chips from multiple Chinese customers and is restarting manufacturing to comply with U.S. export rules:

- NVIDIA secured **export licenses** for Chinese customers and is now **ramping up production**.
- U.S. officials are considering **per-customer caps of ~75,000 H200 units** and an overall limit of up to **1 million units** to China.
- Chinese firms including **Alibaba and ByteDance** have expressed interest in larger volumes.
- NVIDIA currently reports **no data center revenue from China** pending Beijing's approval.
- Due diligence requirements remain in place to prevent military use.

### DOJ Fires Back at Anthropic — Calls Pentagon Lawsuit a "Commercial Dispute"
**Date:** March 17–18, 2026 | **Sources:** [Times of India](https://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/technology/tech-news/pentagon-replies-to-anthropics-lawsuit-says-refusal-to-accept-the-governments-contractual-terms-is-not-protected-by/articleshow/129654744.cms), [Fortune](https://fortune.com/2026/03/12/anthropic-pentagon-lawsuit-supply-chain-risk-china-ai-race/), [AI Today](https://www.aitoday.io/anthropic-seeks-court-stay-pentagon-risk-designation-a-31004)

The U.S. Department of Justice filed a **40-page reply** on March 17 responding to Anthropic's lawsuit challenging its Pentagon "supply chain risk" designation:

- DOJ argues the case is a **commercial contract dispute, not a free speech issue**, rejecting Anthropic's preliminary injunction request.
- Pentagon labeled Anthropic a **"substantial" national security risk** due to potential AI sabotage risks during combat operations.
- DOJ noted that alternatives from **Google, OpenAI, and xAI** are being deployed on defense systems.
- Claude is currently the **only AI cleared for classified systems** amid ongoing military operations.
- **Anthropic's counter-response is due Friday, March 20**.
- **Preliminary injunction hearing scheduled for March 24, 2026** in San Francisco federal court.

### NVIDIA Feynman Platform Roadmap — Post-Vera Rubin Era
**Date:** March 16–19, 2026 | **Sources:** [Tom's Hardware](https://www.tomshardware.com/pc-components/gpus/nvidia-updates-data-center-roadmap-with-rosa-cpu-and-stacked-feynman-gpus-optical-nvlink-groq-lpus-with-nvfp4-and-nvlink-also-on-deck), [Jon Peddie Research](https://www.jonpeddie.com/news/nvidia-gtc-2026-keynote/), [Fudzilla](https://fudzilla.com/nvidia-unveils-more-details-about-future-feynman-ai-chips-including-3d-die-stacking/)

Jensen Huang laid out NVIDIA's post-2027 computing roadmap:

- **Feynman architecture** (targeted 2028) succeeds the Vera Rubin platform currently shipping.
- Features **Rosa CPU**, **LP40 LPU** (co-developed with Groq for dedicated inference), and **BlueField-5 DPU**.
- Uses **3D die-stacked GPUs** on TSMC A16 process node with next-gen HBM.
- **Kyber rack-scale systems** with copper and co-packaged optics for AI factory deployments.
- Signals NVIDIA's integration of **Groq inference hardware** into its platform for the first time.
