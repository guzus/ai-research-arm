## Breaking News

### U.S. Commerce Dept. Forces Anthropic to Globally Disable Fable 5 and Mythos 5

**Date:** June 12, 2026 | **Sources:** [TechCrunch — Anthropic's safety warnings may have just backfired — the government has pulled the plug on its most powerful AI](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/12/anthropics-safety-warnings-may-have-just-backfired-the-government-has-pulled-the-plug-on-its-most-powerful-ai/), [Fortune — Anthropic disables Fable and Mythos AI models following U.S. government export ban](https://fortune.com/2026/06/13/anthropic-disables-fable-mythos-export-controls-national-security-threat/), [NBC News — Anthropic suspends new AI models after government directive](https://www.nbcnews.com/tech/tech-news/anthropic-suspends-new-ai-models-fable-mythos-government-directive-rcna349901), [The Next Web — US orders Anthropic to shut down Fable 5 and Mythos 5](https://thenextweb.com/news/anthropic-fable-mythos-us-government-suspension)

- **What happened:** **Commerce Secretary Howard Lutnick** sent a letter to Anthropic CEO **Dario Amodei** at **5:21 p.m. ET on Friday, June 12**, informing the company that **Claude Fable 5 and Claude Mythos 5** would be subject to **export controls** — ordering Anthropic to block **all foreign nationals** from accessing the two models.
- **The fallout:** Because the models could not be selectively walled off, Anthropic complied within hours by **disabling Fable 5 and Mythos 5 globally for all users — including American users**, affecting hundreds of millions of people. Reporting calls it the **most significant government intervention in frontier-AI deployment to date.**
- **Stated reason:** The government's concern centers on a **claimed narrow, non-universal jailbreak of Fable 5** with potential cybersecurity/biology misuse. Anthropic **disagreed** that a narrow potential jailbreak should justify recalling a commercial model deployed to hundreds of millions, arguing that applying such a standard industry-wide "would essentially halt all new model deployments for all frontier model providers."
- **The irony:** Fable 5 — launched just **three days earlier (June 9)** — was positioned as Anthropic's *more* permissive commercial answer to the heavily restricted Mythos line, fitted with independent classifier systems to block high-risk cyber/bio outputs. The company's own safety disclosures appear to have triggered the directive.
- **Background (not net-new):** This caps a months-long Anthropic–government standoff. Trump directed federal agencies to **cease using Anthropic's technology** earlier in 2026 after the company refused Pentagon contract terms requiring its models be usable "for any lawful purpose" (Anthropic sought carve-outs from autonomous weapons and mass domestic surveillance). The June 12 export directive is a sharp new escalation of that fight.
- **Why it matters:** A frontier lab's flagship models were taken offline worldwide by government order — a precedent that reframes the entire deployment-vs-safety debate and the EU/US regulatory clock. It also strands the **June 9 Fable 5 launch** and reshuffles the competitive field while OpenAI, Google, and xAI ship.
