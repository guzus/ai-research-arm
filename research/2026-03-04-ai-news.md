### Apple Unveils $599 MacBook Neo — Its Cheapest Laptop Ever

### Defense Tech Companies Dropping Claude After Pentagon Blacklist
**Date:** March 4, 2026 | **Sources:** [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/04/pentagon-blacklist-anthropic-defense-tech-claude.html), [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/04/the-us-military-is-still-using-claude-but-defense-tech-clients-are-fleeing/)

Following the Pentagon's designation of Anthropic as a supply chain risk, defense contractors are actively migrating away from Claude. Key details:
- 10 portfolio companies of defense-focused VC firm J2 Ventures have "backed off" Claude for defense use cases and are replacing it with other AI models
- Lockheed Martin and other major defense contractors began swapping out Anthropic's models this week
- Paradoxically, Anthropic's models are still being used to support active U.S. military operations in Iran, even after the blacklist announcement
- Anthropic has pushed back legally, citing a federal statute and arguing that Defense Secretary Hegseth lacks the authority to restrict companies working with Anthropic from doing business with the government

### Sam Altman Tells OpenAI Staff: Military "Operational Decisions" Are Up to the Government
**Date:** March 3–4, 2026 | **Sources:** [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/03/sam-altman-tells-openai-staff-operational-decisions-up-to-government.html), [Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-04/altman-tells-staff-openai-has-no-say-over-pentagon-decisions)

In an all-hands meeting with OpenAI employees on Tuesday (March 4), CEO Sam Altman stated that OpenAI does not get to choose how the military uses its technology. Key quotes and details:
- Altman: "So maybe you think the Iran strike was good and the Venezuela invasion was bad. You don't get to weigh in on that."
- The Pentagon told OpenAI that "operational decisions" rest with Secretary Pete Hegseth
- The Pentagon respects OpenAI's technical expertise and will allow the company to build the "safety stack" it deems appropriate, but deployment decisions are the military's alone
- OpenAI research scientist Aidan McLaughlin posted on X: "I personally don't think this deal was worth it"
- CNN reported many OpenAI employees "really respect" Anthropic for standing up to the Pentagon and are frustrated with how OpenAI's contract was handled

### OpenAI Staff Fuming Over Pentagon Deal
**Date:** March 4, 2026 | **Sources:** [CNN](https://us.cnn.com/2026/03/04/tech/pentagon-anthropic-openai-staff-reactions), [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/04/anthropic-ai-pentagon-defense-business-risk.html)

A detailed CNN report published March 4 reveals deepening internal frustration at OpenAI. Employees feel the Pentagon contract was "rushed" — coming just hours after Anthropic was blacklisted — and that Altman appeared to agree publicly with Anthropic CEO Dario Amodei's red lines while privately negotiating to take Anthropic's place. Messages outside OpenAI's San Francisco offices read "Where are your redlines?" CNBC separately reported on the existential business risk the blacklist poses for Anthropic, noting how quickly a government designation can cascade through the defense-tech supply chain.

### ~900 Tech Workers Sign "We Will Not Be Divided" Letter Demanding Military AI Limits
**Date:** March 3, 2026 | **Sources:** [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/03/anthropic-fallout-iran-war-tech-military-ai.html), [Axios](https://www.axios.com/2026/03/03/ai-race-safety-guardrail)

The open letter by tech workers demanding military AI limits surged from ~500 signatures to nearly 900, with close to 100 signatories from OpenAI and approximately 800 from Google. The letter, titled "We Will Not Be Divided," took aim at the Pentagon's blacklisting of Anthropic and called on AI companies to maintain red lines against autonomous weapons and mass surveillance. Google is now reportedly in talks with the Pentagon about bringing Gemini into classified environments. More than 100 Google AI employees separately signed an internal letter urging leadership to adopt the same red lines as Anthropic.

### U.S. Government Agencies Phasing Out Anthropic for OpenAI
**Date:** March 2–3, 2026 | **Sources:** [Technology.org](https://www.technology.org/2026/03/03/us-government-dumps-anthropic-state-treasury-and-hhs-jump-ship-to-openai/), [Reuters via Yahoo](https://www.yahoo.com/news/articles/state-department-switching-openai-anthropic-221423273.html), [NewsNation](https://www.newsnationnow.com/business/tech/state-department-switching-to-openai-from-anthropics-claude/)

Multiple federal agencies are actively replacing Anthropic's Claude with OpenAI products following the Pentagon blacklist:
- **State Department** is switching its internal chatbot "StateChat" from Claude to OpenAI's GPT-4.1
- **Treasury Department:** Secretary Scott Bessent announced on X that Treasury is ending all Anthropic contracts
- **HHS** is also reportedly phasing out Anthropic products
- The phase-out stems from Trump's executive order designating Anthropic a supply chain risk after it refused to remove safeguards against mass surveillance and autonomous weapons

### China's Government Work Report Mentions "Embodied AI" and "Large AI Models" for First Time
**Date:** March 4, 2026 | **Sources:** [Global Times](https://www.globaltimes.cn/page/202503/1329735.shtml), [South China Morning Post](https://www.scmp.com/news/china/science/article/3345440/two-sessions-two-paths-china-prioritises-ai-integration-amid-us-tech-rivalry), [CNN](https://www.cnn.com/2026/03/03/china/china-two-sessions-tech-future-intl-hnk)

As China's "Two Sessions" opened March 4, the government work report for the first time included "embodied AI" (AI in physical forms like robots) and "large-scale AI models" (represented by DeepSeek) as strategic priorities. The 15th Five-Year Plan recommendations named quantum technology, biomanufacturing, embodied AI, brain-computer interfaces, and 6G as "industries of the future." Chinese leadership is entering the sessions with strong confidence in its AI position following DeepSeek's success.
