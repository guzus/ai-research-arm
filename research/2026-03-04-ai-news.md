## Breaking News

### ~900 Tech Workers Sign "We Will Not Be Divided" Letter Demanding Military AI Limits
**Date:** March 3, 2026 | **Sources:** [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/03/anthropic-fallout-iran-war-tech-military-ai.html), [Axios](https://www.axios.com/2026/03/03/ai-race-safety-guardrail)

The open letter by tech workers demanding military AI limits surged from ~500 signatures to nearly 900, with close to 100 signatories from OpenAI and approximately 800 from Google. The letter, titled "We Will Not Be Divided," took aim at the Pentagon's blacklisting of Anthropic and called on AI companies to maintain red lines against autonomous weapons and mass surveillance. Critically, Google is now reportedly in talks with the Pentagon about bringing its Gemini models into classified environments — reviving the deeply sensitive internal debate over military work at the company. More than 100 Google AI employees separately signed an internal letter urging leadership to adopt the same red lines as Anthropic. The escalation comes as U.S. military strikes on Iran intensified over the weekend.

## New Model Releases

### DeepSeek V4 Still Unreleased Despite "This Week" Timeline
**Date:** March 3–4, 2026 | **Sources:** [TechNode](https://technode.com/2026/03/02/deepseek-plans-v4-multimodal-model-release-this-week-sources-say/), [PYMNTS](https://www.pymnts.com/artificial-intelligence-2/2026/deepseek-poised-to-unveil-latest-ai-model/)

DeepSeek V4, the highly anticipated trillion-parameter multimodal model, has still not launched despite reports it would release during the first week of March — strategically timed ahead of China's "Two Sessions" parliamentary meetings beginning March 4. The model features ~1 trillion total parameters with ~32B active per token (fewer active than V3 despite being vastly larger), a 1M-token context window, and native multimodal capabilities across text, image, video, and audio. Notably, DeepSeek deliberately excluded Nvidia and AMD from the pre-release optimization pipeline, building V4's inference stack around Huawei Ascend and Cambricon chips — a first for a frontier AI model. Expected to be released under an open-source license.

## Industry Moves

### AI Safety Race Intensifies Between Anthropic, OpenAI, and Google
**Date:** March 3, 2026 | **Sources:** [Axios](https://www.axios.com/2026/03/03/ai-race-safety-guardrail), [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/03/anthropic-fallout-iran-war-tech-military-ai.html)

The competitive battle between the three major AI labs is now explicitly framed around safety policy amid military contracts. Key developments:
- **Google** is in active talks with the Pentagon about deploying Gemini in classified environments, reigniting the internal "Project Maven" debate.
- **Anthropic** continues to hold its position against autonomous weapons and mass surveillance despite being blacklisted by the Defense Department.
- **OpenAI** amended its Pentagon contract but critics note it does not explicitly prohibit collection of Americans' publicly available information — a gap Anthropic considers crucial.
- Google DeepMind CEO Demis Hassabis warned that "race conditions" — pressure to outpace rivals or rival nations — can drive reckless decisions as the world nears superhuman AI.

## Regulatory Updates

### FTC AI Policy Statement Deadline Approaching: March 11
**Date:** Deadline set by December 2025 Executive Order | **Sources:** [Paul Hastings](https://www.paulhastings.com/insights/client-alerts/president-trump-signs-executive-order-challenging-state-ai-laws), [King & Spalding](https://www.kslaw.com/news-and-insights/new-state-ai-laws-are-effective-on-january-1-2026-but-a-new-executive-order-signals-disruption)

The FTC faces a March 11, 2026 deadline to issue a policy statement classifying state-mandated AI bias mitigation as a potentially deceptive trade practice — a move that could preempt state-level AI regulations nationwide. The same deadline applies to the Secretary of Commerce, who must publish a comprehensive review identifying state AI laws deemed "overly burdensome." The Colorado AI Act is specifically cited. This stems from President Trump's December 2025 executive order "Ensuring a National Policy Framework for Artificial Intelligence."

### Connecticut Proposes New AI Regulation Bills
**Date:** March 3, 2026 | **Source:** [New Haven Independent](https://www.newhavenindependent.org/2026/03/03/its-not-too-late-ai-regs-proposed/)

Connecticut State Senate President Martin Looney introduced Senate Bills 4 and 5 targeting AI regulation. SB 4 focuses on privacy protections and restrictions on surveillance pricing practices, including AI-driven Electronic Shelf Labels. SB 5 would require employers to notify employees when AI is used in hiring and employment decisions. The bills follow high-profile AI transparency legislation in New York and California.
