### ChatGPT Ads Officially Begin Rolling Out to U.S. Users
**Date:** February 1, 2026

OpenAI's long-anticipated advertising rollout has officially begun, with ads now appearing for free-tier and ChatGPT Go ($8/month) users in the United States. This marks the first time a major AI chatbot has introduced conversation-based advertising at scale.

**How It Works:**
- Ads appear at the bottom of ChatGPT's answers when there's a relevant sponsored product or service based on the user's current conversation
- Ad units are impression-based (pay-per-view), not pay-per-click
- OpenAI is targeting ~$60 per 1,000 impressions (CPM), roughly 2.5x Meta's rates
- A small pool of beta advertisers committed ~$1 million each to participate in the initial trial

**Who Sees Ads:**
- Free-tier and ChatGPT Go users in the U.S. (logged-in adults only)
- Plus ($20/month), Pro ($200/month), Business, and Enterprise tiers remain ad-free
- Users under 18 will not see ads; ads excluded from politics, health, and mental health topics

**Privacy Commitments:**
- Ads do not influence ChatGPT's answers; responses remain optimized for helpfulness
- OpenAI says it will "never" sell user data to advertisers
- Users can turn off ad personalization and clear ad-related data at any time

**Revenue Projections:**
- OpenAI forecasts $1 billion in "free user monetization" revenue in 2026, growing to $25 billion by 2029
- The ad push comes as OpenAI faces $115 billion in projected cash burn through 2030

**Industry Reaction:**
- Google DeepMind CEO Demis Hassabis at Davos said Google has no plans to put ads in Gemini, commenting: "It's interesting they've gone for that so early"
- User sentiment on X has been overwhelmingly negative; Anthropic's Claude and Google's Gemini remain ad-free

### Amazon in Talks for Up to $50 Billion OpenAI Investment — Deal Structure Emerges
**Date:** January 29–31, 2026

As OpenAI's $100 billion mega-round takes shape, new details have emerged about Amazon's proposed $50 billion investment, which would be structured as a "chips-for-equity" arrangement — raising questions about circular financing in the AI industry.

**Deal Structure:**
- Amazon is offering OpenAI massive access to its proprietary Trainium and Inferentia AI chips alongside dedicated AWS server capacity
- OpenAI would use Amazon's AI models in its products and platforms
- Amazon employees could also gain access to OpenAI models for internal use
- Term sheet could be signed in coming weeks

**Full Round Breakdown (Updated):**
- **Amazon:** Up to $50 billion (chips-for-equity)
- **NVIDIA:** Confirmed participation; Huang calls it potentially "the largest investment we've ever made" (but far below $100 billion)
- **SoftBank:** Up to $30 billion additional
- **Microsoft:** Below $10 billion
- **Total Target:** ~$100 billion at $750-830 billion valuation

**Circular Financing Concerns:**
The FTC and European Commission have launched inquiries into "round-trip" AI financing, where cloud providers invest billions in AI startups that then spend the money on the investor's cloud services — potentially creating artificial demand and ecosystem lock-in.

**Amazon's Balancing Act:**
Amazon has already invested ~$8 billion in Anthropic, a direct OpenAI competitor, and opened an $11 billion data center campus for Anthropic in Indiana. A $50 billion OpenAI investment would represent a dramatic hedging strategy across both leading AI labs.

### UK CMA Proposes Forcing Google to Let Publishers Opt Out of AI Overviews
**Date:** January 28, 2026

The UK's Competition and Markets Authority unveiled proposals to grant web publishers control over Google's AI Overviews, allowing them to block content scraping for AI summaries and model training without sacrificing search visibility.

**Key Proposals:**
- **Publisher Opt-Out Controls:** Google must provide publishers with effective controls to withhold content from AI Overviews, AI Mode, and broader generative AI services like Gemini and Vertex
- **Granular Controls:** Publishers can opt out at both directory level (entire website) and individual page level
- **No Retaliation:** Google must not downrank in general search results publishers who have opted out of AI Overviews
- **Proper Attribution:** Google required to ensure publisher content is properly attributed in AI results
- **Search Choice Screens:** Default search engine choice screens to become a legal requirement on Android and Chrome

**Context:**
Google was designated with "strategic market status" (SMS) by the CMA in October, subjecting it to special requirements. Global search referrals to news sites have dropped 33% since AI Overviews launched, per Chartbeat data covering 2,500+ outlets. Consultation closes February 25; ministers will decide on the final approach by March 18.

**Google's Response:**
Google said it is "exploring updates" to allow sites to opt out of AI search features, but cautioned against controls that might result in a "fragmented or confusing" experience.

### Colorado AI Act Effective Date Officially Postponed to June 30, 2026
**Date:** February 1, 2026

Colorado's landmark AI Act (SB 24-205) — which would have been the nation's first comprehensive state law regulating high-risk AI systems — did not take effect on its original February 1 deadline. Governor Polis signed SB 25B-004 delaying enforcement to June 30, 2026.

**What Happened:**
- The Colorado legislature held a special session in August 2025 to revise the law
- Senator Rodriguez's comprehensive 13-page rewrite ("AI Sunshine Act") failed to secure passage
- Rodriguez gutted the bill and replaced it with a find-and-replace: every "February 1, 2026" became "June 30, 2026"
- All substantive obligations remain unchanged — only the effective date moved

**What's Still Coming (June 30):**
- Developers must exercise "reasonable care" to prevent algorithmic discrimination and publish public statements
- Deployers must adopt risk-management policies, perform annual impact assessments, and issue consumer notices
- Attorney General enforcement begins with rebuttable presumptions and affirmative defenses

**Federal Tension:**
The White House's Executive Order 14281 directs federal agencies to abolish "disparate-impact" liability enforcement — directly conflicting with Colorado's Act, which is built around preventing algorithmic discrimination including disparate impact.

### AI Circular Financing Under Regulatory Scrutiny
**Date:** January 30–31, 2026

As Amazon's proposed $50 billion "chips-for-equity" OpenAI investment and NVIDIA's participation in both OpenAI and Anthropic rounds come to light, regulators are intensifying scrutiny of circular financing patterns in the AI industry.

**What Regulators Are Investigating:**
- **FTC:** Examining whether cloud providers investing in AI startups that then spend on the investor's cloud services creates artificial demand

**Examples Under Scrutiny:**

- NVIDIA: Investing in both OpenAI and Anthropic (both major GPU buyers)
