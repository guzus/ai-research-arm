### Morgan Stanley: "Transformative AI Breakthrough" Imminent in First Half of 2026
**Date:** March 13, 2026 | **Sources:** [Fortune](https://fortune.com/2026/03/13/elon-musk-morgan-stanley-ai-leap-2026/), [Morgan Stanley TMT Conference](https://www.morganstanley.com/insights/podcasts/thoughts-on-the-market/ai-adoption-inflection-point-2026-tmt-conference)

Morgan Stanley published a major research note warning that a transformative AI breakthrough is imminent, driven by unprecedented compute accumulation at America's top AI labs:
- The investment bank cites the principle that applying 10x compute to language model training effectively doubles a model's "intelligence," with scaling laws holding firm through 2026.
- OpenAI's GPT-5.4 "Thinking" model scored **83.0% on the GDPVal benchmark**, placing it at or above the level of human experts on economically valuable tasks — cited as evidence the breakthrough is already underway.
- Morgan Stanley projects a **net U.S. power shortfall of 9–18 gigawatts through 2028** — a 12%–25% deficit in power needed to support AI infrastructure expansion.
- Developers are responding by converting Bitcoin mining operations into computing centers, deploying natural gas turbines, and installing fuel cells to bridge the gap.
- Morgan Stanley predicts "Transformative AI" will become a deflationary force as AI tools replicate human work at lower costs. Executives are already executing large-scale workforce reductions.
- Major U.S. model reveals expected between **April and June 2026**.
- The bank estimates ~$2.9 trillion in global data center construction through 2028, with 80%+ still ahead, fueling ~25% of U.S. GDP growth this year.

### Washington State Passes Three Major AI Bills
**Date:** March 12, 2026 | **Sources:** [Transparency Coalition AI](https://www.transparencycoalition.ai/news/ai-legislative-update-march13-2026), [GW Law](https://app.leg.wa.gov/billsummary?Year=2025&BillNumber=2225), [KPQ](https://kpq.com/washington-ai-chatbot-bill-minors/), [JD Supra](https://www.jdsupra.com/legalnews/proposed-state-ai-law-update-march-9-3145302/)

Washington state legislature passed three significant AI bills on Thursday night, March 12, just before adjournment:
- **HB 1170 (AI Disclosure):** Requires informing users when content is developed or modified by artificial intelligence. Passed the Senate 46-3.
- **HB 2225 (Chatbot Safety for Children):** Mandates that AI companion chatbots disclose they are not human (with more frequent disclosures for minors), implement safeguards against self-harm or suicidal ideation with crisis referrals, and protect minors from explicit or manipulative interactions. Passed the Senate 43-5.
- **SB 5395 (AI in Health Insurance):** Regulates the use of AI in health insurance decision-making. Received final legislative approval.
- All three bills now await Governor's signature for formal enactment.
- These laws could be challenged under the Trump administration's federal preemption strategy, as the Commerce Department and FTC delivered their state AI law evaluations on March 11 (see previous report).

### Pew Research: Half of Americans Now More Concerned Than Excited About AI
**Date:** March 12, 2026 | **Sources:** [Pew Research Center](https://www.pewresearch.org/short-reads/2026/03/12/key-findings-about-how-americans-view-artificial-intelligence/)

Pew Research Center published a major survey (8,512 U.S. adults, conducted January 2026) revealing deepening public anxiety about AI:
- **50% of all U.S. adults** now report feeling more concerned than excited about AI's increased use in daily life — up 13 percentage points from 37% in 2021.
- Only **10%** express more excitement than concern; 38% feel roughly equal measures of both.
- **52%** believe AI will mostly hurt rather than help the quality of jobs; only 15% think AI will mostly help.
- **73%** are worried about AI being used to create misleading information, making it the top concern.
- Growing adoption: **55%** of adults have used AI tools in the past year (up from 23% in 2023), with ChatGPT the most widely used.
- Demographic divides: Younger adults (18-29) are significantly more likely to use AI tools but also more likely to express concerns about job displacement.
- Trust gap: Only **22%** trust AI companies to act responsibly, down from 35% two years ago.

### Salesforce Prices $25 Billion Senior Notes to Fund Accelerated Share Repurchase
**Date:** March 13, 2026 | **Sources:** [Salesforce Investor Relations](https://investor.salesforce.com/news/news-details/2026/Salesforce-Prices-Public-Offering-of-Senior-Notes/default.aspx), [Pulse2](https://pulse2.com/salesforce-25-billion-senior-notes-offering-to-fund-accelerated-share-repurchase/), [StockTitan](https://www.stocktitan.net/sec-filings/CRM/8-k-salesforce-inc-reports-material-event-5b29ba1bcc92.html)

Salesforce priced a $25 billion senior notes offering to fund the largest accelerated share repurchase (ASR) in the company's history:
- Net proceeds of approximately **$24.885 billion** will fund ASR agreements with Bank of America, Citibank, JPMorgan Chase, Morgan Stanley, and Banco Santander.
- The notes consist of eight tranches maturing between **2028 and 2066**, with coupons from 4.500% to 6.700%.
- Offering expected to close March 13; ASR prepayment and initial share delivery (~80% of shares) on March 16.
- Final ASR settlement, based on volume-weighted average prices, is scheduled for Q4 2026.
- Salesforce also secured a **$6 billion five-year senior unsecured term loan** to refinance existing term loans.
- The move signals Salesforce's confidence in its cash generation even as it continues heavy investment in AI agent platforms like Agentforce.

### Federal AI Policy Deadlines: Commerce and FTC Deliverables Now Due
**Date:** March 11–13, 2026 | **Sources:** [Baker Botts](https://ourtake.bakerbotts.com/post/102mirs/march-2026-federal-deadlines-that-will-reshape-the-ai-regulatory-landscape), [Ropes & Gray](https://www.ropesgray.com/en/insights/alerts/2026/03/examining-the-landscape-and-limitations-of-the-federal-push-to-override-state-ai-regulation), [S&P Global](https://www.spglobal.com/market-intelligence/en/news-insights/articles/2026/3/companies-face-compliance-limbo-as-trump-administration-targets-state-ai-laws-99326115), [TechFreedom](https://techfreedom.org/ftcs-ai-policy-statement-no-substitute-for-rulemaking/)

Follow-up on the March 11 deadline reported yesterday — key developments as the deliverables landed:
- **Commerce Department** published its evaluation of state AI laws, identifying "onerous" ones that burden U.S. AI dominance, alter "truthful outputs," or raise constitutional concerns. Laws in **Colorado, California, and New York** received particular scrutiny.
- **FTC** issued its Section 5 policy statement arguing that state laws mandating bias mitigation in AI outputs could constitute "deceptive practices" under federal law — an untested legal theory that critics say lacks rulemaking force.
- **BEAD funding leverage:** Commerce issued a policy notice conditioning ~$21 billion in remaining broadband deployment funds on states not maintaining AI laws deemed "onerous."
- **Companies face "compliance limbo"** (S&P Global) as the federal government signals preemption intent but has not yet formally invalidated any state law.
- **TechFreedom** criticized the FTC statement as "no substitute for rulemaking," noting it lacks binding enforcement power.
- The DOJ AI Litigation Task Force (established January 2026) is expected to begin filing challenges to specific state laws in the coming weeks.
- This directly impacts Washington state's newly passed AI bills (see above), which could face federal challenges before they take effect.
