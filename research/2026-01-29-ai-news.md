### Google Integrates Gemini 3 into Chrome with Auto Browse Agentic Browsing
**Date:** January 28-29, 2026

Google announced a major overhaul of Chrome's AI capabilities, embedding Gemini 3 directly into the browser with a persistent side panel, agentic "Auto Browse" feature, image generation via Nano Banana, and a forthcoming "Personal Intelligence" system — positioning Chrome as an active AI agent layer for 3+ billion users.

**Auto Browse (Agentic Browsing):**
- Available to Google AI Pro and Ultra subscribers in the U.S.
- Performs multi-step tasks autonomously: researching hotels, comparing flights, shopping within budgets, scheduling appointments, collecting tax documents, managing subscriptions
- Uses Gemini 3's multimodal capabilities to identify items in photos, search for similar products, add to cart, and apply discount codes
- Can fill forms using information from PDFs and use Chrome's built-in password manager (with permission)
- Pauses for user confirmation before sensitive actions (purchases, social media posts)
- All actions visible in chronological order in the side panel; users can take over at any time

**New Gemini Side Panel:**
- Gemini moves from a floating window to a persistent right-side panel in Chrome
- Enables multitasking: main tab stays open while AI assists in the side panel
- Available on Windows, macOS, and Chromebook Plus in the U.S.

**Nano Banana Image Generation:**
- Integrated image generation directly in Chrome via the Nano Banana model
- Example: placing furniture in an empty room from a browser-loaded image, with results appearing in the side panel

**Personal Intelligence (Coming Soon):**
- Will remember context from past conversations for personalized assistance
- Connects information from Gmail, Search, YouTube, and Google Photos
- Users control opt-in and can connect/disconnect apps at any time
- Connected Apps include Google Workspace, Spotify, YouTube Music, Google Photos, Home, Maps, Shopping, and Flights

**Universal Commerce Protocol (UCP):**
- New open standard for agentic commerce co-developed with Shopify, Etsy, Wayfair, and Target
- Enables AI agents to take purchase actions on users' behalf seamlessly in Chrome

**Security Considerations:**
Security experts have warned Auto Browse may be vulnerable to prompt-injection attacks on malicious websites. Google states users remain responsible for oversight.

**Competitive Context:**
Chrome follows OpenAI Atlas, Microsoft Edge, Perplexity, and Opera in AI-powered browsing, but Google's scale — over 3 billion Chrome users — amplifies its reach significantly.
