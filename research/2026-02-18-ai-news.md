### Meta Signs Multi-Year, Multi-Billion Dollar AI Chip Deal With Nvidia
**Date:** February 17–18, 2026

Meta has signed a multi-year agreement with Nvidia to purchase millions of AI chips, in one of the largest AI infrastructure deals ever disclosed. The agreement covers both current and next-generation hardware, reinforcing Nvidia's dominance in AI compute supply.

**Key Details:**
- **Scope:** Millions of Nvidia AI chips including Blackwell and Rubin GPUs, plus standalone Grace and Vera CPUs
- **First-of-kind:** Meta becomes the first company to deploy Nvidia's Grace CPUs as standalone chips in data centers (not bundled with GPUs)
- **Estimated value:** "Certainly in the tens of billions of dollars" per chip analyst Ben Bajarin of Creative Strategies
- **Context:** Follows Meta's January announcement of plans to spend up to $135 billion on AI infrastructure in 2026
- **Relationship:** Extends a decade-long partnership between the two companies

**Why It Matters:**
This deal cements Meta's position as one of Nvidia's largest customers and signals that hyperscaler AI spending shows no signs of slowing. The standalone Grace CPU deployment is notable — it suggests Meta is building AI-optimized data center architectures that go beyond GPU clusters, positioning for a future where AI inference workloads require diverse compute configurations.

**Key Details:**
- **Target:** Koyeb — Paris-based serverless platform that simplifies AI app deployment at scale
- **Team:** Koyeb's 13 employees and three co-founders (Yann Léger, Edouard Bonlieu, Bastien Chatelard) join Mistral's engineering team under CTO Timothée Lacroix
- **Financial terms:** Not disclosed
- **Strategic fit:** Koyeb's technology enables on-premises model deployment, GPU optimization, and AI inference scaling
- **Background:** Mistral launched Mistral Compute (AI cloud infrastructure) in June 2025; this acquisition accelerates that effort
- **Recent context:** Mistral also announced a €1.2 billion ($1.4 billion) investment in AI data centers in Sweden on February 11, partnering with EcoDataCenter in Borlänge (opening 2027)

**Why It Matters:**
This is Mistral's first acquisition and a clear signal that Europe's leading AI company is evolving from a model provider into a full-stack platform — directly challenging US hyperscalers (AWS, Azure, GCP). Combined with the $1.4B Sweden data center investment announced days earlier, Mistral is building independent European AI infrastructure at a pace not seen from a non-US AI company.

### OpenAI Launches Lockdown Mode and Elevated Risk Labels for ChatGPT
**Date:** February 14–17, 2026

OpenAI introduced Lockdown Mode, an advanced security setting for high-risk users, and Elevated Risk labels across ChatGPT to combat prompt injection and data exfiltration attacks.

**Key Details:**
- **Lockdown Mode:** Optional security setting for executives and security teams at prominent organizations
- **Purpose:** Prevents prompt injection-based data exfiltration from conversations and connected apps
- **Restrictions in Lockdown Mode:**
  - Web browsing limited to cached content (no live network requests leave OpenAI's network)
  - Images disabled in ChatGPT responses
  - Deep Research and Agent Mode disabled
  - Canvas-generated code cannot access the network
  - ChatGPT cannot download files for data analysis
- **Elevated Risk labels:** Warn users when certain AI capabilities may expose sensitive data to security vulnerabilities
- **Availability:** ChatGPT Enterprise, Edu, Healthcare, and Teachers editions; consumer rollout planned
- **Threat model:** Designed specifically against adversaries attempting to exploit tool-use capabilities to exfiltrate data

**Why It Matters:**
This is OpenAI's most significant security feature release since ChatGPT's launch. Lockdown Mode addresses a growing real-world threat: prompt injection attacks that use ChatGPT's browsing and tool-use capabilities to steal data from enterprise conversations. The fact that it disables Deep Research and Agent Mode underscores the inherent security trade-off between AI capability and safety.
