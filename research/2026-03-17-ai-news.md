### xAI Sued Over Grok-Generated CSAM — Tennessee Teens File Class Action
**Date:** March 16, 2026 | **Sources:** [Business Insider](https://www.businessinsider.com/lawsuit-allegation-xai-grok-created-sexualized-deepfakes-minors-2026-3), [MLex](https://www.mlex.com/mlex/artificial-intelligence/articles/2453606/xai-sued-by-tennessee-teens-who-allege-grok-made-sexual-images-of-them-as-minors), [Lieff Cabraser](https://www.lieffcabraser.com/2026/03/lchb-files-class-action-obo-minor-victims-alleging-xais-grok-generated-and-profited-from-ai-sexual-exploitation-images-and-videos/), [SiliconANGLE](https://siliconangle.com/2026/03/16/teens-launch-lawsuit-xai-grok-deepfakes/), [UPI](https://www.upi.com/Top_News/US/2026/03/16/teens-sue-musk-xai-grok/9401773691425/)

Three Tennessee minors filed a proposed class-action lawsuit against Elon Musk's xAI in California's Northern District federal court:

- Alleges a perpetrator used Grok to morph real photos (school pictures, social media images) into nude/explicit deepfake content, distributed on Discord, Telegram, and Mega.
- The perpetrator was arrested in December 2025 after a Tennessee criminal investigation.
- Lawsuit claims xAI knowingly designed Grok **without industry-standard CSAM guardrails**, marketed "Spicy Mode" for sexual content, and profited via subscriptions.

- Invokes **Masha's Law**, Trafficking Victims Protection Act, and California law; seeks damages, punitive damages, and injunctions for a class of thousands of U.S. minors.
- Separately, **Sen. Elizabeth Warren** pressed the Pentagon over its decision to grant xAI access to classified networks, citing Grok's history of harmful outputs as a national security risk.

### Encyclopedia Britannica and Merriam-Webster Sue OpenAI for Copyright Infringement
**Date:** March 16–17, 2026 | **Sources:** [The News International](https://www.thenews.com.pk/latest/1395896-encyclopedia-britannica-sues-openai-over-allegedly-using-its-contents-for-ai-training), [HeyGoTrade](https://www.heygotrade.com/en/news/britannica-sues-openai-copyright-infringement/)

Encyclopedia Britannica and Merriam-Webster filed a joint copyright lawsuit against OpenAI in Manhattan federal court:

- Alleges approximately **100,000 copyrighted articles** were used to train GPT models without permission.
- Claims GPT-4 has **"memorized" significant portions** of Britannica's content and generates responses "substantially similar" to their copyrighted material.
- Argues the practice cannibalizes web traffic and undermines the business model of authoritative reference publishers.
- OpenAI responded that its models use publicly available data under **fair use**.
- Represents the most comprehensive copyright challenge to date against AI training data practices, joining a growing docket of IP litigation that could reshape how AI labs source training data.

### Mistral Small 4 (119B) — Unified Multimodal MoE Model
**Date:** March 16, 2026 | **Sources:** [Nvidia Build](https://build.nvidia.com/mistralai/mistral-small-4-119b-2603/modelcard), [Mistral Docs](https://docs.mistral.ai/models/mistral-small-4-0-26-03), [MarkTechPost](https://www.marktechpost.com/2026/03/16/mistral-ai-releases-mistral-small-4-a-119b-parameter-moe-model-that-unifies-instruct-reasoning-and-multimodal-workloads/), [Simon Willison](https://simonwillison.net/2026/Mar/16/mistral-small-4/)

Mistral AI released Mistral Small 4, a major unified model:

- **119B total parameters** with **6.5B active** via Mixture-of-Experts architecture.
- **262,144 token context** with text and image (RGB) input support.
- Unifies three previously separate models: **Magistral** (reasoning), **Pixtral** (multimodal), and **Devstral** (agentic coding).
- Flexible per-request `reasoning_effort` parameter: "none" for fast responses, "high" for deep step-by-step reasoning.
- **40% reduction** in end-to-end completion time vs Mistral Small 3; handles **3x more requests/second** in throughput-optimized setups.
- Outperforms GPT-OSS 120B on LiveCodeBench while producing **20% less output**.
- NVFP4-optimized version available on NVIDIA hardware.
- Pricing: **$0.15/M input tokens**, **$0.60/M output tokens**.
- Native function calling and JSON output support.

## Regulatory Updates

### EU Parliament Agrees on AI Act Omnibus — Explicit Ban on Non-Consensual AI Deepfakes
**Date:** March 11, 2026 (committee vote scheduled March 18) | **Sources:** [The Next Web](https://thenextweb.com/news/eu-lawmakers-deal-to-ban-ai-non-consensual-intimate-deepfakes), [DPO Centre](https://www.dpocentre.com/news/eu-ai-act-amendments-political-agreement/), [MLex](https://www.mlex.com/mlex/articles/2451856/eu-lawmakers-agree-draft-ai-law-amendments-includes-ban-on-sexual-deepfakes), [IAPP](https://iapp.org/news/a/meps-reach-preliminary-political-agreement-on-AI-omnibus)

EU Parliament lawmakers reached a political agreement on amendments to the AI Act via the Omnibus package:

- **Explicit ban on AI systems generating non-consensual intimate deepfakes**, including CSAM — responding directly to the late 2025 Grok scandal on X where 6,700+ sexualized images of real women and girls were created.
- **Eased compliance** for AI in sector-regulated products (medical devices, industrial machinery).
- **Tightened safeguards** for sensitive data in high-risk AI bias correction.
- Retention of small mid-cap exemptions and simplified database registration.
- Pushed by **France, Spain, Germany, and Slovakia** after the European Commission confirmed existing AI Act provisions lacked this explicit prohibition.
- **Committee vote scheduled March 18, 2026**, followed by further Parliament and Council stages.
- Greens party opposition on industrial deregulation aspects may lead to changes.
- Core AI Act timeline unchanged: high-risk obligations apply **August 2, 2026**; regulated product AI by **August 2, 2027**.
