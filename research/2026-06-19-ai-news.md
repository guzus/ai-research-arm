## Breaking News

### Net-New: Noam Shazeer — Transformer Co-Author and Gemini Co-Lead — Leaves Google for OpenAI

**Date:** June 18, 2026 | **Sources:** [CNBC — Google Gemini co-lead Noam Shazeer leaves for OpenAI](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/18/google-gemini-co-lead-noam-shazeer-leaves-for-openai.html), [Axios — Top AI researcher leaves Google for OpenAI](https://www.axios.com/2026/06/18/noam-shazeer-google-openai-characterai), [Quartz — Google Gemini co-lead Noam Shazeer leaving for OpenAI](https://qz.com/google-gemini-noam-shazeer-openai-061826), [TechTimes — Transformer Architect Behind Gemini Jumps to OpenAI](https://www.techtimes.com/articles/318613/20260618/transformer-architect-behind-gemini-jumps-openai-after-google-spent-27b.htm)

- **What's net-new:** **Noam Shazeer** — Google VP of Engineering, co-lead of the Gemini models, Character.AI founder, and co-author of the 2017 "Attention Is All You Need" paper that introduced the **transformer architecture** underpinning every major LLM — announced **June 18** he is leaving Google to join **OpenAI as Lead for Architecture Research**. CEO Sam Altman publicly welcomed the move, calling Shazeer one of the people he had most wanted to work with since OpenAI's earliest days.
- **The twist:** Google had paid roughly **$2.7 billion** in August 2024 to bring Shazeer (and part of his Character.AI team, including Daniel De Freitas) back into DeepMind. His defection to OpenAI ~two years later reverses that high-priced re-hire.
- **Why it matters:** This is the single most consequential frontier-lab talent move of the window. Losing a co-lead of Gemini — the model line Google is racing to ship as **Gemini 3.5 Pro** before the ~June 30 cliff — to OpenAI's architecture team lands during the most competitive stretch of the coding/frontier-model race, and reads as a direct signal in the OpenAI-vs-Google talent war.

### Net-New: Anthropic Submits Formal Proposal to Lutnick to Lift the Fable 5 / Mythos 5 Export Ban

**Date:** June 18, 2026 | **Sources:** [Reuters via TradingView — Anthropic floats proposal to U.S. Commerce Secretary Lutnick to end ban of Mythos/Fable](https://www.tradingview.com/news/reuters.com,2026:newsml_FWN42Q0QX:0-anthropic-floats-proposal-to-us-commerce-secretary-howard-lutnick-to-end-us-ban-of-powerful-mythos-fable-ai-models-nyp/), [Korea JoongAng Daily — Anthropic confident of re-enabling Mythos/Fable 5 access "in coming days"](https://www.koreajoongangdaily.com/business/anthropic-confident-of-reenabling-mythos-fable-5-access-in-coming-days-executive/12727522), [The Globe and Mail — Anthropic, Trump officials working toward deal to restore Fable 5 and Mythos 5](https://www.theglobeandmail.com/business/article-anthropic-trump-officials-deal-restore-fable-5-mythos-5/)

- **What's net-new (vs. the June 18 report's "negotiations ongoing"):** Anthropic has now **submitted a formal proposal** to Commerce Secretary **Howard Lutnick** to end the U.S. export ban on its two most capable models, reportedly **pledging closer cooperation with the White House** as part of the framework. This is a concrete procedural step — a written proposal on the table — beyond the prior "daily meetings at Commerce" status.
- **On-record optimism:** Anthropic's Managing Director of International, **Chris Ciauri**, told a press conference in **Seoul** that the company is "**very confident that in the coming days, the models will become available again**" — the first explicit Anthropic-side restoration timeline, though it is company optimism, not a confirmed government lift.
- **Status as of June 19:** **Fable 5 and Mythos 5 remain offline** worldwide. No restoration has been confirmed by Commerce; whether any return would be **global or U.S.-only** is unresolved. Prediction markets (Kalshi) were pricing a **July** restoration around the high-50s percent.
- **Why it matters:** It is the first sign the week-long deadlock has a concrete negotiating artifact and a (vendor-asserted) near-term clock — the inflection point the entire cycle has been watching.

## Research Papers

### Net-New (June 17): OpenAI Releases LifeSciBench — a 750-Task Life-Science Research Benchmark

**Date:** June 17, 2026 (just outside the 24h window but not in any prior report) | **Sources:** [OpenAI — Introducing LifeSciBench](https://openai.com/index/introducing-life-sci-bench/), [MarkTechPost — OpenAI releases LifeSciBench, a 750-task benchmark grading AI on real life-science research](https://www.marktechpost.com/2026/06/17/openai-releases-lifescibench-a-750-task-benchmark-grading-ai-models-on-real-life-science-research-with-expert-written-rubric/), [TechTimes — OpenAI Life Science Benchmark Reveals AI Passes Only 1 in 3 Scientific Research Tasks](https://www.techtimes.com/articles/318638/20260618/openai-life-science-benchmark-reveals-ai-passes-only-1-3-scientific-research-tasks.htm)

- **What's net-new:** OpenAI published **LifeSciBench**, a **750-task**, expert-authored evaluation of AI on real-world life-science research, built with **173 PhD-level scientists** across biotech and pharma. Tasks span **seven workflows and seven biological domains**, are **free-response** (not multiple-choice), and are graded against expert-written rubrics.
- **Key finding:** The best-performing model, **GPT-Rosalind**, cleared only **36.1%** of tasks — i.e., nearly **two in three** research-level life-science tasks still defeat the most capable available system. A concrete, sobering benchmark on the gap between frontier models and autonomous scientific research.
- OpenAI also highlighted (June 17) an **AI-chemist** result with Molecule.one using GPT-5.4 to improve a key drug-making reaction.

- **Broader arXiv current (carry):** agentic/reasoning evaluation and inference efficiency (budget-aware agents, over/under-computation in reasoning models, KV-cache memory reduction). **Sources:** [arXiv cs.AI current](https://arxiv.org/list/cs.AI/current), [arXiv cs.LG current](https://arxiv.org/list/cs.LG/current)
