# arXiv AI Research - 2026-04-26

## Highlights (Top 5 Must-Read)

### 1. Nemotron 3 Super: Open, Efficient Mixture-of-Experts Hybrid Mamba-Transformer Model for Agentic Reasoning
**Authors**: NVIDIA Nemotron team
**arXiv**: [2604.12374](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.12374)
**TL;DR**: A 120B-parameter (12B active) hybrid Mamba-Attention MoE model purpose-built for agentic reasoning, combining selective state-space layers with sparse expert routing for long-context tool use. Released open with checkpoints, training recipe, and post-training data.
**Why it matters**: Demonstrates that hybrid SSM-Transformer MoE designs are now production-ready for agent workloads — a credible open competitor to frontier closed reasoners at a fraction of the active-parameter cost.

### 2. Parcae: Scaling Laws For Stable Looped Language Models
**Authors**: Parcae authors (arXiv listing)
**arXiv**: [2604.12946](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.12946)
**TL;DR**: Derives clean power laws for *looping* (recurrent reuse of the same weights) as a way to add training and test-time FLOPs without growing parameter count. Shows that loop count behaves as a third predictable scaling axis alongside data and parameters.
**Why it matters**: Gives the field a principled depth/compute scaling knob distinct from CoT and parameter scaling — particularly attractive for inference-bound deployments and small models that need to "think longer."

### 3. LLM Reasoning Is Latent, Not the Chain of Thought
**Authors**: arXiv:2604.15726 authors
**arXiv**: [2604.15726](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.15726)
**TL;DR**: Position paper arguing that LLM reasoning should be modeled as latent-state trajectory formation rather than treated as faithful surface CoT. Reframes interpretability and RL-for-reasoning around hidden state dynamics, not token traces.
**Why it matters**: Pushes back against an industry consensus that CoT *is* the reasoning, with implications for monitoring, alignment, and how we design verifiers for thinking models.

### 4. Your Agent, Their Asset: A Real-World Safety Analysis of OpenClaw
**Authors**: arXiv:2604.04759 authors
**arXiv**: [2604.04759](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.04759)
**TL;DR**: First real-world red-team of OpenClaw, the most widely deployed personal AI agent of early 2026. Introduces the CIK taxonomy (Capability/Identity/Knowledge) and shows poisoning any one dimension lifts attack success from 24.6% to 64–74% across Claude Sonnet 4.5, Opus 4.6, Gemini 3.1 Pro, and GPT-5.4.
**Why it matters**: Concrete, model-agnostic evidence that persistent agent state is the dominant attack surface in deployed agent systems — sets a baseline for the next year of agent-security work.

### 5. Generalization and Scaling Laws for Mixture-of-Experts Transformers
**Authors**: M. Zoubeirou et al.
**arXiv**: [2604.09175](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.09175)
**TL;DR**: A unified theory cleanly separating *active per-input capacity* from *routing combinatorics* in MoE Transformers, yielding scaling predictions that match empirical curves on language and vision benchmarks.
**Why it matters**: Long-overdue theoretical grounding for MoE design choices (expert count, top-k, granularity) that have been driven mostly by ablations — should accelerate efficient pretraining at frontier scale.
