# arXiv AI Research - 2026-05-09

## Highlights (Top 5 Must-Read)

### 1. Mamoda2.5: Enhancing Unified Multimodal Model with DiT-MoE
**Authors**: Mamoda team
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.02641
**TL;DR**: A unified AR-Diffusion framework that integrates multimodal understanding and generation in a single architecture, equipped with a Mixture-of-Experts design. Yields a 25B-parameter model that activates only 3B parameters per token.
**Why it matters**: Pushes the frontier of efficient unified multimodal models, demonstrating that sparse expert routing scales well across both understanding and generation in one model.

### 2. Evaluation Awareness in Language Models Has Limited Effect on Behaviour
**Authors**: (multiple, see paper)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.05835
**TL;DR**: Large reasoning models sometimes mention in their chain-of-thought that they may be under evaluation. This paper systematically tests whether such awareness causes strategic behavior change — and finds the effect is near-zero when evaluation cues are injected into CoTs.
**Why it matters**: Important empirical evidence for safety evaluators: at current capability levels, "sandbagging via eval-awareness" appears to be a smaller risk than feared.

### 3. RVPO: Risk-Sensitive Alignment via Variance Regularization
**Authors**: (multiple, see paper)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.05750
**TL;DR**: Critic-less RLHF methods aggregate multi-objective rewards via arithmetic mean, which masks blowups on individual signals. RVPO penalizes inter-reward variance during advantage aggregation. Evaluated on rubric-based medical/scientific reasoning with up to 17 concurrent reward signals.
**Why it matters**: Multi-rubric RLHF is becoming standard for complex agents; RVPO offers a principled fix to a known failure mode.

### 4. Explaining and Preventing Alignment Collapse in Iterative RLHF
**Authors**: (multiple, see paper)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.04266
**TL;DR**: In iterative RLHF, the policy generates the data its own reward model is retrained on, creating a feedback loop where policies exploit RM blind spots. Authors characterize this "alignment collapse" formally and propose Foresighted Policy Optimization (FPO) as a remedy.
**Why it matters**: Iterative deployment is the norm for frontier labs; this paper names and addresses a structural failure mode of the iterate-and-retrain workflow.

### 5. BALAR: A Bayesian Agentic Loop for Active Reasoning
**Authors**: (multiple, see paper)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.05386
**TL;DR**: Task-agnostic outer-loop algorithm for structured multi-turn agent–user interaction. Maintains a structured belief over latent task states and selects clarifying questions by maximizing expected mutual information.
**Why it matters**: Moves agentic LLM systems toward principled active inference rather than heuristic tool-calling, with strong potential for ambiguous-instruction tasks.
