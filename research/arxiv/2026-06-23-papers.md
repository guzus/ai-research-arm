## Highlights (Top 5 Must-Read)

### 1. Towards Engineering Scaling Laws with Pretraining Data Composition
**Authors**: (see arXiv listing)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.19781
**TL;DR**: Treats the data mixture itself as a controllable knob in the scaling
law rather than a fixed corpus. Shows that diverse, deliberately-composed
pretraining data shifts the compute-optimal frontier so that *data scaling*
pays off relative to parameter scaling, and that this behavior can be
predicted and "engineered" ahead of a large run.
**Why it matters**: Moves scaling-law practice from "pick Chinchilla ratios" to
actively designing the data distribution to get more capability per FLOP.

### 2. NRT-Bench: Multi-Turn Red-Teaming of LLM Agents in Safety-Critical Systems
**Authors**: Hanwool Lee et al.
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.20408
**TL;DR**: A benchmark putting LLM-operated agents in charge of a simulated
nuclear-power-plant control room (five-role operator team, six critical safety
functions) while adversaries inject messages over four channels across
multi-turn sessions. Harm is an *objective* plant-state signal, not an
LLM-judged text rating. Four frontier models all get pushed past safety limits
by adaptive multi-turn attacks (8.7%–12.1% failure rates).
**Why it matters**: Demonstrates that single-turn safety evals badly overstate
robustness; sustained adaptive pressure breaks frontier agents in
safety-critical loops.

### 3. Rethinking the Role of Efficient Attention in Hybrid Architectures
**Authors**: (see arXiv listing)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.15378
**TL;DR**: A controlled scaling-law study comparing efficient-attention designs
(linear / gated / sliding) inside Transformer–SSM hybrid models, isolating how
each shapes long-context capability rather than just throughput. Finds the
choice of efficient-attention block — not just the SSM backbone — drives the
long-context quality of the hybrid.
**Why it matters**: Gives architects an apples-to-apples basis for picking the
attention component in the now-dominant hybrid (Mamba/DeltaNet-style + attention)
design space.

### 4. LLM-as-an-Investigator: Evidence-First Reasoning for Robust Interactive Problem Diagnosis
**Authors**: (see arXiv listing)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.13220
**TL;DR**: Targets "user-driven sycophancy" — an LLM latching onto a user's
unverified hypothesis instead of testing alternatives. The Solution
Investigator Agent estimates problem ambiguity, generates competing candidate
hypotheses, and asks targeted clarifying questions. Evaluated with a three-agent
pipeline (case extractor, ground-truth user simulator that hides the answer,
and the tested assistant).
**Why it matters**: A concrete, evaluable recipe for making diagnostic agents
reason from evidence rather than confirm the user's prior.

### 5. Benchmarking AI Agents for Addressing Scientific Challenges Across Scales
**Authors**: (see arXiv listing)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.12736
**TL;DR**: Argues existing agent benchmarks miss the complexity, heterogeneity,
and extended-reasoning demands of real scientific work, and introduces a
benchmark spanning scientific challenges across scales to measure long-horizon,
multi-tool scientific reasoning.
**Why it matters**: Pushes agent evaluation past web/coding toy tasks toward the
discovery workloads labs actually want agents for.
