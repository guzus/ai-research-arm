## Highlights (Top 5 Must-Read)

### 1. OpenCoF: Learning to Reason Through Video Generation
**Authors**: Xinyan Chen, Ziyu Guo, et al.
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08763
**TL;DR**: Introduces "Chain-of-Frame" reasoning, where a video generation model reasons over a task by producing temporally connected frames rather than text tokens, backed by a new 17K-example dataset and a fine-tuned model spanning multiple task families.
**Why it matters**: Proposes video generation itself as a reasoning substrate, a genuinely new modality for chain-of-thought beyond text and still images.

### 2. A First-Principles Theory of Slow Thinking and Active Perception
**Authors**: Hongkang Yang, Zhi-Qin John Xu, et al.
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08196
**TL;DR**: Derives "slow thinking" reasoning behavior from an active-lifting theory built on latent sequence sampling and uncertainty reduction, with direct implications for encoder-decoder architecture design.
**Why it matters**: A theoretical foundation for why test-time reasoning works, rather than another empirical reasoning benchmark — useful for grounding future architecture choices.

### 3. When the Judge Changes, So Does the Measurement: Auditing LLM-as-Judge Reliability
**Authors**: Zongyou Yang, Yinghan Hou, Xiaokun Yang
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08535
**TL;DR**: Shows that upgrading the judge model in LLM-as-judge evaluation pipelines produces inconsistent results across datasets, with position and verbosity biases persisting even in stronger judges, and argues for dataset slices plus audit trails.
**Why it matters**: A huge fraction of current LLM evaluation (including in this very pipeline's methodology) leans on LLM-as-judge; this paper questions the stability of that yardstick.

### 4. Token-Flow Firewall: Semantic Runtime Auditing for Persistent AI Agents
**Authors**: Puji Wang, Yingchen Zhang, et al.
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08395
**TL;DR**: A semantic firewall monitors the token flow of long-running agents at runtime, cutting attack success rate to 12.5% while keeping a 97.4% benign pass rate and adding only 0.69s latency.
**Why it matters**: Concrete, low-overhead runtime defense for the exact class of persistent/long-horizon agents that are becoming the default deployment shape.

### 5. Native Video-Action Pretraining for Generalizable Robot Control (LingBot-VA 2.0)
**Authors**: Qihang Zhang, Lin Li, et al.
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08639
**TL;DR**: A foundation model for embodied control built on semantic visual-action tokenization and causal video-action pretraining, aiming for cross-embodiment generalization.
**Why it matters**: Another entrant in the race toward general-purpose robot foundation models trained directly on video-action pairs rather than text-mediated instructions.
