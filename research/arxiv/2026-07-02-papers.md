## Highlights (Top 5 Must-Read)

### 1. AutoMem: Automated Learning of Memory as a Cognitive Skill
**Authors**: Ziyu Chen–style agentic-memory team (see paper)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.01224
**TL;DR**: Treats an LLM agent's memory management (what to encode, when to
retrieve, how to organize files) as an independently trainable skill.
A two-loop framework has a strong LLM revise the memory *structure* from
full trajectories while distilling the agent's own good memory decisions
into *proficiency*. Optimizing memory alone — without touching task-action
behavior — improved base-agent performance ~2–4× across Crafter, MiniHack,
and NetHack, bringing a 32B open-weight model level with Claude Opus 4.5
and Gemini 3.1 Pro Thinking.
**Why it matters**: Reframes long-horizon agent capability as a memory
problem, showing a high-leverage axis for closing the gap to frontier
systems without larger models.

### 2. Is One Layer Enough? Training a Single Transformer Layer Can Match Full-Parameter RL Training
**Authors**: Zijian Zhang, Rizhen Hu, Athanasios Glentis, Dawei Li, Chung-Yiu Yau, Hongzhou Lin, Mingyi Hong
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.01232
**TL;DR**: A systematic layer-wise study of RL post-training (GRPO, GiGPO,
Dr. GRPO across Qwen3/Qwen2.5) finds RL gains are highly concentrated:
training a *single* transformer layer recovers most of full-parameter RL's
improvement and sometimes exceeds it. High-contribution layers sit in the
middle of the stack; layer rankings stay stable across datasets, tasks,
model families, and RL algorithms.
**Why it matters**: Suggests RLHF/RLVR post-training is far more parameter-
sparse than assumed, pointing to dramatically cheaper RL fine-tuning.

### 3. TiRex-2: Generalizing TiRex to Multivariate Data and Streaming
**Authors**: TiRex-2 team (NX-AI / xLSTM lineage)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.01204
**TL;DR**: A recurrent xLSTM-based time-series foundation model that extends
univariate TiRex to multivariate forecasting with past *and* future
covariates. A memory-centric recurrent design gives constant per-patch cost
under streaming (vs. Transformers' quadratic recompute), combining a
bidirectional time mixer with an asymmetric grouped-attention variate mixer
while preserving strict causality. SOTA zero-shot on GIFT-Eval and fev-bench
with only ~38M (univariate) / +44M (multivariate) active params.
**Why it matters**: First TSFM to unify multivariate, future-known
covariates, streaming, and constant inference cost — a credible non-
Transformer foundation-model architecture.

### 4. Message Passing Enables Efficient Reasoning
**Authors**: MPLM team (pre-print)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.01077
**TL;DR**: Introduces Message Passing Language Models (MPLMs): parallel
reasoning threads that communicate directly via lightweight send/receive
primitives instead of transient fork-join workers. This cuts redundant
context sharing and lets threads preempt (terminate early on peers' partial
results). On Sudoku, MPLMs need asymptotically smaller context than serial
CoT or fork-join; a fine-tuned model solves 25×25 puzzles that stump CoT,
FJ, and tool-less frontier reasoners, with gains on 3-SAT and long-context QA.
**Why it matters**: A new inference-time scaling primitive that beats both
sequential CoT and fork-join on communication cost — a fresh axis for
efficient parallel reasoning.

### 5. The Model Organism Lottery: Model Organism Interpretability Strongly Depends on Training Methodology
**Authors**: Model-organism interpretability team
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.01033
**TL;DR**: Builds 54 OLMo2-1B / gemma-3-1b "model organisms" (LMs trained to
exhibit hidden undesired behaviors) via seven training recipes, then
benchmarks activation oracles, steering, logit lens, and SAEs. Interpretability
success depends heavily on training objective, target behavior, architecture,
and data-generation pipeline; large variance persists even after controlling
for behavior strength; and more realistic *integrated* training yields
*less* interpretable organisms than the usual post-hoc SFT.
**Why it matters**: Casts serious doubt on whether current model organisms
are valid proxies for interpretability research — a caution for safety
evals built on them.
