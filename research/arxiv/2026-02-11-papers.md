# arXiv AI Research - 2026-02-11

## Highlights (Top 5 Must-Read)

### 1. Reinforcement Inference: Leveraging Uncertainty for Self-Correcting Language Model Reasoning
**Authors**: Xinhai Sun et al.
**arXiv**: [2602.08520](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.08520)
**TL;DR**: Introduces an entropy-aware inference-time control strategy that uses a model's own uncertainty to selectively trigger a second reasoning attempt. On 12,032 MMLU-Pro questions, accuracy jumps from 60.72% to 84.03% using DeepSeek-v3.2, with only 61% additional inference calls.
**Why it matters**: A training-free method that dramatically improves reasoning accuracy by identifying when models are uncertain and need a second pass — practical and immediately deployable.

### 2. Accordion-Thinking: Self-Regulated Step Summaries for Efficient and Readable LLM Reasoning
**Authors**: Zhicheng Yang et al.
**arXiv**: [2602.03249](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.03249)
**TL;DR**: An RL-trained framework where LLMs learn to dynamically summarize intermediate reasoning steps ("Fold mode"), discarding prior tokens to reduce KV cache overhead. Achieves 3x throughput on 48GB GPUs while maintaining accuracy, and the accuracy gap between Fold and Unfold modes vanishes over training.
**Why it matters**: Directly addresses the practical bottleneck of long chain-of-thought reasoning — KV cache growth — while preserving human-readable reasoning traces.

### 3. Revisiting the Shape Convention of Transformer Language Models (Hourglass FFN)
**Authors**: Not specified
**arXiv**: [2602.06471](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.06471)
**TL;DR**: Challenges the universal narrow-wide-narrow FFN design in Transformers by replacing it with deeper hourglass-shaped (wide-narrow-wide) sub-MLPs connected by residual pathways. Hourglass FFNs outperform conventional FFNs up to 400M params and match at 1B, with improved parameter allocation between attention and FFN.
**Why it matters**: Rethinks a fundamental design choice baked into nearly every Transformer since 2017, showing the standard FFN shape is suboptimal.

### 4. Parallel Track Transformers: Enabling Fast GPU Inference with Reduced Synchronization
**Authors**: Not specified
**arXiv**: [2602.07306](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.07306)
**TL;DR**: Restructures the Transformer into independent "tracks" — smaller parallel sub-transformers requiring synchronization only at input/output boundaries. Achieves 16x fewer synchronization ops vs standard tensor parallelism, 15-30% reduced time-to-first-token, and up to 31.9% higher throughput in TensorRT-LLM and vLLM.
**Why it matters**: Directly tackles the multi-GPU communication bottleneck that limits real-world LLM serving throughput.

### 5. OmniFysics: Exploring Physical Intelligence via Omni-Modal Architecture and Physical Data Engine
**Authors**: Not specified
**arXiv**: [2602.07064](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.07064)
**TL;DR**: A compact omni-modal model unifying understanding across image, audio, video, and text with integrated speech and image generation. Introduces a physical data engine (FysicsAny + FysicsOmniCap) that injects physics-grounded knowledge, and FysicsEval benchmark for measuring physical intelligence emergence.
**Why it matters**: Pushes toward truly unified multimodal models that understand physical properties across sensory modalities — a step toward embodied AI.
