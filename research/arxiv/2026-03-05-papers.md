# arXiv AI Research - 2026-03-05

## Highlights (Top 5 Must-Read)

### 1. Beyond Language Modeling: An Exploration of Multimodal Pretraining
**Authors**: Transfusion research team
**arXiv**: [2603.03276](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.03276)
**TL;DR**: Comprehensive from-scratch pretraining experiments isolating factors governing native multimodal models. Introduces Representation Autoencoder (RAE) as optimal unified visual representation, shows MoE enables efficient multimodal scaling with natural modality specialization, and uncovers scaling asymmetry where vision is significantly more data-hungry than language.
**Why it matters**: Provides the clearest empirical roadmap yet for training native multimodal foundation models beyond language-only paradigms.

### 2. CHIMERA: Compact Synthetic Data for Generalizable LLM Reasoning
**Authors**: Xinyu Zhu, Yihao Feng, Yanchao Sun, Xianzhi Du, Pingzhi Li, Olli Saarikivi, Yun Zhu, Yu Meng
**arXiv**: [2603.00889](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.00889)
**TL;DR**: A 9K-sample synthetic reasoning dataset with long CoT trajectories spanning 8 scientific disciplines. A fine-tuned 4B model achieves performance competitive with DeepSeek-R1 and Qwen3-235B on GPQA-Diamond, AIME24/25/26, HMMT25, and Humanity's Last Exam.
**Why it matters**: Demonstrates that tiny, high-quality synthetic datasets can dramatically close the gap between small and frontier-scale reasoning models.

### 3. Qwen3-Coder-Next Technical Report
**Authors**: Qwen Team (Alibaba Cloud)
**arXiv**: [2603.00729](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.00729)
**TL;DR**: Open-weight coding agent model built on Qwen3-Next-80B-A3B-Base with hybrid attention and MoE. Uses a staged pipeline: continued pretraining on code/agent domains, SFT on agentic coding data, specialization of expert models across SWE workflows, then distillation back into a unified model.
**Why it matters**: Major open-weight release purpose-built for agentic coding, advancing the state of the art for local AI-assisted development.

### 4. Learning When to Act or Refuse: Guarding Agentic Reasoning Models for Safe Multi-Step Tool Use
**Authors**: Aradhye Agarwal et al.
**arXiv**: [2603.03205](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.03205)
**TL;DR**: Introduces MOSAIC, a post-training framework that aligns agents for safe multi-step tool use. Structures inference as a "plan, check, then act or refuse" loop with explicit safety reasoning and refusal as first-class actions.
**Why it matters**: Addresses the critical gap in safety for agentic systems that can take real-world actions through tool use.

### 5. RLAR: An Agentic Reward System for Multi-task Reinforcement Learning on Large Language Models
**Authors**: RLAR research team
**arXiv**: [2603.00724](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.00724)
**TL;DR**: Agent-driven framework that dynamically assigns tailored reward functions to individual queries by transforming reward acquisition into a dynamic tool synthesis task. LLM agents autonomously retrieve optimal reward models and synthesize programmatic verifiers, yielding 10-60% gains across math, coding, translation, and dialogue.
**Why it matters**: Moves beyond static reward models toward dynamic, query-adaptive reward systems for RL alignment.
