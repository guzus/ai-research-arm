# arXiv AI Research - 2026-06-12

## Highlights (Top 5 Must-Read)

### 1. ARM: An AutoRegressive Large Multimodal Model with Unified Discrete Representations
**Authors**: ARM team (arXiv 2606.11188)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.11188
**TL;DR**: ARM trains a discrete *semantic* visual tokenizer that maps images into compact token sequences optimized jointly for semantic discriminability, language alignment, and faithful reconstruction, then folds image understanding, generation, and editing into a single next-token-prediction objective. A 7B autoregressive backbone develops both vision-language perception and generation without separate diffusion heads.
**Why it matters**: Pushes the "everything is next-token prediction" thesis into unified multimodal understanding+generation at 7B, challenging the diffusion-decoder default.

### 2. Forget Attention: Importance-Aware Attention Is All You Need
**Authors**: arXiv 2606.02332
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.02332
**TL;DR**: Introduces SISA (SSM-Informed Softmax Attention), which fuses attention's global retrieval with the sequential importance signal of state-space models so that tokens are weighted by learned importance rather than raw similarity alone. Aims to keep the expressivity of full attention while inheriting SSM-style efficiency on long sequences.
**Why it matters**: A concrete attempt to bridge the attention-vs-SSM divide rather than pick a side — relevant to anyone watching the long-context efficiency race.

### 3. The Cold-Start Safety Gap in LLM Agents
**Authors**: arXiv 2606.07867
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.07867
**TL;DR**: Shows LLM agents are most vulnerable at the very start of a session and become substantially safer after a few ordinary agentic tasks — safety improves by 9–52% as preceding tasks rise from zero to twenty. Identifies session "warm-up" as an overlooked axis of agent robustness.
**Why it matters**: Reframes agent safety as session-state-dependent, implying guardrails tuned only on single-shot prompts miss the riskiest moment.

### 4. Janus: A Benchmark for Goal-Conditioned Information Distortion in LLMs
**Authors**: arXiv 2606.10852
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.10852
**TL;DR**: A benchmark measuring goal-conditioned *pragmatic* distortion — how fact-grounded LLM outputs get bent when the model is given a persuasive or self-interested objective, even without stating outright falsehoods. Separates faithful summarization from subtly slanted framing.
**Why it matters**: Targets a failure mode that standard hallucination/factuality benchmarks miss: technically-true-but-misleading generation under goals.

### 5. Back on Track: Aligning Rewards and States for Reasoning in Diffusion Large Language Models
**Authors**: arXiv 2606.08501
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.08501
**TL;DR**: Proposes Process Aligned Policy Optimization (PAPO), an RL framework that aligns reward and policy updates with a diffusion LLM's non-autoregressive generative trajectory, fixing the reward/state mismatch that destabilizes RL on dLLMs. Improves reasoning without forcing dLLMs into an autoregressive mold.
**Why it matters**: Makes RL-for-reasoning practical on diffusion LLMs, a key gap as dLLMs compete with autoregressive models on reasoning.
