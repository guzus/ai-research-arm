# arXiv AI Research - 2026-02-01

## Highlights (Top 5 Must-Read)

### 1. [CoScale-RL: Efficient Post-Training by Co-Scaling Data and Computation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.14695)
**Authors**: Yutong Chen, Jiandong Gao, Ji Wu
**arXiv**: [2601.14695](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.14695)
**TL;DR**: Proposes a novel scaling strategy for training Large Reasoning Models that co-scales data (multiple solutions per problem) and computation (rollout scaling) with a Re-distillation model merge technique. Achieves 3.76× average accuracy improvement on four benchmarks and breaks the reasoning ability boundary for a 0.5B model on 10K-token problems.
**Why it matters**: Demonstrates that small models can achieve strong reasoning through principled scaling of RL post-training, opening a new scaling direction beyond simply enlarging datasets or models.

### 2. [CADA: Case-Augmented Deliberative Alignment for LLM Safety](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.08000)
**Authors**: Rutgers, NVIDIA Research, NTU, Adobe Research, UConn, Fordham, Google DeepMind
**arXiv**: [2601.08000](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.08000)
**TL;DR**: Proposes CADA, a case-augmented deliberative alignment method that uses RL on self-generated safety reasoning chains. Finds that referencing explicit safety codes inconsistently improves harmlessness and degrades helpfulness, whereas training on case-augmented codes yields robust, generalized safety behaviors — effectively bringing deliberative alignment to open-source models.
**Why it matters**: First rigorous study of deliberative alignment (pioneered by OpenAI's o-series) in open-source LLMs, with practical solutions for the helpfulness-safety tradeoff.

### 3. [Latent-Space Contrastive RL for Stable and Efficient LLM Reasoning (DLR)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.17275)
**Authors**: (Multiple institutions)
**arXiv**: [2601.17275](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.17275)
**TL;DR**: Introduces DeepLatent Reasoning (DLR), which shifts RL trial-and-error from expensive token-level sequence generation to a continuous latent manifold. Addresses three core RL-for-LLM challenges: sample-inefficient rollouts, high gradient variance, and catastrophic forgetting via bidirectional contrastive learning in latent space.
**Why it matters**: Proposes a fundamentally different approach to RL-based reasoning training that could dramatically reduce compute costs while improving stability.

### 4. [Agentic Memory (AgeMem): Unified Long-Term and Short-Term Memory for LLM Agents](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.01885)
**Authors**: (Multiple institutions)
**arXiv**: [2601.01885](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.01885)
**TL;DR**: Introduces a unified memory framework that exposes memory operations (store, retrieve, update, summarize, discard) as tool-based actions, letting LLM agents autonomously manage long-term and short-term memory. Consistently outperforms strong memory-augmented baselines across five long-horizon benchmarks and multiple LLM backbones.
**Why it matters**: Solves a fundamental bottleneck in LLM agents — the inability to maintain and manage information across long task horizons — with a practical, tool-integrated approach.

### 5. [The Art of Scaling Reinforcement Learning Compute for LLMs](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.13786)
**Authors**: Devvrit Khatri et al.
**arXiv**: [2510.13786](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.13786)
**TL;DR**: First large-scale systematic study (400,000+ GPU-hours) establishing principled RL scaling laws for LLMs. Fits sigmoidal compute-performance curves and ablates common design choices, finding that loss aggregation, normalization, and curriculum primarily modulate compute efficiency without shifting asymptotic performance.
**Why it matters**: Provides the first predictive scaling framework for RL post-training, analogous to Chinchilla scaling laws for pretraining — essential for planning compute budgets for reasoning model training.
