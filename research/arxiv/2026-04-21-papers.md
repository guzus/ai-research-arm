# arXiv AI Research - 2026-04-21

## Highlights (Top 5 Must-Read)

### 1. Parcae: Scaling Laws For Stable Looped Language Models
**Authors**: Hayden Prairie, Zachary Novack, Taylor Berg-Kirkpatrick, Daniel Y. Fu
**arXiv**: [2604.12946](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.12946)
**TL;DR**: Recasts looping as a nonlinear time-variant dynamical system over the residual stream to prevent the residual explosion and loss spikes that plague looped architectures. Derives stable training recipes and new scaling laws where FLOPs come from loop iterations instead of added parameters.
**Why it matters**: First principled scaling-law analysis of looped LMs — makes depth-recurrent models a viable, more parameter-efficient alternative to fixed-depth transformers.

### 2. Latent-Condensed Transformer for Efficient Long Context Modeling
**Authors**: (undisclosed at time of preprint)
**arXiv**: [2604.12452](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.12452)
**TL;DR**: Introduces Latent-Condensed Attention (LCA), which condenses context directly inside MLA's latent space. Jointly slashes compute and KV-cache without adding parameters, extending usable context dramatically.
**Why it matters**: A drop-in replacement for MLA-style attention that pushes long-context inference costs down without retraining from scratch.

### 3. LASA: Language-Agnostic Semantic Alignment at the Semantic Bottleneck for LLM Safety
**Authors**: (multiple, see paper)
**arXiv**: [2604.12710](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.12710)
**TL;DR**: Argues LLM jailbreaks in low-resource languages stem from mismatch between language-agnostic semantic understanding and language-dominant safety alignment. LASA anchors alignment directly in semantic bottlenecks, dropping attack success from 24.7% → 2.8% on LLaMA-3.1-8B-Instruct.
**Why it matters**: Practical, architecture-level fix for one of the most reliable classes of jailbreak in deployed frontier models.

### 4. MERRIN: A Benchmark for Multimodal Evidence Retrieval and Reasoning in Noisy Web Environments
**Authors**: (see paper)
**arXiv**: [2604.13418](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.13418)
**TL;DR**: New benchmark where questions carry no explicit modality cues — agents must infer whether text, image, video, or audio sources are needed, then retrieve and reason over noisy real-web content. Average accuracy is 22.3%; the strongest system (Gemini-3.1-Pro with Agentic Multimodal Search) reaches only 40.1%.
**Why it matters**: Exposes how far frontier multimodal agents still are from open-ended real-world research, with video/audio as the weakest legs.

### 5. AI Achieves a Perfect LSAT Score
**Authors**: (see paper)
**arXiv**: [2604.10034](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.10034)
**TL;DR**: Documents the first language model to achieve a perfect score on an officially released LSAT. Prompt variation, answer shuffling, and multi-sampling had no meaningful effect; ablating the thinking phase cut accuracy by up to 8 pts, mostly on logical-reasoning items.
**Why it matters**: Clean ablation evidence that extended "thinking" traces are doing real work on formal-reasoning tasks, not just benchmark contamination.
