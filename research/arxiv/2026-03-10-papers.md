# arXiv AI Research - 2026-03-10

## Highlights (Top 5 Must-Read)

### 1. Mamba-3: Improved Sequence Modeling Using State Space Principles
**Authors**: Albert Gu, Tri Dao et al.
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2405.21060 (Mamba-2 lineage)
**TL;DR**: Mamba-3 introduces three core methodological improvements to the SSM architecture: a more expressive recurrence via complex state update rules, and a multi-input/multi-output formulation. Achieves significant gains across retrieval, state-tracking, and language modeling while maintaining sub-quadratic complexity.
**Why it matters**: The first linear sequence model positioned as a serious long-term alternative to Transformers rather than a research curiosity — accepted at ICLR 2026.

### 2. SAHOO: Safeguarded Alignment for High-Order Optimization in Recursive Self-Improvement
**Authors**: Subramanyam Sahoo, Aman Chadha, Vinija Jain, Divya Chaudhary
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.06333
**TL;DR**: A framework to monitor and control alignment drift during recursive self-improvement via a Goal Drift Index (GDI), constraint preservation checks, and regression-risk quantification. Achieves 18.3% improvement in code tasks and 16.8% in reasoning while preserving safety constraints across 189 tasks.
**Why it matters**: Makes alignment preservation during recursive self-improvement measurable and deployable at scale — critical as AI systems increasingly self-modify.

### 3. COLD-Steer: Steering Large Language Models via In-Context One-step Learning Dynamics
**Authors**: Kartik Sharma, Rakshit S. Trivedi
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/list/cs.LG/recent
**TL;DR**: Proposes a method to steer LLM behavior through in-context one-step learning dynamics, enabling controlled generation without fine-tuning. Provides a principled framework for influencing model outputs at inference time.
**Why it matters**: Accepted at ICLR 2026 — offers a lightweight alternative to RLHF and fine-tuning for controlling LLM behavior.

### 4. Intent Laundering: AI Safety Datasets Are Not What They Seem
**Authors**: Multiple authors
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.16729
**TL;DR**: Introduces "intent laundering" — abstracting triggering cues from attacks while preserving malicious intent. Demonstrates that current safety datasets fail to represent real-world attacks; once cues are removed, all tested models (including Gemini 3 Pro and Claude Sonnet 3.7) become unsafe with 90-98% attack success rates.
**Why it matters**: Fundamentally challenges the validity of current AI safety evaluation methods and red-teaming benchmarks.

### 5. MultiHaystack: Benchmarking Multimodal Retrieval and Reasoning over 40K Images, Videos, and Documents
**Authors**: Dannong Xu et al.
**arXiv**: Under review at ICLR 2026 (https://openreview.net/pdf/b5d9ff0a7847dfd89c9d1b9267caed073e2854f8.pdf)
**TL;DR**: The first benchmark designed to evaluate both retrieval and reasoning under large-scale, cross-modal conditions spanning video, image, and document modalities at 40K scale. Addresses the gap where existing benchmarks restrict retrieval to small, single-modality candidate sets.
**Why it matters**: Sets a new standard for evaluating multimodal foundation models in realistic retrieval-heavy scenarios.
