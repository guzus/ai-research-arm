# arXiv AI Research - 2026-01-20

## Highlights (Top 5 Must-Read)

### 1. Agentic Memory: Unified Long-Term and Short-Term Memory Management for LLM Agents
**Authors**: Research team (arXiv:2601.01885)
**arXiv**: [https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.01885](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.01885)
**TL;DR**: AgeMem introduces a unified framework that integrates long-term and short-term memory management directly into the agent's policy, exposing memory operations as tool-based actions. The agent autonomously decides what and when to store, retrieve, update, summarize, or discard information.
**Why it matters**: Addresses a fundamental challenge in LLM agents by enabling autonomous memory management, consistently outperforming baselines across five long-horizon benchmarks with multiple LLM backbones.

### 2. What Matters For Safety Alignment?
**Authors**: Research team (arXiv:2601.03868)
**arXiv**: [https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.03868](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.03868)
**TL;DR**: Comprehensive empirical study evaluating 32 recent LLMs and LRMs across thirteen model families (3B-235B parameters), using five safety datasets and probing with 56 jailbreak techniques and four CoT attack strategies (4.6M API calls total).
**Why it matters**: Largest systematic evaluation of safety alignment to date, providing essential insights for developing more secure and reliable AI systems.

### 3. Benchmark²: Systematic Evaluation of LLM Benchmarks
**Authors**: Qi Qian, Chengsong Huang, et al. (arXiv:2601.03986)
**arXiv**: [https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.03986](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.03986)
**TL;DR**: Meta-evaluation framework for assessing benchmark quality itself through Cross-Benchmark Ranking Consistency, Discriminability Score, and Capability Alignment Deviation. Examines 15 benchmarks across mathematics, reasoning, and knowledge domains with 11 LLMs.
**Why it matters**: Addresses critical questions about benchmark reliability and validity as LLMs approach human-level performance on traditional metrics.

### 4. BabyVision: Visual Reasoning Beyond Language
**Authors**: Research team (arXiv:2601.06521, January 13, 2026)
**arXiv**: [https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.06521](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.06521)
**TL;DR**: New benchmark evaluating early-vision capabilities of MLLMs targeting foundational visual skills humans acquire before language. Best model achieves 49.7% accuracy versus 94.1% for adult humans (44.4% gap).
**Why it matters**: Reveals fundamental limitations in multimodal models' basic visual perception capabilities, challenging assumptions about their visual understanding.

### 5. Geometric and Dynamic Scaling in Deep Transformers
**Authors**: Research team (arXiv:2601.01014, January 2026)
**arXiv**: [https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.01014](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.01014)
**TL;DR**: Proposes Manifold-Geometric Transformer (MGT) using manifold-constrained hyper-connections and deep delta learning to enable extreme depth scaling without rank collapse, supporting data-dependent non-monotonic updates and feature erasure.
**Why it matters**: Breakthrough in scaling transformers to extreme depth, addressing critical architectural limitations in standard residual connections.
