# arXiv AI Research - 2026-04-29

## Highlights (Top 5 Must-Read)

### 1. TEMPO: Scaling Test-time Training for Large Reasoning Models
**Authors**: (multi-author submission)
**arXiv**: [2604.19295](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.19295)
**TL;DR**: Existing test-time training methods for large reasoning models plateau quickly and fail to benefit from additional compute. TEMPO introduces an updated TTT recipe that scales monotonically with test-time compute, lifting OLMO3-7B on AIME 2024 from 33.0% to 51.1% and Qwen3-14B from 42.3% to 65.8%.
**Why it matters**: Reopens the test-time training axis for reasoning LLMs after months of disappointing results, with model-agnostic gains rivaling much larger fixed-weight inference baselines.

### 2. Hyperloop Transformers
**Authors**: (anonymous arXiv preprint)
**arXiv**: [2604.21254](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21254)
**TL;DR**: Re-uses Transformer layers across depth as a core architectural primitive. The Hyperloop variant outperforms depth-matched dense Transformer baselines while using ~50% fewer parameters, suggesting weight-tying through deep loops is a viable Pareto move.
**Why it matters**: A serious new architecture proposal in an area dominated by minor MoE tweaks — relevant for on-device and inference-cost-sensitive deployments.

### 3. Parcae: Scaling Laws for Stable Looped Language Models
**Authors**: (multi-author submission)
**arXiv**: [2604.12946](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.12946)
**TL;DR**: Establishes scaling laws for looped LMs that send activations through a block of layers in a loop rather than scaling parameters. Parcae achieves up to 6.3% lower validation perplexity than prior large-scale looped models while remaining stable at scale.
**Why it matters**: Pairs naturally with Hyperloop Transformers (above) — together they signal a real revival of recurrence/looping as a scaling axis distinct from depth and width.

### 4. Procedural Knowledge at Scale Improves Reasoning
**Authors**: (multi-author submission)
**arXiv**: [2604.01348](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.01348)
**TL;DR**: Introduces Reasoning Memory, a RAG framework that decomposes prior reasoning trajectories into 32M compact procedural knowledge entries and reuses them at inference. Shifts test-time scaling from per-problem isolation to cross-problem knowledge transfer.
**Why it matters**: Shows that "memory of how to think" — not just facts — is a separate scaling axis, and that procedural retrieval can stack on top of CoT/search-based methods.

### 5. Reward Models Are Secretly Value Functions: Temporally Coherent Reward Modeling
**Authors**: (multi-author submission)
**arXiv**: [2604.22981](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.22981)
**TL;DR**: Reframes reward models as value functions and introduces Temporally Coherent Reward Modeling (TCRM), adding two regularization terms to the standard Bradley-Terry loss to enforce temporal consistency along reasoning trajectories.
**Why it matters**: Addresses a long-standing weakness of process reward models — inconsistent scoring across nearby steps — with a principled fix that plugs into existing RLHF pipelines.
