# arXiv AI Research - 2026-06-18

## Highlights (Top 5 Must-Read)

### 1. Stop When Further Reasoning Won't Help: Attention-State Adaptive Generation in Reasoning Models
**Authors**: (arXiv preprint, cs.CL)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.15070
**TL;DR**: Large reasoning models waste tokens "overthinking," which can even degrade accuracy. The authors introduce ASAG, a training-free method that reads the model's internal reasoning/attention state and adaptively halts or redirects generation, dropping into existing LRMs with no retraining.
**Why it matters**: Test-time-compute control is the dominant efficiency lever for reasoning models, and a training-free, drop-in stopping rule is directly deployable.

### 2. Tying the Loop: Tied Expert Layers in Mixture-of-Experts Language Models
**Authors**: (arXiv preprint, cs.LG)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.16825
**TL;DR**: MoE models cut per-token compute but still must hold the full expert parameter count in memory. This work proposes tying expert layers to attack the compute-vs-memory tension, shrinking the memory footprint that dominates MoE training and inference.
**Why it matters**: Memory, not FLOPs, is the practical ceiling for scaling MoE models; parameter tying targets exactly that bottleneck.

### 3. Predictable Scaling Laws of Optimal Hyperparameters for LLM Continued Pre-training
**Authors**: (arXiv preprint, cs.LG)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.05610
**TL;DR**: The paper shows that optimal learning rate and batch size during continued pre-training follow stable, predictable scaling laws, and proposes a framework relating compute budget to the best hyperparameters for a given checkpoint.
**Why it matters**: Continued pre-training is how most labs adapt base models; predictable HP laws remove costly per-run sweeps.

### 4. Automated Jailbreak Attack Targeting Multiple Defense Strategies
**Authors**: (arXiv preprint, cs.CL/cs.CR)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.16751
**TL;DR**: RLHF/RLAIF refusal training acts as a soft constraint on the response distribution rather than removing hazardous knowledge. The authors build an automated attack that generalizes across several deployed defense strategies, exposing the gap between refusal behavior and actual knowledge removal.
**Why it matters**: Red-teaming that breaks multiple defenses at once is a sharper measure of real-world alignment robustness than single-defense evals.

### 5. A Spatio-Temporal Expert Prefetching Framework for Efficient MoE-based LLM Inference
**Authors**: (arXiv preprint, cs.LG/cs.DC)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.15453
**TL;DR**: Expert selections in MoE LLMs (Qwen, DeepSeek-style) are strongly correlated both across adjacent layers (spatial) and across consecutive tokens (temporal). The framework exploits both correlations to prefetch experts and cut MoE inference latency.
**Why it matters**: Expert-routing I/O is a major serving cost for MoE models; correlation-aware prefetching is a concrete win for inference systems.
