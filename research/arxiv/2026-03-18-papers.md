# arXiv AI Research - 2026-03-18

## Highlights (Top 5 Must-Read)

### 1. [Mamba-3: Improved Sequence Modeling using State Space Principles](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.15569)
**Authors**: Aakash Lahoti et al.
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.15569
**TL;DR**: Introduces three core improvements to SSM-based architectures: a more expressive recurrence via SSM discretization, complex-valued state updates for richer state tracking, and a multi-input multi-output (MIMO) formulation. At 1.5B scale, improves average downstream accuracy by 0.6 points over Gated DeltaNet, with MIMO adding another 1.2 points, while achieving comparable perplexity to Mamba-2 at half the state size.
**Why it matters**: Advances the performance-efficiency Pareto frontier for sub-quadratic sequence models, directly challenging Transformer dominance for inference-efficient LLMs.

### 2. [Demystifying Group Relative Policy Optimization: Its Policy Gradient is a U-Statistic](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.01162)
**Authors**: (Multiple authors)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.01162
**TL;DR**: Provides the first rigorous theoretical analysis of GRPO — the key RL method behind DeepSeek-R1 and other reasoning model breakthroughs. Shows that GRPO's policy gradient is a U-statistic, offering new insights into why eliminating the critic model and using average group rewards works so well. The methodology was formalized in a paper published in Nature.
**Why it matters**: GRPO is arguably the most impactful RL technique for LLM reasoning in 2025-2026; this paper provides the missing theoretical foundation.

### 3. [Towards Next-Generation LLM Training: From the Data-Centric Perspective](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.14712)
**Authors**: Hao Liang et al. (Peking University)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.14712
**TL;DR**: Comprehensive survey arguing that data preparation and utilization remain the major bottleneck for LLM training. Covers data collection, cleaning, synthesis, curriculum design, and the emerging role of agent-based data preparation systems. Provides a roadmap for next-generation data-centric training pipelines.
**Why it matters**: As model architectures converge, data quality and curation become the primary differentiator — this survey maps the entire landscape.

### 4. [What Do Visual Tokens Really Encode? Uncovering Sparsity and Redundancy in MLLMs](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.00510)
**Authors**: Yingqi Fan et al.
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.00510
**TL;DR**: Introduces EmbedLens, a probing tool revealing that visual tokens in multimodal LLMs partition into sink, dead, and alive categories — with only ~60% of tokens carrying image-specific meaning. Uncovers significant semantic sparsity and redundancy in how MLLMs process visual information.
**Why it matters**: Reveals massive inefficiency in current vision-language architectures, opening the door to dramatic token reduction and faster multimodal inference.

### 5. [GR3: Group Relative Reward Rescaling for Reinforcement Learning](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.10535)
**Authors**: (Multiple authors)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.10535
**TL;DR**: Addresses "length inflation" — where RL-trained models produce unnecessarily long outputs. Proposes GR3, a multiplicative rescaling paradigm that acts as a continuous, reward-dependent gating mechanism. Maintains GRPO-level performance while significantly reducing output length across both RLHF and RLVR settings.
**Why it matters**: Directly tackles inference cost, a critical bottleneck as reasoning models generate increasingly long chain-of-thought traces.
