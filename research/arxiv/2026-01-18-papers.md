# arXiv AI Research - 2026-01-18

## Highlights (Top 5 Must-Read)

### 1. Structured Reasoning for Large Language Models
**Authors**: Research team
**arXiv**: [2601.07180](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.07180)
**TL;DR**: Proposes Structured Reasoning (SCR), a framework that decouples reasoning trajectories into explicit, evaluable, and trainable components. Reduces output token length by up to 50% compared to existing reasoning paradigms while maintaining or improving accuracy.
**Why it matters**: Addresses efficiency bottlenecks in chain-of-thought reasoning by eliminating redundant verification steps that occur even after reaching correct answers.

### 2. PROMISE: Process Reward Models Unlock Test-Time Scaling Laws
**Authors**: Research team
**arXiv**: [2601.04674](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.04674)
**TL;DR**: Demonstrates that test-time scaling laws apply to recommender systems - by increasing inference compute through process reward models, smaller models can match or surpass larger models in generative recommendations.
**Why it matters**: Extends test-time compute scaling beyond traditional reasoning tasks, opening a new paradigm for efficient deployment in production systems.

### 3. Batch-of-Thought: Cross-Instance Learning for Enhanced LLM Reasoning
**Authors**: Research team
**arXiv**: [2601.02950](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.02950)
**TL;DR**: Training-free method that processes related queries jointly to enable cross-instance learning. BoT-R consistently improves accuracy and confidence calibration while reducing inference costs by up to 61%.
**Why it matters**: Current LLM systems process queries independently, discarding valuable cross-instance signals. This approach shows significant efficiency gains with no retraining required.

### 4. Interpretable Safety Alignment via SAE-Constructed Low-Rank Subspace Adaptation (SAILS)
**Authors**: Research team
**arXiv**: [2512.23260](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.23260)
**TL;DR**: Uses Sparse Autoencoders to construct an interpretable safety subspace for LoRA initialization. Achieves up to 99.6% safety rate on Gemma-2-9B (exceeding full fine-tuning by 7.4 points) while updating only 0.19% of parameters.
**Why it matters**: Combines interpretability with state-of-the-art safety alignment, providing both effectiveness and built-in mechanistic understanding.

### 5. MedVL-SAM2: Unified 3D Medical Vision-Language Model
**Authors**: Research team
**arXiv**: [2601.09879](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.09879)
**TL;DR**: Unified model concurrently supporting report generation, VQA, and multi-paradigm segmentation for 3D medical imaging with multimodal reasoning and prompt-driven capabilities.
**Why it matters**: First comprehensive 3D medical VLM that unifies multiple critical clinical tasks in a single framework.
