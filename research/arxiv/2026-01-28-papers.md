# arXiv AI Research - 2026-01-28

## Highlights (Top 5 Must-Read)

### 1. Group Distributionally Robust Optimization-Driven Reinforcement Learning for LLM Reasoning
**Authors**: Multiple authors
**arXiv**: [2601.19280](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.19280)
**TL;DR**: Proposes Multi-Adversary Group Distributionally Robust Optimization (GDRO), moving beyond standard GRPO's static uniform prompt sampling and fixed rollouts. Dynamically adapts training distribution to handle heterogeneous, heavy-tailed reasoning data more efficiently.
**Why it matters**: Addresses a fundamental structural inefficiency in current RL-based reasoning training, with potential to significantly improve reasoning quality across diverse problem difficulties.

### 2. MetaGen: Self-Evolving Roles and Topologies for Multi-Agent LLM Reasoning
**Authors**: Multiple authors
**arXiv**: [2601.19290](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.19290)
**TL;DR**: Training-free framework that dynamically adapts both the role space and collaboration topology of multi-agent LLM systems at inference time, generating query-conditioned role specifications and constrained execution graphs instead of relying on fixed role libraries.
**Why it matters**: First framework to jointly evolve agent roles and interaction topology without training, reducing inference cost while improving multi-agent reasoning accuracy.

### 3. AgentDoG: A Diagnostic Guardrail Framework for AI Agent Safety and Security
**Authors**: Dongrui Liu and 42 co-authors
**arXiv**: [2601.18491](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.18491)
**TL;DR**: Introduces a unified three-dimensional taxonomy categorizing agentic risks by source, failure mode, and consequence, alongside ATBench (a fine-grained agentic safety benchmark) and AgentDoG guardrail models (4B/7B/8B). Achieves SOTA safety moderation performance, outperforming LlamaGuard and ShieldAgent.
**Why it matters**: First comprehensive diagnostic guardrail framework for AI agents that provides root-cause analysis beyond binary safe/unsafe labels, with open-sourced models and datasets.

### 4. Save the Good Prefix: Precise Error Penalization via Process-Supervised RL to Enhance LLM Reasoning
**Authors**: Multiple authors
**arXiv**: [2601.18984](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.18984)
**TL;DR**: Proposes Verifiable Prefix Policy Optimization (VPPO), which uses process reward models only to localize the first error step during RL training, bridging the gap between sparse outcome rewards and noisy step-level process supervision.
**Why it matters**: Elegantly combines the reliability of outcome-based RL with the granularity of process supervision, offering a practical middle ground for training reasoning models.

### 5. RPO: Reinforcement Fine-Tuning with Partial Reasoning Optimization
**Authors**: Multiple authors
**arXiv**: [cs.AI new listings](https://arxiv.org/list/cs.AI/new)
**TL;DR**: Plug-and-play algorithm that trains LLMs by generating only suffixes of reasoning paths using an experience cache, reducing token generation during rollouts by ~95% while maintaining reasoning quality.
**Why it matters**: Dramatically cuts the computational cost of RL-based reasoning fine-tuning, making advanced reasoning training accessible at much lower cost.
