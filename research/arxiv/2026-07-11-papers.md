## Highlights (Top 5 Must-Read)

### 1. OpenCoF: Learning to Reason Through Video Generation
**Authors**: Xinyan Chen, Ziyu Guo, Renrui Zhang, et al.
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08763v1
**TL;DR**: Introduces a "Chain-of-Frame" reasoning dataset and the Wan-CoF model, which interleaves visual and textual tokens so a video generator can reason step-by-step in a temporally grounded way rather than just render final frames.
**Why it matters**: Treats video generation itself as a reasoning substrate, a genuinely new angle on multimodal chain-of-thought.

### 2. Super Weights in LLMs and the Failure of Selective Training
**Authors**: Shreyas Subramanian, Adewale Akinfaderin, Akarsha Sehwag
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08733v1
**TL;DR**: Shows that individually critical "super weight" parameters in LLMs — ones whose removal catastrophically hurts performance — do NOT improve when trained in isolation, undercutting the assumption that importance implies trainability.
**Why it matters**: A direct challenge to selective/targeted fine-tuning strategies that assume importance and trainability are the same thing.

### 3. When the Judge Changes, So Does the Measurement: Auditing LLM-as-Judge Reliability
**Authors**: Zongyou Yang, Yinghan Hou, Xiaokun Yang
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08535v1
**TL;DR**: Finds that swapping the judge model in an LLM-as-judge eval pipeline introduces real measurement ambiguity — only "adjacent" judge upgrades (similar capability tier) preserve score reliability, while persistent biases survive judge changes entirely.
**Why it matters**: Nearly every eval pipeline (including this repo's headline dedup judge) leans on LLM-as-judge; this quantifies how fragile that comparison point is.

### 4. UniClawBench: A Universal Benchmark for Proactive Agents on Real-World Tasks
**Authors**: Zhekai Chen, Chengqi Duan, Kaiyue Sun, et al.
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08768v1
**TL;DR**: A capability-driven benchmark of 400 bilingual real-world tasks, evaluated live inside Docker containers with multi-turn feedback, targeting proactive (not just reactive) agent behavior.
**Why it matters**: Live-container, multi-turn evaluation is a meaningfully harder and more realistic bar than static agent benchmarks.

### 5. The Illusion of Equivalency: Statistical Characterization of Quantization Effects
**Authors**: Baha Rababah, Cuneyt Gurcan Akcora, Carson K. Leung
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08734v1
**TL;DR**: Across 8-bit to 2-bit quantization, aggregate benchmark scores stay flat even as underlying model behavior diverges substantially — quantized models look "equivalent" on paper but aren't.
**Why it matters**: Directly relevant to anyone shipping quantized LLMs on the assumption that preserved benchmark numbers mean preserved behavior.
