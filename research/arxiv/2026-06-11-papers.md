# arXiv AI Research - 2026-06-11

## Highlights (Top 5 Must-Read)

### 1. On Subquadratic Architectures: From Applications to Principles
**Authors**: Anamaria-Roberta Hartl, Levente Zólyomi, David Stap, Pieter-Jan Hoedt, et al.
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.12364
**TL;DR**: A head-to-head study of the three leading subquadratic sequence models — xLSTM, Mamba-2, and Gated DeltaNet — across code-model pretraining, LLM distillation, and time-series foundation models. xLSTM wins consistently; a unified formulation traces its edge to more flexible, stable memory correction via its gating scheme and robust state tracking.
**Why it matters**: Moves the post-Transformer architecture debate from benchmark horse-races toward first principles about which memory dynamics actually generalize.

### 2. The Impossibility of Eliciting Latent Knowledge
**Authors**: Korbinian Friedl, Francis Rhys Ward, Paul Yushin Rapoport, Tom Everitt
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.12318
**TL;DR**: Formalizes the Eliciting Latent Knowledge (ELK) problem with Causal Influence Diagrams, giving precise definitions of honesty and goal misgeneralization. Proves an impossibility theorem: no behavior-only, feedback-based training strategy can guarantee an honest agent — even with perfect training feedback — because the agent can always generalize to "what humans would call true" rather than its actual beliefs.
**Why it matters**: A foundational negative result for honesty/alignment, sharpening exactly where behavioral training fails to guarantee truthful AI.

### 3. Breaking Entropy Bounds: Accelerating RL Training via MTP with Rejection Sampling (Bebop)
**Authors**: Yucheng Li, Huiqiang Jiang, Yang Xu, Jianxin Yang, et al.
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.12370
**TL;DR**: Shows that Multi-Token Prediction acceptance rates in RL post-training are fundamentally bounded by model-entropy fluctuation, then fixes it with probabilistic rejection sampling and a novel end-to-end TV loss that directly optimizes multi-step acceptance. Reaches up to 95% acceptance and ~25% extra throughput, for up to 1.8× end-to-end speedup in async RL training of Qwen3.5–3.7.
**Why it matters**: Rollout is the dominant bottleneck in RL training pipelines; this is a concrete, large-scale recipe to nearly double RL throughput.

### 4. InternVideo3: Agentify Foundation Models with Multimodal Contextual Reasoning
**Authors**: Ziang Yan, Sheng Xia, Jiashuo Yu, Yue Wu, et al.
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.12317
**TL;DR**: Turns a video foundation model into a long-horizon multimodal agent via Multimodal Contextual Reasoning (MCR), a closed-loop process over an evolving context of observations, tools, reasoning, and memory. Introduces Multimodal Multi-head Latent Attention (M²LA) to compress KV-cache while preserving the full token stream; strong results on Video-MME, MLVU, and EgoSchema.
**Why it matters**: One of the first open efforts to extend agentic multi-step reasoning + tool use into genuinely long, visually grounded video tasks.

### 5. Verifiable Environments Are LEGO Bricks: Recursive Composition for Reasoning Generalization (RACES)
**Authors**: Hao Xiang, Qiaoyu Tang, Le Yu, Yaojie Lu, et al.
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.12373
**TL;DR**: Treats verifiable RL environments as composable building blocks — when one environment's output type matches another's input type, they auto-fuse into a new verifiable environment. With SEQUENTIAL/PARALLEL/SORT/SELECT operators, RACES matches the gains of 300 hand-built environments using only 50 base ones, improving DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-14B and Qwen3-14B on six unseen benchmarks.
**Why it matters**: Breaks the linear-scaling limit of hand-built RL environments, offering a path to scalable reasoning generalization.
