# arXiv AI Research - 2026-01-19

## Highlights (Top 5 Must-Read)

### 1. PaCoRe: Learning to Scale Test-Time Compute with Parallel Coordinated Reasoning
**Authors**: Research team
**arXiv**: [2601.05593](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.05593)
**TL;DR**: Introduces Parallel Coordinated Reasoning (PaCoRe), a framework that decouples test-time compute scaling from context limits by shifting from sequential depth to coordinated breadth. Iteratively coordinates parallel reasoning trajectories and compresses insights into compact messages.
**Why it matters**: Addresses fundamental limitations of sequential test-time scaling by enabling parallel processing that overcomes context window constraints while maintaining reasoning quality.

### 2. Thinking Long, but Short: Stable Sequential Test-Time Scaling for Large Reasoning Models
**Authors**: Research team
**arXiv**: [2601.09855](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.09855)
**TL;DR**: Presents Min-Seek, a novel sequential test-time scaling method that stabilizes accuracy across wide ranges of induced thoughts, solving the accuracy degradation problem that occurs when reasoning chains extend too long.
**Why it matters**: Solves critical instability issues in test-time compute scaling, making extended reasoning chains practical for production deployment without fine-tuning.

### 3. FLOP-Efficient Training: Early Stopping Based on Test-Time Compute Awareness
**Authors**: Research team
**arXiv**: [2601.01332](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.01332)
**TL;DR**: Shows that intermediate checkpoints with test-time compute can match fully trained model accuracy while requiring substantially fewer training FLOPs. Introduces TTC-aware training with early stopping algorithms to minimize training compute.
**Why it matters**: Fundamentally changes the training efficiency paradigm by recognizing that test-time compute can compensate for less training, enabling significant computational savings.

### 4. Benchmark²: Systematic Evaluation of LLM Benchmarks
**Authors**: Qi Qian, Chengsong Huang, et al.
**arXiv**: [2601.03986](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.03986)
**TL;DR**: Comprehensive framework evaluating LLM benchmarks themselves through Cross-Benchmark Ranking Consistency, Discriminability Score, and Capability Alignment Deviation. Tested across 15 benchmarks, 11 LLMs, and 4 model families.
**Why it matters**: Addresses the proliferation of conflicting benchmarks by providing meta-evaluation tools to assess which benchmarks are most reliable and discriminative.

### 5. The Molecular Structure of Thought: Mapping the Topology of Long Chain-of-Thought Reasoning
**Authors**: Research team
**arXiv**: [2601.06002](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.06002)
**TL;DR**: Proposes that effective Long CoT trajectories feature stable molecular-like structures formed by three interaction types: Deep-Reasoning (covalent-like), Self-Reflection (hydrogen-bond-like), and Self-Exploration (van der Waals-like).
**Why it matters**: Provides theoretical framework for understanding what makes reasoning chains effective, with implications for designing better reasoning algorithms.
