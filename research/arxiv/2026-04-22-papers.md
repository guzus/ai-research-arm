# arXiv AI Research - 2026-04-22

## Highlights (Top 5 Must-Read)

### 1. LLM Reasoning Is Latent, Not the Chain of Thought
**Authors**: Various (position paper)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.15726
**TL;DR**: Position paper arguing that LLM reasoning should be studied as latent-state trajectory formation rather than as faithful surface chain-of-thought. Claims that visible CoT tokens are a projection of internal computation, not the computation itself, with implications for interpretability, evaluation, and safety work that currently treats CoT as the ground truth of model reasoning.
**Why it matters**: Directly challenges methodological assumptions underlying CoT faithfulness research and the current generation of reasoning benchmarks.

### 2. MoBiE: Efficient Inference of Mixture of Binary Experts under Post-Training Quantization
**Authors**: Various
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.06798
**TL;DR**: The first binarization framework tailored specifically for MoE-based LLMs, addressing the high memory and compute cost of sparse expert models. On Qwen3-30B-A3B, MoBiE achieves over 2× inference speedup while reducing perplexity by 52.2% compared to prior PTQ baselines.
**Why it matters**: Makes extreme-compression deployment of large MoEs newly viable, opening the door to running 30B+ expert models on far cheaper hardware.

### 3. EXAONE 4.5 Technical Report — LG's First Open-Weight Vision-Language Model
**Authors**: LG AI Research
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.08644
**TL;DR**: Integrates a 1.2B parameter vision encoder into the EXAONE 4.0 32B base model, using hybrid attention and Grouped Query Attention (GQA) within both the LM and the vision tower. Targeted at industrial intelligence use cases with open weights.
**Why it matters**: Another major non-US open-weights VLM entering the Qwen-VL / InternVL competitive tier, with a GQA-first vision encoder design that is unusual among peers.

### 4. Universal YOCO for Efficient Depth Scaling
**Authors**: Various
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.01220
**TL;DR**: Combines the YOCO (You Only Cache Once) decoder-decoder architecture with recursive depth computation to scale inference-time compute without the KV-cache blowup of naive looping. Produces a synergistic effect where added depth does not linearly increase cache footprint.
**Why it matters**: Test-time compute scaling has been bottlenecked by KV growth; YOCO-U is a plausible architectural answer to that bottleneck.

### 5. QuickScope: Certifying Hard Questions in Dynamic LLM Benchmarks
**Authors**: Various
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.17842
**TL;DR**: Proposes a certification procedure for identifying genuinely hard questions within dynamic, continuously-refreshed LLM benchmarks, with statistical guarantees on difficulty that are robust to contamination and sampling drift.
**Why it matters**: Evaluation integrity is a growing problem; a principled "hardness certificate" is a practical tool for benchmark maintainers.
