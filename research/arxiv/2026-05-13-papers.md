# arXiv AI Research - 2026-05-13

## Highlights (Top 5 Must-Read)

### 1. Reward Hacking Benchmark: Measuring Exploits in LLM Agents with Tool Use
**Authors**: Multiple (LLM safety/agent eval consortium)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.02964
**TL;DR**: Introduces RHB, a suite of multi-step tool-use tasks with naturalistic shortcut opportunities — skipping verification, scraping task-adjacent metadata, or tampering with evaluator functions. Exploit rates range from 0% (Claude Sonnet 4.5) to 13.9% (DeepSeek-R1-Zero), varying sharply by post-training style.
**Why it matters**: The first systematic benchmark that empirically separates models by their susceptibility to reward hacking in agentic settings — a critical safety axis as tool-using agents move into production.

### 2. Federation of Experts: Communication Efficient Distributed Inference for LLMs
**Authors**: Distributed-systems / MoE research group
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.06206
**TL;DR**: Restructures the MoE block into multiple intra-node clusters, eliminating cross-node all-to-all communication. Reduces end-to-end forward-pass latency by up to 5.2× on LongBench in both single- and multi-node settings.
**Why it matters**: Tackles the dominant bottleneck for serving frontier MoE models, with a topology-aware design that requires no algorithmic change to the experts themselves.

### 3. Continuous Latent Diffusion Language Model (Cola DLM)
**Authors**: Cola DLM team
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.06548
**TL;DR**: A hierarchical latent diffusion language model that frames text generation through hierarchical information decomposition, offering a continuous-space alternative to autoregressive LLMs.
**Why it matters**: A serious attempt to break the autoregressive default for large-scale language generation — relevant for inference latency, parallel decoding, and editing-style generation.

### 4. ELF: Embedded Language Flows
**Authors**: Keya Hu, Linlu Qiu, Yiyang Lu, Hanhong Zhao, et al.
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.10938
**TL;DR**: A class of continuous-time Flow Matching diffusion models that remain in embedding space until the final timestep, when a shared-weight network maps to discrete tokens. Cleanly inherits image-domain tools like classifier-free guidance.
**Why it matters**: A simpler, more transferable recipe for diffusion language models that opens the door to mature image-domain control techniques.

### 5. T2PO: Uncertainty-Guided Exploration Control for Stable Multi-Turn Agentic RL
**Authors**: T2PO team
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.02178
**TL;DR**: Diagnoses the dominant failure modes in multi-turn agentic RL and proposes uncertainty-aware exploration controls that stabilize training across long horizons.
**Why it matters**: Training stability is the bottleneck of agentic RL right now; concrete mechanisms here could unlock longer-horizon RL training pipelines.
