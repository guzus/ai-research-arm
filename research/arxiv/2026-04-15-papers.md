# arXiv AI Research - 2026-04-15

## Highlights (Top 5 Must-Read)

### 1. JoyAI-LLM Flash: Advancing Mid-Scale LLMs with Token Efficiency
**Authors**: JoyAI Research Team
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.03044
**TL;DR**: A sparse Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) LLM in the sub-50B regime that activates 8 of 256 experts per token through a learned MLP router atop a pure attention-based backbone. Substantially advances the throughput/quality Pareto frontier at inference time.
**Why it matters**: Demonstrates a practical recipe for mid-scale MoE models where activation sparsity — not parameter count — is the key lever for serving economics.

### 2. Is More Data Worth the Cost? Dataset Scaling Laws in a Tiny Attention-Only Decoder
**Authors**: (see arXiv listing)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.09389
**TL;DR**: Controlled scaling study showing that ~30% of training data recovers ~90% of full-data validation accuracy, with steep diminishing returns beyond that point.
**Why it matters**: Provides quantitative guidance for compute- and data-limited pretraining regimes and challenges the "more is always better" assumption for corpus sizing.

### 3. To Memorize or to Retrieve: Scaling Laws for RAG-Considerate Pretraining
**Authors**: (see arXiv listing)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.00715
**TL;DR**: Jointly sweeps pretraining corpus size and retrieval store size across model scales, producing the first systematic scaling law that maps when parametric memory should give way to retrieval.
**Why it matters**: Reframes pretraining decisions for the RAG era — if retrieval is in the loop, optimal pretraining mixtures look meaningfully different.

### 4. From Reasoning to Agentic: Credit Assignment in Reinforcement Learning for Large Language Models
**Authors**: (see arXiv listing)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.09459
**TL;DR**: Unified analysis of the credit-assignment problem across reasoning RL (token/step-level within one CoT) and agentic RL (multi-turn environment interaction). Identifies "template collapse" failure mode where policies latch onto fixed, input-agnostic scaffolds.
**Why it matters**: Clarifies why many RL-trained reasoners plateau and offers a diagnostic lens for teams scaling RLHF/RLAIF to agent settings.

### 5. InsEdit: Instruction-based Visual Editing via Data-Efficient Video Diffusion Adaptation
**Authors**: (see arXiv listing)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.08646
**TL;DR**: Adapts HunyuanVideo-1.5 for instruction-driven video editing using Mutual Context Attention (MCA), producing aligned video pairs where edits can begin mid-clip rather than only on the first frame.
**Why it matters**: Makes instruction-conditioned video editing practical without the massive paired datasets typically required for video diffusion fine-tuning.
