# arXiv AI Research - 2026-06-10

## Highlights (Top 5 Must-Read)

### 1. You Only Index Once: Cross-Layer Sparse Attention with Shared Routing
**Authors**: (arXiv 2606.06467)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.06467
**TL;DR**: Shares both the KV cache *and* the sparse-routing index across decoder layers — a single indexer computes token-level top-k selection once and reuses it through every layer, instead of re-routing per layer. Reports up to 7.6× decoding speedup and 17.1× overall throughput at 128K context.
**Why it matters**: Routing is usually the hidden tax of sparse-attention inference; amortizing it across depth is a concrete architectural lever for long-context serving cost.

### 2. Latent Reasoning with Normalizing Flows
**Authors**: (arXiv 2606.06447)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.06447
**TL;DR**: Proposes NF-CoT, a latent reasoning framework that models "continuous thoughts" with normalizing flows rather than discrete chain-of-thought tokens, preserving the expressiveness of explicit reasoning while keeping the trajectory in a continuous, tractable-density latent space.
**Why it matters**: Latent/continuous reasoning is one of the most active bets for getting reasoning quality without paying the token-by-token decode cost of long CoT.

### 3. From Symbolic to Geometric: Enabling Spatial Reasoning in Large Language Models
**Authors**: (arXiv 2606.04381)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.04381
**TL;DR**: Introduces a Spatial Language Model (SLM) — described as the first multimodal LLM that treats location information as a first-class modality and performs geometric (not just symbolic/textual) spatial reasoning inside the inference process.
**Why it matters**: Spatial reasoning remains a stubborn failure mode for MLLMs; promoting geometry to a native modality is a different attack than prompting tricks.

### 4. CHASE: Adversarial Red-Blue Teaming for Improving LLM Safety using Reinforcement Learning
**Authors**: (arXiv 2606.05523)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.05523
**TL;DR**: A closed-loop red-blue teaming framework in which a black-box attacker and a safety-aligned defender co-evolve under RL, continuously surfacing fresh jailbreaks while hardening the defender against them in the same loop.
**Why it matters**: Static red-team sets go stale fast; co-evolving attacker and defender is a more durable recipe for safety hardening.

### 5. Synthesize and Reward: Reinforcement Learning for Multi-Step Tool Use in Live Environments
**Authors**: (arXiv 2606.03892)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.03892
**TL;DR**: Presents PROVE, a framework for training LLMs to orchestrate multi-step tool calls using live-execution RL with stateful environments and programmatic (verifiable) rewards, rather than offline/simulated tool traces.
**Why it matters**: Real agentic tool use needs credit assignment across stateful, executable steps — synthetic single-turn supervision doesn't transfer.
