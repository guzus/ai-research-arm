# arXiv AI Research - 2026-04-23

## Highlights (Top 5 Must-Read)

### 1. KnowRL: Boosting LLM Reasoning via Reinforcement Learning with Minimal-Sufficient Knowledge Guidance
**Authors**: (arXiv 2604.12627)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.12627
**TL;DR**: Reframes RL hint design as a minimal-sufficient guidance problem, decomposing guidance into atomic knowledge points and using Constrained Subset Search to build compact training subsets. Reaches 70.08 average accuracy without hints at inference, surpassing Nemotron-1.5B by +9.63 points.
**Why it matters**: Shows that careful hint curation, not just scale, is the lever for unlocking reasoning gains from RLVR on small models.

### 2. SUPERNOVA: Eliciting General Reasoning in LLMs with Reinforcement Learning on Natural Instructions
**Authors**: (arXiv 2604.08477)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.08477
**TL;DR**: A data curation framework for RLVR that moves beyond math and code into causal inference and temporal understanding. Argues that existing RLVR pipelines over-specialize models to formal domains at the cost of general reasoning.
**Why it matters**: A step toward RL training recipes that transfer to everyday reasoning instead of only competition-grade benchmarks.

### 3. COIN: Chain Of Interaction Benchmark - When Reasoning Meets Embodied Interaction
**Authors**: (arXiv 2604.16886)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.16886
**TL;DR**: 50 interactive daily-life robotic manipulation tasks (COIN-50) plus COIN-Primitive and COIN-Composition splits for skill learning and causal-dependency generalization. Designed to stress-test agents on interactive reasoning, not just passive QA.
**Why it matters**: Establishes a grounded evaluation for the interactive-reasoning frontier that current benchmarks miss.

### 4. LASA: Language-Agnostic Semantic Alignment at the Semantic Bottleneck for LLM Safety
**Authors**: (arXiv 2604.12710)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.12710
**TL;DR**: Aligns safety representations at the semantic bottleneck so refusal behavior transfers across languages. Drops average attack success rate on LLaMA-3.1-8B-Instruct from 24.7% to 2.8%, closing the low-resource language safety gap.
**Why it matters**: Addresses a well-documented cross-lingual jailbreak vector with a targeted architectural fix.

### 5. MPDiT: Multi-Patch Global-to-Local Transformer Architecture for Efficient Flow Matching and Diffusion
**Authors**: (arXiv 2603.26357)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.26357
**TL;DR**: Breaks the isotropic DiT assumption by using larger patches in early blocks for global context and smaller patches later for local detail, cutting GFLOPs by up to 50% with comparable ImageNet generation quality.
**Why it matters**: Concrete architectural win against the standard DiT recipe - relevant for anyone training image or video generators.
