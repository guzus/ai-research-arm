## Highlights (Top 5 Must-Read)

### 1. How Transparent is DiffusionGemma?
**Authors**: Joshua Engels, Callum McDougall, Bilal Chughtai, Janos Kramar, Senthoran Rajamanoharan, Cindy Wu, et al.
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.20560
**TL;DR**: Interpretability study of a *diffusion* language model (DiffusionGemma), which performs a large fraction of its computation in continuous latent space rather than discrete autoregressive tokens. The authors ask whether moving reasoning into latent space makes chain-of-thought less faithful/legible, and probe how much of the model's "reasoning" is actually observable.
**Why it matters**: Diffusion LLMs are emerging as a real alternative to autoregressive decoding, and this is among the first hard looks at whether they retain the reasoning transparency that safety and oversight tooling depend on.

### 2. The Token Is a Group Element: On Lie-Algebra Attention over Matrix Lie Groups
**Authors**: Przemyslaw Musialski
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.20547
**TL;DR**: Proposes an attention construction in which each token *is* an element of a matrix Lie group — a bare transformation with no feature payload — claimed to be the first attention mechanism operating directly on group elements via their Lie algebra. Attention scores and aggregation are defined geometrically over the group.
**Why it matters**: A genuinely new architectural primitive for attention that bakes in continuous-symmetry structure, relevant to geometry, vision, robotics, and equivariant modeling.

### 3. UltraQuant: 4-bit KV Caching for Context-Heavy Agents
**Authors**: Inesh Chakrabarti, David Limpus, Aditi Ghai Rana, Bowen Bao, Spandan Tiwari, Thiago Crepaldi
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.20474
**TL;DR**: Targets the agentic serving regime where long prefixes are reused across many short turns and concurrency dominates GPU utilization. Combines TurboQuant-style rotation with codebook quantization to compress the KV cache to 4 bits without the accuracy collapse typical of aggressive KV quantization.
**Why it matters**: KV-cache memory is the binding constraint for long-context, high-concurrency agent deployments; 4-bit KV that holds quality directly expands what agentic systems can serve.

### 4. S-Agent: Spatial Tool-Use Elicits Reasoning for Spatial Intelligence
**Authors**: Yalun Dai, Hao Li, Shulin Tian, Runmao Yao, Yuhao Dong, Fangzhou Hong, et al.
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.20515
**TL;DR**: Argues current VLMs/tool-augmented agents are stuck on static, stateless inference from isolated images, and introduces a spatial tool-use agent that reasons over a continuous, evolving 3D world state. Tool calls let the model actively probe and update its spatial understanding rather than answer one-shot.
**Why it matters**: Bridges agentic tool-use and 3D spatial reasoning — a step toward embodied/robotics agents that maintain and act on a persistent world model.

### 5. What Do Safety-Aligned LLMs Learn From Mixed Compliance Demonstrations?
**Authors**: Sihui Dai, Mann Patel
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.20508
**TL;DR**: In-context demonstrations are known to jailbreak LLMs; this work systematically mixes benign-compliance and harmful-compliance demonstrations to disentangle what models actually internalize about "compliance" vs. "harmfulness." It characterizes how the ratio and composition of demos shift refusal behavior.
**Why it matters**: Clarifies the mechanism behind many-shot / in-context jailbreaks, informing how alignment training should treat compliance signals.
