# arXiv AI Research - 2026-06-03

## Highlights (Top 5 Must-Read)

### 1. Agentic Transformers Provably Learn to Search via Reinforcement Learning
**Authors**: et al.
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.00183
**TL;DR**: Treats tree search as the central abstraction for language-agent reasoning and gives a theoretical account of how transformer-based policies acquire search behavior from RL training dynamics. Shows that agentic transformers provably learn to explore, backtrack, and prune rather than merely pattern-match trajectories.
**Why it matters**: Provides rare theoretical grounding for why RL-trained reasoning agents generalize, bridging the gap between empirical agent results and learning theory.

### 2. Deep Research as Rubric for Reinforcement Learning
**Authors**: et al.
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.01091
**TL;DR**: Targets open-ended reasoning and long-form generation where there is no reliable automatic verification signal for reward-based optimization. Proposes using deep-research-style rubrics as the reward, replacing hand-crafted or prompt-generated reward functions.
**Why it matters**: Rubric-as-reward is emerging as the dominant recipe for RL on unverifiable tasks; this formalizes and scales it.

### 3. RoboDream: Compositional World Models for Scalable Robot Data Synthesis
**Authors**: et al.
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.02577
**TL;DR**: An embodiment-centric world model that synthesizes photorealistic robot demonstrations with novel objects, scenes, and viewpoints, avoiding the embodiment hallucinations and physically infeasible motions of generic video-diffusion augmentation. Generates training data at scale for manipulation policies.
**Why it matters**: Compositional world models are becoming the practical answer to the robotics data bottleneck.

### 4. SafeSteer: Localized On-Policy Distillation for Efficient Safety Alignment
**Authors**: et al.
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.02530
**TL;DR**: Argues safety features are sparse in the output distribution, so alignment should apply localized modifications confined to safety-relevant tokens rather than global trade-offs. Performs on-policy distillation restricted to those tokens, preserving general capability.
**Why it matters**: Reframes the alignment tax as a localization problem — cheaper safety with minimal capability loss.

### 5. ProbeScale: Probing Analysis to Optimize Neural Scaling Laws for Efficient Small Language Model Inference
**Authors**: et al.
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.01806
**TL;DR**: Uses probing analysis of internal representations to refine neural scaling laws for small language models, showing their internal representations carry rich structure that scales predictably with size. Uses this to optimize inference-time efficiency.
**Why it matters**: Connects mechanistic probing to scaling-law prediction, a route to cheaper SLM deployment.
