# arXiv AI Research - 2026-03-07

## Highlights (Top 5 Must-Read)

### 1. [Entropic-Time Inference: Self-Organizing Large Language Model Decoding Beyond Attention](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.03310)
**Authors**: (see paper)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.03310
**TL;DR**: Proposes a fundamentally new decoding paradigm where inference is governed by the flow of uncertainty rather than token index. Introduces a self-organizing architecture that jointly couples scheduling, attention sparsification, and sampling temperature under a unified entropy control objective.
**Why it matters**: A radical rethinking of how LLM inference works, moving beyond standard autoregressive decoding to entropy-driven generation.

### 2. [Helios: Real Real-Time Long Video Generation Model](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.04379)
**Authors**: (see paper)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.04379
**TL;DR**: The first 14B video generation model running at 19.5 FPS on a single NVIDIA H100 GPU, supporting minute-scale generation while matching the quality of strong baselines. Demonstrates that real-time, high-quality long video generation is achievable at scale.
**Why it matters**: Breaks the real-time barrier for large-scale video generation models, a major milestone for practical deployment.

### 3. [PonderLM-3: Adaptive Token-Wise Pondering with Differentiable Masking](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.02023)
**Authors**: (see paper)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.02023
**TL;DR**: Addresses where additional computation should be allocated during inference-time scaling by using differentiable attention masks during pretraining paired with hard pruning rules at inference. Provides an end-to-end differentiable framework for token-wise adaptive computation.
**Why it matters**: Directly tackles the efficiency of test-time compute scaling, enabling models to "think harder" only where needed.

### 4. [Half the Nonlinearity Is Wasted: Measuring and Reallocating the Transformer's MLP Budget](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.03459)
**Authors**: (see paper)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.03459
**TL;DR**: Shows that despite weak per-instance predictability, gating exploits a heavily skewed distribution where most MLP computations are near-linear. Achieves 25-56% linear routing at less than 1% perplexity cost in GPT-2, revealing massive efficiency gains are possible.
**Why it matters**: Reveals fundamental inefficiency in transformer architectures that can be exploited for significant speedups with minimal quality loss.

### 5. [MUSE: A Run-Centric Platform for Multimodal Unified Safety Evaluation of Large Language Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.02482)
**Authors**: (see paper)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.02482
**TL;DR**: An open-source platform integrating automatic cross-modal payload generation, three multi-turn attack algorithms (Crescendo, PAIR, Violent Durian), and an LLM judge with a five-level safety taxonomy. Experiments across six multimodal LLMs show multi-turn strategies achieve up to 90-100% ASR against models with near-perfect single-turn refusal.
**Why it matters**: Demonstrates that current multimodal safety alignment is brittle under multi-turn, cross-modal attacks -- a critical finding for the field.
