# arXiv AI Research - 2026-02-08

## Highlights (Top 5 Must-Read)

### 1. OmniMoE: An Efficient MoE by Orchestrating Atomic Experts at Scale
**Authors**: Jingze Shi, Zhangyang Peng, Yizhang Zhu et al.
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.05711
**TL;DR**: Proposes a novel Mixture-of-Experts architecture that decomposes experts into atomic units and orchestrates them dynamically, achieving better scaling efficiency than standard MoE approaches while reducing parameter redundancy across expert modules.
**Why it matters**: Addresses a core bottleneck in scaling MoE models — expert redundancy — with a practical architectural solution that could influence next-generation large model designs.

### 2. Reinforcement World Model Learning for LLM-based Agents
**Authors**: Xiao Yu, Baolin Peng, Ruize Xu et al.
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.05842
**TL;DR**: Introduces a framework that trains LLM-based agents using reinforcement learning over learned world models, enabling agents to plan and act in complex environments without requiring extensive environment interaction during training.
**Why it matters**: Bridges world models and LLM agents — a key step toward sample-efficient autonomous agents that can reason about consequences before acting.

### 3. EuroLLM-22B: Technical Report
**Authors**: Miguel Moura Ramos, Duarte M. Alves, Hippolyte Gisserot-Boukhlef et al.
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.05879
**TL;DR**: Technical report for EuroLLM-22B, a multilingual European LLM covering all EU official languages. Details architecture choices, training data composition, and benchmark evaluations showing competitive performance with models of similar scale.
**Why it matters**: A significant milestone for European AI sovereignty — an open-weight multilingual model developed with EU language coverage as a first-class design goal.

### 4. f-GRPO and Beyond: Divergence-Based Reinforcement Learning Algorithms for General LLM Alignment
**Authors**: Rajdeep Haldar, Lantao Mei, Guang Lin et al.
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.05946
**TL;DR**: Generalizes Group Relative Policy Optimization (GRPO) by replacing the KL divergence with arbitrary f-divergences, providing a unified theoretical framework that encompasses GRPO, DPO, and other alignment methods as special cases. Shows that divergence choice significantly impacts alignment quality.
**Why it matters**: GRPO variants are dominating LLM post-training; this paper provides the theoretical foundation to understand why and how to choose between them.

### 5. Multi-Token Prediction via Self-Distillation
**Authors**: John Kirchenbauer, Abhimanyu Hans, Brian Bartoldson et al.
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.06019
**TL;DR**: Presents a self-distillation approach to multi-token prediction that enables LLMs to predict multiple future tokens simultaneously without architectural modifications, improving inference throughput while maintaining generation quality.
**Why it matters**: Multi-token prediction is a hot area for inference speedup; this self-distillation approach is elegantly simple and widely applicable to existing models.
