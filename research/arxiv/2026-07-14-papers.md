# arXiv AI Research - 2026-07-14

## Highlights (Top 5 Must-Read)

### 1. Metacognition in LLMs: Foundations, Progress, and Opportunities
**Authors**: Gabrielle Kaili-May Liu, Areeb Gani, Jacqueline Lu, Jordan Thomas, Mark Steyvers, Arman Cohan
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.11881
**TL;DR**: A comprehensive survey of metacognitive abilities in LLMs — how models estimate and report their own uncertainty and competence — covering measurement methodology, training/prompting techniques for improving self-assessment, and downstream applications for reliability.
**Why it matters**: Calibrated self-knowledge is a prerequisite for safe autonomous agent deployment; this consolidates a fragmented research area into a usable reference.

### 2. Xiaomi-Robotics-U0: Unified Embodied Synthesis with World Foundation Model
**Authors**: Xinghang Li, Jun Guo, Qiwei Li, Long Qian, Hang Lai, et al. (Xiaomi Robotics)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.11643
**TL;DR**: A 38B-parameter multimodal world foundation model that unifies multi-view scene generation with embodied policy transfer, letting a single model both imagine physical rollouts and control a robot from them.
**Why it matters**: Another major consumer-electronics player (Xiaomi) entering the embodied foundation-model race at real scale, alongside the broader trend of world models as the substrate for robot control.

### 3. Inside the Unfair Judge: A Mechanistic Interpretability Account of LLM-as-Judge Bias
**Authors**: Zixiang Xu, Sixian Li, Huaxing Liu, Xiang Wang, Shuai Li, Zirui Song, Xiuying Chen
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.11871
**TL;DR**: Traces LLM-as-judge scoring biases to low-dimensional, predictable activation-geometry subspaces, showing biased inputs can be detected and causally steered away from at the representation level.
**Why it matters**: LLM judges now gate everything from RLHF reward signals to eval leaderboards; a mechanistic account of when they're systematically unfair is directly actionable for anyone building judge pipelines.

### 4. Extending LLM Context via Associative Recurrent Memory
**Authors**: Gleb Kuzmin, Ivan Rodkin, Aydar Bulatov, Yuri Kuratov, Mikhail Burtsev, Artem Shelmanov, et al.
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.11614
**TL;DR**: ARMT-augmented models achieve constant-memory scaling for long-context processing, cutting FLOPs by ~30% on in-context-limit tasks versus standard long-context Transformers.
**Why it matters**: A concrete efficiency win on the long-standing quadratic-attention bottleneck, from the group behind Recurrent Memory Transformer — relevant to anyone trading off context length against inference cost.

### 5. MM-ToolSandBox: A Unified Framework for Evaluating Visual Tool-Calling Agents
**Authors**: Kaixin Ma, Di Feng, Alexander Metz, Jiarui Lu, Eshan Verma, Afshin Dehghan
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.11818
**TL;DR**: A benchmark spanning 500+ tools across 16 domains for multimodal agents that must call tools based on visual input, finding that visual-precision errors — not planning or tool-selection errors — are the dominant bottleneck.
**Why it matters**: Most agent benchmarks stress text reasoning; this isolates perception as the actual limiter for visual agentic tool use, redirecting where labs should invest.
