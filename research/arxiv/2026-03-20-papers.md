# arXiv AI Research - 2026-03-20

## Highlights (Top 5 Must-Read)

### 1. [Attention Residuals](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.15031)
**Authors**: Guangyu Chen et al. (Kimi Team)
**arXiv**: [2603.15031](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.15031)
**TL;DR**: Replaces fixed-weight residual connections in transformers with softmax attention over preceding layer outputs (AttnRes), allowing each layer to selectively aggregate earlier representations with learned, input-dependent weights. Block AttnRes partitions layers into blocks to reduce overhead, making it a practical drop-in replacement for standard residual connections at scale.
**Why it matters**: Challenges a foundational assumption of modern transformer design — that uniform residual accumulation is sufficient — opening a new axis of architectural improvement for depth-wise information routing.

### 2. [CARE: Covariance-Aware and Rank-Enhanced Decomposition for Multi-Head Latent Attention](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.17946)
**Authors**: Zhongzhu Zhou, Fengxiang Bie, Ziyan Chen, Zhenyu Zhang, Yibo Yang, Junxiong Wang, Ben Athiwaratkun, Xiaoxia Wu, Shuaiwen Leon Song
**arXiv**: [2603.17946](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.17946)
**TL;DR**: Proposes a principled pipeline for converting pretrained GQA attention into Multi-Head Latent Attention (MLA) that accounts for activation covariance structure and uses non-uniform rank allocation across layers. Eliminates the activation drift caused by naive SVD-based conversions while preserving KV-cache efficiency.
**Why it matters**: Accepted at ICLR 2026 — provides a rigorous path to retrofit existing large models with DeepSeek-style latent attention for cheaper inference without quality loss.

### 3. [CRAFT: Safety Alignment for Large Reasoning Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.17305)
**Authors**: (Multiple institutions)
**arXiv**: [2603.17305](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.17305)
**TL;DR**: Reveals that even RLHF/DPO-aligned reasoning models exhibit Superficial Safety Alignment (SSA) — unsafe internal reasoning persists despite safe final responses. CRAFT addresses this by aligning the full chain-of-thought, achieving the smallest performance reduction among safety alignment methods with +4.7% accuracy improvement on DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Llama-8B and Qwen3-4B-Thinking.
**Why it matters**: Demonstrates that current safety alignment for reasoning models is skin-deep, and proposes the first method to align the reasoning process itself rather than just outputs.

### 4. [Rethinking Attention Output Projection: Structured Hadamard Transforms](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.08343)
**Authors**: (Multiple institutions)
**arXiv**: [2603.08343](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.08343)
**TL;DR**: Replaces the dense output projection in multi-head attention with a fixed, parameter-free Walsh-Hadamard Transform plus lightweight learnable affine rescaling. Eliminates ~25% of attention parameters per block while achieving comparable or slightly superior downstream performance, with up to 7% parameter reduction, 8.9% memory savings, and 6.6% throughput improvement.
**Why it matters**: A simple, elegant architectural change that delivers free efficiency gains — the kind of insight that gets rapidly adopted across the field.

### 5. [BrainBench: Exposing the Commonsense Reasoning Gap in LLMs](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.14761)
**Authors**: (Multiple institutions)
**arXiv**: [2603.14761](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.14761)
**TL;DR**: A benchmark of 100 brainteaser questions spanning 20 categories targeting specific commonsense reasoning failure modes. Best model (Claude Opus 4.6 with extended thinking) achieves only 80.3%; worst (GPT-4o) scores 39.7%. Even top models show 6–16 point gaps between accuracy and consistency, revealing fundamental brittleness.
**Why it matters**: Demonstrates that frontier LLMs still fail questions any human would answer instantly — a sobering counterpoint to benchmark saturation claims.
