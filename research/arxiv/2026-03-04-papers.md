# arXiv AI Research - 2026-03-04

## Highlights (Top 5 Must-Read)

### 1. PRISM: Pushing the Frontier of Deep Think via Process Reward Model-Guided Inference
**Authors**: Rituraj Sharma, Weiyuan Chen, Noah Provenzano et al.
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.02479
**TL;DR**: Introduces a Process Reward Model (PRM)-guided inference algorithm for deep reasoning systems. Uses step-level verification to guide population refinement and solution aggregation. Achieves 90.0% on AIME25 and 75.4% on HMMT25 with gpt-oss-20b, matching or exceeding gpt-oss-120b while lying on the compute-accuracy Pareto frontier.
**Why it matters**: Demonstrates that inference-time compute scaling with process rewards can close a 6x model size gap, reshaping the cost-performance tradeoff for reasoning tasks.

### 2. Scaling Reward Modeling without Human Supervision
**Authors**: Jingxuan Fan, Yueying Li, Zhenting Qi et al.
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.02225
**TL;DR**: Explores scaling reward models through unsupervised reward-based scaling (RBS) — preference learning over document prefixes and suffixes without human labels. A pilot study toward removing the human bottleneck in RLHF pipelines.
**Why it matters**: If reward modeling can scale without human annotation, it removes the single biggest bottleneck in alignment training and could dramatically accelerate safe model development.

### 3. DREAM: Where Visual Understanding Meets Text-to-Image Generation
**Authors**: Chao Li, Tianhong Li, Sai Vidyaranya Nuthalapati et al.
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.02667
**TL;DR**: Unified framework that jointly optimizes discriminative and generative objectives, learning strong visual representations while performing text-to-image generation within a single model. Bridges the long-standing gap between understanding and generation in vision.
**Why it matters**: Unifying visual understanding and generation in one model is a key step toward truly general vision systems and more efficient multimodal architectures.

### 4. Routing Absorption in Sparse Attention: Why Random Gates Are Hard to Beat
**Authors**: Keston Aquino-Michaels
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.02227
**TL;DR**: Shows that when sparse attention is trained end-to-end, Q/K/V projections co-adapt to whatever mask is imposed, absorbing the routing signal. This makes random gates surprisingly competitive with learned routing strategies in sparse attention.
**Why it matters**: A fundamental finding about attention mechanisms that challenges assumptions behind many efficient attention architectures and could redirect sparse attention research.

### 5. MOSAIC: Learning When to Act or Refuse — Guarding Agentic Reasoning Models for Safe Multi-Step Tool Use
**Authors**: Aradhye Agarwal, Gurdit Siyan, Yash Pandya et al.
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.03205
**TL;DR**: Post-training framework for agentic LLM safety using a "plan, check, then act or refuse" loop with preference-based RL. Reduces harmful behavior by up to 50% and increases refusal on injection attacks by 20%+ across Qwen and Phi models.
**Why it matters**: As LLM agents gain real-world tool access, safety guardrails for multi-step agentic actions become critical — this is the first systematic framework addressing it.
