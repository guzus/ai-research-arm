## Highlights (Top 5 Must-Read)

### 1. LLaVA-OneVision-2: Towards Next-Generation Perceptual Intelligence
**Authors**: Xiang An, Yin Xie, Feilong Tang, Yunyao Yan, Huajie Tan, Didi Zhu et al.
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.25979
**TL;DR**: New flagship in the LLaVA-OneVision line built on a native OneVision-Encoder with windowed attention, introducing **codec-stream tokenization** — compressed video is consumed as a bit-cost stream, with bit-cost dynamics driving adaptive temporal grouping and motion-residual cues selecting salient spatial evidence. A shared 3D RoPE places codec canvases, sampled frames, and stills in one spatiotemporal coordinate system; pretraining uses ~8M re-captioned video samples plus a 4M spatial fine-tuning corpus.
**Why it matters**: On the new JumpScore temporal-localization benchmark the 8B model hits 74.9 mAP vs Qwen3-VL-8B's 30.1 (+44.8 pts), and outperforms Qwen3-VL-8B by +4.3 / +5.3 / +15.6 avg points on video / spatial / tracking tasks — strongest open VLM signal of the cycle.

### 2. Language Models Need Sleep
**Authors**: Sangyun Lee, Sean McLeish, Tom Goldstein, Giulia Fanti
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.26099
**TL;DR**: Proposes a sleep-like consolidation mechanism: the model periodically performs *N* offline recurrent passes over accumulated context and writes the result into **persistent fast weights inside SSM blocks** via a learned local rule, then clears its KV cache. Inference latency is preserved; extra compute is shifted to sleep. Evaluated on cellular automata, multi-hop graph retrieval, and a hard math-reasoning task where transformers and SSM-attention hybrids fail.
**Why it matters**: A clean reformulation of long-context handling as periodic state-space consolidation — increasing sleep duration improves accuracy, with biggest gains on deeper-reasoning examples.

### 3. From Model Scaling to System Scaling: Scaling the Harness in Agentic AI
**Authors**: Shangding Gu
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.26112
**TL;DR**: Argues the next bottleneck for agentic systems is not the foundation model but the **harness** — context governance, trustworthy memory, dynamic skill routing, orchestration, verification, and governance. Treats the structured execution layer around the model as a first-class object for design, evaluation, and optimization, and ships `CheetahClaws`, a Python-native reference harness benchmarked against Claude Code and OpenClaw.
**Why it matters**: Reframes agent evaluation away from one-shot task success toward trajectory quality, memory hygiene, context efficiency, and safe evolution — concrete proposal for harness-level benchmarks.

### 4. Looped Diffusion Language Models (LoopMDM)
**Authors**: Sanghyun Lee, Chunsan Hong, Seungryong Kim, Jonghyun Lee, Jongho Park, Dongmin Park
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.26106
**TL;DR**: Selectively loops the early-middle transformer layers of masked diffusion language models. Looping at training time gives a depth-scaling effect without adding parameters; varying loop count at inference enables flexible compute scaling. Matches same-size MDMs with up to **3.3× fewer training FLOPs** and beats them on reasoning (up to +8.5 pts on GSM8K).
**Why it matters**: Strong empirical case that selective looping beats naive depth scaling for diffusion LMs — and unlocks a clean inference-time compute knob.

### 5. MobileGym: A Verifiable and Highly Parallel Simulation Platform for Mobile GUI Agent Research
**Authors**: Dingbang Wu, Rui Hao, Haiyang Wang, Shuzhe Wu, Han Xiao, Zhenghong Li et al.
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.26114
**TL;DR**: Browser-hosted, JSON-state-based simulator for everyday mobile apps with **deterministic state-based judging** and hundreds of parallel rollouts (~400 MB / instance, ~3 s cold start). Ships MobileGym-Bench with 416 templated tasks over 28 apps; GRPO on Qwen3-VL-4B-Instruct gains +12.8 pp on the 256-task test set, and Sim-to-Real retains **95.1%** of simulation training gains on real devices.
**Why it matters**: Cheap, verifiable, massively parallel RL substrate for mobile GUI agents — one of the bottlenecks blocking serious agentic mobile work.
