## Highlights (Top 5 Must-Read)

### 1. On-Policy Delta Distillation
**Authors**: Byeongho Heo, Jaehui Hwang, Sangdoo Yun, Dongyoon Han
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.15161v1
**TL;DR**: Proposes distilling the *difference* between a fine-tuned (e.g. RL-tuned) model and its base checkpoint, rather than the fine-tuned model's outputs directly, as an on-policy training signal. Reports substantially improved transfer of reasoning capability gained during post-training into student models.
**Why it matters**: Reframes distillation around what post-training actually changed, potentially making expensive RL-tuning gains transferable to cheaper models without re-running the RL stage.

### 2. Scaling Behavior Foundation Model for Humanoid Robots
**Authors**: Weishuai Zeng, Kangning Yin, Xiaojie Niu, et al.
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.15163v1
**TL;DR**: Studies how learning paradigm, training data composition, and architecture jointly affect scaling behavior for humanoid-robot foundation models, reporting significant control-performance gains from coordinated scaling choices.
**Why it matters**: Embodied-AI scaling laws are far less charted than LLM scaling laws; this is direct evidence for what actually moves the needle in humanoid foundation models.

### 3. T²MLR: Transformer with Temporal Middle-Layer Recurrence
**Authors**: Ziyang Cai, Xingyu Zhu, Yihe Dong, et al.
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.15178v1
**TL;DR**: Introduces a transformer variant that caches and fuses middle-layer representations across decoding steps, adding a form of temporal recurrence to standard autoregressive decoding to improve reasoning without full retraining.
**Why it matters**: A lightweight architectural change that targets reasoning quality at inference time rather than requiring new pretraining runs — cheap to test against existing checkpoints.

### 4. Video = World + Event Stream
**Authors**: Lianghua Huang, Zhi-Fan Wu, Yupeng Shi, et al.
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.15038v1
**TL;DR**: Reframes video generation/understanding as a persistent "world" context plus an incremental event stream, enabling real-time full-duplex audio-visual interaction with roughly 550ms latency.
**Why it matters**: A practical architectural reframing that pushes multimodal models toward live, streaming interaction rather than clip-at-a-time generation — relevant to real-time agents and assistants.

### 5. Grokipedia vs Wikipedia: An LLM-Based Audit of Political Neutrality along Ideologies
**Authors**: Filippos Vlahos, Guillaume Bied, Tijl De Bie
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.15146v1
**TL;DR**: Uses multiple LLM judges to audit political bias across Grokipedia and Wikipedia articles, finding both encyclopedias skew toward different ideological groups depending on topic and judge model.
**Why it matters**: A concrete, LLM-graded evaluation of a live controversy (AI-generated vs. human-curated encyclopedic neutrality) with direct relevance to how LLMs themselves are used as bias auditors.
