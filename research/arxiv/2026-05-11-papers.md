# arXiv AI Research - 2026-05-11

## Highlights (Top 5 Must-Read)

### 1. ZAYA1-8B Technical Report
**Authors**: Zyphra team
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.05365
**TL;DR**: Reasoning-focused MoE with 700M active / 8B total parameters, built on Zyphra's MoE++ architecture and trained end-to-end on a full-stack AMD compute platform. A 4-stage RL cascade (reasoning warmup → 400-task RLVE-Gym → math/code RL with test-time-compute traces → behavioral RL) plus a novel "Markovian RSA" test-time aggregation that carries only a bounded 4K-token tail across rounds lifts it to 91.9% AIME'25 and 89.6% HMMT'25, matching or exceeding DeepSeek-R1-0528 with under 1B active params.
**Why it matters**: A credible production-scale demonstration that full-stack AMD training and small-active-param MoEs can rival much larger reasoning frontier models — and a new TTC recipe (Markovian RSA) that beats vanilla self-consistency at fixed context budgets.

### 2. Omnilingual MT: Machine Translation for 1,600 Languages
**Authors**: Omnilingual MT Team (Meta FAIR — Costa-jussà et al.)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.16309
**TL;DR**: A massively-multilingual translation system covering 1,600 languages — roughly an order of magnitude beyond NLLB-style coverage — with the supporting datasets and evaluation suite released as part of the report.
**Why it matters**: Pushes MT into the long tail of low- and ultra-low-resource languages and sets a new community baseline for multilingual coverage.

### 3. ViTok-v2: Scaling Native Resolution Auto-Encoders to 5 Billion Parameters
**Authors**: Hansen-Estruch et al. (Meta / FAIR-affiliated)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.05331
**TL;DR**: Replaces LPIPS + adversarial losses with a novel DINOv3 perceptual loss for stable scaling, adds NaFlex native-resolution support, and trains a 5B-parameter ViT autoencoder on ~2B images — the largest image autoencoder reported to date. Matches SOTA at 256p and beats all baselines at ≥512p; joint scaling with flow-matching generators advances the reconstruction–generation Pareto frontier.
**Why it matters**: Establishes that image tokenizers themselves scale predictably alongside generators, removing GAN/LPIPS as the stability ceiling and changing how next-gen generative image stacks should be designed.

### 4. EMO: Pretraining Mixture of Experts for Emergent Modularity
**Authors**: (cs.CL submission)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.06663
**TL;DR**: A pretraining objective for MoEs that encourages experts to learn modular, interpretable specializations from scratch, rather than the entangled routing typical of post-hoc MoE conversions.
**Why it matters**: A potential answer to the long-standing critique that MoE experts don't actually specialize — important for interpretability, sparse routing efficiency, and conditional-compute architectures.

### 5. Rethinking RL for LLM Reasoning: It's Sparse Policy Selection, Not Capability Learning
**Authors**: (cs.CL submission)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.06241
**TL;DR**: Token-level analysis argues RL's effect on math reasoning is a sparse, predictable, low-dimensional correction to the base model — not new capability. Introduces "ReasonMaxxer," which reproduces RL-style gains without RL training, by selecting among existing policy modes.
**Why it matters**: If true, much of the recent RL-for-reasoning literature is measuring decoder selection bias rather than learning — major implications for what RLVR/GRPO is actually buying.
