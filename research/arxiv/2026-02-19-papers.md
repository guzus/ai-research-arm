# arXiv AI Research - 2026-02-19

## Highlights (Top 5 Must-Read)

### 1. Team of Thoughts: Efficient Test-time Scaling of Agentic Systems through Orchestrated Tool Calling
**Authors**: (arXiv listing)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.16485
**TL;DR**: Proposes a unified framework for test-time scaling of agentic systems that orchestrates tool calling across Chain-of-Thought (CoT), Tree-of-Thoughts (ToT), and Graph-of-Thoughts (GoT) paradigms. Enables intermediate reasoning steps to be efficiently generated and verified through coordinated tool invocations.
**Why it matters**: Bridges the gap between reasoning frameworks and tool-use agents, offering a practical path to scale inference-time compute for real-world agentic applications.

### 2. ViCA: Efficient Multimodal LLMs with Vision-Only Cross-Attention
**Authors**: (arXiv listing)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.07574
**TL;DR**: Introduces a minimal multimodal LLM architecture where visual tokens interact with text solely through sparse cross-attention at selected layers. Preserves 98% of baseline accuracy while reducing visual-side computation to just 4%.
**Why it matters**: A 25x reduction in visual computation with near-zero accuracy loss could make multimodal LLMs practical for deployment on resource-constrained devices.

### 3. Convex Dominance in Deep Learning I: A Scaling Law of Loss and Learning Rate
**Authors**: (arXiv listing)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.07145
**TL;DR**: Derives new scaling laws relating loss and learning rate that extrapolate as much as 80X across training horizons and 70X across model sizes. Establishes that convex dominance governs the loss landscape in practical deep learning settings.
**Why it matters**: Enables far more reliable hyperparameter transfer across scales, potentially saving massive compute in large-scale training runs.

### 4. A Geometric Taxonomy of Hallucinations in LLMs
**Authors**: (arXiv listing)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.13224
**TL;DR**: Introduces a taxonomy distinguishing three types of hallucinations by their embedding geometry: unfaithfulness (Type I), confabulation (Type II), and factual error (Type III). Each type has distinct geometric signatures in the model's representation space.
**Why it matters**: Provides a principled, mechanistic framework for detecting and mitigating different hallucination types, moving beyond surface-level heuristics.

### 5. Towards a Science of AI Agent Reliability
**Authors**: Stephan Rabanser, Sayash Kapoor, Peter Kirgis, Kangheng Liu, Saiteja Utpala, Arvind Narayanan
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.16666
**TL;DR**: Establishes a systematic framework for measuring and improving the reliability of AI agents, covering failure modes in planning, tool use, and multi-step reasoning. Proposes metrics and methodologies for principled agent evaluation.
**Why it matters**: From Arvind Narayanan's group at Princeton — sets the foundation for rigorous reliability engineering of agentic AI systems.
