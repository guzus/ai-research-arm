# arXiv AI Research - 2026-05-17

## Highlights (Top 5 Must-Read)

### 1. FeF-DLLM: Factorization-Error-Free Discrete Diffusion Language Modeling
**Authors**: (cs.CL submission, May 2026)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/list/cs.CL/new
**TL;DR**: Replaces independent clean-token prediction with exact prefix-conditioned factorization and incorporates speculative decoding inside the diffusion denoising loop. Reports +5.04pp average gain on GSM8K, MATH, HumanEval and MBPP with a 3.86x inference speedup over prior discrete diffusion LMs.
**Why it matters**: One of the strongest signals yet that discrete diffusion LLMs can match autoregressive quality on reasoning/code while keeping their parallel-decoding speed advantage.

### 2. Hierarchical Language Model with Predictable Scaling Laws and Provable Benefits of Reasoning
**Authors**: (cs.LG submission, May 2026)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/list/cs.LG/new
**TL;DR**: Introduces a hierarchical LM architecture whose loss curves obey a clean, predictable scaling law and provides a theoretical proof that explicit reasoning steps improve sample efficiency. Empirical results corroborate the predicted curves at multiple compute budgets.
**Why it matters**: A rare combination of new scaling-law derivation plus a formal reasoning-benefits result; useful for teams trying to plan compute for reasoning-trained models.

### 3. Gecko: Efficient Neural Architecture for Arbitrary-Length Sequences
**Authors**: Xuezhe Ma, Shicheng Wen, et al.
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/list/cs.CL/new
**TL;DR**: A new sequence backbone designed to natively process sequences of arbitrary length without quadratic attention costs or sliding-window approximations. Reports competitive perplexity against Transformer baselines while extrapolating cleanly to much longer contexts than seen at training time.
**Why it matters**: Adds another credible challenger to the Transformer for long-context regimes, alongside recent state-space and linear-attention work.

### 4. Modality Credit Assignment for Vision-Language Models via Perception-Reasoning RL
**Authors**: (cs.AI / cs.CV submission, May 2026)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/list/cs.AI/new
**TL;DR**: Introduces an RL framework that issues separate, reliable rewards for perception fidelity vs. reasoning correctness, so VLMs no longer get credit for reasoning over hallucinated visual content. Improves grounded VQA without trading off chain-of-thought quality.
**Why it matters**: Targets one of the most reported failure modes of current VLMs (confident reasoning on misperceived images) with a clean training-time intervention.

### 5. You Snooze, You Lose: Automatic Safety Alignment Restoration through Neural Weight Translation
**Authors**: (cs.AI / cs.LG, May 2026)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.04992
**TL;DR**: Treats safety-alignment forgetting during downstream fine-tuning as a weight-translation problem, learning a mapping that restores the safety subspace of an aligned base model after domain fine-tuning. Recovers refusal/safety behavior without retraining on safety data.
**Why it matters**: Practical answer to the well-documented "fine-tuning destroys alignment" problem that has dogged open-weight deployments.
