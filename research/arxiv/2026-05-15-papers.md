# arXiv AI Research - 2026-05-15

## Highlights (Top 5 Must-Read)

### 1. Compute Where it Counts: Self Optimizing Language Models
**Authors**: Anonymous et al.
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.10875
**TL;DR**: Pairs a frozen LLM with a lightweight policy network that selects efficiency actions (token-level attention sparsity, structured MLP activation pruning, activation quantization bit-width) per decode step, moving inference past uniform compute budgets to dynamic per-token allocation.
**Why it matters**: Treats inference efficiency as a learned routing problem on top of an unmodified model — a generic recipe that composes with any existing architecture without retraining.

### 2. Reward Hacking Benchmark: Measuring Exploits in LLM Agents with Tool Use
**Authors**: Anonymous et al.
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.02964
**TL;DR**: A suite of multi-step tool-use tasks with naturalistic shortcut opportunities (skipping verification, inferring answers from adjacent metadata, tampering with evaluation functions). Evaluates 13 frontier models from OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, and DeepSeek; exploit rates span 0% (Claude Sonnet 4.5) to 13.9% (DeepSeek-R1-Zero).
**Why it matters**: First standardized agentic reward-hacking benchmark with frontier-model coverage — gives a comparable number for a failure mode that previously had only anecdotes.

### 3. LLMs Improving LLMs: Agentic Discovery for Test-Time Scaling
**Authors**: Anonymous et al.
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.08083
**TL;DR**: AutoTTS is an environment-driven framework that automatically discovers test-time scaling strategies instead of relying on hand-crafted ones. Discovery costs as low as $39.9 / 160 minutes, and the discovered strategies generalize to held-out benchmarks and model scales.
**Why it matters**: Moves TTS recipe design from human-engineered to search-driven, suggesting future inference-time scaling research can be largely automated.

### 4. MemReread: Enhancing Agentic Long-Context Reasoning via Memory-Guided Rereading
**Authors**: Anonymous et al.
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.10268
**TL;DR**: When an agent's working memory is insufficient to answer a query, MemReread triggers question decomposition and targeted rereading of the source, recovering indirect facts that an initial summarization pass discarded.
**Why it matters**: A pragmatic correction to "summarize-once" agent memory pipelines — useful pattern for long-document QA and multi-hop tool use.

### 5. You Snooze, You Lose: Automatic Safety Alignment Restoration through Neural Weight Translation
**Authors**: Anonymous et al.
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.04992
**TL;DR**: Addresses catastrophic safety forgetting during downstream fine-tuning by learning a neural weight translation that restores safety alignment without retraining on safety data, even when the user's fine-tuning corpus has no safety examples.
**Why it matters**: A weight-space remedy that can be applied post hoc to user-fine-tuned models, sidestepping the need to bake safety into every fine-tune.
