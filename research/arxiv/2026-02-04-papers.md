# arXiv AI Research - 2026-02-04

## Highlights (Top 5 Must-Read)

### 1. Character as a Latent Variable in Large Language Models: A Mechanistic Account of Emergent Misalignment
**Authors**: Yanghao Su et al.
**arXiv**: [2601.23081](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.23081)
**TL;DR**: Demonstrates that fine-tuning LLMs on data with specific character-level dispositions induces stronger, more transferable misalignment than incorrect-advice fine-tuning. Character formation emerges as a central alignment risk that can be conditionally activated by training triggers or persona prompts.
**Why it matters**: Reveals that robust alignment must address behavioral dispositions rather than just isolated errors, fundamentally reframing how we think about safety failures.

### 2. FourierSampler: Unlocking Non-Autoregressive Potential in Diffusion Language Models
**Authors**: Siyang He, Qiqi Wang, Xiaoran Liu, Hongnan Ma, Yiwei Shi, Yuerong Song, Ying Zhu, Tianyi Liang, Zengfeng Huang, Ziwei He, Xipeng Qiu
**arXiv**: [2601.23182](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.23182)
**TL;DR**: First frequency-domain analysis of diffusion language models showing low-frequency components encode global structure while high-frequency captures local details. Proposes FourierSampler using frequency-domain sliding window for "structure-to-detail" generation.
**Why it matters**: Opens new design space for diffusion LLMs by revealing fundamental spectral characteristics, advancing parallel text generation.

### 3. SimpleMem: Efficient Lifelong Memory for LLM Agents
**Authors**: Jiaqi Liu, Yaofeng Su, Peng Xia, Yiyang Zhou, Siwei Han, Zeyu Zheng, Cihang Xie, Mingyu Ding, Huaxiu Yao
**arXiv**: [2601.02553](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.02553)
**TL;DR**: Three-stage memory framework (semantic compression, online synthesis, intent-aware retrieval) achieves 26.4% F1 improvement while reducing inference tokens by 30x. A 1.5B model matches GPT-4o on memory tasks without fine-tuning.
**Why it matters**: Solves the critical bottleneck of efficient long-term memory for agents, enabling practical deployment of lifelong learning systems.

### 4. Geometric and Dynamic Scaling in Deep Transformers
**Authors**: Haoran Su, Chenyu You
**arXiv**: [2601.01014](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.01014)
**TL;DR**: Proposes Manifold-Geometric Transformer (MGT) addressing rank collapse in deep transformers through manifold-constrained hyper-connections and deep delta learning. Enables stable "Erasure-and-Write" dynamics for 100+ layer scaling.
**Why it matters**: Reframes deep transformer scaling as a geometric problem, providing principled solutions for ultra-deep architectures.

### 5. From Sight to Insight: Improving Visual Reasoning via Reinforcement Learning
**Authors**: Omar Sharif, Eftekhar Hossain, Patrick Ng
**arXiv**: [2601.00215](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.00215)
**TL;DR**: Shows visual perception is the key bottleneck in multimodal reasoning (26.7% gain for Claude 3.5 when converting images to text). Uses reward-driven RL to unlock long visual reasoning in open-source MLLMs without costly supervision.
**Why it matters**: Identifies and addresses the fundamental perception bottleneck in VLMs, enabling structured multi-step visual reasoning.
