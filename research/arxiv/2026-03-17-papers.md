# arXiv AI Research - 2026-03-17

## Highlights (Top 5 Must-Read)

### 1. [Scaling Laws Meet Model Architecture: Toward Inference-Efficient LLMs](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.18245v2)
**Authors**: Song Bian et al.
**arXiv**: [2510.18245v2](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.18245v2)
**TL;DR**: Trains 200+ models (80M–3B params) to study how hidden size, MLP-to-attention ratio, and grouped-query attention affect both inference cost and accuracy. Introduces conditional scaling laws extending Chinchilla with architectural information and a search framework for inference-efficient architectures.
**Why it matters**: Published at ICLR 2026 — first systematic study connecting architecture design choices to inference efficiency scaling laws, directly informing how to build cheaper-to-run LLMs.

### 2. [The Synthetic Web: Adversarially-Curated Mini-Internets for Diagnosing Epistemic Weaknesses of Language Agents](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.00801)
**Authors**: Shrey Shah, Levent Ozgur
**arXiv**: [2603.00801](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.00801)
**TL;DR**: Creates procedurally generated mini-internets with ground-truth credibility labels to test language agents' robustness to adversarial search results. Reveals catastrophic failures in six frontier models: accuracy collapses when a single high-plausibility misinformation article is injected at top rank, with minimal search escalation and severe miscalibration.
**Why it matters**: Exposes fundamental positional anchoring vulnerabilities in how frontier models handle conflicting web information — critical for any deployed agent with search capabilities.

### 3. [DeReason: A Difficulty-Aware Curriculum Improves Decoupled SFT-then-RL Training for General Reasoning](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.11193)
**Authors**: (Multiple authors)
**arXiv**: [2603.11193](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.11193)
**TL;DR**: Shows RL alone is insufficient for general STEM reasoning and proposes DeReason, which partitions training data by reasoning intensity: non-reasoning-intensive problems go to SFT for knowledge consolidation, while difficult reasoning problems are reserved for RL. Significantly outperforms SFT-only, RL-only, and random-split baselines.
**Why it matters**: Provides actionable insight into the SFT-vs-RL debate for reasoning — the right data allocation strategy between the two stages matters more than either alone.

### 4. [LemmaBench: A Live, Research-Level Benchmark to Evaluate LLM Capabilities in Mathematics](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.24173)
**Authors**: Antoine Peyronnet et al.
**arXiv**: [2602.24173](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.24173)
**TL;DR**: Establishes an automatically-updating benchmark that extracts lemmas from fresh arXiv papers and evaluates LLMs on research-level theorem proving. Even GPT-5 achieves only ~15% accuracy (pass@1), revealing a massive gap between current LLMs and human-level mathematical research capabilities.
**Why it matters**: First contamination-resistant, continuously updated math benchmark using real research — sets a high bar for measuring genuine mathematical reasoning progress.

### 5. [SPARROW: Learning Spatial Precision and Temporal Referential Consistency in Pixel-Grounded Video MLLMs](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.12382)
**Authors**: Mohamad Alansari, Naufal Suryanto, Divya Velayudhan, Sajid Javed, Naoufel Werghi, Muzammal Naseer
**arXiv**: [2603.12382](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.12382)
**TL;DR**: Introduces a pixel-grounded video MLLM that unifies spatial accuracy and temporal stability through Target-Specific Tracked Features and dual-prompt design. Achieves +8.9 J&F on RVOS, +5 mIoU on visual grounding across three open-source video MLLMs. Accepted at CVPR 2026.
**Why it matters**: Addresses the critical gap between image-level grounding and temporally consistent video understanding — key for real-world video agents.
