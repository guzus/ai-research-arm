# arXiv AI Research - 2026-05-08

## Highlights (Top 5 Must-Read)

### 1. Explaining and Preventing Alignment Collapse in Iterative RLHF
**Authors**: et al. (game-theoretic RLHF group)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.04266
**TL;DR**: Formalizes iterative RLHF as a Stackelberg game and decomposes the policy's true gradient into a standard policy gradient plus a "parameter-steering" term that captures the policy's influence on the reward model's future updates. Standard iterative RLHF drops this term, causing alignment collapse — the policy exploits RM blind spots and the resulting feedback reinforces the errors. The authors propose Foresighted Policy Optimization (FPO), which restores the missing term and prevents collapse on Llama-3.2-1B alignment pipelines.
**Why it matters**: Provides the first mechanistic explanation for why RLHF degrades over training rounds and a principled fix, directly relevant to every frontier post-training stack.

### 2. AgentFloor: How Far Up the Tool-Use Ladder Can Small Open-Weight Models Go?
**Authors**: AgentFloor team
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.00334
**TL;DR**: A 30-task benchmark organized into a six-tier capability ladder spanning single-tool use, multi-step coordination, and long-horizon planning. Evaluates 16 open-weight models alongside GPT-5 and exposes sharp tier boundaries where small models fail.
**Why it matters**: Gives the open-source community a structured progress map for closed-vs-open agent capability, replacing the "single-pass-rate" benchmarks that have saturated.

### 3. Physically Native World Models: A Hamiltonian Perspective on Generative World Modeling
**Authors**: Physically Native World Models team
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.00412
**TL;DR**: Argues current generative world models lack physical reliability, action controllability, and long-horizon stability. Reformulates world modeling around Hamiltonian dynamics to enforce conservation laws as a structural prior, yielding stable rollouts where standard video models drift.
**Why it matters**: A serious architectural alternative to "scale up video diffusion" approaches for embodied AI / robotics world models.

### 4. Low-Cost Black-Box Detection of LLM Hallucinations via Dynamical System Prediction
**Authors**: Black-box hallucination team
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.05134
**TL;DR**: Treats the LLM as a black-box dynamical system and fits a small linear dynamical model on a handful of responses. Detects hallucinations in a single sample without the heavy multi-sample consistency checks used by SelfCheckGPT and friends.
**Why it matters**: Practical detection that scales to production inference cost — important for retrieval, agent, and clinical deployments.

### 5. WorldJen: An End-to-End Multi-Dimensional Benchmark for Generative Video Models
**Authors**: WorldJen team
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.03475
**TL;DR**: Replaces binary VQA in video-gen benchmarks with VLM-graded Likert questionnaires at native resolution and adversarially-curated prompts that exercise up to 16 quality dimensions simultaneously. Targets the saturation of existing metrics like VBench.
**Why it matters**: Video-generation evaluation has lagged the model frontier; this gives labs a discriminating yardstick for the next generation of releases.
