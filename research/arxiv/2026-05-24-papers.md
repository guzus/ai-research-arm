# arXiv AI Research - 2026-05-24

## Highlights (Top 5 Must-Read)

### 1. Same Architecture, Different Capacity: Optimizer-Induced Spectral Scaling Laws
**Authors**: et al. (see arXiv)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.21803
**TL;DR**: The same Transformer realizes markedly different spectral scaling laws depending on the optimizer it is trained with. Muon induces near-linear effective-rank scaling (β≈1.02) while AdamW yields weak hard-rank scaling (β≈0.44), meaning the optimizer — not just parameter count — sets the model's usable capacity.
**Why it matters**: Reframes scaling laws as optimizer-dependent, suggesting frontier labs may be leaving capacity on the table by defaulting to AdamW.

### 2. MOOD: Benchmarking and Improving Monitors for Out-Of-Distribution Alignment Failure in LLMs
**Authors**: et al. (see arXiv)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.21602
**TL;DR**: Introduces the first benchmark for evaluating LLM "monitors" against diverse out-of-distribution alignment failures, spanning seven failure modes including sycophancy, deception, and scheming. Shows current monitors generalize poorly across failure types.
**Why it matters**: Monitoring is a load-bearing assumption in AI control proposals; MOOD gives the field a concrete way to measure whether monitors actually catch novel misbehavior.

### 3. Vision-OPD: Learning to See Fine Details for Multimodal LLMs via On-Policy Self-Distillation
**Authors**: et al. (see arXiv)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.18740
**TL;DR**: Identifies a regional-to-global perception gap in MLLMs — answers often hinge on small but decisive image evidence the full-image policy misses. Vision-OPD distills the model's own privileged regional perception into its full-image policy via on-policy self-distillation.
**Why it matters**: A training-side fix for fine-grained visual grounding that needs no extra labels or external teacher, attacking a persistent MLLM failure mode.

### 4. Spreadsheet-RL: Advancing LLM Agents on Realistic Spreadsheet Tasks via Reinforcement Learning
**Authors**: et al. (see arXiv)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.22642
**TL;DR**: An RL fine-tuning framework that trains agents inside a realistic Microsoft Excel environment, with an automated pipeline that scrapes paired start–goal spreadsheets from online forums. Lifts Qwen3-4B-Thinking Pass@1 on SpreadsheetBench from 12.0%→23.4% and on a domain set from 8.4%→17.2%.
**Why it matters**: Spreadsheets are among the most economically valuable, under-served agent surfaces; near-doubling task success via RL is a concrete capability gain.

### 5. ElasticDiT: Efficient Diffusion Transformers via Elastic Architecture and Sparse Attention
**Authors**: et al. (see arXiv)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.15684
**TL;DR**: A diffusion transformer that trades quality for speed at inference by dynamically adjusting spatial compression ratios and DiT block depth, paired with Shift Sparse Block Attention and a Tiny DWT-distilled VAE. Targets high-resolution image generation on mobile devices.
**Why it matters**: Pushes high-res diffusion toward on-device deployment with a single elastic model rather than a family of fixed-cost checkpoints.
