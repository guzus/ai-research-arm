# arXiv AI Research - 2026-04-19

## Highlights (Top 5 Must-Read)

### 1. Generalization and Scaling Laws for Mixture-of-Experts Transformers
**Authors**: arXiv 2604.09175
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.09175
**TL;DR**: Develops a theory of generalization and scaling for MoE Transformers that cleanly separates active per-input capacity from routing combinatorics. Provides conditional scaling laws tying expert count, top-k routing, and generalization error.
**Why it matters**: Gives practitioners a principled knob-set for provisioning MoE compute instead of trial-and-error — a major gap in the Chinchilla-style literature for sparse models.

### 2. LASA: Language-Agnostic Semantic Alignment at the Semantic Bottleneck for LLM Safety
**Authors**: arXiv 2604.12710
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.12710
**TL;DR**: Targets the mismatch between language-agnostic semantic understanding and high-resource-dominated safety alignment. LASA intervenes at a shared semantic bottleneck; attack success rate drops from 24.7% → 2.8% on LLaMA-3.1-8B-Instruct and stays at 3–4% across Qwen2.5/Qwen3 Instruct.
**Why it matters**: Closes the low-resource-language jailbreak gap, one of the most exploited vectors in production LLM deployments.

### 3. INCRT: An Incremental Transformer That Determines Its Own Architecture
**Authors**: arXiv 2604.10703
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.10703
**TL;DR**: A Transformer that grows/prunes attention heads during training — starts with one head, adds heads when the current configuration is provably insufficient, prunes redundant ones.
**Why it matters**: Moves architecture search from expensive offline sweeps into a single training run, potentially cutting capacity-tuning compute by an order of magnitude.

### 4. MERRIN: Benchmark for Multimodal Evidence Retrieval and Reasoning in Noisy Web Environments
**Authors**: arXiv 2604.13418
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.13418
**TL;DR**: Human-annotated benchmark for search-augmented agents that must pick the right modality, retrieve multimodal evidence, and do multi-hop reasoning across noisy web sources.
**Why it matters**: Existing RAG evals are largely text-only and clean; MERRIN stresses the realistic pipeline that web agents actually face.

### 5. Filtered Reasoning Score (FRS): Evaluating Reasoning Quality on a Model's Most Confident Traces
**Authors**: arXiv 2604.11996
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.11996
**TL;DR**: Argues accuracy alone hides reasoning rot (memorization, right-answer-wrong-steps). FRS scores traces along faithfulness, coherence, utility, and factuality and filters by model confidence.
**Why it matters**: As RL-tuned reasoners saturate math/code benchmarks, metrics that separate real reasoning from shortcut behavior become load-bearing.
