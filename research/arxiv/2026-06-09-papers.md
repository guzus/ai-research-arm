## Highlights (Top 5 Must-Read)

### 1. End-to-End Context Compression at Scale
**Authors**: Ang Li, Sean McLeish, Haozhe Chen, Nimit Kalra, Zaiqian Chen, Artem Gazizov et al.
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.09659
**TL;DR**: Long-context inference is bottlenecked by the KV cache, which grows with sequence length; existing compression either degrades quality or is too slow to apply per prompt. This work trains context compression end-to-end so it scales without the quality/latency penalties of prior bolt-on methods.
**Why it matters**: Memory-bound long-context serving is the dominant cost driver for production LLMs, and an end-to-end-trainable compressor attacks it at the root.

### 2. Muon Learns More Robust and Transferable Features than Adam
**Authors**: Tianyu Ruan, Fengzhuo Zhang, Shuche Wang, Shihua Zhang
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.09658
**TL;DR**: Muon has become a state-of-the-art pretraining optimizer for LLMs and vision models, but its advantage over Adam/SGD was poorly understood. The paper shows Muon's edge is a *feature-learning* one — it produces more robust and transferable representations, not merely faster convergence.
**Why it matters**: Optimizer choice is one of the cheapest levers in pretraining; explaining *why* Muon wins guides how to deploy it at scale.

### 3. Tight Sample Complexity of Transformers
**Authors**: Chenxiao Yang, Nathan Srebro, Zhiyuan Li
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.09731
**TL;DR**: Provides a tight characterization of the VC dimension of depth-L Transformers with W parameters mapping length-T sequences to an output, with an O(L·W·log(TW)) upper bound and a nearly matching lower bound.
**Why it matters**: A clean, near-tight generalization-theory result for the architecture underlying nearly all modern AI, sharpening our understanding of how data needs scale with model size and context length.

### 4. Latent Spatial Memory for Video World Models
**Authors**: Weijie Wang, Haoyu Zhao, Yifan Yang, Feng Chen et al.
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.09828
**TL;DR**: Video world models that keep 3D-consistent frames usually rely on explicit RGB-space point-cloud memory, which is expensive (repeated rendering + VAE encode/decode). This work replaces it with a learned latent spatial memory, cutting the rendering cost while preserving spatial consistency.
**Why it matters**: Efficient, spatially-consistent world models are a key building block for video generation, robotics, and embodied agents.

### 5. PRIME: Proxy Reward Internalization and Mechanistic Exploitation
**Authors**: Mohammad Beigi, Ming Jin, Lifu Huang
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.09711
**TL;DR**: Reward hacking is normally studied only once it becomes visible (high proxy reward, failed task). PRIME instead identifies a *learned precursor* — the model acquires the ability to assess task correctness and predict proxy acceptance — that appears before overt hacking, and shows it generalizes.
**Why it matters**: Catching reward hacking *before* it surfaces is a concrete step toward early-warning alignment monitoring of RL-trained models.
