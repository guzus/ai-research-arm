## Highlights (Top 5 Must-Read)

### 1. Vision as Unified Multimodal Generation (SenseNova-Vision)
**Authors**: Xiaoyang Han, Jianhua Li, Kewang Deng, ..., Ziwei Liu, Lei Yang, Dahua Lin, Quan Wang
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.06560
**TL;DR**: Formulates all of computer vision as unified multimodal generation — detection, OCR, keypoints, segmentation, depth, surface normals, point maps, and camera pose are expressed in the native text + image generation spaces of a single multimodal model, with no task-specific heads or architectural changes (48 pages, 22 figures).
**Why it matters**: A single unified model matches leading task-specialized systems across structured visual understanding, dense geometric prediction, segmentation, and multi-view geometry — a scalable route to fold CV capabilities into general-purpose foundation models.

### 2. Multiplayer Interactive World Models with Representation Autoencoders
**Authors**: Anthony Hu, Václav Volhejn, ..., Lucia Specia, Michael Black, Patrick Pérez (Valence Labs)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.05352  *(major updated preprint, v2)*
**TL;DR**: The first multiplayer world model — a 5B-parameter latent diffusion model trained on 10,000 hours of Rocket League gameplay that generates four-player matches in real time (20 fps on a single Nvidia B200), with rollouts stable for hours despite being trained only on short clips.
**Why it matters**: Demonstrates world models can attribute scene changes to the correct player and stay coherent under arbitrary combinations of multi-agent actions — a milestone for real-time interactive simulation and "physical AI."

### 3. Doomed from the Start: Early Abort of LLM Agent Episodes
**Authors**: Kai Ruan, Zihe Huang, Ziqi Zhou, Qianshan Wei, Xuan Wang, Hao Sun
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.06503
**TL;DR**: Lightweight probes on an agent's hidden activations predict eventual episode failure as early as the first interaction turn (where behavior-only scorers are near-chance); a distribution-free calibrated abort cascade meets 90–97% recall targets while saving ~47% (Qwen-2.5-7B) / ~37% (Llama-3.2-3B) of inference compute.
**Why it matters**: Failure is visible in internal representations long before it surfaces in behavior, turning agent monitoring into a cheap internal-state signal and roughly halving wasted agent compute.

### 4. Danus: Orchestrating Mathematical Reasoning Agents with Fact-Graph Memory
**Authors**: Jihao Liu, Guoxiong Gao, Zeming Sun, ..., Bin Dong (Peking University)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.06447
**TL;DR**: An orchestration system for research-level mathematics built around a shared fact graph as global memory: a main agent coordinates parallel worker proof-search, and a stateless verifier admits only checked claims, enabling long incremental proofs across algebraic geometry, singularity theory, and combinatorics. Open source.
**Why it matters**: Danus autonomously produced a real mathematical result (the matroid tangent-class paper arXiv:2607.05835) before the human-authored version was public — a concrete demonstration of scaled agentic research.

### 5. DT-Guard: Intent-Driven Reasoning-Active Training for Reasoning-Free LLM Safety Guardrail
**Authors**: He Liu, Changtao Miao, ..., Wei Lu, Wei Wang, et al.
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.06326
**TL;DR**: Trains a safety guardrail with reasoning supervision during training but emits only structured safety labels at inference (no reasoning traces), using a progressive Intent→Category→Safety decision process and rollout-guided hard-case optimization.
**Why it matters**: A 4B backbone reaches a dual-side average F1 of 0.878, outperforming strong 8B guardrail baselines — distilling the reasoning into weights so deployment stays low-latency.
