## Highlights (Top 5 Must-Read)

### 1. Scalable Visual Pretraining for Language Intelligence
**Authors**: Yiming Zhang, Zhonghan Zhao, Wenwei Zhang, et al.
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.09657
**TL;DR**: A systematic study of unsupervised visual pretraining on documents and web pages — leveraging raw page images without OCR/text extraction — shows it consistently outperforms text-only pretraining for building foundation language models.
**Why it matters**: A direct challenge to the text-only pretraining paradigm; if visual pretraining scales better, it changes how the next generation of foundation models sources training signal.

### 2. Statistically Undetectable Backdoors in Deep Neural Networks
**Authors**: Andrej Bogdanov, Alon Rosen, Neekon Vafa
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.09532
**TL;DR**: Proves that backdoors can be planted in deep feedforward networks that remain statistically undetectable in white-box settings (total-variation-close to a clean model), while producing invariance-based adversarial examples that are themselves cryptographically hard to generate without backdoor access.
**Why it matters**: A theoretical worst case for supply-chain trust in trained models — no black-box or even white-box statistical test can rule out this class of backdoor.

### 3. Agora: Enhancing LLM Agent Reasoning via Auction-Based Task Allocation
**Authors**: Kaiji Zhou, Ales Leonardis, Yue Feng
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.09600
**TL;DR**: Introduces an incentive-compatible auction mechanism that lets multiple expert models bid on reasoning sub-steps based on rectified competence, replacing static routing with a market-based allocation across five benchmarks.
**Why it matters**: Reframes multi-agent orchestration as a mechanism-design problem, offering a principled (and tunable cost/quality) alternative to hand-tuned routing heuristics.

### 4. Failure as a Process: An Anatomy of CLI Coding Agent Trajectories
**Authors**: Xiangxin Zhao, Han Li, Shuaiting Li, et al.
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.09510
**TL;DR**: Analyzes 1,794 annotated trajectories (63,000+ steps) from seven frontier CLI coding agents and finds failures are dominated by epistemic errors that begin early and stay hidden until unrecoverable.
**Why it matters**: Directly relevant to anyone running autonomous coding agents in production — argues for earlier validation checkpoints rather than end-of-trajectory review.

### 5. What VGGT Knows About Overlap: Probing Geometric Foundation Models for Co-Visibility
**Authors**: Filippo Ziliotto, Luciano Serafini, Lamberto Ballan, Tommaso Campari
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.09503
**TL;DR**: Shows a geometric foundation model (VGGT) implicitly learns co-visibility with an LLM-like hierarchical structure, including a single layer acting as a "negative anchor" for non-overlapping views; a lightweight probe head beats the human baseline by 25% on Co-VisiON.
**Why it matters**: Evidence that emergent, interpretable internal structure isn't unique to language models — it shows up in geometric vision transformers too.
