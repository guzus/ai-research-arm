# arXiv AI Research - 2026-06-22

## Highlights (Top 5 Must-Read)

### 1. DeepSeek-V4: Towards Highly Efficient Million-Token Context Intelligence
**Authors**: DeepSeek-AI
**arXiv**: [2606.19348](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.19348)
**TL;DR**: A new MoE family — DeepSeek-V4-Pro (1.6T total / 49B active) and DeepSeek-V4-Flash (284B / 13B active) — both supporting 1M-token context. The series introduces a hybrid attention stack combining Compressed Sparse Attention (CSA) and Heavily Compressed Attention (HCA) to make long-context inference economical.
**Why it matters**: The first frontier-scale open MoE to push a genuine million-token context with a custom sparse-attention design rather than brute-force scaling.

### 2. Nemotron 3 Ultra: Open, Efficient MoE Hybrid Mamba-Transformer for Agentic Reasoning
**Authors**: NVIDIA
**arXiv**: [2606.15007](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.15007)
**TL;DR**: A 550B-total / 55B-active Mixture-of-Experts hybrid Mamba-Attention model pre-trained on 20T tokens with context extended to 1M. The hybrid Mamba-Attention layout targets the inference-throughput-to-accuracy frontier for agentic workloads.
**Why it matters**: A fully open frontier model combining state-space and attention layers at scale, validating Mamba hybrids as a production architecture rather than a research curiosity.

### 3. Kwai Keye-VL-2.0 Technical Report
**Authors**: Kuaishou Keye Team
**arXiv**: [2606.10651](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.10651)
**TL;DR**: An open-source MoE multimodal foundation model that adapts DeepSeek Sparse Attention (DSA) to enable lossless 256K-token context processing across image, video, and text.
**Why it matters**: Brings the sparse-attention long-context recipe into the multimodal regime, a domain where context blowup from visual tokens is the binding constraint.

### 4. Closing the Loop on Latent Reasoning via Test-Time Reconstruction
**Authors**: Xiaopeng Yuan, Haibo Jin, Ye Yu, Peng Kuang, Lijun Yu, Yushun Dong, Haohan Wang
**arXiv**: [2606.06252](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.06252)
**TL;DR**: Moves intermediate reasoning out of natural-language traces and into latent / cache-level representations to cut token overhead, then adds a test-time reconstruction step to recover interpretable reasoning when needed.
**Why it matters**: Targets the central cost problem of reasoning models — long CoT token budgets — while preserving an auditable trace.

### 5. What Do Safety-Aligned LLMs Learn From Mixed Compliance Demonstrations?
**Authors**: (see arXiv listing)
**arXiv**: [2606.20508](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.20508)
**TL;DR**: Studies how mixing benign and harmful compliance demonstrations affects safety. Finds benign and harmful demos are not interchangeable, that preference optimization is the critical stage preventing benign demos from increasing harmful compliance, and that demonstration ordering shows strong recency bias.
**Why it matters**: Concrete, mechanism-level evidence about which alignment stage actually carries the safety load — directly informs RLHF/DPO pipeline design.
