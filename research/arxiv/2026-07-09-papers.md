# arXiv AI Research - 2026-07-09

## Highlights (Top 5 Must-Read)

### 1. SciReasoner
**Authors**: Chen Tang, Yizhou Wang, Jianyu Wu, et al.
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.07708
**TL;DR**: A multimodal foundation model for structural scientific reasoning across proteins, molecules, and crystals that discretizes coordinates, topologies, and periodic connectivities into a unified structure-aware vocabulary. Hits state-of-the-art on 67 of 86 benchmarks, and expert evaluators preferred its reasoning traces 98% of the time.
**Why it matters**: A rare foundation model that treats scientific structure (not just text/pixels) as a first-class modality, pointing toward general-purpose scientific reasoning agents.

### 2. The Key to Going Linear: Analysis-Driven Transformer Linearization
**Authors**: Anna Kuzina, Paul N. Whatmough, Babak Ehteshami Bejnordi
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.07706
**TL;DR**: Traces quadratic attention cost to softmax's reliance on key-dependent, rank-1 orthogonal projections, then introduces structural interventions (sink tokens, cache routing) to linearize attention, validated at 32B-parameter scale.
**Why it matters**: A principled, analysis-first path to sub-quadratic attention rather than another ad hoc linear-attention variant — and it actually scales.

### 3. Institutional Red-Teaming: Deployment Rules and Multi-Agent Safety
**Authors**: Yujiao Chen
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.07695
**TL;DR**: A red-teaming methodology for the deployment rules governing multi-agent AI systems, finding that changing just the "consequence rule" shifts mean fatality outcomes by 22–58 percentage points across model populations, with identity-targeting rules never decisively safe.
**Why it matters**: Quantifies how much governance/deployment-policy design — not just model capability — determines catastrophic outcome rates in multi-agent settings.

### 4. Recursive Self-Improvement in AI
**Authors**: Mingguang Chen, Licheng Wang, Bo Qu
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.07663
**TL;DR**: A 1,250-paper survey of recursive self-improvement (RSI) organized along two axes — what improves and how closed the loop is — finding that demonstrated self-improvement strength tracks the verification hierarchy, from formal verifiers down to intrinsic self-assessment.
**Why it matters**: The most comprehensive map yet of RSI research, directly relevant to forecasting AI capability trajectories and where verification bottlenecks will bind first.

### 5. Agon: Competitive Cross-Model RL
**Authors**: Vladislav Beliaev
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.07690
**TL;DR**: Has competing models grade each other during training to produce an implicit process reward signal; on hard DeepMath problems it roughly doubles GRPO's pass@1, about eight times the gain from an untrained Mixture-of-Agents baseline.
**Why it matters**: Turns model-vs-model competition into a cheap, label-free reward signal for reasoning RL — a scalable alternative to human/verifier-labeled process rewards.
