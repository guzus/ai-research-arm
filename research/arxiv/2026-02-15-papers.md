# arXiv AI Research - 2026-02-15

## Highlights (Top 5 Must-Read)

### 1. MiniCPM-SALA: Hybridizing Sparse and Linear Attention for Efficient Long-Context Modeling
**Authors**: OpenBMB Team
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.11761
**TL;DR**: A 9B-parameter hybrid architecture combining sparse attention (InfLLM-V2) with linear attention (Lightning Attention) in a 1:3 ratio. Achieves 3.5x inference speedup at 256K tokens on a single A6000D GPU, supports up to 1M token contexts, and reduces training costs by ~75% via continual training from pretrained Transformers.
**Why it matters**: Demonstrates a practical path to million-token context windows without sacrificing quality, with an efficient continual training recipe that makes hybrid attention accessible.

### 2. iGRPO: Self-Feedback-Driven LLM Reasoning
**Authors**: (arXiv:2602.09000)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.09000
**TL;DR**: A two-stage extension of GRPO that adds self-conditioning through model-generated drafts. Stage 1 samples exploratory drafts and selects the highest-reward one; Stage 2 trains the model to refine beyond its best prior attempt. Achieves SOTA on AIME24 (85.62%) and AIME25 (79.64%) using OpenReasoning-Nemotron-7B.
**Why it matters**: Introduces iterative self-feedback into RL-based reasoning training, mimicking how humans revise solutions, setting new math reasoning benchmarks with a 7B model.

### 3. Multi-Head LatentMoE and Head Parallel
**Authors**: (arXiv:2602.04870)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.04870
**TL;DR**: Proposes a new MoE architecture and parallelism strategy achieving O(1) communication cost regardless of the number of activated experts, with completely balanced traffic and deterministic communication. Trains up to 1.61x faster than standard MoE with Expert Parallel while matching performance.
**Why it matters**: Addresses a fundamental bottleneck in scaling MoE models — communication overhead — making larger and more fine-grained expert configurations practical.

### 4. Tabula RASA: Exposing and Breaking the Relational Bottleneck in Transformers
**Authors**: Jonas Petersen et al.
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.02834
**TL;DR**: Introduces Relation-Aware Sparse Attention (RASA) with edge-type embeddings and sparse masking that restricts attention to graph-adjacent positions, reducing the attention search space from O(2^n²) to O(2^m). Outperforms standard transformers and matches GPT-4 on multi-hop reasoning at lower cost (+7.1 points on 3-hop).
**Why it matters**: Provides a minimal, principled modification to transformers that dramatically improves multi-hop relational reasoning — a key weakness of current architectures.

### 5. CARE-RFT: Confidence-Anchored Reinforcement Finetuning for Reliable Reasoning in LLMs
**Authors**: Shuozhe Li, Jincheng Cao, Bodun Hu, Aryan Mokhtari, Leqi Liu, Amy Zhang
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.00085
**TL;DR**: Replaces standard reverse KL regularization in reinforcement finetuning with skew reverse KL divergence, providing a confidence-sensitive penalty that enables reasoning exploration while preserving calibration. Matches unconstrained RFT reasoning performance while recovering base model trustworthiness.
**Why it matters**: Solves the critical trade-off between reasoning capability and model trustworthiness in RL-based training, ensuring models don't hallucinate more as they reason better.
