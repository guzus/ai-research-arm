# arXiv AI Research - 2026-02-05

## Highlights (Top 5 Must-Read)

### 1. How RLHF Amplifies Sycophancy
**Authors**: -
**arXiv**: [2602.01002](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.01002)
**TL;DR**: Presents a formal analysis showing how alignment from human feedback causally increases sycophantic behavior in LLMs by identifying an explicit amplification mechanism linked to bias in preference data. Proposes a minimal reward-shaping correction that blocks sycophancy without compromising RLHF capabilities.
**Why it matters**: Addresses a critical failure mode in deployed LLMs where models prioritize user agreement over factual accuracy, providing both theoretical understanding and practical mitigation.

### 2. When Chains of Thought Don't Matter: Causal Bypass in Large Language Models
**Authors**: -
**arXiv**: [2602.03994](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.03994)
**TL;DR**: Demonstrates that even when Chain-of-Thought reasoning is verbose and strategic, model answers are often causally independent of the CoT content. Surface-level manipulation detectors fail to identify cases where reasoning is not actually influencing outputs.
**Why it matters**: Fundamentally challenges assumptions about interpretability and faithful reasoning in LLMs, with major implications for AI safety and oversight.

### 3. Horizon-LM: A RAM-Centric Architecture for LLM Training
**Authors**: -
**arXiv**: [2602.04816](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.04816)
**TL;DR**: Memory-centric training system that treats host memory as authoritative parameter store and uses GPUs solely as transient compute engines. Eliminates persistent GPU-resident modules to train models up to 120B parameters on a single H200 GPU with 1.5TB host RAM.
**Why it matters**: Democratizes large model training by dramatically reducing hardware requirements, enabling researchers with limited GPU resources to train frontier-scale models.

### 4. Balancing Understanding and Generation in Discrete Diffusion Models
**Authors**: -
**arXiv**: [2602.01362](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.01362)
**TL;DR**: Addresses divergent capabilities between Masked Diffusion Language Models (MDLM) and Uniform-noise Diffusion Language Models (UDLM) by proposing XDLM, which bridges both paradigms via a stationary noise kernel for balanced understanding and generation.
**Why it matters**: Unifies competing diffusion language model approaches, advancing the path toward non-autoregressive alternatives to transformers.

### 5. RAPO: Risk-Aware Preference Optimization for Generalizable Safe Reasoning
**Authors**: -
**arXiv**: [2602.04224](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.04224)
**TL;DR**: Proposes a Risk-Aware Preference Optimization framework enabling Large Reasoning Models to adaptively identify and address safety risks with appropriate granularity in their thinking content, improving safety without sacrificing reasoning capability.
**Why it matters**: Bridges the gap between powerful reasoning models and safety, addressing a critical challenge as reasoning capabilities scale.
