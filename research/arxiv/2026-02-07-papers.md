# arXiv AI Research - 2026-02-07

## Highlights (Top 5 Must-Read)

### 1. DFPO: Distributional Flow Policy Optimization for Robust LLM Post-Training
**Authors**: (Distributional RL team)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.05890
**TL;DR**: DFPO models value distributions as continuous flows across time steps, capturing richer state information for more accurate advantage estimation. Experiments on dialogue, math reasoning, and scientific tasks show it outperforms PPO, FlowRL, and other robust baselines under noisy supervision, achieving improved training stability and generalization.
**Why it matters**: A significant step forward in making RL-based LLM post-training more robust and generalizable, addressing a core bottleneck in alignment pipelines.

### 2. LongR: Unleashing Long-Context Reasoning via Reinforcement Learning with Dense Utility Rewards
**Authors**: Bowen Ping, Zijun Chen, Yiyao Yu et al.
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.05758
**TL;DR**: Proposes a unified framework combining a dynamic "Think-and-Read" mechanism that interleaves reasoning with document consultation, paired with a contextual density reward. Achieves a 9% gain on LongBench v2 and consistent improvements on RULER and InfiniteBench.
**Why it matters**: Directly tackles the challenge of reasoning over extremely long contexts, one of the most important open problems in LLM capabilities.

### 3. ASTER: Agentic Scaling with Tool-Integrated Extended Reasoning
**Authors**: (ASTER team)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.01204
**TL;DR**: Circumvents interaction collapse through a targeted cold-start strategy prioritizing interaction-dense trajectories. With just 4K interaction-dense trajectories, ASTER-4B achieves state-of-the-art results on competitive math benchmarks, reaching 90.0% on AIME 2025.
**Why it matters**: Demonstrates that small models (4B) can reach frontier-level math reasoning when equipped with proper agentic tool-use training — a major efficiency win.

### 4. f-GRPO and Beyond: Divergence-Based RL Algorithms for General LLM Alignment
**Authors**: (f-GRPO team)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.05946
**TL;DR**: Introduces f-GRPO, a family of divergence-based RL algorithms that generalizes GRPO. Shows consistent reasoning improvement over GRPO while maintaining computational efficiency and superior performance among on-policy algorithms.
**Why it matters**: Provides a principled theoretical framework extending GRPO, the workhorse behind many reasoning model training pipelines including DeepSeek-R1.

### 5. Alignment Drift in Multimodal LLMs: A Two-Phase Longitudinal Evaluation
**Authors**: (Alignment Drift team)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.04739
**TL;DR**: Conducts a longitudinal study of safety alignment across eight model releases from GPT, Claude, Pixtral, and Qwen families. Finds that GPT and Claude models exhibited increased Attack Success Rates across generations, while Pixtral and Qwen showed modest decreases.
**Why it matters**: First large-scale empirical evidence of alignment drift in production multimodal models — a critical warning for the safety community.
