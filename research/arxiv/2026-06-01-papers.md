## Highlights (Top 5 Must-Read)

### 1. Stateful Online Monitoring Catches Distributed Agent Attacks
**Authors**: Davis Brown, Samarth Bhargav, Arav Santhanam, Kasper Hong, Ivan Zhang, Matan Shtepel, Steffi Chern, Alexander Robey, Eric Wong, Hamed Hassani (UPenn)
**arXiv**: [2605.31593](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.31593)
**TL;DR**: Builds the first distributed-agent cyberattack — a multi-agent scaffold that splits one harmful task across many accounts so each transcript looks benign — and shows that standard single-context safety monitors catch it only ~1/5 as often as conventional agent attacks. Defends with an online stateful monitor that clusters weak suspicion signals across users and escalates rarely to an LLM judge, catching distributed attacks 30% earlier at negligible latency for ~99% of traffic.
**Why it matters**: Reframes agent safety from per-transcript filtering to population-level reasoning — directly relevant to anyone deploying agentic LLMs at scale.

### 2. Lumos-Nexus: Efficient Frequency Bridging with Homogeneous Latent Space for Video Unified Models
**Authors**: Jiazheng Xing, Hangjie Yuan, Lingling Cai, Xinyu Liu, Yujie Wei, Fei Du, Hai Ci, Tao Feng, Jiasheng Tang, Weihua Chen, Fan Wang, Yong Liu (Alibaba DAMO)
**arXiv**: [2605.31603](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.31603)
**TL;DR**: Two-stage unified video generator: train only a lightweight generator aligned with the understanding block, then hand off to a high-capacity pretrained generator at inference via Unified Progressive Frequency Bridging in a shared latent space. Introduces VR-Bench for reasoning-driven video generation; reports SOTA on VBench plus strong reasoning-aware generation.
**Why it matters**: Most "unified" video models trade visual fidelity for tractable training — this decouples the two and shows reasoning-to-video can scale without retraining a giant generator.

### 3. LongTraceRL: Learning Long-Context Reasoning from Search Agent Trajectories with Rubric Rewards
**Authors**: Nianyi Lin, Jiajie Zhang, Lei Hou, Juanzi Li (THU KEG)
**arXiv**: [2605.31584](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.31584)
**TL;DR**: Generates multi-hop questions via knowledge-graph random walks, then mines *tiered distractors* from real search-agent trajectories — documents the agent read but did not cite (hard) vs. documents that only appeared in result lists (easy) — yielding RL training contexts far more confusable than random sampling. Adds an entity-level rubric reward applied only to correct rollouts (positive-only) to give process supervision without reward hacking. Beats strong baselines on 5 long-context benchmarks across 4B–30B reasoning LLMs.
**Why it matters**: Moves RLVR beyond sparse outcome rewards by using one agent's trajectory to build the next agent's training data — a scalable recipe for long-context process supervision.

### 4. CHARM: Multimodal JEPA for Semantic Time-Series Embeddings
**Authors**: Utsav Dutta, Gerardo Pastrana, Sina Khoshfetrat Pakazad, Henrik Ohlsson
**arXiv**: [2605.31580](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.31580) (ICML 2026)
**TL;DR**: Channel-Aware Representation Model that injects per-channel textual descriptions into a channel-permutation-equivariant transformer, trained with a JEPA objective plus an informativeness/temporal-stability loss. Linear probes match SOTA on anomaly detection, classification, and forecasting; descriptions act as channel identifiers enabling cross-dataset transfer.
**Why it matters**: First convincing demonstration that JEPA-style latent prediction (not reconstruction) gives heterogeneous sensor data its own "tokenizer-like" semantic interface to LLMs.

### 5. TxFM: Effective Biological Representation Learning by Masking Gene Expression
**Authors**: Kian Kenyon-Dean, Alina Selega, Ihab Bendidi, Jordan M. Sorokin, Luca Bertinetto, David Errington, Hayley Donnella, Oren Kraus (Recursion)
**arXiv**: [2605.31562](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.31562) (ICLR 2026 Workshop)
**TL;DR**: Masked-autoencoder transformer for RNA-seq trained on a curated 1.4M-sample corpus (DiverseRNA-1.4M). Outperforms transcriptomic foundation models trained on atlas-scale corpora >100× larger, and finally beats linear baselines on inductive transfer — a bar that prior bio-FMs largely failed to clear.
**Why it matters**: Concrete counter-example to "more data wins": for noisy heterogeneous scientific corpora, curation + architecture choice beats raw scale by two orders of magnitude.
