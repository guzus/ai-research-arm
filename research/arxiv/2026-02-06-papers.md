# arXiv AI Research - 2026-02-06

## Highlights (Top 5 Must-Read)

### 1. Learning to Reason in 13 Parameters
**Authors**: John X. Morris, Niloofar Mireshghallah, Mark Ibrahim, Saeed Mahloujifar
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.04118
**TL;DR**: Introduces TinyLoRA, scaling low-rank adapters down to a single parameter. Achieves 91% accuracy on GSM8K with only 13 trained parameters (26 bytes) on Qwen2.5-8B. RL-trained models require 100-1000x fewer parameters than SFT to reach the same performance.
**Why it matters**: Fundamentally challenges assumptions about how many parameters need updating for reasoning — suggests RL unlocks capabilities already latent in pretrained weights.

### 2. Effective Frontiers: A Unification of Neural Scaling Laws
**Authors**: (See paper for full list)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.02593
**TL;DR**: Proposes a unified framework treating learning as progressive coverage of patterns from a Zipfian distribution, introducing the "Effective Frontier" threshold. Proves Kaplan and Chinchilla scaling laws are equilibrium solutions to the same constrained optimization under different active bottlenecks.
**Why it matters**: First principled unification of competing neural scaling law theories — provides a single framework to reason about capacity, data, and compute tradeoffs.

### 3. WideSeek-R1: Width Scaling for Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning
**Authors**: Zelai Xu et al.
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.04634
**TL;DR**: Introduces a lead-agent-subagent framework trained via multi-agent RL (MARL) for broad information seeking. WideSeek-R1-4B surpasses larger multi-agent systems (OWL-8B, MiroFlow-8B) by jointly optimizing orchestration and parallel execution across 20k tasks.
**Why it matters**: Demonstrates "width scaling" as a complementary dimension to depth scaling — multi-agent coordination trained end-to-end via MARL outperforms ad-hoc multi-agent pipelines.

### 4. DPPO: Rethinking the Trust Region in LLM Reinforcement Learning
**Authors**: Penghui Qi, Xiangxin Zhou, Zichen Liu, Tianyu Pang, Chao Du, Min Lin, Wee Sun Lee
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.04879
**TL;DR**: Shows PPO's ratio clipping is ill-suited for large LLM vocabularies — over-penalizing low-probability tokens while under-constraining high-probability ones. Proposes DPPO with principled divergence constraints (Total Variation/KL) using efficient Binary and Top-K approximations.
**Why it matters**: Addresses a fundamental architectural mismatch between PPO and LLMs, offering a drop-in replacement with superior stability and efficiency for RLHF.

### 5. CARE-RFT: Confidence-Anchored Reinforcement Finetuning for Reliable Reasoning
**Authors**: (See paper for full list)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.00085
**TL;DR**: Replaces standard reverse KL penalty with confidence-anchored skew reverse KL divergence during reinforcement finetuning. Matches unconstrained RFT reasoning performance while recovering base model trustworthiness and calibration.
**Why it matters**: Solves the critical reasoning-vs-trustworthiness tradeoff — unconstrained RFT amplifies hallucinations, but CARE-RFT preserves both reasoning gains and model reliability.
