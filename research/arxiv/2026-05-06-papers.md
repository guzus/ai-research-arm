# arXiv AI Research - 2026-05-06

## Highlights (Top 5 Must-Read)

### 1. MultiBreak: A Scalable and Diverse Multi-turn Jailbreak Benchmark for Evaluating LLM Safety
**Authors**: (anonymous submission, May 3, 2026)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.01687
**TL;DR**: Introduces 10,389 multi-turn adversarial prompts spanning 2,665 distinct harmful intents — by far the most diverse multi-turn jailbreak corpus to date. Provides a scalable construction pipeline and head-to-head safety evaluation across frontier models.
**Why it matters**: Single-turn safety benchmarks have saturated; this raises the bar to genuinely multi-turn adversarial settings where current alignment techniques are far weaker.

### 2. ContextualJailbreak: Evolutionary Red-Teaming via Simulated Conversational Priming
**Authors**: (arXiv 2605.02647, May 4, 2026)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.02647
**TL;DR**: An evolutionary red-teaming framework that uses simulated conversational priming to generate jailbreak prompts. Reports 100% attack success rate on several open models and 90% on gpt-oss:120B, beating baselines by 31–96 percentage points.
**Why it matters**: A new SOTA on jailbreak attack effectiveness, suggesting that current safety post-training transfers poorly to evolved multi-turn attack strategies.

### 3. Enhanced LLM Reasoning by Optimizing Reward Functions with Search-Driven Reinforcement Learning
**Authors**: (arXiv 2605.02073)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.02073
**TL;DR**: Treats the *reward function itself* as an object of optimization: a frontier LM proposes candidate rewards, which are screened via short 500-step GRPO runs on Llama-3.2-3B-Instruct with LoRA. The full pipeline runs ~40 hours on a single consumer GPU.
**Why it matters**: A practical recipe for closing the gap with frontier reasoning training without any manual reward engineering or large clusters.

### 4. LEAP: Layer-wise Exit-Aware Pretraining for Efficient Transformer Inference
**Authors**: (arXiv 2605.01058, May 1, 2026)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.01058
**TL;DR**: An auxiliary pretraining objective that reconciles the long-standing incompatibility between layer-aligned distillation and early-exit inference, enabling models that natively support adaptive-depth decoding.
**Why it matters**: Early-exit and distillation have been mutually exclusive in practice; LEAP unifies them at the pretraining stage, opening a cleaner path to efficient inference.

### 5. SpecKV: Adaptive Speculative Decoding with Compression-Aware Gamma Selection
**Authors**: (arXiv 2605.02888)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.02888
**TL;DR**: A lightweight controller that dynamically selects the speculation length γ at each step using signals extracted from the draft model itself, jointly accounting for KV-cache compression effects.
**Why it matters**: Most speculative-decoding deployments still use fixed γ; an adaptive, signal-driven controller yields free latency wins on existing model stacks.
