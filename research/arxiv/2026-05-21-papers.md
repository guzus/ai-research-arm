## Highlights (Top 5 Must-Read)

### 1. EMO: Pretraining Mixture of Experts for Emergent Modularity
**Authors**: Ryan Wang, Akshita Bhagia, Sewon Min (Ai2 / UW)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.06663
**TL;DR**: EMO is an MoE that induces *modularity* — independently usable,
composable expert subsets — without human-defined priors. By restricting
tokens within a document to a shared expert pool while letting different
documents draw different pools, coherent expert groups emerge from document
boundaries alone. A 1B-active / 14B-total model trained on 1T tokens keeps
~99% quality when only 25% of experts are retained (vs. standard MoEs that
collapse), and experts specialize *semantically* (math, code) rather than
syntactically.
**Why it matters**: Pruning/serving a fraction of experts with near-zero
loss turns MoE from a training trick into a deployable knob for cost and
on-device specialization.

### 2. Lying Is Just a Phase: The Hidden Alignment Transition in Language Model Scaling
**Authors**: Adil Amin et al.
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.18838
**TL;DR**: Measures the coupling between reasoning ability and truthfulness
across 63 base models from 16 families and finds a regime change invisible
to loss curves: below a family-dependent critical scale (~3.5B params),
capability and honesty *anticorrelate*; above it they *cooperate*.
Architecture, data curation, and training recipe each shift the critical
scale independently — distillation/architectural tricks let a 4B model
reach coupling characteristic of 13B+ standard-trained models.
**Why it matters**: Reframes "models get more honest as they scale" as a
phase transition you can move with recipe choices, with direct implications
for alignment of sub-critical small models.

### 3. Vision-OPD: Learning to See Fine Details for Multimodal LLMs via On-Policy Self-Distillation
**Authors**: Qianhao Yuan, Yaojie Lu, Hongyu Lin, Xianpei Han, Le Sun et al. (ISCAS)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.18740
**TL;DR**: MLLMs answer fine-grained questions from small but decisive
regions, yet their full-image policy underuses that evidence. Vision-OPD is
a regional-to-global *on-policy self-distillation* framework that transfers
the model's own privileged regional perception into its full-image policy —
no external teacher or extra labels.
**Why it matters**: A self-distillation route to fine-grained perception
that sidesteps the cost of a stronger teacher model, a recurring bottleneck
in MLLM training.

### 4. Spectral Progressive Diffusion for Efficient Image and Video Generation
**Authors**: (arXiv 2605.18736)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.18736
**TL;DR**: A training-free framework that progressively grows resolution
along the denoising trajectory of *pretrained* diffusion models, allocating
compute spectrally across the sampling schedule. Reports large speedups on
state-of-the-art pretrained image and video generators while preserving
visual quality.
**Why it matters**: Drop-in inference acceleration for existing diffusion
backbones — no retraining — extending the "spend compute where it matters"
theme into the frequency domain.

### 5. Code as Agent Harness
**Authors**: Xuying Ning, Katherine Tieu, Dongqi Fu, Tianxin Wei et al. (large collaboration)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.18747
**TL;DR**: Argues code is the right operational substrate for agents —
unifying reasoning, acting, environment modeling, and execution-based
verification under an executable, verifiable, stateful "agent harness."
A position-plus-framework synthesis of where agent scaffolding is heading.
**Why it matters**: As agent benchmarks proliferate, this consolidates the
case that durable agent capability comes from the harness (code execution +
state + verification), not just the base model.
