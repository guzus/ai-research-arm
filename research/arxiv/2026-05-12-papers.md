# arXiv AI Research - 2026-05-12

## Highlights (Top 5 Must-Read)

### 1. MISA: Mixture of Indexer Sparse Attention for Long-Context LLM Inference
**Authors**: (arXiv 2605.07363 contributors)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.07363
**TL;DR**: Proposes a drop-in replacement for DeepSeek Sparse Attention that treats indexer heads as a mixture-of-experts. A lightweight router picks a query-dependent subset of active heads and scores tokens with only those routed heads, cutting indexer compute while preserving recall quality.
**Why it matters**: Long-context efficiency is the binding constraint on agentic and multi-doc systems; treating the sparse-attention indexer as an MoE is a clean architectural move other labs will likely copy.

### 2. f-Divergence Regularized RLHF: Two Tales of Sampling and Unified Analyses
**Authors**: (arXiv 2605.06977 contributors)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.06977
**TL;DR**: Establishes the first performance bounds for online RLHF under general f-divergence regularization (not just KL), with O(log T) regret and O(1/T) sub-optimality gap. Unifies analyses across rejection-sampling and direct-optimization pipelines.
**Why it matters**: Most production alignment stacks default to KL because it was the only choice with theory; this opens the door to better-behaved regularizers without giving up provable guarantees.

### 3. When Does a Language Model Commit? A Finite-Answer Theory of Pre-Verbalization Commitment
**Authors**: Long Zhang et al.
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.06723
**TL;DR**: Introduces "finite-answer preference stabilization" to formally characterize the moment during chain-of-thought when an LM's eventual answer becomes locked in, even though reasoning continues. Provides probes to detect commitment from internal states before the answer is verbalized.
**Why it matters**: If commitment can be detected mid-trace, you can early-exit, redirect, or audit reasoning — a primitive for both efficiency and safety tooling.

### 4. Explaining and Preventing Alignment Collapse in Iterative RLHF
**Authors**: (arXiv 2605.04266 contributors)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.04266
**TL;DR**: Identifies a failure mode where iterative RLHF with a static reward model exhibits "alignment collapse" — the policy systematically exploits reward-model blind spots. Proposes foresighted policy optimization (FPO) that regularizes the policy's parameter-steering effect on subsequent reward-model updates.
**Why it matters**: Reward hacking has been described informally for years; formalizing it as a coupled policy/reward-model dynamics problem makes it tractable to fix.

### 5. Long Context Pre-Training with Lighthouse Attention
**Authors**: (arXiv 2605.06554 contributors)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.06554
**TL;DR**: A training-only hierarchical attention algorithm that wraps around standard scaled dot-product attention and can be cleanly removed near the end of pre-training. Avoids the quadratic memory bottleneck during long-context training without altering the deployed model.
**Why it matters**: Most efficient-attention proposals lock you into a deployment-time tradeoff. Lighthouse keeps training cheap while shipping vanilla SDPA, which is far easier to adopt.
