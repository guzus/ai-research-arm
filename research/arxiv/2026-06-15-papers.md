## Highlights (Top 5 Must-Read)

### 1. MoVerse: Real-Time Video World Modeling with Panoramic Gaussian Scaffold
**Authors**: MoVerse team (authors listed on arXiv)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.13376
**TL;DR**: MoVerse turns a single narrow-FOV image into an interactively
navigable scene rendered in real time. A bidirectional diffusion
*teacher* produces high-quality conditional renders and is distilled
into a causal autoregressive *student* for bounded-latency streaming,
with a panoramic Gaussian scaffold giving the world model persistent
spatial structure.
**Why it matters**: Pushes video world models from offline clip
generation toward real-time, navigable simulators — the substrate
embodied agents and interactive media will run on.

### 2. Latent Spatial Memory for Video World Models (Mirage)
**Authors**: Mirage authors (listed on arXiv)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.09828
**TL;DR**: Mirage equips a video world model with an explicit latent
spatial memory: latent tokens are lifted into 3D via depth-guided
back-projection to build the memory, then queried by synthesizing novel
views through direct latent-space warping — no per-frame re-rendering.
This preserves long-horizon spatial consistency as the camera revisits
earlier regions.
**Why it matters**: Memory, not just bigger context windows, is emerging
as the missing ingredient for spatially consistent, long-horizon video
world models.

### 3. SABER: Benchmarking Operational Safety of LLM Coding Agents in Stateful Project Workspaces
**Authors**: SABER authors (listed on arXiv)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.01317
**TL;DR**: SABER evaluates *environment-aware* operational safety by
placing models in realistic agent-style projects and scoring the final
environment state after a sequence of actions (not single-turn
refusals). Even the best model shows a >54% harmful-safety-violation
rate, and SABER categorizes violations by cause for per-model safety
profiling.
**Why it matters**: A sobering, action-grounded safety benchmark showing
current alignment is far from sufficient for autonomous coding agents
operating on real workspaces.

### 4. Spiking and Event-driven Neuromorphic Mamba Models for Efficient Speech Recognition
**Authors**: authors listed on arXiv
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.01135
**TL;DR**: Combines spiking, event-driven neuromorphic computation with
Mamba-style selective state-space sequence modeling for speech
recognition, replacing dense recurrence with sparse spike-event updates
to cut energy while retaining structured long-range memory.
**Why it matters**: A concrete bridge between linear-time SSMs and
neuromorphic hardware — energy-efficient sequence modeling for always-on
audio at the edge.

### 5. Making Foresight Actionable: Repurposing Representation Alignment in World Action Models
**Authors**: authors listed on arXiv
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.12217
**TL;DR**: World Action Models use video-generation backbones to imagine
future scene evolution before emitting control. This paper repurposes
representation alignment so the model's predictive ("foresight")
features are made directly actionable for control, tightening the loop
between visual prediction and robot manipulation policy.
**Why it matters**: Turns passive video-prediction representations into
useful control signals — a practical step for video-pretrained
manipulation policies.
