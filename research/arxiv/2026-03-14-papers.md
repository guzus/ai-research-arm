# arXiv AI Research - 2026-03-14

## Highlights (Top 5 Must-Read)

### 1. Why Is RLHF Alignment Shallow? A Gradient Analysis
**Authors**: Robin Young (University of Cambridge)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.04851
**TL;DR**: Proves mathematically that gradient-based alignment (RLHF/DPO) inherently concentrates updates on early token positions where harm is decided, with zero gradient signal beyond the "harm horizon." This explains why aligned models can be easily jailbroken via prefilling and why KL divergence from base models concentrates on early tokens.
**Why it matters**: Provides the first rigorous theoretical explanation for why safety alignment is shallow, with direct implications for the entire alignment field.

### 2. SWE-ABS: Adversarial Benchmark Strengthening Exposes Inflated Success Rates
**Authors**: (Multiple authors)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.00520
**TL;DR**: Shows that 1 in 5 "solved" patches from top-30 SWE-Bench agents are semantically incorrect, passing only due to weak test suites. Their adversarial test augmentation framework drops the top agent's score from 78.80% to 62.20%, causing major leaderboard reshuffling.
**Why it matters**: Reveals that code agent benchmarks significantly overstate real capability, reshaping how we should evaluate AI coding systems.

### 3. What Do Visual Tokens Really Encode? Uncovering Sparsity and Redundancy in MLLMs
**Authors**: Yingqi Fan et al.
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.00510
**TL;DR**: Introduces EmbedLens, a probing framework revealing that visual tokens in multimodal LLMs partition into sink, dead, and alive categories -- with only ~60% of tokens carrying image-specific meaning. This semantic sparsity suggests major efficiency gains are possible.
**Why it matters**: Opens a new direction for dramatically compressing visual inputs to multimodal models without losing performance.

### 4. VisRef: Visual Refocusing while Thinking Improves Test-Time Scaling in Multi-Modal Large Reasoning Models
**Authors**: Soumya Suvra Ghosal, Youngeun Kim, Zhuowei Li et al.
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.00207
**TL;DR**: Proposes visual refocusing during chain-of-thought reasoning in multimodal models, allowing models to re-attend to relevant image regions while thinking. Accepted to CVPR 2026 and demonstrates improved test-time scaling for visual reasoning tasks.
**Why it matters**: Bridges test-time compute scaling (a hot topic in LLMs) to the multimodal domain with a practical mechanism.

### 5. LiveAgentBench: Comprehensive Benchmarking of Agentic Systems Across 104 Real-World Challenges
**Authors**: (Multiple authors)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.02586
**TL;DR**: A benchmark of 104 real-world scenarios requiring tool use, where tasks must satisfy non-retrievability (can't be solved by RAG alone) and tool dependency. Uses Social Perception-Driven Data Generation to ensure real-world relevance and verifiability.
**Why it matters**: Addresses critical gaps in agent evaluation by requiring genuine tool use and real-world problem solving rather than synthetic tasks.
