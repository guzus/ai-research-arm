# arXiv AI Research - 2026-05-07

## Highlights (Top 5 Must-Read)

### 1. Why Do LLMs Struggle in Strategic Play? Broken Links Between Observations, Beliefs, and Actions
**Authors**: (Llama 3.1, Qwen3, gpt-oss study authors)
**arXiv**: [2605.00226](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.00226)
**TL;DR**: Identifies two fundamental gaps inside LLMs playing incomplete-information games: (1) an observation→belief gap where internal beliefs are more accurate than verbal reports but degrade with multi-hop reasoning and exhibit primacy/recency bias; (2) a belief→action gap where steering hidden states toward correct beliefs barely changes outputs, with a persistent first-item bias in the action space.
**Why it matters**: Combines game theory with mechanistic interpretability to show that strategic-reasoning failures are an internal wiring problem, not just a capability gap — a concrete diagnostic agenda for agent reliability.

### 2. GR-Ben: A General Reasoning Benchmark for Evaluating Process Reward Models
**Authors**: GR-Ben team
**arXiv**: [2605.01203](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.01203)
**TL;DR**: Introduces the first cross-domain process-level benchmark (science + logic, 9 subdomains) for PRMs, evaluating 22 PRMs/LLMs. Existing PRMs are markedly weaker outside math; PRMs miss knowledge errors while LLMs miss computational errors — they fail in complementary ways.
**Why it matters**: Test-time scaling depends on reliable PRMs; this benchmark exposes that today's PRMs do not generalize beyond mathematics.

### 3. Enhancing Agent Safety Judgment: ROME + ARISE for Deceptive OOD Scenarios
**Authors**: Zuoyu Zhang, Yancheng Zhu
**arXiv**: [2605.03242](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.03242)
**TL;DR**: ROME is a red-team orchestrated multi-agent pipeline that rewrites 100 unsafe source trajectories into 300 deceptive challenge instances (contextual ambiguity, implicit risk, shortcut decisions). ARISE adds inference-time analogical retrieval of ReAct trajectories to recover safety-judgment quality without retraining.
**Why it matters**: Frontier agents look safe on explicit-risk benchmarks but degrade sharply on hidden-risk variants — the paper offers both the diagnostic and a training-free mitigation.

### 4. RSAT: Structured Attribution Makes Small Language Models Faithful Table Reasoners
**Authors**: Jugal Gajjar, Kamalasankari Subramaniakuppusamy (GWU)
**arXiv**: [2605.00199](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.00199)
**TL;DR**: Two-phase recipe (SFT on JSON traces + GRPO with NLI-faithfulness reward) trains 1B–8B Qwen 2.5 / Llama 3 models to produce step-by-step table reasoning with cell-level citations. Faithfulness improves 3.7× over SFT (0.224→0.826) with citation validity 0.992. Post-hoc attribution collapses below 13% format success — attribution must be co-trained with reasoning, not bolted on.
**Why it matters**: A reproducible path to grounded, auditable reasoning at sub-8B scale, with a clean negative result against retrofit-style citation methods.

### 5. CgRAG: Chain-of-Question Guided Retrieval-Augmented Generation for Multimodal LLMs
**Authors**: CgRAG authors
**arXiv**: [2605.03790](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.03790)
**TL;DR**: CoVQD prompting fuses Chain-of-Thought with Visual Question Decomposition to guide retrieval; the resulting CgRAG framework feeds MLLMs more coherent external knowledge for KB-VQA. Gains reported on E-VQA, InfoSeek, and OKVQA.
**Why it matters**: Practical recipe for stitching structured visual reasoning into RAG, addressing brittleness of single-shot retrieval in MLLMs.
