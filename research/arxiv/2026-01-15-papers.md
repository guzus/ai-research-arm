# arXiv AI Research - 2026-01-15

## Highlights (Top 5 Must-Read)

### 1. Benchmark²: Systematic Evaluation of LLM Benchmarks
**Authors**: Qi Qian, Chengsong Huang, et al.
**arXiv**: [2601.03986](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.03986)
**TL;DR**: Proposes a comprehensive framework to evaluate benchmark quality itself, with three key metrics: Cross-Benchmark Ranking Consistency, Discriminability Score, and Capability Alignment Deviation. Evaluated 11 LLMs across 15 benchmarks spanning mathematics, reasoning, and knowledge domains.
**Why it matters**: Addresses the critical need to assess whether our evaluation tools are reliable as LLMs become increasingly sophisticated.

### 2. From Sight to Insight: Improving Visual Reasoning via Reinforcement Learning
**Authors**: Multiple authors
**arXiv**: [2601.00215](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.00215)
**TL;DR**: Uses reinforcement learning to elicit reasoning chains before generating final answers in multimodal LLMs, addressing the problem that MLLMs often generate reasoning that lacks integration of visual information, limiting their ability to solve visual puzzles and perception-demanding problems.
**Why it matters**: Bridges the gap between visual perception and reasoning in multimodal models through RL-based training.

### 3. TreePS-RAG: Tree-based Process Supervision for RL in Agentic RAG
**Authors**: Multiple authors
**arXiv**: [2601.06922](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.06922)
**TL;DR**: Formulates question answering as multi-step interaction between reasoning and information retrieval, presenting an online, tree-based RL framework that enables step-wise credit assignment while retaining standard outcome-only rewards.
**Why it matters**: Advances agentic RAG systems by combining tree-based reasoning with reinforcement learning for better multi-step question answering.

### 4. Where Does Vision Meet Language? Understanding Visual Fusion in MLLMs
**Authors**: Multiple authors
**arXiv**: [2601.08151](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.08151)
**TL;DR**: Examines how MLLMs internally integrate visual and textual information through systematic layer-wise masking analysis across multiple architectures, revealing where and how vision-language fusion occurs.
**Why it matters**: Provides crucial insights into the internal mechanisms of multimodal models, enabling better architecture design.

### 5. Geometric and Dynamic Scaling in Deep Transformers
**Authors**: Multiple authors
**arXiv**: [2601.01014](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.01014)
**TL;DR**: Addresses how scaling Transformer architectures to extreme depth often leads to rank collapse, arguing this is fundamentally a geometric problem and proposing solutions.
**Why it matters**: Critical for understanding and overcoming limitations in scaling transformers to greater depths.
