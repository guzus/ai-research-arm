# arXiv AI Research - 2026-03-16

## Highlights (Top 5 Must-Read)

### 1. ∇-Reasoner: LLM Reasoning via Test-Time Gradient Descent in Latent Space
**Authors**: Peihao Wang et al.
**arXiv**: [2603.04948](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.04948)
**TL;DR**: Introduces Differentiable Textual Optimization (DTO), which applies gradient descent over token logits at inference time using reward model signals, enabling continuous optimization of reasoning chains without modifying model weights. Incorporates rejection sampling and acceleration for robust decoding.
**Why it matters**: First practical framework for gradient-based test-time reasoning optimization in the output space — a paradigm shift from discrete search methods like beam search or best-of-N sampling.

### 2. IsoCompute Playbook: Optimally Scaling Sampling Compute for LLM RL
**Authors**: Zhoujun Cheng, Yutao Xie, Yuxiao Qu, Amrith Setlur, Shibo Hao et al.
**arXiv**: [2603.12151](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.12151)
**TL;DR**: Establishes compute-optimal scaling laws for on-policy RL in LLMs, showing that the optimal number of parallel rollouts per problem increases predictably with compute budget then saturates. Validated across base models and data distributions.
**Why it matters**: Extends Chinchilla-style scaling laws to the RL post-training regime — critical for efficient RLHF/RLAIF training of frontier models.

### 3. The Synthetic Web: Adversarially-Curated Mini-Internets for Diagnosing Epistemic Weaknesses of Language Agents
**Authors**: Shrey Shah, Levent Ozgur (Microsoft)
**arXiv**: [2603.00801](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.00801)
**TL;DR**: Creates procedurally-generated web environments with ground-truth credibility labels to test agent robustness. Injecting a single high-plausibility misinformation article causes catastrophic accuracy collapse in six frontier models despite access to truthful sources.
**Why it matters**: Exposes fundamental limitations in how current LLM agents handle conflicting information — essential reading for anyone deploying search-augmented AI systems.

### 4. GR-SAP: Generative Replay for Safety Alignment Preservation during Fine-Tuning
**Authors**: Zhouxiang Fang et al.
**arXiv**: [2603.10243](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.10243)
**TL;DR**: Proposes a continual-learning-inspired framework that synthesizes domain-specific alignment data from LLMs and integrates them during fine-tuning to preserve safety alignment — without requiring access to original alignment datasets.
**Why it matters**: Solves a critical practical problem: maintaining safety alignment when fine-tuning open-weight models without access to proprietary alignment data.

### 5. General AgentBench: Benchmark Test-Time Scaling of General LLM Agents
**Authors**: Multiple authors
**arXiv**: [2602.18998](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.18998)
**TL;DR**: Unified benchmark evaluating LLM agents across search, coding, reasoning, and tool-use. Reveals substantial performance degradation when moving from domain-specific to general-agent settings, and that neither scaling methodology yields effective improvements in practice.
**Why it matters**: Challenges the assumption that test-time compute scaling straightforwardly improves agent capabilities — a reality check for the agentic AI hype.
