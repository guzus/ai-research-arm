# arXiv AI Research - 2026-01-30

## Highlights (Top 5 Must-Read)

### 1. [Visual Generation Unlocks Human-Like Reasoning through Multimodal World Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.19834)
**Authors**: Jialong Wu, Xiaoying Zhang, Hongyi Yuan, Xiangcheng Zhang, Tianhao Huang, Changjing He, Chaoyi Deng, Renrui Zhang, Youbin Wu, Mingsheng Long (Tsinghua / ByteDance Seed)
**arXiv**: [2601.19834](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.19834)
**TL;DR**: First principled study of when and how visual generation benefits reasoning from a world-model perspective. Shows that unified multimodal models (UMMs) using visual generation as a reasoning pathway unlock human-like physical and spatial intelligence that verbal-only chain-of-thought cannot achieve.
**Why it matters**: Establishes a theoretical and empirical foundation for multimodal reasoning beyond text, opening a new paradigm for world-model-based AI.

### 2. [LongCat-Flash-Thinking-2601 Technical Report](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.16725)
**Authors**: Meituan LongCat Team (161+ authors)
**arXiv**: [2601.16725](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.16725)
**TL;DR**: 560B-parameter open-source MoE reasoning model (27B active) achieving SOTA among open-source models on agentic benchmarks. Introduces domain-parallel expert training, Heavy Thinking Mode for test-time scaling, and noise-robust agentic training. Scores 73.1% BrowseComp, 88.2% tau2-Bench, 77.7% RWSearch.
**Why it matters**: Sets a new open-source bar for agentic reasoning with a comprehensive training framework spanning pre-training to post-training, released under MIT license.

### 3. [From RLHF to Direct Alignment: A Theoretical Unification of Preference Learning for LLMs](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.06108)
**Authors**: Tarun Raheja et al.
**arXiv**: [2601.06108](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.06108)
**TL;DR**: Provides a theoretical unification of the proliferating alignment methods (DPO, IPO, KTO, SimPO, etc.), showing they reduce to principled choices along three axes: preference model, regularization mechanism, and data distribution. Explains why DPO requires diverse data, why SimPO works, and how overoptimization arises.
**Why it matters**: Essential reading for anyone training aligned LLMs -- brings clarity to a fragmented landscape and provides actionable method-selection guidance.

### 4. [MiMo-V2-Flash Technical Report](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.02780)
**Authors**: Xiaomi LLM-Core Team (Bangjun Xiao et al., 125 authors)
**arXiv**: [2601.02780](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.02780)
**TL;DR**: 309B-parameter MoE model (15B active) rivaling DeepSeek-V3.2 and Kimi-K2 using 1/2 to 1/3 of their parameters. Introduces Multi-Teacher On-Policy Distillation (MOPD) and achieves 2.6x decoding speedup via Multi-Token Prediction speculative decoding. Scores 73.4% SWE-Bench Verified.
**Why it matters**: Demonstrates that smaller, efficient MoE architectures with clever distillation can match frontier models, pushing the efficiency frontier for open-source.

### 5. [RLVRR: From Verifiable Dot to Reward Chain for RL of Open-ended Generation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.18533)
**Authors**: Yuxin Jiang, Yufei Wang, Qiyuan Zhang, Xingshan Zeng, Liangyou Li, Jierun Chen, Chaofan Tao, Haoli Bai, Lifeng Shang
**arXiv**: [2601.18533](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.18533) (Published at ICLR 2026)
**TL;DR**: Extends reinforcement learning with verifiable rewards (RLVR) from reasoning tasks to open-ended generation. RLVRR decomposes rewards into content (keyword verification) and style (LLM-based verification) chains, outperforming SFT trained with 10x more data.
**Why it matters**: Bridges the gap between verifiable RL (math/code) and general-purpose alignment, published at ICLR 2026 with code released.
