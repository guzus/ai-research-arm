# arXiv AI Research - 2026-02-16

## Highlights (Top 5 Must-Read)

### 1. LoopFormer: Elastic-Depth Looped Transformers for Latent Reasoning via Shortcut Modulation
**Authors**: (Multiple authors, submitted to OpenReview/ICML track)
**arXiv**: [2602.11451](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.11451)
**TL;DR**: Introduces a looped Transformer that can flexibly adapt its computational depth at inference time via a shortcut-consistency training protocol. Each loop step is conditioned on time and step size, allowing coarser trajectories to approximate fine-grained ones with fewer steps.
**Why it matters**: Enables compute-budgeted inference (elastic depth) without retraining — a major step toward practical adaptive-compute language models.

### 2. Agent World Model: Infinity Synthetic Environments for Agentic Reinforcement Learning
**Authors**: Snowflake Labs team
**arXiv**: [2602.10090](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.10090)
**TL;DR**: Proposes a fully synthetic environment generation pipeline scaling to 1,000 code-driven environments with rich toolsets (35 tools/env on average). Large-scale RL for multi-turn tool-use agents trained exclusively in synthetic environments shows strong out-of-distribution generalization across three benchmarks.
**Why it matters**: Solves the critical bottleneck of environment scarcity for training agentic AI — training in synthetic envs generalizes to real benchmarks.

### 3. RLCER: Reinforcing Chain-of-Thought Reasoning with Self-Evolving Rubrics
**Authors**: Leheng Sheng, Wenchang Ma, Ruixin Hong, Xiang Wang, An Zhang, Tat-Seng Chua
**arXiv**: [2602.10885](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.10885)
**TL;DR**: Goes beyond rewarding what LLMs answer to explicitly rewarding how they think. RLCER uses self-proposed, self-evolving rubrics to provide CoT supervision without human annotation, outperforming outcome-centric RLVR approaches.
**Why it matters**: First method to autonomously reward reasoning quality (not just final answers) via self-evolving rubrics — enables scaling RL for reasoning without verifiers.

### 4. TaperNorm: Gated Removal of Normalization in Transformers
**Authors**: (Multiple authors)
**arXiv**: [2602.10408](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.10408)
**TL;DR**: Introduces TaperNorm, a drop-in RMSNorm/LayerNorm replacement that smoothly tapers normalization during training, eventually eliminating per-token statistics entirely. At inference, normalization layers fold into adjacent linear projections for up to 1.22× throughput improvement.
**Why it matters**: Identifies scale anchoring as the key role of normalization and provides a practical path to norm-free Transformers with real inference speedups.

### 5. Parallel Track Transformers: Enabling Fast GPU Inference with Reduced Synchronization
**Authors**: Chong Wang, Nan Du, Tom Gunter, Tao Lei, et al. (Apple)
**arXiv**: [2602.07306](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.07306)
**TL;DR**: Restructures Transformer computation to minimize cross-device dependencies, achieving up to 16× reduction in synchronization operations. Integrated into TensorRT-LLM and vLLM, yielding 15-30% reduced TTFT and up to 31.9% higher throughput.
**Why it matters**: Apple's architectural redesign for inference parallelism delivers practical serving speedups on production stacks.
