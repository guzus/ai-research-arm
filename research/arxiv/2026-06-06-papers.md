## Highlights (Top 5 Must-Read)

### 1. Pretraining Recurrent Networks without Recurrence
**Authors**: Akarsh Kumar, Phillip Isola (MIT)
**arXiv**: [2606.06479](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.06479)
**TL;DR**: Training RNNs with backpropagation through time is sequential and suffers vanishing/exploding gradients over long sequences. The authors propose a scheme that pretrains recurrent networks *without* unrolling recurrence in time, recovering long-range credit assignment while restoring parallelism.
**Why it matters**: A credible path to training recurrent/state-space models at scale could reopen RNNs as a competitive efficiency-first alternative to attention for long-context modeling.

### 2. You Only Index Once: Cross-Layer Sparse Attention with Shared Routing
**Authors**: Yutao Sun, Yanqi Zhang, Li Dong, Jianyong Wang, Furu Wei (Microsoft Research)
**arXiv**: [2606.06467](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.06467)
**TL;DR**: Sparse-attention methods trade quality for speed; structured block-sparse variants accelerate but degrade quality. "Index once" computes a shared token-routing index a single time and reuses it across all layers, cutting the per-layer selection overhead that bottlenecks reasoning-heavy long-context decoding.
**Why it matters**: Long chain-of-thought decoding is increasingly the dominant cost in deployed reasoning models; amortizing the sparse-attention routing across layers directly attacks that bottleneck.

### 3. Latent Reasoning with Normalizing Flows
**Authors**: Guancheng Tu, Xiangjun Fu, Suhao Yu, Yao Tang, Haoqiang Kang, Lianhui Qin, et al.
**arXiv**: [2606.06447](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.06447)
**TL;DR**: Textual chain-of-thought forces every reasoning step through a discrete, serial token stream. This work moves the intermediate computation into a continuous latent space modeled by normalizing flows, letting the model reason in parallel without verbalizing each step.
**Why it matters**: Latent reasoning is one of 2026's hottest directions; using normalizing flows gives a tractable, invertible density over reasoning states rather than opaque cache edits.

### 4. MLEvolve: A Self-Evolving Framework for Automated Machine Learning Algorithm Discovery
**Authors**: Shangheng Du, Xiangchao Yan, Jinxin Shi, Zongsheng Cao, Shiyang Feng, Zichen Liang, et al.
**arXiv**: [2606.06473](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.06473)
**TL;DR**: Existing ML-engineering agents suffer from inter-branch information isolation, memoryless search, and no hierarchical control. MLEvolve adds cross-branch memory and hierarchical control so an LLM agent can sustain self-evolution over long-horizon algorithm-discovery tasks.
**Why it matters**: Sustained self-improvement on open-ended scientific/ML-engineering tasks is the key missing capability separating today's agents from autonomous researchers.

### 5. From Reward-Hack Activations to Agentic Risk States: Context-Calibrated Mechanistic Monitoring in LLM Agents
**Authors**: Patrick Wilhelm, Odej Kao
**arXiv**: [2606.06223](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.06223)
**TL;DR**: ReAct-style agents are instrumented with activation-based reward-hacking probes in gameable ALFWorld and WebShop environments. The paper shows that internal "reward-hack" activations can be calibrated against environment context to flag emerging agentic risk states during a rollout.
**Why it matters**: As agents act on real credentials and infrastructure, mechanistic monitors that read internal state — rather than only outputs — are a promising line of defense against reward hacking.
