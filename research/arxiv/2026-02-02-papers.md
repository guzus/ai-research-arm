# arXiv AI Research - 2026-02-02

## Highlights (Top 5 Must-Read)

### 1. Scaling Embeddings Outperforms Scaling Experts in Language Models
**Authors**: Hong Liu, Jiaqi Zhang, Chao Wang, Xing Hu, Linkun Lyu, Jiaqi Sun, Xurui Yang, Bo Wang, Fengcun Li, Yulei Qian, Lingtong Si, Yerui Sun, Rumei Li, Peng Pei, Yuchen Xie, Xunliang Cai (Meituan LongCat Team)
**arXiv**: [2601.21204](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.21204)
**TL;DR**: Introduces N-gram embedding scaling as an alternative to MoE expert scaling. Their LongCat-Flash-Lite (68.5B params, ~3B activated) allocates 30B+ parameters to embeddings and surpasses MoE baselines, achieving 54.4% on SWE-Bench with superior inference efficiency via O(1) embedding lookups.
**Why it matters**: Challenges the dominant MoE scaling paradigm by showing embeddings offer a more efficient sparsity dimension with lower I/O overhead.

### 2. Hybrid Linear Attention Done Right: Efficient Distillation and Effective Architectures for Extremely Long Contexts
**Authors**: Yingfa Chen, Zhen Leng Thai, Zihan Zhou, Zhu Zhang, Xingyu Shen, Shuo Wang, Chaojun Xiao, Xu Han, Zhiyuan Liu
**arXiv**: [2601.22156](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.22156)
**TL;DR**: Presents HALO (distillation pipeline) and HypeNet (hybrid RNN-attention architecture with novel position encoding) that converts Qwen3 models into efficient hybrids using only 2.3B tokens (<0.01% of pretraining data). HypeNet matches Transformer quality while enabling 1M+ context lengths where Transformers OOM.
**Why it matters**: Makes long-context hybrid architectures accessible without expensive pretraining from scratch, democratizing efficient long-context inference.

### 3. The Depth Delusion: Why Transformers Should Be Wider, Not Deeper
**Authors**: Md Muhtasim Munif Fahim, Md Rezaul Karim
**arXiv**: [2601.20994](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.20994)
**TL;DR**: Proposes architecture-conditioned scaling laws showing optimal width grows 2.8x faster than depth (W* ~ C^0.34 vs D* ~ C^0.12). Identifies a critical depth beyond which adding layers increases loss despite more parameters. Validated across 30 architectures from 17M to 7B params (R²=0.922).
**Why it matters**: Overturns the "deeper is better" assumption in transformer design, with direct implications for how next-generation models should be shaped.

### 4. Self-Compression of Chain-of-Thought via Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning
**Authors**: Yiqun Chen et al.
**arXiv**: [2601.21919](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.21919)
**TL;DR**: Proposes SCMA, a multi-agent RL framework with Reasoning, Segmentation, and Scoring agents that selectively compress redundant reasoning chains while preserving essential logic. Significantly reduces CoT length while maintaining reasoning performance, unlike crude length penalties.
**Why it matters**: Addresses the critical deployment bottleneck of verbose reasoning in LRMs, enabling practical use of chain-of-thought models in latency-sensitive applications.

### 5. Meta Context Engineering via Agentic Skill Evolution
**Authors**: Haoran Ye, Xuning He, Vincent Arak, Haonan Dong, Guojie Song
**arXiv**: [2601.21557](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.21557)
**TL;DR**: Introduces MCE, a bi-level optimization framework where a meta-agent evolves context engineering skills while a base-agent optimizes context artifacts. Achieves 5.6-53.8% improvement over SOTA context engineering methods. MCE-enhanced general LLMs outperform domain-specific fine-tuned models.
**Why it matters**: Demonstrates that learned context can substitute for expensive domain-specific fine-tuning, pointing to a new paradigm for LLM adaptation.
