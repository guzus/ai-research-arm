# arXiv AI Research - 2026-05-05

## Highlights (Top 5 Must-Read)

### 1. State Stream Transformer (SST) V2: Parallel Training of Nonlinear Recurrence for Latent Space Reasoning
**Authors**: anonymous (arXiv preprint)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.00206
**TL;DR**: Introduces an FFN-driven nonlinear recurrence at each decoder layer where latent states are streamed horizontally across the full sequence via a learned blend, enabling parameter-efficient continuous-latent reasoning. The same mechanism supports per-position latent deliberation at inference, dedicating extra FLOPs to abstract reasoning before token commit.
**Why it matters**: A clean, parallel-trainable recurrence is one of the most plausible paths beyond pure attention for long-horizon reasoning without exploding compute.

### 2. Uniform-Correct Policy Optimization: Breaking RLVR's Indifference to Diversity
**Authors**: anonymous (arXiv preprint)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.00365
**TL;DR**: Identifies that RLVR raises Pass@1 but collapses Pass@K because probability mass concentrates on a narrow set of correct solutions. UCPO redistributes mass uniformly across verified-correct trajectories, recovering coverage without sacrificing accuracy.
**Why it matters**: Diversity collapse is the silent failure mode of frontier RL post-training; this is a clean, drop-in fix.

### 3. ResRL: Boosting LLM Reasoning via Negative Sample Projection Residual Reinforcement Learning
**Authors**: Zihan Lin, Xiaohan Wang, Jie Cao, Jiajun Chai, Li Wang, Xiaodong Lu, Wei Lin, Ran He, Guojun Yin
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.00380
**TL;DR**: Projects negative-token hidden representations onto an SVD-based low-rank positive subspace and uses the residuals to modulate negative gradients. Beats NSR by 9.4% Avg@16 and 7.0% Pass@128 on math and outperforms across 12 benchmarks (math, code, agents, function calling).
**Why it matters**: Solves the "RLVR kills diversity" problem from the gradient side — complementary to UCPO above and easy to combine.

### 4. AEM: Adaptive Entropy Modulation for Multi-Turn Agentic Reinforcement Learning
**Authors**: anonymous (arXiv preprint)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.00425
**TL;DR**: A supervision-free credit-assignment method that adaptively modulates entropy dynamics during multi-turn agent RL, avoiding both premature collapse and runaway exploration without per-step rewards.
**Why it matters**: Multi-turn agent training is bottlenecked by credit assignment; supervision-free entropy control is one of the few scalable knobs that work.

### 5. Borrowed Geometry: Computational Reuse of Frozen Text-Pretrained Transformer Weights Across Modalities
**Authors**: anonymous (arXiv preprint)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.00333
**TL;DR**: Shows that frozen text-pretrained transformer weights carry geometry that transfers to non-text modalities with only thin adapters, suggesting language pretraining produces a near-modality-agnostic computational substrate.
**Why it matters**: If the geometry is truly shared, multimodal stacks can be far thinner than today's adapter-heavy or jointly-trained recipes.
