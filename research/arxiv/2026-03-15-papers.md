# arXiv AI Research - 2026-03-15

## Highlights (Top 5 Must-Read)

### 1. [InternVL-U: Democratizing Unified Multimodal Models for Understanding, Reasoning, Generation and Editing](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.09877)
**Authors**: OpenGVLab Team
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.09877
**TL;DR**: A lightweight 4B-parameter unified multimodal model that handles understanding, reasoning, generation, and editing within a single framework. Despite its small size, it consistently outperforms unified baselines with 3x+ larger scales such as BAGEL (14B) on various generation and editing tasks.
**Why it matters**: Demonstrates that unified multimodal capabilities don't require massive scale — a major step toward accessible, efficient multimodal AI.

### 2. [Reasoning Theater: Disentangling Model Beliefs from Chain-of-Thought](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.05488)
**Authors**: Siddharth Boppana et al.
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.05488
**TL;DR**: Provides evidence of "performative" chain-of-thought in reasoning models, where models become confident in their answer early but continue generating tokens without revealing internal beliefs. Reveals a fundamental gap between visible reasoning traces and actual model computation.
**Why it matters**: Challenges the assumption that CoT faithfully reflects model reasoning — critical implications for interpretability and safety.

### 3. [IsoCompute Playbook: Optimally Scaling Sampling Compute for LLM RL](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.12151)
**Authors**: IsoCompute Team
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.12151
**TL;DR**: Establishes compute-optimal allocation rules for on-policy RL post-training of LLMs, framing scaling as optimization over parallel rollouts, problems per batch, and update steps. Validated across base models and data distributions.
**Why it matters**: First prescriptive scaling laws for RL post-training — essential guidance as RLVR becomes the dominant paradigm for reasoning model training.

### 4. [Omni-Diffusion: Unified Multimodal Understanding and Generation with Masked Discrete Diffusion](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.06577)
**Authors**: Omni-Diffusion Team
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.06577
**TL;DR**: First any-to-any multimodal language model built entirely on mask-based discrete diffusion, unifying understanding and generation across text, speech, and images. Outperforms or matches existing multimodal systems that handle two or more modalities.
**Why it matters**: Novel architecture that replaces autoregressive generation with discrete diffusion for multimodal models — a potential paradigm shift.

### 5. [SafeDPO: Safety Alignment Without Reward Models](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2505.20065)
**Authors**: SafeDPO Team (Published at ICLR 2026)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/pdf/2505.20065
**TL;DR**: Revisits the safety alignment objective, derives a closed-form optimal policy, and proposes SafeDPO — a lightweight method that eliminates the need for reward models, cost models, and online sampling, requiring only preference data and safety indicators.
**Why it matters**: Makes safe alignment dramatically simpler and cheaper — published at ICLR 2026 as a significant advance in practical safety methods.
