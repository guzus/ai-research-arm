## Highlights (Top 5 Must-Read)

### 1. How Transparent is DiffusionGemma?
**Authors**: Joshua Engels, Callum McDougall, Bilal Chughtai, ... Rohin Shah, Neel Nanda (Google DeepMind)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.20560
**TL;DR**: A large interpretability team decomposes "transparency" into *variable* transparency (can we read intermediate states?) and *algorithmic* transparency (can we reconstruct the process?). Diffusion LLMs naively look 28.6× less transparent than autoregressive Gemma 4 because of opaque serial depth, but mapping the inter-step information flow through an interpretable token bottleneck collapses that gap to 1.1×. They surface diffusion-specific phenomena (non-chronological reasoning, token/sequence "smearing") and find DiffusionGemma stays similarly *monitorable* to autoregressive Gemma 4.
**Why it matters**: As diffusion language models gain traction, this is the first serious answer to whether their latent-space computation is a safety/interpretability liability — and the answer is largely "no."

### 2. Current World Models Lack a Persistent State Core (WRBench)
**Authors**: Jinpeng Lu, Dexu Zhu, Haoyuan Shi, et al.
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.20545
**TL;DR**: Introduces WRBench, the first diagnostic benchmark treating camera motion as an *intervention on observability*. Across 9,600 videos from 23 models, every current world model behaves like a "tracking shot" — when a target leaves and re-enters frame, it resumes in the state it was abandoned rather than advancing the event while unobserved. The failure persists across control paradigms, model families, and scale.
**Why it matters**: Reframes world-model evaluation away from frame fidelity toward whether a model maintains an evolving internal physical state — a prerequisite many treat as the road to AGI.

### 3. SSD: Spatially Speculative Decoding Accelerates Autoregressive Image Generation
**Authors**: Shilong Xiang, Zirui Zhang, Lijun Yu, Chengzhi Mao
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.20543
**TL;DR**: Autoregressive image models flatten 2D images into 1D token sequences, discarding spatial locality. SSD predicts the adjacent horizontal token *and* the token directly below simultaneously, exploiting 2D correlation to break the memory wall — up to 13.3× faster generation with maintained fidelity on DPG-Bench and GenEval.
**Why it matters**: A clean architectural insight (respect image geometry) yielding order-of-magnitude inference speedups, pointing toward real-time high-resolution autoregressive generation.

### 4. HumanScale: Egocentric Human Video Can Outperform Real-Robot Data for Embodied Pretraining
**Authors**: Juncheng Ma, Jianxin Bi, Yufan Deng, et al.
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.20521
**TL;DR**: A controlled study comparing egocentric human video vs. teleoperated real-robot trajectories as pretraining data under fixed protocols. With equal data, egocentric-pretrained models hit 24% lower validation loss and 52.5%/90% higher in-/out-of-distribution success rates — human video is not just a substitute but can be superior.
**Why it matters**: Real-robot data is the dominant but bottlenecked source for embodied foundation models; this validates a far cheaper, more diverse scaling paradigm.

### 5. The Token Is a Group Element: Lie-Algebra Attention over Matrix Lie Groups
**Authors**: Przemyslaw Musialski
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.20547
**TL;DR**: A novel attention construction where each token is a bare element of a matrix Lie group. The attention score is the closed-form algebra norm of relative pose (no learned kernel, no irreps/Clebsch-Gordan machinery), making equivariance tautological and reaching affine full-frame groups prior methods exclude. Matches/outperforms learned MLP kernels on SE(2)/SO(3)/Aff(2) with 50–80× fewer score parameters.
**Why it matters**: A first-principles rethink of geometric attention that is parameter-efficient and covers group families standard equivariant methods can't handle.
