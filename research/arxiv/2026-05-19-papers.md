# arXiv AI Research - 2026-05-19

## Highlights (Top 5 Must-Read)

### 1. Deep Reasoning in General Purpose Agents via Structured Meta-Cognition
**Authors**: (see arXiv listing)
**arXiv**: [2605.11388](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.11388)
**TL;DR**: Introduces "Deep Reasoning," an inference-time approach that builds task-specific scaffolds through structured meta-reasoning rather than baking scaffolds in at training time. Reports an average 24.8% improvement over the strongest evaluated scaffold across three model sizes and two model families.
**Why it matters**: Suggests inference-time meta-cognition can substitute for fine-tuned reasoning models, with cross-family generalization that prior scaffold work has struggled to demonstrate.

### 2. Predictive Maps of Multi-Agent Reasoning: A Successor-Representation Spectrum for LLM Communication Topologies
**Authors**: (see arXiv listing)
**arXiv**: [2605.11453](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.11453)
**TL;DR**: Proposes a successor-representation framework to predict how multi-agent LLM systems will behave under different communication topologies (chain, star, mesh, hybrids), giving practitioners a principled way to pick a topology before deploying.
**Why it matters**: Multi-agent design choices are currently empirical and brittle; this is one of the first attempts to give topology selection a theoretical handle.

### 3. MemReread: Enhancing Agentic Long-Context Reasoning via Memory-Guided Rereading
**Authors**: (see arXiv listing)
**arXiv**: [2605.10268](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.10268v1)
**TL;DR**: Tackles long-context reasoning without the quadratic cost of full attention by letting the agent decide when to re-read prior context via an explicit memory module. The agent reads selectively rather than carrying everything in the context window.
**Why it matters**: A pragmatic alternative to ever-longer context windows — pushes long-context handling toward agentic retrieval rather than brute-force scaling.

### 4. XL-SafetyBench: A Country-Grounded Cross-Cultural Benchmark for LLM Safety and Cultural Sensitivity
**Authors**: (see arXiv listing)
**arXiv**: [2605.05662](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.05662)
**TL;DR**: 5,500 test cases across 10 country-language pairs, with a Jailbreak Benchmark of country-grounded adversarial prompts and a Cultural Benchmark of innocuous-looking requests that embed local sensitivities. Evaluates 10 frontier and 27 local LLMs.
**Why it matters**: Finds jailbreak robustness and cultural awareness are *not* coupled in frontier models, and local models trade off attack success rate against neutral-safe rate near-linearly — a sharper picture of the safety/utility frontier than monolingual benchmarks give.

### 5. Not All Tokens Need 40 Steps: Heterogeneous Step Allocation in Diffusion Transformers for Efficient Video Generation
**Authors**: (see arXiv listing)
**arXiv**: [2605.06892](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.06892)
**TL;DR**: Training-free inference algorithm (HSA) that assigns varying denoising step budgets to spatiotemporal tokens based on their velocity dynamics, with a KV-cache synchronization mechanism so active tokens still attend to the full sequence while inactive tokens are skipped entirely.
**Why it matters**: Compute-per-token in video DiTs is wildly uneven; making the step budget heterogeneous is a clean way to cut inference cost without retraining.
