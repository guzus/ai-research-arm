# arXiv AI Research - 2026-05-20

## Highlights (Top 5 Must-Read)

### 1. Code as Agent Harness
**Authors**: Xuying Ning, Katherine Tieu, Dongqi Fu, et al. (42 authors)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.18747
**TL;DR**: A comprehensive survey reframing code not as an agent's output but as its operational substrate — the "harness" through which agents reason, act, model their environment, and verify results by execution. Organizes the field into three layers: the harness interface (code linking agents to reasoning/action/environment), harness mechanisms (planning, memory, tool use for long-horizon execution), and feedback-driven control that keeps the harness reliable and adaptive.
**Why it matters**: Consolidates a fast-moving design space and gives a shared vocabulary for building executable, verifiable, stateful agent systems.

### 2. Practical Scaling Laws: Converting Compute into Performance in a Data-Constrained World
**Authors**: Christopher M. Bryant, Hao Liu
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.09189
**TL;DR**: Argues the Chinchilla scaling-law form was calibrated for data-rich, single-epoch pretraining and breaks down outside that regime. Proposes a closed-form extension that decomposes loss into undercapacity, undertraining, and overfitting terms, modeling the multi-epoch, data-constrained world that frontier labs now operate in.
**Why it matters**: As high-quality web text is exhausted, scaling-law guidance that accounts for repeated data and overfitting directly shapes compute-allocation decisions.

### 3. The Unlearnability Phenomenon in RLVR for Language Models
**Authors**: (cs.CL / cs.LG, 2026)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.16787
**TL;DR**: Shows that among hard examples a model initially fails, a substantial subset stays *unlearnable* under RL with verifiable rewards even when correct rollouts are present. These examples have fundamental representation issues — low gradient similarity with the rest of the data and ungeneralizable reasoning patterns.
**Why it matters**: Identifies a structural ceiling on RLVR-based reasoning gains, pointing to representation (not reward) as the bottleneck.

### 4. HEED: Density-Weighted Residual Alignment for Hybrid Vision-Language Model Distillation
**Authors**: (cs.CV / cs.CL, 2026)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.17093
**TL;DR**: Distills a full-attention VLM (Qwen3-VL-8B-Instruct) into a faster 3:1 Mamba-2/attention hybrid using density-weighted residual alignment. The student stays within ~2 points of the teacher on visual-reasoning benchmarks (MMStar, MMBench, MMMU-Pro) but drops ~13 points on OCR/document tasks — a clean map of where linear-attention hybrids hold up and where they don't.
**Why it matters**: Quantifies the accuracy-vs-efficiency tradeoff of hybrid sub-quadratic architectures for multimodal inference.

### 5. Why Do Aligned LLMs Remain Jailbreakable: Refusal-Escape Directions, Operator-Level Sources, and Safety-Utility Trade-off
**Authors**: (cs.CL / cs.CR, 2026)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.08878
**TL;DR**: Identifies Refusal-Escape Directions (RED) — local perturbations around a harmful input that flip the model from refusal to compliance while it still interprets the input as harmful. Traces these to operator-level sources and characterizes the resulting safety-utility trade-off.
**Why it matters**: Offers a mechanistic account of why surface-level alignment leaves a persistent jailbreak surface, informing more robust refusal training.
