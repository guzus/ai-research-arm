## Highlights (Top 5 Must-Read)

### 1. MaxProof: Scaling Mathematical Proof with Generative-Verifier RL and Population-Level Test-Time Scaling
**Authors**: Jiacheng Chen, Xinyu Zhang, Shunkai Zhang, Yanmohan Wang, et al. (MiniMax)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.13473
**TL;DR**: The MiniMax-M3 model is trained for three proof skills — generation, verification, and critique-conditioned repair — fused into one model with a defense-in-depth generative verifier tuned for low false-positive rate. At test time MaxProof treats the model as generator/verifier/refiner/ranker, searches a population of candidate proofs, and returns one via tournament selection, reaching 35/42 on IMO 2025 and 36/42 on USAMO 2026.
**Why it matters**: A reproducible recipe for exceeding the human gold-medal threshold on competition math proofs, showing generative-verifier RL plus population test-time search is now a frontier capability.

### 2. HYDRA-X: Native Unified Multimodal Models with Holistic Visual Tokenizers
**Authors**: Guozhen Zhang, Xuerui Qiu, Yutao Cui, Tianhui Song, Changlin Li, et al.
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.13289
**TL;DR**: The first unified multimodal model to tokenize both images and video inside a single ViT. Ablations show frame-level causal temporal attention suffices for reconstruction (full spatiotemporal attention hurts) and hierarchical temporal compression beats single-step; a lightweight decompressor injects joint image-video semantics, and editing is moved to the latent level inside the tokenizer.
**Why it matters**: A clean architectural blueprint for the next generation of unified-tokenizer multimodal models spanning image and video understanding and generation.

### 3. Beyond the Commitment Boundary: Probing Epiphenomenal Chain-of-Thought in Large Reasoning Models
**Authors**: Daniel Scalena, Sara Candussio, Luca Bortolussi, Elisabetta Fersini
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.13603
**TL;DR**: Estimating each CoT step's causal importance via early-exit reveals a sharp "commitment boundary" — reasoning transitions from transient guesses to a stable answer in a single step, after which remaining CoT is epiphenomenal. Attention probes can linearly decode the answer-formation stage and generalize to unseen tasks, enabling early exit that cuts CoT length up to 55% with negligible accuracy loss.
**Why it matters**: Both an interpretability result on *how* reasoning models commit to answers and a practical, large lever for inference-cost reduction.

### 4. Learning to Reason by Analogy via Retrieval-Augmented Reinforcement Fine-Tuning
**Authors**: Zilin Xiao, Qi Ma, Chun-cheng Jason Chen, Xintao Chen, Avinash Atreya, et al.
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.13680
**TL;DR**: RA-RFT trains a retriever via gold-relevance distillation to rank contexts by *expected reasoning benefit* rather than semantic overlap, then fine-tunes the policy with reinforcement fine-tuning over retrieved analogous demonstrations under verifiable rewards. It lifts AIME 2025 avg@32 by +7.1 / +2.8 points over GRPO for Qwen3-1.7B / 4B.
**Why it matters**: Establishes reasoning-aware retrieval as an axis of improvement orthogonal to reward design and training curricula — analogy, not similarity, is what helps reasoning.

### 5. SpikeDecoder: Realizing the GPT Architecture with Spiking Neural Networks
**Authors**: Claas Beger, Florian Walter, Alois Knoll
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.12287
**TL;DR**: A fully spiking implementation of the Transformer *decoder* block for NLP (prior SNN-Transformer work was vision and encoder-only). The paper systematically ablates which blocks to spike, the role of residual connections and SNN-compatible normalization, and text-to-spike embeddings — cutting theoretical energy by 87–93% vs. the ANN baseline.
**Why it matters**: A concrete step toward energy-efficient generative language models on neuromorphic hardware.
