# arXiv AI Research - 2026-02-09

## Highlights (Top 5 Must-Read)

### 1. SwimBird: Eliciting Switchable Reasoning Mode in Hybrid Autoregressive MLLMs
**Authors**: Accio Lab
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.06040
**TL;DR**: A multimodal LLM that dynamically switches between text-only, vision-only, and interleaved vision-text reasoning modes. Uses a hybrid autoregressive formulation supporting both next-token and next-embedding prediction, with dynamic visual token allocation based on query complexity.
**Why it matters**: First model to remove the fixed-budget constraint in visual reasoning, achieving state-of-the-art on multimodal benchmarks by adapting reasoning modality per-query rather than using a one-size-fits-all approach.

### 2. Dr. Kernel: Reinforcement Learning Done Right for Triton Kernel Generations
**Authors**: HKUST NLP Group
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.05885
**TL;DR**: Introduces KernelGYM (a distributed GPU environment) and TRLOO (Turn-level Reinforce-Leave-One-Out) for training LLMs to generate optimized Triton GPU kernels. Achieves 31.6% of kernels with ≥1.2x speedup over PyTorch, surpassing Claude-4.5-Sonnet (26.7%) and GPT-5 (28.6%).
**Why it matters**: Demonstrates that RL-trained open models can outperform frontier LLMs at GPU kernel generation — a critical capability for AI infrastructure scaling.

### 3. Accordion-Thinking: Self-Regulated Step Summaries for Efficient and Readable LLM Reasoning
**Authors**: (arXiv 2602.03249)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.03249
**TL;DR**: LLMs learn to self-regulate reasoning granularity through dynamic summarization. A "Fold" mode periodically compresses thought processes, achieving 3x throughput on 48GB GPUs while maintaining accuracy. Through RL training, the accuracy gap between efficient Fold and exhaustive Unfold modes vanishes.
**Why it matters**: Addresses the fundamental KV cache scaling bottleneck in chain-of-thought reasoning, offering a practical path to efficient test-time compute scaling.

### 4. f-GRPO and Beyond: Divergence-Based RL Algorithms for General LLM Alignment
**Authors**: (arXiv 2602.05946)
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.05946
**TL;DR**: Proposes f-GRPO (f-Group Relative Policy Optimization) and f-HAL (f-Hybrid Alignment Loss), a unified family of on-policy and hybrid RL objectives based on f-divergences with theoretical guarantees for improving average reward after alignment. Validated on both math reasoning (RLVR) and safety alignment tasks.
**Why it matters**: Provides a theoretically grounded, general framework that unifies preference alignment and verifiable reward settings — two previously separate LLM alignment paradigms.

### 5. DeepControl: Scaling Search-Augmented LLM Reasoning via Adaptive Information Control
**Authors**: Siheng Xiong et al.
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.01672
**TL;DR**: Introduces a formal notion of "information utility" to measure the marginal value of retrieved evidence during reasoning. Achieves 9.4% and 8.6% relative improvements over Search-R1 with Qwen2.5-7B and 3B respectively, consistent across in-distribution and OOD benchmarks.
**Why it matters**: Solves the "when to search" problem for reasoning agents with a principled framework rather than heuristics, enabling more efficient retrieval-augmented reasoning at scale.
