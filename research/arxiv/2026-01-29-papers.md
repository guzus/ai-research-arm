# arXiv AI Research - 2026-01-29

## Highlights (Top 5 Must-Read)

### 1. Conditional Memory via Scalable Lookup (Engram): A New Axis of Sparsity for Large Language Models
**Authors**: Xin Cheng, Wangding Zeng, Damai Dai, Qinyu Chen, Bingxuan Wang, Zhenda Xie, Kezhao Huang, Xingkai Yu, Zhewen Hao, Yukun Li, Han Zhang, Huishuai Zhang, Dongyan Zhao, Wenfeng Liang (DeepSeek-AI & Peking University)
**arXiv**: [2601.07372](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.07372)
**TL;DR**: Introduces Engram, a conditional memory module that adds O(1) lookup as a new sparsity axis alongside MoE. By reallocating ~20-25% of sparse parameters from routed experts to a scalable n-gram embedding table, the 27B model matches or exceeds an iso-parameter MoE baseline across reasoning (BBH +5.0), knowledge (MMLU +3.4), and code tasks (HumanEval +3.0), while boosting long-context retrieval from 84.2 to 97.0 on Multi-Query NIAH.
**Why it matters**: Establishes a fundamentally new architectural primitive for LLMs — conditional memory lookup — with a U-shaped scaling law for optimal compute-memory tradeoff; widely believed to be the core innovation behind DeepSeek V4.

### 2. GAVEL: Towards Rule-Based Safety through Activation Monitoring
**Authors**: Shir Rozenfeld, Rahul Pankajakshan, Itay Zloczower, Eyal Lenga, Gilad Gressel, Yisroel Mirsky
**arXiv**: [2601.19768](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.19768)
**TL;DR**: Introduces a rule-based activation safety paradigm inspired by cybersecurity. Models activations as interpretable "cognitive elements" (CEs) that can be composed into logical rules for fine-grained safety constraints, enabling practitioners and regulators to configure nuanced policies without retraining models or detectors. Accepted at ICLR 2026.
**Why it matters**: Shifts AI safety from opaque classifiers to auditable, shareable rule-based governance — analogous to how YARA/Sigma rules work in cybersecurity — enabling scalable, interpretable safety monitoring.

### 3. K-EXAONE Technical Report: Journey to Frontier-Level Performance
**Authors**: LG AI Research
**arXiv**: [2601.01739](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.01739)
**TL;DR**: 236B-parameter fine-grained MoE model activating only 23B parameters per token. Features 128 experts (top-8 + shared expert routing), 256K native context with 3:1 hybrid attention, Multi-Token Prediction for 1.5x inference speedup, and SuperBPE tokenizer with 150K vocabulary. Achieves frontier-level performance ranking 7th globally on the Intelligence Index while supporting 6 languages.
**Why it matters**: First Korean-origin model to reach frontier performance tier, demonstrating that efficient MoE architecture with hybrid attention can match top US/Chinese models at a fraction of inference cost (runs on A100-class GPUs).

### 4. Mechanistic Analysis of Catastrophic Forgetting in Large Language Models During Continual Fine-tuning
**Authors**: Olaf Yunus Laitinen Imanov
**arXiv**: [2601.18699](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.18699)
**TL;DR**: First systematic mechanistic decomposition of catastrophic forgetting across transformer components, identifying three primary mechanisms: gradient interference in attention weights (~15-23% of attention heads severely disrupted), representational drift in intermediate layers, and loss landscape flattening. Studies models from 109B to 1.5T parameters including Llama 4, DeepSeek-V3.1, GPT-5.1, Claude Opus 4.5, and Gemini 2.5 Pro.
**Why it matters**: Provides actionable predictive signatures for forgetting based on early gradient interference metrics, enabling practitioners to anticipate and mitigate catastrophic forgetting during continual fine-tuning of production models.

### 5. Geometric and Dynamic Scaling in Deep Transformers (Manifold-Geometric Transformer)
**Authors**: Haoran Su
**arXiv**: [2601.01014](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.01014)
**TL;DR**: Proposes the Manifold-Geometric Transformer (MGT), which solves rank collapse in ultra-deep transformers through two orthogonal principles: manifold-constrained hyper-connections (mHC) that restrict residual updates to valid tangent space directions, and deep delta learning (DDL) that enables data-dependent non-monotonic updates supporting feature erasure rather than unconditional accumulation.
**Why it matters**: Provides a principled geometric framework for stable ultra-deep transformer scaling, moving beyond ad-hoc normalization schemes to mathematically grounded "Erasure-and-Write" dynamics.
