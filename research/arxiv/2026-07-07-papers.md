## Highlights (Top 5 Must-Read)

### 1. LLM-as-a-Verifier: A General-Purpose Verification Framework
**Authors:** Jacky Kwok, Shulu Li, Pranav Atreya, Yuejiang Liu, Yixing Jiang, Chelsea Finn, Marco Pavone, Ion Stoica, Azalia Mirhoseini
**arXiv:** [2607.05391](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.05391) · cs.AI/CL/LG/MA/RO

**TL;DR:** Identifies *verification* — judging whether a solution is correct — as a new scaling axis alongside pre-training, post-training, and test-time compute. Instead of prompting an LM judge for a discrete score, it takes the *expectation over the distribution of scoring-token logits* to get continuous scores, which then scale along three dimensions: score granularity, repeated evaluation, and criteria decomposition. No additional training required.

**Why it matters:** A training-free verifier that hits SOTA on Terminal-Bench V2 (86.5%), SWE-Bench Verified (78.2%), RoboRewardBench (87.4%), and MedAgentBench (73.3%) — and the fine-grained signals double as dense RL reward (improving SAC/GRPO sample efficiency) and as a task-progress proxy. Ships as a Claude Code extension. The author list (Finn, Pavone, Stoica, Mirhoseini) signals this is meant as a broadly reusable primitive for agentic systems.

### 2. Nemotron-Labs-3-Puzzle-75B-A9B: Compressing Hybrid MoE LLMs
**Authors:** Akhiad Bercovich, Pavlo Molchanov, Ran El-Yaniv, and the NVIDIA Nemotron-Labs team (~100 authors)
**arXiv:** [2607.04371](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.04371) · cs.AI

**TL;DR:** A heavily compressed variant of **Nemotron-3-Super** built from a multi-stage pipeline combining the Iterative Puzzle compression framework with knowledge distillation, RL, quantization, and a multi-token-prediction head. It jointly prunes heterogeneous MoE experts, the active-parameter budget, and the Mamba layers.

**Why it matters:** Measured deployment wins, not just quality scores — ~2× server throughput vs. the parent model at matched user-throughput constraints on a single 8×B200 node, and 1M-token concurrency raised from **1 request to 8** on a single H100. A concrete data point that large hybrid MoE+Mamba models can be aggressively optimized for serving while keeping downstream accuracy. New-model-architecture + efficiency, priority 1+2.

### 3. Unified Audio Intelligence Without Regressing on Text Intelligence (Audex)
**Authors:** Zhifeng Kong, Bryan Catanzaro, Mohammad Shoeybi, Wei Ping, and the NVIDIA team (20 authors)
**arXiv:** [2607.05196](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.05196) · cs.CL/AI/LG/SD · *checkpoints released*

**TL;DR:** Introduces **Nemotron-Labs-Audex-30B-A3B**, a unified audio-text LLM built on the text-only Nemotron-Cascade-2-30B-A3B MoE. A single Transformer decoder projects encoded audio into the text embedding space and treats text and quantized-audio output tokens uniformly. Trained on 157.4B audio + 320.5B text tokens with multi-stage SFT, text-only Cascade RL, and multi-domain on-policy distillation.

**Why it matters:** State-of-the-art across audio understanding, ASR/translation, TTS, audio generation, and speech-to-speech — *while preserving* the reasoning, alignment, knowledge, long-context, and agentic capabilities of the text backbone with marginal or no regression. The "add audio without losing text IQ" problem is the open question for unified speech-language models, and NVIDIA ships the weights.

### 4. Weak-to-Strong Generalization via Direct On-Policy Distillation (Direct-OPD)
**Authors:** Shiyuan Feng, Huan-ang Gao, Wei-Ying Ma, Ya-Qin Zhang, Hao Zhou, et al. (Tsinghua)
**arXiv:** [2607.05394](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.05394) · cs.LG/AI/CL

**TL;DR:** RLVR is powerful but expensive to re-run on every new strong model. Direct-OPD runs RL once on a *small* model, then transfers the teacher's **RL-induced policy shift** — the log-ratio of post-RL teacher vs. its own pre-RL reference — as a dense implicit reward applied on the stronger student's own on-policy states. No explicit reward model, no sparse-reward RL on the target.

**Why it matters:** A clean answer to "how do you reuse a weak model's RL gains for a stronger one?" Concretely, it lifts **Qwen3-1.7B on AIME 2024 from 48.3% → 62.4% in 4 hours on 8×A100**, beats step-matched direct RL, and supports chaining multiple policy shifts sequentially. Scaling-laws/efficiency + reasoning, with a result that's cheap enough for any lab to reproduce.

### 5. DSpark: Confidence-Scheduled Speculative Decoding with Semi-Autoregressive Generation
**Authors:** Xin Cheng, Damai Dai, Dongyan Zhao, Wenfeng Liang, et al. (DeepSeek)
**arXiv:** [2607.05147](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.05147) · cs.AI/CL

**TL;DR:** Parallel speculative drafters propose long token blocks in one forward pass but suffer rapid acceptance decay (no inter-token dependency) and waste batch capacity on high-rejection tokens. DSpark pairs a **semi-autoregressive drafter** (parallel backbone + lightweight sequential module for intra-block dependency) with **confidence-scheduled verification** that adapts verification length per request from estimated prefix-survival probabilities and engine throughput profiles.

**Why it matters:** Deployed in the **DeepSeek-V4 serving system under live traffic** — 60–85% faster per-user generation vs. the MTP-1 production baseline at matched throughput, and it unlocks performance tiers that were previously unattainable under strict interactivity constraints (a Pareto-frontier shift). A rare paper with both a clean method and production telemetry.
