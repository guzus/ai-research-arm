## Highlights (Top 5 Must-Read)

### 1. Streaming Communication in Multi-Agent Reasoning (StreamMA)
**Authors**: Zhen Yang, Xiaogang Xu, Wen Wang, Cong Chen, Xander Xu, Ying-Cong Chen
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.05158
**TL;DR**: Instead of the usual "generate-then-transfer" multi-agent pipeline,
StreamMA streams each reasoning step to downstream agents as soon as it is
produced, pipelining adjacent agents. Counter-intuitively this *improves*
accuracy (because reliable early steps are forwarded before error-prone late
steps can mislead), with a closed-form analysis of stream vs. serial vs. single
protocols and gains up to +22.4 pp on HMMT 2026 (Claude Opus 4.6).
**Why it matters**: Introduces a "step-level scaling law" — a new test-time
scaling axis orthogonal to and composable with agent-count scaling.

### 2. Reinforcement Learning from Rich Feedback with Distributional DAgger (DistIL)
**Authors**: Rishabh Agrawal, Jacob Fein-Ashley, Paria Rashidinejad
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.05152
**TL;DR**: Argues the dominant RLVR recipe (one correctness bit per rollout)
wastes the rich feedback available from traces, tool outputs, and corrections.
A distributional variant of DAgger yields a forward cross-entropy objective with
*provable monotonic policy improvement* and regret guarantees — properties that
reverse-KL / Jensen-Shannon self-distillation lack — and improves Pass@N across
math, science, and code.
**Why it matters**: A principled training objective that beats RLVR while fixing
a known failure mode of self-distillation, directly relevant to reasoning models.

### 3. AutoLab: Can Frontier Models Solve Long-Horizon Auto Research and Engineering Tasks?
**Authors**: Zhangchen Xu, Junda Chen, Yue Huang, Dongfu Jiang, Jiefeng Chen, et al.
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.05080
**TL;DR**: A benchmark of 36 expert-curated, ultra-long-horizon closed-loop
optimization tasks (system optimization, puzzles, model development, CUDA kernel
tuning), each starting from a correct-but-suboptimal baseline under a strict
wall-clock budget. Across 17 frontier models, the dominant predictor of success
is *persistence* (repeated benchmark-edit-feedback loops), not initial-attempt
quality; claude-opus-4.6 leads while most models terminate early or stall.
**Why it matters**: Exposes time-awareness and iterative persistence as a core,
under-measured capability gap for autonomous research/engineering agents.

### 4. STRIDE: Training Data Attribution via Sparse Recovery from Subset Perturbations
**Authors**: Rishit Dagli, Abir Harrasse, Luke Zhang, Florent Draye, Amirali Abdullah, Bernhard Schölkopf, Zhijing Jin
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.05165
**TL;DR**: Recasts training-data attribution from expensive parameter-space
gradient tracking to a sparse-recovery / compressive-sensing problem in
*activation* space. Lightweight "steering operators" mimic the behavioral shift
of training on data subsets; perturbing them recovers per-example influence,
reaching SOTA on LLM pre-training attribution while being ~13× faster.
**Why it matters**: Makes attribution tractable at LLM scale, with direct
applications to data selection, contamination detection, and provenance.

### 5. Audio Interaction Model
**Authors**: Zhifei Xie, Zihang Liu, Ze An, Xiaobin Hu, Yue Liao, Shuicheng Yan, et al.
**arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.05121
**TL;DR**: Unifies today's offline Large Audio Language Models and single-task
streaming audio models into one always-on "perceive-decide-respond" online LALM.
The SoundFlow framework spans streaming-native data, comprehension-aware
training, and asynchronous low-latency inference; a 2.6M-item StreamAudio-2M
corpus (7 abilities, 28 sub-tasks) plus Proactive-Sound-Bench back it.
**Why it matters**: Pushes audio models from turn-based responders toward
real-time, proactive agents that decide *when* to respond from stream semantics.
