### Anthropic Funding Round Expands to $25 Billion with Sequoia Joining
**Date:** January 18, 2026

Sequoia Capital is joining Anthropic's massive funding round, which now aims to raise at least $25 billion at a $350 billion valuation, according to reports from the Financial Times and Bloomberg published on January 18, 2026.

**Key Details:**
- **Total Funding Goal**: The round now aims to raise $25 billion at a $350 billion valuation, up from the initially reported $10 billion
- **Valuation**: $350 billion, representing more than double Anthropic's $170 billion valuation from just four months ago
- **Major Investors**: Sequoia Capital joining Singapore's GIC and U.S. investor Coatue
- **Investment Amounts**: GIC and Coatue are each contributing $1.5 billion
- **Additional Backing**: Microsoft and Nvidia secured commitments totaling up to $15 billion
- **Expected Close**: The deal is expected to close soon

**Sequoia's Notable Shift:**
This investment marks a significant departure from Sequoia's traditional approach of not backing competing companies. Sequoia has already backed OpenAI and Elon Musk's xAI, and is now backing Anthropic as well. The move follows a leadership change at Sequoia, after Roelof Botha was replaced by Pat Grady and Alfred Lin late last year.

**Future Plans:**
Anthropic is reportedly preparing for an IPO that could come as soon as this year.

### OpenAI Announces Advertising on ChatGPT
**Date:** January 16, 2026

OpenAI announced on Friday, January 16, 2026, that it will begin testing advertisements within ChatGPT in the coming weeks, marking a significant shift in the company's business model.

**Key Details:**
- **Where Ads Will Appear**: OpenAI will be testing ads in its free version for logged-in, adult US users, as well as in ChatGPT Go ($8/month)
- **Ad-Free Tiers**: Higher-tier Plus and Pro subscriptions will remain ad-free
- **Ad Format**: Ads will be tested at the bottom of answers in ChatGPT when there's a relevant sponsored product or service based on the current conversation
- **User Protections**:
  - ChatGPT's responses will not be influenced by ads
  - OpenAI will "never" sell users' data to advertisers
  - Users under 18 will not see ads
  - Ads will not appear near certain topics, including politics, health and mental health
  - Users can turn off ad personalization based on their chats
- **Timeline**: Testing to begin in the coming weeks

**Business Context:**
The move comes as OpenAI is urgently trying to figure out how to bring in more revenue from its 800 million monthly users to help it afford the $1.4 trillion it has committed to spending on AI infrastructure over the next eight years.

### Meta Llama 4 Launch with Major Controversy
**Date:** January 2026

Meta introduced Llama 4 Scout and Llama 4 Maverick, the first open-weight natively multimodal models with unprecedented context length support and built using a mixture-of-experts (MoE) architecture. The company is also previewing Llama 4 Behemoth, described as one of the smartest LLMs in the world.

**Key Features:**
- **Llama 4 Scout and Llama 4 Maverick**: First open-weight natively multimodal models
- **Architecture**: Built using mixture-of-experts (MoE) architecture
- **Context Length**: Unprecedented context length support
- **Llama 4 Behemoth**: Preview of their most powerful model yet
- **Availability**: Users can try Meta AI with Llama 4 in WhatsApp, Messenger, Instagram Direct, and on Meta.AI website

**Benchmark Controversy:**
A major controversy emerged when Yann LeCun, Meta's departing Chief AI Scientist, revealed that Meta researchers "fudged a little bit" by using different versions of Llama 4 Maverick and Llama 4 Scout models on different benchmarks to improve test results. This benchmark manipulation contributed to internal frustration and a loss of confidence among Meta leadership, including CEO Mark Zuckerberg.

**Leadership Changes:**
LeCun is departing Meta after more than a decade to start a new AI research venture called Advanced Machine Intelligence Labs. He has offered an unusually candid account of why he left Meta, pointing to a research culture he says tilted too heavily toward large language models (LLMs) and away from foundational science.

**Strategic Shifts:**
Rumors suggest that while the Llama 4 Maverick and Scout models will remain open-weight, the flagship "Behemoth" (a 2-trillion-plus parameter model) and the upcoming Avocado model may be semi-proprietary or closed-source.
