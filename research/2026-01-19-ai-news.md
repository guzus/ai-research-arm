## Regulatory Updates

### Trump AI Executive Order: State Law Conflicts Continue
**Date:** January 2026 (Ongoing Development)

Following President Trump's December 11, 2025 executive order "Ensuring a National Policy Framework for Artificial Intelligence," federal-state tensions over AI regulation continue to escalate into January 2026.

**Key Developments:**

**AI Litigation Task Force Deadline (January 10, 2026):**
The Attorney General was required to establish a task force by January 10, 2026, to challenge state AI laws that the Administration views as unconstitutional or preempted by federal law.

**Commerce Department Evaluation (Due March 11, 2026):**
The Secretary of Commerce must publish an evaluation by March 11, 2026, identifying "onerous" state AI laws that conflict with federal policy.

**Funding Leverage:**
The Executive Order instructs the Department of Commerce to condition $42 billion in previously allocated broadband infrastructure funding under the Broadband Equity, Access and Deployment (BEAD) program on the repeal of state AI regulations deemed onerous.

**State Laws in Effect (January 1, 2026):**
Despite the executive order, several state AI laws went into effect on January 1, 2026:
- **California**: Transparency in Frontier Artificial Intelligence Act (California TFAIA)
- **Texas**: Responsible Artificial Intelligence Governance Act (Texas RAIGA)
- **Colorado**: Algorithmic discrimination law
- **California AB 489**: Prevents deceptive AI representations in healthcare

**Current Legal Status:**
The executive order does not presently invalidate any state or local AI laws. Unless a court blocks a law via an injunction or Congress enacts a federal law preempting the state or local counterpart, such laws remain enforceable.

**Industry Impact:**
The federal-state conflict creates regulatory uncertainty for AI companies operating across multiple states, with some companies facing potential conflicts between federal policy direction and state legal requirements.
