## Breaking News

### Alibaba Bans Employees From Using Claude Code, Effective July 10

**Date:** July 4, 2026 | **Sources:** [TechCrunch — Alibaba reportedly bans employees from using Claude Code](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/04/alibaba-reportedly-bans-employees-from-using-claude-code/), [South China Morning Post — Alibaba bans staff from using Claude Code over Anthropic spyware concerns](https://www.scmp.com/tech/big-tech/article/3359375/alibaba-bans-staff-using-claude-code-over-anthropic-spyware-concerns), [The Information — Alibaba Bans Employees From Using Claude](https://www.theinformation.com/briefings/alibaba-bans-employees-using-claude), [Cybernews — Alibaba bans Claude Code over security concerns](https://cybernews.com/security/alibaba-ban-claude-code-security-risk/)

- **What's net-new:** Alibaba will bar staff from using Anthropic's Claude
  Code coding-agent tool starting **July 10**, directing employees to its
  in-house alternative, **Qoder**, instead. Alibaba cites security
  concerns after Anthropic acknowledged embedding code in Claude Code that
  could detect whether a user was based in China or affiliated with a
  Chinese AI lab.
- **Anthropic's explanation:** Engineer **Thariq Shihipar** said on X the
  detection code was "an experiment we launched in March that was meant to
  prevent account abuse from unauthorized resellers and protect against
  distillation" — not user surveillance.
- **Why it matters:** This is the direct sequel to Anthropic's late-June
  allegation that operators linked to Alibaba's Qwen lab used roughly
  25,000 fraudulent accounts to run ~29 million exchanges against Claude
  for distillation purposes — now Alibaba is banning the tool itself,
  producing a tit-for-tat access war playing out on both sides of the
  Pacific simultaneously with Anthropic's own crackdown on Chinese-routed
  Claude access (covered in yesterday's report).
- **Skeptic's note:** Reported via TechCrunch/SCMP/The Information citing
  sources and an internal notice; Alibaba has not issued an on-the-record
  public statement, and Anthropic has not independently confirmed the
  July 10 effective date.

## Industry Moves

### Midjourney Asks Court to Force Disney, Universal, and Warner Bros. to Disclose Their Own AI Practices

**Date:** July 4, 2026 | **Sources:** [TechCrunch — Midjourney wants Hollywood studios to reveal the details of their AI usage](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/04/midjourney-wants-hollywood-studios-to-reveal-the-details-of-their-ai-usage/), [Variety — Midjourney Seeks to Reveal Studios' Use of AI in High-Stakes Copyright Battle](https://variety.com/2026/film/news/midjourney-studios-ai-copyright-discovery-1236800902/), [Engadget — Midjourney wants the Hollywood studios that sued it to show the court how they use AI](https://www.engadget.com/2207936/midjourney-wants-studios-that-sued-show-court-ai-use/)

- **What's net-new:** In its copyright suit brought by Disney, Universal,
  and Warner Bros., Midjourney filed to overturn a prior discovery ruling
  that limited the studios' AI-usage disclosure obligations to only
  "consumer-facing" outputs. Midjourney is now demanding the studios turn
  over their internal **AI business plans, research reports, training
  datasets, model weights, and board-level AI presentations**.
- **The argument:** Midjourney's filing contends the withheld documents
  would show the studios doing "exactly what they are suing Midjourney for
  doing" — using AI trained on copyrighted material internally while suing
  Midjourney over the same practice.
- **Studios' response:** Lead attorney **David Singer** called the request
  a "fishing expedition," maintaining the suit is only about Midjourney
  copying the studios' characters and IP, not about stopping AI broadly.
- **Why it matters:** A discovery ruling either way could set precedent for
  how much AI companies can compel plaintiffs to reveal about their own
  internal AI usage in copyright litigation — relevant well beyond this
  one case as similar suits against OpenAI and others proceed.
- **Skeptic's note:** This is a discovery-scope filing, not a ruling; the
  court has not yet decided whether to expand the studios' disclosure
  obligations.

## Bottom Line

The Anthropic–Alibaba conflict that has simmered since late June's
distillation accusations escalated into open access warfare: **Alibaba
banning Claude Code for its own staff** effective July 10 is the most
concrete new flashpoint in a dispute that already had Anthropic
tightening enforcement against Chinese-routed Claude access. On the legal
front, **Midjourney's push to force Disney, Universal, and Warner Bros. to
disclose their own internal AI practices** escalates one of the highest-
profile AI copyright suits into a fight over what "everybody's doing it"
actually looks like inside the studios themselves. With no new frontier
model release, funding round, or regulatory action clearing the 48-hour
bar, these two access/disclosure stories — one geopolitical, one legal —
carried the news cycle.
