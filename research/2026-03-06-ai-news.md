### Anthropic Announces Lawsuit Against Pentagon Over Supply Chain Risk Designation
**Date:** March 5–6, 2026 | **Sources:** [Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-06/anthropic-says-it-has-no-choice-but-to-fight-pentagon-in-court), [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/05/anthropic-to-challenge-dods-supply-chain-label-in-court/), [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/05/anthropic-ceo-says-no-choice-but-to-challenge-trump-admins-supply-chain-risk-designation-in-court.html), [Business Standard](https://www.business-standard.com/technology/tech-news/anthropic-to-fight-pentagon-in-court-over-supply-chain-risk-label-126030600184_1.html)

Anthropic CEO Dario Amodei announced the company will challenge the Pentagon's supply chain risk designation in court:
- In a blog post, Amodei wrote: "We do not believe this action is legally sound, and we see no choice but to challenge it in court."
- The lawsuit is expected to be filed in federal court in Washington, D.C.
- The designation — normally reserved for foreign adversaries — bars government contractors from using Anthropic's technology in Pentagon work.
- Legal experts note the law behind the designation limits the usual ways companies can challenge government procurement decisions, making the case a significant test of executive authority.
- Despite the designation, Anthropic said the government cannot forbid it from working with companies in non-military capacities.

## Regulatory Updates

### Oregon Passes First Chatbot Safety Bill of 2026
**Date:** March 5, 2026 | **Sources:** [Transparency Coalition](https://www.transparencycoalition.ai/news/oregon-lawmakers-pass-major-chatbot-bill-in-significant-win-for-kids-and-ai-safety), [Yahoo News](https://www.yahoo.com/news/articles/bill-save-lives-oregon-passes-153000615.html), [KOIN](https://www.koin.com/news/politics/this-bill-will-save-lives-oregon-passes-ai-safeguards-for-youth-mental-health/)

Oregon gave final approval to SB 1546, the first chatbot safety bill to pass in any U.S. state in 2026:
- Requires chatbot operators to implement safety protections for minors, including reminders that the user is talking to AI (not a human), hourly break reminders, and referrals to the 988 crisis lifeline for mental health concerns.
- Prohibits chatbots from producing sexually explicit content or using engagement-maximizing reward systems when interacting with minors.
- Includes a private right of action with $1,000 in statutory damages.
- A similar measure awaits approval in Washington state.

### Vermont Governor Signs AI Deepfake Elections Bill
**Date:** March 5, 2026 | **Sources:** [WCAX](https://www.wcax.com/2026/03/05/gov-scott-signs-bill-regulating-ai-election-campaign-media/), [Vermont Public](https://www.vermontpublic.org/local-news/2026-02-18/vermont-lawmakers-approve-bill-disclosure-deceptive-ai-political-ads)

Governor Phil Scott signed Vermont S.23 into law on March 5:
- Any campaign media using AI-generated images, audio, or video within 90 days of an election must include a visible disclosure statement.
- Targets "deceptive and fraudulent synthetic media" of real people distributed with intent to injure a candidate's reputation, influence an election, or deceive voters.
- Enforcement authority given to the Vermont Attorney General under Consumer Protection Law.
- Exempts satire and parody.
