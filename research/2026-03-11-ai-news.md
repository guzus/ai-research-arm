### Microsoft Files Amicus Brief Urging Court to Block Pentagon's Anthropic Ban
**Date:** March 10, 2026 | **Sources:** [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/10/microsoft-says-court-should-temporarily-block-pentagon-ban-anthropic.html), [The Star](https://www.thestar.com.my/tech/tech-news/2026/03/11/microsoft-files-amicus-brief-in-support-of-anthropic039s-lawsuit-with-us-dod), [European Business Magazine](https://europeanbusinessmagazine.com/business/microsoft-backs-anthropic-in-pentagon-lawsuit-over-mass-surveliinace-and-military-ai-use/), [PYMNTS](https://www.pymnts.com/politics/2026/microsoft-backs-anthropic-against-pentagon-ban/), [Seeking Alpha](https://seekingalpha.com/news/4563016-microsoft-backs-anthropic-in-legal-fight-over-pentagon-blacklisting)

Microsoft filed its own amicus brief on Tuesday advocating for a temporary restraining order (TRO) to block the Pentagon's supply chain risk designation of Anthropic:
- Microsoft called the Defense Department's actions "drastic," "unprecedented," and carrying "broad negative ramifications" for the entire U.S. technology industry.
- The brief argues the TRO is needed to prevent costly disruptions for suppliers who would otherwise have to rapidly rebuild offerings that rely on Anthropic's products.
- Microsoft noted that while the Pentagon gave itself six months to phase out Anthropic, it did not provide the same transition period to contractors that use Anthropic's products under DOD contracts.
- This is separate from the amicus brief filed Monday by 30+ OpenAI and Google employees (covered in yesterday's report).
- Pentagon technology chief Emil Michael told Bloomberg the Pentagon was "moving on" from its dispute with Anthropic and that the issue would not be resolved through the courts.
- Anthropic disclosed in court filings that it has generated more than $5 billion in total revenue since launching commercial products in 2023, but has spent over $10 billion training and deploying models and remains deeply unprofitable.

### Google Deploys Custom AI Agents for Pentagon's 3 Million Workforce
**Date:** March 10, 2026 | **Sources:** [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/10/google-deepens-pentagon-ai-push-after-anthropic-sues-trump-admin.html), [Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-10/google-to-provide-pentagon-with-ai-agents-for-unclassified-work), [DefenseScoop](https://defensescoop.com/2026/03/10/dod-genai-agent-designer-custom-ai-assistants-google-gemini/), [PYMNTS](https://www.pymnts.com/google/2026/google-deploys-ai-agents-to-help-pentagon-automate-jobs/)

A day after Anthropic sued the Trump administration, Google announced a major expansion of its Gemini AI role inside the Defense Department:
- Google is rolling out **Agent Designer**, a no-code/low-code tool on GenAI.mil (the Pentagon's enterprise AI portal) that lets civilian and military personnel build custom AI agents for unclassified work.
- Eight ready-made agents will ship initially, automating tasks like summarizing meeting notes, creating budgets, and checking proposals against national defense strategy.
- Google's AI chatbot on GenAI.mil has already been used by 1.2 million Defense Department employees, running 40 million unique prompts and uploading 4 million+ documents since December.
- Classified network expansion is underway: "We're starting with unclassified because that's where most of the users are, and then we'll get to classified and top secret," said Emil Michael.
- Until recently, Anthropic provided the only AI system operating in the Pentagon's classified cloud. The DOD has now struck deals with OpenAI, xAI, and Google for restricted networks.
- Internally, Google AI chief Jeff Dean and ~900 Google employees signed an open letter urging the company to maintain the same guardrails Anthropic demanded.

### FTC AI Policy Statement & Commerce Department Review — Deadline Day
**Date:** March 11, 2026 | **Sources:** [Mondaq/Baker Botts](https://www.mondaq.com/unitedstates/new-technology/1755166/march-2026-federal-deadlines-that-will-reshape-the-ai-regulatory-landscape), [Digital Applied](https://www.digitalapplied.com/blog/ftc-ai-policy-deadline-march-11-compliance-readiness), [Bankwatch](https://bankwatch.ca/2026/03/08/ftc-ai-policy-statement-due-tuesday-march-11/), [Butzel Long](https://www.butzel.com/alert-department-of-commerce-report-on-state-artificial-intelligence-laws-expected-by-march-11-2026)

Today is the 90-day deadline for two major federal AI policy deliverables under Trump's December 2025 executive order:
- **FTC Policy Statement:** Chairman Andrew Ferguson must publish a policy statement on how Section 5 of the FTC Act (unfair and deceptive practices) applies to AI. A leaked draft reportedly covers AI-generated advertising, consent frameworks for training data, and automated decision-making transparency in credit scoring, underwriting, and employment.
- **Commerce Department Review:** The Secretary of Commerce must publish a comprehensive review of existing state AI laws, identifying those deemed overly burdensome or in conflict with federal policy. Laws flagged could be referred to the DOJ AI Litigation Task Force for federal court challenges.
- **State preemption stakes:** Colorado's AI Act (implementation delayed to June 30, 2026), Illinois' AI Video Interview Act, and California's proposed AB-331 could all be superseded. States that don't rescind laws classified as "burdensome" risk losing undisbursed BEAD broadband grants.
- **FTC leadership:** Commissioner Melissa Holyoak has stepped down, leaving only Chairman Ferguson and Commissioner Meador. Current leadership is expected to favor conduct-based enforcement targeting fraud and deception rather than regulating AI tools themselves.
- This is the first binding federal AI governance signal since Trump's December 2025 executive order and could reshape the compliance landscape for every enterprise AI deployment in North America.

### Anthropic Launches the Anthropic Institute
**Date:** March 11, 2026 | **Sources:** [Anthropic Blog](https://www.anthropic.com/news/the-anthropic-institute), [NewsBytesApp](https://www.newsbytesapp.com/news/science/anthropic-launches-dedicated-research-body-to-study-ai-s-societal-impact/story), [BusinessToday](https://www.businesstoday.in/technology/news/story/anthropic-announces-anthropic-institute-to-study-ai-risks-and-impact-on-society-520146-2026-03-11), [Help Net Security](https://www.helpnetsecurity.com/2026/03/11/anthropic-institute-ai-challenges/), [Yahoo Finance](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/anthropic-launches-ai-institute-pentagon-190915320.html), [Engadget](https://www.engadget.com/ai/anthropic-is-opening-an-office-in-dc-while-battling-pentagon-in-court-115700127.html)

Anthropic launched the Anthropic Institute, a new interdisciplinary research body aimed at confronting the most significant challenges powerful AI will pose to society:
- The Institute consolidates three existing research teams under co-founder **Jack Clark**, who takes on the new title of Head of Public Benefit: the Frontier Red Team (stress-testing AI systems), Societal Impacts (studying real-world AI use), and Economic Research (tracking AI's impact on jobs and the economy).
- **Sarah Heck**, Head of External Affairs, replaces Clark as Head of Policy.
- Launches with approximately 30 members — machine learning engineers, economists, and social scientists.
- **Key hires:** Matt Botvinick (previously Senior Director of Research at Google DeepMind, Resident Fellow at Yale Law School) will lead work on AI and the rule of law. Anton Korinek (Professor of Economics, University of Virginia) will lead research on how transformative AI could reshape economic activity. Zoë Hitzig (previously at OpenAI) will connect economics research to model development.
- Anthropic's Public Policy team is opening its first office in **Washington, DC** this spring.
- Anthropic stated: "We predict that far more dramatic progress will follow in the next two years," calling this effort essential as AI development accelerates.

### OpenAI Codex Security Reaches Research Preview
**Date:** March 6–9, 2026 | **Sources:** [OpenAI Blog](https://openai.com/index/codex-security-now-in-research-preview/), [SecurityWeek](https://www.securityweek.com/openai-rolls-out-codex-security-vulnerability-scanner/amp/), [The Hacker News](https://thehackernews.com/2026/03/openai-codex-security-scanned-12.html), [Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-06/openai-releases-ai-agent-security-tool-for-research-preview), [CSO Online](https://www.csoonline.com/article/4142354/openai-says-codex-security-found-11000-high-impact-bugs-in-a-month.html)

OpenAI launched Codex Security, an AI-powered AppSec agent that detects hard-to-find vulnerabilities and proposes fixes:
- Evolved from "Aardvark," OpenAI's private-beta security tool from October 2025.
- Tested against 1.2 million commits over 30 days, identifying ~800 critical vulnerabilities and 10,000+ high-severity issues.
- The agent builds a project-specific threat model, validates findings in sandboxed environments, and proposes fixes aligned with system behavior.
- In early internal deployments, it surfaced a real SSRF and a critical cross-tenant authentication vulnerability that were patched within hours.
- False positive rates dropped 50%+ across all repositories; noise reduced by 84% since initial rollout.
- Available in research preview to ChatGPT Pro, Enterprise, Business, and Edu customers with free usage for 30 days (starting March 9).
- Competes directly with Anthropic's Claude Code Security, which launched weeks earlier.
