### Tesla — Unsupervised Robotaxi Service Goes Live in Dallas and Houston
**Date:** April 18, 2026 (operational into April 19–20) | **Sources:** [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/04/18/tesla-brings-its-robotaxi-service-to-dallas-and-houston/), [Electrek](https://electrek.co/2026/04/18/tesla-robotaxi-launches-dallas-houston-small-geofences/), [Not a Tesla App](https://www.notateslaapp.com/news/4004/tesla-launches-unsupervised-robotaxi-service-in-dallas-and-houston), [Electric Vehicles](https://eletric-vehicles.com/tesla/tesla-launches-unsupervised-robotaxi-in-dallas-and-houston/)

Tesla launched **fully unsupervised** Robotaxi service (no human driver, no front-seat monitor) in Dallas and Houston on Saturday April 18, the first expansion beyond the Austin core (live unsupervised since January) and the SF Bay Area (still chaperoned).

- **Geofences:** Houston ~12–25 sq mi (Northwest, e.g., Jersey Village/Willowbrook); Dallas ~30–35 sq mi (urban core, Park Cities/Highland Park). Austin's geofence has expanded from a 20 sq mi launch to ~245 sq mi for reference.
- **Fleet:** Crowdsourced trackers see ~1 vehicle per new city at launch versus ~46 vehicles in Austin (4–12 unsupervised Model Ys); no official disclosure.
- **Competitive context:** Waymo has run **fully driverless** service in both Dallas and Houston since February 2026 with no monitors or chase cars and ~500K weekly rides nationally.
- **Catalyst:** Launch is staged ahead of Tesla's **Q1 2026 earnings on April 22**; Street consensus is $21.4–$22.7B revenue and adj. EPS $0.30–$0.37, with the call expected to refocus the narrative on Robotaxi geofence expansion (planned: Phoenix, Miami, Orlando, Tampa, Las Vegas in H1 2026), Cybercab production, and the Optimus humanoid roadmap.

### Apple — App Store Releases Up 104% YoY in April; AI Coding Tools Cited as the Cause
**Date:** April 18, 2026 | **Source:** [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/04/18/the-app-store-is-booming-again-and-ai-may-be-why/)

Worldwide mobile-app releases are **+60% YoY in Q1 2026** (iOS alone +80%) and **+104% YoY across both stores month-to-date in April**, according to data cited by TechCrunch on April 18.

- Cause cited: agentic-coding tools — **Claude Code, Cursor, Replit** — making app creation accessible to non-traditional developers.
- Top growth categories: mobile games (still leading), utilities, productivity, lifestyle, health & fitness.
- Significance: first quantitative read-out (rather than vendor-reported revenue numbers) suggesting AI-native developer tooling is materially expanding the *supply* side of the consumer software market, not just compressing time-to-ship for existing teams. This is also a leading indicator for Apple ahead of its earnings.

### Cursor — Talks for $2B Raise at $50B Valuation Confirmed (Reported April 17–18)
**Date:** April 17–18, 2026 | **Sources:** [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/04/17/sources-cursor-in-talks-to-raise-2b-at-50b-valuation-as-enterprise-growth-surges/), [TechBuzz](https://www.techbuzz.ai/articles/cursor-chases-2b-raise-at-50b-valuation-in-ai-coding-boom), [Benzinga](https://www.benzinga.com/markets/private-markets/26/04/51901426/ai-coding-startup-cursor-set-for-2-billion-funding-round-nearly-doubling-valuation-with-nvidia-backing-report)

Coding-agent leader **Cursor (Anysphere)** is in advanced talks for a **$2B primary raise** at a **$50B pre-money** ($52B post-money) valuation, nearly **doubling** its $29.3B post-money mark from October 2025.

- **Lead investors:** **Thrive Capital** and **Andreessen Horowitz**; **Battery Ventures** and **Nvidia** expected to join.
- **Revenue:** ARR of ~$2B as of February 2026, on track to hit **$6B run-rate by end of 2026**, driven by enterprise growth and margin expansion from the proprietary **Composer** model reducing reliance on third-party APIs.
- **Round status:** oversubscribed; terms not yet final. Round is a direct read-through on the same coding-agent thesis funding Factory's Series C (covered April 18) and on the App Store supply-side expansion above.
- **Strategic read:** at $50B, Cursor would price as the second-most-valuable private AI application company after OpenAI, ahead of every horizontal application competitor.
