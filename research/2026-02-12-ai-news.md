### Anthropic Donates $20 Million to Political Group Backing AI Regulation
**Date:** February 12, 2026

Anthropic is donating $20 million to Public First Action, a political advocacy organization that will spend money on candidates from both parties who support AI safety regulation — positioning the company as an outlier in an industry where most major players are lobbying for lighter oversight.

**Key Details:**
- **Public First Action** plans to back **30 to 50 candidates** from both parties in state and federal 2026 midterm races
- The organization is tied to **two super PACs** — one Democratic, one Republican — that will support candidates who favor AI safeguards
- Policy priorities include **transparency requirements for AI companies**, a **federal AI framework**, **export controls on AI chips**, and **regulation to prevent AI-enabled biological weapons**
- The donation directly counters **Leading the Future**, a billionaire-backed super PAC planning to spend **$125 million** this year to elect candidates who support lighter AI regulation
- Anthropic has faced **heat from the White House** for its pro-regulation stance

**Why It Matters:**
While most of Silicon Valley is lobbying to weaken AI regulation, Anthropic is spending tens of millions to elect candidates who would strengthen it. This makes Anthropic arguably the most politically contrarian major AI company — and directly sets up a spending war with the $125M anti-regulation PAC.

### Google Reveals Massive Distillation Attack on Gemini — 100,000+ Prompts Used to Clone AI
**Date:** February 12, 2026

Google disclosed that commercially motivated attackers bombarded its Gemini AI chatbot with over 100,000 prompts in a systematic attempt to extract and clone its reasoning logic — one of the largest documented "model extraction" attacks on a commercial AI system.

**Key Details:**
- Attackers used **100,000+ prompts** in a methodical sweep across tasks, languages, and formats to map how Gemini generalizes
- Many prompts were specifically crafted to extract Gemini's **reasoning algorithms** — how it processes and evaluates information
- Google describes the activity as **"model extraction"** — essentially intellectual property theft
- Perpetrators are believed to be **private companies or researchers** pursuing a competitive edge, not state actors
- Google detected the campaign and **adjusted Gemini's defenses** to better protect against future extraction attempts
- Separately, Google also reported **state-backed hackers** from China (APT31), Iran (APT42), North Korea (UNC2970), and Russia are using Gemini for **reconnaissance, phishing, and vulnerability testing**

**Why It Matters:**
This is one of the first public disclosures of a large-scale commercial distillation attack on a frontier AI model, highlighting a growing threat: as AI models become more valuable, competitors will increasingly try to reverse-engineer them through their public APIs.

### Meta Breaks Ground on $10 Billion AI Data Center Campus in Indiana
**Date:** February 11–12, 2026

Meta officially broke ground on a massive 1-gigawatt data center campus in Lebanon, Indiana — a $10 billion+ investment that will be one of the largest single-site AI infrastructure projects in the United States.

**Key Details:**
- **Investment:** Over $10 billion
- **Scale:** 4 million square feet, designed for **1 gigawatt** of power
- **Location:** 1,500-acre site in the LEAP District, Lebanon, Indiana
- **Timeline:** Expected operational in **late 2027 or early 2028**
- **Jobs:** 4,000+ construction jobs at peak; ~300 permanent positions
- Meta investing **$45 million in public road improvements** for surrounding communities
- Donating **$1 million/year for 20 years** to Boone REMC Community Fund for energy cost assistance
- Will be Meta's **second Indiana facility** — significantly larger than the ~$800M Jeffersonville campus nearing completion

### Algorized Raises $13M Series A for Industrial Robot Safety AI
**Date:** February 11, 2026

Swiss-American startup Algorized raised $13 million in Series A funding to scale its Predictive Safety Engine — an edge-AI system that lets industrial robots operate at full speed around humans without constant emergency stops.

**Key Details:**
- **Led by:** Run Ventures, with participation from **Amazon Industrial Innovation Fund** and Acrobator Ventures
- Uses **wireless sensors** (UWB, mmWave, Wi-Fi) instead of cameras to detect human presence and predict intent
- Robots can "see" through **occlusion, dust, and darkness** — environments where camera-based systems fail
- Following its **CES 2026 debut with KUKA and ASUS**, Algorized is deploying with major automotive and industrial leaders in Europe and the U.S.
- Funds will expand installations and grow engineering teams in **Switzerland and Silicon Valley**
