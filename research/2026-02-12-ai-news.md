### Anthropic Expands Claude Free Tier With File Creation, Connectors, and Skills
**Date:** February 11–12, 2026

Anthropic significantly expanded the features available to free Claude users, giving non-paying customers access to capabilities that previously required a subscription — a move widely seen as a direct competitive response to OpenAI's rollout of ads in ChatGPT.

**New Free Tier Features:**
- **File Creation:** Free users can now generate and edit Excel spreadsheets, PowerPoint slides, Word documents, and PDFs directly within Claude conversations (powered by Sonnet 4.5)
- **Third-Party Connectors:** Free access to integrations with **Slack, Asana, Zapier, Stripe, Canva, Notion, Figma, and WordPress**
- **Skills:** Repeatable, filesystem-based tools that give Claude domain expertise — users can also build custom skills using their own workflows
- **Conversation Compaction:** Automatic summarization of earlier parts of discussions, enabling longer effective conversations

**Strategic Context:**
The announcement came just two days after OpenAI began serving ads to ChatGPT Free and Go users on February 9, and days after Anthropic's Super Bowl ad campaign mocking the concept of ads in AI assistants. Anthropic is clearly positioning Claude's ad-free experience + expanded free features as a competitive wedge.

### Apple's Gemini-Powered Siri Revamp Delayed Again — Pushed Beyond iOS 26.4
**Date:** February 11, 2026

Bloomberg's Mark Gurman reported that Apple's long-planned AI overhaul of Siri has hit fresh snags in internal testing, pushing key features beyond the iOS 26.4 release planned for March.

**Key Details:**
- Siri was found to **make mistakes processing queries** and **take too long** to handle requests during recent testing
- Features now being **spread across iOS 26.5 (May)** and potentially **iOS 27 (September)**
- Delayed capabilities include Siri's ability to tap into **personal data** (old text messages) and perform **multiple in-app actions** from a single command
- The revamped Siri is powered by **Google Gemini**, designed to make it more conversational like LLM chatbots
- Apple originally unveiled Apple Intelligence in **June 2024** — nearly two years ago — and has repeatedly delayed the Siri overhaul since

**Why It Matters:**
This is the latest in a series of delays for Apple's AI strategy. Competitors like Google (Gemini) and OpenAI (ChatGPT) have had full LLM-powered assistants in market for over a year, while Apple continues to struggle with quality and latency in Siri's AI upgrade.

### Amazon Restricts Employees From Using Claude Code for Production Work
**Date:** February 11–12, 2026

Amazon has restricted its engineers from using Anthropic's Claude Code for production work without formal approval, even as it remains one of Anthropic's largest investors and sells Claude via AWS Bedrock.

**Key Details:**
- Internal guidance directs staff to use Amazon's in-house AI coding assistant **Kiro** instead of third-party tools like Claude Code for production systems
- An Amazon spokesperson said there's **no explicit ban**, but **stricter requirements** apply for tools used in production code development
- Approximately **1,500 Amazon employees** supported a request on internal forums to formally approve Claude Code
- Engineers noted the irony of being asked to **sell Claude via AWS Bedrock** while being unable to use Claude Code for their own production work
- **Microsoft**, by contrast, recently asked its engineers to test Claude Code alongside GitHub Copilot and share comparative feedback

**Why It Matters:**
The restriction highlights the awkward dynamics of Amazon's $8B+ investment in Anthropic: Amazon sells Claude to enterprise customers but won't let its own engineers use Anthropic's most popular developer tool. It also underscores the intensifying competition in AI coding tools, with each major tech company pushing its own offering.

### ApplyBoard Wins IEEE Best Paper for AI Safety Breakthrough in Document Extraction
**Date:** February 11, 2026

ApplyBoard's research team won the Best Paper Award at the IEEE 15th International Conference on Pattern Recognition Systems (ICPRS) for their work on ensuring accuracy and reliability of AI-generated outputs in high-stakes applications.

**Key Details:**
- Paper: **"Embedding Confidence to Enhance Trust in AI Document Entity Extraction"**
- The system acts as a quality assurance layer, analyzing mathematical embeddings of AI outputs to assign confidence scores
- Achieved **98% accuracy** in distinguishing between correct extractions and errors
- Addresses a critical problem: how to trust AI outputs in document processing where errors have real consequences

## Industry Moves

### OpenAI Retires GPT-4o and Legacy Models Tomorrow — 800K Users Affected
**Date:** February 12, 2026 (retirement effective February 13)

Tomorrow, February 13, OpenAI will formally retire GPT-4o, GPT-4.1, GPT-4.1 mini, and o4-mini from ChatGPT, completing the transition to GPT-5.2 as the default model.

**Key Details:**
- Only **0.1% of daily users** still choose GPT-4o, but that amounts to roughly **800,000 affected users**
- All existing GPTs will have their default model **automatically updated to GPT-5.2**
- **Business, Enterprise, and Edu customers** retain GPT-4o access in Custom GPTs until **April 3, 2026**
- **ChatGPT Voice and Images** are not affected (they use separate models)
- GPT-4o's beloved warm conversational style has been partially incorporated into GPT-5.1 and GPT-5.2 via presets

**Backlash Context:**
The retirement announcement (made in late January) sparked significant backlash from users who had formed emotional attachments to GPT-4o's conversational personality. TechCrunch wrote that the reaction "shows how dangerous AI companions can be," as some users described feeling genuine loss at losing "their" GPT-4o.

**ChatGPT Ads Details:**
