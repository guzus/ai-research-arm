800 VDC: the rack that broke the 54-volt bus, and the supply chain it inherited from Porsche

NVIDIA's 800 V direct-current architecture is the AI industry's answer to 1 MW racks. The physics are real. The supply chain is mostly second-hand from electric vehicles. The grid still isn't ready.

In May 2025 NVIDIA published a developer blog announcing that the next generation of AI factories would run on 800 volts direct current end-to-end, replacing the 415-volt AC plus 54-volt DC stack that has powered server rooms for two decades. Five months later, at the OCP Global Summit, twenty-plus vendors lined up behind the architecture. Six months after that, Texas Instruments shipped a complete reference design. The bet is that 800 VDC unlocks the 1-megawatt rack — and that the SiC power devices, breakers, and isolation regimes the auto industry built for 800-volt EVs since 2019 will arrive at hyperscale just in time for Rubin Ultra in 2027. This is a structural rewrite of how electrons get to a GPU, and a wager on a supply chain that does not entirely exist yet.

Direct answer

800 VDC architecture is high-voltage DC power distribution inside (and increasingly to) AI datacenter racks. The reference design replaces the legacy 415/480 VAC plus 54 VDC chain with: a perimeter solid-state transformer (SST) that converts 13.8 kV AC straight to 800 VDC16, a single 800 VDC busbar feeding each rack, and one in-rack DC-DC step down to the GPU's ~6 V intermediate bus1. NVIDIA claims ~5% better end-to-end efficiency, ~45% less rack copper, and ~30% lower TCO versus the 54 V baseline1. Production deployment is targeted for the Rubin Ultra "Kyber" rack in 2027, with 100 kW–1 MW per rack as the design envelope1. The standardization vehicle is the OCP Diablo 400 specification (codename "Mt. Diablo"), co-authored by Google, Meta, and Microsoft9. The supply chain is largely inherited from 800 V electric vehicles, where 1200 V SiC MOSFETs and the surrounding isolation, fuses, and contactors have been in production since 20192122.

01The 1 megawatt rack broke the 54-volt bus

The architecture exists because Ohm's law stopped cooperating. A 1 MW rack at 54 VDC needs about 18,500 A of bus current to deliver its power. At 800 VDC, the same rack draws 1,250 A — a 14.8× reduction7. Because conductor losses scale as the square of current, that 14.8× current drop is roughly a 219× drop in resistive loss for the same delivered power8. The corollary is also true: holding the rack at 54 V at megawatt scale would, by NVIDIA's own modeling, consume up to 64 U of rack space just for power shelves — leaving no room for compute6.

Why now? NVIDIA's Vera Rubin generation arrives in 2026 at the NVL72 form factor; the Rubin Ultra "Kyber" NVL576 rack — 576 GPU dies in one chassis — lands in 2027 at roughly 600 kW per rack1. Meta's current production high-power rack, Catalina built on Open Rack v3, tops out at 140 kW at the 48 V ORv3 distribution voltage39. Google's deployed TPU v7 "Ironwood" rack is in the ~90 kW class, well under the 100 kW threshold the Mt. Diablo specification was designed to cross48. The trajectory from 140 kW to 600 kW in three years is what makes the voltage upgrade non-negotiable: at 1 MW per rack on 54 V, the busbar alone consumes ~200 kg of copper per rack and the I²R loss makes thermal management absurd68. The 800 V transition is not a marketing flourish — it is the path of least resistance once a single rack exceeds about 300 kW, which every NVIDIA, Meta, and Google roadmap now does.

The counterpoint, worth holding in mind through what follows: the rack-side copper that 800 V actually eliminates is small compared to the utility-side copper required to deliver a gigawatt to the campus fence. On-rack savings are roughly 200 tonnes of copper per gigawatt of capacity1. The utility side needs 25,000–40,000 tonnes per gigawatt for new substations and transmission52. The 800 V transition solves a thermal and density problem inside the rack. It does not solve the grid.

02How 800 VDC actually works

The legacy AC pathway, as documented by IEEE Spectrum, runs five conversion stages from medium-voltage utility AC down to the server PSU: MV AC step-down → LV AC → DC at the UPS → AC back out of the UPS → LV DC at the rack37, with a sixth voltage regulator step inside each server to reach sub-1 V at the die. The 800 VDC reference design collapses this. A perimeter solid-state transformer converts 13.8 kV (or higher) AC directly to 800 VDC at the row level. One vertical busbar carries the 800 VDC into the rack. Inside the rack, a single bus converter drops 800 V to about 6 V, and conventional point-of-load regulators handle the last step to the GPU rail164.

The vocabulary "800 VDC" already hides a fork in the road. The OCP Diablo 400 specification (codename "Mt. Diablo"), authored by named engineers from Meta and Google9, defines a three-wire bus: +400 V, −400 V, and a common/midpoint return — a bipolar topology that yields 800 V differential between the rails. The spec also documents a two-wire 800 V monopolar design option at the rectifier output9. NVIDIA's published reference architecture runs the monopolar 800 V design end-to-end1. Google's blog explicitly states the choice of 400 V nominal is "to leverage the supply chain established by electric vehicles, for greater economies of scale, proven quality, and more efficient manufacturing"10.

The reason the spec accommodates both topologies is that they answer different questions. The Google/Meta/Microsoft choice optimizes for components already at planet scale: 400 V SiC MOSFETs, contactors, and capacitors built for the EV market. The NVIDIA choice optimizes for fewer conversion stages and a simpler in-rack busbar — at the cost of needing 1200 V-class parts that exist but are not yet at automotive volume14. Whether the "consensus" is real or fragmented is the single biggest open question in this lane — and the answer ABB has given by buying a minority stake in DG Matrix and pursuing a different (SST-led) topology suggests the answer is not yet14.

03The vendor land-rush

NVIDIA's October 2025 OCP Global Summit post named more than twenty partners, of which the datacenter-power-systems tier comprises ABB, Eaton, GE Vernova, Heron Power, Hitachi Energy, Mitsubishi Electric, Schneider Electric, Siemens, and Vertiv47. The silicon partner list includes ADI, Infineon, Innoscience, MPS, Navitas, OnSemi, Renesas, ROHM, ST, and Texas Instruments116. Two large names are conspicuously absent from NVIDIA's named ecosystem: Vicor (which publicly rejects the 800-to-6 V direct conversion topology17) and Wolfspeed (still digesting an emergence from Chapter 11)24. ABB appears in the system partner list but is also independently pursuing a competing SST architecture via its DG Matrix stake14.

Schneider matched with a named product: an 800 VDC Sidecar capable of powering racks up to 1.2 MW, with modular conversion shelves and Live Swap capability12. Eaton is one step earlier in the funnel: an 800 VDC reference architecture at OCP using supercapacitor ride-through rather than batteries, ORv3-compatible busbars, and DC connectors with hot-aisle containment13.

The supplier that has gone furthest on actual SKUs is Delta Electronics. At NVIDIA GTC 2025, Delta unveiled a 72 kW AC-DC server power shelf converting 400–480 Vac to 800 Vdc at up to 98% efficiency51. Its OCP-shown portfolio adds a 1U 90 kW DC-DC shelf converting 800 Vdc to 50 Vdc at up to 98.5% efficiency, and a 1.1 MW-scale In-Row Power product yielding up to 92% end-to-end efficiency15. Cascaded, the 98%/98.5% stage numbers imply roughly 96.5% AC-to-50-V-DC, versus the sub-90% that the legacy 415 VAC plus 54 VDC stack typically achieves7. NVIDIA's "up to 5% end-to-end" headline number is precisely consistent with this stage math115.

The exponential growth of AI computing demands a fundamental rethinking of how we deliver power in data centers.

Kannan Soundarapandian, VP & GM High-Voltage Power, Texas Instruments16

Texas Instruments closed the ecosystem on 16 March 2026 with a complete 800 VDC architecture co-developed with NVIDIA, covering all stages from 800 V down to GPU core voltage in two main conversion stages. TI's 800V-to-6V bus converter is rated at 97.6% peak efficiency, with companion parts including a 30 kW 800 V AC/DC PSU, an 800 V hot-swap controller, and a 6 V to sub-1 V multiphase buck16. This is the single most complete vendor-shipping architecture announced to date and the moment where 800 VDC moved from "viable" to "buildable from one supplier's catalog."

04Wall Street already priced the rerating

The financial market did not wait for 2027. In the twelve months to late May 2026, Vertiv stock returned about +152% ($128.41 → $323.91), with a single-month step-change of roughly +28% in October 2025 bracketing the NVIDIA collaboration announcement and the Q3 earnings call on which Albertazzi tied the 800 VDC portfolio to Rubin Ultra18. Vicor — which publicly rejects the headline topology — returned about +634% over the same window19. Navitas, the GaN pure-play, returned roughly +385%; Wolfspeed, after Chapter 11 emergence in September 2025, ran approximately +179% in eight months24. Eaton, which is shipping a reference design rather than a productized portfolio, was up only +13%18.

The valuation gap is now wide. Vertiv trades at roughly 48× forward earnings versus Eaton at ~32× and Schneider Electric at ~24×20. The premium is partly justified by Vertiv guiding 2026 organic sales growth of 27–29%20. It is partly priced for execution at the H2 2026 product launch and 2027 shipping date Albertazzi disclosed11. And it is partly the standard hyperscale capex trade running through any pickaxe-seller: Microsoft is signaling a multi-hundred-billion FY26 capex level on the strength of capacity constraints disclosed at the Q1 FY26 print27, and the Big Four combined head toward roughly $630 billion in 2026 by independent aggregation, the multiplier on any pickaxe-seller compounds27.

We believe the initiative to go directly from 800 volts to 6 volts is ill-conceived and internally inconsistent.

Patrizio Vinciarelli, Chairman & CEO, Vicor, Q1 2026 earnings call, 21 April 202617

Vinciarelli's argument is physics, not pique: collapsing 800 V down to 6 V is a 133:1 step-down. The point-of-load step from a hypothetical 48 V intermediate to 6 V is only 8:1; the squared current penalty he flagged is meaningful. The counter-argument is that the actual NVIDIA reference does not run the bus converter at 6 V — it runs it at an in-rack intermediate close to where the GPU lives, with the highest-current sub-volt step happening millimeters from the die. The dissent is real, but the topology debate is narrower than Vinciarelli framed it. The market still bid Vicor up 634%, which says either that the market thinks Vinciarelli is wrong, or that Vicor's VPD point-of-load wins sockets even if NVIDIA's bus topology wins the row19.

Vertiv's own Q1 2026 call sharpened the bear case. Albertazzi confirmed the products are "on time" but that "shipping will be a little bit further away" — i.e., revenue recognition slips into 202711. A premium-multiple stock priced for H2 2026 shipping that ends up shipping in mid-2027 is the kind of setup that produces multiple compression even on a thesis that is broadly right.

05The hyperscaler split — bipolar versus monopolar

The cleanest read on whether the 800 VDC consensus is real is that the Mt. Diablo specification deliberately makes room for two answers. Google's April 2025 blog announcing the project at OCP EMEA used the phrasing "+/-400 VDC power delivery that can support up to 1 MW per rack" rather than 800 V monopolar48. The choice was explicit: 400 V nominal lets the spec ride the EV component supply chain10. Meta and Microsoft co-authored. The named contributors include six Meta engineers and ten Google engineers on the v0.5.2 document, with Microsoft as the originating "Mt. Diablo" sponsor9. NVIDIA's reference design — which targets a single 800 V rail end-to-end — is a different architectural choice nailed to a different cadence.

This matters operationally because the two topologies have different fault behavior, different breaker requirements, and different ground-fault-detection schemes. Bipolar ±400 V offers a midpoint ground reference and a natural way to isolate a single rail under fault; monopolar 800 V requires either an ungrounded (IT) bus with insulation monitoring or a different protection regime. The hyperscaler community has not, as of mid-2026, given a public name to which side it will collapse toward — and the OCP 800 VDC sub-committee is now explicitly trying to pull the two designs back toward convergence9.

The biggest issue we are now having is not a compute glut, but it's power… I don't have warm shells to plug into.

Satya Nadella, Microsoft CEO, on the BG2 podcast, November 202525

The Nadella quote is, in its way, the strongest single argument for 800 VDC: a hyperscaler running flat out on chips it cannot energize will accept architectural disruption it would otherwise refuse. Andy Jassy said the same thing on the Amazon Q2 2025 call, calling power "AWS' single biggest constraint" and predicting "several quarters" before resolution26. Microsoft's Q1 FY26 print echoed it: Amy Hood said Microsoft will be "capacity-constrained through at least the end of the fiscal year," short of space and power rather than chips27. When the consumers of the equipment are publicly saying their problem is power-not-chips, the suppliers' alignment behind a denser power architecture follows mechanically — even if the suppliers cannot agree on whether the rails should be bipolar.

06The EV inheritance — six years of learning, compressed

The single best reason to believe 2027 is achievable is that almost every component class 800 VDC needs has been in volume automotive production since 2021. The first production 800-volt EV was the Porsche Taycan, world-premiered on 4 September 2019 and in customer production at Zuffenhausen six days later21. Hyundai's E-GMP platform, which underpins the Ioniq 5, Kia EV6, and Genesis GV60, debuted in early 2021 and brought 800 V architecture into the $40,000–55,000 crossover segment for the first time22. By the mid-decade, BYD had shifted the Han EV (ex-base trim) to 800 V in September 2024, and Chinese 800 V passenger-car sales reached 840,000 units (6.9% penetration) in 2024, with 9.5% projected for 2025 and more than 70 distinct 800 V models on sale in China alone23.

The single most important transferable lesson is at the device level. Wolfspeed's FY2024 design-ins ran predominantly automotive, with a large share tied to 800 V platforms24. ST, Infineon CoolSiC, OnSemi, ROHM, Navitas, and (in GaN) Innoscience and EPC all built their 1200 V SiC and 650 V GaN portfolios around the EV ramp. NVIDIA's 800 VDC reference design now pulls from exactly the same die families. The auto industry's six years of 800 V experience also surfaced safety practices the datacenter industry is importing wholesale: ISO 6469-3 Voltage Class B (defined as DC voltages above 60 V) mandates isolation-resistance monitoring, HV interlock loops, equipotential bonding, and automatic discharge of DC-link capacitors to under 60 V within 5 seconds of disconnect — practices that datacenter rack designers now port over38. The orange-colored HV harness convention transfers too.

There are limits to the analogy. An automotive 800 V battery pack is roughly 100 kWh and lives behind a single contactor; a datacenter 800 V busbar carries 1 MW continuous and must be safely serviceable while live. The fault currents are an order of magnitude larger. The arc-flash physics, examined in §08, do not transfer cleanly. But the components do, and that is the supply-chain bet — Wolfspeed's Mohawk Valley 200 mm SiC fab is the same fab now feeding NVIDIA's partner roster.

07The grid is the harder problem

The 800 VDC story is mostly framed as a thermal and density story, which it is. But the binding constraint for the AI buildout sits one layer up, at the meter. The OpenAI–Oracle–Crusoe Stargate Abilene project is 1.2 GW across eight AI factory halls running roughly half a million NVIDIA GPUs — the first node in a planned ~7 GW Stargate footprint28. Meta's Hyperion is on the same scale. xAI's Memphis Colossus is past 1 GW. These are utility loads, not server rooms.

The interconnect queue tells the story. PJM data shows AI infrastructure projects entering service in 2025 took an average of more than seven years to reach operational status29. ERCOT's large-load queue ballooned to roughly 226 GW in November 2025, nearly 4× the 63 GW at end-2024, with about 77% from datacenters targeting 2030 interconnection30. Dominion Energy reported ~40 GW of datacenter capacity in various stages of contracting as of December 2024, up from ~21 GW in July 2024 — a near-doubling in five months in a single utility territory31. Conventional power transformer lead times have stretched to roughly four years, with prices up about 80% in five years46.

Hyperscalers have responded by routing around the regulated grid. Brookfield committed $5 billion to deploy up to 1 GW of Bloom Energy solid-oxide fuel cells behind-the-meter at AI datacenters in October 2025, with an initial $140M seed deployment as the first node50. GE Vernova's gas-turbine slot-reservation backlog reached ~80 GW by end-2025, with the CEO expecting turbine reservations sold out through 2030 by end-202634. Caterpillar's order backlog grew from $30.0B (end-2024) to $51.2B (end-2025), with Power & Energy the largest contributor — much of it datacenter generators with 18-month lead times46.

The 800 VDC on-rack copper savings are real but modest at this scale. At a 45% reduction in busbar copper, a 1 GW campus saves roughly 200 tonnes of copper inside the building1. That same campus, however, requires roughly 25–40 tonnes of copper per megawatt on the utility side for new substations and transmission — order 25,000–40,000 tonnes per gigawatt of campus capacity52. BNEF projects AI datacenter copper demand will average ~400,000 t/yr over the next decade, peaking at 572,000 t in 2028 and locking 4.3 Mt cumulatively into datacenters by 203543. COMEX copper (HG=F) traded near $6.47/lb in May 2026, up roughly 49% from $4.33/lb the prior July44. The on-rack savings matter — they relieve the inside-the-rack thermal constraint at MW scale, which is binding — but the headline copper story is dominated by what happens between the substation and the campus fence.

08The safety codes haven't caught up

By the letter of the codes, 800 VDC is still low voltage. Under IEC 61140 plus IEC 60038, the LV band caps at ≤1000 V AC or ≤1500 V DC; 800 V sits inside the LV regime36. NEC 2026 reorganizes around the same threshold via new Article 270 and keeps 800 V DC under low-voltage rules36. By the letter of operations, 800 V is a different planet from 48 V. OSHA fixes the "energized" threshold at 50 V AC or DC35. NFPA 70E specifies DC approach boundaries from 100 V to 800 kV, and explicitly places an 800 VDC rack squarely in qualified-electrical-worker territory — meaning live work requires an Energized Electrical Work Permit, arc-rated PPE, and insulated tools, not a data-tech with a screwdriver35.

Where the codes haven't caught up

DC arcs do not self-extinguish. AC arcs are quenched 100–120 times per second at the current zero crossing. DC has none. A vacuum interrupter's DC rating is typically only 15–30% of its AC rating for this reason — a 12 kV-AC bottle may only be rated 1.5–3 kV DC37. Today's 800 VDC deployments rely on fuse-and-disconnect combinations because the resettable 800 VDC breaker ecosystem is still maturing37. Hybrid solid-state breakers (mechanical contact in parallel with an IGBT or SiC commutation path, plus MOV energy absorption) are the path forward, but they are not yet sold as commodity rack components. If a single arc-flash incident at a pilot 800 VDC site makes the trade press, deployment slows.

The arc-flash math reinforces the point. The peer-reviewed Max Power Method, which IEEE's PES committee references as the validated empirical model for DC arc incident energy, is explicitly capped at 1000 V DC and produces validated numbers only up to ~350 V (1.7 cal/cm² at typical UPS-bus parameters)37. At 800 V DC with multi-kA prospective fault current, PPE Category 2 (8 cal/cm²) is easily exceeded and Category 4 (>40 cal/cm²) is plausible without fast-clearing protection — extrapolated, not measured, because the published empirical envelope does not yet cover this regime37. The codes are quietly catching up — IEC 62548-1:2023 plus AMD1:2025 contains the most current codified DC arc-fault language, written for PV but borrowed liberally by datacenter HVDC vendors37.

The transferable EV practice is straightforward. ISO 6469-3 Voltage Class B requires active isolation-resistance monitoring (an IMD — typically ≥100 Ω/V), HV interlock loops so any service disconnect breaks the contactor before pins are exposed, equipotential bonding of conductive enclosures, and automatic discharge of DC-link capacitors to <60 V within 5 seconds of disconnect38. These ported almost intact into the 800 VDC datacenter literature. The not-yet-ported piece is first-responder protocol: the datacenter facilities and fire-service community has not yet codified the orange-harness, ISO 17840 labeling regime that automotive uses for first responders. The EV industry had a noisy 2019–2024 cycle of incidents that produced its current discipline. Datacenter 800 V will likely repeat it. Two recent pre-800 V incidents — three workers burned in a 2022 arc-flash at Google's Council Bluffs facility, and an electrocution at QTS Fayetteville in 2025 — already show the baseline rate of electrical injury, both at voltages below 800 V49.

09What could break the thesis

The strongest case against 800 VDC is not that DC is wrong but that the announced architecture is operationally unready in three load-bearing places.

Supply slip. Solid-state transformers are the gating upstream component. Enphase's IQ SST roadmap, indicative of the broader non-NVIDIA-partner timeline, runs late 2026 demo → 2027 pilot → 2028 volume45. NVIDIA's 2027 Kyber/Rubin Ultra target requires the SST input stage to be in volume before the racks ship into facilities. Wolfspeed shipped its first commercial 10 kV SiC MOSFET in calendar Q1 202624 — the discrete enabling 13.8 kV-AC-to-800 VDC SST without device stacking — but the device is at sampling, not volume. ABB's strategic bet via DG Matrix is that this gap is precisely where it overtakes the others14. If SSTs slip, NVIDIA's racks ship into facilities still running legacy AC distribution, blunting the TCO case.

Architectural fragmentation. The Mt. Diablo spec deliberately allows both ±400 V bipolar and 800 V monopolar9. Google's blog explicitly framed the bipolar choice as supply-chain pragmatism over technical elegance10. The ecosystem has not yet committed to either as the production default. ABB has chosen a third path. The "800 VDC consensus" is currently four architectures sharing one label.

The safety regime is on a knife edge. A single high-profile arc-flash incident at a pilot site — particularly one involving a fatality or a high-profile worker injury — would likely trigger a regional or operator-level pause. NFPA 70E is conservative by construction; OSHA enforcement could escalate without a new rule. The pre-800 V baseline rate of datacenter electrical injury already produced two notable events in three years49. The math of 800 V at multi-kA prospective fault current does not favor an unblemished pilot phase.

The "30% TCO" headline is contested. Independent analyses put the OpEx win in the 7–20% range and note that front-loaded SST capex of roughly $1.0–1.5 M/MW partially offsets the on-rack savings45. NVIDIA's "up to 30%" is a vendor ceiling. Vertiv's Q1 2026 CEO commentary already moved the shipping date "a little bit further away" from H2 2026 into 202711, which is the kind of disclosure that compresses a 48× forward-earnings multiple even on a thesis that is broadly right1820.

The voltage leapfrog. The first-principles I²R logic that justifies 48 V → 800 V equally justifies 800 V → 1500 V. Photovoltaic systems already operate at 1500 V DC and the IEC LV ceiling tolerates it36. Heron Power and several other infrastructure vendors have published whitepapers framing 1500 V as the next plateau36. If the industry leapfrogs to 1500 V before 800 V hits volume, today's 800 V designs become a stranded interim step rather than the architecture of the AI era.

None of these alone breaks the thesis. Together they make 2028–2029 the more honest commercial-volume window than 2027. The bull case requires the SST supply to land, the safety regime to hold without incident, the topology debate to converge, the headline savings to be defensible after capex normalization, and the leapfrog to 1500 V to be at least a decade away. The fact that all of those have to go right does not mean any will go wrong. It does mean the multiples being paid for it today bake in execution that has not yet happened.

10Why this matters

Three reasons. First, this is the rare moment when the binding constraint on AI scale visibly moves from silicon to electrons — not as rhetoric, but as a measurable shift in CFO disclosures (Hood, Jassy, Nadella) and a measurable capex-multiplier flowing through the pickaxe sellers (VRT, ETN, ABB, IFX, WOLF, NVTS, MPWR, TXN). When the constraint moves, the prices move; the prices in §04 are the constraint moving. Second, the standardization fork inside the Mt. Diablo specification is one of the rare cases where a hyperscaler triumvirate (Google + Meta + Microsoft) and a chip vendor (NVIDIA) have publicly disagreed on a load-bearing infrastructure choice while pretending to converge. Reading the architecture as monolithic understates the fragility. Third, the EV-to-datacenter component crossover is one of the cleanest case studies anywhere of a supply chain bootstrapped for one industry being repurposed for another — every named SiC vendor in NVIDIA's silicon list owes their 1200 V die volume to Porsche, Hyundai, BYD, and the Wolfspeed Mohawk Valley fab. The fact that this transfer is invisible in most coverage of the AI buildout is what makes it most interesting: the next decade of AI infrastructure is being built on parts the auto industry already qualified.

Red-team pass: of the three load-bearing claims most likely to be contested — NVIDIA's "up to 5% efficiency" / "45% copper" / "30% TCO" headline numbers, the 2027 timing alignment, and the universality of the 800 V choice across hyperscalers — the red-team adversarial review surfaced no fully independent contradicting primary source for the headline efficiency and copper figures (they recur across NVIDIA, Vertiv, Delta, and Flex with the same logical derivation), but the timing and universality claims show real fissures: Vertiv's own CEO has already moved shipping into 2027, and the Mt. Diablo spec encodes a bipolar/monopolar fork that the OCP sub-committee is still trying to close. The case for the architecture is grounded in physics that the EV industry has already validated. The case against is grounded in operational readiness that the announcing vendors have not yet demonstrated at MW scale. Both can be true at once.
