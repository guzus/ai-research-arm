The Aschenbrenner book, name by name

Thirty parallel agents combed every primary source they could reach. What they brought back is a portfolio that reads less like a hedge fund and more like a thesis statement with prices attached.

Situational Awareness LP's December 31, 2025 13F lists $5.52 billion across just 25 unique names. Two-thirds of the book is concentrated in five positions, every one of them a wager on the same proposition: that the binding constraint to superintelligence is not the model, it is the megawatt and the modulator. This article walks every position — what the company actually does, why it earned a seat in a manifesto-funded book, and which 2025–2026 catalyst would have to break for the thesis to break with it.

01From manifesto to brokerage account

Leopold Aschenbrenner — Columbia '21 valedictorian at 19, FTX Future Fund alumnus, OpenAI Superalignment researcher until his April 2024 dismissal — published the 165-page "Situational Awareness: The Decade Ahead" two months after leaving OpenAI.3 The essay extrapolates the GPT-2 to GPT-4 jump forward another four years, layers in compounding compute and algorithmic gains, and lands on AGI by ~2027 with a corollary that "trillions of dollars will go into GPU, datacenter, and power buildout before the end of the decade." The fund is the literal financial expression of that corollary.

The other name on the door is Carl Shulman — Director of Research, longtime AI-safety researcher at Oxford's Future of Humanity Institute and Open Philanthropy, and, critically for an LP base allergic to first-time-PM risk, a Clarium Capital alumnus from the Peter Thiel era.2 The barbell is deliberate: Aschenbrenner supplies the forecast and the founder Rolodex, Shulman supplies the buy-side scar tissue.

The disclosed LP roster matches the founder Rolodex tightly — Patrick Collison, John Collison, Nat Friedman, Daniel Gross, plus East Rock's Graham Duncan as advisor.2 Terms reported in trade press: 2-and-20, $25M minimum check, two-year lock with a two-year quarterly redemption tail. Reported gross return was +47% net of fees in H1 2025 against S&P 500 +6% and the PivotalPath tech HF index +7%.2 Full-year 2025 net returns have not been disclosed in any primary source we could fetch.

Frame

The fund is structurally long the buildout its founder's essay warns about. That tension — safety alarm in prose, picks-and-shovels long in book — is the most interesting thing about Situational Awareness LP, and it is what the rest of this article is really about.

02The four sleeves

Group the 25 names by what they actually do, and the book sorts itself into four sleeves: compute (neocloud + miner-converters), pipes (foundry, optics, memory), power (gas, fuel cells, mobile turbines, EPC, real estate), and a single short. The proportions:

Compute & miners 44% Pipes 30% Power 25% Other / hedge 1%
  • Compute & miners — CRWV (calls+common), CORZ, IREN, APLD, CIFR, RIOT, HUT, WYFI, BTDR, CLSK, BITF
  • Pipes — INTC calls, LITE, SNDK, COHR, TSEM
  • Power — BE (common+calls), EQT (common+calls), SEI, KRC, PSIX, LBRT, PUMP, BW
  • Other — INFY puts (the only short)

The absences are loud. There is no Nvidia, no TSMC, no Broadcom, no Microsoft, no Meta, no Alphabet — the names that a generic "AI portfolio" would buy first. The thesis is that those equities already price the buildout; the value-capture is one layer below, where supply rather than demand sets the price.

03Power: the binding constraint

Aschenbrenner's essay leans hard on a single number: the LBNL DOE-commissioned 2024 report projecting US data-center load from 176 TWh in 2023 to 325–580 TWh by 2028 (4.4% → 6.7–12% of national load).4 The corollary that makes a power sleeve buyable rather than just thematic is grid-interconnect duration: LBNL's "Queued Up 2025" puts median completion time at 5+ years across PJM, SPP, ERCOT, NYISO and CAISO, with 77% of 2000–2019 queue capacity withdrawn before energizing.5 Behind-the-meter (fuel cell, gas turbine, recip engine) compresses 5-year waits into 16–30 months. The fund's power sleeve is essentially a basket of vendors selling that compression.

Bloom Energy (BE) — 16.5% combined, the keystone

Solid-oxide fuel cells deployed behind the meter on natural gas. Q1 2026 revenue $751M, +130% YoY, with data-center demand the dominant driver.6 The two catalysts: on 2026-04-13 Bloom and Oracle expanded their MSA to up to 2.8 GW with 1.2 GW firm — the stock printed +22% on the announcement; in 2026-01 AEP signed a 20-year offtake for up to 1 GW of SOFCs ($2.65B headline value per Utility Dive).7 Plug Power has neither the bankability nor the hyperscaler-scale shipments; Caterpillar's gas gensets are cheaper per kW but lose at gigawatt scale on permitting. Bloom is the only US vendor shipping grid-independent power at this pace. Risks: Oracle is plausibly >40% of forward backlog (management says "over half" of data-center backlog is non-Oracle — not independently verified), and most "GW" figures are MSAs / options rather than firm POs.

EQT Corp (EQT) — 3.1% combined, the molecule

Largest US natural-gas producer, pure-play Appalachian (Marcellus/Utica), vertically integrated post the July 2024 Equitrans midstream acquisition. Production guide 2,275–2,375 Bcfe in FY26 (~6.2 Bcfe/d).8 The marquee AI tie-in: an April–July 2025 supply deal with the Homer City Energy Campus for up to 665,000 MMBtu/d over multi-year term, feeding a 4.4 GW gas plant powering AI compute starting 2027 — described in the trade press as one of the largest single-site gas purchases in North American history.9 Calls on top of common express convex exposure to the gas-price spike scenario where AI build-out and LNG export demand converge.

Solaris Energy Infrastructure (SEI) — 1.6%, the mobile-MW rental

Frac-sand logistics shop transformed by the September 2024 Mobile Energy Rentals acquisition into a fleet of behind-the-meter gas turbines. Power Solutions overtook Logistics as the larger segment by Q2 2025; ~910 MW earning revenue in Q1 2026.10 April 2026 saw a 600+ MW, 10-year deal with an investment-grade global tech affiliate; the xAI/Stateline JV at Southaven, MS targets 1.1+ GW by Q2 2027. Risk flag: the Morpheus Research short report (Apr 2025) documents 96% Stateline revenue concentration in 2024, unpermitted Memphis turbines, and 25-year depreciation life vs. peer Aggreko's 8–12.11

The smaller power names — same trade, different equipment

  • Power Solutions International (PSIX, 0.4%) — Weichai-controlled (46.5% post-Aug-2025 trim) fuel-agnostic genset OEM; data-center exposure is real but management refuses to quantify named hyperscaler revenue.12
  • Liberty Energy (LBRT, 0.2%) — Permian fracker with a Liberty Power Innovations subsidiary that signed a 1 GW / 5-year framework with Vantage Data Centers (2026-01-05) and a 500+ MW Bergen Engines genset order for AI loads (May 2026). Founder Chris Wright is the sitting US Energy Secretary as of 2025-02-03 — divestiture done, but the political optics overhang remains.13
  • ProPetro (PUMP, 0.2%) — Permian frac plus the PROPWR division: a confirmed 60 MW hyperscaler contract delivers Q2 2026, and an April 28 2026 Caterpillar framework reserves 1.5–2.1 GW of capacity (~$1.1B equipment commitment). 99% of today's revenue is still frac — the power business is the option.14
  • Babcock & Wilcox (BW, 0.2%) — 150-year-old boiler shop with a $2.4B EPC contract (March 2026) to design-build 1.2 GW of gas-fired capacity for Base Electron / Applied Digital AI campuses, Siemens Energy supplying turbines. A securities class action alleges B&W misled investors on the same contract.15
  • Kilroy Realty (KRC, 0.9%) — the second-derivative power play: West Coast Class-A office REIT whose SoMa 201 Third Street tower has absorbed Harvey AI (155K sf signed-then-expanded through Q1 2026) and Hive AI. AI tenants drove ~40% of JLL's Q1 2026 SF leasing.16 Not "Kilroy is AGI," but "Kilroy is AGI's landlord."

04Compute: the picks of the picks-and-shovels

The largest sleeve. The single trade idea is that energized substations and IT-ready shells — built originally to mine bitcoin — are now the cheapest path to deployed AI hosting capacity through 2027, and that the neocloud (CoreWeave) is the customer that monetizes them.

CoreWeave (CRWV) — 22% combined, the keystone customer

Pure-play GPU-as-a-service neocloud running multi-year take-or-pay contracts. Q1 2026 revenue $2.08B (+112% YoY), revenue backlog $99.4B, 3.5 GW contracted power.17 OpenAI cumulative commitments now $22.4B after the September 25 2025 expansion (+$6.5B). The use of calls on top of common sizes the position to maximum delta — a near-term re-rating bet on Q2–Q4 2026 backlog conversion and Stargate ramps. The downside-asymmetric concentration risk: Microsoft remained ~60%+ of revenue (the S-1 disclosed 62% for FY24), and any reallocation toward Maia in-house silicon is binary. Net loss $740M in Q1 2026, with $31–35B 2026 capex against a debt stack measured in the tens of billions — the model is a long-duration interest-rate bet wrapped in a compute story.

Core Scientific (CORZ) — 7.6%, the substation-converter

Post-Chapter 11 (emerged January 2024) infrastructure operator that reconverted mining substations into AI hosting under a 12-year, ~$10B take-or-pay anchored by CoreWeave (~590 MW critical IT load expanding to 800 MW gross).18 On 2025-10-30, shareholders rejected the $9B all-stock CoreWeave merger (203.4M against vs. 20.8M for) — the standalone path stays. Q1 FY26 closed $3.3B senior secured notes @ 7.75% due 2031 to fund the build-out from 245 MW billable to 590 MW by early 2027. Single-customer concentration on CoreWeave is the whole bear case in one sentence.

IREN — 6.0%, the hyperscaler-direct miner-converter

Australian-founded, BC + West Texas footprint, ~4.5 GW+ of secured power across Childress (750 MW) and Sweetwater (2 GW). Q3 FY26 revenue $144.8M with AI Cloud already $33.6M (+94% QoQ).19 The catalysts that defined the position: 2025-11-03 Microsoft 5-year / $9.7B AI cloud contract with 20% prepayment and 200 MW of liquid-cooled Childress for GB300 GPUs; 2026-05-07 Q3 FY26 added an NVIDIA $3.4B / 5-year AI cloud contract plus a $2.1B NVIDIA option on 30M shares. Gold-tier counterparty status — hyperscaler-direct, not neocloud-dependent.

Applied Digital (APLD) — 5.0%, the single-tenant landlord

Polaris Forge 1 in Ellendale, ND: 400 MW critical IT, 100% leased to CoreWeave under ~15-year triple-net leases totalling ~$11B contracted revenue across three tranches signed through August 2025.20 October 7 2025 Macquarie executed a $5B perpetual preferred equity facility ($112.5M first draw, $787.5M expected by November). Cloud business spinning out into ChronoScale via the EKSO reverse merger, with APLD retaining ~97%. The concentration risk is severe — 100% of HPC backlog rests on CoreWeave, which itself rests on Microsoft/OpenAI.

Cipher Mining (CIFR) — 2.8%, the Google-backstopped option

Texas miner with $0.028/kWh power and three hyperscale leases totalling $11.4B contracted revenue, anchored by Fluidstack (Google backstop raised to $1.73B on the November 2025 56 MW expansion).21 700 MW contracted across three hyperscale leases as of Q1 2026; Barber Lake topped out construction in 127 days. Zero HPC revenue delivered yet — the entire thesis depends on H2 2026 energization.

Hut 8 (HUT) — 0.7%, the BTC ringfence + AI landlord

Vertically integrated power and digital infrastructure across Canada and the US Sun Belt. The two contracts that justify the seat: 2025-12 $7B / 15-year River Bend lease with Fluidstack (Anthropic offtake, Google backstop, 245 MW + 1 GW option); 2026-05-06 Beacon Point 352 MW 15-year, $9.8B base-term lease with an undisclosed investment-grade hyperscaler.22 The BTC business is ringfenced inside the 80%-retained American Bitcoin (ABTC) subsidiary — Trump-family-associated, which adds headline risk.

The smaller miners

  • Riot Platforms (RIOT, 1.4%) — Corsicana 1 GW substation energized, 756 MW of planned IT capacity remaining. The first AI tenant signed in May 2026: AMD exercised a 25 MW expansion to 50 MW at Rockdale, $636M / 10-yr.23 Corsicana hyperscaler lease still pending — that's where the re-rating sits.
  • WhiteFiber (WYFI, 0.5%) — Bit Digital's GPU-cloud carve-out, IPO'd August 7 2025 at $17. Q1 2026 revenue $21.9M (Cloud 77%, Colo 22%, +190% YoY). ~70.7% still owned by Bit Digital; a single anchor customer (Cerebras is widely speculated, not officially confirmed) drives most revenue.24
  • Bitdeer (BTDR, 0.4%) — Jihan Wu's Bitmain-descended Singapore-listed vehicle. Q1 2026 revenue $188.9M (+169% YoY); shipping commercial AI cloud from Tydal Norway and Malaysia (GB200 NVL72 live 2026-01-13). Tether raised its stake to 20.1% on 2026-02-20.25
  • CleanSpark (CLSK, 0.3%) — Late-mover: hired SVP AI Data Centers late 2025, acquired 447-acre Brazoria TX site with 300 MW ERCOT-approved in January 2026. Still ~100% mining revenue; AI is "advanced negotiations" with first delivery late-2027.26
  • Bitfarms / Keel Infrastructure (BITF, 0.3%) — Rebranded to Keel (ticker shifting to KEEL) April 1 2026. 2.2 GW pipeline including 350 MW Panther Creek PA via Stronghold legacy power assets, Macquarie project-financed. Zero signed HPC tenant leases as of Q1 2026; entire thesis depends on landing leases by year-end.27
Customer-of-customer risk

Roughly 35%+ of the miner sleeve's contracted AI-hosting revenue routes through CoreWeave, Fluidstack, or other neoclouds — not end-tenants. The fund itself is also long CoreWeave (the largest combined position), so the same counterparty risk shows up twice. JPMorgan's "Sell" on IREN explicitly flagged the circular Nvidia-as-customer-and-supplier dynamic. A single neocloud refinancing failure cascades through the stack.

05Pipes: the bottleneck inside the bottleneck

Nvidia is absent. So is TSMC. The bet is that one layer below the obvious AI longs — domestic foundry, optical interconnect, silicon-photonics fab, AI-adjacent memory — supply rather than demand is what sets the price.

Intel (INTC) — 13.5%, the foundry option

The Intel position is calls plus a single token share — a deliberately bimodal structure. Intel 18A entered HVM in January 2026 with Panther Lake shipping; reported yields are stabilizing near ~60% versus the ~70–80% profitability threshold (yield figures from KeyBanc note, not Intel-disclosed).28 Three buttressed downside floors at near-identical strikes: the August 22 2025 US government 9.9% stake (433.3M shares @ $20.47, $8.9B funded by remaining CHIPS grants + Secure Enclave), SoftBank's $2B at $23 (Aug 18 2025), and Nvidia's $5B at $23.28 closed December 2025.29 Critically, the Nvidia tie-up is for x86+GPU co-developed SKUs and explicitly does not include Foundry — the Foundry call remains naked optionality on 18A/14A external customer wins. External foundry revenue was only $174M in Q1 2026.

Lumentum (LITE, 8.7%) + Coherent (COHR, 1.6%) — the optical duopoly

AI back-end networks are migrating from 800G to 1.6T in 2026–2027. The gating component is EML (electro-absorption modulated laser) chips plus indium-phosphide capacity — and Lumentum is the only volume supplier of 200G-per-lane EMLs, which means Coherent and the Chinese transceiver houses must source from LITE.30 Q3 FY26 (Apr 2026) Lumentum revenue $808M (+90% YoY), Q4 guide $960M–$1.01B. Nvidia validated the bottleneck in March 2026 by writing $2B equity checks into each of LITE and COHR with multi-year EML/transceiver supply commitments. Holding both is a duopoly bet on the only two firms that can supply the laser chips whether the form factor stays pluggable or shifts to co-packaged optics.

Tower Semiconductor (TSEM, 1.5%) — the silicon-photonics merchant fab

Specialty foundry (Migdal Haemek + Newport Beach + 300mm via Intel New Mexico). On 2026-05-13 Tower disclosed $1.3B in signed 2027 silicon-photonics wafer contracts with $290M of customer prepayments banked, plus a CPO foundry platform launched November 12 2025 and $300M of SiPh capex doubling capacity by year-end 2025.31 Tower is the merchant SiPh foundry of record — the pure-play exposure to silicon-photonics independent of which fabless designer (Marvell, Lightmatter, Ayar Labs, Ranovus are widely speculated, not officially disclosed by Tower) wins the CPO race.

Sandisk (SNDK, 4.5%) — the AI-NAND pure play

Spun from Western Digital on 2025-02-21. Q3 FY26 revenue $5.95B (+251% YoY) with Data Center segment at $1.46B (+645% YoY, now ~25% of revenue) — the fastest mix shift in memory.32 The forward catalyst: on 2026-02-25 Sandisk and SK Hynix announced joint OCP standardization of High Bandwidth Flash (HBF) at 512GB/stack and 1.6TB/s — 8–16× HBM capacity at comparable bandwidth — targeting AI inference KV-cache and weight storage adjacent to GPUs. First samples 2H 2026, inference silicon early 2027. The bet vs. Micron/Hynix is purer torque to a NAND supercycle plus the only HBM-adjacent AI roadmap on a US-listed pure-play.

06The short: a single put on Indian IT services

The only short-equivalent position in the entire 13F is a $8.9M notional put on 500,000 Infosys ADRs (0.16% of fund). Position sizing is symbolic — but the position itself is doctrinal: the essay's claim that AGI displaces remote knowledge work first lands directly on the Indian IT-BPM industry, ~5M employees doing the application maintenance, L1/L2 support, testing/QA and BPO that LLMs increasingly automate.

The income statement has started to bend the right way for the short. Infosys shed 8,440 heads in Q4 FY26 alone, its first material sequential drop; FY27 constant-currency guidance is just 1.5–3.5%; CFO Jayesh Sangra conceded on the Q4 call that "productivity will get passed back to the client largely."33 HCL Tech's CEO publicly forecast a 3–5% revenue decline from "AI deflation"; the top-5 Indian IT firms shed a combined ~7,000 net heads in FY26, with TCS alone cutting 23,460.

Productivity will get passed back to the client largely. Jayesh Sangra, Infosys CFO, Q4 FY26 call (April 2026)

The counter-case is real and not priced as if it weren't. Infosys won $14.9B TCV in FY26 (55% net-new), signed a strategic OpenAI partnership in April 2026, and has Topaz Fabric inside 90% of its top 200 clients across 4,600+ AI projects. Forward PE ~17× sits below the cycle peak. Picking Infosys vs. TCS is a liquidity choice — TCS has bigger displacement exposure but a 70%+ Tata Sons float and thin US options; Infosys ADR has deep listed options and no controlling shareholder.34

07What could break the book

Five vectors, ranked by how much would actually break:

  1. Premise
    2027 passes without AGI.

    The fund's name is a date as much as a phrase. The same names every other AI fund holds become a crowded unwind, and the customer-of-customer chain (miner → neocloud → hyperscaler) compresses fastest. The fund's two-year lock and quarterly redemption tail give some, not much, cushion.

  2. Counterparty
    CoreWeave or Fluidstack stumbles.

    22% of the book is CoreWeave directly; another ~15% (CORZ, APLD, parts of HUT/CIFR) is one layer removed. A neocloud refinancing failure, a Microsoft Maia reallocation, or a single Fluidstack tenant slip cascades twice through the book.

  3. Bottleneck
    The grid catches up.

    FERC's December 2025 PJM colocation order and PJM's Tapestry/Google AI-assisted queue review target 1–2 year queue reviews. If interconnect timelines actually compress, the Bloom / Solaris / Liberty / ProPetro premium for "fast power" compresses with them.

  4. Tail
    18A yields stall.

    The Intel call is structured to limit downside, but a yield miss this year converts the Foundry option into common equity in a structurally loss-making segment. The gov + NVDA + SoftBank floors stop the bleed; they don't pay the option premium back.

  5. Reputational
    The Solaris short report becomes the next 2025 hedge-fund tape-bomb.

    Morpheus Research's April 2025 piece flagged 96% Stateline revenue concentration, unpermitted Memphis turbines, an MER co-founder's prior environmental-crime conviction, and 25-year depreciation life vs. peer Aggreko's 8–12. SEI is only 1.6% of the book, but the optics of a marquee Aschenbrenner power name being a governance story would compound the rest.

Premise / behavior gap

The essay warns of an AI-safety catastrophe. The fund is structurally long the buildout that catastrophe would require. Aschenbrenner has not publicly reconciled the two stances since launch — there is no major podcast appearance confirmed in 2025–26, and Shulman has been silent on the fund. If the answer is "you can't slow it down, so own the suppliers," that is a defensible position — but it deserves to be stated, and so far it has not been.

08Methodology and sources

Thirty research agents ran in parallel: 25 per-issuer analysts (one per unique 13F holding) plus five thematic deep dives (the fund itself, the power-bottleneck thesis, the miner-pivot economics, the AGI compute supply chain, and the Infosys-short thesis). Each agent was instructed to reason from first principles, stress-test the AGI premise, and cite only URLs it had actually fetched. Findings were synthesized into the article above, with material unverified claims flagged inline using ara-mark.
