## 01. From $17 to $46 in nine weeks

PENG has done something rare for a $2B semicap-adjacent name: it broke its own 200-day moving average, doubled, and then doubled again. The price closed at $17.60 on the last Friday of March 2026 and at $45.71 on the third Tuesday of May[^1]. {accent}Three catalysts, in a fortnight window, did almost all of the work.{/}

:::

The setup begins in October 2024, when shareholders approved the rebrand from SMART Global Holdings to Penguin Solutions, the ticker flipping from SGH to PENG on October 15[^2]. The pitch was clean: rename the conglomerate after its best growth story (Penguin Computing, acquired for up to $85M in 2018[^3]) and let the multiple re-rate toward AI-infrastructure peers. For a year, that didn't work. The stock stayed in a $17–$26 channel as Advanced Computing — the segment that includes Penguin Computing and Stratus — disappointed quarter after quarter.

Then the move:

:::timeline
- {date: 2026-01-30, headline: "CEO transition", body: "Mark Adams steps down after five years; Kash Shaikh appointed President & CEO, pivots messaging to ‘AI factory platform’.[^32]"}
- {date: 2026-03-16, headline: "OriginAI inference launch", body: "CXL-based MemoryAI KV-cache server (up to 11 TB) launched; Tier-1 financial institution named first customer.[^33]"}
- {date: 2026-04-01, headline: "Q2 FY26 print + guidance raise", body: "GAAP EPS $0.58 vs $0.19 consensus; FY26 revenue-growth guide doubled from +6% to +12%.[^7]"}
- {date: 2026-04-14, headline: "AMD/Shell Houston deployment", body: "864-system EPYC 9654 immersion-cooled HPC cluster disclosed; AMD reseller credit lights up the AI-infra narrative.[^34]"}
- {date: 2026-05-13, headline: "Rosenblatt $54 PT + ‘60% AI mix’ disclosure", body: "Rosenblatt raises target from $32 to $54; management discloses AI-driven business >60% of H1 sales. Best single session in 22 months.[^35]"}
:::

Two structural conditions made the move violent rather than orderly. First, short interest at the late-April Nasdaq settlement was 8.71M shares — 17.7% of float, 5.2 days to cover — and had been as high as 22.85% earlier in the year[^4]. Second, free float is small (roughly 50–51M shares outstanding, with the passive-index block — ==unverified: BlackRock ~14%, Vanguard ~12%, FMR ~9%, State Street ~6% per the December 2025 DEF 14A==[^5]) crowding the deliverable supply. When AI-infra momentum funds chased the May 13 disclosure, there were not many marginal shares to buy. The result was a tape that gapped, not drifted.

The reflex story — "PENG is the next SMCI, just three years earlier in the cycle" — does not survive a careful reading of the Q2 8-K[^7]. The price action is real; the catalyst chain is real. The {accent}composition{/} of what re-rated is what we spend the rest of this note on.

## 02. Anatomy of the Q2 print

The April 1 release was a {accent}beat-and-raise{/} on every consensus comparable analysts watch — and the headline metric was the cleanest blowout most subscribers have seen all year:

:::stats
- {label: GAAP EPS,      value: $0.58, note: "vs $0.19 cons. (+205%)"}
- {label: Non-GAAP EPS,  value: $0.52, note: "vs $0.52 PY (flat)"}
- {label: Net sales,     value: $343M, note: "−6% YoY, missed $374M cons."}
- {label: Non-GAAP GM,   value: "31.2%", note: "+40bps YoY"}
- {label: Adj. EBITDA,   value: $50.4M, note: "+12% YoY"}
- {label: FY26 rev guide, value: "+12%", note: "raised from +6%"}
:::

The 3x EPS beat looks heroic until you read the cash-flow statement. The quarter contained a $27.036M gain on the {accent}disposition of an equity investment{/} — Penguin's residual 19% stake in Zilia Technologies, the Brazilian memory-module business it had divested back in November 2023, was sold to Lexar Europe (a Longsys affiliate) on March 30, 2026 for $46.08M[^6]. Roughly $0.50 of pre-tax EPS — most of the gap between $0.58 GAAP and $0.19 consensus — came directly from that one-shot gain[^7]. Non-GAAP EPS strips the gain out, and on that line PENG earned exactly what it earned a year earlier: $0.52, flat.

Revenue, the lower-quality but more honest metric, was a {accent}miss{/}: $343M against a $374M sell-side number, down 6% year-on-year[^8]. The headline mix of beat-on-EPS-miss-on-revenue is exactly the print profile of a company {accent}selling lower volumes at better margins{/} — usually a yellow flag for a company that markets itself as a growth name. Non-GAAP gross margin was 31.2% — up 40bps YoY — but GAAP gross margin slipped 130bps to 27.3% on a $5.78M stolen-shipment inventory write-off[^9]. Management is asking investors to ignore the stolen shipment, the $10M Q1 equity-investment impairment[^36], the $27M of Zilia gain, $15.2M of H1 stock-based comp, $7.5M of intangibles amortization, and $4.7M of restructuring[^26]. The non-GAAP bridge is busy.

What did move the price was the guide. Management raised FY26 net-sales growth from +6% ±10% to +12% ±5%, non-GAAP EPS from $2.00 ±$0.25 to $2.15 ±$0.15, and GAAP EPS from $0.85 ±$0.25 to $1.30 ±$0.15[^10]. Note that {accent}gross-margin guidance went down{/} — non-GAAP GM from 29% ±1% to 28% ±0.5%, GAAP from 27% ±1% to 26% ±0.5%[^10]. So the guide is volume-and-mix led, with margin compression baked in. That is consistent with the actual Q2 print: revenue weaker than expected, margin slightly better[^7], EBITDA lifted by a one-time. It is not consistent with the stock acting as if it had just walked into a Super Micro-style supercycle.

:::callout(kind=warn, label="Low-quality beat")
$0.58 GAAP - $0.27 (after-tax) Zilia gain = roughly $0.31 of underlying GAAP EPS. The same arithmetic against Q2 FY25's $0.09 GAAP is roughly +240% YoY on a clean basis — still a strong quarter, just not a 3x consensus blowout. Most of the headline came from a divestiture closing on the last business day of the fiscal quarter.
:::

## 03. The hyperscaler that disappeared

If you only read Q2's segment table, you would think Advanced Computing — the segment that markets the OriginAI factory platform, ClusterWare software, and Stratus fault-tolerant systems — had a catastrophic quarter[^11]:

:::slope(left-label="Q2 FY25", right-label="Q2 FY26", unit=$M)
| Segment            | Q2 FY25 | Q2 FY26 |
|--------------------|---------|---------|
| Advanced Computing | 200.2   | 115.7   |
| Integrated Memory  | 105.3   | 171.6   |
| Optimized LED      | 60.1    | 55.7    |
:::

Advanced Computing fell 42.2% year-on-year[^11]. The segment that is supposed to be the AI growth story is in absolute decline. Management's explanation, given on the Q1 call in January and repeated on the Q2 call in April: PENG is {accent}deliberately exiting hyperscaler hardware{/}[^37].

> Our AI infrastructure business is transitioning from a hyperscaler concentration toward a more diversified non-hyperscaler customer base across enterprise, neocloud and sovereign AI.
> — Kash Shaikh, President & CEO, Q2 FY26 release[^12]

Read carefully: "transitioning … may negatively impact net sales during the transition." That is the language a CEO uses when a contract has not renewed. The FY2024 10-K disclosed that one Advanced Computing customer represented {accent}18% of total net sales in FY24, 23% in FY23, and 20% in FY22{/}[^13]. The FY25 10-K kept the format: one Advanced Computing customer was 14.1% of total, and top-ten concentration jumped from 58% to 66%[^14]. PENG has never named the customer in its filings, but Penguin Computing's own marketing site has identified Meta as its anchor AI-research partner since 2022, when the joint Meta AI Research SuperCluster was disclosed at 760 NVIDIA DGX A100s and 6,000+ GPUs[^15]. The arithmetic — a ~10-year partnership, 18-23% of consolidated revenue per year, and a hyperscaler hardware program that just stopped recurring — points hard at the same name.

The diversification PENG is offering in its place is real, but not yet large:

:::rank-list
- {label: "Tier-1 financial institution (MemoryAI CXL KV-cache customer)", value: "Q2 win", pct: 92}
- {label: "Deepgram (via Dell partnership)", value: "H1 logo", pct: 60}
- {label: "Georgia Tech research cluster", value: "H1 logo", pct: 55}
- {label: "Haein — Korean sovereign-AI deployment", value: "Q2 win", pct: 75}
- {label: "Penguin OriginAI standard pipeline (4 named in Q2 call)", value: "Q2 wins", pct: 50}
:::

Seven new AI/HPC logos in H1 FY26 versus three in H1 FY25 is a real pipeline indicator[^37]. But none of them is worth what Meta was worth. To stress-test scale: PENG's Advanced Computing segment generated $648.4M for the full fiscal year 2025[^16]. Super Micro guided its FY26 revenue range at $38.9B–$40.4B[^17]; Dell ISG's AI-server revenue alone is guided to ~$50B for FY27 against a $43B AI backlog already on the books[^18]. PENG is roughly {accent}3% of SMCI's revenue scale{/} and is shrinking, not growing, in its AI segment. The reframing of the business as "platform, not hardware" — ClusterWare, OriginAI managed services, the new CXL MemoryAI server[^33] — has to do an enormous amount of work to bridge the gap. Even if every new logo PENG signs in H2 FY26 closes at the average of its disclosed wins, the dollar value would not refill the hyperscaler hole.

The Q2 disclosure that "AI-driven business" is more than 60% of H1 sales, on its own, was the headline that re-rated the stock on May 13[^19,35]. Read alongside the segment table, it means {accent}roughly $412M of H1 revenue is AI-driven{/} — which can only be true if you include the integrated-memory revenue going into AI servers, not just Advanced Computing systems revenue. "AI-driven" is therefore a {accent}management-defined mix metric{/}, not a clean segment cut. The auditable line — Advanced Computing — is down 22% across H1 FY26 versus H1 FY25[^11,24].

## 04. The Korean anchor, not the Japanese one

The single most-cited piece of bull thesis on PENG is that the stock has a "SoftBank-style" strategic investor behind it. That framing is wrong. The investor is {accent}SK Telecom{/}, the South Korean carrier and SK Group affiliate — not SoftBank, the Japanese holding company. Both invest in AI infrastructure; the deal mechanics and the strategic rationale are different. An EDGAR full-text search across all filings under PENG's CIK returns {accent}zero hits for "SoftBank"{/}; the only major preferred-equity strategic in this name is SK Telecom, acting through a Delaware special-purpose vehicle called Astra AI Infra LLC[^20].

The terms, taken from the 8-K filed on closing day (December 16, 2024):

:::kv
- {term: Closing date,        def: "December 13, 2024"}
- {term: Investment size,     def: "$200M (single tranche)"}
- {term: Security,            def: "200,000 convertible preferred shares at $1,000 face"}
- {term: Conversion price,    def: "$32.80784 / share (30% premium to 15-day VWAP to 2024-07-12)"}
- {term: Coupon,              def: "6% cumulative, quarterly, PIK or cash at issuer's option"}
- {term: Force-convert,       def: "VWAP ≥ 150% of strike (~$49.21) for 15 consecutive trading days, post 2026-12-13"}
- {term: As-converted shares, def: "~6,096,103 ordinary; ~10.4% on a fully-diluted basis"}
- {term: Board seat,          def: "1 (Min Yong Ha, SKT Chief Development Officer); 2 if board ≥12"}
- {term: Lockup,              def: "1-year on all CPS/Ordinaries; 2-year disposition ban to Competitors/Activists"}
- {term: Voting (standstill), def: "Vote with the Board majority recommendation"}
:::

A 6% cumulative dividend, paid in-kind, at PENG's option, accrues a slow {accent}dilution overhang{/}: every quarter PENG can elect to pay SK Telecom in additional preferred shares rather than cash, which compounds the as-converted count[^21]. Through year two of the deal the dilution effect is small (~6% per annum on 200,000 CPS = 12,000 additional CPS per year ≈ 366K incremental common-equivalents)[^20]. Beyond year five, if the stock has not triggered force-conversion, SK Telecom can call for cash redemption, which is the deal's harder constraint on PENG's free cash flow[^31].

The force-conversion trigger ($49.21 for 15 consecutive trading days, on or after December 13, 2026) is the price level the market should care about[^21]. The stock closed at $45.71 on May 19, 2026 — about 7.6% below the trigger[^1]. {accent}If PENG holds above ~$49 from late 2026 forward, SK Telecom converts and the overhang resolves cleanly into common.{/} If PENG trades back into the $20s, SK Telecom continues to accrue 6% PIK on a $32.81-strike preferred — a position {accent}structurally similar to a deeply in-the-money convertible bond{/} that the issuer must amortize at par.

Strategically, SK Telecom framed the deal as the procurement leg of its broader AI stack — Anthropic + Lambda + Perplexity on the model and cloud sides; Penguin + SK Hynix on the systems-and-memory side[^22]. The CEO press-conference framing leaned on Penguin's 75,000-GPU installed base[^22], not on its hyperscaler customer roster. That is the right read of the rationale: SK Telecom wanted a deployment partner for sovereign-style AI factories in the Korea / Indo-Pacific corridor; PENG wanted strategic validation, an HBM supply ally via SK Hynix, and $200M of clean balance-sheet capital. It is not the AI-capex thesis of Stargate. {accent}Stargate is funded by SoftBank, MGX, OpenAI and Oracle; PENG is not a Stargate participant.{/}

## 05. Memory carried the quarter

The segment doing the actual revenue work in FY26 is {accent}Integrated Memory{/} — the legacy SMART Modular business that, until 2023, depended heavily on Brazilian operations under the Lei de Informática tax regime. After PENG divested 81% of SMART Modular Brazil to Lexar Europe in November 2023[^23] and the final 19% on March 30, 2026, the segment is now a {accent}Malaysia-and-US specialty-memory business{/}: high-reliability DRAM modules for servers and embedded systems, with growing AI-server attach.

:::donut(center-label="Q2 FY26 mix")
- {label: Integrated Memory,  value: 50}
- {label: Advanced Computing, value: 34}
- {label: Optimized LED,      value: 16}
:::

A year earlier (Q2 FY25), the same mix was 29% / 55% / 16%[^11]. The flip is dramatic: Integrated Memory grew {accent}+63% YoY{/} in Q2 alone, +41% in Q1, and is on a +53% pace for H1 FY26[^24,19]. Three forces compose this:

- The HBM/DDR5 pricing cycle is in a tailwind regime. SK Hynix is shipping HBM3e at record gross margins and the broader server-DRAM ASP path is favorable.
- One specialty-memory customer is 14.1% of consolidated FY25 revenue[^25] — a different name from the Advanced Computing 18% customer of FY24[^13]. PENG has therefore moved from one large customer concentration in AI systems to another large concentration in memory modules.
- AI servers are memory-rich. Every Blackwell-class rack ships with more DDR5 than the Hopper rack it replaces, and PENG's specialty SI/storage memory modules attach into both the server build (their primary historical channel) and PENG's own OriginAI inference appliances (a small but growing internal channel)[^33].

The signal that worries us is the {accent}inventory build{/}. Total inventory rose from $151M at FY24 close to $255M at FY25 close, and to $322M at Q2 FY26 close[^26]. Finished goods more than doubled YoY in the FY25 10-K disclosure[^14]. Some of that build is customer-funded (21% under logistics-services arrangements in FY25 vs 14% prior year, per the 10-K)[^14], which insulates PENG from a pure write-down. But the absolute level is elevated, the cycle is well-advanced (HBM is now in its third year of tightening), and memory write-downs have ended every prior memory rally in PENG/SGH history. {accent}This is the line item that breaks the bull thesis fastest if the memory cycle inverts.{/}

:::callout(kind=info, label="Optimized LED")
The third segment — Optimized LED — sits in a 51/49 joint venture with San'an Optoelectronics of China and operates the assets PENG bought from Cree in March 2021 for up to $300M[^38]. LED revenue was $256M in FY25 and $55.7M in Q2 FY26, down 7.4% YoY[^14,11]. Cree's annual LED revenue peaked at $834M in 2014 before halving in the six years before the sale to SGH[^27]. The melting-ice-cube framing fits: this is not the AI segment; it is what is left of a structurally cyclical commercial-LED business. PENG has not signaled a divestiture in disclosures, but a strategic separation would clean the equity story considerably.
:::

The honest read of the segment table is that PENG today is roughly {accent}a half-memory, one-third systems-integrator, one-sixth LED business{/}, with the memory leg accelerating, the systems leg shrinking, and the LED leg flat. The systems leg is the one investors are buying on the AI thesis. The memory leg is the one paying the bills.

## 06. Priced as AI infrastructure, executing as a memory specialist

At $45.71, PENG's market cap is roughly $2.32B, enterprise value $2.34B (about $20M net debt, $489M cash against $443M long-term and $0 current debt as of Q2 FY26)[^28]. On the FY26 consensus revenue of $1.57B and EPS of $2.23, that's 1.48x forward sales and 20.5x forward earnings; on the FY27 consensus ($1.82B, $2.67), 1.28x sales and 17.1x earnings[^29]. The peer comp does not flatter PENG on either revenue multiple or earnings multiple — though it does flatter PENG on gross margin:

So PENG trades at the {accent}highest revenue multiple{/} of its scaled peer set despite running the third-highest gross margin and the smallest scale. The trade is therefore that PENG either grows faster than the consensus +12% guide implies, or earns higher margins than the guide bakes in (28% GAAP GM, with the guide trending lower), or executes a multiple convergence the other direction (down to the peer average around 1.0x P/S, implying ~$35 fair value). Nine analysts cover the name; six are Strong Buy or Buy, three are Hold or Negative on near-term setup[^30]. The dispersion is wide: $54 at the top, $24 at the bottom[^30,35]. {accent}Only Rosenblatt's $54 sits above spot.{/} The trade today is not a bet on consensus; it is a bet against consensus. The bull bet is that the May 13 mix disclosure has reset baseline expectations and that the Q3 FY26 print (early July 2026) will validate the +12% guide and lift FY27 numbers[^29]. The bear bet is that the Zilia gain is non-recurring[^6], the AC drag continues[^12], the memory cycle peaks within four quarters, and the multiple compresses back toward the peer average.

One useful frame: SMCI guided FY26 revenue growth of {accent}~80%{/} year-on-year; PENG guides {accent}~12%{/}. SMCI is priced at 10.3x forward earnings; PENG at 18.8x. PENG bulls argue gross-margin quality justifies the spread (28% vs 8%). PENG bears argue {accent}absolute growth is what re-rated SMCI in 2024-25{/}, and on absolute growth PENG is the wrong instrument.

## 07. What breaks the thesis

The cleanest bear case on PENG is a stack of disclosed-and-verifiable items, not a Hindenburg-style activist short. There is no live short report; there is no live class action; there is no smoking gun. There is a careful read of the disclosures.

:::callout(kind=danger, label="Bear inventory")
**Quality of Q2 beat.** Roughly $27M of pre-tax gain on the Zilia sale produced most of the GAAP EPS surprise; underlying non-GAAP EPS was flat YoY. {flag:yellow}

**Hyperscaler exit.** Advanced Computing is -22% across H1 FY26 vs H1 FY25; FY26 guidance explicitly excludes hyperscaler hardware revenue. The 18% concentration customer (FY24) is not in the run-rate. {flag:red}

**Memory dependence.** 50% of Q2 revenue, one customer at 14.1% of consolidated FY25, $322M of inventory on the balance sheet. Cyclical. {flag:yellow}

**Sub-scale vs peers.** PENG's full-year AC revenue ≈ $648M against SMCI's $40B and Dell ISG AI backlog $43B; mgmt acknowledges the bake-off economics. {flag:red}

**SBC + intangibles add-back.** $79M of non-GAAP add-backs in FY25 (~5.8% of revenue) — mostly intangibles amortization (less cash-equivalent than SBC), but still a material reconciliation item. {flag:yellow}

**SK Telecom 6% PIK preferred.** At $45 the overhang resolves cleanly above $49.21 force-convert; below the conversion price the preferred behaves like a deep-in-the-money convert with a 5-year cash-redemption tail. {flag:yellow}

**Insider tape on the rally.** Director Maximiliane Straub sold 15,000 shares between $44 and $49 across May 11–14, 2026, for ~$680K. CEO transition mid-rally; outgoing CEO's recent Form 4s are tax-withholding (Code F), not open-market. {flag:yellow}

**No backlog disclosure.** PENG provides no AC backlog or signed-contract figure — peers (Dell $43B, SMCI quarterly book-to-bill) do. {flag:yellow}

**Consensus PT $33.57.** The stock is already 27% above the average sell-side target. Only Rosenblatt's $54 is above spot. {flag:red}
:::

The single bear claim we tested and rejected: the "SoftBank preferred is a down-round trap" framing in some short-side commentary. The deal is not SoftBank's, and the anti-dilution language in PENG's 8-K is standard weighted-average — not full ratchet — adjusted only for splits, certain dividends, and reclassifications[^31]. The dilution math is the dilution math; it is not a structured trap.

## 08. Counter-arguments, and what would change the verdict

Steel-man for the bull. There is a real version of the AI-infrastructure rerating thesis on PENG, and it requires three things to be true.

First, the {accent}OriginAI platform must monetize software and managed services at attach rates Dell and SMCI cannot match{/}. ClusterWare is a real differentiator (PENG's installed base claim of 75,000+ GPUs under management is the strongest single platform-economics data point in the file). The MemoryAI CXL KV-cache server, paired with NVIDIA Dynamo, is a credible inference-tier differentiation if CXL hits its installed-base growth curve through 2027. PENG could become the boutique platform vendor for AI factories in the Korea-Indo-Pacific corridor — too small to win hyperscaler bake-offs, too sticky to lose enterprise/sovereign deployments. That is a coherent strategic identity. It is not yet the financial reality.

Second, the {accent}sovereign-AI demand wave must continue past 2026{/}. Korea (Haein), the Middle East (UAE, Saudi data-center programs), and select European national champions are real buyers; SK Telecom's $200M validates PENG as the procurement vehicle for the SK Group AI stack. The Korea contribution alone could replace half the lost hyperscaler revenue by FY27. The question is durability: sovereign-AI capex cycles are political and lumpy, and PENG is not the prime in any of them — it is a tier-one systems integrator working through partners.

Third, the {accent}short squeeze setup is not over{/}. Average daily volume is ~2M shares; short interest at the April 30 settlement was 8.7M; days to cover 5.2. If Q3 FY26 (early July) reaffirms or raises guidance, the same mechanical squeeze that took PENG from $17 to $45 could extend it through the $49.21 SK Telecom trigger, force conversion, and remove the largest single overhang on the equity story. This is a setup, not a thesis.

What would change our verdict in either direction? We are watching three specific data points across the next two prints.

:::stats
- {label: Q3 FY26 AC sales,      value: "≥$170M", note: "needed to ratify the diversification claim"}
- {label: Q3 FY26 inventory,     value: "≤$330M", note: "above = cycle-top warning"}
- {label: "Days >$49.21 by Q1 FY27", value: "≥15", note: "triggers SK Telecom force-convert"}
:::

A clean Q3 print with Advanced Computing back at $170M+ (a meaningful sequential snap-back from $116M) would mean the new logos are scaling faster than even bulls expect. An inventory print above $330M would mean PENG is building into a demand wave it cannot yet name. Fifteen consecutive trading days above $49.21 between December 2026 and any forward window would resolve the SK Telecom overhang into common — a cleaner cap table and a higher quality of the equity story.

Until then, the bet on PENG at $46 is the bet that the {accent}consensus price target is wrong{/}. That is sometimes the right bet. It is not, on this evidence, an obviously right bet.
