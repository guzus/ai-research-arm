IBM projects production adoption within approximately five years, but Reuters and TNW noted IBM has not yet named a manufacturing partner. "0.7nm" is a generation label, not a literal physical feature size — TNW correctly cautioned against reading it as one. The more fundamental skepticism: IBM has demonstrated beautiful process technology before (its 2nm node announced May 2021 was never commercialized by a foundry) and the real question is whether any foundry will manufacture this at volume. Without a committed partner, this is a research paper with market hype attached. The strongest counter-hypothesis: this is IBM's "strategy theater" — technically impressive but arriving too late or too expensive for broad AI deployment, similar to the 2nm trajectory.</p>
