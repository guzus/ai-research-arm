#### 3. Meta acquires Assured Robot Intelligence — first dedicated robotics AI acquisition signals the "VR-first" era is over
**Evidence**: Meta has finalized its acquisition of Assured Robot Intelligence, a startup building AI models that allow robots to predict human movement and adapt to real-world environments. Founders Lerrel Pinto (NYU) and Xiaolong Wang (UCSD) are joining Meta Superintelligence Labs. The acquisition was reported by @QuikInsightz at 18:27 UTC May 1 and corroborated by @techportalntw at 18:13 UTC. The framing: "This marks the definitive end of the 'VR-first' era and a massive leap into Physical AI."

The acquisition is Meta's first dedicated robotics AI play, targeting the same humanoid robotics market Tesla (Optimus) and Figure are contesting. Meta's prior AI investments were primarily software — Llama, ad targeting, recommendation systems, VR/AR interfaces. This is a talent and IP acquisition that positions Meta in the embodied AI race. The founders are not anonymous — Pinto and Wang are established robotics researchers with publication records in robot learning, human-robot interaction, and sim-to-real transfer.

The "end of VR-first" framing is overstated (Meta's core business remains ads, and Quest/VR continues), but the signal is real: Meta is building AI for the physical world, not just the digital one. Combined with Meta's $125-145B 2026 capex guidance (from the April 30 earnings cycle), this acquisition suggests some of that infrastructure spend is destined for robotics training.

- @QuikInsightz (18:27 UTC May 1): "Meta has finalized its acquisition of Assured Robot Intelligence, a startup building AI 'brains' for humanoid forms. This marks the definitive end of the 'VR-first' era..." — https://x.com/QuikInsightz/status/2050281059678580975
