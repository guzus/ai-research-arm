If enacted, it would be the most aggressive US AI regulation to date, exceeding the Biden-era executive order on safety testing.

The mechanism is ambiguous: "discussing" doesn't mean "drafting an EO," and even a draft EO doesn't mean it will be signed. But the signal is clear: the administration that campaigned against AI regulation is now discussing pre-release government review.

The replies to the NYT tweet reveal the fault lines: anti-regulation voices call it "big government picking winners and losers," pro-safety voices call it "too late," industry voices worry about "the same bureaucracy killing AI progress that killed Starship." One reply notes the irony: "The President of reducing federal oversight, everyone." Another: "This is simply to punish Anthropic for not cooperating."
