### 🚩 Skeptic's corner
- **"Anti-doomer vs doomer" framing oversimplifies the AI policy debate** — The Axios/Politico framing presents the EO derailment as a binary conflict between "doomers" (who want safety rules) and "anti-doomers" (who want deregulation). The actual policy question is more nuanced: the draft EO reportedly gave Treasury a leading role in coordinating security vulnerability response — an unusual assignment that legitimately raises questions about agency competence. Sacks, Zuckerberg, and Musk have a self-interested position (they run AI companies), but the "anti-doomer" label obscures legitimate questions about whether a hastily-drafted Treasury-led working group would produce better outcomes than no framework at all. The question isn't "regulation vs no regulation" — it's "what kind of regulation, enforced by whom, with what authority." The draft EO may have been genuinely poorly constructed, and its derailment might lead to a better version rather than permanent inaction.
- **Bindu Reddy's LLM quality assessment — one user's experience vs published benchmarks** — Reddy claims Flash 3.5 is "looping and expensive," Opus 4.7 is "a regression," and GPT 5.5 xHigh "gives up." But Flash 3.5 just ranked #1 on Zapier Automation Bench (per @OfficialLoganK, prior cycle). Opus 4.7 is widely considered best-in-class for complex reasoning. The gap between published benchmarks and practitioner experience is real and persistent, but one CEO's tweet doesn't overturn systematic evaluation. That said, Reddy is not a random user — Abacus AI runs production AI workloads, and her choice to use Sonnet 4.5 over the newer models is a revealed-preference data point worth tracking. If multiple credible builders independently report similar regressions, it becomes a story.
- **"Defiance AI niche ETFs" — SEC filing, not live product** — The four ETFs (Inference Chip, Packaging & Testing, Memory & Photonics, Plumbing & Electrical) are filed but not approved. ETF filings are cheap — many never launch or gather meaningful AUM. The filing is a market-structure signal, but the products themselves may not materialize. Treat as an indicator of how the AI investment thesis is fragmenting, not as a tradeable development.
