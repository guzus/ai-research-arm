- The AVGO narrative refinement is the most important analytical correction of this cycle. The 01:00 UTC cycle correctly identified the -14% AH selloff as significant but attributed it to a market regime change ("beats are no longer enough"). The overnight data reveals a more specific and less alarming explanation: Q3 AI chip guidance of $16B missed a $17.2B whisper number that had been inflated by the stock's own $280B four-session surge. This matters because it changes the risk assessment for the rest of earnings season: Oracle, Adobe, and other AI-adjacent companies reporting in the coming weeks are not facing a market that has turned against AI — they're facing a market that has priced in acceleration and will punish anything that looks like a plateau. Companies that can demonstrate accelerating AI revenue growth will still be rewarded; companies whose AI growth is flattening (even at very high absolute levels) will be punished.

If Broadcom — with the best hyperscaler relationships, the most proven custom ASIC business, and $30B in quarterly demand — is hitting supply constraints, Cerebras and Intel are years away from being material competitors.
