#### 3. Microsoft and OpenAI formally end exclusivity — partnership restructured with revenue caps, multi-cloud freedom
**Evidence**: In a joint amendment posted on both companies' websites, the Microsoft-OpenAI partnership has been restructured to end exclusivity. Key changes: (1) OpenAI can now offer its latest models through other cloud providers, stripping Microsoft of exclusivity rights; (2) Microsoft remains "primary cloud partner" with products shipping first on Azure; (3) Microsoft's license to OpenAI models through 2032 is no longer exclusive; (4) Microsoft no longer pays a revenue share to OpenAI, but OpenAI continues to pay revenue share to Microsoft until 2030, now subject to a total cap. Sam Altman posted on X that the company is "now able to make our products and services available across all clouds." This is the formal codification of tensions visible since February when OpenAI signed a $50B AWS compute deal that Microsoft threatened to sue over. The amendment arrives on the eve of Musk's trial (see Story 1) — the very exclusivity Musk is suing over has now been formally dismantled.

#### 4. David Silver's Ineffable Intelligence raises $1.1B seed at $5.1B — the biggest AI seed round in history
**Evidence**: Ineffable Intelligence, founded by David Silver (lead researcher behind AlphaZero, AlphaGo, and former DeepMind RL lead), raised $1.1B in seed funding at a $5.1B post-money valuation. The company is building "AI superlearners" — models that learn without human data using reinforcement learning. SiliconANGLE broke the story. The bet is entirely on Silver's pedigree and the thesis that RL-based learning can produce capabilities text-prediction-based models cannot.
