### Goldman Sachs Deploys Anthropic's Claude for AI Agents — Stock Jumps 4.3%
**Date:** February 6–10, 2026

Goldman Sachs revealed it has been working with embedded Anthropic engineers for six months to co-develop autonomous AI agents, in a move that sent GS stock up 4.3% on Friday and continued to generate market buzz heading into Monday.

**Key Details:**
- Goldman is building AI agents using **Claude Opus 4.6** with its 1M-token context window
- Two initial agent use cases: **trade accounting** (processing transactions) and **client vetting/onboarding** (KYC compliance)
- Goldman executives were "surprised" at how capable Claude was at tasks beyond coding — particularly accounting and compliance that require parsing large documents while applying judgment
- Agents are in "early stages" with plans to launch "soon" — no specific date provided
- Future applications could include **employee surveillance** and **investment banking pitchbooks**

**Market Impact:**
Goldman Sachs stock ended February 7 up 4.3%, with analysts noting the Anthropic partnership as a significant catalyst for the bank's AI transformation strategy.
