### Elon Musk Unveils Grok 4.5 — From-Scratch 1.5T-Parameter Model in Private Beta, Claims Opus-Level Performance

**Date:** June 28, 2026 | **Sources:** [Analytics Insight — Elon Musk Unveils Grok 4.5 Beta, Claims it Beats Claude Opus](https://www.analyticsinsight.net/news/elon-musk-unveils-grok-45-beta-claims-it-beats-claude-opus-ai), [Investing.com — Musk says Grok 4.5 enters private beta at SpaceX and Tesla](https://www.investing.com/news/company-news/musk-says-grok-45-enters-private-beta-at-spacex-and-tesla-93CH-4764089), [Social News XYZ — Grok 4.5 enters private testing at SpaceX, Tesla](https://www.socialnews.xyz/2026/06/28/grok-4-5-enters-private-testing-at-spacex-tesla-elon-musk/), [@elonmusk announcement](https://x.com/elonmusk/status/2071184354756477041)

- **What's net-new:** On June 28 Musk announced **Grok 4.5**, built on xAI's new
  **1.5-trillion-parameter "V9" foundation model trained from scratch**, with
  **Cursor data added in supplemental training**, now in **private beta at
  SpaceX and Tesla**. Musk said early internal evals show performance "close
  to, perhaps exceeding **Opus**," and committed to releasing **completely
  from-scratch new models every month** for the rest of 2026 (released via
  SpaceX). It's xAI's first from-scratch model since the Grok 3 series and a
  ~3× parameter jump over Grok 4.3 (~500B).
- **Skeptic's note:** No third-party benchmarks exist — the model is internal
  beta only and the "exceeding Opus" claim is unaudited and version-ambiguous.
  A "monthly from-scratch" cadence is extraordinarily aggressive for true
  pretraining cycles; it may describe fine-tuned variants.
- **Why it matters:** A genuinely Opus-competitive Grok that ships
  **unrestricted** — while GPT-5.6 Sol and Claude Mythos 5 are government-gated
  — would expose an asymmetry in the de facto US frontier-model licensing
  regime.

### DeepSeek Closes First External Round — $7.4B at $50B+ Valuation, Largest in Chinese AI History

**Date:** June 26–28, 2026 | **Sources:** [TechFundingNews — DeepSeek raises $7.4B at $50B valuation](https://techfundingnews.com/deepseek-raises-7-4b-at-50b-valuation-in-first-ever-external-funding-round/), [FinSMEs — DeepSeek Raises Over $7.4 Billion at $50B+ Post-Money](https://www.finsmes.com/2026/06/deepseek-raises-over-7-4-billion-in-maiden-funding-at-a-post-money-valuation-exceeding-50-billion.html), [Trending Topics — Only the Chinese State Gets Voting Rights](https://www.trendingtopics.eu/deepseek-raises-7-4-billion-only-the-chinese-state-gets-voting-rights/), [Seeking Alpha — DeepSeek completes record $7B-plus fundraising](https://seekingalpha.com/news/4603859-deepseek-completes-record-7b-plus-fundraising-valuation-tops-50b---report)

- **What's net-new:** DeepSeek raised **>¥50B (~$7.4B)** at a **post-money
  valuation exceeding $50B** — its first-ever external round. **Founder/CEO
  Liang Wenfeng personally contributed ~$3B** (¥20B, the single largest check,
  retaining voting control); **Tencent** put in ~$1.5B (¥10B); **CATL** ~$740M
  (¥5B); other backers include **NetEase, JD.com, IDG Capital**.
- **Unusual structure:** Most outside investors received **no voting rights and
  a five-year lock-up**; only China's state-backed **National AI Industry
  Investment Fund** got direct equity with voting rights and no lock-up. The
  reporting frames Anthropic's Mythos preview as the catalyst that convinced
  Liang DeepSeek needed far more capital; the lab plans to **double every
  department** toward AGI.
- **Why it matters:** A $50B+ valuation for a lab whose primary product is a
  discount API sets a sharp benchmark against Anthropic (~$965B) and OpenAI
  (~$852B), and shows Chinese capital concentrating to fill the US
  export-control gap — with the state as the privileged shareholder.

### OpenAI Ships GPT-5.6 Sol / Terra / Luna — But Gated to a US-Government-Approved Preview

**Date:** June 26, 2026 | **Sources:** [OpenAI — Previewing GPT-5.6 Sol](https://openai.com/index/previewing-gpt-5-6-sol/), [VentureBeat — only accessible to limited preview partners, per US Gov](https://venturebeat.com/technology/openai-unveils-gpt-5-6-sol-terra-and-luna-models-but-only-accessible-to-limited-preview-partners-for-now-per-us-gov), [Axios — OpenAI releases powerful new GPT model under restrictions](https://www.axios.com/2026/06/26/openai-gpt-sol-terra-luna-trump), [MacRumors — OpenAI Launches GPT-5.6 Sol, Terra, and Luna in Limited Preview](https://www.macrumors.com/2026/06/26/openai-gpt-5-6-sol/)

- **What's net-new:** OpenAI began a **limited preview of the GPT-5.6 family**:
  **Sol** (flagship), **Terra** (balanced), **Luna** (fast/cheap). Sol sets a
  new state of the art on **Terminal-Bench 2.1** and improves agentic coding,
  biology, and cybersecurity. New **"max" reasoning effort** and an **"ultra"
  mode** that spawns subagents were introduced.
- **Gating:** At the **US government's request**, access is restricted to a
  small group of trusted partners (shared with the government) before broader
  release — the same national-security posture now applied to Anthropic's
  frontier tier. This resolves the "GPT-5.6 unconfirmed/leak-only" carry from
  the June 23 report.
- **Pricing (per 1M tokens):** Sol **$5 in / $30 out**; Terra **$2.50 / $15**;
  Luna **$1 / $6**. GA "in the coming weeks."

### OpenAI + Broadcom Unveil "Jalapeño" — OpenAI's First Custom Inference Chip

**Date:** June 24, 2026 | **Sources:** [OpenAI — OpenAI & Broadcom unveil Jalapeño inference chip](https://openai.com/index/openai-broadcom-jalapeno-inference-chip/), [TechCrunch — OpenAI unveils its first custom chip, built by Broadcom](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/24/openai-unveils-its-first-custom-chip-built-by-broadcom/), [CNBC — OpenAI and Broadcom reveal Jalapeno](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/24/openai-and-broadcom-reveal-jalapeno-first-ai-chip-in-partnership.html), [Tom's Hardware — reticle-sized ASIC built in a nine-month cycle](https://www.tomshardware.com/tech-industry/artificial-intelligence/broadcom-and-openai-unveil-custom-built-jalapeno-inference-processor-openais-first-chip-is-a-massive-reticle-sized-asic-built-in-an-ultra-fast-nine-month-development-cycle)

- **What's net-new:** OpenAI's first custom **"Intelligence Processor,"**
  co-designed with Broadcom and **purpose-built for LLM inference**. Taped out
  in a claimed **nine-month design-to-tape-out cycle** (said to be the fastest
  high-performance ASIC cycle ever), partly accelerated using OpenAI's own
  models. Early testing shows **performance-per-watt substantially above
  current SOTA** and roughly **50% cost savings vs. typical AI GPUs**.
- **Deployment:** Initial deployment by **end of 2026**, ramping toward
  **gigawatt-scale** data centers with Microsoft and other partners.
- **Why it matters:** A concrete move to cut OpenAI's Nvidia dependence and
  inference cost amid the industry-wide shift from "tokenmaxxing" to
  efficiency.

**Date:** June 29, 2026 | **Sources:** [Fortune — Samsung, SK reportedly to invest $1.3 trillion over 10 years](https://fortune.com/2026/06/28/samsung-sk-hynix-chip-fabs-investment-fabs-1-3-trillion-10-years-nand/), [CNBC — Samsung and SK Hynix investing in AI, semiconductor mega-projects](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/29/samsung-sk-hynix-reported-1point3-reported-trillion-spending-plans.html), [Benzinga — South Korea unveils $520 billion AI push, four new fabs](https://www.benzinga.com/markets/tech/26/06/60148890/samsung-sk-hynix-to-build-four-new-chip-plants-as-south-korea-unveils-520-billion-ai-push), [Cryptobriefing — up to $1.3 trillion in tech investments](https://cryptobriefing.com/south-korea-samsung-sk-hynix-tech-investment/)

- **What's net-new:** At a June 29 Blue House briefing, President **Lee Jae-myung**
  unveiled the "Three Mega-Projects" industrial strategy under which **Samsung and
  SK Hynix will invest as much as ₩2,000T (~$1.3T) over a decade**. The hard,
  genuinely-new core is **~₩800T / ~$518–520B for four new semiconductor fabs**
  (Samsung + SK Hynix each building 4–5 fabs around Gwangju), plus Samsung chip-
  packaging plants in South Chungcheong, SK Hynix NAND expansion in North
  Chungcheong, and what would be **Korea's largest AI data center in Asan**, with
  robotics infrastructure.
- **Market reaction — sold the news:** **Samsung Electronics fell ~4.7%** and
  **SK Hynix ~3.1%** Monday as investors braced for front-loaded capex; the KOSPI
  shed value even as smaller-cap names rallied.
- **Skeptic's note:** The $1.3T headline is a cross-sector aggregate (chips +
  data centers + batteries + displays + robotics) that **substantially overlaps
  Samsung's previously announced ~$600B plan**; the genuinely new chip money is
  closer to the ~$518B four-fab figure.
- **Why it matters:** Samsung + SK Hynix anchor global **DRAM and the vast
  majority of HBM** supply — the exact bottleneck for AI training/inference.
  New fab/memory capacity at this scale reshapes the 2028–2030 HBM outlook and
  Micron's competitive position.

## Bottom Line

The June 24–29 window — the first covered since June 23 — was the busiest in
weeks. **OpenAI shipped two firsts:** the **GPT-5.6 Sol/Terra/Luna** model
family (gated to a US-government-approved preview) and **Jalapeño**, its first
custom inference chip with Broadcom. **Musk answered with Grok 4.5**, a
from-scratch 1.5T model in private beta claimed near/above Opus — unrestricted,
which would test whether US model-gating applies evenly. **DeepSeek closed
$7.4B at $50B+**, the largest Chinese AI round ever, with the state as the only
voting investor. And the US–China frontier fight sharpened on two fronts:
**Anthropic's Alibaba "distillation" letter to Washington** and the
**GLM-5.2 cyber-capability** debate. The through-line: a hardening **de facto
licensing regime** for US frontier models, Chinese capital and capability
concentrating to route around it, and Europe (per Austria's letter) realizing
it has been locked out of both.
