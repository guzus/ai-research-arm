### **Trump AI Executive Order Full Text Published On Whitehouse.gov — "Promoting Advanced Artificial Intelligence Innovation And Security" Is A VOLUNTARY Framework (Correcting Yesterday's "Mandatory" In-Window Reporting): No Mandatory Licensing / Preclearance / Permitting; Establishes Voluntary "Covered Frontier Model" Benchmarking By Treasury / NSA / CISA, Up-To-30-Day Government Pre-Release Access For Participating Developers, And A New Treasury-Run "AI Cybersecurity Clearinghouse" (To Be Stood Up Within 30 Days) To Coordinate Vulnerability Scanning / Validation / Patch Distribution With Industry And Critical-Infrastructure Operators; Google's Kent Walker Calls It "An Important Step Forward," Sam Altman Says It Sets "The Balance Right"**

**Date:** Tuesday June 2, 2026 (order signed/published; coverage and industry reaction extending into June 3) | **Sources:** [The White House — Promoting Advanced Artificial Intelligence Innovation and Security](https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/2026/06/promoting-advanced-artificial-intelligence-innovation-and-security/), [NPR — Trump's new AI safety order seeks voluntary review of new models](https://www.npr.org/2026/06/02/nx-s1-5844347/ai-safety-trump-executive-order), [CNBC — Trump signs AI executive order asking companies to give government early access to models](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/02/trump-executive-order-ai.html), [Roll Call — Executive order sets voluntary cyber reviews for advanced AI](https://rollcall.com/2026/06/02/executive-order-sets-voluntary-cyber-reviews-for-advanced-ai/), [WilmerHale — New Executive Order Addressing Early Government Access to Frontier AI Models](https://www.wilmerhale.com/en/insights/client-alerts/20260602-new-executive-order-addressing-early-government-access-to-frontier-ai-models), [Federal News Network — AI executive order sets stage for new cybersecurity directives](https://federalnewsnetwork.com/cybersecurity/2026/06/ai-executive-order-sets-stage-for-new-cybersecurity-directives/), [Council on Foreign Relations — Assessing Trump's Executive Order on AI Oversight](https://www.cfr.org/articles/assessing-trumps-executive-order-on-ai-oversight), [BusinessToday — Trump's new AI order could change how OpenAI, Google, and Anthropic launch models](https://www.businesstoday.in/technology/artificial-intelligence/story/trumps-new-ai-order-could-change-how-openai-google-and-anthropic-launch-models-534614-2026-06-03)

- **The correction (load-bearing):** Yesterday's PM addition relayed CNN/CNBC in-window reporting that the order *required* AI companies to give the government 30-day pre-release access — i.e., a **mandatory** regime. **The published text is voluntary.** The order's operative language states that **nothing in it creates a mandatory governmental licensing, preclearance, or permitting requirement** for the development, publication, release, or distribution of new AI models.
- **What the order actually does:**
  - **Voluntary "covered frontier model" benchmarking** — developers may engage the federal government to determine whether a model meets the "covered frontier model" designation, assessed against benchmarks for **"advanced cyber capabilities."**
  - **Up-to-30-day pre-release access (voluntary)** — participating developers may provide the government access to covered frontier models for **up to 30 days before releasing them to other trusted partners**, subject to confidentiality and cybersecurity protections; developers collaborate with the government to select trusted partners for early access.
  - **AI Cybersecurity Clearinghouse** — within **30 days**, the **Secretary of the Treasury**, in consultation with the **National Cyber Director** and others, forms a clearinghouse in **voluntary** collaboration with industry and critical-infrastructure operators to coordinate **scanning for software vulnerabilities, discovery/validation, and remediation/patch distribution.**
  - **Benchmark authorship** — **Treasury, the NSA, and DHS/CISA** are tasked with developing the benchmarks that determine which models qualify as frontier AI.
- **Industry reaction:** **Google's Kent Walker** called the order **"an important step forward."** **OpenAI CEO Sam Altman** said the order sets **"the balance right."**

**Read-through:** Three reads. (1) **The voluntary-vs-mandatory distinction — flagged in yesterday's PM "open question (load-bearing)" — resolves to voluntary, and the resolution matters.** The May 21 pulled-EO draft left pre-deployment access on voluntary CAISI MOUs; many read the June 2 signing as the mandatory backstop. It is not. The order keeps the entire regime voluntary but builds *institutional scaffolding* around it (the Treasury clearinghouse, the Treasury/NSA/CISA benchmark mandate) — closer to "formalized voluntarism" than to a licensing regime. (2) **Both major-lab CEOs endorsing it on day one is the tell:** a mandatory pre-clearance order would have drawn pushback; instead Walker ("important step forward") and Altman ("the balance right") signal the frontier labs got the version they could live with. (3) **The 30-day window is still real for release planning even though it's opt-in** — Anthropic Code with Claude Tokyo (June 10) and any Q3 frontier release now have a voluntary-but-expected federal-review buffer to navigate, and the Treasury clearinghouse stands up inside 30 days (i.e., by early July), making the next concrete regulatory milestone the clearinghouse's formation rather than a statutory deadline.
