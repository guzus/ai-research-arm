### Senator Blackburn Releases TRUMP AMERICA AI Act Discussion Draft
**Date:** March 18, 2026 | **Sources:** [Senate.gov](https://www.blackburn.senate.gov/2026/3/technology/blackburn-releases-discussion-draft-of-national-policy-framework-for-artificial-intelligence/3b3b6458-b6c7-478b-9859-374949586765), [Axios](https://www.axios.com/2026/03/18/blackburn-updated-ai-plan-trump), [Bloomberg Government](https://news.bgov.com/bloomberg-government-news/national-ai-framework-to-override-state-laws-released-by-senate), [IAPP](https://iapp.org/news/a/us-sen-blackburn-proposes-ai-framework-to-protect-children-copyrights), [Politico](https://www.politico.com/live-updates/2026/03/18/congress/blackburn-ai-framework-seeks-to-codify-trump-ratepayer-pledge-00834829)

Senator Marsha Blackburn (R-Tenn.) released a discussion draft of the **TRUMP AMERICA AI Act**, a federal framework to codify President Trump's AI executive order and preempt state-level AI regulations with a single national rulebook:

- **"4 Cs" protection framework**: Children, Creators, Conservatives, and Communities.
- **Children**: Requires "duty of care" for AI chatbot/companion developers; incorporates the Kids Online Safety Act.
- **Creators**: Federal transparency rules via NIST for watermarking, authentication, and detection of AI-generated content; includes the NO FAKES Act; enables lawsuits for defective AI designs.
- **Conservatives**: Mandates third-party audits for political bias in AI; restricts federal procurement to "neutral, truthful" large language models.
- **Communities**: Quarterly DOL reports on AI job displacement; Energy Department agreements to shield consumers from data center electricity rate hikes; AI evaluation program for catastrophic risks including loss-of-control scenarios.
- **Innovation provisions**: NIST AI standards center, national lab testbeds, and prize competitions.
- **State preemption**: Overrides patchwork state AI laws with a single federal standard.
- Developed with White House input; seeks bipartisan support.

### Anthropic Counter-Response Due Today in Pentagon Lawsuit
**Date:** March 20, 2026 | **Sources:** [Times of India](https://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/technology/tech-news/pentagon-replies-to-anthropics-lawsuit-says-refusal-to-accept-the-governments-contractual-terms-is-not-protected-by/articleshow/129654744.cms), [CBS News](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/anthropic-pentagon-supply-chain-risk-lawsuit/), [Defense One](https://www.defenseone.com/business/2026/03/pentagons-war-anthropic-based-dubious-legal-thinking-and-ideologynot-real-risk-sources-say/411849/)

Anthropic's counter-response to the DOJ's 40-page reply is **due today, Friday March 20**, ahead of the **March 24 preliminary injunction hearing** in San Francisco federal court:

- The dispute centers on a **$200 million Pentagon contract** — Anthropic sought contractual guarantees that Claude would not be used for mass domestic surveillance or fully autonomous lethal weapons; the Pentagon demanded "all lawful use" access.
- After negotiations failed before a February 27 deadline, Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth designated Anthropic a **supply chain risk**, and President Trump ordered all federal agencies to cease using the company's technology.
- DOJ's March 17 reply framed the case as a **commercial contract dispute, not a First Amendment issue**.
- Claude remains the **only AI system cleared for classified military systems** amid ongoing operations.
- The March 24 hearing will determine whether to **pause the supply chain risk designation** while litigation continues.

### Mistral AI Launches Forge Enterprise Platform at NVIDIA GTC
**Date:** March 17, 2026 | **Sources:** [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/17/mistral-forge-nvidia-gtc-build-your-own-ai-enterprise/), [Economic Times](https://economictimes.com/tech/artificial-intelligence/mistral-ai-launches-privacy-focussed-ai-tool-forge-to-target-enterprise-clients/articleshow/129648479.cms), [Dataconomy](https://dataconomy.com/2026/03/18/mistral-launches-forge-platform-to-help-enterprises-build-cu/)

Mistral AI unveiled **Forge**, an enterprise platform for building fully custom AI models trained on private data, at NVIDIA GTC:

- Enables companies to **train models from scratch** on their own data — goes beyond fine-tuning or RAG.
- Runs **on-premises** for enhanced privacy and control, unlike cloud-reliant competitors.
- Supports Mistral's open-weight models including **Mistral Small 4** (119B MoE, Apache 2.0).
- **Agent-first design** with reinforcement learning, synthetic data pipelines, and forward-deployed engineers.
- Early adopters include **ASML, Ericsson, European Space Agency, Singapore's DSO/HTX, and Reply**.
- Targets government, finance, manufacturing, and tech sectors.
- Aligns with NVIDIA's push for on-premises GPU adoption; Mistral currently valued at **$14 billion**.

## New Model Releases

### Mistral Small 4 — Unified Hybrid Model
**Date:** March 16, 2026 | **Sources:** [Mistral AI](https://mistral.ai/news/mistral-small-4), [Silicon Republic](https://www.siliconrepublic.com/machines/mistral-ai-france-small-4-forge-enterprise), [NYU Shanghai](https://rits.shanghai.nyu.edu/ai/mistral-small-4-four-models-unified-in-one-open-source-moe/)

Mistral released Small 4, consolidating four separate model families into a single system:

- **119B total parameters**, 6B active per token (MoE with 128 experts, 4 active).
- **256K-token context window** with native multimodal (text + image) support.
- Unifies: **Mistral Small** (instruction following), **Magistral** (reasoning), **Pixtral** (multimodal), and **Devstral** (agentic coding).
- **40% latency reduction** and **3x throughput improvement** over Mistral Small 3.
- Matches or surpasses **OpenAI's GPT-OSS 120B** on long context reasoning, live coding, and math benchmarks.
- Released under **Apache 2.0** license; available via Mistral API, Hugging Face, and NVIDIA NIM.
- Minimum hardware: 4x NVIDIA HGX H100, 2x HGX H200, or 1x DGX B200.

### AWS and Cerebras Partner for Fastest AI Inference on Bedrock
**Date:** March 13, 2026 | **Sources:** [Cerebras](https://www.cerebras.ai/press-release/awscollaboration), [Cerebras Blog](https://www.cerebras.ai/blog/cerebras-is-coming-to-aws), [Data Center Dynamics](https://www.datacenterdynamics.com/en/news/aws-partners-with-big-chip-co-cerebras-for-ai-inference-disaggregation/)

AWS and Cerebras announced a multiyear partnership for AI inference, with deployment now underway:

- **Disaggregated inference architecture**: AWS Trainium handles parallel prefill (prompt processing); Cerebras **CS-3** wafer-scale chips accelerate serial decode (token generation).
- Claims **5x faster inference** than current GPU-based systems for real-time applications.
- **CS-3 specs**: Wafer-scale WSE-3 with 900,000 AI cores, 44 GB on-chip SRAM, up to 2,000+ tokens/second standalone.
- Will be accessible via **Amazon Bedrock** API supporting open-source LLMs and Amazon Nova models.
- General availability expected **second half of 2026**.
